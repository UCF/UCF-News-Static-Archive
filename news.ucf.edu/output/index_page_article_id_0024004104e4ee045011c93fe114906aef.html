UCF Remembers Victims During Domestic Violence Awareness Month

By Courtney Gilmartin Updated Sept. 29, 2008

Photo: Courtesy of UCF Victim Services

This Clothesline Project, put together by Verizon Wireless, is touring the state this fall, displaying T-shirts dedicated to those affected by domestic violence.

In recognition of October as National Domestic Violence Awareness Month, the University of Central Florida will host activities to remember victims and promote healthy relationships.

Through Friday, Sept. 26, a Clothesline exhibit in Millican Hall is dedicated to past Florida victims of dating and domestic violence.

The Clothesline Project is a national initiative that began in Cape Cod, Mass., to address violence against women and serves as a vehicle for women affected by violence to express themselves.

The display is made up of T-shirts that each tells a story of a domestic violence victim from Florida. The shirts are color-coded based on the type of abuse suffered and decorated by survivors of domestic violence or by the loved ones of victims lost.

One of this year’s shirts is designed by the parents of Michael Ruschak, a UCF student and victim of dating violence homicide in fall 2007.

The display, put together by Verizon Wireless, is touring the state this fall.

UCF is also creating its own Clothesline Project for the university community, to be displayed next month in the Student Union Atrium. Beginning on Wednesdays in October, a table will be located in front of the Student Union where students and faculty can decorate T-shirts for the project.

“It is important that members of our community understand that dating and domestic violence can happen to anyone,” said Christine Mouton, coordinator of UCF Victim Services.  “It impacts a person’s ability to socialize, work or attend school, and it can greatly impact a person’s health along with their mental and emotional wellbeing.”

On Oct. 6, the “Light Up the Night” event will take place by the Reflecting Pond.  Guest speakers, a live band and memorial walkers will recognize victims and survivors of dating and domestic violence in the community at the vigil, which begins at 6 p.m.  Attendees are asked to bring their cell phones, which will be used to light the event.

As part of the memorial walk, participants will stop for a moment of silence at a tree planted to honor UCF students Michael Ruschak and Tiffany Barwick. The Barwick-Ruschak bill will go into effect on Oct. 1 and will provide victims of dating violence the same rights as domestic violence victims.  During “Light Up the Night,” UCF Police Chief Richard Beary and other speakers will address the impact of the bill.

On Oct. 22, the fourth-annual “Strike Back Against Dating/Domestic Violence” event will be held in front of the Student Union.  “Strike Back” will bring members of the UCF community together to learn more about dating and domestic violence.

Campus dignitaries will kick off the event at 10:30 a.m. by signing the Pledge to End Violence banner.  Throughout the day, members of the UCF community are invited to sign the banner, which will be displayed at Victim Services’ events.

The “Walk a Mile In Her Shoes” event will also be happening throughout the day on Oct. 22.  The event, sponsored by Harbor House of Orange County Domestic Violence Shelter, will have male volunteers walking around the Union in women’s shoes in an effort to raise awareness about the plight of female abuse victims.

For more information on domestic violence or for support, visit http://victimservices.ucf.edu.
