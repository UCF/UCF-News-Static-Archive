Fourteen UCF Professors, Librarian Granted Emeritus Status

By Kristi Bartlett April 5, 2006

Photo: Jacque Brund

Fourteen UCF professors and a librarian received emeritus status during Founders' Day festivities on Wednesday.

Fifteen University of Central Florida faculty members who have made major professional contributions and achieved eminence in their work at UCF were awarded emeritus status Wednesday at the annual Founders Day ceremony.

Emeritus professors have worked at the university for at least five years, and many are retired or will do so later this year. Emeritus status grants them a lifetime link to UCF.

Karl-Heinrich Barsch, Professor Emeritus, will retire this spring from the Department of Modern Languages after 29 years on the faculty. Barsch, who speaks nine languages, developed the core French classes at UCF and directed the UCF Intensive Summer Programs in France and Québec for two decades. He established partnerships with many universities in Europe, the Middle East, Asia and Latin America as coordinator of international studies, and he also was director of the Soviet Area Studies Program.

Steve Lotz, Professor Emeritus, was a charter member of the UCF faculty and the universitys first art instructor. He served as the Art Departments first chairman from 1968 to 1978. Lotz retired from UCF four years ago. His peers consider him a top exhibiting painter, and his artwork has been displayed at the 1972 Munich Olympics, Orlando International Airport, Universal Studios and Walt Disney World. He has received two State of Florida Individual Artist Fellowships.

Bruce Pauley, Professor Emeritus, will retire this spring from the Department of History. He joined the faculty in 1971 and was promoted to full professor in 1977. Pauley is one of the most respected historians on Central Europe and has written five books on the subject. He also played a prominent role in the establishment of the graduate program in history. He received two Teaching Incentive Program Awards and a College Excellence in Undergraduate Teaching Award.

Roseann White, Professor Emeritus, has taught in the Department of Molecular Biology and Microbiology for 36 years. Whites many teaching honors include the Outstanding Educator of the Year and two Teaching Incentive Awards from UCF and the Golden Apple Award from the Pre-Med American Medical Student Association. She is an invited fellow at the American Academy of Allergy, Asthma and Immunology and has overseen the expanding instructional programs at the Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences.

Martin Wanielista, Professor Emeritus, has taught in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering since 1970. He also is director of the UCF Stormwater Management Academy. Wanielista became a full professor in 1976 and served as dean of the College of Engineering and Computer Science from 1993 to 2002. He was director of the Environmental Engineering Institute from 1971 to 1974 and was named Engineer of the Year by the state and Central Florida Engineering Societies in 1999.

Dalton Bandy, Professor Emeritus, will retire from the Kenneth G. Dixon School of Accounting in May. He helped to bring national recognition to the accounting program during his 20 years as a UCF faculty member. Bandy served as program advisor for the Masters in Taxation program and as interim director of the Dixon School for two years. He has also received the National Association of Accountants Certificate of Merit and the Beta Alpha Psi Outstanding Faculty Vice President Award.

Thomas Evans, Professor Emeritus, joined the Dixon School of Accounting in 1990 as a professor and director of the school. While serving on the Deloitte Wildman Medal Award Committee, he brought international recognition to UCF. Evans also represented the School of Accounting on the Central Florida Chapter of the Financial Executives Institute Board of Directors. In 2003-2004, he served as a faculty fellow at the UCF Faculty Center for Teaching and Learning.

Lloyd Fernald, Professor Emeritus, has been a member of the Management Department faculty for 22 years. He came to UCF after a successful career in the U.S. Navy. He is a past president and fellow of the International Council of Small Business and a fellow of the United States Association of Small Business and Entrepreneurship. Fernald also served as project coordinator of the National Entrepreneurship Center, which embodies UCFs mission to be the nations leading partnership university.

Frank Rohter, Professor Emeritus, was a charter faculty member in the College of Education in 1968. From 1968 to 1974, he served as the director of athletics and the chair of the Department of Physical Education. Rohter directed the Institute of Exercise Physiology and Wellness from 1985-2005. He received the College of Education Teacher of the Year Award in 1992 and was recently inducted into the UCF Intercollegiate Hall of Fame. He also has competed in more than 350 triathlons.

Steven Sorg, Professor Emeritus, has served on the College of Education faculty for 28 years. As a part of the Department of Educational Research, Technology and Leadership, he has been a pioneer in distance learning initiatives and was one of the first UCF professors to teach an online course. UCF has since received many awards for distance-learning programs. Sorg won the 1995 Governors Peace at Home Award: Stopping Domestic Violence for his research on the educational needs of women at shelters.

Mary Van Hook, Professor Emeritus, has been a member of the faculty in the School of Social Work since 1995, and she became a full professor in 1997. She served as the director of the school from 1998 to 2003 and was acting director in 2005. Van Hook was instrumental in developing the doctoral program in Public Affairs. She received the national Victory Howard Award for her contributions to the field of rural mental health. Van Hook is a former director of an Iowa community mental health program.

Haven Sweet, Professor Emeritus, will retire from the Department of Biology in July after 35 years at UCF. He spent 10 of those years as assistant and associate dean of the College of Arts and Sciences. He has received three teaching awards from UCF, including the University Excellence in Undergraduate Teaching award in 1988. Sweet is the author of two textbooks and many research articles, and his use of imaging techniques to determine vegetation cover from satellite photographs has aided conservation efforts.

Richard Tucker, Professor Emeritus, joined the Department of Psychology in 1972. He served as the department chair for 14 years and was chair of the Creative School for Children for four years. Tucker organized the Learning Institute for Elders, known as LIFE. The adult residential treatment facility at Threshold Inc. was named Tucker House in his honor. He has received many awards for his research in gerontology, including the Distinguished Service Award from the Gerontology Society of America.

Margaret Thomas, Professor Emeritus, has been a Department of Psychology faculty member since 1971. She has served as an assistant dean and as interim chair of Psychology. She played a prominent role in establishing the Clinical Psychology doctoral program and in developing the Psychology program at regional campuses. Thomas was one of the first professors to teach online courses. Her research has been published in many top-tier journals, including the Journal of Personality and Social Psychology.

Phyllis Hudson, Librarian Emeritus, began her career in the UCF Library in 1972. After her retirement in 2002, she remained as an adjunct librarian until 2004. She played an important role in UCFs initiation of online cataloging as a charter member of the Southeastern Library Network. Hudson was known statewide and on campus for her work in collective bargaining. From 2000 to 2002, she served as a member of the Orange County Library System Board of Directors.
