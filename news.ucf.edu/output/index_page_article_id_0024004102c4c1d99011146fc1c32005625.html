'Lend Me a Tenor' Performances Set for July 12 to 22

By UCF Staff July 9, 2007

Photo: Joseph Rusnock

Tad Ingram, Taylor Jeffers, Amanda Wansa and Michael Pettey (left to right) star in the UCF Conservatory Theatre production of "Lend Me a Tenor."

Full Photo

Its a night of chaos, mayhem and tomfoolery when the UCF Conservatory Theatre winds up the SummerStage season with the award-winning comedy Lend Me a Tenor.

The hilarious farce Lend Me a Tenor, mixing mistaken identities, competing opera stars and a symphony of slamming doors, is the third offering in the 2007 SummerStage season. The sensation on Broadway and in Londons West End includes a fast-paced plot and features mistaken identities, thwarted love and status reversals, Max, the opera company gofer, is thrilled to hear that his idol, the great Tito Merelli, will be performing in his home city of Cleveland. But joy turns to terror when events conspire to push him on stage in place of the celebrated tenor.

Lend Me a Tenor will be performed Thursday, July 12, through Sunday, July 15, and Thursday, July 19, through Sunday, July 22. Evening shows start at 8 p.m., and Sunday matinees begin at 2 p.m. The show takes place in the Mainstage Theatre on the UCF main campus. Tickets cost $15, $13 for seniors over 55 and $10 for students with IDs. The show includes some adult content.

Reservations are recommended and available through the UCF Conservatory Theatre Box Office by calling 407-823-1500. UCF offers accommodations to make the theater more accessible to patrons with disabilities  call the box office ahead for assistance. Group rates are available.

Premiering in 1989, Lend Me a Tenor has received two Tony Awards, four Drama Desk Awards and three Outer Critics Circle Awards. Audiences worldwide have enjoyed the famous comedy, which has been produced in eight languages.
