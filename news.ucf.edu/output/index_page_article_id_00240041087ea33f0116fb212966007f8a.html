UCF College of Medicine Scholarship Drive Reaches Milestone

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala Dec. 21, 2007

Photo: Jacque Brund

The College of Medicine has secured half of the scholarship commitments needed to cover its first class.

Full Photo

  • Charles Roberts, Assistant Vice President for Development, College of Medicine

    • 407-823-4539

  • College of Medicine

In just six months, the University of Central Florida has secured half of the 40 scholarship commitments it needs to cover tuition and living expenses for the College of Medicine’s first class.

As the college awaits word from the Liaison Committee on Medical Education about whether it gets preliminary accreditation and can start recruiting students next year, university employees and volunteers continue to raise the $6.4 million needed to provide scholarships for those future students. It’s the first time a medical school is offering full scholarships to all members of its incoming class.

UCF received funding for its 20th scholarship this week.

The scholarships will cover tuition, fees, and room and board for the length of the program. Dean Deborah German’s vision is to have all students in that first class on scholarship so their financial burden is lessened and they can concentrate on becoming the best doctors they can be. She benefited from scholarships when she went to Harvard Medical School and personally knows what a difference they can make.

“I am thrilled we have reached this milestone,” Dr. German said. “I am thankful for those leaders who have stepped forward and invested in the health of our community. I can think of no better contribution to our future.”

She also reminded local residents that as the year comes to a close, a scholarship is a gift that benefits not just the recipient, but generations to come.

“As you finish your holiday shopping, consider giving a student an opportunity to attend medical school at virtually no cost,” she said. “One of these students could one day be treating your grandchildren or great-grandchildren.”

To see a list of scholarship donors visit http://www.foundation.ucf.edu. To help with the remaining scholarships contact Charles Roberts, Assistant Vice President for Development, College of Medicine at 407-823-4539 or cwrobert@mail.ucf.edu.

The college hopes to begin instruction leading to the M.D. degree in fall 2009 and it eventually will graduate 120 medical students each year. The college, along with its medical library, will be located at the UCF Health Sciences Campus at Lake Nona on 50 acres. The site also will include the UCF Burnett Biomedical Sciences Building and other health science programs and organizations. The California-based Burnham Institute for Medical Research will also call Lake Nona home, turning the area into a medical city.

UCF Stands for Opportunity: Established in 1963, the University of Central Florida is a diverse metropolitan research university that ranks as the 6th-largest in the country with more than 48,000 students. Located in Orlando, Fla., UCF offers high-quality undergraduate and graduate education, student development, and continuing education, while conducting cutting-edge research that powers the region’s economic development.
