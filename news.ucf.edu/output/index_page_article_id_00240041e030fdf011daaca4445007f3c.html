UCF Celebrates International Education With Peace Advocate Naomi Tutu

By Courtney Gilmartin Updated Nov. 20, 2008

Internationally known human-rights advocate Nontombi “Naomi” Tutu encouraged a University of Central Florida audience to embrace each other’s differences Monday during a breakfast that kicked off UCF’s annual International Education Week.

Tutu is the daughter of Nobel Peace Prize recipient South African Archbishop Desmond Tutu and the founder of the Tutu Foundation. A passionate advocate for peace and human rights, Naomi Tutu is known for her consulting work in South Africa, bettering educational and professional opportunities for black women.

At UCF’s International Breakfast, a new event this year, Tutu shared her experiences as an African woman during apartheid in South Africa. The end of apartheid, she said, was “not simply a miracle, it was about people saying we need to find that place we have in common.”

Tutu also encouraged people to embrace each other’s differences by seeking a common ground.

“So very often when we look at those who are different, we try to simplify things by giving a label,” said Tutu.  “Those attempts to simplify are the things that set us against each other.”

“When you start saying we are seeking common ground, the first step is allowing the other person to tell their own story.”

Among the other activities planned for this year’s International Education Week are: international cuisine prep workshops; a genealogy expo that will teach people the basics of tracing their roots; a special photo exhibit of China by UCF students; 20-minute language classes; and the annual Study Abroad Fair. All of those events are open to the public.

International Education Week is a celebration of international education and exchange worldwide. Held at schools around the country, the event is a joint initiative between the U.S. departments of State and Education. At least a dozen UCF departments are involved with this year’s activities.

The nearly 1,560 international students enrolled at UCF this semester came from 129 different countries. 

As an international student, Tutu was educated in Swaziland, England and the United States.

“International education is something I feel a personal passion for,” said Tutu. “If there is nothing else that international education offers, it is the opportunity to hear different stories from around the world and find in them our common ground.”

UCF President John Hitt spoke at the breakfast about his goal to promote international education within the university and to help students become global citizens. Orisi Risi, a Nigerian husband-and-wife team, shared African folklore through interactive storytelling, song and dance. The breakfast also featured a performance by Vasily Yurin, a Russian classical guitarist who also is a UCF student.

More than 1,000 international faculty members, students, staff and others with multicultural backgrounds are expected at the annual Thanksgiving Dinner, also part of this week’s festivities. This “traditional American Thanksgiving dinner” will be hosted by UCF’s International Students Association in the UCF Arena, and is aimed at sharing the meaning of a holiday that celebrates diversity and kindness. The dinner will run from 6 to 8 p.m. Friday, Nov. 21.

At 2 p.m. Thursday, Nov. 20, Dr. Ellen Mack, a California physician, will share her experiences related to Iran's people, culture, arts and architecture in room 226 of the Psychology Building. Much of her story is told through an architecture that includes engineering solutions to the harsh environmental conditions of the desert, new innovations that revolutionized Muslim architecture, and classic examples of architecture that signify some of the great dynasties that ruled the world of the time.

UCF departments participating in International Education Week include the International Services Center, Office of International Studies, Office of Global Perspectives, Center for Multilingual Multicultural Studies, College of Education, Burnett Honors College, Student Development and Enrollment Services, Office of the Provost and Executive Vice President, Faculty Relations, Office of Internationalization, Recreation and Wellness Center and Dining Services.

For a schedule or more information, go to www.intl.ucf.edu/internationaleducationweek.
