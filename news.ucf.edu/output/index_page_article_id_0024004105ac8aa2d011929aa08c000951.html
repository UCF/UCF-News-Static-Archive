10 Years Strong: UCF's Burnett Honors College Celebrates Its Students, Programs

By Christine Dellert April 7, 2008

Photo: Jacque Brund

The Burnett Honors College unveiled its new statue, "Wind Dancer," at Friday's 10th anniversary reception.

From cramped quarters in Phillips Hall to its own building and meditation garden, UCF’s honors college has grown in more than size during the past decade.

Its students and faculty are part of a culture of academic excellence, which inspires lifelong learning and civic responsibility.

On Friday, April 4, The Burnett Honors College celebrated its 10th anniversary at the University of Central Florida and kicked off a year of events and activities for honors students, faculty and alumni.

“I’m so proud of its history and its accomplishments,” UCF President John Hitt told a crowd of about 75 gathered in the college building’s atrium Friday evening.

University leaders and Burnett Honors College Dean Alvin Wang unveiled a new aluminum flying-horse sculpture, “Wind Dancer.” It is the largest 3-D Pegasus statue on campus and was created by a former UCF employee and award-winning sculptor, Mindy Colton.

Also announced was the recipient of the first Allyn M. Stearman Scholarship, named after the college’s founding dean. Naomi Brownstein, a senior in the college majoring in actuarial science and mathematics, was recognized. She is also an Honors in the Major student, part of UCF’s oldest and most prestigious undergraduate research program. 

The college’s yearlong anniversary celebration will continue with student research showcases and alumni events.

“It’s amazing how much everything has grown,” said Nancy Burnett, who attended Friday’s reception with her husband, Al. “When we first set foot on this campus, there were two or three buildings.”

The Burnetts made possible the college’s 10,250-square-foot-building, which opened in 2002. The couple has generously donated to the honors college and other campus institutions, including the Burnett School of Biomedical Sciences in the College of Medicine.

While The Burnett Honors College is “young,” UCF’s honors program is in its 26th year, Wang said. The college enrolls about 500 new students each fall, compared to fewer than 30 who started in UCF’s original Honors Program.

“Each year our class is growing in terms of quality,” Wang said. “We are truly taking the best and the brightest, and we are increasingly the honors college of first choice among our freshmen.”

The latest batch of students boasted an average SAT score of 1361 and an above-4.0 high school GPA.

In the classroom, the college offers in-depth, advanced and interdisciplinary courses ranging from genetic engineering to the history of baseball.

The college also exposes students to a variety of academic enrichment and community or service-learning programs.

The college’s Honors Educational Reach Out, or HERO program, has touched thousands of elementary school students in Central Florida through teaching leadership in the classroom and at after-school programs and helping children learn to read. In just more than three years, its freshman symposium has become the largest service-learning partnership in the country linking an honors college, public schools and the nonprofit Junior Achievement.

“We want to give our students a well-rounded experience,” said Kelly Astro, the director of Research and Civic Engagement for the college. “We want them to do well academically, but also experience what it means to give back to the community and to be engaged and responsible citizens.”

Honors students are also encouraged to get involved on campus. Last year, the college partnered with Housing and Residence Life to create a living-learning community in Tower III. A state-of-the-art classroom extends learning opportunities to the residence halls, giving faculty the chance to participate in student gatherings.

Honors students also have access to a reading room, computer lab and multimedia classrooms at six-year-old The Burnett Honors College building, which also features a tropical meditation garden.

Notable honors alumni include UCF’s first Rhodes Scholar, Tyler Fisher, who is now studying European literature at Oxford University.  Fisher graduated in 2002 with a 4.0 GPA and degrees in English and Spanish after successfully defending his thesis -- an English translation of Cuban Jose Marti’s “Ismaelillo.”

While Fisher sought help from the college’s Honors Study Abroad Scholarship, Lalita Booth worked with the Office of Student Scholarship and Fellowship Advisement -- also housed in the college -- on her recent Truman Scholar application. Last week, Booth, a Burnett Honors College junior, was announced as UCF’s first winner of the highly prestigious national award for college student leaders.

“Ultimately, the success of The Burnett Honors College is measured by the success of its alumni who embrace the pursuit of life-long learning, become engaged citizens of the global community, and go on to become leaders in their field,” Wang said.

A History of The Burnett Honors College:

1982 -- The Honors Program is established.

1988 -- Honors Freshman Symposium is offered for the first time.

1989 -- Mark Stern is appointed the first director of the Honors Program.

1989 -- Honors in the Major is offered for the first time.

1994 -- Katherine Keller becomes the program’s director.

1996 -- Allyn Stearman is appointed program director and later becomes the founding dean of The Burnett Honors College.

1998 -- The Honors Program is granted college status.

2001 -- The Honors Alumni chapter is chartered.

2002 -- Honors student Tyler Fisher is named UCF’s first Rhodes Scholar.

2002 -- The Burnett Honors College building is completed with the generous gift of Al and Nancy Burnett.

2002 -- Inaugural year of The Burnett Honors College Summer Institute.

2004 -- President’s Scholars Program is created.

2005 -- Alvin Wang is appointed as the college’s second dean.

2007 -- Tower III inaugurated as UCF’s Honors Living-Learning Community.

2008 -- University Honors and Honors in the Major student Lalita Booth becomes UCF’s first Truman Scholar.

About the college: The Burnett Honors College combines the intimacy of a small liberal arts college with the benefits of a large, metropolitan research university. The mission of the college is to provide a challenging academic program and a foundation for future achievement to UCF's most academically talented students. The college strives to be the best in creating a diverse learning community that fosters the pursuit of excellence, a sense of social and civic responsibility, and a passion for life-long learning.

To learn more, visit http://www.honors.ucf.edu.
