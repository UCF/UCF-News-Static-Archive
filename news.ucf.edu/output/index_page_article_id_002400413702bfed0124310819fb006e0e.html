UCF to Enrich Kennedy Space Center Experience for Students, Teachers

Oct. 16, 2009

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Michael Hynes, College of Education

    • 407-823- 2005

  • Print this Page

Share this story:

  • Seed Newsvine

The UCF College of Education signed a new agreement that will help area teachers bring NASA’s vast resources in life science, astronomy, energy and more into their classrooms.

A team of UCF educators will help to manage Kennedy Space Center’s Educator Resource Center (ERC), a vast collection of audio-visual and printed materials about NASA research and technology. UCF’s goal is to provide current and future teachers with more opportunities for developing exciting, real-world lessons based on shuttle missions, cutting-edge scientific research and more.

“This leadership role gives us the opportunity to link our math and science students to ERC and the great work they do there,” said Michael Hynes, chair of the Department of Teaching and Learning Principles. “The center is a rich resource for teachers all over the state.”

The facilities and staff at the center assist teachers in gathering ideas, conducting research and duplicating selections from an outstanding collection of audio-visual and printed materials based on years of NASA work.

The materials explain NASA research and technology development in such curriculum areas as life science, physical science, astronomy, energy, earth resources, the environment, mathematics, geography and careers in aerospace.

The ERC serves all of Florida, and special opportunities to visit and study at the ERC are offered to audiences that may enhance the education of children from pre-kindergarten through high school. For example, minority college students who are aspiring teachers have studied for two weeks at the ERC. 

Hynes and associate professors Bobby Jeanpierre and Mary Romjue will be leading UCF’s efforts over the next two years.

Hynes said the partnership will provide students at UCF and other Florida universities, as well as teachers across the country, access to unique NASA experiences that will get them excited and motivated to teach science and math.

For more information, visit http://education.ksc.nasa.gov/erc/erc.htm
