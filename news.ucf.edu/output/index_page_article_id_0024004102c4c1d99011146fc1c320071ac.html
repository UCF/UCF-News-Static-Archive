UCF Trustees Approve Revised Financing Plan for Health Sciences Buildings

By Tom Evelyn April 9, 2007

Photo: UCF Staff

Site work has begun on the UCF Health Sciences Campus in Lake Nona, as shown in this March aerial photo.

The University of Central Florida Board of Trustees on Monday approved a revised financing plan to pay for construction of the buildings that will house the Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences and the College of Medicine.

The plan was revised following updated building designs that increased the square footage of the College of Medicine and reduced the initial cost of the Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences. The colleges will form the foundation for the UCF Health Sciences Campus at Lake Nona that eventually will include other UCF health sciences programs such as the College of Nursing.

The financing plan includes a combination of private donations, matching funds, state construction dollars and bond issues. The bonding portion will be forwarded to the Florida Board of Governors for final approval.

Site work has started at Lake Nona for the Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences building. The 198,000-square-foot building is scheduled to be completed by April 2009. One of the five floors will be left unfinished and will be completed later as the program expands.

Construction of the College of Medicine building is set to begin in December and be completed by June 2009, in time for the college to welcome its inaugural class that fall. The size of the building was increased from an early design to about 175,000 square feet to accommodate plans for the program being developed by College of Medicine Dean Deborah German.

German is building the curriculum and hiring associate deans and faculty to meet accreditation requirements. The university will submit the initial database to the accrediting body, the Liaison Committee on Medical Education, this month. The LCME is scheduled to hold a site visit in December, and the college hopes to receive preliminary accreditation by February, after which it can begin recruiting the first class.

Temporary Space, Scholarships

The college is scheduled to move into temporary space next week in the University Tower building in the Central Florida Research Park, adjacent to the main UCF campus. The college will occupy more than 16,000 square feet on the third floor that will include administrative and faculty offices, lab space, a lecture hall and a library.

The Research Park space will allow the college to grow while the Lake Nona space is being built. If completion of that building were to be delayed, the college would be able to teach the first class of 40 students at University Tower.

The college is raising money to provide full, one-year scholarships that include living expenses for that entire first class. German has commitments for three of the scholarships worth $40,000 each.

UCF has raised more than $100 million in private donations and state matching funds to pay for construction of the first two buildings at Lake Nona. Donations and matching funds will cover the entire cost of the estimated $68 million building that will house the medical college and library.

The university is proposing to borrow up to $60 million in bond issues to pay for the cost of the Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences building that is not covered by pledges, matching funds and state construction dollars already appropriated. The Burnett building is now budgeted at nearly $99 million.

The financing plan for the Burnett building also must be approved by the Florida Board of Governors because it involves the bond issues. The Board of Governors is expected to consider the plan at its June 14 meeting.
