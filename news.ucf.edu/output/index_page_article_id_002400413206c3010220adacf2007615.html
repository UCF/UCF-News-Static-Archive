Students Can Learn More About Journalism Jobs at Upcoming Fair

By Christine Dellert March 27, 2005

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Christine Dellert

    • 727-460-0267

  • Print this Page

Recruiters from more than 30 newspapers, television and radio stations and other media outlets have been invited to meet with students at the UCF Multimedia Job/Internship Fair on Monday, April 4.

 

All students are invited to attend the fair from 10 a.m. to 3 p.m. in the Cape Florida Ballroom, Room 316, at the Student Union.

 

Media that plan to attend include the St. Petersburg Times, the Tampa Tribune, the Orlando Sentinel, WESH-Channel 2, Bay News 9, Create Magazine, Florida Media, Orlando Weekly, the Florida Times-Union and the Central Florida Advocate.

 

The fair is the largest journalism job fair students at the Nicholson School of Communication have hosted. It was organized by three student groups: the Society of Professional Journalists, the National Association of Black Journalists and the Radio Television News Directors Association.

 

This is an excellent opportunity for students to make connections that could lead to an exciting job or internship in reporting, editing or production, Brunson said.

 

Students interested in interviewing with specific companies are advised to sign up in advance. Interview schedules are posted outside journalism instructor Rick Brunsons office, which is room 212 in the communications building.

 

For more information, contact Christine Dellert, UCF SPJ president, at ucf_spjprez@hotmail.com or Rick Brunson at rbrunson@pegasus.cc.ucf.edu. Casual business attire is recommended at the event, and students who attend should remember to bring copies of their resumes.
