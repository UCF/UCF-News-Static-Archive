New Health Center Offers More Services, Shorter Waits for UCF Students

By Chad Binette Oct. 6, 2005

Photo: Jacque Brund

The pharmacy in the new Student Health Center is much larger, and it carries a broader selection of healthy food.

The University of Central Floridas new Student Health Center offers expanded services in womens health, shorter waits for students who need medical care and a larger pharmacy that sells healthy food.

Three times as large as the previous Student Health Center, the 48,000-square-foot building began full operations last week. The three-story center includes six color-coded medical units that focus on areas of medicine such as primary care, womens health, chiropractic care and sports medicine.

The new center also has a larger waiting area and 48 examination rooms, up from 13 in the old building. X-Rays are given using the latest digital technology, allowing technicians to transmit them electronically to doctors on other floors and radiologists off campus.

The quality of our students health care is extremely important to us, said Bob Faust, director of Health Services. We have eagerly awaited the opening of a cutting-edge health center to complement our high-quality doctors and nurses and help to ensure that our students receive the best care possible.

The Health Center saw 87,000 patients and filled 67,000 prescriptions during the 2004-05 school year. The additional examination rooms will help UCFs medical staff see more patients. There often were few or no extra waiting rooms available in the old building. Now a doctors next patient can be ready in an examination room as soon as a prior appointment is completed in another room.

Were hoping that our patient care will be quicker and in a nicer environment, said Dr. Michael Deichen, associate director of clinical services. The added space also will allow us to grow in a variety of directions that will be good for our students. Well be better suited to expand into other types of specialty care because well have space to lease to an orthopedist, dermatologist or other specialists.

The new center includes about 3,700 square feet that has not been designated yet. Some or all of that space could be leased to specialists.

Students who are injured playing intramural sports or sustain other athletic-related injuries will benefit from a medical unit designed to eventually accommodate sports medicine. Varsity athletes will be able to receive treatment for injuries that their trainers do not handle. Massage therapists also will work out of the new Student Health Center.

The center includes an isolation room where students with highly communicable diseases could be temporarily placed if necessary. Those rooms have ventilation systems that prevent air inside the room from escaping.

A larger laboratory that can analyze blood work and other tests while patients wait is run by the Cognoscenti Health Institute, a company based in the Central Florida Research Park.

The $8 million center, which was funded primarily with student health fees, was built by Skanska USA of New Jersey and designed by Hunton Brady Architects of Orlando and Mateu Architecture Inc. of Miami. Construction began in October 2004.

The former Student Health Center is being completely renovated and will house the UCF Counseling Center, Alcohol and Other Drug Prevention Programming offices and some services of REACH for Wellness.
