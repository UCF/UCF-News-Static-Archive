Orlando City Council Votes to Lease Expo Centre to UCF

By Chad Binette Oct. 11, 2004

Photo: Jerry Klein

Renovation work should begin shortly at the Expo Centre. The renovations will take care of hurricane damage, such as the loss of letters on the building, in addition to a new roof and heating and air-conditioning system.

The Orlando City Council agreed Monday to lease the Expo Centre to UCF and contribute up to $4.4 million to renovate the building for use by the School of Film and Digital Media.

UCFs new Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy, where experienced software programmers and artists will teach students to design, program and test video games, will open in fall 2005 at the Expo Centre. Other programs in the School of Film and Digital Media will gradually move downtown once the building is remodeled.

The School of Film and Digital Media enrolls about 1,300 students and offers programs related to film production and distribution, the Internet, and the art of telling a story through animation or other visual methods.

The Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy, which is part of the School of Film and Digital Media, will open in fall 2005 with as many as 50 students and about 20 faculty and staff members.

UCF estimates that nearly 400 FIEA and other School of Film and Digital Media students, faculty and staff will be based in the Expo Centre in 2006. That total is projected to reach nearly 1,600 by 2008 and nearly 3,400 by 2014.

We look forward to increasing UCFs presence in a downtown that is becoming more vibrant thanks to the visions of Mayor Dyer and the City Council, UCF President John Hitt said. We also hope our new program will help companies in Central Floridas interactive entertainment and simulation industries find the exceptional employees they need to thrive and grow in our region.

The City Council voted Monday afternoon to approve the lease, which gives UCF use of the Expo Centres second and third floors as of Nov. 1. The first floor will be given to UCF in phases from 2005 to 2007. The citys Community Redevelopment Agency will contribute up to $4.4 million toward the estimated $6.3 million of renovations. UCF will fund the other $1.9 million.

UCF will pay the city $1 a year during the 40-year term of the lease. The city and UCF can agree to renew the lease for two additional 10-year terms. If UCF complies with the lease, the universitys foundation will have an option to negotiate with the city to buy the Expo Centre after March 2007.

Renovating the Expo Centre, 500 W. Livingston St., meets one of the goals of the citys Downtown Community Redevelopment Plan.

Mayor Buddy Dyer praised UCFs expansion in the downtown area and joked that hes looking forward to a time when the main campus becomes known as the East Campus because there will be as many students downtown as there are on the main campus.

Hitt addressed the City Council during its meeting Monday afternoon. Provost Terry Hickey and Dean Kathryn Seidel of the College of Arts and Sciences spoke during a council workshop Monday morning.

The Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy eventually could have as many as 300 students at one time. Students in the 16-month program focus either on the artistic or programming aspects of creating entertainment. Classes will deal with topics such as business and entrepreneurial skills, project management and the critical study of games.

Students who complete the program will receive graduate certificates, which allow for more flexibility than masters degrees, and will be well-prepared for careers in the video game, simulation and animation industries.
