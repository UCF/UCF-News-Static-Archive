Library, African American Studies Employees Win Monthly Honors

By Mallery Laing Dec. 16, 2004

Photo: Courtesy of Human Resources

Raynette Kibbee won the October Employee of the Month award.

Full Photo

Two UCF alumni who coordinate events for the Library and African American Studies respectively were honored as the October and November employees of the month.

 

Raynette Kibbee, an administrative assistant at the Library and a UCF employee for 19 ½ years, was named the USPS employee of the month for October. Novembers employee of the month, Koren Bedeau, coordinates and promotes lectures and special programming for African American Studies.

 

Kibbee works as director of the Librarys calendar, correspondence and general support. She sets up meetings, assists and coordinates events and updates Web pages. She said her most important job is to take care of the people with whom she works.

 

I try to listen when they have concerns, even personal issues, she said. For those I supervise, I try to be sure they are growing in their jobs and getting the reinforcement that they need to gain confidence and better their skills. I think any organization operates better when employees feel appreciated and know that what they do is important.

 

Kibbee graduated in 1974 from UCF. Her mother, Shirley Shofner, worked in the deans office and the theater and history departments from the 1960s until she retired in the 1980s. Kibbee started working at UCF in 1985 after she was an employee of Publix supermarkets.

 

The day I started working here was one of the most important days of my life, she said. Working here has given me a sense of confidence and pride that I didnt have before. Ive learned and grown professionally and personally in ways that I would never have dreamed possible. The environment or growth, change, and knowledge pervades everything we do and its so exciting and enriching. I feel like I should be giving the university an award.

 

Kibbees supervisor says she is creative, resourceful and brings dedication, a strong sense of responsibility and wonderful experience to UCF libraries. Working with her is a joy.

Koren Bedeau has worked in the African American Studies program for four years. She advises students pursuing the African American studies minor and provides administrative support to the director and faculty in the program.

 

After attaining her bachelors degree in liberal studies from UCF, Bedeau has worked hard to gain her master's in the same subject. Her bachelors degree focused on business, behavioral science, and health science, while her masters degree specialty is research in cultural tourism.

 

I like to experience new and different environments and enjoy meeting new people, Bedeau said.

 

Her supervisor says, Korens work as program assistant has been invaluable. Her attention to detail and ability to see the whole picture has been a great asset in planning and presenting the JT Washington Luncheon each year. Additionally, she has done an outstanding job in training the student assistants who work in our office. She works patiently with them in teaching them how to do their job.

 

Surprised and happy to learn of the award, Bedeau said its great knowing her efforts are genuinely appreciated. Id really like to thank Dr. Kevin Meehan for taking the time to nominate me and for showing his support in this way, she said.
