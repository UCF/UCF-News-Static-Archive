UCF Wins National Society of Experiential Education Program of the Year Award

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala Oct. 11,2006

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Sheri Dressler

    • 407-823-2667

  • Print this Page

A University of Central Florida program that helps students find internships, co-op courses and service-learning opportunities has been recognized as the program of the year by the National Society of Experiential Education.

UCF will receive the award Thursday, Oct. 12, during the NSEE national conference in Nashville, Tenn. The award is given to one educational institution each year for its commitment, quality and collaboration efforts in blending academics with real-world experiences for students in college.

We maintain academic rigor for students participating in experiential learning, said Sheri Dressler, director of Experiential Learning  We also provide a seamless system of programs and services by collaborating in an umbrella organization with Career Services and partnering with other UCF departments, faculty and community partners. 

Students at UCF get real-world experiences related to their majors through a menu of options. Internships are offered in many majors. There are also co-op courses such as those offered in the Rosen College of Hospitality Management. Co-op courses occur over several semesters, usually with the same employer. As the student learns from his academic classes, the responsibility in the work environment also increases. The experience occurs off campus and is paid.

Another option is service learning, in which students apply course information to complete service projects in the community and learn about civic responsibility. Last year, UCF service-learning students provided 80,000 hours of service to the community and government agencies through these projects.

Experiential Learning coordinates those programs and supports facultys efforts to create quality courses and learn from each other. The Office of the Provost provides extra funding to support faculty who integrate real-life experiences into their coursework, and campus-wide criteria have been adopted to promote high-quality courses.

Our mission is to have all UCF students graduate with some applied learning experience because it will make a significant difference to their future success, Dressler said.

Last year more than 23,000 UCF students participated in experiential learning courses. Experiential Learning works with more than 1,500 employers and nonprofits that provide student-learning experiences. For more information, go to www.coop.ucf.edu .
