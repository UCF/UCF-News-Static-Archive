UCF's 'Frozen Smoke' Fires Up Huffington Post

By Grant J. Heston March 8, 2011

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Grant J. Heston

    • 407.823.5988

  • Print this Page

A spongy substance that could be mistaken for packing material has national media buzzing about the University of Central Florida.

Researchers have engineered the world’s lightest carbon material in such a way that it could be used to detect pollutants and toxic substances, improve robotic surgery techniques and store energy more efficiently.

The Huffington Post, MSNBC and Discovery News are among the national and industry media that have covered this story. Once the Huffington Post published its story on the research, an accompanying UCFTV video went from several hundred page views to nearly 30,000.
