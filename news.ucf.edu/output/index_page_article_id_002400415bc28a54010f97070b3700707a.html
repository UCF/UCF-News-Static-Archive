UCF Wins Eight Regional Awards in Marketing and Communications

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala Feb. 7, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Terry Helms, University Marketing

    • 407-823-5977

  • Tom Evelyn, News & Information

    • 407-823-6312

  • UCF Stands for Opportunity

  • CASE District III

  • Print this Page

The UCF Stands for Opportunity branding campaign and other University of Central Florida marketing and communications initiatives earned top awards this week from the Council for Advancement and Support of Education.

The university earned eight awards in several marketing and communications categories, and representatives from UCF were presented with certificates during a dinner ceremony Tuesday in Nashville. More than 1,100 entries were received from schools throughout the southeastern United States.

We are honored that our peers have recognized the tremendous efforts of our team to tell UCF's story, said Tom Huddleston, vice president for Marketing, Communications and Admissions. It's a story about our successful partnerships with business and technology, which drive economic development. It's a story about our faculty working on innovative research, which is unlocking nature's mysteries. It's a story about our outstanding students and alumni who improve the world through their leadership and service. UCF truly stands for opportunity.

CASE, an international professional organization, strives to advance and support education worldwide in alumni relations, communications and development. More than 3,300 public and private colleges, universities and independent elementary and secondary schools in 54 countries are part of CASE.

UCF Marketing won four Awards of Excellence for a series of UCF Stands for Opportunity television commercials, print advertisements in the same campaign, a research newsletter and materials related to the new College of Medicine.

Marketing also won Special Merit Awards for the What If? campaign related to the universitys Quality Enhancement Plan, a fund-raising brochure about Bright House Networks Stadium and the design and implementation of the www.UCFopportunity.com Web site.

UCF News & Information won a Special Merit Award for its efforts to reach out to Hispanic media.

Contest submissions came from CASEs third region, which includes schools from Florida, Alabama, Georgia, Kentucky, Mississippi, North Carolina, South Carolina, Tennessee and Virginia.
