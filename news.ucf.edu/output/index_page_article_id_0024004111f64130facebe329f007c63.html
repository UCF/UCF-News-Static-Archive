Online Classes Effort Honored

By UCF News November 13, 2003

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Barbara Truman (UCF)

    • 407-823-6624

  • Keith Bourne (Sloan Consortium)

    • 781-292-2533

  • Print this Page

Citing the university's extensive training and support for professors who teach online courses, a renowned national nonprofit group has given UCF a top award for online teaching and learning.

The Massachusetts-based Sloan Consortium, which is supported by the Alfred P. Sloan Foundation, will present UCF with its "Excellence in Online Teaching & Learning Faculty Development" award during a conference in Orlando Friday through Sunday, Nov. 14-16. UCF President John Hitt will deliver the keynote address at the conference at 6:30 p.m. Nov. 14 at the Rosen Centre Hotel, 9840 International Drive, Orlando.

The Sloan Consortium, known as Sloan-C, is an association of more than 400 universities and institutions involved in advancing the use of online learning in higher education. Members of the panel that selected the winners of the top awards represented several top universities and Microsoft. The panel included James Duderstadt, president emeritus of the University of Michigan, and John Lombardi, chancellor of the University of Massachusetts at Amherst and president emeritus of the University of Florida.

The consortium honored UCF primarily for the extensive help it provides professors who develop and teach online classes. Professors take classes showing them how to create online courses and how to adapt existing courses to the Web format. They also work one-on-one with instructional designers who help them create the courses.

Nearly 75 percent of UCF professors teach courses that are partially or fully online. UCF has developed nearly 2,000 online classes, and the university offers nine online degree programs and six online graduate certificate programs.

Hitt said he believes faculty development is the cornerstone of the universityÂ’s success with online learning. Surveys also show that online courses receive excellent ratings more often than do face-to-face classes.

"Recognition from the prestigious Sloan Consortium reflects well on our university and the efforts of our hard-working faculty to provide the best online education to our students," Hitt said. "Online learning at UCF is serving as a catalyst to improve student learning, expand access to high-demand programs and help manage growth on campus."

Hitt recognized key individuals for their efforts: Joel Hartman, vice provost for information technologies and resources; Barbara Truman, director of course development and Web services; and Steven Sorg, assistant vice president and director of the Center for Distributed Learning.
