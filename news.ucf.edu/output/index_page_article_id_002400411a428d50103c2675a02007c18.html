Penn Professor to Discuss Findings of Regional Growth Study on Wednesday

By UCF Staff May 23, 2005

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Carla Novak

    • 407-823-0108

  • UCF Metropolitan Center for Regional Studies

  • PennDesign Central Florida Mid-term Report

  • Print this Page

A University of Pennsylvania professor and graduate students will present a smart growth plan for the Central Florida region Wednesday, May 25, during a presentation organized by the University of Central Floridas Metropolitan Center for Regional Studies.

Urbanist Jonathan Barnett will present the findings of the PennDesign Central Florida study, which suggests changes in land use, transportation systems and development patterns for the region to consider during the next 45 years.

The presentation, which is free and open to the public, will begin at 8:30 a.m. in the Camellia Room at Harry P. Leu Gardens, 1920 S. Forest Ave., Orlando. Registration and a continental breakfast begin at 8 a.m. To RSVP, call 407-823-2741 or send an e-mail to metro@mail.ucf.edu.

The graduate students, who toured Central Florida and met with residents and officials, developed their suggestions based on the regions current planning policies, transportation plans and population forecasts. It is hoped that the study will spark a community dialogue about the best ways to deal with growth.

The region is at a crossroads today, and must make a difficult choice, the final report says. Central Florida can follow the trend down the easy path, allowing low-density, automobile-reliant development to continue eating away at the natural landscape; or, the region can choose the more difficult, but ultimately far more rewarding, path of healthier ecosystems, diverse economies, balanced transportation options, and higher densities.

The Central Florida region in the study includes Brevard, Lake, Orange, Osceola, Polk, Seminole and Volusia counties.

PennDesign Central Florida is led by the Metropolitan Center for Regional Studies in partnership with myregion.org, the East Central Florida Regional Planning Council, the Central Florida Metropolitan Planning Organization Alliance and the Home Builders of Mid-Florida.
