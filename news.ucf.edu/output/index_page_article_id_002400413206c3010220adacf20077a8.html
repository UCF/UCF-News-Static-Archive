Art Gallery Hosted Memorial Exhibit for Graduate Student

By Mallery Laing March 23, 2005

Photo: Jeff Everett

The art gallery displayed some of Victoria Vevera's best artwork on Tuesday and Wednesday.

Victoria Lynn Veveras passion for art and different view of the world showed through in her work with everyday objects such as hangers, windows, scissors and nails.

 

Vevera, a 36-year old UCF student pursuing a Master of Fine Arts degree in the Studio Art and the Computer program, died Sunday of injuries sustained from a traffic accident on I-4.

 

Vevera had been working on an exhibit that showed an autobiographical look into her life, and her family and colleagues carried on with the exhibit as a memorial to her. Scott F. Hall, an assistant professor and graduate program coordinator for the Art Department, said the show, which was displayed Tuesday and Wednesday, was one of the best he has seen in the art gallery.

 

"Vevera had a great deal of sensitivity to materials she chose to work with," Hall said. "Her work was full of conceptual depth and also exhibits formal excellence that is on a par with the best trends in international art.

 

Victoria worked with visual language, and it speaks just as loudly as the language of words  This is a terrible loss of one of this universitys best and brightest.

 

Vevera was teaching a class in 3-D design while trying to attain the MFA degree, which is typically the highest degree obtained by a studio artist. She had worked as Hall's primary graduate assistant for four semesters.

 

Vevera was one of the first students admitted into the Studio Art and the Computer program, and she was the first student in the department to receive the Provost Fellowship.

 

Vevera has taught in local schools, including Red Bug Elementary and Eccleston Elementary.

 

Vevera will be missed by family, friends and the UCF community, in the words of one of her pieces, with a certainty like no other before.
