UCF Board of Trustees Approves Master Plan after Hearing from Residents

By Tom Evelyn Nov. 30, 2004

The University of Central Florida Board of Trustees held the final of three public hearings on the 2005-2015 Campus Master Plan on Tuesday before unanimously approving the update of UCFÂ’s projected growth and development.

At its regular meeting, the board also voted to increase the student activity and service fee from $8.09 to $9.37 per credit hour and approved naming UCFÂ’s School of Accounting for alumnus Kenneth G. Dixon, who donated more than $2.5 million to the school, and a new field house after Tony and Sonja Nicholson, who gave $2 million to the project.

The master plan is a blueprint for campus growth and development that supports the universityÂ’s academic and research missions. The plan, which looks out 10 years and is updated every five years, includes academic buildings, student housing, roads, environmental protection and other projects.

Key elements of the update approvedÂ Tuesday include:

-- Academic and research buildings to support 20 new degree programs.

-- Student housing that will add 2,000 beds, increasing housing on campus by about 50 percent.

-- A 10,000-seat convocation center that will host graduation ceremonies, sporting events and concerts and include retail shops and recreation for students.

Twenty-three members of the public, including students, residents and business people, spoke during TuesdayÂ’s public hearing. UCF also held an open house in April and two public hearings in July and October to give residents an opportunity to learn more about the plan and provide suggestions.

The master plan is available at www.fp.ucf.edu, the UCF Library on the Orlando campus or at FedEx KinkoÂ’s at 899 N. Alafaya Trail.

The activity and service fee, which was last increased in fall 2002, provides funds for student government, student activities and the operation and maintenance of the Student Union and Recreation and Wellness Center. The increase approvedÂ Tuesday will help pay for new facilities, including a new recreational pool, and for repairs to the Recreation and Wellness Center and other buildings damaged by the three hurricanes that hit Central Florida this year.

In other action, the board:

-- Concurred with conferral of 3,087 bachelorÂ’s, masterÂ’s and doctoral degrees that will be awarded during the fall 2004 commencement ceremonies Dec. 13 and 14.

-- Approved an honorary doctorate for Lester Mandell, a Central Florida builder who has supported and contributed to a number of UCF projects, including the UCF-Orlando Shakespeare Festival, the Wayne Densch Sports Center and endowed funds in the colleges of Education and Engineering and Computer Science.

The next Board of Trustees meeting will be held Jan. 18. For more information about the board, visit www.ucf.edu/bot.
