H1N1 Update for UCF Community (Video)

By UCF Staff Updated Sept. 18, 2009

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Orange County Health Department

  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention

  • Print this Page

As the H1N1 flu spreads throughout the United States, the University of Central Florida continues to make more resources available to help faculty, staff and students stay healthy.

The health and well-being of our students, faculty and staff are our top priorities, and UCF has been working diligently to help our campus community stay healthy. A multidisciplinary team of UCF staff, including Health Services, Housing & Residence Life and many other departments, meets regularly to discuss H1N1 and prepare for the additional cases we expect to see.  We also are working closely with our local and state health departments. 
 
Faculty and staff are invited to attend “Understanding H1N1” courses offered by Environmental Health & Safety. The courses will cover symptoms, prevention and treatment. Upcoming sessions will be at 1 p.m. Sept. 25 and 10 a.m. Oct. 5. Classes also will be held at 2 p.m. Oct. 15, 9 a.m. Oct. 23 and 1 p.m. Oct. 28.

To register, visit http://www.ehs.ucf.edu/formsUCFonly/h1n1class.html.

We also encourage you to visit UCF’s H1N1 Web site, www.ucf.edu/flu, to view frequently asked questions about H1N1, its symptoms and what you can do to stay healthy. We will continue to update the Web site as the fall semester progresses.

Students: Professional health care workers at UCF Health Services are dedicated to helping you stay healthy so you can succeed in your classes and enjoy your UCF experience.

We encourage you to visit UCF’s H1N1 Web site, www.ucf.edu/flu, to view frequently asked questions about H1N1, its symptoms and what you can do to stay healthy. We will continue to update the Web site as the fall semester progresses.

Vaccines: The best way to prevent the spread of flu is by vaccination, and UCF urges faculty, staff and students to get both the seasonal and H1N1 flu vaccines. UCF will be offering seasonal flu vaccinations early in the semester and wants to vaccinate as many people as possible during a special event from noon to 7 p.m. Wednesday, Sept. 30, at the UCF Arena.

A separate vaccine against H1N1 flu is expected to become available later, and it also will be offered at UCF. More information about those vaccinations will be provided as it becomes available.
 
As a reminder to the entire campus community, wash your hands often with soap, water or alcohol-based hand sanitizer. Cover your mouth and nose with a tissue when you cough or sneeze. If you don’t have a tissue, cough or sneeze into your elbow or shoulder. Avoid touching your eyes, nose, or mouth; germs are spread that way.

For more information, please visit the following Web sites:
 
UCF’s H1N1 Web site: www.ucf.edu/flu
 
Orange County Health Department: www.orchd.com

Centers for Disease Control and Prevention: www.cdc.gov/h1n1flu or www.flu.gov

In addition, the Florida Department of Health has established a toll-free number, 877-352-3581, to provide public health information and updates on the H1N1 flu. The line is staffed 8 a.m. to 8 p.m. seven days a week, with information available in English, Spanish, and Creole.
