Trustees Approve New Master's Program in Biotechnology

By Chad Binette March 18, 2008

Photo: Jacque Brund

President John Hitt thanked Trustee Brandie Hollinger for her year of service on the board during Tuesday's meeting, Hollinger's last as Student Government Association president.

Striving to provide more employees for the region’s burgeoning biotech industry, the University of Central Florida will offer a new master’s degree in Biotechnology beginning this fall.

The UCF Board of Trustees approved the new master’s degree – along with new bachelor’s programs in Golf and Club Management and International and Global Studies – during its regular meeting Tuesday.

The two-year Biotechnology program will prepare students for a variety of biomedical careers, including local opportunities at the UCF College of Medicine, Burnham Institute for Medical Research and planned Veterans Administration hospital.

Also, degree recipients will be well prepared to work for biotech companies that will become more prevalent in Central Florida as the “medical city” at Lake Nona takes shape, Provost Terry Hickey said.

“It’s that cluster of spinoff companies that’s really going to employ the bulk of the graduates,” he said.

The backbone of the new Biotechnology program will be existing courses in the Ph.D. program in Biomedical Sciences already offered at the university, and no additional faculty members will be hired. Three of the faculty members who will teach in the program have started their own biotechnology companies.

Students will have the option to earn an MBA degree in addition to a master’s in Biotechnology if they remain on campus for a third year.

Trustees also approved a new degree that will prepare students for management positions at golf and fitness clubs. The bachelor’s degree expands on a current track offered at the Rosen College of Hospitality Management. Dean Abraham Pizam told trustees there is a high demand for professionals in that area. The start date for that degree program has not been determined.

The third new degree will be a bachelor’s program in International and Global Studies. Nearly 300 students are enrolled in an existing International Studies track of the Department of Political Science, and the trustees voted to convert the track into a full degree program in the fall. Global Studies was added to the program name to reflect transnational issues such the growing role of non-governmental organizations, the environment, ethnicity and religion.

In other action on Tuesday, the board:

--Recognized the record eight UCF researchers who received prestigious National Science Foundation awards that recognize the nation’s most outstanding and promising young scientists and engineers. The faculty who received the CAREER awards are Lei Zhai, Andre Gesquiere, Saiful Khondaker, Huiyang Zhou, Enrique del Barco, Stephen Kuebler, Nina Orlovskaya and Pawel Wocjan. To view a full story on the winners, click here.

-- Honored the 15 students who will receive the Order of Pegasus, the most prestigious award students can receive from the university. The four graduate students are Kimberly E. Bryant-Davis, Tai T. Do, Samiullah “Sami” K. Durrani and Maribeth L. Kuenzi. The 11 undergraduate winners are Ayoolabode “Ayo” A. Ajaja, Bryan E. Arnette, Lalita D. Booth, Tabatha L. Calabrese, Ashley E. Conyers, Scott L. Kirkman, Cassandra A. Korsvik, Brittany L. Moscato, Maria V. Pecoraro, Megan A. Policastro, Stephanie Kyla Tan.  To read a more detailed story on the Order of Pegasus winners, click here.

-- Heard an update from Dean Deborah German, M.D. on the College of Medicine’s preliminary accreditation and scholarship drive to fund its charter class. The scholarship drive has helped the community develop a “sense of pride and ownership” in the college, which will help ensure its long-term success, President John Hitt said.

-- Approved an expansion of Academic Village that will include 840 beds and additional parking.

-- Approved the demolition of Building 15, contingent upon an Educational Plant Survey recommendation, to make room for an expansion of the adjacent Recreation and Wellness Center. The request also must be approved by the state Board of Governors.

-- Thanked trustee Brandie Hollinger for her year of service as the Student Government Association representative on the board. They also welcomed Michael “Micky” Grindstaff, a Winter Park real estate attorney, as a new member. Gov. Charlie Crist recently appointed Grindstaff, a UCF alumnus, to the board.
