UCF Reaps Success in 2006, Lays Groundwork for Triumphs in New Areas in 2007

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala Dec. 29, 2006

Photo: UCF News File Photo

UCF's new on-campus football stadium is scheduled to open in September, when the Golden Knights will host the Texas Longhorns.

The University of Central Florida achieved many milestones in 2006 and continues building the foundation for success in new arenas.

In January, the Orlando Sentinel named UCF President John Hitt the Central Floridian of the Year for his leadership spanning the past 14 years. Later in the year Florida experienced that leadership when UCF was given approval to open a new medical school at Lake Nona.

It was under Hitt's guidance that all the planning for the new college took place, and he was instrumental in bringing the Burnham Institute to the same site. The California-based research facility is a major cancer research force.

In December, Hitt also announced that UCF will have its 12th college -- the College of Nursing. It will join the medical school and the Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences at Lake Nona.

Together these new institutions -- along with the likelihood of a new veterans hospital in the same location -- will create what Hitt envisioned: a biomedical research cluster. Not only will it benefit the local and state economy, it will give Central Florida access to experts in medicine and students wanting to pursue a career in medicine a way to do it close to home.

The medical school was just one of many projects launched in 2006. A new on-campus football stadium was planned, funded and is under construction. The same is true of a new convocation center that will host graduation ceremonies, basketball games and other events. The Engineering III building opened in 2006, as did new student housing on campus.

Much of this was made possible thanks to a record year of fund-raising. In its first capital campaign, UCF received $356 million in donations, dwarfing its initial goal of $108 million. The money is going toward improving classroom and laboratory facilities and enriching the quality of academic programs. Many of the donations are invested in endowments that support faculty and academic programs for many years.

The money will benefit the medical college, the Rosen College of Hospitality Management campus, UCF Athletics and the College of Business Administration, among others.

Research funding also continued to break records. About $104 million in project funding was recorded in 2005-06. Its the second consecutive year that research funding has surpassed the $100 million mark. Education ($16.3 million), engineering ($15.3 million) and optics ($14.3 million) continue to capture largest amount of funding. The Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences ($7.7 million) and the Nanoscience Center ($2 million) also saw their funding increase significantly.

Students and faculty are the heart of the university, and they again made UCF proud in 2006. Hitt awarded the 100,000th degree at the spring commencement ceremonies marking how far UCF has come since its modest beginnings in the 1960s.

NASA also tapped an alumnus to lead the Kennedy Space Center beginning Jan. 1. Bill Parsons, who earned a masters of science degree in engineering management at UCF will take over when current director James Kennedy retires.

Several professors had their research findings published in such prestigious journals as Science. President George Bush named UCF breast cancer researcher and nursing Professor Karen Dow Meneses to the National Cancer Advisory Board, where she will have an impact on a national level.

The coming year promises to be just as exciting.

College of Medicine Dean Deborah German is already hard at work at building the foundation of the program. Groundbreaking is expected in early 2007 as well as hiring of faculty. The college is scheduled to start classes in fall 2009.

The stadium will be complete in time for a scheduled game against the University of Texas in September. Nearby, the new convocation center will host its first commencement ceremonies near the end of the year. And the new Psychology Building along with a new parking garage should be completed as well.

Professors are also working on important research with several well-known partners, including NASA and the National Institutes of Health. Several anticipate publishing their findings in 2007.
