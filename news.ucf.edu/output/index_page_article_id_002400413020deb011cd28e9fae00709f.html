UCF Alumni Honored at College of Business Administration Hall of Fame

By Joanne Chive Oct. 6, 2008

Photo: Courtesy of the College of Business Administration

Fifty College of Business Administration alumni are now featured on the Hall of Fame.

Four Central Florida community leaders, a New York investor and a packaging expert from Oregon were inducted into the University of Central Florida’s College of Business Administration Hall of Fame last week. The six distinguished alumni were honored during an Oct. 3 dinner attended by 200 guests.

Induction into the program is the highest recognition given by the college. Inductees must be a graduate of the college and are selected based on their career achievements and their commitment to UCF, specifically the business school. “Hall of Fame inductees give back to the college by serving as mentors to our students and hiring our graduates,” said Thomas L. Keon, dean of the College of Business Administration.

Class of 2008 inductees include:

Alan C. Charron, ’84, is president of Real Property Specialists Inc., Orlando, a full-service commercial real estate company. He is active in the Central Florida community and has volunteered with the Central Florida Commercial Real Estate Society and the Habitat for Humanity of Osceola County.

Melanie B. Fernandez, ’86 and ’91, is a partner at Cross, Fernandez & Riley LLP, a locally owned public accounting firm headquartered in Orlando, with additional offices in Winter Haven and Tampa. She is active with the Orlando Regional Chamber of Commerce and is a graduate of Leadership Orlando and Leadership Central Florida.

Michael J. “Micky” Grindstaff, ’78, is a partner at Shutts & Bowen LLP, a Florida-based law firm that represents individuals and business entities nationally and internationally. He is a member of the UCF Board of Trustees and is chairman of the UCF Foundation Board of Directors.

Alex Martins, '01, is the chief operating officer of the Orlando Magic, and oversees the day-to-day business operations of the organization. Martins was instrumental in helping to secure a new state-of-the-art events center in Orlando. He is a member of the Board of Governors at the Orlando Regional Chamber of Commerce and was recently appointed to the Central Florida Partnership Board of Directors.

Mark L. Plaumann, ’74 and ’79, lives in New York and co-founded Greyhawke Capital Advisors LLC to invest capital in and apply management expertise to corporate and asset restructuring opportunities. He took advanced classes while at UCF, which enabled him to become a Certified Public Accountant (CPA) at just 19 years old.

John D. Vander Werf, ’77, is the president and owner of Technapack Inc., a company based in Oregon that manufactures protective packaging for a variety of products. Though he no longer resides in Central Florida, he remains loyal to his alma mater and is a member of the College of Business Administration Dean’s Executive Council and has served as a judge in the student Capstone Competition.

With the addition of this latest class, 50 graduates have been inducted into the Hall of Fame since 1999. For a complete list, visit www.bus.ucf.edu/alumni.

About the UCF College of Business Administration: Established in 1968, the UCF College of Business Administration offers degrees at the bachelor’s, master’s, doctoral and executive levels. All undergraduate and graduate programs, as well as the Kenneth G. Dixon School of Accounting, are accredited by AACSB International -- the Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business. With more than 9,000 students, UCF’s college is one of the largest AACSB accredited business schools in the nation.

UCF Stands For Opportunity: The University of Central Florida is a metropolitan research university that ranks as the 6th largest in the nation with more than 50,000 students. UCF's first classes were offered in 1968. The university offers impressive academic and research environments that power the region's economic development. UCF's culture of opportunity is driven by our diversity, Orlando environment, history of entrepreneurship and our youth, relevance and energy. For more information, visit http://news.ucf.edu.
