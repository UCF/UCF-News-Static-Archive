Message From Rick Walsh, UCF Board of Trustees Chair

By Rick Walsh Jan. 14, 2010

Rick Walsh

Full Photo

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Contact the UCF Board of Trustees

  • Print this Page

As chair of the UCF Board of Trustees, I'd like to share with you the action your board took at this month's board meeting regarding the faculty contract with the university. 

To review: in May 2009, an impasse was declared in contract negotiations between the UCF Board of Trustees and the UCF chapter of the United Faculty of Florida. The impasse came after more than two years of continuous negotiations.

Following the declaration of impasse, both sides made presentations to a special third party magistrate that focused on three contract articles in dispute: faculty member evaluations (Article 10), disciplinary action and job abandonment (Article 16), and salaries (Article 23). Late in 2009, the special magistrate issued recommendations about these three articles.

The administration and the union accepted the magistrate’s recommendations concerning disciplinary action and job abandonment, along with some—but not all—of the recommendations concerning evaluations and salaries.

Under state statute, it is the responsibility of the board of trustees to settle issues that continue to be disputed after the magistrate’s ruling. At our meeting today, the Board voted to adopt the version of evaluations that continues to give faculty members at the departmental level the responsibility for crafting their evaluation standards, in cooperation with the university’s administration. This version allows the evaluation process to include limited flexibility and the use of professional judgment, which the Board believes is preferable to a formulaic standard for evaluating faculty members, which the UFF prefers.

In regard to salaries, the special magistrate agreed with the university’s position on promotion raises, bonuses, merit-raise pools, TIPs, RIAs and SOTLs for 2008-09. The Board supported the magistrate’s recommendation.  This means that these practices will remain in place until we negotiate a new salary article for 2009-2010. Those negotiations have already begun.

The next step in the impasse process is for the UFF membership to vote whether to accept or reject the Board’s action.

I believe our talented and dedicated faculty members have been, and will continue to be, central to our university’s success.  The actions your Board has taken support a professional faculty rewarded for its meritorious efforts.  As we move forward, the Board’s continuing priorities will be to provide high quality education for our students and to reward our exceptional faculty members.

On behalf of your UCF Board of Trustees and as a proud UCF graduate, I thank all of you for your dedication, hard work, and many contributions to our university. I look forward to continuing to build our great university together for present and future generations.
