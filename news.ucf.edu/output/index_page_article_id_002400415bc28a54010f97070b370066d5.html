EntrepreneurshipWeek USA Celebrated at UCF

By UCF Staff March 5, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Christa Santos

    • 407-230-7018

  • EntrepreneurshipWeek USA

  • Print this Page

UCF last week participated in EntrepreneurshipWeek USA, one of the nation's largest organized events celebrating entrepreneurship. EntrepreneurshipWeek USA is a nationwide initiative that provides activities designed to inspire, educate and prepare young people to consider entrepreneurship and innovation in their everyday lives. 

UCF is showcasing the intricate array of entrepreneurship-related programs and services that the university provides to its students and faculty and the Orlando community, said Tom ONeal, assistant vice president for research with the UCF Office of Research & Commercialization.

UCF develops and establishes quality programming and courses that foster entrepreneurship, while at the same time fueling economic growth in the community, ONeal said.

Several educational, mentoring and networking events were organized to highlight EntrepreneurshipWeek.

Events already held this week included the King of the Court Quick Pitch Competition last week. UCF students presented their business ideas to leading members of the Orlando business community during the competition at the Fairwinds Alumni Center. For more information, go to www.cei.ucf.edu/Competitions/KingCourt.

There was also the UCF Entrepreneurship Network Expo, a self-guided exhibit that allowed visitors to learn more about the ways UCF can provide support in developing and launching new business ideas. That exhibit was open Monday through Thursday.

A free mini-seminar Thursday at the Disney/SBA National Entrepreneur Center entitled Cash Flow Starts with Sales provided tactical and practical tips on how to accomplish sales results. The center also hosted an open house on Thursday evening.

According to Cameron Ford, founder of UCFs Center for Entrepreneurship and Innovation, research suggests that the most important aspect of economic development is putting the entrepreneurial process into motion. UCF supports a sophisticated and integrated suite of entrepreneurship and economic development initiatives designed to help entrepreneurs launch their visions and grow their ventures.

UCF offers the following resources that assist entrepreneurs in creating, launching and growing their businesses:

Center for Entrepreneurship and Innovation (CEI) - www.cei.ucf.edu
Venture Lab - www.venturelab.ucf.edu
Disney SBA National Entrepreneur Center (NEC) - www.floridanec.org
Office of Technology Transfer - www.tt.research.ucf.edu
UCF Small Business Development Center - www.bus.ucf.edu/sbdc
UCF Technology Incubator -  www.incubator.ucf.edu

For additional information about this special week celebrating entrepreneurship visit www.entrepreneurshipweekusa.com.
