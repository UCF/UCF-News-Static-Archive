Whats the Big Idea Business Competition to Feature 24 UCF, Rollins Teams

By UCF Staff Nov. 7, 2005

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Technological Entrepreneurship Center

    • 407-823-3683

  • Technological Entrepreneurship Center

  • Print this Page

Twenty-four teams will compete Friday, Nov. 11, in the first joint elevator pitch business competition between the University of Central Florida and Rollins College.

Whats the Big Idea? will feature presentations from 12 teams of UCF faculty, staff, students and alumni and 12 teams representing Rollins College. The competition, which is open to the public, will be from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. at Rollins Crummer Business School SunTrust Auditorium.

Contestants will be judged on how clearly they address a problem, how well their proposal would solve that problem and evidence that they present to support the market for their business. Central Florida business leaders and other community members will judge the presentations, and four teams will earn cash prizes totaling $1,000.

During each of two rounds, teams will be given two minutes each to present their plans and one minute to respond to judges questions.

We are especially interested in contributing to the university's efforts to create technology business ventures that produce attractive local jobs and improve the regions quality of life, said Cameron Ford, founder of the UCF Technological Entrepreneurship Center and coordinator of the management track of the College of Business Administrations doctoral program. Business planning events like the Whats the Big Idea? competition are an important part of our efforts to showcase interesting ideas and technologies to Central Floridas business community.

Participating teams must not have received more than $100,000 in prior funding. The winner of the competition will automatically qualify to participate in the UCF Joust Business Plan Tournament in April 2006.

For more information, go to www.tec.ucf.edu.
