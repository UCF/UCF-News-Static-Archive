UCF, Bentley Study Will Aim to Make Financial Reports More Understandable

By UCF Staff Oct. 31, 2006

Two University of Central Florida accounting professors will lead a new study focused on making financial reports more understandable, accessible and accurate for investors.

The NASD Investor Education Foundation has awarded UCF a $324,000 grant for the study, which the foundation hopes will help investors make better decisions. UCF professors Steve G. Sutton and Vicky Arnold from the Kenneth G. Dixon School of Accounting and Professor Jean C. Bedard at Bentley College in Waltham, Mass., will conduct the research during the next two years.

The complexity of the financial reporting model has come under heavy scrutiny amidst criticism from stakeholders and during recent congressional hearings on efforts to improve the transparency of corporate reports, said Arnold, who is UCFs Ernst & Young Professor of Accounting.

The annual financial reports of companies have become so voluminous and have so much redundancy across different sections that their usability is questionable, Arnold added. Both preparers and users of these reports are advocating simplification. Standards-setters are likewise feeling intense pressure to improve the quality, integrity and transparency of these corporate reports.

The study will analyze the value of disclosures provided with core financial statements to determine whether different structures may help investors make better decisions. In particular, the research will examine disclosures provided in managements discussion and analysis and in footnotes.

The Enhanced Business Reporting Consortiums Reporting Simplification Task Force requested the help of the UCF researchers. The consortium is an independent, international collaboration of investors, creditors, analysts, regulatory agencies, professors and others aiming to achieve better markets through better information.

Our research will assist the Enhanced Business Reporting Consortium as it considers recommendations for improving the financial reporting model through simplification, increased transparency and hopefully leveling the playing field between institutional and retail investors, said Sutton, who is the KPMG Professor of Accounting at UCF.

NASD is a private-sector provider of financial regulatory services dedicated to investor protection and market integrity through effective and efficient regulation. The NASD Investor Education Foundation is a natural outgrowth of the NASDs longstanding mission to protect investors and uphold the integrity of the markets. The NASD Investor Education Foundation, established in 2003 by NASD, supports innovative research and educational projects that give investors the tools and information they need to better understand the markets and the basic principles of saving and investing. For details about grant programs and other new initiatives of the Foundation, visit www.nasdfoundation.org.

UCFs Kenneth G. Dixon School of Accounting is housed within the College of Business Administration. The school was endowed with a $5.2 million contribution from alumnus Kenneth G. Dixon and accompanying state matching funds. The NASD Foundation-funded project is one of several ongoing research projects in the school that examine accounting,  auditing and taxation issues with a focus on the public interest.
