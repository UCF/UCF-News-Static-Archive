Film About U.S. After 9/11 Attacks Wins Top Award at Student Showcase

By Kristi Bartlett April 12, 2006

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Mary Price

    • 407-823-4195

  • View the films

  • Connect the UCF Community

  • Print this Page

UCF film student Josh Hurtado won Best in Show honors Tuesday at a showcase of short digital films and public service announcements sponsored by the Office of Undergraduate Studies.

Part of The New America initiative, the MeStories Showcase featured films that addressed aspects of 21st century life, including the environment, the war in Iraq and the dangers of drinking and driving. Faculty members served as judges.

Hurtados film, The New Americans, is a reflection on the state of the nation after the September 11 attacks. He won a $750 scholarship and a copy of Apple Final Express film editing software. The New Americans and other MeStories films can be viewed at http://mestories.ucf.edu/showcase.htm.

This particular video demonstrated all of the criteria we were looking for (content, form, and creativity), said Robert Kenny, a judge of the competition and assistant professor in the School of Film and Digital Media. As with the other entries, it was a well-produced piece. Being able to see all the entries was a wonderful experience.

For Best in Form, Kelly Rodak won first place for The Nightmare. Second place went to Kassandra Schwartz for Central Florida Memory.

For Best in Content, the short Mr. Right Now, by Allyson Meek, Jodi Clark, Marissa Donoho, Eric Werner and Sara Toriano took first place. Ryan Hamilton won second place for Possibilities.

Some of the students used an issue based form, while others took a more personal look at the different notions of patriotism, said Mary Price, program coordinator for the Office of Undergraduate Studies.

The competition consisted of two rounds. In round one, students submitted either a summary or a storyboard. The summaries and storyboards were reviewed by the judges, who selected a group of students to enter round two, the production phase. Students chosen for the second round had to complete a competition workshop and were given three weeks to shoot and edit their digital films. The competition was open to all current UCF students, individually or in groups of up to five members.

This was just another way to bring the curriculum outside of the classroom, to allow for reflection, Price said.

The UCF community was invited to vote for the Audience Favorite Award, and all students who voted were entered into a drawing for iTunes gift cards. Scholarships for the winners were sponsored by the Office of Undergraduate Studies and the UCF Learning Institute for Elders.

The New America: Mirroring the Present & Mapping the Future, the latest effort in the Connecting the UCF Community series, promotes civic engagement and is designed to create meaningful dialogues among students, faculty and the community. UCF students are examining the forces shaping the social, political and technological landscape of the United States. In addition to the student films, the initiative includes lectures, readings and an open art call. For more information on The New America series and a full calendar of events, visit http://www.undergraduatestudies.ucf.edu/community/2005_2007/events.html
