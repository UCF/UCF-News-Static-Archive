Charter Trustees Chair Dick Nunis Steps Down; Rick Walsh to Finish Term

By Tom Evelyn Jan. 19, 2007

Photo: UCF File

Dick Nunis, left, and Rick Walsh

Full Photo

  • Tom Evelyn

    • 407-823-5988

  • UCF Board of Trustees

  • Print this Page

Dick Nunis, a charter member of the University of Central Florida Board of Trustees who has devoted his time and money to help the university grow, stepped down from his post as board chair this week.

Nunis, who will remain on the board, nominated charter trustee Rick Walsh, president of the KnobHill Group and recently retired Darden Restaurants Executive and UCF alumnus, to serve out the term of the chairmanship. The board agreed unanimously.

Nunis has served as chair of the UCF Board of Trustees since its inception in 2001. The retired Walt Disney Attractions executive said he was going back into the corporate world and felt he could not dedicate enough time to being chair.

Nunis, of Windermere, has served on the UCF Foundation Board of Directors since 1988 and was national chairman of the eight-year Central to Our Future capital campaign, which raised $356 million to help the university improve classroom and laboratory facilities and enhance the quality of academic programs. The campaign concluded in June. He and his wife, Mary Nunis, personally became founding donors of the new College of Medicine by pledging a $1 million gift in 2005.

Dick Nunis has provided years of invaluable and unflagging service to our university, UCF President John Hitt said. He offered the strength of leadership, honed from his days at Disney, and served as a model of generosity in giving time, talent and financial assistance to UCF. His dedication to this university has made this a better place.

We are delighted he will remain a trustee and that he and Mary will remain close members of the UCF family.

Walsh, who retired last year as Darden Restaurants senior vice president for corporate affairs, is president of KnobHill Group, an investment and strategic counseling company.

In addition to being a charter member of the UCF Board of Trustees, Walsh serves as a trustee for the UCF Foundation and is on the Deans Advisory Council for the College of Business Administration. Walsh received UCFs Distinguished Alumni Award in 1985, having earned two degrees from UCF: a bachelors degree in political science in 1977 and a masters degree in public policy in 1983.

Walsh is the immediate past chairman of the Florida Chamber of Commerce. He served on the Governors Task Force on Access to Affordable Health Insurance, which was created by Gov. Jeb Bush in 2003. He is also a member of the Hubbs-SeaWorld Research Institute Board of Trustees.

Walsh has been recognized as the Orlando Business Journals Businessman of the Year, one of Floridas 100 Most Influential by Florida Trend magazine, one of 25 Most Powerful People in Central Florida by the Orlando Sentinel, and one of the 50 Most Powerful People in Orlando by Orlando Magazine.

Walsh will finish Nunis current term as chairman, which expires in June.
