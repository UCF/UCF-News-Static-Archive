Spring Semester to Feature Stadium Construction, Medical School Decision

By Kristi Bartlett Jan. 9, 2006

Photo: Jacque Brund

UCF plans to begin building a 45,000-seat football stadium during the spring semester, which begins today, and also should learn whether it will get permission to create a medical college.

The stadium, which the Board of Trustees approved in the fall, will be built east of the Anthony and Sonja Nicholson Fieldhouse on the north side of campus and will be open for the 2007 football season.

The Florida Board of Governors plans to vote in March on proposals by UCF and Florida International University to create new medical schools. If approved by the state, UCFÂ’s medical college would be established in the fall at Lake Nona.

The proposed degree calls for a four-year curriculum that begins clinical education in fall 2008 and would produce about 120 medical graduates each year. Students also would have opportunities to study at the University of Puerto Rico School of Medicine and to participate in shared research programs.

The spring semester, which concludes with commencement ceremonies May 4 to 6, also marks the beginning of the four-year Bachelor of Science in Nursing program at the UCF at Daytona Beach campus. About 25 students have been admitted.

On the main campus, construction will continue on the 21,000-square-foot addition to the Center for Research and Education in Optics and Lasers building. The addition is scheduled to open at the end of the year.
