UCF Public Administration Conference to Focus on Innovation in Government

By Brandon Bielich March 24, 2008

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Naim Kapucu, Public Administration

    • 407-823-6069

  • Conference information

  • Print this Page

 The innovation of government will be the focus of the second-annual Public Administration Conference at UCF’s Fairwinds Alumni Center on Friday, March 28.

Stephen Goldsmith from Harvard University’s Kennedy School of Government will give the keynote address at 9 a.m. Goldsmith served two terms as the mayor of Minneapolis, where he reduced government spending and used the money to improve the city’s downtown area. 

The conference will include four breakout sessions focusing on key issues in public administration.

-- Innovations in Public Safety Research & Practice will feature a look at the efficiency of police organizations, including an analysis of Orlando crime using GIS technologies by Bahadir Sahin, a graduate research assistant for UCF’s Department of Public Administration, and Joe Ferrendino, a UCF doctoral student in Public Affairs. Detective Jim McClure of the Orange County Sheriff’s Office will discuss information-sharing systems for law enforcement, and there will be a session on preparation and responses for emergencies and disasters.

-- Innovations in Governance and Leadership will feature a presentation on “Democratic Innovation and Governance in the Aftermath of a Great Fall” by UCF Public Administration professor Jay Jurie, a specialist in urban and regional planning. Other sessions will cover planning for succession in public agencies and the Urban Academies program in Broward County.

-- Innovations in Public Participation and Collaboration will feature a presentation on public participation in planning by Assistant Professor Thomas Bryer of Public Administration and a discussion on the impact of citizen participation. Longwood Mayor John C. Maingot and City Administrator John J. Drago will discuss “Awareness and Participation Plan in the City of Longwood.”

-- Innovations in Cross-Sectoral Partnerships will feature discussions on jurisdiction and sector partnerships, reconstructing government to build high performance, and contracting services for local governments.

The conference will begin at 8:30 a.m. and conclude at 3:30 p.m. The registration fee is $75 for the public and $40 for students.

For more information on the conference, contact UCF Continuing Education at 407-882-0260 or visit http://www.ce.ucf.edu/pc_course.asp?prog=1012.
