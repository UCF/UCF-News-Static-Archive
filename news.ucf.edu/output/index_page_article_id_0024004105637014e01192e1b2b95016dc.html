New Contract to Provide Easier Government Access to UCF Simulation Technology

By Barb Abney April 8, 2008

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Barb Abney, Office of Research and Commercialization

    • 407-823-5139

  • Institute for Simulation and Training

  • Print this Page

A new contract between the University of Central Florida and the U.S. Army Research Development and Engineering Command, Simulation and Training Technology Center (RDECOM STTC) will enable the university to receive up to $75 million in research funds over five years from any government agency.

The intent of the contract is to support modeling, simulation and training research, particularly within the Simulation, Training and Technology Center, a 27,000-square-foot facility in the Central Florida Research Park. The STTC includes a cultural awareness laboratory, virtual world research laboratory, medical modeling and simulation research laboratory and a super-computer research laboratory. The building is intended to facilitate state-of-the-art research for the military and simulation communities.

The new contract will establish a collaborative research laboratory and will enable technology transfer across U.S. Army programs, to other branches of the military and throughout the simulation technology community.

A signing ceremony featuring UCF President John Hitt and Orange County Mayor Richard Crotty will be held at 10 a.m. Wednesday, April 9, at the technology center, 12423 Research Parkway. Following the ceremony, media will be invited to tour STTC and Institute for Simulation and Training research labs. The ceremony and tours are open to the public.

The $75 million contract is the largest research contract ever signed by UCF.

“This collaboration is recognition of the quality of UCF research in modeling and simulation and the importance it holds for U.S. military services as well as our region’s technology-based economy,” said M.J. Soileau, vice president for research at UCF.

More than 80 personnel from Army research and training institutes, as well as industry partners and staff and students from UCF’s Institute for Simulation and Training and other units on campus, work in the STTC. 

The contract, titled Simulation Training Technology Center Research, Academic, Operations & Support, will enable UCF to work directly with any governmental agency approved by the STTC. Currently, the Army works with RDECOM STTC in a variety of areas, including embedded training, individual and team performance, computer-generated forces, medical training simulation and game-based learning.
