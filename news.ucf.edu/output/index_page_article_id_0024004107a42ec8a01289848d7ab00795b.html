Cooking Healthier Meals and Exercising Around a Busy Schedule

By Jessica Wycoff May 17, 2010

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Jennefier Collier, Soldiers to Scholars

    • 407-445-5500

  • Print this Page

Elementary school students and their families will learn how to cook healthier meals and exercise around their busy schedules during a nutrition and obesity seminar Saturday, May 22.

Students enrolled in UCF’s Soldiers to Scholars program organized the free seminar for at-risk youths whom they mentor and their families. The public is invited to attend the event, which will be from 11 a.m. to 2 p.m. at the Metro Place Community Center, 1049 S. Kirkman Road, Orlando.

Dr. David Jones and Heather Parker-Jones will educate participants on how to reduce the risk of diabetes and other health problems by changing their diets without giving up food that tastes great. Seminar attendees will watch cooking demonstrations and learn how to cook healthy foods and modify recipes. They also will learn about basic kitchen safety.

“Living a healthy lifestyle does not mean the end to everything that you enjoy,” said Parker-Jones. “It does not mean that you can no longer eat great-tasting food or that you must spend all of your time at the gym. What healthy living entails is readjusting your attitudes toward food and exercise in a manner that works with, not against, your busy schedule.”

Jones is a physician at the Orlando VA Medical Center who is board certified in family medicine. Parker-Jones is an associate professor of History and the chair of Social Sciences at Saint Leo University. Her research focuses on the historical effort to categorize obesity as a medical issue and not an aesthetic problem.

Soldiers to Scholars was formed in 1996 by state Rep. Alzo Reddick. The program allows veterans to take classes for free at Valencia Community College and UCF, preparing them for careers in teaching, nursing and social work.

Soldiers to Scholars students spend at least 20 hours a month mentoring at-risk youths of all ages in Orlando in addition to completing all of the requirements for their degrees. Their mentoring efforts include one-on-one interactions as well as group sessions. The 35 Soldiers to Scholars students are mentoring students from Eagle’s Nest and Pineloch elementary schools, Jones High School and the Rio Grande Charter School of Excellence this year.

For more information or to register for the seminar, call Jennefier Collier at 407-445-5500.
