Congdon and Hoffman Ruddy Win Top Teaching Awards at Founders' Day

By Chad Binette April 5, 2006

The following awards were given to UCF faculty and staff members and students during the annual Founders Day ceremony. The winners of the Pegasus Professor Awards and professors who were granted emeritus status are featured in separate stories.

University Award for Excellence in Undergraduate Teaching: Kristin G. Congdon, College of Arts and Humanities. Congdon has taught a variety of classes in Philosophy, Art and Digital Media and has served as chair for nine Honors in the Major theses. She also has taught in correctional facilities, treatment centers and elder hostels. She is a previous winner of the Distinguished University Researcher Award.

Winners from each college were Cecilia Rodríguez Milanés, Jeffrey M. Moore and Lindee H. Owens, Arts and Humanities; Nicole Howatt and Kenneth R. White, Business Administration; Juli K. Dixon, Education; Thomas X. Wu and Lei Zhao, Engineering and Computer Science; Jennifer E. Kent-Walsh and Abby Fromang Milon, Health and Public Affairs; and Terri Susan Fine, Jonathan Knuckey, Drew Lanier and Rani Vajravelu, Sciences.

University Award for Excellence in Graduate Teaching: Bari Hoffman Ruddy, College of Health and Public Affairs. Hoffman Ruddy has focused on helping patients with voice disorders through her work at the David B. Ingram Voice Care Center at the Community Disorders Clinic. Along with local doctor Jeffrey Lehnman, she also directs a Voice Care Clinic for Seminole County students. She also is credited with innovative efforts in developing Web-based classes.

Winners from each college were: Humberto López Cruz, Arts and Humanities; Donna D. Bobek, Business Administration; William Gaudelli, Education; Wasfy Mikhael, Engineering and Computer Science; and M.G. Moharam, Optics and Photonics.

University Award for Excellence in Faculty Academic Advising: Judith P. Ruland, College of Health and Public Affairs. Ruland was lauded for advising nursing students in a partnership between UCF and Seminole Community College at Lake Mary.

University Award for Excellence in Professional Academic Advising: Kristy L. Belden, Student Development and Enrollment Services. Belden is the lead academic advisor for the UCF football and mens tennis teams. She helped the football team achieve its highest team grade point average ever.

Founders Awards for Exceptional Ability and Performance in a Particular Area of Study: Jonathan Scarboro, Arts and Humanities; Seth James Trifiro, Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences; Jennifer Hartzler, Business Administration; Nichole Shorter, Education; Megan Clementi, Engineering and Computer Science; Andrea Melissa Coral; Health and Public Affairs; Jade A. Frey, Rosen College of Hospitality Management; Erin K. Riley, Sciences; and Jessica K. Leet, Undergraduate Studies.

Honors in the Major Awards for Outstanding Thesis: Arts, Humanities and Social Sciences: Robert R. Grassel, first place, and Bonnie Jean Taylor, runner up. Science, Engineering and Technology: Venkata Sri Cherukumilli, first place, and April K. Pope, runner up.

University Award for Excellence in Distinguished Research: Shin-Tson Wu, College of Optics and Photonics. Wu, UCFs PREP professor in optics, studies liquid crystal photonics and displays. His research has led to advancement in clearer and brighter liquid crystal displays used in televisions, cell phones and other electronic devices. He is also exploring tunable-focus liquid lenses that could be used as zoom lenses for cell phones and future eyeglasses. He is a fellow of three major professional societies in photonics and has received more than $6 million in research funding while at UCF.

Winners from each college were: Moshe Pelli, Arts and Humanities; Saleh A. Naser, Biomedical Sciences; Robert Folger, Business Administration; Bobby Jeanpierre, Education; Jay Kapat, Engineering and Computer Science; Ermalynn M. Kiehl, Health and Public Affairs; Youcheng Raymond Wang, Hospitality Management; Eduardo Salas, Sciences; and C. Shawn Burke, Institute for Simulation and Training.

Award for Excellence in Librarianship: Kimberly Montgomery, Cataloging, was praised for improving library cataloging to provide patrons with accurate information quickly. 

University Awards for Excellence in Professional Service: Melody Bowdon, Arts and Humanities, founded UCF's online professional writing certificate program and has served on service-learning committees. She also has performed community service to help AIDS patients. K. Tom Liou, Health and Public Affairs, guided the Department of Public Administration through its first accrediting process and serves as an unofficial UCF ambassador to China. He visits universities there to help suggest administrative reforms.
