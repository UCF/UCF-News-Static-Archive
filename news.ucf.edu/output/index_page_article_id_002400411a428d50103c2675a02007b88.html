UCF Trustees to Consider Two New Regional Campus Degrees

By Tom Evelyn May 24, 2005

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Linda Gray

    • 407-823-2947

  • Tom Evelyn

    • 407-823-5988

  • UCF Board of Trustees

  • UCF Regional Campuses

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Florida Board of Trustees on Thursday, May 26, will consider creating two degree programs in business administration and applied sciences to be offered at the universitys regional campuses.

At its regular meeting, which begins at 1 p.m. in the Cape Florida Ballroom at the Student Union, the board also will consider approving a 5 percent increase in tuition and fees approved by the Florida Legislature this past session. Board committee meetings begin at 8:30 a.m.

The new Bachelor of Arts in Business Administration and Bachelor of Applied Science are designed to provide full-time workers with associates degrees who cannot travel to the Orlando campus with foundations in business and management related to their careers.

The business administration degree would be offered at UCF at Cocoa, Daytona Beach and Palm Bay. The applied science degree would be offered with a supervision and administration track at UCF at Daytona Beach and Cocoa and with a track in industrial operations at Cocoa. If approved, both programs would begin this fall.

The Bachelor of Applied Science degree is available to all Florida community college associate of science graduates looking for career advancement, new careers or personal growth, said Dave Harrison, vice provost for Regional Campuses. It is designed for students working in technical, public services, health or other fields who need convenience while working toward their bachelors degree. The new degree will allow those holding A.S. degrees to grow in their careers and differentiate themselves in the marketplace.

The proposed 5 percent increase in tuition and fees for all students would raise tuition for in-state undergraduate students, for example, from $68.16 to $71.57 a credit hour. Last year, the state mandated tuition increases of 7.5 percent for all in-state undergraduate students and 12.5 percent for other students.

The board also will consider increasing the parking decal fee for students and employees by 5 percent to help pay for parking services, the shuttle system and parking garages. The transportation access fee, charged to students at the main campus and Rosen College of Hospitality Management, would increase by 44 cents to $4.34 a credit hour, if approved. All increases would take effect this fall.

Also on Thursday, Faculty Senate Chairman Manoj Chopra and Student Government Association President Willie Bentley will join the board as new members. Faculty representative Arlen Chase and former student President Kevin Peters have completed their terms.

Go to www.bot.ucf.edu for more information on the Board of Trustees and a complete agenda.
