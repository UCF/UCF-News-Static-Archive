New Conservation Group to Improve UCF's Natural Lands

By Christine Dellert June 2, 2009

Local conservationists are calling on volunteers to improve natural lands at the University of Central Florida by weeding out invasive species.

The Central Florida Invasive Species Working Group is hosting its first “dig out” workday on Saturday, June 6, at UCF’s main campus in East Orlando. From 8:30 to 10:30 a.m., volunteers will be weeding and removing invasive plant species from protected lands around campus.

The effort will help maintain the biological, aesthetic and recreational values of UCF’s natural areas, organizers say. The university’s natural lands are unique because they’re mostly surrounded by a metropolitan area, said Alaina Bernard, a member of the steering committee and assistant director for the UCF Environmental Initiative.

Invasive species are known to have a wide range of effects on ecosystems by disturbing the structure and function of healthy native populations. UCF has 47 known species of exotic plants, which interfere with the university’s management goals, including the application of prescribed fire, restoration and management of natural communities and listed species preservation.

“We are highly connected to other protected lands, so we need to be good stewards and make sure that invasive species on our land are not impacting other areas,” Bernard added.

The Central Florida Invasive Species Working Group consists of more than 100 members from city, county, state and federal agencies, along with the St. Johns River Water Management District, Progress Energy, The Nature Conservancy, University of Florida, Disney, Deseret Ranch, Valencia Community College and Rollins College.  

To volunteer for the June 6 event, contact arboretum@mail.ucf.edu. For more information, visit www.green.ucf.edu.
