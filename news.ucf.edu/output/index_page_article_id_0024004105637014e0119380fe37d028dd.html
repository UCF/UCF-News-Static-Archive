Seventh-Graders to Experience UCF Life During College Shadow Day

By Courtney Gilmartin April 10, 2008

Photo: UCF News file

Diane Wink, a College of Nursing professor, gave a presentation to seventh-grade students visiting UCF during a prior College Shadow Day.

More than 500 seventh-graders will practice nursing on patient dummies, explore bone structure using animal skulls and learn about theater improvisation during the fourth-annual College Shadow Day on Friday, April 11.

The University of Central Florida's Burnett Honors College has teamed up with Junior Achievement of Central Florida to help local students from five underserved middle schools experience a day in the life of a college student. 

“We want to introduce the students to unique fields of study that they may not know are available in college,” said Kelly Astro, director of The Burnett Honors College’s Office of Research and Civic Engagement. “It’s important for these students to see that college is exciting, beneficial and accessible to them.” 

Carver, Lockhart, Meadowbrook, Memorial and Stonewall Jackson middle schools each will send about 100 students to the event, held from 10:30 a.m. to 3 p.m.

The students will be welcomed with a spirit rally by UCF’s cheerleaders and then will spend the day learning about many of the programs UCF offers.

Students will be separated into 20 teams that will follow different schedules that will include stops at the Library and Recreation and Wellness Center. UCF President John Hitt will talk with the students about the importance of education, and the youngsters will participate in several interactive sessions with UCF students and faculty.

Students will also attend presentations from SGA President Brandie Hollinger and dance team Rukus. Knightro, the UCF mascot, will join the students by the Reflecting Pond for lunch, which will feature food donated by Firehouse Subs.

Throughout the day, more than 100 UCF volunteers will deliver the same message of staying in school for a brighter future. A strong emphasis will be placed on good study habits and financial aid.  Organizers hope that students will leave with a desire to make higher education a part of their futures.

“For most of these seventh-graders, they have never been to a college before or known anyone who has,” said Brenna Egan, a UCF honors student who volunteered for College Shadow Day last year. “They may believe that college is not even an option for them to consider. But then they come here. I will never forget the looks on the students’ faces.  They were amazed that they can go to college to study music, video gaming, sports management or, really, anything they want.”

The event concludes a six-week program called the Economics of Staying in School. The Junior Achievement program was taught in the middle schools by student volunteers from UCF’s Burnett Honors College and College of Business Administration.
