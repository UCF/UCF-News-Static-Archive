Local Investor, Strategist Named Eminent Scholar in UCF's Real Estate School

By Joanne Chive and Christine Dellert May 20, 2008

Randy Anderson, owner of a Winter Park investment banking firm, will direct research and education at UCF's Dr. P. Phillips School of Real Estate.

Randy I. Anderson has been named the inaugural Howard Phillips Eminent Scholar Chair in Real Estate at the University of Central Florida.  In this role, he will direct the research and education institute at the Dr. P. Phillips School of Real Estate, located in the College of Business Administration.

Anderson has been active in the real estate business since 1991 as a researcher, owner, investor and strategist. He is the owner of 1776 Financial Services, a boutique investment banking firm in Winter Park that provides debt, mezzanine and equity financing for real estate projects throughout the United States. The company also participates in developing and acquiring multi-family properties in the southeast. 

Nearly 200 students are either majoring or minoring in UCF’s undergraduate Real Estate program, which started in 2006 with a $2.5 million donation from Dr. Phillips Inc. Together with a matching state grant, the endowment supports the Howard Phillips Eminent Scholar Chair in Real Estate, the Dr. P. Phillips Institute for Research and Education in Real Estate and the Dr. P. Phillips scholarship program for Real Estate students.

“I look forward to being a part of this program and intend to build it into a top 10 School of Real Estate within the next five years,” Anderson said. “Central Florida has a dynamic real estate market, and UCF has already received tremendous support from the industry as evidenced by the generous gift from Dr. Phillips Inc. and the Central Florida Chapter of the National Association of Industrial and Office Properties.”

UCF leaders hope to expand the school in coming years, adding innovative research initiatives and eventually a master’s program. College of Business Administration administrators say Anderson’s impressive local professional experience and his national reputation make him ideal for his new positions.

“After conducting a national search, we were pleased to find the perfect candidate right here in Orlando,” said UCF’s College of Business Administration Dean Thomas L. Keon. “Randy’s unique background of working, teaching and conducting research within the real estate industry will benefit the program immensely. I am confident that he will help this new program grow and that he will serve as a great mentor to our Real Estate students."

Prior to forming 1776 Financial Services, Anderson served as president of CNL Real Estate Advisors in Orlando, where he was responsible for overseeing CNL’s non-Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT) transactions, including research, origination, acquisition, finance and management. Under his leadership, CNL Real Estate Advisors was one of the most successful startups in the national company’s history and managed nearly $700 million of real estate deals in its first year of operation.

Before joining CNL, he served as the Ryder Eminent Scholar Chair and a professor of Real Estate/Finance at Florida International University. While working at FIU, Anderson also served as the chief economist and director of research for Marcus & Millichap, one of the nation’s largest real estate firms. Prior to joining FIU, he was vice president of investment research at Prudential Real Estate Investors. He also held the William Newman Endowed Chair of Real Estate Finance at Baruch College, the City University of New York.

Anderson has published more than 50 articles, many in premier real estate journals. He also serves as a co-editor of the Journal of Real Estate Portfolio Management and the Journal of Real Estate Literature, and he has lectured at Harvard and Johns Hopkins universities. Recently, the American Real Estate Society awarded him with the Kinnard Young Scholar Award, which recognizes an outstanding young real estate scholar under the age of 40. Anderson holds a Ph.D. in Finance from the University of Alabama.
