Members of Congress, 'Daily Show' Comedian to Analyze Obama's First 100 Days

By Roxanna Malidelis April 7, 2009

Photo: UCF News File

Lou Frey represented Central Florida in Congress from 1969 to 1979.

Several current and former U.S. representatives will join Daily Show “senior black correspondent” Larry Wilmore at a University of Central Florida political symposium analyzing President Barack Obama’s first 100 days in office.

The spring Lou Frey Institute of Politics and Government symposium on Wednesday, April 15, will combine serious and comedic analyses of how the Obama administration has handled economic policies, national security and more. The daylong event, held in the Pegasus Ballroom of the Student Union, is free and open to the public.

The morning session of “The First 100 Days: Policy Initiatives and Approaches for the New Administration” will begin at 9:30 a.m. and include presentations by Democratic U.S. representatives Suzanne Kosmas and Allen Boyd. Former members of Congress William H. “Bill” Zeliff Jr., a New Hampshire Republican, and Jim Slattery, a Kansas Democrat, also will speak.

Wilmore, an actor and comedian best known for his role on Comedy Central’s “The Daily Show With Jon Stewart,” will give the keynote address at 7 p.m.

Other speakers will include UCF political science professors and Stephen Wayne, a professor of government at Georgetown University and the author of “The Road to the White House 2008.”

The symposium’s overview of the new administration will cover Obama’s relationship with the 111th Congress, an analysis of his economic policy and the future, a discussion on his security policies and a view of his administration from the perspective of historical comparisons.

“Perhaps never before has a new administration’s first 100 days come during a more pivotal moment in American history,” said Lou Frey, founder of the Lou Frey Institute of Politics and Government and a congressman who represented Central Florida from 1969 to 1979. “Never before has a new administration spent so much money.”

Wilmore has been a TV producer, writer, comedian and actor for more than 30 years for shows such as “The Office,” “In Living Color,” “The Jamie Foxx Show,” “The Bernie Mac Show” and “The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air.” He also wrote his first book, titled “I’d Rather We Got Casinos and Other Black Thoughts.”

The Lou Frey Institute of Politics and Government is a nonpartisan organization housed in the UCF Department of Political Science. The institute promotes civic education and engagement, particularly among young people. Institute programs encourage informed discussion of issues from diverse viewpoints, stimulate participation in the political process and support research on politics and policy.

Sponsors for the symposium include Industrial Enterprises of America; Lowndes, Drosdick, Doster, Kantor & Reed, P.A.; the United States Association of Former Members of Congress; the U.S. Department of Education; and the UCF College of Sciences, Department of Political Science, Global Perspectives Office, Kurdish Political Studies Initiative, Middle Eastern Studies Program and Terrorism Studies Program.

Visit www.loufrey.org for more information and a full symposium schedule.
