UCF's Huddleston Named to National Higher Education Commission

By Grant Heston Jan. 8, 2008

Photo: Courtesy of Tom Huddleston

Tom Huddleston is UCF's vice president for Marketing, Communications and Admissions.

The Council for Advancement and Support of Education (CASE) has appointed Thomas Huddleston, the University of Central Florida’s vice president for Marketing, Communications and Admissions, to its Commission on Communications and Marketing.

Huddleston, with the university since 1993, is a higher education leader in addressing undergraduate enrollment issues, admissions, brand identity and integrated communications and marketing. In his career he has focused on the development of comprehensive enrollment management models, institutional identity and strategies to strengthen student enrollment and the student-learning environment. 

CASE is the professional organization for higher education advancement professionals at all levels who work in alumni relations, communications, fund raising, marketing and other areas. CASE helps its members build stronger relationships with their alumni and donors, raise funds for campus projects, produce recruitment materials, market their institutions to prospective students, diversify the profession and foster public support of education.

“It is a privilege for me to represent UCF on this commission,” Huddleston said. “I am excited to be part of the discussion that will address many of the important higher education issues we face locally and nationally. This is a critical time for universities in terms of funding, strategic advancement and academic mission.”

The Commission on Communications and Marketing provides advice and counsel to the CASE leadership to help ensure that CASE's programs, services, and research respond to member needs and reflect current knowledge, professional trends, emerging issues and the highest standards within the field.

Huddleston previously served as UCF’s vice president for Student Development and Enrollment Services and vice provost for Enrollment and Academic Services. He has published frequently for a variety of journals, books and magazines, and he is a featured speaker at national institutes, seminars and forums.

Huddleston received bachelor’s and master’s degrees from Texas A&M University-Commerce. His doctorate is from Oklahoma State University.

CASE's membership includes more than 3,300 colleges, universities, independent elementary and secondary schools, and educational associates in 59 countries around the world. This makes CASE one of the largest nonprofit education associations in terms of institutional membership. CASE serves more than 51,000 advancement professionals on the staffs of member institutions and has more than 22,300 professional members. Visit www.case.org for more information.

UCF Stands for Opportunity: Established in 1963, the University of Central Florida is a diverse metropolitan research university that ranks as the 6th largest in the country with more than 48,000 students. Located in Orlando, Fla., UCF offers high-quality undergraduate and graduate education, student development and continuing education, while conducting cutting-edge research that powers the region's economic development.
