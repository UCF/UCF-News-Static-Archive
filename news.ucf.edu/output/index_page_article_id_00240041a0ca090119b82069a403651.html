FIEA Grads And mtvU Challenge HIV Stereotypes In New Online Game

By Christine Dellert and Todd Deery May 5, 2008

Photo: Courtesy of FIEA

"The Face of AIDS" game was a FIEA class project that won MTV's "Changing the Course of HIV" gaming challenge in November.

Five University of Central Florida graduates are the inspiration behind a new mtvU online game that challenges stigmas and stereotypes of people living with HIV/AIDS.

The game, called “Pos or Not,” is part of an HIV-awareness initiative that takes its concept from the popular Web site HotorNot.com. Instead of judging attractiveness, visitors are asked to decide if someone is HIV positive or negative, based on a photo and some personal facts.

“It’s gratifying to be part of something that hopefully, in some small way, can help people be better educated about this terrible disease,” said Brendan McLeod, now a lead designer at n-Space in Orlando and recent graduate of UCF’s graduate video-game development program, the Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy.

McLeod and FIEA teammates Matthew Laurence, Chris Camilleri, Gabriel Montagne and Chip Lundell won MTV’s “Changing the Course of HIV” gaming challenge in November for a class project. Their game, “The Face of AIDS," was designed, rapid-prototyped and developed in about a month as a fast-paced puzzle game designed to raise AIDS awareness while remaining fun and engaging. In it, players helped communities of ever-increasing size stop the spread of AIDS.

Months later, it inspired the new “Pos or Not” site, www.PosorNot.com, which also provides information about HIV prevention and the addresses to local HIV and STD testing centers. The Web site launched last week in partnership with the Kaiser Family Foundation and POZ Magazine.

“‘Pos or Not’ was created to shatter myths, challenge assumptions and promote responsible sexual behavior -- and we salute every participant, as well as the team of college students who conceived the game, for breathing life into it,” said Stephen Friedman, general manager for mtvU.

Several big-name stars -- Wyclef Jean, Fall Out Boy, Alyssa Milano, Say Anything and Will.i.am -- have already agreed to share the game with fans, family and friends.

All five of the FIEA graduates are now working in the local gaming industry. While McLeod works at n-Space with Camilleri, Montagne is a software engineer at Electronic Arts Tiburon. Lundell and Laurence work at 360Ed in downtown Orlando.

About FIEA: Part of the University of Central Florida, the Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy offers an industry-based graduate gaming education in a world-class facility in downtown Orlando. Work on the latest game engines and software and be mentored by faculty with decades of game-making experience. In 16 months, learn to be a producer, programmer or artist as you earn a Master’s degree in Interactive Entertainment. Financial assistance is available. Learn more at http://www.fiea.ucf.edu.

UCF Stands For Opportunity: The University of Central Florida is a metropolitan research university that ranks as the 6th largest in the nation with more than 48,000 students. UCF's first classes were offered in 1968. The university offers impressive academic and research environments that power the region's economic development. UCF's culture of opportunity is driven by our diversity, Orlando environment, history of entrepreneurship and our youth, relevance and energy. For more information visit http://news.ucf.edu.
