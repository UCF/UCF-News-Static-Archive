DMAC to Host Manhattan Short Film Festival Sept. 15 to 18

By UCF Staff Sept. 12, 2005

Related Contacts and Links:

  • DMAC: Rich Grula

    • 407-823-9200

  • Manhattan Short Film Festival: Nicholas Mason

    • 212-529-8640

  • Downtown Media Arts Center

  • Manhattan Short Film Festival

  • Print this Page

Central Florida movie fans can watch a dozen short films made in seven countries and vote for their favorites Sept. 15 to 18 at UCFs Downtown Media Arts Center.

The films are the finalists in the Manhattan Short Film Festival, which will showcase the next generation of filmmakers from around the world at 54 theaters in 31 states. The 12 finalists were chosen among 504 entries from 30 countries.

The Manhattan Short Film Festival, now in its eighth year, has featured short filmmakers who have gone on to win Oscar nominations and direct feature films such as Robots, The Animal, and The Girl Next Door. Past judges for the festival have included Susan Sarandon, Tim Robbins and Laura Linney.

The Downtown Media Arts Center, known as DMAC, will show the finalists at 7:30 and 9:30 p.m. Thursday and Friday, Sept .15 and 16. Additional screenings will be at 1 and 4 p.m. Saturday, Sept. 17, and 1, 4 and 7 p.m. Sunday, Sept. 18. The theater is located at 39 S. Magnolia Ave.

Films from Ireland, Israel, England, Wales, Australia, Spain and the United States make up this years finalists. Films are no more than 14 minutes in length, and the program lasts about 2 ½ hours. Ticket prices are $7 for evening shows and $5 for matinees; discounts are available for students, senior citizens and DMAC members.

The winner will be announced at a final screening Sept. 25 in New York City and will be given the opportunity to direct a feature film that will be shown in many theaters that are participating in the festival.

For more information, including descriptions of the 12 finalists, visit the festivals Web site at www.MSfilmfest.com. Go to www.dmacorlando.com for more information on DMAC and the film schedules.
