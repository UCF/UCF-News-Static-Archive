UCF to Host Conference on Iranian Foreign Relations, Society

April 8, 2004

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Professor Houman Sadri, program chairman

    • 407-823-6023

  • Center for the Iranian Research and Analysis

  • Print this Page

Experts on Iranian foreign policy and culture and the countrys economy will speak Friday, April 9, and Saturday, April 10, during a conference hosted by the University of Central Florida.

 

The theme of the 22nd annual conference of the Center for Iranian Research and Analysis is The Making of a New Middle East: Iran, the Region and the World. The conference will be held from 9 a.m. to 5:15 p.m. Friday and 9:30 a.m. to 5 p.m. Saturday in Room 316 of the UCF Student Union.

 

Speakers include Shireen Hunter, director of the Islamic Studies Program at the Center for Strategic and International Studies in Washington, D.C.; Professor Farhang Rajaee of Carleton University in Ottawa, Canada; and several UCF professors.

 

Admission is free for UCF students, $25 for other students and $45 for the general public.

 

For more information and a conference schedule, go to http://faculty.quinnipiac.edu/libarts/monshipouri/cira and click on CIRA 2004.
