Football Game Day Update: Louisiana-Lafayette Game

By UCF Staff Sept. 27, 2007

Photo: Jacque Brund

The first evening game at Bright House Networks Stadium will be played Saturday, when the Knights host Louisiana-Lafayette at 6 p.m.

Full Photo

  • Joe Hornstein, UCF Athletics

    • 407-823-2729

  • Chad Binette, News and Information

    • 407-823-6312

  • UCF Game Day Web site

Fans getting ready for the first evening home game at Bright House Networks Stadium are encouraged to review the following game day procedures and to note some changes that are a result of the later game time.

The Knights and Louisiana-Lafayette Ragin Cajuns will kick off at 6 p.m. Saturday.

Up-to-date information and procedures are available at www.ucfgameday.com. UCF has also set up a special telephone help desk that will be active on home football game days at 407-882-FANS or 407-882-3267.

Tickets: Tickets for the UCF-Louisiana-Lafayette game can be purchased online at www.ticketmaster.com or by calling the UCF Athletics Ticket Box Office at 407-823-1000. The UCFAA Ticket Box Office is located in Bright House Networks Stadium on the ground floor off the IOA west entry plaza.

Pre-game entertainment: The Marching Knights band will play a free concert at the new UCF Arena at 2:30 p.m. Also, an interactive football area sponsored by Coca Cola will be open next to the Roth Tower at the stadium starting at 3 p.m.

Knightwalk: The UCF football team is expected to arrive around 3:30 p.m. at IOA Plaza on the west side of the stadium (by gates 2 and 3). The Marching Knights and cheerleaders will join the team for the Knightwalk through the plaza and into the stadium.

Good Sport Zones: UCFs Good Sport program will host fan zones with games and music, free pizza, Coca-Cola products, water bottles and other giveaways. The spots are located at Memory Mall from 10 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. and at Pegasus Landing from 2:30 to 4:30 p.m. The Good Sport fan zones are tied into the Do the Knight Thing pledge that encourages positive fan behavior.

Fans with disabilities: Parking for fans with disabilities is available on the first floor of Garage D across from the UCF Arena. Fans with valid handicapped parking permits are allowed to enter the Gold Zone via Libra Drive or Orion Boulevard to access Garage D. While there are other handicapped parking spaces throughout the campus, shuttles to and from the stadium will be available only from Garage D.

Student parking: Students are reminded that parking in any of the Gold Zone lots (C 1-2, D 1-2 and E 1-8) without a Gold Zone permit is prohibited from 7 a.m. until midnight on game day. Vehicles parked without the appropriate permits in Gold Zone lots will be towed.

Students who live in the Towers residence halls will not be able to access parking garages E and G between 3 p.m. and 11 p.m. Saturday. They can leave their vehicles in the garages during that time or move them to another location outside of the Gold Zone before 3 p.m. 

Parking in Garage B, located across from the Recreation and Wellness Center, is prohibited from 11 p.m. Friday until 7 a.m. Sunday except for students, faculty, staff and others who are on the pre-approved list for official university business on Saturday.

Libra Drive open for two-way traffic: Libra Drive, which connects UCF and the Central Florida Research Park, will remain open to two-way traffic for the entire day on Saturday.

Student entry: Ticketed students with valid IDs are encouraged to enter at the following gates:  2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 11, 12, 13, 14, and 15.

Sportsmanship: This years fan motto  Do the Knight Thing  is all about being a great fan, having fun and staying safe. Fans are encouraged to show respect to UCFs guests from Louisiana-Lafayette. 

No glass: Fans are reminded that glass is not allowed in the tailgating areas on campus.

Updated list of items prohibited in stadium: To ensure that all guests have a safe and enjoyable visit to Bright House Networks Stadium, guests are not permitted to bring the following items into the stadium:

-- Aerosol cans (hair spray, mace, pepper spray, etc.)

-- Horns, whistles or artificial noisemakers

-- Alcoholic beverages

-- Bags (grocery, duffle, backpacks, luggage, large bags, large purses, large camera cases, etc.) 8 1/2" x 14" x 10" is maximum size for entry

-- Balloons (air or helium)

-- Banners, signs and flags

-- Balls (beach balls, footballs, baseballs, etc.)

-- Cans/bottles

-- Coolers (hard or soft sided)

-- Confetti or glitter

-- Fireworks/flares

-- Frisbees

-- Food and drink

-- Illegal substances

-- Laser pointers

-- Personal chair back seats

-- Picnic baskets

-- Strollers (A stroller check-in is located at Gate 18)

-- Tripods

-- Umbrellas

-- Video cameras or Camcorders

-- Still cameras (professional) with lenses over five inches long and/or 50mm and above

-- Weapons. No weapons are allowed in the stadium. Possession of a firearm or any other weapon is strictly forbidden. Possession of such a weapon will result in immediate confiscation, ejection and possible arrest.

The following items will be permitted after inspection:

-- Binoculars and binocular cases

-- Cameras and small camera cases (must comply with the camera/lens limits)

-- Diaper bags (only if accompanied by a child)

-- Small bags, purses and fanny packs (must comply with the bag limits)

-- Small radios (must be used with an earpiece)

All personal belongings will be subject to inspection upon entry into the stadium. Prohibited items that are discovered during security inspections at the stadium entrances must either be returned to the owner's vehicle or discarded. Except for childrens strollers, items will not be held for later pickup. Unlawful items that are discovered during inspections are subject to being confiscated, and the person in possession of those items is subject to ejection and/or arrest.

Additional information about parking, traffic, tailgating and other details of game day is below.

Parking

There will be about 12,000 parking spots available on campus. Parking is divided into three sections  Gold, Red and Blue.

The Gold Zone, closest to Bright House Networks Stadium, requires a reserved game day parking permit and is only for members of the Golden Knights Club, credentialed media, stadium operations personnel, patrons with disabilities and students with special permits.

Only Gold Zone patrons and those with disabilities will be allowed to drive on and off campus via Orion Boulevard from three hours prior to kickoff until two hours after the game ends.

Almost all parking in the Red Zone and Blue Zone is open to everyone. The spaces are free and on a first-come, first-serve basis. 

Parking is not allowed along campus roadways, in residential housing parking lots or garages, or on any open grassy areas throughout campus. 

For a more detailed story on parking, click here.  

Traffic

Preferred routes for each of the three parking zones are listed at www.ucfgameday.com , and fans are encouraged to follow them.

Gemini Boulevard North will be closed to vehicle traffic from Alafaya Trail to North Orion Boulevard from three hours prior to game time until two hours after the game ends. This road closure is designed to ensure pedestrian safety between the main and north sections of campus.

UCF will operate about 25 shuttle buses for students to and from several nearby apartment complexes, from five hours prior to the start of the game until three hours after the game ends. The service is free. The on-campus shuttle routes (Black and Gold routes) will not be operating on game days.

For a more detailed news story on traffic, click here.

Tailgating

Tailgating is allowed from 7 a.m. until two hours after the game ends on game days. It is allowed in any ground-level parking lot. It is not permitted in any parking garage on any level. Tailgating activities without vehicles are allowed on most other areas on campus. 

Only those 21 and older are permitted to drink alcoholic beverages. Kegs, beer funnels and glass containers are not allowed on campus.

Fans should recycle plastic and aluminum cans in the UCF Recycles! white cardboard containers located around campus and in the stadium. For a more detailed story on tailgating, click here.

Neighbors

Fans should be respectful of UCFs neighbors. Posted No Parking signs will be along portions of McCulloch Road and Lockwood Boulevard. Law-enforcement officers will be driving through several subdivisions on game day to encourage fans not to block driveways or park on private property. Parking on campus is free. 

Orange and Seminole counties ordinances prohibit open containers of alcohol on public streets or parking lots. For a more detailed news story on UCF working with neighbors, click here.
