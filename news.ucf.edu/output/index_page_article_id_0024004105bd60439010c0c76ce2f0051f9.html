PBS Education Correspondent to Discuss Higher Education

By Kristi Bartlett Oct. 13, 2006

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Fritzlaine Powell

    • 407-823-2626

  • Student Development and Enrollment Services

  • Print this Page

PBS education correspondent John Merrow, author of Choosing Excellence and executive producer of the documentary Declining by Degrees: Higher Education at Risk, will give a public lecture on Wednesday, Oct. 18, at the University of Central Florida.

Higher Education: Does it Really Need Washingtons Help? will be at 1 p.m. in Pegasus Ballroom D-E-F of the Student Union. The presentation will be free and open to the public and will be followed by a book-signing.

The presentation will explore some of the challenges and problems facing higher education in America. Those issues were documented over two years on college campuses around the country. The documentary provided candid insights from students, faculty and administrators.

According to Merrows documentary, students encounter numerous obstacles such as being unprepared for college, large lectures on large campuses, lack of a demanding curricula and reduced access to higher education due to reduced financial aid.

Other market influences affecting higher education, according to Merrow, are reduced budgets leading to more part-time faculty, the arms race in building and creating campus amenities to attract students, the increasing importance of college rankings by the media and big-time college sports.

The event is sponsored by Student Development and Enrollment Services, the Office of Diversity Initiatives and the Faculty Center for Teaching and Learning. Eligible students who attend will receive 1,000 LINK Loot points.
