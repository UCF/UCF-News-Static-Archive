Undergraduates to Present Research at New Showcase

By Mallery Laing

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Richard Harrison II

    • 407-823-5880

  • Office of Undergraduate Studies

  • Print this Page

Jennifer Harrison understood many reasons why married women often stay in abusive relationships, but she couldnt figure out why so many women remain with boyfriends who abuse them.

For a paper she is presenting at a UCF undergraduate research showcase on Friday, April 9, Harrison addressed violence in dating relationships, an issue that is particularly applicable to college students.

Harrison, a senior sociology major, is one of more than 100 undergraduates who will present research at the showcase from 1 to 4 p.m. in the Education Building gymnasium.

I am really curious about what my friends and other students are doing. Harrison said. This showcase really gives us a chance to see whats going on around us."

The entire UCF community and the public are invited to attend the showcase, which was created by the Undergraduate Research Council at the request of Provost Terry Hickey.

"This program actually gives students a chance to present their hard work and research, said Jana Jasinski, a sociology professor and a member of the council. Its amazing to have these opportunities here at UCF.

Presentation topics include: Attitudes toward Corporal Punishment as a Function of Ethnicity, Gender Stereotyping and Membership Bias in Greek Organizations and Identifiable Muslim Females, Reasons for Veiling and Reconsiderations after September 11, 2001."

The best three showcase projects will be awarded scholarships donated by the UCF Student Government Association.

For more information, go to www.undergraduatestudies.ucf.edu/research/home.html or contact the Office of Undergraduate Studies at (407) 823-4197.
