New Academic Field Fuses Humanity With Evolving Technology

By Christine Dellert March 13, 2008

Photo: Courtesy of the Interactive Performance Lab

"Inter-actors" and the public mingled in real and virtual worlds last year during the Interactive Performance Lab's staged art heist in downtown Orlando. The lab is hosting the nation's first Interactive Performance Conference this weekend to explore how technology empowers people to "play" inside an interactive story.

In today’s Wi-Fi and wiki-filled world, a group of UCF students and faculty wants to interject a little humanity into our myriad of computerized gizmos.

The University of Central Florida Interactive Performance Lab is hosting its first conference and public showcase beginning Friday to explore how technology empowers people to “play” inside an interactive story.

“It used to be that our exposure to the world beyond our personal lives was delivered by what we saw on television,” said Jeff Wirth, executive director of the Interactive Performance Lab and a faculty member in Digital Media. “Now we’re part of an ‘experience culture,’ with kids using cell phones or video games to talk to people on the other side of the world.

“We want to make sure that this technology allows us to more fully express our humanity, instead of overwhelming it,” he said.

That’s the point of the work of the Interactive Performance Lab, which combines live and digital simulation for entertainment, training and education.

The lab traveled to Jacksonville to simulate emergency-room patients for doctors in training last year and staged a fictional art heist in downtown Orlando that played out in real and virtual worlds.

This weekend, it will bring together ideas and projects from nearly 100 academics and creative professionals from around the country at the Interactive Performance Conference. From emotion-based video gaming to Shakespearian virtual worlds, the gathering is meant to drive new partnerships and projects in this emerging academic field.

“Most conferences ask people to share the work they have already done, but what we want is to generate new ideas and future work that people can go out and do as a partnership with our lab, and with each other” said Dana Mott, the conference producer.

Alongside UCF Digital Media faculty and students, academics from Indiana University’s Synthetic Worlds Initiative, the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Carnegie Mellon University, and the University of Southern California’s Institute for Creative Technologies will attend. So will creative professionals from Disney, Universal Creative, Crispin, Porter + Bogusky and Snibbe Interactive.

Chris Crawford, a founding member of Atari game studios and author on interactive game and story design, will be the keynote speaker.

“Our idea is to make interactive performance into an internationally recognized discipline that can be realized in many ways that we haven’t even dreamed of yet,” Mott said.

The invitation-only conference runs from Friday, March 14, through Sunday, March 16, at UCF’s Digital Media campus in downtown Orlando, 500 W. Livingston St.

The conference will be open to the public from 2 to 4 p.m. Saturday, March 15, for a showcase featuring artificial intelligence, virtual worlds, live simulation, theme park design displays and more.

On Saturday, UCF’s interactive performance team will set up “The Cell,” which will allow participants to step inside an interactive story-based experience set inside a prison cell. With sets and costumes provided by Universal Studios, “The Cell” invites the public to enter and become part of an interactive performance story while letting others watch on television screens.

“It will be the first time the public really gets to interact with all this new technology,” said Kimberly Mergenthaler, a UCF Digital Media senior helping to organize the conference. “We’re creating a hub, and we want people to come ‘play’ with us. Come be a part of our new study.”

To learn more about UCF’s Interactive Performance Lab and the conference, go to http://www.iplonline.org/projects/conference/.
