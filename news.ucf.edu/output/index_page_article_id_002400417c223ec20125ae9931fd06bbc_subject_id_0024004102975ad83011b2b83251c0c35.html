The following honors were awarded to UCF faculty members at the annual Founders’ Day ceremony.  Stories on the winners of the Pegasus Professor Awards, professors who were granted emeritus status and students who were awarded Founders Day honors are featured separately.

University Award for Excellence in Undergraduate Teaching: Bruce M. Wilson, College of Sciences. Wilson, a Political Science professor, specializes in Latin American Politics. In three years, he has taught nine different classes, and the research he has conducted about the impact of online teaching on students has helped faculty members learn how to best use technology in the classroom. From 1998-2006, Wilson served as the departmental Honors Program coordinator and oversaw the growth of the Honors in the Major program from two students in 1998 to 27 in 2006.

Winners from each college were: Farrah M. Cato, Jane Compson, Kristin G. Congdon and Rudy McDaniel, College of Arts and Humanities; Xin He and Sean Robb, College of Business Administration; Judit Szente, College of Education; Dima Nazzal and Alireza Rahrooh, College of Engineering and Computer Science; Susan D. Gosnell and Abby Fromang Milon, College of Health and Public Affairs; Mohtashem Samsam, College of Medicine; Jacqueline LaManna, College of Nursing; Enrique del Barco, Drew Noble Lanier, Mustapha Mouloua and John F. Weishampel, College of Sciences.

University Award for Excellence in Graduate Teaching: Necati Catbas, College of Engineering and Computer Science. The associate chair of Civil, Environmental and Construction Engineering, Catbas is known for attracting some of the best and brightest graduate students in the world, challenging them to excel and integrating cutting-edge research into his courses. Since coming to UCF in 2003, he has created world-class research groups that have received wide recognition from his peers as well as from other universities.

Winners from each college were: Rudy McDaniel, College of Arts and Humanities; Melissa B. Frye, College of Business Administration; Glenn W. Lambie, College of Education; Naim Kapucu, College of Health and Public Affairs; Ken Teter, College of Medicine; Christopher W. Blackwell, College of Nursing; Winston V. Schoenfeld, College of Optics and Photonics; Jana L. Jasinski, College of Sciences.

University Award for Excellence in Research: Eduardo Salas, College of Sciences and Institute for Simulation and Training. A leading international authority in team effectiveness and team training, Salas also studies how to minimize human errors in health care. He has garnered more than $20 million in research funding, and he received the 2007 and 2010 M. Scott Myers Award for Applied Research in the Workplace. He has been appointed to the National Academy of Science’s committee for patient safety, and in 2009 was elected President of the Society of Industrial and Organizational Psychology.

Winners from each college were: Humberto López Cruz, College of Arts and Humanities; Axel Stock, College of Business Administration; Scott M. Waring, College of Education; Mohamed A. Abdel-Aty, College of Engineering and Computer Science; Naim Kapucu, College of Health and Public Affairs,; David J. Hagan, College of Optics and Photonics; Brian Goldiez; Institutes and Centers.

University Award for Excellence in Faculty Advising: Robin M. Kohn, College of Health and Public Affairs, and Susan Scott Ricci, College of Nursing.

Kohn has served as the Bachelor of Social Work coordinator for the School of Social Work for the past 10 years. She devotes considerable time to advising pre-admission students who wish to enter the program, meticulously monitors the progress of current students and acts as their tireless advocate. Kohn is national leader in the social work profession and has been named Social Worker of the Year by Florida’s chapter of the National Association of Social Workers.

Ricci served as an adjunct faculty member in the College of Nursing for more than 10 years and has been a full time instructor since 2006.  She coordinates the Concurrent ASN/BSN nursing program housed at Seminole State College, and her recruitment efforts have greatly increased enrollment in the UCF Nursing program. She has served as chair of the Undergraduate Nursing, Progression and Graduation committee for two years and has acted as an ambassador for the college.

University Award for Excellence in Professional Academic Advising: Marva A. Ellington, College of Health and Public Affairs. Ellington is responsible for academic advising of two the largest programs in the College of Health and Public Affairs. She mentors students, listens carefully to student issues and concerns, and assists students in looking ahead and exploring options for growth and self-development. Ellington is a strong advocate for students and looks for solutions in her work that support students, departments and the university.

Award for Excellence in Librarianship: Min Tong, UCF South Lake Campus. Hired as the first full-time librarian at the South Lake Campus in 2007, Tong voluntarily provided library instruction at the Leesburg and Ocala campuses as well. She actively participates in as many student orientations and faculty welcome-back sessions as possible and has made herself available to students and faculty at all three campuses via online chats.

University Awards for Excellence in Professional Service: Manoj Chopra, College of Engineering and Computer Science, and Ross Wolf, College of Health and Public Affairs.

Chopra, an associate professor in the Department of Civil, Environmental, and Construction Engineering, has also served on Faculty Senate as well as the Board of Trustees. He is active in local chapters of professional societies in civil and environmental engineering and is currently serving as the advisor to the UCF chapter of the American Society of Civil Engineers. He spends many hours volunteering his time with the students and traveling with them to competitions.

Wolf, an assistant professor of Criminal Justice, came to UCF in 1999 and since then has participated in countless committees within the Department of Criminal Justice & Legal Studies, the College of Health & Public Affairs and the university. He has served on the UCF Alumni Association board of directors and as faculty advisor to Lambda Alpha Epsilon, the American Criminal Justice Association. Wolf also serves as a division chief in the Orange County Sheriff’s Office.
