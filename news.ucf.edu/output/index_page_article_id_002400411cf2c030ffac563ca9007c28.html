UCF to Celebrate Homecoming with Concert, Spirit Splash, Parade

By Tom Evelyn Oct. 26, 2004

Photo: File photos

The UCF cheerleaders will perform during the Homecoming parade Nov. 6.

The University of Central Florida will kick off Homecoming with Orlandos biggest black-and-gold party and wrap it up a week later with a salute to veterans in the annual downtown parade.

KnightFest-UCF Homecoming 2004 will celebrate the university, its Golden Knights football team and the community it serves during several events for students, alumni and residents.

The annual, weeklong celebration starts this year with the Homecoming Kickoff Concert in downtown Orlando on Saturday, Oct. 30, and ends with the Homecoming Parade and football game versus Ohio University on Saturday, Nov. 6. This years parade is being combined with the city of Orlandos annual Veterans Day march.

Thousands of students will jump into the Reflecting Pond on the main Orlando Campus during Spirit Splash, a giant pep rally held the Friday before the game. The event includes live music, performances by the UCF cheerleaders and an introduction of the football team.

In its Best of Florida Schools issue, Florida Leader magazine named Knightmare, the eight days of associated student events, the states Best Homecoming.

Saturday, October 30

UCF Homecoming 2004 Kickoff Concert
Featuring The Ataris, Further Seems Forever and Ludacris

Downtown Orlando, Church Street Station, 8 p.m.

The streets of downtown become a big black-and-gold party. For more information and show times, visit www.ucfknightmare.com.


Monday, November 1
Movie Knight
Memory Mall -- Between Classroom I and the College of Health and Public Affairs on the main UCF campus, 6:30 p.m.
Bring chairs and blankets to watch a free movie, Spiderman 2, under the stars. The Cartoon Network late-night show, [adult swim] also will be shown.


Wednesday, November 3
Black & Gold Gala
Hard Rock Live, 6:30 p.m.

Celebrate the achievements of UCF alumni who have Reached for the Stars at this awards banquet. The evening includes a cocktail reception (open bar) from 6:30 to 7:30 p.m. and a sit-down dinner in an elegant atmosphere. Cost is $45 for UCF Alumni Association members and UCF faculty, staff and students and $55 for all others. A corporate table for 10 may be purchased for $700.

Thursday, November 4
KnightFest Golf Tournament
Champions Gate; 11 a.m. registration, noon shotgun start
UCF alumni and friends will compete in one of the largest golf tournaments in Central Florida. Cost is $75 for UCF Alumni Association members and $95 for non-members. Price includes range balls, cart, greens fees, a box lunch during registration and an awards dinner. Hole sponsorships are also available for $600 and include four players, a hole sponsorship sign and recognition in Pegasus magazine and at the tournament.

 

Friday, November 5
Black and Gold Day
Black and gold is the uniform of the day for alumni and others to show school spirit.

 

Spirit Splash
UCF Reflecting Pond, 3 p.m.

Thousands of students and others will jump in the Reflecting Pond for this unique pep rally.

 

Hullabaloo
UCF Student Union, 8:30 p.m. to midnight
Reunite with all your old friends and classmates at the UCF Alumni Associations biggest event of the year. Enjoy free food and beverages, music and a great opportunity to reminisce. The Presidents Leadership Council will give campus tours starting at 7 p.m. at the Student Union. This event is free to all UCF Alumni Association members and UCF faculty and staff. The cost is $15 for all others. The UCF Community Volunteer Committee will also be holding a book drive at this event. Bring books for any age group to be donated to area community centers.

 

Saturday, November 6

Veterans Day/Homecoming Parade

Downtown Orlando, 10 a.m.

UCF will set the mood for the big game and salute veterans as it joins the Veterans Day Parade down Orange Avenue.

 

KnightFest Fan Faire

Tinker Field, 1 p.m.

Homecoming continues with food and live entertainment for everyone, including the team march to the stadium. Food can be purchased from individual vendors. Game tickets are needed for admission.

 

Homecoming Football Game vs. Ohio
Citrus Bowl, 4 p.m.

Cheer on the Golden Knights as they take on Ohio. For game tickets, call the Ticket Office at 407-UCF-GOLD.

 

This schedule is subject to change. For more information and updates on Homecoming, call the Alumni Association at 407-UCF-ALUM or 800-330-2586 or go to www.ucfalumni.com
