Center for Entrepreneurship and Innovation Celebrates Grand Opening

By Joanne Chive March 13, 2009

Cameron Ford is the founding director of UCF's Center for Entrepreneurship and Innovation, which helps students, faculty and the community.

Aspiring entrepreneurs and inventors at the University of Central Florida have a new place to create and pursue business opportunities. The Center for Entrepreneurship and Innovation (CEI), a university-wide initiative developed by the College of Business Administration, has moved to a larger space within the college. The center is holding a grand-opening celebration from 4 to 7 p.m. Friday, March 20, in the Business Administration building.

While the center is located in the business school, its mission is to serve students in all undergraduate and graduate programs. UCF continues to offer more entrepreneurship courses and consultations for students and the community. These efforts are especially important during times of economic downturn when more people are attracted to independent business ventures.

Media are invited to attend the grand-opening event, which will include a networking reception followed by a formal program at 5:15 p.m. Scheduled to speak are UCF President John C. Hitt; College of Business Administration Dean Thomas Keon; Vice President for Research and Commercialization M.J. Soileau; CEI Director Cameron Ford; and representatives from several student groups who work with the center.

“The College of Business Administration created this academic center to provide the campus community with courses, programs, coaching facilities and special events designed to create a culture of entrepreneurship throughout the university,” said Ford, an associate professor and director of the CEI.

“We believe, as President Obama recently noted, that the answers to our problems exist in our laboratories and universities and in the imaginations of our entrepreneurs,” he added. “The CEI hopes to inspire, educate and support those trying to benefit society with economically sustainable innovations.”

The many entrepreneurs whom the CEI has helped include the founders of GroupTable, a Web-based software company and success network developed to help student groups improve document management, project planning and communication.

“My experience with the CEI has enabled me to take full advantage of all the resources available and has given me so much more,” said David Brim, founder of GroupTable and a recent UCF graduate. “I have had the opportunity to network with many different investors and business leaders, learn from the wisdom and experience of CEI’s coaches and fund my business by winning several different business plan competitions.”

Brim is one of the student success stories that will be featured during the grand opening.

The College of Business Administration offers a variety of entrepreneurship courses for undergraduate and graduate students. More than 1,000 students are currently enrolled in entrepreneurship classes.

To learn more about CEI, visit www.cei.ucf.edu.

UCF Stands For Opportunity: The University of Central Florida is a metropolitan research university that ranks as the 5th largest in the nation with more than 50,000 students. UCF's first classes were offered in 1968. The university offers impressive academic and research environments that power the region's economic development. UCF's culture of opportunity is driven by our diversity, Orlando environment, history of entrepreneurship and our youth, relevance and energy. For more information visit http://news.ucf.edu.
