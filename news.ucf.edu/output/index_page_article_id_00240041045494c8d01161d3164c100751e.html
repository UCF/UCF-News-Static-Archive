UCF Arena to Host First Commencement Ceremonies Dec. 14-15

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala Nov. 28, 2007

Photo: UCF News file

Graduates will walk across the stage in the new UCF Arena for the first time during the December commencement ceremonies.

The University of Central Florida’s December graduates will be the first to cap their academic careers in the new UCF Arena.

More than 3,700 UCF students expect to graduate during three ceremonies Friday, Dec. 14, and Saturday, Dec. 15, in the 10,000-seat Arena, which opened in August.

Graduation ceremonies will be held at 9 a.m. and 3 p.m. Friday, Dec. 14, and at 9:30 a.m. Saturday, Dec. 15. Graduates will begin processing into the Arena 20 minutes before the start of each ceremony.

The Arena is part of the changing face of the north end of UCF’s campus, which also includes Bright House Networks Stadium, four new residence halls, stores, restaurants and parking garages. With nearly double the seating capacity of the old Arena, the new facility has allowed UCF to reduce the number of ceremonies held each semester.  

The Arena opened Sept. 8 with a concert by country singer and actress Reba McEntire, and it has since hosted several concerts, basketball games and other entertainment.

To usher in the new era, graduates will be wearing newly designed caps and gowns with the UCF logo.

Graduates and their guests are urged to get to campus at least 1 ½ hours before their scheduled ceremony. Gemini Boulevard will be closed at Alafaya. Those attending will have to travel south and around east Gemini or travel north on Alafaya to McCullock and then onto Orion to access the Arena and parking garages.

Students and their guests may park in garages C and D as well as lots C2, D1 and D2 free of charge. Disabled guests may park in garage D. For details and a map, visit http://www.registrar.ucf.edu/comminfoucf.07.fall.map.pdf.

Guests must have tickets to attend the ceremonies. Guests without tickets can watch live, closed-circuit telecasts of each ceremony at the Fairwinds Alumni Center, which is located across from the UCF Arena. Ceremonies also can be viewed online at http://webcast.oir.ucf.edu.

Graduates of the College of Education, College of Health and Public Affairs, College of Medicine and College of Nursing will walk across the stage at 9 a.m. Dec. 14. The featured speaker is UCF alumnus and Circuit Judge Walter Komanski.

The afternoon ceremony on Dec. 14 will feature students in the College of Arts and Humanities, the Rosen College of Hospitality Management, College of Sciences, Office of the Vice Provost, Undergraduate Studies and Graduate Studies. The speaker is Margot Knight, president and CEO of United Arts of Central Florida.

State Rep. Dean Cannon of Winter Park will be the speaker at Saturday’s 9:30 a.m. ceremony for the College of Business Administration, College of Engineering and Computer Science and the College of Optics and Photonics.

Among the graduates, 3,029 students filed intent forms for bachelor’s degrees, 626 for master’s degrees, 11 for education specialist degrees and 80 for doctoral degrees.

The total number of degrees awarded by UCF will be about 175,000 following the December ceremonies.
