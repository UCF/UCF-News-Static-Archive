UCF Experts Available to Talk with Reporters about Forest Fires, Impact on Ecology

May 1, 2006

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Tom Evelyn

    • 407-823-5988

  • UCF Biology Department

  • Print this Page

University of Central Florida researchers are available to talk with news reporters about forest fires and their impact on ecology and the environment.

Martin Quigley, UCF Arboretum director-- Expert in urban land development and ecology, forest fires, forest restoration and development and construction. Quigley helped manage a prescribed at UCF earlier this year. 407-823-3146, 407-823-1539 or 407-722-4727 (cell), mquigley@mail.ucf.edu

Reed Noss, biology professor -- Expert in ecology and the role fire plays in the health of the ecology. Also has done research into threatened and endangered species that benefit from fires in their natural habitat. 407-823-0975, 407-489-5778 (cell), rnoss@mail.ucf.edu

Pedro Quintana-Ascencio, assistant biology professor -- Expert in forest fires and how they should be used to encourage a good ecology. His specialty is in plants that depend on the natural cycle of fires. Also can speak to other development and its impact on water resources. Available for interviews in Spanish and English. 407-823-1662, pquinto@mail.ucf.edu

Jack Stout, biology professor -- Expert in the role of forest fires in ecology. 407-823-2919. jstout@pegasus.cc.ucf.edu
