Founding President to Speak at Commencement

By Chad Benette December 11, 2003

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Chad Binette, University Writer

    • 407-823-6312

  • Tom Evelyn, Assistant Director, News and Information

    • 407-823-5988

  • Print this Page

Charles Millican, who led what was then known as Florida Technological University from 1965 to 1978, will address graduates of the College of Business Administration and Rosen School of Hospitality Management during a ceremony at 10 a.m. Monday, Dec. 15.

About 3,200 students are expected to be graduated during that ceremony and three others Monday and Tuesday at the UCF Arena. UCF expects to award about 2,520 bachelor's degrees, 625 master's degrees and 55 doctoral degrees.

Commencement ceremonies for the College of Health and Public Affairs, College of Engineering and Computer Science, and School of Optics will begin at 2 p.m. Monday. Graduates of the College of Education will receive their degrees at 10 a.m. Tuesday, Dec. 16, and College of Arts and Sciences students will be graduated at 1:30 p.m.

Tickets given out by graduates are required to enter all of the ceremonies. Guests without tickets can watch live broadcasts of the ceremonies in Room 101 of the Computer Science Building at UCF or on the Web at webcast.oir.ucf.edu.

In addition to Millican, speakers at UCF's commencement ceremonies will be Debra Austin, Florida chancellor for colleges and universities; Tammy Lytle, Washington bureau chief for the Orlando Sentinel; and Capt. Andy Mohler, commanding officer of the NAVAIR Orlando Training Systems Division.

Austin, who works with the state Board of Governors to administer and promote higher education in Florida, will speak at the College of Education ceremony on Tuesday. Lytle, president of the National Press Club and a journalist for more than 20 years, will address College of Arts and Sciences graduates Tuesday.

Mohler, who has served 25 years in the U.S. Navy, will speak at the ceremony Monday afternoon. The NAVAIR Orlando Training Systems Division helps provide better training for military personnel through the development and acquisition of sophisticated simulation and modeling devices.
