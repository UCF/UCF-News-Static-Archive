International Flute Camp Offered at UCF this Summer

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala May 21, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala

    • 407-823-6120

  • Music Summer Camps at UCF

  • Print this Page

Nora Lee Garcia, a music professor at the University of Central Florida, has cultivated her passion for the flute since she was a child living in Puerto Rico.

Now an internationally known flute player, Garcia passes on her love of the instrument through her annual summer Flute Institute.

The little ones, they get so excited, Garcia said. A lot of them have never had private lessons, and the time we give them here is so special to them.

The Flute Institute is open to middle and high school students who play or want to learn to play the flute. This year several students from Chile have already registered. The institute runs from June 25 to 29. Middle school student sessions are from 9 a.m. to 3 p.m., and high school students participate from 1 to 6 p.m. The cost is $300 per student. Registrations are due June 1 and are limited to the first 25 children who sign up.

The institute provides an opportunity for students to receive instruction from experts in the field and a chance to play some rare instruments, including the double contra  the only one in the United States.

Garcia and her team work with children individually and in small groups based on their experience. Each child is placed into a choir that exposes him or her to full instrumentation, including the C flute, piccolo, alto and bass flute. This helps to develop intonation, rhythm and ensemble skills. Guest instructors add specialties, and this year those instructors include Paige Long, Skip Healy and Anna Garcia Flores.

Long brings all of the big flutes, such as the double contra flute, the contra flute, and others creating a complete flute orchestra. Healy brings Celtic sounds as well as the sound of the fife, a traditional instrument that was used during colonial times to signal the start of a battle. Flores brings the sounds of Latin America.

Students who attend will be shown many aspects of playing the instrument as well as performing in front of an audience. The one-week camp culminates with a free recital at UCF for parents and friends.

For information about the Flute Institute, visit www.noraleegarcia.com or call Nora Lee Garcia at 407-823-3696.

Piano Camp Offered at UCF

Piano Professor Laurent Boukobza and others in the Music Department will offer a piano summer camp for middle and high school students. Students can sign up for one or both of two weeklong sessions. The first will be June 4 to 8, and the second will be June 11 to 15.

Boukobza, who chairs Piano Studies at UCF, is the recipient of the First Prize of Piano and Chamber Music from the Conservatoire National Superieur de Musique de Paris. He has performed around the world and has been honored with many prizes. He is also a Steinway Performing Artist.

During the piano camp students will be exposed to music theory, ear training and sight singing, composition, jazz fundamentals and improvisation. Private lessons and master classes also will be part of the program. Children can attend one week for $300 or both for $580.

For information about the piano camp, contact Boukobza at 407-823-1529 or lboukobz@mail.ucf.edu or Diane Cardarelli at 407-823-3581 or dcardare@mail.ucf.edu.
