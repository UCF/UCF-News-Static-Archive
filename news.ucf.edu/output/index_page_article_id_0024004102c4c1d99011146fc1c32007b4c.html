Spotlights: An Evening of Solo Cabaret Acts Unveils Original One-Person Shows

By Kathleen Lake March 27, 2007

Photo: Brandon Dunnick illustration

Shows written and created by the student performers and highlighting their unique styles will be part of "Spotlights: An Evening of Solo Cabaret Acts."

Six students in the University of Central Floridas Musical Theatre program each will take their moment in the spotlight in April at the News-Journal Center in Daytona Beach, where they will perform Spotlights: An Evening of Solo Cabaret Acts.

In two evenings of original theater performance full of music, humor, dialogue and audience interaction, each of the MFA students will present his or her own show, written and created by the performers and highlighting their unique styles.

On Monday, April 9, Spotlights Evening One premieres, with Margaret-Ellen Jeffreys Southern-influenced A Little Luck Below the Mason-Dixon Line; Kip Taiseys Blame it On My Youth, full of rich and lively jazz; and The Dark Side of the Tune by Michael Biggs, featuring musical theater villains. An encore performance for Spotlights Evening One will be held Tuesday, April 17.

The opening of Spotlights Evening Two, with three different shows, will be Tuesday, April 10. Tara Snyders The Girl Next Door Speaks Up examines a wholesome, perky stereotype; Timothy Ellis finds a song in any moment in These Voices in My Head; and Leah Pages Reflections portrays a journey from childhood to womanhood. An encore performance will be held Monday, April 16.

With the two shows taking audiences from heartbreak to horror to laughter and back, Spotlights: An Evening of Solo Cabaret Acts promises to celebrate the art of musical cabaret through the insight, verve and creativity of six exceptional performers.

As an added bonus, audience members who want to see evenings one and two can show their ticket stubs from one to get in to the other show free.

Spotlights is directed by UCF faculty member Nicholas Wuehrmann. He recently appeared as The Chairman in Seaside Music Theaters production of The Mystery of Edwin Drood. The musical director is Nathaniel Beversluis.

Ticket prices are $15 for adults, $13 for seniors over 55 and $10 for students with ID. Reservations are recommended and available through the UCF Conservatory Theatre box office by calling 407-823-1500 or at the Seaside Music Theater box office by calling 386-252-6200. A $2 service charge will be added for tickets purchased at the Seaside box office. UCF offers accommodations to make the theater more accessible to patrons with disabilities; call the box office for assistance.
