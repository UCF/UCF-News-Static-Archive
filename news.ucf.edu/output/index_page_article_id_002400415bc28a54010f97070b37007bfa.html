More Than 2,100 Community College Students Sign Up for DirectConnect to UCF

By Chad Binette Jan. 11, 2007

Photo: Courtesy of Orlando Regional Chamber of Commerce

At an April 2006 forum, presidents Charles Mojock of Lake-Sumter Community College, Sanford "Sandy" Shugart of Valencia Community College, John Hitt of UCF, Ann McGee of Seminole Community College and Thomas Gamble of Brevard Community College discussed the initiative now known as DirectConnect to UCF.

More than 2,100 students at four Central Florida community colleges have signed up for DirectConnect to UCF, a new program that guarantees them admission to the University of Central Florida after they earn associate degrees.

DirectConnect to UCF is the new name for the program that UCF co-founded last year with Brevard, Lake-Sumter, Seminole and Valencia community colleges. Created to expand students access to higher education, DirectConnect to UCF also will strengthen partnerships in academic programs, advising and financial aid.

Students can sign up before they are admitted or during their studies at the four community colleges. As of last week, 2,184 students have elected to participate in DirectConnect to UCF. They include 1,339 from Valencia, 601 from Seminole, 125 from Lake-Sumter and 119 from Brevard.

About three-fourths of the graduates of the four community colleges who transfer to public universities in Florida attend UCF. 

UCF and each of the community colleges are working together to hire shared advisors who will work with existing advisors from UCF and the community colleges to ensure that students are well-prepared for their transitions to the university.

Many of UCFs most successful alumni began their academic careers at community colleges, said Dave Harrison, vice provost for UCF Regional Campuses. Through DirectConnect to UCF, we are committed to helping future community college graduates make smooth transitions to UCF and enjoy the convenience of earning bachelors degrees in their home counties.

UCF also will be expanding its degree offerings at regional campuses. Planning is under way for a new joint-use facility that UCF will share with Seminole Community College at the Sanford/Lake Mary Campus. UCF and Valencia Community College also have proposed the construction of a new academic building that the schools would share. Joint-use facilities shared by UCF and Brevard Community College in Cocoa and Palm Bay have space available for more daytime classes. There also is room to expand programs at UCF and Lake-Sumter Community Colleges shared facilities at the South Lake Campus.

DirectConnect to UCF originated in late 2005, when the presidents of UCF and Brevard, Lake-Sumter, Seminole and Valencia community colleges decided that an innovative solution was needed to accommodate an increased demand for higher education that is a result of population growth and more students aspiring to attend college. The five schools formed the Central Florida Higher Education Consortium and developed DirectConnect to UCF as the consortiums first major initiative. The name DirectConnect to UCF was selected in late fall 2006.

For more information on DirectConnect to UCF, go to http://www.regionalcampuses.ucf.edu/directconnect.asp.
