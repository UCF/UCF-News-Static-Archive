UCF Students Conserve Enough Energy to Power 115 Homes

By Stacey Radnor March 26, 2007

Photo: Jerry Klein

Shaina Duffey turns off the light as she leaves her Lake Claire room. Students were encouraged to turn off lights when they're not in their rooms and to shut off computers they are not using as part of a competition aimed at conserving energy.

A friendly competition among the University of Central Floridas residence halls in February led to students saving enough energy to power 115 homes for a month.

Throughout February, UCF educated students about how they can conserve energy by turning off lights when they leave a room, turning off computers when they are not in use and taking other simple, everyday steps. Students responded by cutting their February energy use by 136,000 kilowatt hours, or 8 percent, compared to their consumption during February 2006.

About 5,200 UCF students live in on-campus residence halls and were involved in the competition. They saved the university a total of $12,000 compared to what the energy bills would have been had energy consumption remained the same as in February 2006.

It is extremely exciting to see that students made a concerted effort to monitor their consumption. said Assistant Director of University Housing Jeff Novak. It shows how a little individual effort has a major cumulative effect. It is our hope that students will carry this message forward for a lifelong focus of conservation and sustainability."

The Business and Professional Womens Scholarship House, known as the BPW House, led all residence halls with a 22 percent decrease in energy use compared to its average consumption during February of the past three years.

The residents of the BPW House, located behind the Barbara Ying Center, will write essays on what they did to conserve energy and will be entered into a drawing for scholarships donated by the Student Government Association.

Its very easy to live on campus and not watch our energy consumption, especially since students on campus do not get a separate bill for such things, said BPW House Resident Assistant Kaamna Mirchandani. I asked the residents in our house to turn out lights in our public areas such as the living room, kitchen, and dining room. Eventually, my residents even started coming into my room and asking me if I was conserving energy like I was supposed to be doing.

The runner-up was a team from Academic Village buildings 104 and 105. Those students posted a 19 percent reduction compared to their baseline average.

UCF plans to expand the competition next year to include students who live in UCF-affiliated housing and residence halls at the Rosen College of Hospitality Management.
