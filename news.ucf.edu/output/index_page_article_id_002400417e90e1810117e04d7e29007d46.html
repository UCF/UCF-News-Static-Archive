Mayor Crotty, President Hitt Announce Task Force on Underage Alcohol Issues

By Chad Binette Feb. 7, 2008

Photo: Jacque Brund

UCF President John Hitt (left) and Orange County Mayor Rich Crotty announced the formation of the Underage Drinking Task Force on Thursday.

Orange County Mayor Richard Crotty and University of Central Florida President John Hitt today announced the creation of a task force that will address countywide concerns about underage drinking.

Leaders in law enforcement, education and health care, along with retailers, will focus on alcohol education and prevention efforts, access to alcohol by underage residents and other concerns related to the most pervasive drug problem facing youth.

The Underage Drinking Task Force will meet regularly for the next four to six months, as a full panel and in subcommittees focused on prevention, enforcement and public health, before producing a report and action plan for Crotty and Hitt. Orange County Director of Public Safety Mike McCoy and Rich Morrison, a regional vice president with Florida Hospital, will serve as the panel’s co-chairs.

In announcing the task force’s creation, Crotty cited national statistics showing that about 5,000 people under age 21 die annually as a result of underage drinking. In Orange County, a 2006 survey indicated that 38.4 percent of Orange County high school students reported using alcohol in the past 30 days.

“As a father of a teenager that will soon be pursuing his college education, this is an area of concern to me.  Alcohol getting into the hands of our youth is troubling and happens far too often,” stated Mayor Crotty.

For Hitt, the off-campus deaths last semester of two underage UCF students who had consumed alcohol reinforced the need to bring community partners together to discuss solutions that can help to save lives and improve students’ chances of succeeding in college.

“Alcohol abuse can have a severe impact on the lives of our students and their friends and families,” Hitt said. “It is our responsibility as leaders to work together and to achieve solutions that will minimize alcohol’s impact and help students focus on their educational goals.”

On college campuses, a 2005 study showed that 599,000 students ages 18 to 24 are unintentionally injured under the influence of alcohol and 1,700 college students between the ages of 18 and 24 die each year from unintentional alcohol-related injuries, including car accidents.

Six years ago, Hitt created UCF’s Office of Alcohol and Other Drug Prevention Programming, one of the first offices of its kind in the country. The office provides students with the information they need to make responsible choices about alcohol. In one program, freshmen take an online course detailing the risks of alcohol use. The UCF Health Center also screens all of its patients for alcohol misuse.

Rollins College, Full Sail and Valencia Community College each will join UCF in appointing an administrator to the panel. The Underage Drinking Task Force’s approximately 20 members also will include retailers, college students, a high school representative and student, and a member of a local homeowners’ association.

The state Division of Alcoholic Beverages and Tobacco (ABT) will help task force members find solutions to reduce underage residents’ access to alcohol. ABT’s goals include setting up a Responsible Retailing Forum to engage retailers and their suppliers and to uphold responsible retailers as models for ideal business practices. The agency also wants to identify “problem retailers” in the county and make it less profitable and desirable for businesses to sell alcohol to minors.

“Through the establishment of this task force, Florida has the ingredients to provide a comprehensive public-private initiative to prevent sales to underage and intoxicated customers and to improve cooperation across public and private sectors,” Capt. Mark Bong said.

The Orange County Office for A Drug Free Community is a coalition of 10 committees working to identify and utilize the resources within Orange County to effectively provide education, prevention, intervention, and treatment of substance abuse issues resulting in a thriving Drug Free Community.
