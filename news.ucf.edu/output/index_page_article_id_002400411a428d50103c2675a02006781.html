UCF Trustees Back Joint-Use Facilities at Valencia, Seminole Community Colleges

By UCF Staff July 28, 2005

Photo: Jacque Brund

The University of Central Florida Board of Trustees at its regular meeting Thursday, July 28, approved a resolution supporting the creation of new joint-use facilities at Valencia and Seminole community colleges.

Valencia and Seminole community colleges trustees already have supported resolutions that call for those institutions and UCF to seek funds from the state Legislature beginning next year for new buildings at Valencias West Campus and Seminoles Sanford/Lake Mary Campus.

We already have strong relationships with both of these community colleges, Provost Terry Hickey told trustees.

The joint-use facilities, which could open by fall 2009, would allow UCF to expand its baccalaureate degree offerings at Valencia and Seminole. David Harrison, vice provost for regional campuses, said adding the buildings will increase capacity by about 1,000 students at each location. More than 2,600 students transferred to UCF from the two community colleges last year.

The trustees also approved an updated capital improvement plan that covers 2006-07 through 2010-11. The plan includes requests to the Legislature for $10.5 million for the Seminole Community College joint-use facility during the next two years and $11 million for the Valencia Community College joint-use facility during the next three years.

During a campus construction update, Administration and Finance Vice President Bill Merck pointed out that a sightline study had shown that residents of University Estates to the east of campus should not be able to see the proposed on-campus football stadium from their homes.

Merck also discussed the disadvantages of placing the stadium in a south location that some neighbors favor over the north location in the intercollegiate athletics complex that includes the UCF Arena. The south location would require building new access roads, expanding Libra Drive, eliminating parking that serves student housing, moving intramural fields, expanding ponds and mitigating wetlands.

President John Hitt also provided an update on UCFs plans to ask the Board of Governors in November to approve the creation of a medical school on campus and announced that the university would work to raise $20 million to $25 million before that meeting to bolster its case.

Staff will provide an updated proposal for the medical school at the next Board of Trustees meeting Sept. 22.

In other action, the trustees approved creating a direct-support organization, or DSO, to finance the $104 million convocation center project on behalf of UCF.

The planned 10,000-seat convocation center will be built adjacent to the UCF Arena in the athletics complex and will be the centerpiece of a project that will eventually include 2,000 beds of student housing and about 100,000 square feet of restaurants and shops. Construction on the convocation center, which will host sporting events, concerts and other entertainment, could begin this fall and be completed by fall 2007.

Board members also approved policies and procedures for academic learning compacts required by the state Board of Governors. UCF and other public universities in Florida are creating such compacts to measure student achievement in baccalaureate degree programs, and the compacts will be available to prospective and enrolled students.

The board also voted to amend university rules on student organizations, student rights and responsibilities, academic appeals, student discipline and other matters in accordance with state law and Board of Governors policy regarding regulations.

The board re-elected for one-year terms Dick Nunis as chairman and Tom Yochum as vice chairman.
