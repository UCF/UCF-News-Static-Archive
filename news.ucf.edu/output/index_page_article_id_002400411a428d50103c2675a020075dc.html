UCF at Cocoa, Daytona Beach to Hold Information Sessions on New Programs

By UCF Staff June 10, 2005

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Karen Jans

    • 386-506-4003

  • Chad Binette

    • 407-823-6312

  • UCF Regional Campuses

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Floridas Cocoa and Daytona Beach campuses will host information sessions later this month about new bachelors degree of applied science programs that will be offered beginning in the fall.

 

A.S. to Bachelor Degree Options Information/Decision Days will be held from 10 a.m. to noon and 5 to 7 p.m. Tuesday, June 21, in Building 150, Room 101, of the Daytona Beach campus. Sessions will be held at the same times Thursday, June 23, at the Community Dining Hall, Building 11, at the Cocoa campus.

 

Advisors and representatives from UCFs admissions and financial aid offices will be available to answer questions from prospective students. Students who want to apply and get an on-the-spot admissions decision must bring a completed application, their college transcripts and a $30 check or money order.

 

The bachelors degree of applied science program provides students with a new way to build on the technical or professional skills acquired while obtaining associate of science degrees. They will improve their abilities to manage, communicate and think critically. The curriculum consists of core courses in ethics, management and communication and a concentration chosen by the student. In Cocoa, students can choose either the Supervision and Administration or the Industrial Operations track. Daytona Beach students can pursue the Supervision and Administration track.

 

If necessary, students can complete remaining general education or foreign language courses at a community college or UCF. Students may also take internships or elective courses relevant to their professional and educational goals.

 

The degree programs provide students with the opportunity for a high-quality education at convenient locations, with flexible class scheduling and with affordable state tuition rates, said Dave Harrison, vice provost for UCF Regional Campuses.

 

More information about the information/decision days and UCFs programs is available at www.regionalcampuses.ucf.edu. Students also can visit the Cocoa campus at 1519 Clearlake Road or the Daytona Beach campus at 1200 W. International Speedway Blvd. They can call UCF at Cocoa at 321-433-7889 or UCF at Daytona Beach at 386-506-4021.
