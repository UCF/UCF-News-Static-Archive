More Than 2,600 Students Expect to Graduate During Summer Ceremonies Aug. 5

By Chad Binette Updated Aug. 2, 2006

Photo: Courtesy of Dr. Cecil Wilson

Dr. Cecil Wilson, a Winter Park internist who serves as chair of the American Medical Association’s Board of Trustees, will be one of the three commencement speakers this summer.

More than 2,600 University of Central Florida students expect to graduate during three commencement ceremonies Saturday, Aug. 5, at the UCF Arena.

The anticipated graduates include 2,019 who filed intents to receive bachelors degrees, 576 for masters degrees, two for education specialist degrees and 77 for doctoral degrees.

The first ceremony, at 8:30 a.m., will feature graduates of the College of Arts and Humanities, College of Sciences, Rosen College of Hospitality Management and also Liberal Studies and Social Science majors through the Undergraduate Studies and Graduate Studies offices.

Jim Lewis, senior vice president and general manager for Disney Vacation Development Inc., will be the guest speaker at the morning ceremony.

At noon, College of Business Administration and College of Education students will graduate. The guest speaker will be Orange County Commissioner Mildred Fernandez.

The final ceremony at 3:30 p.m. will feature graduates of the College of Health and Public Affairs, College of Engineering and Computer Science, College of Optics and Photonics and Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences. Dr. Cecil Wilson, a Winter Park internist who serves as chair of the American Medical Associations Board of Trustees, will be the speaker.

Following the summer ceremonies, UCF will have awarded about 161,000 degrees since starting classes in 1968. President John Hitt conferred the 100,000th degree of his 14-year tenure during the spring commencement ceremonies in May.

Guests must have tickets to attend the ceremonies. Guests without tickets can watch live, closed-circuit telecasts of each ceremony at the Fairwinds Alumni Center, which is located across from the UCF Arena.

Ceremonies also can be viewed online at http://webcast.oir.ucf.edu.
