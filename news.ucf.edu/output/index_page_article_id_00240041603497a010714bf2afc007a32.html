NEW: UCF Health Sciences Campus/College of Medicine site

The state Board of Governors on March 23, 2006, approved creating the College of Medicine at the University of Central Florida. The university has begun planning the UCF Healthcare Campus at Lake Nona (map), where the colleges of medicine and biomedical sciences and a medical library will be located. UCF will soon begin a search for the medical college dean.

The university has raised more than $100 million for construction of the Healthcare Campus buildings. That total includes private donations, state matching funds and the value of the Tavistock Groups gift of 50 acres at Lake Nona. Tavistock also led the group of donors with a pledge of $12.5 million toward the project.

The medical college (fact sheet) and its degree program, which calls for a four-year curriculum that will eventually produce about 120 medical graduates each year, will capitalize on UCFs existing strengths in biomedical sciences, modeling and simulation, and optics and photonics. Other programs in materials science, psychology, chemistry, film and digital media, and nursing also will support the program.
