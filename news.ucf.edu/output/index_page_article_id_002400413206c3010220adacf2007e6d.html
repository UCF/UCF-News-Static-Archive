Illinois Couple Makes Largest Donation Ever to UCF Art Department

By Matthew Dunn March 2, 2005

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Sae Schatz

    • 407-823-5164

  • UCF Art Department

  • Print this Page

George and Norma Kotteman of Peoria, Ill., have donated a prized modern art collection to the UCF Art Department. The collection, appraised at $2.2 million, is the largest ever given to the university.

The donation is comprised of mostly abstract, late 20th century work and includes more than a dozen of the finest examples of contemporary outdoor sculpture. It features pieces by vital artists such as Isamu Naguchi and George Rickey. The collection also contains valuable works of glass and clay, paintings and prints.

The art will be placed prominently throughout the campus and incorporated into the life of the university. It will enhance the classroom experience of art students and the look of the school. Students will not have to rely solely on modern art textbooks when they can see contemporary art first-hand, and a walking tour will be set up for visitors to view the collection. The donation also will be a key attraction for visiting scholars and a significant step toward the addition of other important art collections.

The Kottemans are longtime art collectors with a genuine belief in the importance of art. George Kotteman, a retired orthodontist, began with the purchase of a watercolor painting, and his collecting blossomed into a lifelong passion. Eventually, a stunning assemblage of modern sculpture filled the backyard of their home.

UCF art professor Robert Reedy, who organized the donation, has known the Kotteman family since he was chairman of fine arts at Bradley University. He has kept close ties with the couple since then. When the Kottemans began to consider a future and permanent home for their collection, Reedy pursued the opportunity to display such important artworks at UCF.

The Kottemans wished to keep the collection together where it could be seen, appreciated and cared for properly. UCF offered the setting of a large, diverse campus with the resources to maintain and display the work as well as security to insure its safe housing.

The acquisition of this collection will underscore the university's commitment to visual arts, as well as ensure UCF's position of leadership and prominence regarding the exhibition of outdoor sculpture in our region, Reedy said.
