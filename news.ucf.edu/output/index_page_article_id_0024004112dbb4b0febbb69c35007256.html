UCF to Honor Hurricane Heroes with Free Tickets to Football Game

By Linda Gray Sept. 10, 2004

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Tom Evelyn

    • 407-823-5988

  • UCF Athletics

  • Citrus Bowl directions

Calling them hurricane heroes, the University of Central Florida is thanking its employees who worked to secure the campus before, during and after hurricanes Charley and Frances by giving them tickets to the UCF-West Virginia football game Saturday, Sept. 11, at the Florida Citrus Bowl.

The university administration wanted to do something special for the emergency response team members who worked Aug. 13-16 and Sept. 3-6 to shore up the campus and then, throughout those weekends, take inventory, make repairs and clean up campus roads and sidewalks.

The more than 350 employees who worked as part of the team have been offered the free tickets and parking passes them and their immediate families. As of Friday afternoon, more than 1,200 tickets had been distributed to the employees and their families.

Emergency team employees who have not received their tickets and parking passes should contact their supervisors Friday.

Free shuttle buses also will be available to the emergency team employees. The schedule is as follows:

3 p.m.  First bus (or buses) leaves UCF Education parking lot.

4 p.m.  Second bus (or buses) leaves UCF Education parking lot.

4:30 p.m.  Third bus (or buses) leaves UCF Education parking lot.

8 p.m.  First bus (or buses) leaves Citrus Bowl for UCF.

9:30 p.m.  Second bus (or buses) leaves Citrus Bowl for UCF.

10 p.m.  Last bus (or buses) leaves Citrus Bowl for UCF after game.

We owe a special thanks to our emergency response team that worked throughout the storm and its aftermath to maintain or restore vital campus services, UCF President John Hitt said the week following Charley. They put UCF ahead of their own families and concerns to ensure that we have a successful fall opening.

UCF Director of Environmental Health and Safety Jim Uhlir called the recovery effort by the emergency team amazing.

An area conference call of cities on the Sunday after Hurricane Charley showed that only Reedy Creek (Disney) was significantly more recovered than UCF, said Uhlir. For our team to provide Disney levels of service with our existing resources is simply amazing.

Following each of the hurricanes, the UCF emergency team within 24 hours had cleared all roads and sidewalks of fallen trees and debris and had inspected all campus buildings to determine the extent of damage and whether or not they could be occupied safely.

The worst damage to campus was done by Hurricane Charley to the state-of-the-art student Recreation and Wellness Center, which lost most of its roof. UCF Physical Plant employees estimate that as many as 1,000 trees were uprooted, split or otherwise destroyed by the two hurricanes, changing the look of the verdant UCF campus. Hurricane Frances did not damage the UCF campus as much as Charley, though there was minor water damage and the loss of more trees.

The UCF emergency team consists of employees from Physical Plant, Dining Services, University Police, Environmental Health and Safety and other campus departments.
