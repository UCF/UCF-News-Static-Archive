UCF Moves Up in Best Colleges Ranking

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala Aug. 17, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala

    • 407-823-1620

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Florida improved its position in this years U.S. News & World Reports annual rankings of national universities.

UCF ranks in the third tier of national universities, which includes colleges ranked 131th through 187th. Last year UCF ranked in the fourth tier. The Americas Best Colleges guide will be in the Aug. 27 edition of U.S. News & World Report, which hits newsstands on Monday, Aug. 20.

We are pleased that U.S. News & World Report has recognized the quality of our students and faculty by ranking UCF among its Best Colleges, said Provost Terry Hickey. We wont rest on past performance since there is always room for improvement. We will continue to look for ways to provide our students and faculty opportunities to excel.

U.S. News & World Report ranks schools based on categories such as the average freshman retention rate, graduation rates, acceptance rates and the percentage of freshmen, who ranked in the top 10 percent of their high school classes.

UCF improved its performance in the area of the acceptance ratio of students, SAT scores, graduation rates and freshman retention rate among others. The rankings were based on data from the 2005-2006 academic year.

The University of Florida ranked 49th nationwide, while the University of Miami tied with Yeshiva University in New York for the 52nd spot. Florida State University tied with five other schools for 112th place.

Schools in the third and fourth tiers are listed alphabetically and are not assigned specific rankings. The Florida Institute of Technology and University of South Florida joined UCF in the third tier. Florida Atlantic University, Florida International University, Nova Southeastern, Florida A&M and Barry University were ranked in the fourth tier.
