Psychology Department Wins Department of the Year Award

By Sae Schatz July 7, 2004

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Richard Tucker

    • 407-823-3576

  • American Psychological Association of Graduate Students

  • UCF Psychology Department

  • Print this Page

The American Psychological Association of Graduate Students has named UCFs Psychology Department as its 2004 Department of the Year.

The award recognizes a graduate psychology department that has shown an outstanding commitment to graduate students through student-faculty relations and by attending well to student needs.

"I think [the award] shows what a strong graduate department we have, even though our programs are relatively new," said Interim Chair Richard Tucker, adding that hes pleased "just knowing that our graduate students think so well of us."

The association, which was founded in 1988 and has more than 41,000 members, is the largest organization of graduate psychology students worldwide. The universities of Florida and South Florida are among the past winners of the Department of the Year award.

Renée E. DeRouin, a doctoral student in industrial and organizational psychology, nominated UCF for the award and collaborated with Tucker to write an essay for the nomination [ view the essay ]. Their essay emphasized the department's research strengths, involvement with professional organizations and attentiveness to student input during the recent departmental chair search, along with the Student Teaching Apprentice Mentorship Program (STAMP).

DeRouin and Tucker will be presented with the Department of the Year award at an American Psychological Association conference in August in Honolulu. The Psychology Department will also receive $1,000, which will be used to help graduate students.

For more information, go to www.apa.org/apags.
