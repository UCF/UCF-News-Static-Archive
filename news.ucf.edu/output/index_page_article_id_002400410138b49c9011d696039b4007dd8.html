UCF Film Graduate Premieres a Unique, Behind-the-Scenes Video Podcast Series

By Tom Hurter Nov. 5, 2008

Related Contacts and Links:

    • 407-913-5398

  • The Attic Door

  • Print this Page

  • Share on Facebook
  • Seed Newsvine

A recent University of Central Florida film graduate, Danny Daneau, has launched an innovative, online podcast series that sheds light on the trials and tribulations of independent filmmaking.

The series is called “Behind The Attic Door” and closely examines the personal journeys of Daneau, his cast and crew as they attempt to complete production on Daneau’s first feature-length film, “The Attic Door.” While awaiting their film festival premiere, the filmmakers hope the weekly episodes will build an online audience invested in the success of the film by playing a part in the production process.

The videos are currently available at www.theatticdoormovie.com/video.

“More than anything, we wanted to make an amazing film,” said writer-director Daneau. “With all the complications of working on a low budget, with the only two child-actor cast, shooting in the middle of the desolate American West, we thought there might be a chance to tell another type of story. I hope this will inspire and enlighten other young filmmakers as they follow their dreams toward the daunting big screen.”

Video podcasts to promote independent film are a new medium for filmmakers. Recently, the feature film “Four Eyed Monsters” made the news by hosting a series of podcasts that focused on the real-life personal struggles of a young filmmaking couple trying to get the film distributed.

The creative team behind “The Attic Door” saw an opportunity to take an online audience on an even larger journey. The podcast series begins with the young director developing the screenplay and continues through trials and tribulations while shooting this turn-of-the-century Western-inspired tale in the middle of the harsh landscape of southern Utah. Each episode offers unique insights on how an indie feature film is made, while at the same time documenting the struggles of the team as they try to realize their personal dreams.

“The Attic Door” is a story about a young brother and sister who are abandoned in the vast and lonely landscape of the 19th-century American West. Each day, they struggle to keep up the family farm, anxiously waiting for their parents to return. With nowhere to escape, the two siblings are about to discover that they are not entirely alone. As much as they try to deny the truth, something behind the attic door has awakened and they must now face their greatest fear. “The Attic Door” is the story of love, loss, loneliness and the truth behind childhood fears.

New episodes of “Behind The Attic Door” premiere every Friday and can be found on the official Web site of the feature film at www.theatticdoormovie.com.
