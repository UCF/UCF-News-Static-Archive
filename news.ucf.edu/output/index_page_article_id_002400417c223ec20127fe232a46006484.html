CNN, Time Magazine and CBS Evening News Talking About UCF

By Grant J. Heston April 27, 2010

  • Grant J. Heston

    • 407.823.5988

Please see below for a review of selected print, broadcast and online media coverage for the University of Central Florida. Follow UCF news on Twitter and Facebook, watch UCF TV and learn more about Strategy, Marketing, Communications & Admissions.
 

NATIONAL

CNN (Live): Future of the U.S. Space Program
-- This live interview in the UCF TV studios features Political Science Chair Roger Handberg.

Business Week: UCF Gives You Most for Your Money
Undergraduate business students looking to get the most for their money should attend the UCF College of Business Administration, according to a ranking released by Bloomberg BusinessWeek.

Time Magazine: Crist Moves Closer to Running as an Independent
Florida Governor Charlie Crist has to decide by April 30 whether he wants to run as an independent for his state's open U.S. Senate seat this fall — an option he finally conceded this week he's considering.
-- This story quotes UCF Political Science professor Aubrey Jewett.

CBS Evening News: From homeless to Harvard
Everyone has baggage, but Lalita Booth's is heavier than most. CBS News correspondent Michelle Miller reports Booth can laugh now, but as a young teen she nearly destroyed her life.
-- This is a feature about UCF graduate and current Harvard student Lalita Booth.

CNN: NCAA Basketball Graduation Rates
The analysis of the graduation success teams in the big dance shows both progress and problems.
-- This features research from Richard Lapchick, director of UCF's Institute for Diversity and Ethics in Sport.

The Chronicle of Higher Education: March Madness and Political Grudge
It seemed innocent enough. The majority leader of the U.S. House of Representatives, Rep. Steny Hoyer of Maryland, took a few minutes late Tuesday on the House floor to show a little Terrapin pride.
-- This features research from Richard Lapchick, director of UCF's Institute for Diversity and Ethics in Sport.

The Washington Post: NASA's $500M Launcher Missing Just One Thing: a Rocket
Anyone need a $500 million, 355-foot steel tower for launching rockets into space? There's one available at NASA's Kennedy Space Center. Brand new, never been used.
-- This story quotes Dale Ketcham, director of the Spaceport Research and Technology Institute.

LOCAL / STATE

Orlando Sentinel: UCF Vows to Go Green in Big Way
UCF pledges to go 'climate-neutral' by doing such things as covering roof with plants.

Orlando Sentinel: UCF Featured in New Energy-Saving Video
UCF plays the leading role in a new public service message from the Department of Energy that encourages college students to conserve power by taking simple steps such as unplugging curling irons and switching to energy-efficient light bulbs.

Orlando Sentinel: $13M Microchip Plant in Brevard Donated to UCF
California-based Intersil Corp. said Wednesday it has given a former microchip factory in Palm Bay to the University of Central Florida – one of the biggest building donations UCF has ever received.

News 13: John Young History Maker Award Honors UCF’s Millican
The Historical Society of Central Florida honored Dr. Charles Millican, the founding president of the University of Central Florida.

Space Coast Medicine: Building Community Partnerships
Michael Frumkin is a man on a mission.
-- This story features the UCF College of Health and Public Affairs.

Tampa Tribune: Experts Differ on Merits, Impact of McCollum's Health Care Lawsuit
Florida Attorney General Bill McCollum grabbed national attention and praise from fellow Republicans with a lawsuit challenging the newly passed health care reform bill.
-- This story quotes UCF Political Science professor Aubrey Jewett.
