Women Leaders to Discuss Ethics and Human Rights Thursday at UCF Forum

By Mark Freeman Oct. 28, 2008

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Mark Freeman, Global Perspectives

    • 407-823-0935

  • UCF Global Perspectives

  • Print this Page

Prominent women leaders will bring their ideas about women, leadership, ethics and human rights to the University of Central Florida on Thursday, Oct. 30, for a forum titled, “Women and Leadership in the Evolving 21st Century World: Ethics and Human Rights.”

The forum, organized by the UCF Global Perspectives Office, will take place in the Cape Florida Ballroom of the Student Union from 2 to 5 p.m. It is free and open to the public.

Ambassador Harriet Elam-Thomas, director of UCF’s Diplomacy Program, will give the first presentation and serve as facilitator for the forum. From 2003 to 2005, she served as diplomat in residence at UCF under the auspices of a national U.S. Department of State program. Until fall 2005, she was a Senior Foreign Service officer with the rank of career minister. Previously, she served as U.S. ambassador to Senegal from 1999 to 2003 and in several other governmental positions.

Following Elam-Thomas’ speech, three separate presentations will be given by Colette Rausch, Mary C. Rawlinson and Kay Semion.

Rausch is the deputy director of the Rule of Law program at the United States Institute of Peace.  Her focus is on criminal justice and police reform initiatives that have included missions and projects in Afghanistan, Liberia and Nepal. Before joining the institute, Rausch worked at the Organization for Security and Cooperation in Europe Mission in Kosovo, serving first as head of the Rule of Law Division and then as director of the Department of Human Rights and Rule of Law.
 
Rawlinson is the editor of the International Journal of Feminist Approaches to Bioethics and an associate professor at Stony Brook University. Rawlinson served four years as associate dean for Curriculum in the College of Arts and Sciences and founded the Learning Communities Program. She is an affiliated faculty member in Comparative Literature and Women's Studies.  Rawlinson is also the co-editor of “The Voice of Breast Cancer in Medicine and Bioethics” and “Derrida and Feminism” and the editor of five issues of the Journal of Medicine and Philosophy.

Semion, the president of Semion Communications, is an award-winning journalist who has demonstrated dedication to international news and commentary. A past president of the National Conference of Editorial Writers, she has traveled abroad on multiple fact-finding tours, surveying freedom of speech in the news media in places such as the Middle East and Central Asia. She is also a former editor of NCEW's quarterly, The Masthead, which features articles on global press freedom.
 
Lt. General (Ret.) Jay Garner, former director of the Office of Reconstruction and Humanitarian Assistance for Post-war Iraq, will give opening remarks at the forum.  In addition to his role in Post-war Iraq, Garner held many positions within the U.S. Army, ranging from commanding general of the U.S. Army Space and Strategic Defense Command to assistant deputy chief of staff for Force Development.

In addition to the Global Perspectives Office, sponsors of this forum include Lawrence J. Chastang and The Chastang Foundation, UCF’s Diplomacy Program, the UCF Women’s Research Center, Women’s Studies Program and Political Science Department, UCF LIFE and the Global Connections Foundation.
