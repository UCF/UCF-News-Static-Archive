UCF Year's End: Knights Came Home in 2007 And Plan More Successes for '08

By Christine Dellert Dec. 28, 2007

Photo: Jacque Brund

A new Charging Knight statue by local artist Don Reynolds stands outside Bright House Networks Stadium. The stadium opened its gates to fans on Sept. 15 for the first on-campus football game.

Full Photo

The University of Central Florida enjoyed a historic year in 2007, highlighted by the groundbreaking for the College of Medicine, innovative research achievements, the first on-campus football game and inaugural commencement in the new UCF Arena.

And as the calendar turns to 2008, UCF officials expect continued success in the new year.

The UCF College of Medicine hit several milestones this year, including participating in a groundbreaking ceremony at Lake Nona with its new neighbor, the Burnham Institute for Medical Research, in October, securing a visit from the national accrediting board in December and obtaining half of the 40 scholarships it has promised to all its incoming students. The scholarships will cover tuition, fees, room and board for all four years of the program. The college eagerly awaits word about its accreditation and plans to accept applications once preliminary accreditation is granted next year.

Research Out of This World

Innovative research ideas led to record-setting funding this past year. UCF's researchers received $121.4 million in outside grant funding in the 2006-07 fiscal year -- the third consecutive year that they've surpassed $100 million.

Notable research initiatives in 2007 include Professor Henry Daniell's efforts to prevent diabetes with insulin capsules produced in genetically modified lettuce. His research holds promise for millions of Americans who suffer from insulin-dependent diabetes.

UCF Physics Professor Joseph Harrington was one of several scientists this year who captured enough light from an exoplanet, a planet outside our solar system, to spread it apart and find signatures of molecules in the planet's atmosphere. The landmark achievement is a key step toward being able to detect life on alien worlds -- and comes years before astronomers had thought possible.

The Changing Face of Campus

The Knights "came home" in September, emulating the slogan lauded by UCF's Athletics and Alumni organizations. The team played its first on-campus football game in Bright House Networks Stadium and ended a 10-3 season with an invitation to the AutoZone Liberty Bowl Saturday in Memphis.

The games drew thousands of students and fans from the community to the changing face of UCF's north end -- where a new stadium, residence halls, restaurants and shops offer quality on-campus amenities for enhanced student life.

And new Knights keep coming. UCF enrolled its largest class yet of 48,497 in fall 2007, making it the sixth-largest university in the country.

A 10,000-seat arena also opened on campus this fall. So far, the UCF Arena has hosted basketball games and concerts from big-name performers, such as Reba McEntire, Elton John and Bill Cosby. With nearly double the seating capacity of the old Arena, the new facility held its first ceremonies earlier this month. More than 3,000 students graduated.

UCF's growth will continue in the new year.

Construction has already begun on a 68,000-square-foot Physical Sciences building on the main campus. The building will house UCF's growing Planetary Sciences Group -- among others -- whose cutting-edge Ritchey-Chretien telescope opened to the public in August. Experts say the 20-inch robotic telescope is one of the best in the state.

UCF also expects to build more industry and education partnerships at the former Expo Centre downtown -- home to the Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy. UCF leaders are committed to enhancing students' opportunities by partnering with well-known companies, such as Vicon Entertainment's House of Moves. The 3,500-square-foot motion capture studio is expected to celebrate its grand opening in early 2008.

Black, gold and green

UCF President John Hitt accepted an invitation earlier this year to join the American College & Presidents Climate Commitment leadership circle following his emphasis on several energy conservation and sustainability efforts. UCF started using biodiesel fuel for all diesel-powered university vehicles. And UCF's Center for Energy and Sustainability also became the first in the state to turn a gas-powered fleet vehicle into a plug-in hybrid.

UCF's medical school also recently announced that it will be "going green." The university has adopted the U.S. Green Building Council's Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED) standards for buildings that are new or undergo major renovations.

UCF Stands for Opportunity: Established in 1963, the University of Central Florida is a diverse metropolitan research university that ranks as the 6th-largest in the country with more than 48,000 students. Located in Orlando, Fla., UCF offers high-quality undergraduate and graduate education, student development and continuing education, while conducting cutting-edge research that powers the region's economic development.
