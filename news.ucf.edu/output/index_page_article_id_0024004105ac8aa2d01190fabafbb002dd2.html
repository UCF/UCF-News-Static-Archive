Several University of Central Florida students were honored for their commitment to excellence in academics, leadership and community service at the annual Founders’ Day Awards Ceremony.

College of Arts and Humanities

Founders’ Award: Kristina Kopic, an English major, earned praise for her outstanding writing skills and community service. She mentors at-risk high school students and teaches fourth graders as part of UCF’s partnership with Junior Achievement. Kopic is a peer ambassador for The Burnett Honors College and secretary of Sigma Tau Delta, the English honor society.

Founders’ Scholar Awards: Kelly M. Allen, Jennifer R. Anderson, Kristin D. Blyar, Steven H. Cohen, Mark W. Convoy, Brenna M. Egan, Thomas Gillan, David L. Givens, Ashley M. Inguanta, Christian D. Newth, Jeffrey E. Parks, Heather K. Reagan, Michael S. Reid, Nathan V. Seta, Megan E. Speer and Nicholas A. Walsh.

College of Business Administration

Founders’ Award: Lalita D. Booth, a Finance and Accounting double major and Burnett Honors College student, became UCF’s first Truman Scholar, an award for outstanding college juniors devoted to public service. She is a member of Phi Theta Kappa, Phi Beta Lambda and Tau Sigma national honor societies. Booth is the founder and program director for the Lighthouse for Dreams Financial Literacy Project and is a tax advocate and preparer for low-income filers. She hopes to enroll in Harvard University’s concurrent Master in Public Policy and Juris Doctorate program and to establish a foundation dedicated to helping the poor transition from poverty to self-sufficiency.

Frazier, Cynthia K. Gabriel, Daniel J. Gilardi, Jr., Cameron G. Gomes, Christopher A. Johnston, Scott L. Kirkman, Puja J. Mandolfo, Marketa Martin, Carla L. McKnight, Ashlee M. Moses, Atikur Motiwala, Elaina S. Naughton, Mau V. Nguyen, Blake M. Perrault, Barbara D. Plevy, Alison E. Provost, Tristan J. Reiniers, Evan M. Savarick, Jeffrey D. Slanker, Oliver W. Snavely, Francisco D. Soriano, Malorie A. Sterner, Aaron J. Stimmel, Laura M. Stockbridge and Kelly D. Wallace.

College of Education

Founders’ Award: Tara Wilson, an Elementary Education major with a Spanish minor, has made many contributions to UCF over the past three years. She has served as president of the UCF chapter of the Student National Education Association (SNEA), vice president of the Student Florida Education Association and as a UCF resident assistant. After reviving SNEA at UCF and making it a vital part of the College of Education, she organized workshops integrating art into reading curricula and dealing with bullying in the classroom. She has encouraged the UCF community to participate in Read Across America events with neighboring elementary schools.
 
Founders’ Scholar Awards: Stephanie H. Boates, Angela Colbert, Joseph D. Dalesandro, Christina M. Garcia del Busto, Gina A. Kalis, Wendy A. Kearns, Vanessa M. Marino, Connie Moravecky, Tiffany M. Morley, Shalay K. Nash, Andres M. Rincon, Sandra L. Schramm, Meire D. Silva, Courtney M. Stark and Sarah S. Stoeckel.

College of Engineering and Computer Science

Founders’ Award: Katherine A. Luetzow, a senior majoring in Civil Engineering and a Burnett Honors College student, has earned a 4.0 grade point average and many academic honors at UCF. She also volunteers with Habitat for Humanity, the Howard Phillips Center for Children and Families and her church. She is a member of the American Society of Civil Engineers and several honor societies. She serves as a research assistant in the Department of Civil, Environmental and Construction Engineering, and she specialized in roadway design as an intern with Reynolds, Smith and Hills. She plans to work as a transportation engineer in roadway design after graduating this semester.

Founders’ Scholar Awards: Joan Baldriche, Ryan C. Clapp, Christopher J. Cordo, Matthew B. Davis, Robert A. Fischer, Jr., Jesse M. Kelly, Ross A. Kerley, Wesley M. Lenzen, Jonathan R. Mohlenhoff, Paul J. Nelson, Eric M. O’Sullivan, Cliff S. Roberts, Lenora K. Scholl, David D. Scitney, Patrick A. Stauffer, Willie J. Stephens and Melissa A. Wenck.

College of Health and Public Affairs

Founders’ Award: Megan Policastro, a Legal Studies major with a minor in Political Science, is a Burnett Honors College student and the President’s Leadership Council chair. A LEAD Scholar, Policastro currently serves as director of university relations for the Student Government Association.  She volunteers with Habitat for Humanity, the Coalition for the Homeless, the American Heart Association and the American Cancer Society’s Relay for Life. She has raised more than $20,000 for charity while at UCF.  She plans to pursue a career in environmental law after graduating this year. 

Founders’ Scholar Awards: Lauren Albers, Samuel Bird III, Denise Cespedes, Jamie A. Cummings, Kelly B. Davis, Lynn S. Deem, Audrey M. Flanegin, Stephen J. Hajdas, Nicole J. Hudson, Borica Karan, Marla Laloo, Angela K. Little, Elisabeth S. Lottman, Giselle M. Lugo, Eileen B. Stana, Candace S. Stuart and Janine R. Vance.

Rosen College of Hospitality Management

Founders’ Award: Steven Matthew Vagnini, a junior from Central Florida, is a Hospitality Management major and Burnett Honors College student. He joined Walt Disney World as an attraction host during his senior year of high school, and he worked at the Walt Disney Archives in Burbank, Calif., at the heart of the Walt Disney Company’s Operations. He is a member of the Eta Sigma Delta Honor Society and intends to continue his studies into graduate school and to seek leadership roles in the hospitality industry.

Founders’ Scholar Awards: Danielle R. Bennett, Marian E. Garcia, Lydia A. Hueneke, Kevin M. Maker, Nicole P. Mills, Catherine I. Rash, Melanie A. Reese and Ashley E. Van Ryn.

College of Medicine

Founders’ Award: Sareh S. Shoraka, a Molecular and Microbiology major from Jacksonville, is a LEAD Scholar, treasurer of UCF’s chapter of the Pre-Medical American Student Medical Association and a mentor for out-of-state students with Learning and Interacting with New Knights (LINK). She played an integral role in creating a new UCF living-learning community for out-of-state students that will begin in the fall. She studied Alzheimer’s disease during a Mayo Clinic fellowship and published her work in a prestigious research journal. She has volunteered at many hospitals and charitable organizations throughout Central Florida and plans to become a physician.

Founders’ Scholar Awards: Bianca R. Badaro, Rose M. Crutcher, Jennifer K. Johnston, Rajiv K. Nathoo, Carmen E. Parrilla, Barbara A. Swift and Mohamad Zetir.

College of Nursing

Founders’ Award: Mark “Brian” Ligad represents the future of nursing. His professors say he’s bright, knows his science and has an exceptional ability to translate his knowledge to tender patient care. He works as an advanced clinical nurse tech in the intermediate critical care unit at Orlando Regional Medical Center, and he holds health care certifications from the American Heart Association and the American Lung Association. Ligad has served as vice president for the UCF Student Nurses Association.

Founders’ Scholar Awards: Brooke N. Edenfield, Veronica J. Farmer, Christi G. Johnson, Amanda M. Pyatt and Kimberly D. Stafford.

College of Sciences

Founders’ Award: Alexandra Orlova, a Biology student, was born and educated in Russia. After winning a contest to complete her senior year of high school in the United States, she realized her love for veterinary medicine and relocated to America to pursue her higher education. Her professors describe her as a diligent student who loves to learn and who sets a high standard that other students find hard to achieve. Orlova mentors younger students, volunteers as a technician in a local clinic and helps distressed animals.  She has been accepted at several veterinary schools.

Office of the Vice Provost

Founders’ Award: Nelsi Mora, of Cape Coral, has studied Letters, Modern Languages, Spanish and Natural Sciences and plans to pursue a career in medicine. She participated in LEAD Scholars and is a coordinator of UCF’s Orientation Team. She also plays women’s soccer. Originally from the Dominican Republic, Mora moved to the United States when she was 5, and she credits her parents with instilling in her the importance of education.
