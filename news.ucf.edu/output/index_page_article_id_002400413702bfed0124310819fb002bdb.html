Reach for the Stars: Alumni Honor Founding President With Symbolic Statue

By Zenaida Kotala and Christine Dellert Nov. 13, 2009

Photo: Jacque Brund

When Charles Millican arrived on the UCF campus more than 40 years ago, he had a hard time finding it. There weren’t many people to ask for directions, just a few cows. But he saw beyond the 1,200 acres of scrubland and dirt roads and envisioned what the University of Central Florida is today — a thriving research-based metropolitan university that is now the 3rd largest in the nation.

Millican was honored for his courage, dedication and vision on Friday with the unveiling of a bronze statue commissioned by UCF alumni. The dedication ceremony was one of the highlights of this week’s Homecoming activities.

“I feel highly honored and deeply, deeply grateful,” Millican told the crowd of more than 300 just before the statue’s unveiling. “I thank you very much for making my impossible dream about this university come to pass.”

Local artist Don Reynolds created the 500-pound statue to represent the vision and confidence that Millican brought to UCF. The statue features Millican with his right foot at the apex of a labyrinth taking that “first step, while gesturing to us all to look up and dream with inner confidence and faith, knowing that we, too, can make it if we try,” said Reynolds. The Sanford artist has created several other sculptures on UCF’s campus, including “The Charging Knight” outside Bright House Networks Stadium.

UCF President John C. Hitt attended Friday’s ceremony, as did several alumni who remembered the early days of UCF when it was still called Florida Technological University.

Millican had quite the challenge, Hitt told the crowd. As the newly appointed president of a newly established state university, he had a budget of $75,000 and an office above a drugstore in downtown Orlando.

“Charlie dreamed, but he also worked—worked very hard—and he molded his dreams into reality,” Hitt said. “We follow in the footsteps of a humble man of strong faith, a private man who has created a lasting legacy, and a public servant whose wisdom and counsel continue to benefit us all.”

The nearly six-foot tall statue is located in front of the university’s administration building, also named in Millican’s honor. The idea for a statue was initiated by a group of UCF alumni nearly two years ago and the artwork entirely paid for with private donations.

Millican, considered the founding father of UCF, was chosen by the Florida Legislature in 1965 to help plan and build the university. He choose the university's new motto: "Reach for the Stars." Millican left the presidency on Feb. 1, 1978, but remained a faculty member teaching in the College of Business Administration until 1981. He was given the title of president emeritus and he and his wife Frances have remained active supporters of UCF all these years.

Commending Millican’s lifelong belief in the good of others, UCF Board of Trustees Chairman and alumnus Rick Walsh said the statue would be a reminder to future generations of the founding president’s hard work and commitment to making the university a leading institution.

Millican joked that when he first heard of the idea to create a statue with his likeness, he said, “you guys have lost your mind.” But now, he said, “This statue says something very simple to all of us, and it is to ‘reach for the stars.’”
