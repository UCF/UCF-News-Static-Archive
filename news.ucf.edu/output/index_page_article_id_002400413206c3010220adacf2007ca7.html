Robotics Competition Returns to UCF Arena

By Mallery Laing March 9, 2005

Photo: UCF News File Photos

Non-stop action at last year's robot battle.

Robots designed and programmed by about 50 high school teams will face off at the colorful, high-energy FIRST Robotics Florida Regional competition Thursday, March 10, through Saturday, March 12, at the UCF Arena.

Â 

The competition encourages and prepares students to pursue careers in science and technology and teaches them about budgeting and teamwork. Mentors from corporations such as Walt Disney World, Motorola, Harris Corp., Lockheed Martin and Devry University worked with the students for about six weeks before the contest to help design, program and build the robots. The competition, which features several Central Florida teams, is coordinated by UCF and NASA/Kennedy Space Center.

Â 

Controlled by students wearing colorful costumes, the robots will compete in a game called ÂTriple Play,Â in which they will pick up 3-D triangles and place them on top of or in goals in a 27-foot-by-54-foot playing surface. The event, which is free and open to the public, will begin Thursday with practices from 11 a.m. to 6 p.m. and conclude at 3 p.m. Saturday with the final awards ceremony.

Â 

Gov. Jeb Bush plans to welcome the competitors at 9 a.m. Friday, March 11, when matches will be held from 9:20 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. U.S. Rep. Tom Feeney, R-Oviedo, is scheduled to address the students at 9 a.m. Saturday, March 12, when matches are scheduled from 9:20 a.m. to 11:45 a.m. and 1 to 3 p.m.

Â 

Jim Kennedy, director of Kennedy Space Center, will speak at an invitation-only luncheon that begins at 11:45 a.m. Friday at the UCF Arena skybox. Top executives from Lockheed Martin Simulation, Motorola and Progress Energy will join the school superintendents from Seminole and Brevard counties at the luncheon.

Â 

FIRST Robotics participants will come to Orlando from nine states, including California, Texas and New Jersey. More than 20,000 students will participate in 30 regional competitions in the United States. Winners will compete in the national championships at the Georgia Dome in Atlanta in late April.

FIRST (For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology) is a nonprofit, multinational organization created in 1989 to enhance student learning and show the importance of math, science and technology. For more information, go to www.firstfloridaregional.org.
