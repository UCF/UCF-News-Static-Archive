UCF Announces Creation of Hospitality, Optics Colleges at Trustees Meeting

By Tom Evelyn and Chad Binette May 13, 2004

Photo: Jerry Klein

CREOL will need a new sign.

Two University of Central Florida schools nationally renowned for their education and research in hospitality and optics were designated colleges Thursday, May 13, to recognize their growth and success.

UCF Provost Terry Hickey announced at a Board of Trustees meeting that the Rosen School of Hospitality Management has become the Rosen College of Hospitality Management and that the School of Optics is now the College of Optics and Photonics.

At the meeting, the trustees also approved new tuition rates mandated by the Florida Legislature and a 5 percent pay raise for UCF President John Hitt.

The new designation for the colleges recognizes their top rankings in their respective fields and more accurately reflects their functions and structure within the university, Hickey said. Unlike schools, which are embedded in colleges and headed by directors, colleges are free-standing, degree-granting entities headed by deans.

The optics program is the first in the country to become its own college within a university, said Eric Van Stryland, who was promoted from director of the school to dean of the college. The college also houses the Center for Research and Education in Optics and Lasers (CREOL) and the Florida Photonics Center for Excellence (FPCE).

Van Stryland said becoming a college will make it easier for optics faculty to work more closely with professors from other colleges at UCF. He also said the new structure should help with the recruiting of faculty and students.

It will be regarded nationally as a step up, since well be the only one (college of optics) in the country, Van Stryland said. Thats a rather distinctive honor, and its a recognition by our administration of our worth to the university.

Nearly 150 graduate students are enrolled in the college. Under the new structure, undergraduate degrees in optics could be offered within the next several years, Van Stryland said.

The Rosen College of Hospitality Management opened a new, $28.1 million campus in January on Universal Boulevard near International Drive. Students are a short drive away from the second-largest convention center in the United States, some of the worlds best-known theme parks and first-rate hotels and restaurants.

The elevation of our school to a college increases our visibility in the community and the hospitality industry and brings us to the same level as other first-rate colleges such as the Harrah Hotel College at UNLV and the Hilton Hotel College at the University of Houston, Rosen College Dean Abe Pizam said.

The college has about 1,000 students, and the new campus can hold about 2,000. Students study in spacious, high-tech laboratories for food and beverage preparation and use a library stocked with hospitality-related materials and a training dining room that seats 190. Residence halls are planned for 2005.

Board increases tuition, presidents pay

In other business, the trustees approved the universitys proposed 2004-05 operating budget of nearly $743 million -- an increase of 8.6 percent over the current year -- and an increase in tuition and fees that will take effect in the fall.

The state budget approved by the Florida Legislature mandated tuition increases of 7.5 percent for all in-state undergraduate students and 12.5 percent for other students. Total tuition and fees for an in-state undergraduate student taking an average class load will go from $3,013 a year to $3,180.

Legislators also gave universities the option of raising tuition another 2.5 percent for out-of-state and graduate students, which trustees elected not to do on the advice of staff.

The trustees increased the student health fee by 60 cents a credit hour and parking decal fees for students, staff and faculty by 5 percent.

Based on an outstanding performance rating, the board gave President Hitt a 5 percent raise that will increase his salary from $296,400 to $311,220, effective July 1. The raise will be covered by private foundation funds, since state law allows only $225,000 of presidential salaries to be paid out of state funds.

The raise will make Hitt the fourth-highest paid president in the state university system. UCF is the second-largest university in Florida.

The trustees also approved amendments to the campus master plan that were ordered by the governor and Cabinet following a challenge of several items in the plan.

The board honored Central Florida state legislators -- senators Lee Constantine and Daniel Webster and representatives David Mealor and David Simmons -- for their support during this past session and welcomed the newest trustee, Kevin Peters, who replaced Brian Battles as student body president.

The next Board of Trustees meeting is scheduled for July 29. For more information, visit www.ucf.edu/bot .
