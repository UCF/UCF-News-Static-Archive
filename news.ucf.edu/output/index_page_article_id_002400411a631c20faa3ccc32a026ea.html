Spring Semester Begins

By Chad Binette January 5, 2004

Photo: Jerry Klein

With the new semester comes old questions... such as "where is T105 anyway?"

Full Photo

The University of Central Florida opened the spring semester Monday with a projected enrollment of nearly 39,000 and a new school of hospitality management in the heart of the Central Florida tourism corridor.

As of opening day, UCFs preliminary spring enrollment was 38,819, a 3.8 percent increase over last springs final enrollment of 37,390. The 2004 figure included 32,339 undergraduates and 6,480 graduate students. The preliminary data also included 3,066 new students. All these numbers will change throughout the year. For example, as of January 14, 40,242 students were enrolled.

The new Rosen School of Hospitality Management campus opened Monday on Universal Boulevard, near the Orange County Convention Center and some of Central Floridas best theme parks, hotels and restaurants. The $28.1 million campus includes high-tech laboratories for food and beverage preparation, a library, a bookstore and a 400-seat auditorium.

The Rosen School, which had been operating on the main campus, opened its new facilities with a projected enrollment of more than 900 students. Dean Abe Pizam hopes UCF will have 1,000 undergraduate and 100 graduate students by next year.

Spring classes are scheduled to end April 19 and final examinations will follow from April 20 to 26. Commencement ceremonies are scheduled for April 30 and May 1.
