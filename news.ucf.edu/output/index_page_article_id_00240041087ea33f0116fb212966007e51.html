Excitement Building for Liberty Bowl Game

By Chad Binette Dec. 24, 2007

Photo: Jacque Brund

The Marching Knights will travel with UCF's team to the Liberty Bowl in Memphis.

  • Joe Hornstein, UCF Athletics

Get ready, UCF. The Knights are set to play in their highest-profile bowl game ever this week. And the excitement is already building among fans, band members and cheerleaders eagerly awaiting the trip to Memphis.

The Knights will face Mississippi State in the AutoZone Liberty Bowl at 3:30 p.m. Central (4:30 p.m. Eastern) Saturday. Several events leading up to the game will showcase the UCF Marching Knights and cheerleaders and allow alumni, students and fans to show their school spirit while indulging in some of the barbecue food for which Memphis is well known.

The atmosphere will be much more electric than the Knights’ first bowl game two years ago in Hawaii. Because of the cost and distance involved to travel there -- and the fact that the game was on Christmas Eve -- most Knights fans and the Marching Knights were not able to make the trip.

The Knights will have much more support this year when the sold-out Liberty Bowl kicks off. The fans’ excitement about the team may be at its highest point ever, as the Knights played their home games on campus for the first time this season at Bright House Networks Stadium. They won the Conference USA championship on their home field, and star running back Kevin Smith is the nation’s leading rusher.

"I’m super-excited about the trip to Memphis," said Cristina Venturini, who plays the mellophone, which resembles the French horn, for the Marching Knights. "On the band side, everybody is just super pumped to go."

Nearly 300 members of the Marching Knights will travel by bus to Memphis, leaving on Wednesday night. They plan to rehearse at a Memphis high school on Friday morning before performing in the Liberty Bowl Parade at 4 p.m. Central that day. The band also will play at the "Battle of the Bands," which is part of the Liberty Bowl’s pre-game bowl party at noon Saturday in the arena next to the stadium.

The Marching Knights will have a couple of nights free to explore Memphis. And the long road trip surely will help them get to know each other better.

"On the bus, 14 hours next to somebody else creates a lot of bonds," said Venturini, who helps the Marching Knights recruit as a staff member. "We watch movies and bring snacks, and everybody sings or has games to play. You get to hang out with your old friends, and you make new ones at the same time."

Seventeen members of UCF’s national-champion cheerleading squad will make the trip to Memphis along with mascot Knightro, Coach Linda Gooch said. Only one of the cheerleaders, Tyson Walters, was part of the smaller squad that traveled to the Hawaii Bowl in 2005.

"For the band and for the cheerleaders, as well as the players, it really is a reward for a lot of hard work during the year," Gooch said. "We feel like the team has a great opportunity here to win, and we want to do everything we can to help them in that effort."

The cheerleaders will arrive in Memphis on Thursday and take a trip to Graceland before performing with the band during the parade on Friday and the pre-game party and game on Saturday.

The cheerleaders traveled to every Knights road game this season. Tom Mangan, a senior majoring in Accounting, saw four of the six away games with his friends. They drove to all of the other games but will be flying to Memphis.

Mangan said he’s looking forward to the Liberty Bowl’s pre-game party and hopes that he’ll be able to see Kevin Smith -- whom he calls "his hero" -- break the single-season rushing record during the game.

"The bowl game is going to be a great game," he said. "They have a pretty good run defense, and we have a good run offense."

Fans are invited to attend the UCF Alumni Association’s "Knights Bowl Bash" at BB King’s Blues Club in Memphis immediately following the parade on Friday. A post-game party on Saturday will be held at the same location.

More information about the Liberty Bowl and events leading up to the game can be found at www.libertybowl.org or www.ucfalumni.com. For fans staying at home, the game will be televised live on ESPN.

For coverage of the Knights football team, go to www.ucfathletics.com.
