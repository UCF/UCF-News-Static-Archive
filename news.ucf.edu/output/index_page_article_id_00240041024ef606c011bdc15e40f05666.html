UCF's Main Campus to Reopen Wednesday, Aug. 20; All Activities to Resume

By UCF Staff Aug. 19, 2008

Full Photo

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Print this Page

Updated 3:50 p.m.

The University of Central Florida’s main campus will reopen to the public Wednesday morning, Aug. 20. Employees on the main campus should begin reporting to work for their regular shifts as normal, unless they are given other instructions from their supervisors.

UCF’s Rosen College of Hospitality Management, Downtown, South Orlando, South Lake and Leesburg campuses also will reopen Wednesday morning. UCF West Orlando and UCF Osceola will be closed on Wednesday.

The main campus and almost all other UCF campuses were closed to the public Tuesday, Aug. 19, due to Tropical Storm Fay’s potential impact to Central Florida. The storm’s impact in the Orlando area has been minimal.

Decisions on the status of other regional campuses will be announced later at www.ucf.edu, through local media and on the Campus Information Line at 407-823-2000 and Regional Campuses hotline at 407-823-5334. UCF's regional campuses follow the lead of their community college partners when determining whether they will be open or closed.

The UCF Web site will be updated regularly. The Web site and Information Line are the official sources of news for the university.

Wednesday’s orientation session will be held as scheduled. UCF is advising parents and students to monitor the storm and determine when it is safe to travel, as students’ safety is our top priority. Students scheduled for Wednesday’s orientation can attend a session later this week. Students who planned to go to Tuesday’s orientation session, which was canceled, should plan to attend Friday’s orientation. For more information, go to www.orientation.sdes.ucf.edu.

The Stone Temple Pilots concert scheduled for Tuesday at the UCF Arena has been canceled. For updates, go to www.ucfarena.com.

The busiest time of the hurricane season is here. So now is the time to review your plans and make preparations in the event a storm threatens Central Florida. Visit local Web sites such as Orange County and Seminole County for more information about how to prepare yourself, family and friends.
