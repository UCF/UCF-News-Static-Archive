Conservation Biology Doctoral Program Set

By STAFF January 26, 2004

Photo: CAS Photo

UCF has long been involved in conservation biology, such as the on-going study of sea turtles.

Full Photo

Related Contacts and Links:

  •  

  • Print this Page

A new doctoral program will focus on conservation strategies for plants and animals in Florida as development continues to encroach on their habitats.

The state Board of Governors on Thursday gave UCF the go-ahead to start the program, the 24th doctoral program offered by the university. Classes will begin in the fall of 2004.

"The Conservation Biology program is focusing a great deal of effort on actually finding solutions to conservation problems," said David Kuhn, chairman of the Department of Biology. "The program is extremely multidisciplinary, and it has already forged very prominent working relations with some of the economic giants of Central Florida. Plus, its going to focus on urban ecological problems. We will deal with the conservation issues that are facing Central Florida."

Students interested in working in government and industry can pursue the degree with a track in Applied Conservation Biology. Students who want a more traditional research approach can specialize in Ecology and Organismal Biology.

Research specialties of professors who will teach in the program include how different animals are affected by their habitat being divided by roads and how environmental conditions and the availability and quality of food impact the survival and growth of marine mammals.
