UCF to Make Modifications to Bright House Networks Stadium

By By UCF Staff Aug. 1, 2008

Photo: UCF File

The Conference USA champion Knights kick off their college football season Aug. 30 with a home game against South Carolina State.

Full Photo

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Grant J. Heston

    • 407.823.5988

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Florida will make modifications to Bright House Networks Stadium to improve fan comfort and avoid excessive wear on specific areas of the stadium.

The modifications will be made to lessen the “synchronized motion” that some fans found uncomfortable and to extend the longevity of the stadium. Independent third-party reviews by engineering firms have reaffirmed the stadium’s structural integrity and safety.

When the song “Kernkraft 400” by Zombie Nation was played last season, fans produced a synchronized motion that caused the stadium structure to move. Movement in stadiums and other large structures is common -- for example, buildings and bridges are designed with the flexibility to move under high winds.

“These modifications will help ensure that all of our fans have an outstanding experience at our stadium,” said Lee Kernek, associate vice president of administration and finance. “The modification work will not be noticeable to fans but will increase comfort and lessen the wear caused by the synchronized motion.”

Many stadiums around the country have experienced similar issues with this type of synchronized motion, which is different than the usual fan celebrations during a game.

UCF’s contribution to the project is expected to be between $100,000- $130,000. Project partners and donors will contribute the remaining funds, not expected to exceed about $300,000

All UCF contributions toward the stadium work will come from existing stadium project funds. No new money will be used, and the project will have no affect on students or other university areas.

Work is scheduled to begin before the season and continue for several weeks. Game days will not be impacted by the modifications.

The modifications will focus on stiffening support beams in the two end-zone sections of the stadium. To reduce the movement associated with the synchronized motion, additional steel will be bolted to the bottom of flanges of beams and girders in certain locations.

The Conference USA champion Knights kick off their college football season Aug. 30 with a home game against South Carolina State, then a nationally televised home game Sept. 6 against the University of South Florida.
