Pep Rally to Kick Off Championship Week Events

By UCF Athletics Nov. 27, 2007

Photo: Jacque Brund

The UCF Knights will face the Tulsa Golden Hurricane at noon Saturday with the Conference USA championship on the line.

Full Photo

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Joe Hornstein, UCF Athletics

    • 407-823-2729

  • UCF Game Day Web site

  • UCF Athletics

  • Print this Page

With all of the excitement brewing for this Saturday's Conference USA Championship match-up between the UCF Knights and Tulsa Golden Hurricane, our local governments have declared it “C-USA Championship Week.”

The Knights (9-3, 7-1), winners of the East Division, and the Golden Hurricane (9-3, 6-2), winners of the West Division, will play a noon showdown at Bright House Networks Stadium.

The UCF Athletics Association is receiving a lot of help from its friends and partners to show support for Head Coach George O'Leary and his 2007 Knights as they host Tulsa in the third-annual Conference USA Championship this weekend.  The winners will earn the C-USA Championship and a certain berth in the prestigious AutoZone Liberty Bowl against a Southeastern Conference opponent on Dec. 29.

Joining the list of those showing their support for UCF this week are Orange County Mayor Rich Crotty and City of Orlando Mayor Buddy Dyer, who have each declared this week "Conference USA Championship Week." Crotty, Dyer and UCF President John Hitt also declared Friday as "Black and Gold Day."

Great seats to this Saturday's Conference USA Football Championship showdown vs. Tulsa can still be purchased through Ticketmaster by calling (407) 839-3900 or by going online at UCFAthletics.com.  The UCFAA Ticket Office located at Bright House Networks Stadium has also extended its operating hours this week to 8 a.m. to 6 p.m. through Friday. The office will open at 8 a.m. on Saturday.

Below is a list of announcements and events that are part of C-USA Championship Week:

Wednesday, November 28

SGA Pep Rally for the Football Team

12 p.m.; UCF Student Union Atrium

The SGA will host a pep rally to show support for the football team featuring the captains of this year's squad, along with performances from the cheerleaders and members of the Marching Knights.

Applebee's UCF Fan Rally Day

3-7 p.m.; Applebee's on University Boulevard (near the UCF Campus)

Come out to the local Applebee's with other UCF fans and enjoy an evening with 740 AM's Jerry O'Neill and the Shot Doctor, as they do their show live.  Wear UCF gear and receive a discount on your food purchase.  Also, while at the show you can win tickets to this week's big game.

Thursday, November 29

George O'Leary Call-In Show

6-7 p.m.; Buffalo Wild Wings

Along with host Marc Daniels, Head Coach George O'Leary talks about the game on 740 AM The Team.

Friday, November 30

Show your support, it's Black and Gold Day in Orlando.

All Day; UCF campuses, Orange County and the City of Orlando

It's the day before the big game and University of Central Florida President John Hitt, Orange County Mayor Rich Crotty and City of Orlando Mayor Buddy Dyer have declared Friday Black and Gold Day. All UCF alumni, fans and supporters are encouraged to show their support for the Knights as they make their stand for the C-USA title.

Lunch with George

1 p.m.; Recruiting Club Room at Bright House Networks Stadium

Presented by Tropical Ford, Lunch with George is the fans’ best one-on-one chance to talk with the head football coach in addition to joining several UCF fans for lunch.

Saturday, December 1

UCF Alumni Association Pre-Game Party

9-11 a.m.; FAIRWINDS Alumni Center

Breakfast will be served. Tickets are $10 each for UCF Alumni Association members and GKC donors. Members and donors will be permitted one guest at the $10 rate. Tickets are $45 for all additional non-members.

KnightWalk

Approximately 9:30 a.m.; IOA Plaza

The team will be dropped at the corner of East Plaza and Victory Way and will walk to the stadium led by the national-champion UCF cheerleaders and Marching Knights. We encourage fans to line up the entire length of the Knightwalk.

2007 Conference USA Championship Game

12 p.m.; UCF vs. Tulsa at Bright House Networks Stadium

The Knights defeated Tulsa earlier this season, 44-23, in Orlando on Oct. 20.  However, Tulsa overcame the Knights in the inaugural C-USA title game, 44-27, at the Florida Citrus Bowl.  UCF is led by All-America candidate running back Kevin Smith, who impressively leads the nation in both rushing and scoring.  He will go up against Tulsa quarterback Paul Smith, whose electrifying numbers have been blazing defenses all year. Don't forget to wear gold to the game.

Don't miss out on this exciting and historical match-up at Bright House Networks Stadium.  Great seats can be purchased through Ticketmaster by calling (407) 839-3900 or by going online at UCFAthletics.com. 

If you have interest in participating in Conference USA Championship Week or Black and Gold Day this Friday, please let us know how.  E-mail us how you are showing your support for the UCF Knights this week by going online at UCFAthletics.com and submitting through our feedback link: http://ucfathletics.cstv.com/feedback/ucf-feedback.html

For the latest news and information on the Knights, tickets or apparel, log on to www.UCFAthletics.com -- the official site for UCF varsity sports. Also check out www.UCFPhotos.com, the exclusive fan source for UCF action sports photos.
