Europe Specialists See Mixed Future of Opportunities, Challenges

By Jessica Gagnon and Gabriela Othon Nov. 22, 2006

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Gabriela Othon, Global Perspectives

Experts from the United Kingdom, Hungary, Spain, Greece, the United States and other countries gathered at UCF last week as part of the universityÂ’s 2006-2007 focus on Europe.

On issues ranging from conflict and cooperation to democracy and terrorism, speakers evaluated what the future holds for Europe and how that might affect its relations with other parts of the world, including the United States.

The event was hosted by the UCF Global Perspectives Office, in cooperation with the National Conference of Editorial Writers, the Orlando Sentinel, C.T. Hsu and Associates and the Global Connections Foundation.

The next Europe-themed event at UCF will be Jan. 23 and will feature Cherie Booth Blair, the wife of British Prime Minister Tony Blair and a lawyer with the Matrix Chambers firm in London. She will speak on human rights issues, including those in the Middle East.

In addition, Reg Jordan, dean of International Medical Education at the University of Newcastle upon Tyne in England, will come to UCF in late January or early February. UCF will also participate in the Istanbul Conference on Democracy and Global Security in June.

At the conference last week, Gerard Baker, U.S. editor and assistant editor of The Times of London, gave the morning keynote address. Baker noted the vast diversity of Europe and asked audience members to realize that each country is as much an autonomous actor as it is part of a greater whole. Because the European Union disregards the diversity of its member states to the point that it disrupts their economies, the organization ultimately might not survive as a solidly functioning entity, Baker said.
