Three employees honored for enthusiastic attitudes, extraordinary skills

By Mallery Laing Sept. 30, 2004

Photo: Human Resources

Jennifer Coelho

Full Photo

Story Images

Click thumbnails for full-size images.

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Print this Page

Jennifer Coelho expected a fun evening and a nice meal when she attended a summer banquet honoring the 12 USPS employees of the month. She was stunned when she found out she won the Employee of the Year award, along with a check for $2,500 and free parking at UCF for a year.

 

I cannot say thank you enough to my supervisors for nominating me for the recognition of my contributions to both CARD (the Center for Autism and Related Disabilities) and UCF, Coelho said. The appreciation is mutual and I could not ask for a better environment to work in.

 

Coelho was selected as the top employee among the 12 Employee of the Month award winners between July 2003 and June 2004. Since she won the top honor at a banquet hosted by the Human Resources Department, Elvis Wanamaker was named the Employee of the Month for July and Sandra Jaggernauth won that award in August.

 

A UCF employee for more than four years, Coelho serves as executive secretary for the Center for Autism and Related Disabilities. Her supervisor described her in nominating papers as wonderful and as someone who possesses extraordinary computer skills and is an outstanding employee in everyway.

 

Coelho, who won the Employee of the Month award in August 2003, said she will spend the award money to improve her first home or to take a trip with her husband on their first wedding anniversary.

 

The selection committee for the Employee of the Year award is comprised of USPS and Administrative and Professional employees and faculty members.

 

Jaggernauth, the August 2004 employee of the month, has worked at UCF for seven years. She started in the Student Health Center and now works as executive secretary in the deans office at the College of Health and Public Affairs. She works with faculty, staff and students and provides administrative support for the assistant dean for Undergraduate Studies.

 

In Jaggernauths nomination, her supervisor praised her for a tremendous growth in workload in her job and added that Sandra has done her all with the utmost patience, enthusiasm and can-do attitude. She is dependable, loyal and a consummate team player.

 

Receiving this award was a surprise, she said. I am honored to have received such recognition by the university and my peers.

 

 

Wanamaker, the July winner, is used to dealing with hurricanes because he was born and raised in Tampa.

 

Wanamaker worked at the University of South Florida in Tampa for ten years. He said the damage UCF had during Hurricane Charley was worse than anything USF experienced while he worked there.

 

Wanamaker began working at UCF in 1994 in the Registrars Office. After two years, he moved to Transfer Services and then First Year Transitions. He now works directly for Assistant Vice President Mark Allen Poisel and helps oversee new student orientations.

 

In his nomination papers, Wanamakers supervisor said his work is always superb and done efficiently, and it is a pleasure to work with him.

 

Outside of work, Wanamaker enjoys playing for a city softball  team.
