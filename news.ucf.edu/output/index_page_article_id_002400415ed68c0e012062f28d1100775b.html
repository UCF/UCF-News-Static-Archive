UCF Students Awarded Funding, Mentoring to Start Their Own Business

By Christine Dellert Updated April 10, 2009

Winners received $12,000 and a year's worth of mentoring in the UCF Technology Incubator.

It’s getting harder to find a job during this recession. But try starting your own business, from scratch, while you’re still in school.

On Thursday, April 9, four student teams from the University of Central Florida competed for the chance to become successful entrepreneurs. This year’s winner -- a student-led home health care consulting and installation service -- received $12,000 and a year’s worth of professional mentoring to help launch the company.

Known as “The Joust,” this annual challenge is hosted by UCF’s Center for Entrepreneurship & Innovation, in the College of Business Administration. The business plan competition was held in the Live Oak Room on UCF’s main campus.

The winning company, Helping Hands Healthcare, is the brainchild of four UCF business students: Mary Huizenga, Tabia Steele, Jennifer Jernigan and Vinny Tursi. It’s described as a service-based firm committed to providing superior consulting and installation of daily living aids to families with in-home healthcare needs.

During the competition, the four teams pitched their ideas to a panel of judges from the business community. Panel members, all of whom are UCF graduates, included Rick Walsh, president of the Knobhill Group and chair of the UCF Board of Trustees; Charlotte Williams, the owner of several real estate businesses; Thomas G. Bland Jr., president and CEO of AquaFiber Technologies; and David Brim, last year’s winner of “The Joust.”

The students’ proposals were judged on several factors, including the quality of the service or product, financial feasibility and presentation skills. A total of $25,000 in prizes was awarded, and Helping Hands Healthcare also received free rent for a year at the UCF Technology Incubator in the Central Florida Research Park.

The other finalists, in order of placement, were:

Mercury Mobile Software -- A firm currently developing an application for smart phones. By providing all vital information on the home screen, the firm’s application improves the efficiency and productivity of users.

Orlando Search Engine Marketing -- The search engine optimization service provider helps companies procure top placements from search engines to gain long-term traffic. The firm also targets niche markets that are underserved online to produce what is effectively an incubator for online business.

Ski Florida -- America’s first indoor snow ski, snowboard and kids’ snow play facility. It would feature everything from bunny slopes to steeper runs with jumps and rails.

The Center for Entrepreneurship & Innovation (CEI) is an initiative from the College of Business Administration that serves as a hub for academic entrepreneurship across UCF’s colleges and programs. CEI offers education and research resources to those who create value with new business ventures. To learn more, visit http://www.cei.ucf.edu.

UCF Stands For Opportunity: The University of Central Florida is a metropolitan research university that ranks as the 5th largest in the nation with more than 50,000 students. UCF's first classes were offered in 1968. The university offers impressive academic and research environments that power the region's economic development. UCF's culture of opportunity is driven by our diversity, Orlando environment, history of entrepreneurship and our youth, relevance and energy. For more information visit http://news.ucf.edu.
