H.O.P.E. for Haiti: UCF Keeps Up Volunteer Projects, Support

By Christine Dellert Updated Feb. 5, 2010

Photo: James Crawford/UCF Engineers Without Borders

UCF students remembered earthquake victims and vowed to help survivors recover during a recent candlelight vigil.

The UCF community continues organizing relief efforts for victims of the Jan. 12 earthquake in Haiti, with opportunities for students and faculty and staff members to assist in the recovery.

More than 120 student volunteers sorted much-needed clothing, baby formula and medical supplies last weekend for earthquake victims in Haiti.

Saturday’s event was the conclusion of a two-week, universitywide donation drive, organized by Volunteer UCF and supported by many other student groups and faculty and staff members at the University of Central Florida.

It was just one of the many efforts with which UCF has been involved since the 7.0 magnitude struck Port-au-Prince, Haiti, last month and caused the deaths of an estimated 200,000 people.

Next week, a new task force established by President John Hitt will meet for the second time to focus on how UCF’s skills, services and resources can best support Haiti in the years it will take to recover and rebuild the country.

Task Force H.O.P.E. (Healing, Outreach, Partnership and Education) is made up of students, faculty and staff members and administrators, many with personal ties, experience or expertise regarding Haiti.

“The recovery and rebuilding efforts will continue for years, and our university community is committed to providing the enduring assistance that the Haitian people desperately need,” said task force chair Al Harms, vice president for Strategy, Marketing, Communications and Admissions.  



The task force will coordinate recovery and support projects on campus and beyond in collaboration with outside assistance organizations.

Many in the UCF community already have given time, donations and money to the thousands in need.
On Saturday, the student volunteers delivered the three busloads of donations items to Harvest Time International, a nonprofit organization in Sanford that has been shipping supplies to Haiti.

“It was amazing,” said André Smolinsky, Harvest Time’s chief operating officer. “They did work within two hours that we couldn’t do in a week.”

The event was so successful that organizers say they’ll be holding another volunteer day at Harvest Time later this month. See Volunteer UCF’s Web site for upcoming details.

“A lot of members of the UCF community have been directly affected by the crisis,” said Volunteer UCF Student Director Jessica Schwendeman. “All of us working together can make a bigger impact.”

Help ‘on the ground’

Several students and faculty members have offered aid to the thousands of people arriving in Central Florida from Haiti.

Psychology professors Deborah Beidel and Jeff Cassisi served as Disaster Mental Health Workers for the Central Florida American Red Cross. Working out of Orlando Sanford International Airport, they assisted passengers who were flown out of Haiti by the U.S. military.

Both professors also coordinated a donation drive for the Red Cross, providing baby supplies to mothers and children who arrived at the airport.

Several Creole-speaking students from the School of Social Work responded to requests from Catholic Charities of Central Florida for translators at the airport in Sanford.

To learn more about similar volunteer opportunities, visit the Web sites for Catholic Charities of Central Florida and Central Florida American Red Cross.

Employee donations

Starting today, UCF faculty and staff members will have the option to deduct from their paychecks donations to three outside Haiti relief funds -- the American Red Cross, Clinton Bush Haiti Fund and the Harris Rosen Foundation Haiti Relief and Sustainability Efforts.

To sign up for employee payroll deductions, click here.

UCF also encourages students, faculty, staff and alumni to consider other donation drives or relief efforts, but cautions the community to fully research each organization before pledging money as the potential for fund drive scams has already been reported.

‘Heal Haiti’ concert

On Thursday, Feb. 4, a group of UCF alumni and students helped throw a relief concert for Haiti in downtown Orlando, with all cover charges and T-shirt sales benefiting three charities involved in earthquake recovery efforts -- Doctors Without Borders, Mercy Corps and Yéle Haiti.

The Heal Haiti Relief Show was organized by Monster Nature, a local marketing company run by six UCF alumni and students.

Click here for more information.

Want to help?

Task Force H.O.P.E. will soon launch a Web site to update UCF and the Central Florida communities on its initiatives and solicit feedback from students and faculty and staff members on future projects.

Send suggestions on how UCF can help Haiti and affected communities to cdellert@mail.ucf.edu.
