Prescribed Burn Successful; No Additional Burns This Week

By UCF Staff Updated Feb. 26, 2009

Photo: Courtesy of UCF Environmental Initiative

The burn on Wednesday covered much of area 12, near the UCF Softball Complex.

Full Photo

The University of Central Florida conducted a successful prescribed burn Wednesday, Feb. 25, on about 15 acres on the campus' northeastern corner, just east of the UCF Softball Complex. No additional burns will be conducted this week.

Prescribed burns, which remove dead, decaying or low-lying plants on the forest floor, are a management technique to maintain healthy ecosystems and to prevent unplanned fires from endangering nearby areas. Wednesday's burn will help to protect the homes of UCF's neighbors who live near the eastern edge of the campus.

The state Division of Forestry helped UCF Environmental Initiative staff conduct the burn.

UCF’s burn team includes 13 staff and students, and Wednesday's burn was the sixth led by the UCF team. One of the first-UCF led burns, in 2005, helped the university win a Campus Ecology Recognition award from the National Wildlife Federation. The award is given to universities committed to restoring wildlife habitat and slowing global warming.

For more information on this week’s burns and UCF’s prescribed fire program, visit http://green.ucf.edu/landManagement/burns.php.
