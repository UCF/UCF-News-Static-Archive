Three UCF Students Win Scholarships Named in Honor of UCF First Ladies

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala Sept. 21, 2007

Photo: Jacque Brund

Scholarship winner Peggy Hightower, UCF first lady Martha Hitt, Bloomingdale's representative Jamie Habanek and scholarship winner Lisa M. Roman at the UCF Women's Club luncheon.

Three University of Central Florida students earned $1,500 scholarships Sept. 19 during the UCF Womens Club annual luncheon.

One of the winners returned to school to pursue a nursing degree after seeing how well a hospice nurse took care of her mom when she was dying of cancer. Another winner has been a single mother of two for the past 18 years, and the third began college in the early 1980s but withdrew for medical reasons.

At the luncheon, club president Janice Fisher also announced a new graduate student scholarship that will be given out next year. That scholarship is in honor of first lady Martha Hitt, who was on hand to celebrate the accomplishments of this years winners.

Each of the three scholarships awarded this week is in honor of a former or current UCF first lady, including Frances Millican, Beryl Colbourn and Hitt.

Jennifer Santiago, of Merritt Island, negotiated contracts for the Navy and Air Force before she put her career on hold to have her son. Then her life took another turn. Her mother was dying of cancer, and Santiago took care of her during the last few months of her life. A hospice nurse visited them daily to guide them. Santiago decided she wanted to become a hospice nurse and give other families the comfort that she received. Santiago is pursuing a nursing degree at UCF.

Lisa M. Roman, of Orlando, began college in the early 1980s, but she withdrew for medical reasons. Since then she has worked and attended school part-time. She is seeking a degree in business and hopes to become an attorney who opens her own mortgage and real estate title company.

Peggy Hightower, of Chuluota, has been a single mother of two for the past 18 years. Her daughter is studying at Seminole Community College, and her son is in the Navy and will deploy to Iraq by the end of the year.  She is pursuing a masters degree in social work at UCF and hopes to eventually counsel women, children and families.

The UCF Womens Club is a group of volunteers that aims to advance the ideals and goals of the university; provide scholarships to full-time, non-traditional students; and welcome new employees to the university community.

The club was established by Frances Millican in 1969 and held its first scholarship fundraising event the following year. Millican attended the event on Wednesday.

The three existing scholarships are now endowed, and a Bloomingdales representative announced that the company was donating $1,500 toward the new graduate scholarship.

Ambassador Harriet Elam-Thomas, who is creating a new diplomacy program at UCF, spoke at the luncheon. Elam-Thomas served as a senior Foreign Service officer with the U.S. Department of State until fall 2005, and she is a former U.S. ambassador to Senegal.
