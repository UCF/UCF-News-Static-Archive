Trustees Approve Two New Regional Campus Degrees

By Tom Evelyn May 26, 2005

Photo: Jerry Klein

The University of Central Florida Board of Trustees on Thursday approved creating two degree programs in business administration and applied sciences that will be offered beginning this fall at the universitys regional campuses.

At its regular meeting the board also approved a 5 percent increase in tuition for all students and voted to spend $500,000 of private funds to continue preparing a business plan for the proposed on-campus football stadium.

The new Bachelor of Arts in Business Administration and Bachelor of Applied Science are designed to give full-time workers with associates degrees who cannot travel to the Orlando campus foundations in business and management needed for career advancement.

The goal here was to serve a population of students that was not being served, Provost Terry Hickey told the board.

The business administration degree will be offered at UCF at Cocoa, Daytona Beach and Palm Bay. The applied science degree will be offered with a supervision and administration track at UCF at Daytona Beach and Cocoa and with a track in industrial operations at Cocoa.

The Bachelor of Applied Science degree is available to all Florida community college associate of science graduates looking for career advancement, new careers or personal growth, said Dave Harrison, vice provost for Regional Campuses. It is designed for students working in technical, public services, health or other fields who need convenience while working toward their bachelors degree. The new degree will allow those holding A.S. degrees to grow in their careers and differentiate themselves in the marketplace.

The 5 percent increase in tuition and fees approved by the board Thursday was included in the state budget approved by the Legislature this past session. Under the increase, tuition and fees for an in-state undergraduate student taking a full load of 30 credit hours a year, for example, will go up from $3,180 to $3,339.

The board also approved increasing the parking decal fee for students and employees by 5 percent to help pay for parking services, the shuttle system and parking garages. The transportation access fee, charged to students at the main campus and Rosen College of Hospitality Management, will increase by 44 cents to $4.34 a credit hour. All increases will take effect this fall.

Athletics Director Steve Orsini and Vice President for Development Bob Holmes updated the board on planning and fund-raising for the proposed on-campus stadium. A thorough examination by UCF staff of feasibility studies conducted by consultants appears to support cost estimates and the findings that an on-campus stadium would pay for itself through ticket sales and sponsorships, Orsini said.

The board approved spending up to $500,000 of privately raised money to continue developing a business plan, which Orsini said he hoped to present to the board before the end of June. Trustees also approved spending up to $250,000 in private funds to build new practice fields that may be displaced if the stadium is approved.

In April, the board approved the concept of the proposed 45,000-seat stadium and accepted an anonymous gift of $1 million to pursue feasibility studies. A formal vote on the stadium could be scheduled in the next several weeks.

In other action, the board:

-- Approved a $795.6 million 2005-06 operating budget for the university.

-- Approved a supplemental educational plant survey to include the Biomedical Science Center building, which has been recommended for funding in 2005-06 with Public Education Capital Outlay, or PECO, funds.

-- Approved tenure for nearly three dozen faculty members and tenure with hire for James Rippe, who will direct UCFs new Center for Lifestyle Medicine.

Also on Thursday, the board recognized and thanked area lawmakers, several of whom attended and made remarks, for their help in securing funding for the university during this past legislative session.

Faculty Senate Chairman Manoj Chopra and Student Government Association President Willie Bentley joined the board as new members. Faculty representative Arlen Chase and former student President Kevin Peters have completed their terms.

The next board meeting is scheduled for July 28 at the Student Union. Go to www.bot.ucf.edu for more information.
