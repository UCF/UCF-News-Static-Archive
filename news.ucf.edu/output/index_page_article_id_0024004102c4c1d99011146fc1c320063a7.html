Brandie Hollinger Becomes First Woman to Lead UCF Student Government

By Kristi Bartlett May 10, 2007

Photo: UCF Marketing

The first female Student Government Association president in UCF history, Brandie Hollinger said she believes her campaign empowered many women on campus.

Hollinger, who grew up in Jacksonville and now lives in Orlando, began her one-year term as student body president on Tuesday, May 8. She will oversee a budget of $13.6 million and represent UCFs 47,000 students as a member of the Board of Trustees.

I consider it a great honor, and I hope that it convinces other women to run, Hollinger said, adding that many women whom she met during the campaign were surprised to learn that UCF had never had a female student body president.

Hollinger, who graduated this month with a bachelors degree in Nursing, has been active in student government since her freshman year at UCF. She served as the youngest member of the cabinet when she was a sophomore. Seeing the tangible results of good government while she served as director of student involvement and campus life fueled her passion for helping others and motivated her to run for president.

The same desire to help others led Hollinger to choose pediatric nursing as her major.

I love kids, and I think that working in the pediatric intensive care unit will allow me to have a positive impact on the kids and serve as a role model, she said.

After gaining hands-on experience in nursing, Hollinger wants to pursue a Ph.D. in Nursing. She eventually wants to teach nursing at a university so she can share her knowledge with students.

Nancy Marshall, assistant chief of staff for President John Hitt, worked with Hollinger when she was a student assistant for the Board of Trustees and a member of the Presidents Leadership Council.

Brandie is mature and focused; she is a grounded person who gets the big picture, Marshall said.

Hollingers platform focused on campus safety and school spirit. She said students should be able to focus on their studies and feel secure that they are in a safe environment.

My parents got the same feeling that I did when we visited UCF; they also felt that it was a safe campus, and I want to keep it that way, she said.

Logan Berkowitz, the new SGA vice president, met Hollinger through student government, and they decided to run together because they shared a similar vision for UCF. He also said he believed that Hollinger was the right person for the position.

She commands respect and treats everyone as an equal, Berkowitz said. People will see her passion for the university and will work hard to meet the goals that we have planned.

Hollinger would like to create a student identification card that includes emergency numbers on the back so that students have the information readily at hand. Hollinger also said she hopes to increase the number of escorts in the Safety Escort Patrol Services program, known as SEPS.

In addition to her emphasis on security, Hollinger wants to generate more school spirit and serve as an advocate for the students.

I want to create an open environment in student government, she said. There are 33 cabinet members available to speak with students; it is important that the student body knows that they are accessible and approachable.
