UCF Technologies Included in Statewide Research Conference

By Barb Abney May 19, 2006

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Barb Abney

    • 407-823-5139

  • Florida Research Consortium

  • Print this Page

UCF experts in nanoscience, biomolecular science and technology transfer will participate in a statewide conference featuring cutting-edge technologies being developed at universities across the state.

The third annual Florida Technology Transfer conference being held in Sarasota is focusing this year on the areas of pharmaceuticals and biopharmaceuticals, engineering and materials sciences, medical devices and other biotechnologies and information and communications.

Walter H. Moos, vice president and head of SRI Internationals Biosciences Division, will deliver a keynote presentation and update on the state of the biotechnology and pharmaceutical industries at the event. Silicon Valley-based SRI International (www.sri.com) is one of the world's leading independent research and technology development organizations.

We are extremely pleased to have Dr. Moos present at the third annual Florida Tech Transfer Conference, said David Day, 2006 Florida Tech Transfer Conference chairman and director of technology transfer at the University of Florida. His perspectives on and success with moving research from university labs to the marketplace are sure to enrich our program and excite the interest of our attendees.

The conference, which starts at 8 a.m. Tuesday, May 23, is being held in conjunction with the 9th PHARLEX® pharma license exchange at the Ritz Carlton Sarasota. PHARLEX® attendees will be welcomed at the Florida Tech Transfer Conference to facilitate business opportunities between conference delegates and PHARLEX®'s international biotechnology and pharmaceutical company executive members.

James Hickman, director of UCFs Nanotechnology Science Center; James E. Pearson, director of research and administration for The College of Optics & Photonics and interim director of the Advanced Materials Processing and Analysis Center; and Tom ONeal, associate vice president for research and executive director of the UCF Technology Incubator, are among those who will be leading or moderating panel discussions on specific technologies.  Mark Muller, a professor of molecular biology and microbiology and chief scientific officer, TopoGEN Inc., will be available to answer questions about UCFs biomedical science-related research.

The 2006 Florida Tech Transfer Conference is a collaboration among Florida A & M University, Florida Atlantic University, Florida International University, Florida State University, Nova Southeastern University, the University of Central Florida, the University of Miami, the University of Florida, the University of North Florida, the University of South Florida and the University of West Florida.

The conference is targeting industry executives and entrepreneurs, university researchers, tech transfer, commercialization and licensing specialists, investors, attorneys and economic development professionals.

The Florida High Tech Corridor Council, Florida Trend Magazine, Research Florida, and Senniger Powers are major sponsors of the Florida Tech Transfer Conference.

For more information or to register for the conference, visit www.floridaresearch.org.
