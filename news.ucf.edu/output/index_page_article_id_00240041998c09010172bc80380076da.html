Three UCF Theatre Students Invited to National Kennedy Center Festival

By Chad Binette Feb. 14, 2005

Related Contacts and Links:

Three UCF Conservatory Theatre students won top honors at a regional theater festival in Palm Beach County and received all-expenses-paid trips to the national Kennedy Center American College Theatre festival in April in Washington, D.C.

Desmond Newson, Edward Davis II and Diana Calderazzo will attend the national festival at the John F. Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts from April 18 to 24. Newson, a senior from Jacksonville, also received the Marvin Sims Award for Acting and the Musical Theatre Acting Award. Davis, a senior from Delray Beach, received an honorable mention for the Best Partner Award.

Calderazzo, a graduate student from Melbourne, was selected as a national finalist in the Region IV Student Dramaturgy Competition. Dramaturgy involves playwriting and producing.

Tramaine Berryhill, a senior from Miami, received the Marvin Sims Award in Design and won first place in the David Weiss Regional Scenic Design Competition.Â  He may become the fourth UCF student invited to attend the national festival at the Kennedy Center.

Other UCF participants included Regina Fernandez, a junior from Boca Raton, and Matt Depasquale, a sophomore from Dunedin, who competed together in the semifinals of the Irene Ryan Acting Competition. Sarah Yates, a graduate student from Orlando, received an honorable mention in the David Weiss Regional Scenic Design Competition.

The UCF students competed at the Kennedy Center American College Theatre Festival from Feb. 3 to 5 on the campus of Palm Beach Community College in Lake Worth.

Started in 1969 by Roger L. Stevens, the Kennedy Center American College Theatre Festival is a national theater program involving 18,000 students from colleges and universities. It has served as a catalyst for improving the quality of college theater in the United States. The Irene Ryan competition is funded through an endowment from the estate of actress Irene Ryan, who played the role of Granny on the Beverly Hillbillies.
