Journalism Instructor Receives National Advising Award

By Christine Dellert Sept. 25, 2007

Photo: Courtesy of Rick Brunson

Rick Brunson, a journalism instructor at UCF, was named the outstanding student-chapter adviser in the nation by the Society of Professional Journalists.

Full Photo

Journalism instructor and professional copy editor Rick Brunson will be honored in Washington, D.C., next month for his work with the University of Central Floridas chapter of the Society of Professional Journalists.

Brunson was recently named the outstanding student-chapter adviser nationwide by the Society of Professional Journalists, the countrys most broad-based journalism organization dedicated to promoting the free practice of journalism and high ethical standards.

A UCF alumnus, Brunson is a full-time journalism instructor and part-time copy editor at the Orlando Sentinel. He has served as UCFs SPJ adviser since the group reorganized in August 2004. During the past three years, he has worked with the student organization to host the Nicholson School of Communications largest multimedia fair, which attracted more than 30 news organizations around the state and has helped more than 100 students land jobs or internships. He has also led many professional development and resume-building workshops for student journalists.

I spend my days riding on the coattails of my students, Brunson said. Their hard work and dedication make me look good. My colleagues in the profession, especially at the Orlando Sentinel, have always come to campus when called to generously share their experience and expertise with our students. There should be a lot more names on that award besides mine.

Brunson will be bestowed the David L. Eshelman Outstanding Campus Adviser Award during a luncheon at the 2007 SPJ Convention & National Journalism Conference in Washington, D.C.
 
From the beginning, Rick has guided the chapter as a whole and each individual member to levels of energy and excellence no one could ever have imagined, UCF journalism Assistant Professor Rick Kenney wrote in his nomination letter. He gets his reward out of knowing hes helping young, aspiring journalists  many of them the future of SPJ and no doubt even the future leadership of SPJ  realize their potential and possibilities.

For the 2007 academic year, Brunson received the Student Adviser of the Year Award from UCFs Knights of the Round Table, an advisory council that honors members in UCF organizations.

To learn more about UCFs SPJ chapter, visit http://spj.org/ucf.
