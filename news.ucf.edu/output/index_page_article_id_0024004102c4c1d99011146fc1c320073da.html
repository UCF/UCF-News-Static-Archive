Several University of Central Florida students with outstanding academics and community service received Founders Day awards from their colleges on Wednesday.

College of Arts and Humanities

Founders Award: Kelly Ames Hould, a senior from Neptune Beach with a double major in Theatre and English. She has received a National Council of Teachers of English achievement award in writing. She served as stage manager for the Project Spotlight theater shows for emerging performers. She has volunteered during the summer with Jacksonville Marine Charities and with Girls and Gaming, which strives to get more girls interested in math and science.

Founders Scholar Awards: Jennifer R. Anderson, Kathryn H. Arrington, Ashley L. Boroski, Brittany L. Camp, Thomas Gillan, David L. Givens, Christine M. Hanavan, Kristina Kopic, Jeffrey E. Parks, Mary L. Pratile, Paul G. Sepulveda, Nathan V. Seta, Amy E. Shaw, Daniel S. VanderBaan, Nicholas A. Walsh and Julie A. Zimmerman.

Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences

Founders Award: Cassandra Korsvik, a third-year double major in Music and Molecular and Microbiology from Richmond, Texas. She plans on earning her Ph. D. in Microbiology and working in medical research. Korsvik volunteers as a Disney Sports Enthusiast and has worked as a docent for the Houston Museum of Natural Science. She also is president of the American Society of Microbiology Student Branch and works on campus as a research assistant in Professor William Self's lab.

Founders Scholars Awards: Bianca R. Badaro, William B. Cutting, Justin J. McNamee, Johnny Nguyen, Evan W. Pederson, and Ahmed J. Qureshi.

College of Business Administration

Founders Award: Ana Livia Suarez, originally from Cuba, is pursuing a double major in Marketing and Advertising/Public Relations. Most recently, she was selected as one of 11 graduating seniors at UCF to receive the prestigious Order of Pegasus honor. Suarez activities include memberships in Golden Key International Honor Society, the Entrepreneur Club, the Society of Marketing Professionals and Quotes, a public relations club. She has completed several internships with local corporations, including Lockheed Martin, NIH Publishing and Marriott Vacation Club International. Suarez also has volunteered with Easter Seals, the Boys and Girls Club, Habitat for Humanity and Give Kids the World. She plans to pursue her MBA and work in the nonprofit sector as a marketing professional.

Gilardi, Jr., Cameron G. Gomes, Erica L. Hardee, Wendy M. Harrington, Hina Z. Hussain, Lindsey E. Johns, Scott L. Kirkman, Emily Kuhn, Michelle L. Moore, Atikur Motiwala, Mau V. Nguyen, Michelle A. Phillips, Barbara D. Plevy, Jennifer L. Resh, Maria L. Sick, Jeffrey D. Slanker, Francisco D. Soriano, John D. Spano, Michael J. Stewart, Stephanie A. Thieman, Kali L. Titus, Brittany L. Walbert, Dale W. West, Jr., Laurie T. Yonge and Peggie P. Yu.

College of Education

Founders Award: Reshma Ramkellawan, a senior majoring in English and Language Arts Education. She is from Queens, New York and is the first in her family to attend college. Ramkellawan works as a substitute teacher in Osceola County, and she has worked with Junior Achievement of Osceola County for two years. She volunteers with the Summerbridge Breakthrough Collaborative, which helps students from low-income families reach their potential. She wrote her Honors in the Major thesis on Indian and Caribbean authors who write for young adults, and she is presenting her thesis in Switzerland and California this year. She has been accepted to graduate education programs at Columbia University and New York University, but she first will spend a year teaching disadvantaged children in Osceola County.

Founders Scholars Awards: Ashley L. Abernathy, Melissa J. Burkhard, Lorrie A. Chappell, Angela Colbert, Meghan K. Fallis, Cristina M. Garcia del Busto, Emilee George, Brittany N. Kester, Laura C. McGinty, Shalay K. Nash, Tania M. Rich, Lisa A. Schurr, Jessica H. Schwartz and Madi E. Thomas.

College of Engineering and Computer Science

Founders Award: Brian Becker, a senior from Vero Beach, is a Burnett Honors College student who has been pursuing research in artificial intelligence and robotics under the guidance of Professor Avelino Gonzalez. He volunteers in the UCF robotics lab. He received a scholarship from the Astronaut Scholarship Foundation, which honors students whose talent foreshadows a career leading to advancement of scientific knowledge and technology.

Founders Scholars Awards: Chris D. Adams, Jason R. Beck, Brian C. Becker, William J. Ceely, Ivan K. Chi, Ryab C. Clapp, Matthew B. Davis, Joshua J. Emmerling, Katherine A. Luetzow, David P. Marchetti, Denitsa M. Milanova, Paul J. Nelson, Mark C. Olson, Eric M. OSullivan, Volodymyr Pryyma, Hernan Ramirez, Lenora K. Scholl, and Krzysztof Zaleski.

College of Health and Public Affairs

Founders Award: Chaya Stark is a senior majoring in Communication Sciences and Disorders. She plans to attend graduate school in the fall and work as a speech language pathologist. Stark creates literacy materials for children with complex communication needs as part of her volunteer work with the Florida Alliance for Assistive Services and Technology (FAAST). She also has volunteered with the Down Syndrome Association and UCF Center for Autism and Related Disabilities (CARD), where she helped to provide child care while parents attending training sessions and meetings. Stark has been involved in several community service efforts with Give Kids the World, the UCF LEAD Scholars and the Coalition for the Homeless.

Rosen College of Hospitality Management

Founders Award: Christina Smith is a junior majoring in Hospitality Management with an emphasis on Lodging Management. A native of central Maine, she graduated from Olympia High School in 2003.  After graduation, she plans to earn a management internship with one of the major hotel chains and continue her education at the Rosen College. She is treasurer of the UCF Equestrian Club and competes in Intercollegiate Horse Show Association events. Smith has worked with Angels on Hooves, a program offering physically and mentally challenged children and adults the opportunity to ride and interact with horses.

Founders Scholars Awards: Marian E. Garcia, Amanda DGerow, Natalie M. Kraus Schlichtmann, Katrina N. Leavitt, Wendy V. Rosales, Jason D. Rowitt, Danielle R. Thomas, and Michelle Y. Williams.

College of Sciences

Founders Award: Michelle Lee Josey is a Forensic Science major on the Biochemistry track with minors in Anthropology, Chemistry and Microbiology/Molecular Biology. The Spring Hill native is in her fourth year at UCF and plans to graduate this summer. She plans on obtaining her Ph.D in Biochemistry from the University of California-Berkeley and continuing forensic research as she works towards establishing herself as a respected forensic biochemist. Josey is part of a research team led by chemistry professor Kevin Belfield that developed a new Two-Photon 3-D Optical Data Storage technology allowing users to record and store massive amounts of data onto a single DVD. She has volunteered with UCFs Honors Educational Reach Out (HERO) program and Alternative Spring Break.

Office of the Vice Provost

Founders Award: Ashley Conyers, of Altamonte Springs, is a third-year Liberal Studies major with a minor in Interpersonal Communication. Her other specialties are Interpersonal Communication and Behavioral and Social Sciences. After graduation, Conyers plans to remain at UCF to earn her masters degree in Educational Leadership and work at a college or university in student development. She has volunteered with Junior Achievement in Orange and Seminole counties and worked with Read2Succeed, the Second Harvest Food Bank and Give Kids the World. She is a member of the Presidents Leadership Council, LEAD Scholars and the UCF Orientation Team.
