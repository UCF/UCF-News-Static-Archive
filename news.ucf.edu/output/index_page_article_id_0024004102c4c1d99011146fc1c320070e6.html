UCF Students, Public Can Drive Electric Car Monday at Focus the Nation Event

By Kristi Bartlett April 12, 2007

Photo: Courtesy of Focus the Nation

"Who Killed the Electric Car?” chronicles the creation of the electric car and its demise while examining the cultural and economic effects of the vehicle.

University of Central Florida students and the public can test drive an electric car and watch the documentary film Who Killed the Electric Car?  Monday, April 16, at the first UCF Focus the Nation event.

Focus the Nation is a national effort to coordinate faculty and students at more than 1,000 colleges, universities and K-12 schools to engage in a nationwide, interdisciplinary discussion about Global Warming Solutions for America.

Students and the public will be able to drive or ride in a Toyota RAV4 electric vehicle from 4 to 6 p.m. in the G-2 parking lot, which is between Centauras Drive and the West Parking Garage on the East Orlando campus. A free public screening of the documentary Who Killed the Electric Car? will follow at 6 p.m. in Room 101 of the Communications Building.

Who Killed the Electric Car? chronicles the creation of the electric car and its demise while examining the cultural and economic effects of the vehicle. Chris Paine, director of the documentary, and Chelsea Sexton, executive director of Plug In America, will participate in a discussion with the audience after the screening.

The Monday events are sponsored by the Florida Electric Car Association and the Orlando Utilities Commission.

Focus the Nation is based in educational institutions but also is engaging Americans in their churches, mosques, synagogues, businesses and civic organizations. The intent is to focus the growing concern in the country about global warming and to create a sustained, national discussion about clean energy solutions. Focus the Nation will culminate with symposia that will be held Jan. 31 at campuses across the nation.

The UCF branch was formed by the Department of Sociology and the College of Sciences, and Sociology Professor Penelope Canan said it will serve as a model for how to start Focus the Nation initiatives on other campuses.

Focus the Nation is one of many efforts to raise environmental and energy awareness at UCF. In February, President John Hitt signed on with colleagues to join the American College & University Presidents Climate Commitment leadership circle. UCF has also adopted the U.S. Green Building Councils Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design standards for buildings that are new or undergoing renovations and started using biodiesel fuel for all diesel-powered university vehicles.

For more information on Focus the Nation, visit http://focusthenation.cos.ucf.edu.
