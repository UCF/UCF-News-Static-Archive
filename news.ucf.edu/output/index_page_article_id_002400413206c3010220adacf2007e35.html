Innovative Video Game Program Accepting Applications

By Justin Campfield March 2, 2005

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Justin Campfield

    • 407-423-8006

  • Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy

  • Print this Page

A comprehensive new program that promises to prepare the next generation of artists and technologists for the video game development industry opened its doors to applicants Tuesday at the University of Central Florida.

 

The Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy, known as FIEA, is accepting applications from students vying to be in the academys inaugural class, which begins this fall. Interested students may apply for admission by visiting http://fiea.ucf.edu/.

 

FIEAs interactive entertainment program introduces an innovative approach to preparing students for a video game development career.  By meticulously emulating the real environment of video game development studios, FIEA aims to produce graduates that are highly sought-after by employers looking to hire for video game development jobs such as texture artists, character modelers, technical directors, software engineers and production managers.

 

FIEA has designed every aspect of the academy, from its work space and curriculum to its student projects and technology, to reflect real-life production conditions, said Mike Moshell, interim executive director of FIEA. The academy believes that students are better prepared for video game development careers when they are comfortable and familiar with the environment in which games are made, not just the technology and skill sets that create them.  FIEA has taken this approach from day one because it is the best way to make sure that graduates of the program are well-equipped to enter the ever-evolving environment of video game development.

 

FIEA, which is supported by the School of Film and Digital Media, will pursue an immersive experience for its students by placing an emphasis on projects that are professional in scope as well as expectations. Comprising a majority of the learning experience, these projects will utilize the academy facultys real-world experiences to teach students current industry techniques and technology.

 

Strategically located in downtown Orlando, FIEAs graduate certificate in entertainment technology is an intensive, 16-month program consisting of four 15-week terms.  New groups of students will begin their studies each term, the first of which begins this fall.  Each new class will have approximately 30 to 50 students.  Areas of academic specialization include 3-D modeling, animation and motion, technical artistry, software development, and production management. Students are required to have undergraduate degrees in fields such as computer science, art or digital media before enrolling.  Rare exceptions will be made for those with vast industry experience.

 

Further differentiating FIEA from other institutions are its relationships with industry leaders such as Electronic Arts Orlando, Fla., -based studio, EA  Tiburon.  EA  Tiburon was a vocal proponent of the Academys creation, helping to develop its curriculum and collaborating with students and faculty.  Additionally, as much as half of the Academys faculty will be comprised of EA  Tiburon employees, further enhancing course offerings relevancy to industry practices.  EA  Tiburon develops many of the worlds top-selling video games such as Madden NFL Football, NASCAR® Racing, NFL Street and NCAA® Football.

 

The academy got its start last May, when the Florida Legislature provided $3.2 million in start-up costs and $1 million annually in operation funds.  The states funding allows FIEA to focus on excellent students who finished their undergraduate careers in or near the top 10 percent of their classes, have high GRE scores and impressive portfolios.

 

The states funding will allow FIEA to be more selective in admitting students because its admission policies wont be driven by profit motives, said Moshell.  This may leave out some students who are seeking a career in video game development, but FIEA is looking for world-class students to complement its world-class instructors, facility and curriculum.

 

FIEAs opening comes at a time when the role of video games in the entertainment industry continues to grow.  According to leading marketing information provider, The NPD Group, the $10 billion dollar U.S. video game industry generates more revenues than the movie industry and many analysts predict it will surpass music industry revenues by 2006.
