Florida Sports, Political Leaders Addressed UCF's 2,900 Graduates

By Christine Dellert Aug. 2, 2008

Photo: Jacque Brund

More than 2,900 students were expected to graduate during today's commencement ceremonies.

Two leaders in Florida sports and politics spoke during today’s commencement ceremonies at the University of Central Florida.

Alex Martins, the Orlando Magic’s chief operating officer, and Daniel Webster, state Senate majority leader, addressed the audience at the two ceremonies, where nearly 2,900 UCF students expected to graduate.

Martins, an alumnus of UCF’s MBA program, also helped to establish UCF’s nationally recognized DeVos Sport Business Management Program, a master’s program that focuses on ethics, diversity, leadership and community service.

“As sports has taught us, with goals, hard work and perseverance, anything is possible,” Martins told graduates at UCF’s 125th commencement ceremony, which included the College of Arts and Humanities, College of Business Administration, College of Nursing, College of Optics and Photonics, Rosen College of Hospitality Management and College of Engineering and Computer Science.

Martins also challenged the graduates to strive for their dreams with an “outstanding positive attitude” and help from “teammates.” As someone who aspired long and hard to reach his goal of working in professional basketball, he joked about his first sports job -- “scooping Italian ice at Giants Stadium.”

“You can control your entire life through your attitude,” Martins said. “You are the coach of a team called ‘you.’”

After 28 years in the Florida Legislature, UCF’s other guest speak, Webster, will be retiring this year. He has been a strong supporter of UCF’s College of Medicine and Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy. He also played a major role in funding the Partnership I and II buildings in the Central Florida Research Park, which have helped UCF become a national leader in simulation.

Webster called on the graduates from the College of Education, College of Health and Public Affairs, College of Medicine and College of Sciences to learn the value of time, reading and serving others.

“Life is not about us,” he said. “It’s about serving other people.”

Webster also encouraged graduates to continue learning and remember that their diploma is “more than words on a piece of paper,” but a “picture of success.”

“Graduating is a great achievement,” Webster added. “This ceremony is an end and a beginning…You’re commencing today on a life that holds lots of promise.”

This was the third commencement ceremony in the new UCF Arena since it opened its doors in September 2007.  The anticipated graduates included 2,290 who filed intents to receive bachelor’s degrees, 511 for master’s degrees, five for education specialist degrees and 84 for doctoral degrees.

The ceremonies can be viewed online beginning Aug. 11 at http://webcast.oir.ucf.edu.
