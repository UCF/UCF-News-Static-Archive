Concerts to Feature Orchid Ensemble, UCF Opera, UCF Symphony Orchestra

By UCF Staff Oct. 25, 2006

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Orchid Ensemble: Jim Meckley

    • 407-823-5973

  • Opera: Thomas Potter

    • 407-823-4680

  • Orchestra: Laszlo Marosi

    • 407-823-5002

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Florida will host performances by the Orchid Ensemble, the UCF Opera and the UCF Symphony Orchestra during the next few weeks. Details of the concerts follow:

Orchid Ensemble

The Orchid Ensemble, which blends ancient musical traditions and instruments from China and beyond, will perform at 3 p.m. Sunday, Nov. 5, at the UCF Music Rehearsal Hall on the main campus. The ensemble, which is based in Canada, has performed at venues throughout North America, including the John F. Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts in Washington, D.C., and the Canadian National Gallery in Ottawa.

The concert is free to UCF students, faculty and staff; general admission is $10. Go to www.orchidensemble.com for more information about the ensemble. For more information on the concert, call 407-823-2869.

UCF Opera

The UCF Opera will perform Saturday, Nov. 4, at a concert entitled Its a Golden Knight for Singing: Scenes and Arias in Concert. The performance will begin at 7 p.m. at the UCF Music Rehearsal Hall on the main campus. The concert will feature selections from Mozart and Strauss. Admission is $5 for the general public and free for UCF students, staff and faculty.

For more information, go to www.music.ucf.edu/opera or call 407-823-2869.

UCF Symphony Orchestra

The UCF Symphony Orchestra, directed by Laszlo Marosi, will perform three concerts entitled Music of the Great Polish Composers Nov. 17 to 19. The program will feature Ayako Yonetani, a professor of violin and viola, and Laurent Boukobza, the UCF chair for piano studies. Music composed by Frederic Chopin, Karol Szymanowski and Stanislaw Moniuszko will be performed.

The concerts will be at 8 p.m. Friday, Nov. 17, at the Visual Arts Building auditorium on the UCF main campus; at 7 p.m. Saturday, Nov. 18, at St. Luke Lutheran Church, 2021 W. State Road 426, Oviedo; and at 3 p.m. Sunday, Nov. 19, at Hawthorne Clubhouse on U.S. 27 south of Leesburg.

All of the concerts are free and open to the public. For more information, call 407-823-2869.

For information on other UCF Music Department events, go to www.music.ucf.edu.
