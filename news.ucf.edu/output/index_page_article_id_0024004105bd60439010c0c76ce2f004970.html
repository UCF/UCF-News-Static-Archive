Student Entrepreneurs Win Inaugural Business Plan Event Hosted by UCF, Rollins

By UCF Staff Nov. 2, 2006

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Cameron Ford

    • 407-823-3683

  • UCF College of Business Administration

  • Print this Page

Student entrepreneurs Ryan Tseng and Alexis Nogueras won the inaugural Florida Collegiate Business Plan Competition hosted by UCF and Rollins College last weekend.

The two-day competition at the Gaylord Palms Resort in Orlando featured 12 teams from nine Florida colleges and universities. The teams delivered written business plans and gave live presentations to a panel of judges interested in start-up business investments. Nearly 100 business, government and academic leaders attended the competition.

Conceived as a joint effort among Floridas leading academic entrepreneurship centers, the competition was sponsored by the Florida High Tech Corridor Council, Enterprise Florida and the Florida Venture Forum. The grand prize was an opportunity to present to Florida Venture Forum investors. 

It was great to see academic, government and business leaders from across the state working together to support the ambitions of these great students, said Associate Professor Cameron Ford, founder of UCFs Center for Entrepreneurship and Innovation. Events like this can help connect and focus Floridas dispersed universities and colleges to foster new companies, well-paying jobs and a bright economic future. Were all looking forward to doing this again next year.

Presentations were delivered in two categories -- investment intensive businesses that will require multimillion dollar venture capital investments and businesses that could be launched using revenue, personal funds, loans, or small outside investments.

Tseng, the winner of the investment intensive track, is a recent graduate of the University of Florida. His company, PowerPad, features a patent-pending, wireless power transmission system designed to power consumer electronic devices. Alexis Nogueras, a recent graduate from Florida International University, is CEO and founder of G-Force Tools. G-Force Tools core product is a line of ergonomic garden tools featuring a lighter and more ergonomic shaft, providing better comfort with a unique appearance. His entry won in the limited investment category. 

Pat Schneider, director of the Florida Venture Forum, invited Ryan to present PowerPad during the forums annual conference in Boca Raton in February.

This was leadership training at its best, not just for student participants, for everyone involved in the event, said Gary May, an executive judge from Hopewell Land Partners.
