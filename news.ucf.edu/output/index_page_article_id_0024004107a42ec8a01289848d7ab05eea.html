In Latest Story About UCF, New York Times Features Med School

By Grant J. Heston Sept. 9, 2010

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Print this Page

Share this story:

  • Share on Facebook
  • Save This Page
  • Seed Newsvine

The New York Times has featured UCF and our experts eight times since May, most recently this week in a feature about the College of Medicine and the Lake Nona medical city. Other Times coverage of UCF has included breakthroughs in archaeology research in Belize and high-tech anti-cheating efforts.

Please see below for a review of selected UCF print, broadcast and online media coverage. Follow UCF news on Twitter and Facebook, watch UCF TV and learn more about Strategy, Marketing, Communications & Admissions.

NATIONAL

The New York Times: Orlando’s Newest Attraction Is Medical
Just off State Road 417, a five-minute drive east from Orlando International Airport, a 650-acre parcel of land is fast becoming a $2 billion medical campus, including a medical school, research laboratories and hospitals.

CNN: Beyond Theme Parks in Central Florida
Everyone thinks the only thing in Orlando is theme parks and tourists. Though Disney World is the biggest employer, Central Florida is home to large defense contractors, the University of Central Florida (with its new medical school) and emerging nanotechnology and modeling industries.

USA Today: Lance Armstrong's Next Battle May Be in Court of Opinion
What began as a summer of hope for Lance Armstrong could end on a different note.
-- This story quotes Richard Lapchick, director of UCF's Institute for Diversity and Ethics in Sport.

The Chronicle of Higher Education: Reality and Sustainability -- a Philosopher's View
Two articles in The Chronicle use the word "reality" to refer to an economic situation that is being used to trump environmental and public-health concerns surrounding actions of entrenched industries, "conventional" agriculture, and natural-gas drilling by hydraulic fracturing.
-- This is a letter to the editor from Philosophy professor Ronnie Hawkins.

Inside Higher Ed: Any Given Weekday
Midweek college football games have become commonplace in recent years, but like so many aspects of college sports, the scheduling of games separates the haves from the have-nots.
-- This quotes Consuelo Stebbins, assistant vice president for internationalization, and Joe Hornstein, associate athletics director for public relations and communications.

STATE/LOCAL

Orlando Sentinel: UCF is Cyber Leader for Online Courses
As students descend on the University of Central Florida's main campus this week for a new academic year, a growing form of traffic is set to rival the school's notorious morning rush to find a parking space. Online traffic is burgeoning as UCF students take more courses by computer.

Gainesville Sun: UF Falls Further Behind State’s Biggest University
The University of Florida lost its title last year as the biggest university in the state. It looks like the enrollment gap with the University of Central Florida will get even bigger this year.

Orlando Sentinel: Med School Builds Reputation for Research
This summer, administrators and faculty members at UCF's College of Medicine celebrated a milestone, one quite separate from moving into the college's new building at Lake Nona.

Orlando Sentinel: Once Laid Off, UCF's Oldest Band Member Marches On
Don Cupo had been a laid-off financial analyst when he decided to go back to college. While at the University of Central Florida orientation in July, Cupo noticed a booth for the Marching Knights.

Orlando Business Journal: Engineering, Computer Science Ranked High
The University of Central Florida’s College of Engineering and Computer Science is one of the best 100 in the world, according to the Academic Ranking of World Universities.

Orlando Sentinel: UCF Tries Novel Approach for Stroke-Rehabilitation Patients
Betty Jo Starks is reading a sentence designed to trip her up. "You must remember to eat sunscreen when you go outside to work in the garden," she says, reading the sentence aloud. Then the 83-year-old retired teacher looks up and wrinkles her brow. "That doesn't make sense," she says.

The Florida Times Union: Can Rick Scott Win Over the Florida GOP?
Spending $50 million of his own money to win the Republican nomination for governor might have been the easy part for Rick Scott.
-- This story quotes Political Science professor Aubrey Jewett.

Orlando Sentinel: Is Our Weather Getting Wacky?
A study by University of Central Florida researchers of decades of weather data and the flowering dates of 70 native and non-native plants found "significant" temperature increases in more than a fourth of the state's counties in the summer and more than a third of them in the fall.
