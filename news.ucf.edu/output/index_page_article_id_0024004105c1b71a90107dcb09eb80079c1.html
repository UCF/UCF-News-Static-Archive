UCF Trustees to Review Financing, Consider Nonprofit Corporation for Stadium

By Tom Evelyn Updated Dec. 14, 2005

Photo: UCF

Rendering of the planned on-campus football stadium.

The University of Central Florida Board of Trustees will hold a workshop and regular meeting today, Dec. 14, to discuss funding for the planned on-campus football stadium and to consider creating a nonprofit corporation to finance the project.

The workshop will begin at 1 p.m. in the Cape Florida Ballroom in the Student Union on the East Orlando campus. The meeting will follow at 3:30 p.m.

The board last month approved construction of the stadium, pending final approval of financing, and amended the campus master plan to include it.Â Today, the board will consider creating the Golden Knights Corporation, a direct-support organization that would finance the stadium on behalf of UCF.

The stadium, expected to cost $50 million to $60 million, would be built and supported by ticket and concessions sales, donations, corporate sponsorships and advertising. No public funds or tuition would be used.

Construction of the planned 45,000-seat stadium could begin within the next few months and be completed before the 2007 football season. It would be located just east of the UCF Arena in the north intercollegiate athletics complex.

UCF has played at the Florida Citrus Bowl stadium near downtown Orlando since the football teamÂs inaugural season in 1979. Earlier this month, the Golden Knights hosted the inaugural Conference USA Championship at the Citrus Bowl. Nearly 52,000 fans attended.

Also today, the board is expected to clarify and finalize a deferred compensation retirement plan for UCF President John Hitt that it approved in July.

Visit www.bot.ucf.edu for an agenda and more information on the Board of Trustees.
