Progress Energy Grant Helps UCF Create Leadership Institute for Educators

By UCF Staff May 10, 2004

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Gail Klein, Leadership Insititute director,

    • 407-823-1358

  • Tom Evelyn, News and Information,

    • 407-823-5988

  • Print this Page

The Progress Energy Foundation has donated $1 million to help UCF provide leadership training for Florida educators through the new Progress Energy-UCF Leadership Institute.

The Leadership Institute is designed to strengthen the leadership roles of school administrators in their communities and to productively focus on the core mission of schools: the improvement of student performance.

Participants in the Leadership Institute, which is supported by faculty from the UCF College of Education and Academy for Teaching, Learning and Leadership, are exposed to the best practices in education and the private sector during the two-year program. Topics include sound business practices such as responsive customer service and effective employee recruitment and retention. The two-day sessions also address data-driven decision making and the importance of a quality organizational culture.

"Our grant is an investment in the future of Florida schools and the region we serve," said Bill Habermeyer, president and CEO of Progress Energy Florida. "We are excited to work and partner with UCF, and to provide school administrators with lessons learned by private sector leaders in areas like customer service and employee training."

Habermeyer joined UCF President John Hitt, College of Education Dean Sandra Robinson, Seminole County Schools Superintendent Bill Vogel and others to announce the grant Monday, May 10, at the UCF Teaching Academy on the main Orlando campus.

Participants in the Leadership Institute are selected based on leadership track records, potential to make leadership contributions in their communities and a commitment to Florida students. The initial classes of the Leadership Institute in February and April included 134 participants from school systems in Brevard, Citrus, Flagler, Lake, Marion, Orange, Osceola, Pasco, Pinellas, Polk, Seminole, Sumter and Volusia counties. Each participating school district selected a team including the superintendent, central office administrators and school principals.

In addition to the grant, the Progress Energy-UCF Leadership Institute will utilize company facilities such as the state-of-the-art Customer Service Center and the Leadership Development Center in Seminole and Pinellas counties. Progress Energy's internal training staff will also assist the Leadership Institute in delivering its curriculum.

"Progress Energy's gift is a wonderful example of what can happen when business partners with a university," said Hitt. "Their corporate generosity will help UCF provide a forum for Florida educators to work with business leaders to learn best practices. We will provide school leaders with the tools they need to face the critical educational challenges in our state. They, in turn, can use those tools to build stronger schools and students in Florida."

Along with classroom training, the Leadership Institute allows the participants to learn from their peers in other Florida counties and to share their passion for learning and innovation across county lines.

"Florida schools deserve world-class training and leadership," said Habermeyer. "Providing this training will help develop greatness in not only these educators, but the teachers, students and communities they touch for decades to come."
