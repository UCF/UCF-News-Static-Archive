Patricia Cooke Wins May Employee of the Month Honors

By Kristi Bartlett June 6, 2006

Photo: Courtesy of UCF Human Resources

Patricia Cooke is the May Employee of the Month winner.

Full Photo

Related Contacts and Links:

Patricia Cooke, the administrative assistant in University Audit, is the May UCF Employee of the Month.

Cooke serves as the system administrator for the University Audits computer network and also handles all of the travel arrangements and distribution of audit reports. Her co-workers say that she is a delight to supervise because she takes initiative, needs little direction, has great organizational skills and is very dedicated.

The employees of the month for the last six months were Linda Anderson, Latrecia Rice, Denise Devlin, Ron Knappenberger, Sherri Pilkington and Glema Butler respectively.

Anderson, a program assistant at the University Testing Center and the April Employee of the Month, coordinates national and state standardized exams and also is instrumental in running the annual Supervisor/Proctor Training Workshop.

Andersons co-workers state that she continuously improves her contributions to the department by seeking new ways to enhance testing practices. She exemplifies how individuals can consistently contribute to the development of an organization.

Rice, the Faculty Senate administrative assistant who won top employee honors in March, coordinates all aspects of Senate activities. She is described by co-workers as the one continuing thread between faculty chairs and vice chairs. The faculty Senate is a one person operation and it is critical for the person to be independent and self-sufficient. Latrecia certainly exhibits both of these qualities.

Devlin, the instructional facilities scheduler in the Registrars Office and the February winner, is highly involved in developing the schedule for the entire university.

She is an exceptional, conscientious worker who exercises outstanding judgment, her co-workers wrote. Denise is a great team player who responds to the needs of others.

Knappenberger works as a senior engineer in the Office of Instructional Resources, where he supervises engineers and attends to multimedia classrooms. He won the Employee of the Month award for January.

His audio/video expertise is invaluable for the growing multimedia needs of UCF, his co-workers wrote. Although he is not able to leap over buildings in a single bounce, he just requires a running start.

Pilkington, the office manager for Student Disability Services and the December winner, supervises the student assistants and helps UCF students with disabilities.

Co-workers state that she is an employee who always performs above expectation, making excellence her daily commitment. Sherri is of inestimable value, who, one person at a time, is making UCF a better place to study, learn and work.

Butler is a program assistant for the Psychology Department, where some of her duties include assisting with the set up of hours for undergraduate teams. She won the November Employee of the Month award.

Her co-workers wrote that the students adore her, and we appreciate all of her over and above work for the department. She is a tremendous asset to both the department and UCF.

Each UCF employee of the month receives a $25 gift certificate from Barnes and Noble, a certificate and letter of recognition, UCF Athletics apparel and a goody bag from Health Services. Winners are also recognized at the Annual Employee Awards dinner, on the UCF marquee and at Board of Trustees meetings.
