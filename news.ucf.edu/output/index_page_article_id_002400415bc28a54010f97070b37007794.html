Contemporary Music Festival to Feature Music of Czech Composer Karel Husa

By Kristi Bartlett Jan. 25, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Laszlo Marosi, UCF Symphony Orchestra

    • 407-823-5002

  • Chad Binette, News & Information

    • 407-823-6312

  • UCF Music Department

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Florida Symphony Orchestra will hold a Contemporary Music Festival from Feb. 8 to 11 featuring the music of Karel Husa, a Pulitzer Prize winning Czech composer.

A performance at 7 p.m. Saturday, Feb. 10, at St. Lukes Lutheran Church, 2021 W. State Road 426, Oviedo, will be preceded by a 6 p.m. Masterworks classical presentation sponsored by WMFE-FM.

The Feb. 10 orchestra program, led by Conductor Laszlo Marosi, includes performances by soprano Priscilla Bagley and pianist Laurent Boukobza, both of whom are UCF music faculty members. Concert highlights include selections from Husa, Antonin Dvorak, and Friedrich Smetana.

Husa will join Marosi and Boukobza in the Masterworks discussion of his career and the creativity required to compose music. The performance and Masterworks discussion are free and open to the public.

The festival opens on Thursday, Feb. 8, with a UCF Faculty Chamber Music concert at 8 p.m. at Rehearsal Hall on UCFs East Orlando campus.

The UCF Wind Ensemble and University Chorus will perform in the Visual Arts Building on Friday, Feb. 9, at 8 p.m. The festival concludes with a UCF Symphony Orchestra concert at 4 p.m. Sunday, Feb. 11, at First Presbyterian Church, 106 E. Church St., Orlando.

Husa received a Pulitzer Prize in Music in 1969 for his String Quartet No. 3. Many of his works have become a part of the modern repertory, including Music for Prague 1968 and Apotheosis of this Earth. His Concerto for Cello and Orchestra earned him the 1993 Grawemeyer Award. In 1995, Husa received the State Medal of Merit, First Class, the highest civilian recognition in the Czech Republic.

All of the concerts in the Contemporary Music Festival are free and open to the public.

Clarinet Day

The 17th-annual UCF Clarinet Day will be Saturday, Feb. 10, at Rehearsal Hall with Argentinean clarinetist and Director of the Conservatory Cordoba Oscar Gieco. Events run from 1 to 5:30 p.m., and registration is $20 and begins at 12:30 p.m. Events include performances, store displays, clinics and a clarinet choir reading session.

For more information on UCF Music Department events, visit www.music.ucf.edu.
