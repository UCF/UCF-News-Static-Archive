UCF Commencement Ceremonies Under Way

By Chad Binette April 26, 2005

Photo: Jacque Brund

Students from the College of Health and Public Affairs and the Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences graduated Thursday morning during the first of six spring commencement ceremonies.

U.S. Sen. Mel Martinez and Florida Secretary of State Glenda Hood will address University of Central Florida graduates on Friday, May 6, the second of three days of ceremonies to be held at the UCF Arena.

Martinez will speak at 2 p.m. to graduates of the College of Arts and Sciences and Rosen College of Hospitality Management. At 10 a.m., Hood will address another group of College of Arts and Sciences graduates, including one of UCFs most well-known students, former Miss America Ericka Dunlap.

More than 4,800 students have declared their intent to graduate during six ceremonies this week. The expected graduates include 3,874 who have signed up to receive bachelors degrees, 877 for masters degrees and 76 for doctoral degrees. In addition, 19 students are scheduled to receive education specialist degrees.

The first commencement ceremony, which was at 10 a.m. Thursday, May 5, featured Harriet Elam-Thomas, a retired U.S. ambassador to Senegal who now serves as a diplomat-in-residence at UCF. Elam-Thomas, whose four decades of international experience include work as a Foreign Service Personnel officer in Washington and as a U.S. cultural attaché in Athens, Greece, was the keynote speaker, and she also received an honorary doctoral degree in public service. The morning ceremony was for College of Health and Public Affairs and Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences graduates.

Lynda Keever, publisher of the business magazine Florida Trend, addressed graduates of the College of Business Administration on Thursday afternoon.

On Saturday, May 7, U.S. Rep. Ric Keller, R-Orlando, will speak to graduates of the College of Education at 10 a.m. and retired Navy Admiral Stanley Arthur, president of Lockheed Martins Orlando-based Missiles and Fire Control division, will address graduates of the College of Engineering and Computer Science and the College of Optics and Photonics at 2 p.m.

Guests must have tickets to attend the ceremonies. Guests without tickets can watch live broadcasts of each ceremony via closed-circuit television in Room 121 of the Classroom I building or online at http://webcast.oir.ucf.edu.

 

The webcasts draw viewers from around the world. At one point during the Thursday morning ceremony, viewers from Brazil, Egypt, India, Morocco and several other countries were online watching the ceremony.

 

After the spring commencement ceremonies, the total number of degrees awarded by UCF since classes began in 1968 will increase to about 148,700.
