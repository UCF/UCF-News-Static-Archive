Rosen College Hosts Distinguished Chefs Series

By Jackie Toops Dec. 17, 2008

Photo: Etta Jean Juge

The guests enjoyed decadent fare in the elegant atmospheres conceptualized by the class. Botella was the final restaurant organized by the students.

Rosen College of Hospitality Management students got a taste of what it takes to run a restaurant during five dinners at the campus’ Disney Dining Room.

As part of the Intermediate Food Preparation course taught this semester by Professor and Certified Executive Chef Vivian Ray, teams of juniors and seniors gained real-world experience by creating and managing their own restaurants. For each dinner, the teams created the restaurant name, theme, menu, décor and appropriate budget.  Two students managed each dinner, one from the dining area and the second from the kitchen.

About 11 students majoring in hospitality, event or restaurant and food management hosted the dinners for groups of 40 to groups of 150.

“This sort of class isn’t about grades,” said Donald Evans, a graduate assistant with the class. “It’s about the experience and what you take away from it.”

The guests enjoyed decadent fare in the elegant atmospheres conceptualized by the class.  The themed dinners included The Citrus -- Sept. 18, Crystal Garden -- Oct. 16, Otono -- Nov. 6 and Bellissimo Ristorante! -- Nov. 20.  The final event, Botella, was held on Dec. 2 and wrapped up the semester on a festive note.  The evening featured a five-course meal, live entertainment, holiday décor and special guest Sean Woods, executive chef from the Ritz Carlton Orlando, Grande-Lakes.

Woods, who has more than 16 years of experience with The Ritz-Carlton Hotel Company, assisted the students with tastings, recipes, preparation and more. 

“It was a pleasure to come over, have fun and give them a bit of knowledge from my experience to help them have the best event they could have,” he said. 

The students are raving about their experiences as well. Otono manager Adrienne Morales said the class has been “the highlight of my Rosen career. It allowed me to combine my own passion and creativity for cooking with some of my real-world experiences to develop a restaurant, which is my ultimate goal after graduation.” 

Molly McAllister, manager for Bellissimo Ristorante!, added, “This experience is priceless. The class has taught me hands-on skills that will carry over into my future career … This class has also enhanced my desire to be a leader. I thoroughly enjoy the responsibility and freedom of creativity that this class has provided me."

The price of each meal ranged from $15 to $25.  Each event had a waiting list as a result of positive word-of -mouth advertising, and the final event sold out before the students had begun to promote it.

“Guests are already asking about the spring schedule,” said Jackie Toops, the college’s assistant director of public relations. “The spring semester dinner schedule is coming soon and will be posted on our new Web site in January. The public is encouraged to reserve early, as seats are limited and they book very quickly. Don’t miss this delicious opportunity!”

To learn more about the Distinguished Chefs Series at the Rosen College, please visit www.hospitality.ucf.edu/dcs. 

About UCF Rosen College of Hospitality Management: Located in the heart of the tourism and hospitality industry, Orlando, the Rosen College of Hospitality Management is dedicated to the values of professionalism, leadership and service.  The mission of the Rosen College of Hospitality Management is to develop future generations of global hospitality and tourism leaders representing all industry segments in the hospitality capital of the world, through innovative academic programs, cutting edge research and strong industry and community partnerships.

UCF Stands For Opportunity: The University of Central Florida is a metropolitan research university that ranks as the 6th largest in the nation with more than 50,000 students. UCF's first classes were offered in 1968. The university offers impressive academic and research environments that power the region's economic development. UCF's culture of opportunity is driven by our diversity, Orlando environment, history of entrepreneurship and our youth, relevance and energy. For more information, visit http://news.ucf.edu.
