"Infinite Possibilities" Art at Rosen

By Susan Loden February 25, 2004

Photo: Susan Loden

Carla Poindexter, playful yet profound images.

Playful, yet profound abstract figures drawn on a grand scale from the imagination of Orlando artist Carla Poindexter greet all who enter the new Rosen School of Hospitality Management Darden Auditorium.

PoindexterÂ’s acrylic masterwork on seven canvas panels adorns the 36-by-40-foot convex wall inside the main entrance to the theater. It is the signature art piece of the $28.1 million University of Central Florida Rosen School of Hospitality Management, which opened Jan. 5 on Universal Boulevard.

The mural was entitled "Infinite Possibilities" by UCF trustee and hotelier Harris Rosen, who donated more than $19 million to build the new campus and to provide scholarships to UCF students.

Unhampered by the barriers of language or culture, its quirky, colorful characters fill the 10-by-28-foot visual carnival.

"Infinite Possibilities" is populated by PoindexterÂ’s trademark forms, which she calls characters. They might be exotic flora and fauna oddities or Rube Goldberg-type devices related only slightly to elements in anyoneÂ’s reality other than the artistÂ’s.

"This piece is going to outlive me, no doubt," says Poindexter, who after 20 years as a professional artist joined the UCF Art Department faculty to teach advanced painting five years ago. "It communicated to me as I worked and took on a life of its own. When I started developing the piece it was very improvisational, as is all of my work. I wanted to move from a very dense interior space on the far left into the more open middle area the showing mechanics of the life we are living and to even outer space. I was thinking about Orlando to the far right, beneath a rich tropical environment where things slowly give way."

Poindexter, who has a masterÂ’s degree in fine art from Texas Tech University, adds, "I donÂ’t want the meaning to be pinned down. I see things and they are the impetus for the work. But, hopefully the viewers will conjure their own meaning and will be inspired to see things in a different way."

Just as she saw something new each time she approached the project, beginning in September 2003, Poindexter says each exposure to "Infinite Possibilities" should bring new discoveries and new interpretations by viewers.

Poindexter says the $30,000 piece, commissioned by the Florida Art in State Buildings Program, communicates without language. "That is the value of art," she says. "I wanted it to be whimsical, yet dramatically dark and brooding in places. Mysterious. I was inspired by music, as well as by how these images stand in for human interaction. My work is always about metaphor and in response to the time we are in and my place in that time."

In the summer of 2003, Poindexter became the first artist-in-residence at the Orlando Museum of Art, where the public was invited to watch her work and contributed their own drawing to a couple of pieces. Her work has been seen in solo exhibitions at the UCF Art Gallery, Miami University in Oxford, Ohio, and at other universities. She has been in more than 50 curated or juried exhibitions nationwide and was an artist-in-residence at the world-class Atlantic Center for the Arts in New Smyrna Beach. She and UCF Art Gallery Director Kevin Haran will be the only two artists in the "Tralfamadore and Trilobites" exhibition throughout April in the Edinburgh College of Art main gallery, in Scotland.

"The committee was very concerned about extending hospitality to the community," he says. "CarlaÂ’s mural is a delightful, warm invitation welcoming visitors and students to the new campus."

"Carla usually works so small, and for her to take on something on that large scale with every ounce of her being is commendable," says Theo Lotz, director of UCFÂ’s Art in State Buildings Program. Since 1979, the program has commissioned or bought art as a budgeted item for each new building on campus.
