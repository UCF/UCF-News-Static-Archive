UCF Aims to Raise Awareness, Prevention of Unwelcome Sexual Conduct

By Chad Binette Jan. 24, 2006

Responding to a national survey regarding the pervasiveness of unwelcome sexual advances and conduct on college campuses, the University of Central Florida next month will begin surveying its students to determine how to best increase awareness and prevention programs.

The American Association of University Women on Tuesday announced the creation of new initiatives at UCF and 10 other campuses while releasing the results of Drawing the Line: Sexual Harassment on Campus, an online survey of more than 2,000 college students around the country.

In the AAUW survey, 62 percent of undergraduates said they have encountered some type of unwelcome sexual conduct. Thirty-five percent of female students and 29 percent of male students indicated that they experienced physical harassment, such as being touched, grabbed or pinched in a sexual way.

The survey showed that unwelcome sexual conduct especially affected female students: 68 percent of female students who experienced it felt very or somewhat upset right after it. Thirty-five percent of male students admitted to being very or somewhat upset by unwelcome sexual conduct that they experienced.

At UCF on Tuesday, students and leaders of the Womens Research Center and Victim Services office gathered outside the Student Union for brief presentations on the survey results and to hand out literature about how to prevent and cope with unwelcome sexual conduct and harassment.

Many UCF students will begin taking a Web-based survey next month, and those results likely will be presented in May. The survey will be given to undergraduates in classes with more than 100 students and to some students on the Daytona Beach and Cocoa campuses.

We hope to increase awareness of what sexual harassment and abuse are, and we hope this will be the beginning of creating a campus climate that is more conducive for people coming forward if they feel like theyve been sexually harassed or abused, said Leslie Sue Lieberman, an anthropology professor who is director of the Womens Research Center at UCF.

Led by the Womens Research Center, the Office of Diversity Initiatives and Victim Services, UCF will examine the results of the local survey and evaluate what changes should be made to improve awareness of unwelcome sexual conduct and harassment and better help students who experience it. Possible changes could relate to how the topic is addressed at orientation sessions or how students report instances of harassment.

The UCF survey also could help the university better focus efforts on groups that might be identified as more likely to experience unwelcome sexual conduct or less likely to report it.

This will allow us to compare how many students were working with and how many students there are who we believe need our services, said Christine Mouton, director of Victim Services. Then we can evaluate what some of the barriers might be to students accessing those services.

UCFs five victim advocates attend classes and student organization meetings to give presentations on sexual harassment and abuse and other topics. UCF students, faculty and staff can call Victim Services to get confidential help from trained advocates about any crime.

The university's Equal Opportunity and Affirmative Action Programs office is designated to receive reports of sexual harassment. Students, faculty and staff may discuss situations and request investigations under federal and state discrimination statutes

The AAUW awarded UCF a $5,000 grant to conduct the survey on campus. Ten other universities, including Indiana, Penn State and Louisiana State, are participating in the initiative and received grants from the AAUW to help their students, faculty and staff understand the scope of sexual harassment, raise awareness of the issue and implement projects to change the campus climate.

The American Association of University Women, with its nationwide network of more than 100,000 members, 1,300 branches and 550 college and university partners, has been a leading advocate for equity and education for women and girls since 1881. Local AAUW branch member Joan Rinaldi is assisting with the UCF grant.

More information on Drawing the Line: Sexual Harassment on Campus, including a copy of the report, can be viewed at www.aauw.org/dtl.

The Womens Research Center strives to enhance UCFs prominence in research relevant to women and to strengthen the research efforts of female faculty members.
