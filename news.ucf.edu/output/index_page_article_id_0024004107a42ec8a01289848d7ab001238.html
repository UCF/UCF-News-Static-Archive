New Accounting School Director Named

By UCF Staff July 12, 2010

Sean Robb joined UCF in 2005 and has published more than a dozen articles in leading academic accounting journals.

UCF College of Business Administration Dean Thomas L. Keon has named Sean Robb the director of the Kenneth G. Dixon School of Accounting.

Robb, an associate professor, joined the University of Central Florida in 2005. His research interests are in the general areas of financial accounting and international accounting, with specific interests in earnings management and financial disclosure.

He has published more than a dozen articles in leading academic accounting journals, including Contemporary Accounting Research; Auditing: A Journal of Practice & Theory; the Journal of Accounting, Auditing and Finance; and the Journal of Business, Finance and Accounting, among others.

Robb also serves as an ad hoc reviewer for several journals, and he has been on the editorial board of The International Journal of Accounting since 2001.

“Sean has been a great addition to our college,” Keon said. “He is respected by both faculty and students, and he has the leadership skills and vision to be successful as the new director of the Kenneth G. Dixon School of Accounting.”

Robb earned his Ph.D. in Business Administration from the University of Florida in 1995, and he is an Institute of Chartered Accountants of Ontario (ICAO) Doctoral Fellow and Chartered Accountant. He has received several awards for teaching and research excellence.

“These are exciting times for the Kenneth G. Dixon School of Accounting, and I am looking forward to facing the challenges of managing a premier accounting program,” Robb said. “With our tremendous group of talented and dedicated faculty, I sure that we can continue to build upon the fine reputation of the school.”

The Kenneth G. Dixon School of Accounting is one of the largest schools of accounting in the nation with 22 faculty members; more than 1,300 undergraduate, graduate and doctoral students; and a total endowment of more than $10 million.

The school includes the Kenneth G. Dixon Eminent Scholar Chair, the Burnett Eminent Scholar Chair and three endowed professorships: C.G. Avery, Ernst & Young and KPMG.

The School of Accounting, as well as all undergraduate and graduate programs in the UCF College of Business Administration, is accredited by AACSB International – the Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business.
