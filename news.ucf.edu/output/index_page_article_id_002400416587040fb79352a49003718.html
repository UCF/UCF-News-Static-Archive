Board of Trustees to Consider Nonprofit Management Degree, Retirement Plans

By Tom Evelyn March 23, 2004

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Tom Evelyn

    • 407-823-5988

  • UCF Board of Trustees

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Florida Board of Trustees will consider approving a masters degree program in nonprofit management and new retirement options for university employees at its meeting Thursday, March 25.

The trustees also are scheduled to vote on tenure recommendations for nearly two dozen professors and Neal Gallagher, the new dean of the College of Engineering and Computer Science. 

Committee meetings will begin at 8:30 a.m. in the Cape Florida Ballroom at the Student Union. The full board will meet at 1 p.m.

The masters degree in nonprofit management in the Department of Public Administration would allow students and faculty to partner more with Central Floridas nonprofit community, which includes more than 2,600 public charities and nonprofit organizations. Students would be required to complete a 33-hour course of online study with extensive service-learning activities.

The board will also consider a sociology doctorate that would focus on metropolitan and urban issues such as crime, domestic violence and social inequality.

The retirement plans being considered include an option that would allow employees not covered under an existing plan to redirect Social Security and Medicare contributions to a UCF retirement plan. The other option would allow employees to place payments for leave balances in a qualified pension plan.

For a complete meeting agenda and more information on the Board of Trustees, visit www.ucf.edu/bot.
