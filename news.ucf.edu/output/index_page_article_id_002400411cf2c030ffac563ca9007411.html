Public Comment Helps UCF Draft Master Plan

By UCF Staff Nov. 23, 2004

Photo: UCF File Photo

Residents learned more about the 2005-2015 Campus Master Plan at an open house at the UCF Arena in April. It was the first of several public meetings for the plan update.

UCFs Facilities Planning team presented the 2005-15 Campus Master Plan at several public meetings this past year, providing venues for individuals and organizations to review and voice their concerns about the plan.

The master plan is a blueprint for growth at the university. The 10-year outlook supports the universitys strategic plan, which outlines academic, research and operational goals.

This is a collaborative process, in which the comments and concerns of our neighbors assist us in developing a plan to responsibly manage UCFs future growth, said Peter Newman, director of Facilities Planning. The people who make their voices heard at public meetings and share comments with us during the planning process are helping us write a script for the continued success of this university.

Comments from citizens along with community organizations and public agencies were taken into consideration in the final drafting of the master plan, which will be presented to the UCF Board of Trustees during a public meeting Nov. 30. UCF also held an open house in April and two public hearings in July and October to give residents an opportunity to learn more about the plan and provide suggestions on how it could be improved.

State law requires that universities update their master plans every five years, reassessing future needs for growth and stewardship of the environment.

The cornerstone of this plan is its conservation focus, said William Merck, vice president for Administration and Finance. Our main campus is situated in an area of East Orange County that is blessed with many environmental treasures, and UCF remains committed to the protection of its ecosystems and lands of significant environmental importance.

Key elements of the master plan include:

Academic Facilities: 20 proposed degree programs would require large increases in classroom space, and teaching and research laboratories each year.

Student Housing: Increasing inventory by 50 percent or 2,000 beds.

Support Facilities: Enhancing the on-campus environment with facilities such as the addition of a convocation center for sporting events, concerts and community activities; retail shops; and dining and recreational facilities for students.

Traffic Management: Continued emphasis on UCFs shuttle bus systems, increased multilevel parking facilities and cooperative efforts with Orange County to help mitigate impacts on public services.

To review the master plan visit www.fp.ucf.edu, the UCF Library or FedEx Kinkos at 899 N. Alafaya Trail.
