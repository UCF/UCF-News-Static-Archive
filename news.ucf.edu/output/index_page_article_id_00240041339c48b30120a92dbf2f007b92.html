UCF Students Celebrate Earth Day With Organic Garden, New Recycling Efforts

By Courtney Gilmartin and Christine Dellert April 20, 2009

Photo: Courtesy of the UCF Environmental Initiative

Students who help plant and maintain the garden will share the healthy crops.

In anticipation of Earth Day, a group of University of Central Florida students is promoting more eco-friendly actions across campus.

Only in its second month, the Organic Community Garden sees a steady stream of student and staff volunteers. The half-acre plot will produce healthy, sustainable fruits, veggies and herbs that will be shared among those who helped with planting.

“To date, more than 100 students have contributed more than 500 hours of volunteer work to support the growth of plants within the garden,” said Alaina Bernard, assistant director of the Environmental Initiative & Arboretum and the Student Sustainability Alliance adviser. “Their hard work is a meaningful sign of the commitment of UCF’s student body to eco-friendly projects on campus.”

On-campus environmental activities will continue this week through Earth Day, Wednesday, April 22. Most outreach efforts are organized by the UCF Student Sustainability Alliance, which cares for the garden, and the UCF Environmental Initiative, which maintains the 80-acre Arboretum on UCF’s main campus.

The Student Sustainability Alliance (SSA) will kick off this week by speaking out against single-use plastic bags as part of Orange County’s Take Your Own Tote campaign. Students will volunteer at local grocery and retail stores and encourage customers to carry reusable tote bags rather than wasteful paper or plastic bags.

As part of those efforts, new recycling containers and stations will be placed around campus specifically for plastic bags. Everyone is encouraged to fill the containers will their old bags.

On Wednesday, April 22, Earth Day, students from the SSA will set up a table outside the Student Union and advocate for the “reduce, reuse, recycle” model of sustainability. Activities outside the Student Union will take place from 10 a.m. to 2 p.m.

Equipped with sewing machines, SSA members will teach students and faculty how to make reusable tote bags from their old T-shirts. SSA will also be giving out prizes, such as organic T-shirts, to students who bring in old plastic bags.

SSA will also be collecting gently used school supplies to donate to underprivileged children in Central Florida.  Items such as backpacks and notebooks will be collected Wednesday at the table in front of the Student Union and in the Environmental Initiative’s office throughout the week. The Environmental Initiative office is located just behind the Harris Corporation Engineering Center and CREOL.

“We’re constantly committed to making this university more sustainable,” said Tina Richards, environmental educator and volunteer coordinator for the UCF Environmental Initiative. “As good as recycling is, reusing is better. We really try to promote reusable products.”

Richards said she hopes that UCF’s “Earth Week” efforts remind students and others to “be more conscientious of their consumption and think about the bigger picture.”

Throughout April, students have spread the message about being green by restoring pine flatwoods and educating Cocoa Beach residents on smart living at the Sunsational EarthFest event. In addition to special Earth Month events, SSA members and student volunteers have continued to set up a table every week in front of the Student Union.

Also during Earth Week, the UCF Foundation will sponsor three tree planting ceremonies for UCF employees in the adjacent Central Florida Research Park.
