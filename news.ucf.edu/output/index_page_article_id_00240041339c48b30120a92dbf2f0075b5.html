16 UCF Students Join Order of Pegasus

By Courtney Gilmartin April 23, 2009

Photo: Jacque Brund

Twelve undergraduates and four graduate students joined the prestigious Order of Pegasus on Wednesday.

Sixteen University of Central Florida students received the Order of Pegasus, UCF’s most prestigious student award, at a ceremony Wednesday evening.

The award honors outstanding undergraduate academic achievement, university involvement, leadership and community service. Graduate students are selected separately based on academic achievement, professional or community service, leadership and publication or research experience.

The names and pictures of the inductees will be added to the wall of the Pegasus Lounge, which is located on the second floor of the Student Union overlooking the Pegasus Ballroom. Inductees receive commemorative medals, Student Government Association scholarships and lifetime memberships to the UCF Alumni Association.

After being nominated by members of the UCF community, students completed a formal application, provided recommendations and interviewed with a selection committee. To qualify for Order of Pegasus, undergraduates must have senior standing at the end of the summer term. Graduate students must have completed at least two semesters of graduate work at UCF.
 
The following four graduate students won Order of Pegasus honors:

Gregory D. Austin Jr. is a master’s student in the DeVos Sport Business Management program in the College of Business Administration. As a graduate assistant in the Office of Fraternity and Sorority Life, Austin implemented workshops and retreats related to diversity and responsible alcohol consumption. Austin will continue working as an event manager at Disney’s Wide World of Sports Complex after graduation, and he hopes to one day work for the National Football League.

Satyender Goel is a Ph.D. candidate in Chemistry in the College of Sciences. He is chair of the NanoScience Technology Center’s student body and the Indian Awareness Council.  Goel plans to pursue a career in management and planning at a research-oriented company. He hopes to stay close to his research work and serve as a UCF ambassador to his native India.   

Mustafa Gul is a Civil, Environmental and Construction Engineering Ph.D. candidate in the College of Engineering and Computer Science, while simultaneously pursuing a graduate degree in Electrical Engineering at UCF.  In 2008, the National Science Foundation sponsored his trip to Korea to attend the Asian Pacific Summer School on Smart Structures. Gul hopes to continue his post-doctoral studies at UCF and become a college professor.

Margaret Ann Shillingford is a Counselor Education Ph.D. candidate in the College of Education. She is a member of many counseling-related organizations and the winner of a Ross Trust Scholarship Award. After graduation, she will be an assistant professor of Counselor Education at the College of William and Mary in Williamsburg, Va.

The following 12 undergraduates were also selected:

Gaston Blanchet is a Business Management major and International Business minor in the College of Business Administration. A member of the Burnett Honors College and former Mr. UCF, Blanchet is also an International Business major at the ICN Business School in Nancy, France. After graduation, he will work in Dubai to get first-hand experience in international business.

Tamara Chaplin is an Elementary Education major in the College of Education who has studied at UCF’s Regional Campuses. She is a member of the President’s Leadership Council, Homecoming Advisory Board and Golden Key International Honour Society.  Chaplin will pursue a graduate degree after graduation, and she hopes to work in higher education helping first-generation students.

Katherine Ferstadt is a Microbiology and Molecular Biology major in the Burnett School of Biomedical Sciences in the College of Medicine with a Chemistry minor. She is an active member in UCF’s chapter of the American Medical Students Association and the Pre-Professional Medical Society. Ferstadt will be a part of the UCF College of Medicine’s charter class, and she hopes to open her own surgery practice.

Demetrius Frazier is an Accounting major in the College of Business Administration. A member of the Burnett Honors College, he is also a LEAD Scholar and founding member of the Iota Theta Chapter of Theta Chi Fraternity. His community service includes Junior Achievement and Habitat for Humanity. Frazier will pursue an MBA, and he hopes to work for a Fortune 500 company and start his own consulting firm.

Samantha Gil is an Advertising and Public Relations major in the Nicholson School of Communication in the College of Sciences with a minor in Dance. She is a LEAD Scholar, a member of Pi Beta Phi Fraternity for Women, and the 2008 Homecoming queen.  Gil plans to pursue a graduate degree in higher education with a focus on student personnel, and she would like to work at a university.

Ashley Jordan is a Political Science major in the College of Sciences with a Spanish minor. She has participated in the Criminal Justice Study Abroad program in Russia and has been on the President’s Honor Roll and Dean’s List.  Jordan plans to attend law school with a focus on international relations, and she would like to pursue a career with the United States Department of State.

Hye Jung Kim is a Mathematics major in the College of Sciences. She studied “Differential Equations in Classical and Quantum Physics” in a Research Experience for Undergraduates program sponsored by the National Science Foundation and received the Florida Excellence in Housing award for her work as a resident assistant. After graduation, Kim will pursue a Ph.D. in mathematics and a career in teaching.

Stephanie Morales is a Microbiology and Molecular Biology major in the Burnett School of Biomedical Sciences in the College of Medicine with a minor in Health Services Administration. A member of the Hispanic American Student Association, she was awarded Pre- Professional Medical Society honor status. Morales will attend medical school and pursue a career as a physician, with a focus on helping the underserved.

Michelle Murphy is double majoring in History in the College of Arts and Humanities and Legal Studies in the College of Health and Public Affairs, with a minor in Political Science. She is a member of the Student Government Association and the Pi Alpha Delta Pre-Law Fraternity and an advisor to the Student Sustainability Alliance. Murphy hopes to attend law school with a focus on environmental law and work for a nonprofit environmental organization.

Ruth Strakosha is a Microbiology and Molecular Biology major in the Burnett School of Biomedical Sciences in the College of Medicine. She spent a summer studying abroad at the University of Cambridge and has interned with the Oak Ridge Institute for Science and Education, part of the National Center for Toxicological Research. After graduation, Strakosha plans to attend medical school and study infectious diseases.

Thomas Blake VanBrunt Jr. is a Microbiology and Molecular Biology major in the Burnett School of Biomedical Sciences in the College of Medicine. He is a member of the President’s Leadership Council and Alpha Tau Omega fraternity, and he is a Pegasus High Achievement Scholar. He also was a LEAD Scholar. VanBrunt will attend medical school and would like to pursue a career in either orthopedics or pediatrics.

Carissa R. Van Dalen is a Vocal Music major in the College of Arts and Humanities with a double minor in Religious Studies and English Linguistics. She is a member of the President's Leadership Council and Campus Crusade for Christ, and she has completed an Honors in the Major thesis. After graduation, Van Dalen hopes to work in a music ministry and earn a master’s degree in applied linguistics.
