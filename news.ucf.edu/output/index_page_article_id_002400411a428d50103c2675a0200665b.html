Study Shows Disadvantages of South Location for Proposed On-Campus Stadium

By Tom Evelyn July 29, 2005

Photo: UCF Staff

Building a proposed on-campus football stadium on a south campus site that some residents prefer would require displacing intramural fields, building new roads, mitigating wetlands and other measures, UCF Administration and Finance Vice President Bill Merck told the Board of Trustees at its regular meeting Thursday.

During a campus construction update, Merck also pointed out that a sightline study had shown that residents of University Estates to the east of campus should not be able to see the proposed on-campus football stadium from their homes.

Merck discussed the disadvantages of placing the stadium in the south location that some neighbors favor over the north location in the intercollegiate athletics complex that includes the UCF Arena. The south location would require building new access roads, expanding Libra Drive, eliminating parking that serves student housing, moving intramural fields, expanding ponds and mitigating wetlands.

Several residents who spoke July 25 at the first of two public hearings on amending the campus master plan to include the stadium said they would support a stadium if it were placed in the south location.

The university contends the north site makes more sense because the stadium would be adjacent to existing intercollegiate facilities, new housing for 2,000 students, the alumni center, existing and planned parking garages, shops, restaurants and the 10,000-seat convocation center, which will host sporting events and other entertainment.

These amenities would encourage fans who attend football games to arrive earlier and stay later, spreading out traffic over a longer period and reducing a rush at game time.

If approved, the proposed 45,000-seat stadium could be completed by the 2007 season. The stadium, which would host an average of six games a year, would be built and supported by ticket and concessions sales, donations, corporate sponsorships and advertising.

The second public hearing and expected vote by the Board of Trustees on the stadium amendment is scheduled for Nov. 9.

The Board of Trustees endorsed the concept of the stadium in April and directed staff to negotiate finance and construction agreements. The board has not yet voted formally whether to build the stadium.

Like all campus buildings, the stadium must be included as an amendment to the campus master plan, a blueprint for growth and development at the university. The 2005-15 campus master plan was approved by the governor and Cabinet on June 1. UCF decided then to go through the major amendment process, which includes two public hearings and review by about a dozen state agencies, to add the proposed stadium.

University officials met informally with residents at a July 12 information session about the stadium amendment and again after the July 25 public hearing. Since announcing in early January that it was considering building a stadium on campus, UCF has conducted more than 15 public meetings with neighborhood groups, organizations and others.

Updates on the proposed stadium and amendment process will be posted here and on www.stadium.ucf.edu .

Next meeting:

Second Public Hearing/Trustees Vote: 1 p.m. Wednesday, Nov. 9, Cape Florida room, Student Union
