88°F, Fair

National Media Shine Spotlight on UCF Students, Grads

By Grant J. Heston May 31, 2011

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Print this Page

UCF students and alumni recently made local and national news, earning the attention of Time Magazine, the New York Times and the Orlando Sentinel.

Please see below for a review of selected UCF print, broadcast and online media coverage. Follow UCF News on Twitter and Facebook, watch UCF TV and learn more about Strategy, Marketing, Communications & Admissions.

NATIONAL

Time Magazine: New App Diagnoses Malaria Just from Photographs
For once, here's a smartphone app that does something more productive than knocking over pig-built structures or killing time in a waiting room.
-- This features research from UCF engineering student Tristan Gibeau.

The New York Times: Fountains of Optimism for Life Way Out There
For those who hunt for life on other worlds, water in its liquid form is perhaps the leading indicator. Life as we know it on Earth is based on water and carbon.
-- This story features the research of Physics professor Humberto Campins.

The Huffington Post: Top 25 Underrated Creative Writing MFA Programs
The 25 programs listed here fully fund a sizable percentage of incoming students, yet still receive less attention from applicants than they deserve.
-- UCF is included on this list.

The New York Times: The Internship as Inside Track
Want to land a full-time job after college? Get an internship or two, or even four or more.
-- This story features UCF graduate Lauren Berger, better known as the "Intern Queen."

The Wall Street Journal: Economists See Growth Accelerating Later in Year
The U.S. economic recovery is expected to gain momentum over the rest of the year, despite a sharp pullback in growth during the just-finished first quarter, according to economists surveyed by The Wall Street Journal.
-- This story quotes Sean Snaith, director of the Institute for Economic Competitiveness.

Associated Press: Percentage of Black Players in MLB Drops Again
The percentage of black players in Major League Baseball dropped again on opening day this year even as the league again received a top overall grade for racial diversity.
-- This story, which appeared in publications around the country, features research from Richard Lapchick, director of UCF's Institute for Diversity and Ethics in Sport.

STATE/LOCAL

Miami Herald: Orlando Pulling Florida Out of Recession
Florida’s economic slide reversed thanks to Miami and Orlando, though Central Florida is posting a stronger recovery.
-- This story features UCF and the medical city.

Orlando Sentinel: Scott Maxwell -- Speaking of activists …
I want to shine a light on a different breed of advocates — those involved with Orlando nonprofits making an impact far beyond Central Florida. First up is HOPE for Haiti, a relatively new program established by UCF President John Hitt in the aftermath of the Haiti earthquake.

Orlando Sentinel: UCF's Biggest Losers Drop 231 Pounds
Sixteen University of Central Florida students were focused on the beer keg perched in the sand outside the campus fitness center.

NEWS 13: The Next Generation
For years, the University of Central Florida has offered engineering classes in building racing engines.

Orlando Sentinel: New Restaurants Gamble on Success
Three University of Central Florida alumni, all in their mid-20s, are part of a partnership that has opened up Terrace 390 in the old Harvey's Bistro downtown.

Orlando Sentinel: Opera Finds Second Act in Central Florida
Two years ago, opera in Orlando seemed as dead as the leading lady in “Tosca.”
-- This story features UCF's opera program.
