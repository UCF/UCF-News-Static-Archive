Fall Classes, New Programs Began Monday as UCF Welcomed 44,000 Students

By Chad Binette Aug. 23, 2004

Photo: Jerry Klein

Opening day, confusion and resolve.

University of Central Florida students began classes and the pursuit of several new degrees Monday, Aug. 23, on a main campus with fully restored utilities and roads and sidewalks that were clear of debris from Hurricane Charley.

UCF expected an enrollment of about 44,000 on the main campus and at nearly two dozen regional campuses and instructional centers. UCFs anticipated fall enrollment represents a 5.6 percent increase over the fall 2003 final enrollment of 41,685. This years incoming freshmen earned an average SAT score of 1187, an increase of 11 points over last year.

Most of the UCF campus never lost power in the wake of Hurricane Charley. Students began moving in to campus residence halls on Tuesday and Wednesday after campus Physical Plant and Building Services employees worked over the weekend and Monday to ensure that the buildings, roads and sidewalks were safe and clear of debris when the students arrived.

We begin the fall semester this year with a particular sense of pride having weathered the effects of Hurricane Charley, President John Hitt said. Thanks to our dedicated employees, our university is in great shape as our students begin classes. Were excited to welcome our high-achieving students and faculty and to introduce many new academic programs that will allow us to better serve our region.

Some of UCFs new programs this fall will allow students to obtain a doctoral degree in conservation biology and masters degrees in computer art and design, forensic science and nonprofit management. The university also expanded its offerings in theater, political science, communicative disorders and undergraduate nanoscience research. UCFs Latin American studies program will have a broader focus, including courses and other activities related to the Hispanic population in the United States.

In a new pilot program called Grad on Track, 60 UCF freshmen declared a major and signed a contract listing all of the classes they plan to take. If they stick with the major and successfully complete all of the listed courses, UCF will ensure that they get a spot in all of their classes and graduate in four years.

Construction projects on the UCF campus this academic year include the new Engineering III building, which is scheduled to open in the spring or summer, and a new psychology building, on which construction is slated to start in December or January.

Construction is scheduled to start in September on campus housing with about 500 beds by the UCF Arena. Site work for a new, 10,000-seat convocation center, which will host sporting events, concerts and graduation ceremonies, is scheduled to begin in January.
