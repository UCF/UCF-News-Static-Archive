Miss UCF Megan Clementi Wins First Runner-Up Honors at State Pageant

By Kristi Bartlett and Chad Binette Updated July 9, 2006

Photo: Richard Trainor

Christine Cooper, Denesha Jenkins, Rachel Potter and Megan Clementi (from left to right) are four of the seven contestants representing UCF at the Miss Florida pageant. They posed with First Lady Martha Hitt and President John Hitt.

Miss UCF Megan Clementi, an electrical engineering major and Orlando Magic dancer, was selected as the first runner-up at the Miss Florida pageant Saturday.

Clementi, who also placed in the top 10 last year, will represent Florida at the National Sweetheart Pageant in Illinois, which invites the first runner-up from each state pageant. Since 1970, eight eventual Miss America winners have competed in the National Sweetheart Pageant, which will be held during Labor Day weekend this year.

Denesha Jenkins, an elementary education major, also finished in the Miss Florida top 10 for the second consecutive year. UCF student Rachel Potter, an advertising and public relations major, and recent graduate Christine Cooper placed in the top 15.

Seven UCF students and recent graduates competed for Miss Florida honors. About 40 women vied for $60,000 in scholarships during the four-day competition in Miami.

Clementis platform focuses on helping children with life-threatening diseases. She enjoys helping children experience fairy tale weeks and distractions from their illnesses.

Jenkins, who won the Miss South Florida Fair title, was last years third runner-up. She chose Get Excited About Reading: Literacy Education as her pageant platform.

Potter, the reigning Miss Winter Park, is an advertising and public relations major. Her platform is Kidz Under Construction: Building Character and Constructing a Brighter Future.

Miss Apopka Christine Cooper graduated summa cum laude from UCF in 2004 with a bachelors degree in psychology. Now a doctoral student in psychology at the Florida Institute of Technology, Cooper chose a platform of advocating for the rights of the mentally ill.

Danielle Marino, who won the Miss Ocala/Marion County title, graduated in 2004 with a bachelors degree in theater performance. Her platform is Character First -- The Power of Self Respect.

Miss Florida Citrus Sheena Howell, who graduated this year from UCF with a bachelors degree in molecular and microbiology, is a dental medicine student at Nova Southeastern University. Her platform, ENABL -- Education Now, Babies Later, promotes the importance of abstaining from sex until marriage.

Miss Bay Area Lauren Meyer, a visual language major, set her pageant platform as Links for Life: Connecting Our Youth to a Healthier Tomorrow.

Two years ago, Ericka Dunlap, an advertising and public relations major, won the Miss Florida and Miss America pageants. Dunlap graduated from UCF in spring 2005.

For more information on the pageant, go to www.missflorida.org.
