First Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy Class Graduates

By Chad Binette Updated Dec. 12, 2006

Photo: Jacque Brund

Hundreds of dreams come true and are shared with family at commencement.

The first 12 graduates of the University of Central Floridas new video game development program received their masters degrees during a ceremony Monday, Dec. 11, at the UCF Arena.

The Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy graduates enrolled in summer 2005, when UCF first offered the program at the former Expo Centre in downtown Orlando. They spent 16 months preparing to become producers, artists and programmers in the video-game industry. FIEAs current fall enrollment is 63, including 17 students who are scheduled to graduate in the spring.

The FIEA graduates were among a total of about 3,500 UCF students scheduled to graduate during four ceremonies Dec. 11 and 12. The anticipated graduates include 2,877 who filed for bachelors degrees, 575 for masters degrees, five for education specialist degrees and 87 for doctoral degrees.

FIEA graduates were part of the 2 p.m. ceremony on Dec. 11, which featured students from the College of Arts and Humanities and College of Sciences. The speaker was Rita Bornstein, who served as Rollins Colleges 13th president from 1990 to 2004.

UCF President John Hitt awarded Bornstein with an honorary doctoral degree in Humane Letters during the ceremony.

Bornsteins accomplishments as Rollins president include more than quintupling the colleges endowment and raising standards for student admissions and faculty evaluations. After her tenure as president concluded, Bornstein was named president emerita and the George D. and Harriet W. Cornell Professor of Philanthropy and Leadership Development at Rollins.

During the ceremony she urged students to keep in mind opportunity, civility and philanthropy when they left UCF.

"Keep on learning and put it to good use," Bornstein said. "Leave the world a little better than you found it."

The first of the four commencement ceremonies, at 10 a.m. Dec. 11, featured graduates of the College of Health and Public Affairs, College of Engineering and Computer Science, Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences and College of Optics and Photonics. The speaker was Leila Jammal Nodarse, a 1982 UCF graduate who is the founder of Nodarse & Associates, Inc., a geotechnical, environmental and construction materials testing firm based in Winter Park. Nodarse has served on the UCF Foundation Board of Directors.

Nodarse spoke about her days at UCF -- about the search for parking, the construction and her belief that she was done on graduation day. But shortly afterwards she was back on campus earning her master's degree. She also continued to build a relationship with UCF that eventually led to the launch of her own company a few years later.

"Relish the moment," she told graduates on Monday. "For it is only a moment and return to UCF with pride and generosity."

College of Business Administration students graduated at 10 a.m. Dec. 12. The speaker was Jacob V. Stuart, who has served as president of the Orlando Regional Chamber of Commerce since 1984. Stuart also serves on the UCF Foundation Board of Directors.

Stuart talked to the graduates about all the changes at the university over the past few years -- the medical college, the on-campus stadium and several new degree programs, for example -- and the changes still to come for the graduates.

Everything on earth has its own time and its own season, Stuart said, quoting from the Book of Ecclesiastes. Its your moment, its your graduation, its your special season.

He then shared a graduation gift of lessons for success in business and in life that he has learned: Play nice with one another; tell the truth; do whats right; pay your bills; share your sandwich, or your success, with others; turn out the lights, or take care of our planet; say please and thank you, or mind your manners; go to church to learn more about yourself; and talk to mom and dad, especially when you dont need anything.

It will enrich everything you do and all that you say, Stuart said, addressing particularly the last lesson. When youre able to turn your good fortunes  into a chance for others to succeed  you will become the leaders of America.

The 2 p.m. ceremony Dec. 12 featured College of Education and Rosen College of Hospitality Management graduates. The speaker was Marcos R. Marchena, the senior partner of Marchena and Graham, P.A., an Orlando law firm that he founded in 1988. A recipient of a bachelors degree in political science from UCF, Marchena now serves on the UCF Foundation Board of Directors.

Speaking to the education and hospitality management students, Marchena said there is a growing need for both in Florida. Just as more visitors come to the state every year, more people decide to call it home. As a result, the demands on the states school systems are growing at an incredible rate.

All these schools will need teachers, principals and others, he said. They need people like you.

Marchena closed by offering advice to the graduates as they entered the next phase of their lives: Thank God for your blessings; focus part of each and every day on your family; be a loyal friend; find ways to contribute to your community and country; and strive to be the very best at whatever you choose to do.

UCF has awarded about 164,000 degrees since starting classes in 1968.

Commencement ceremonies can be viewed online at http://webcast.oir.ucf.edu. For more information on commencement, go to www.registrar.ucf.edu/Comsked.html.
