UCF Health Services Receives Renewal of National Accreditation

By Holly MacLeod Jan. 19, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Dr. Bob Wirag

    • 407-823-2094

  • UCF Health Services

  • Print this Page

UCF Health Services has achieved a renewal of its national accreditation from the Accreditation Association for Ambulatory Health Care.

Status as an accredited organization means UCF Health Services has met a series of rigorous and nationally recognized standards for the provision of quality health care set by the association.

More than 2,300 ambulatory health care organizations across the United States are accredited by the AAAHC. UCF, the University of Florida and Florida State University are the only three universities in the state with AAAHC-accredited health centers.

Accreditation underscores our longstanding commitment to providing the highest possible levels of quality care to the community we serve, said Dr. Bob Wirag, director of UCF Health Services. We are pleased and proud to have our efforts recognized with this accreditation.

Ambulatory health care organizations seeking accreditation by the AAAHC undergo an. extensive, on-site survey of facilities and services by other health officials. Organizations that can seek AAAHC accreditation include ambulatory and office-based surgery centers, group practices, community health centers and managed care organizations.

UCF Health Services, which serves nearly 47,000 students, received its first accreditation in 2000. Each accreditation is valid for three years.

Health Services moved into a new, 48,000-square-foot building in fall 2005. The Health Center offers expanded services in womens health, shorter waits for students who need medical care and a larger pharmacy that also sells healthy food.
