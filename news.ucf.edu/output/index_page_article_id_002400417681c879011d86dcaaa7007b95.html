Trustees Discuss Growth in UCF Students' Academic Quality, Diversity

By Chad Binette Nov. 13, 2008

Photo: Jacque Brund

Retiring Vice President and Chief of Staff Beth Barnes was honored during the Board of Trustees meeting on Thursday. The new chief of staff, John "Rick" Schell, joined the board and audience in applauding her service to the university.

A presentation about the dramatic growth in the academic quality of UCF students, their increased diversity and the stronger demand for a UCF education highlighted the Board of Trustees meeting on Thursday.

Tom Huddleston, vice president for Marketing, Communications and Admissions, told the board that the average SAT score for incoming freshmen starting in the fall semester – currently 1219 -- has increased more than 130 points since 1994. UCF also enrolled a record 63 National Merit Scholars this fall.

UCF’s student body has become much more diverse during the past 14 years. Fifteen percent of undergraduates enrolled this semester are Hispanic, and 9 percent are African-American. In 1994, 10 percent of undergraduates were Hispanic, and 6 percent were African-American.

The number of applications from freshmen has more than tripled, rising from 8,835 in 1994 to 29,333 this year.

In other action Thursday,

-- At President Hitt’s request, the board honored retiring Vice President and Chief of Staff Beth Barnes by granting her emeritus status during her final meeting with the board. Emeritus status provides a lifetime link to UCF and honors major professional contributions and distinction.

A charter member of the UCF faculty and a member of President Hitt’s leadership team since 1994, Barnes also serves as the board’s associate corporate secretary.

“We appreciate all that you’ve done to help us,” said Board of Trustees Chair Rick Walsh.

-- Honored five UCF professors – tied for the most among Florida universities – who are traveling abroad this year on Fulbright Scholar grants, the flagship cultural education exchange program sponsored by the U.S. Department of State. To read more, click here.

-- Recognized trustee Michael J. “Micky” Grindstaff, ’78, for his induction into the UCF College of Business Administration’s Hall of Fame. To read more, click here.

-- Honored M.J. Soileau, vice president of UCF’s Office of Research and Commercialization, and College of Optics and Photonics faculty members Leon Glebov and Shin-Tson Wu for receiving awards from SPIE, the world’s premier scientific society in optics and photonics. To read more, click here.

-- Recognized Helen Donegan, vice president for Community Relations; Dr. Deborah German, founding dean of the College of Medicine; and Linda Chapin, director of the Metropolitan Center for Regional Studies, for being named “Women Who Shape Orlando” by CEO Orlando magazine.

-- Honored Cynthia J. Hutchinson of the College of Education for receiving the Mary L. Collins Award for Excellence in Teacher Education by the Florida Association of Teacher Educators.

-- Recognized Kevin D. Belfield of the College of Sciences for earning a four-year, $1.8 million Collaborative Research in Chemistry grant from the National Science Foundation. The project provides a pathway to the Ph.D. for Hispanic undergraduate students through a collaboration with New Mexico Highlands University.

-- Heard presentations on the Center for Public and Nonprofit Management within the College of Health and Public Affairs and the Marriage and Family Institute in the College of Education.  The Center for Public and Nonprofit Management is dedicated to applied research, education and training, and service in community-oriented research, community capacity building networks, emergency management, civil society, and public participation. The mission of the Marriage and Family Research Institute is to support healthy relationships and marriages for couples and families through research and training in marriage education.

UCF Stands For Opportunity: The University of Central Florida is a metropolitan research university that ranks as the 6th largest in the nation with more than 50,000 students. UCF's first classes were offered in 1968. The university offers impressive academic and research environments that power the region's economic development. UCF's culture of opportunity is driven by our diversity, Orlando environment, history of entrepreneurship and our youth, relevance and energy. For more information visit http://news.ucf.edu.
