Wilson on Iraq's Alleged Nuclear Links

By Chad Binette November 10, 2003

Related Contacts and Links:

    • www.ucfglobalperspectives.org
    • 407-823-0935 or 407-823-0688

  • Keisha Biggs

    • 407-823-0935 or 407-823-0688

  • Print this Page

A former U.S. ambassador who was at the center of a controversy this summer involving evidence the Bush administration used to justify the war in Iraq will speak Thursday, Nov. 13, at the University of Central Florida.

Joseph Wilson IV, the last American diplomat to meet with Saddam Hussein before the Gulf War, traveled to Niger in 2002 to investigate whether the African nation was helping Iraq obtain nuclear weapons.

He will discuss his trip, along with the war in Iraq, in a speech entitled "What I Didnt Find in Africa: Examining Iraqs Alleged Nuclear Links to Niger" at 1:30 p.m. in the Pegasus Ballroom at the UCF Student Union building.

The forum is free and open to the public. It is sponsored by the UCF Office of Global Perspectives and Department of Political Science and by Global Connections for Central Florida, a nonpartisan group that strives to improve Orlando area residents understanding of international issues.

Wilson wrote in a New York Times column this summer that "intelligence related to Iraqs nuclear weapons program was twisted to exaggerate the Iraqi threat." A week later, Robert Novak mentioned in his syndicated column that Wilsons wife is a CIA operative, prompting a Justice Department inquiry into whether that information was leaked by administration officials.

Wilson now is chief executive officer of JC Wilson International Ventures Corp., a consulting firm that specializes in strategic management and international business development. He is a former senior director for African affairs at the National Security Council and was acting U.S. ambassador in Iraq during the buildup to the Gulf War in 1990. Wilson was U.S. ambassador to Gabon under the first President Bush.
