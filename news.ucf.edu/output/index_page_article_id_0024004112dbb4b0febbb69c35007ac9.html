STORM UPDATE: UCF to Open for Employees Tuesday; Classes to Resume on Wednesday

By UCF Staff Sept. 6, 2004

Photo: UCF Housing

Safe in a shelter on the UCF campus, students played games and watched the UCF-Wisconsin football game Saturday while waiting for Hurricane Frances to make its way across Florida.

University of Central Florida offices at the main campus in Orlando will reopen for employees at 7 a.m. Tuesday, Sept. 7. However, the university has canceled classes on Tuesday to give students more time to return to Central Florida after the hurricane. Classes are scheduled to resume at 7 a.m. Wednesday, Sept. 8.

The above schedule also applies to the UCF Downtown and UCF South Orlando regional sites. Classes at UCF Sanford/Lake Mary will resume on Tuesday.

UCF's campuses at Cocoa, Daytona Beach, Deland, Melbourne and Palm Bay will be closed for employees and classes on Tuesday. The date when classes will restart at those sites has not been determined. 

Students returning to the Orlando campus are urged to carefully check their travel routes, to monitor weather forecasts and to be sure they have enough fuel.  Hurricane Frances is still impacting many parts of Florida with unpredictable weather, and UCFs plans to resume classes Wednesday could be revisited if the weather conditions remain a serious problem.

Though several campus buildings had minor water damage and more trees were felled by bad weather, the university will be ready for business on Tuesday. Concerns for students safety and their ability to return in time prompted the cancellation of Tuesday classes.

Physical Plant employees began a detailed assessment of the storm damage Sunday evening. When the assessment is finished, the university plans to announce a timeline for students to return to campus residence halls.

Students who are unable to return to campus by Wednesday should talk with their professors immediately upon their return to classes. Faculty or staff unable to return to campus by Tuesday should contact their immediate supervisors.

Students enrolled in classes at regional campuses and instructional sites should check with their particular campus as soon as possible to determine when classes will resume.

UCF area campuses at community colleges follow the schedule of those colleges.

About 1,000 UCF students, employees and nearby residents stayed Saturday and Sunday at a handful of shelters on campus.

The group staying on campus included about 440 students, who played board games and watched movies and the UCF-Wisconsin football game to pass the time while waiting for the brunt of the storm to reach Central Florida. UCF provided pizza for the students Saturday night, and Assistant Director of Housing and Residence Life Joe Paulick reported that everything was going well in the shelters. He said most students slept in Sunday morning.

The Education Building hosted about 300 UCF employees involved in emergency preparations, along with their families. Another 385 people, mostly area residents, were staying in an Orange County-operated shelter at the UCF Arena.

UCF emergency response officials updated President John Hitt on Saturday evening and again on Sunday. Hitt; his wife, Martha; and Vice President of Administration and Finance Bill Merck and his wife, Ksenia, spent time in the campus response center with employees both days.

As the storm's effects reached Central Florida, UCF employees continued to prepare the campus by adding sandbags outside of building entrances. On Saturday, before the storm arrived, they delivered food to shelters, cleared debris and cut down trees that were damaged by Hurricane Charley. Students who work as resident assistants guided other students into the appropriate shelters.

On-campus student residents who remained at UCF were directed to designated campus shelters, as were residents of Pegasus Pointe and Pegasus Landing, UCF's affiliated housing. On-campus student residents not sheltered in their residence halls are staying in the Classroom and Communication buildings. For more details, go to www.housing.ucf.edu . 

Tuition deadline extended

Students will be able to pay tuition and fees without being assessed late payments through Sept. 17. The Cashier's Office will be closed until 8:30 a.m. Tuesday, Sept. 7. Payments may still be made at https://connect.ucf.edu or by mail.

Campus Events Canceled

The Isaac Bashevis Singer Centennial program scheduled for Sept. 9 at the UCF Library has been canceled. Henry Abramson, associate professor of history and Judaic studies at Florida Atlantic University, had been scheduled to talk about Singer's life and writing.

Individuals planning to attend other events scheduled on campus may want to check with the planners of the activity, since most campus events have been cancelled or postponed.
