UCF-Tulane Football Game Moved to 7 p.m. Friday Because of Hurricane

By UCF Staff Oct. 19, 2005

Photo: AP

UCF teammates Brandon Marshall, left, Travonti Johnson, center, and Leger DouzabLe, right, celebrate a fumble recovery against Marshall. The team will face Tulane on Friday.

Due to the impending threat of Hurricane Wilma to the state of Florida, UCF Director of Athletics Steve Orsini announced today that this weekends football game between UCF and Tulane will be played at 7 p.m. Friday, Oct. 21, in the Florida Citrus Bowl. The game was originally scheduled for Saturday, but UCF and Tulane, in consultation with Conference USA, agreed to the schedule change after reviewing the latest storm projections.

To ensure the safety of the student-athletes, fans, alumni and game workers, it is in the best interest of everyone that we move the game, Orsini said. Moving the contest to Friday should give everyone involved with the game plenty of time to return home safely.

UCF officials are watching Hurricane Wilma and plan to meet Thursday morning to discuss other preparations. Check this site for updates. 

Family Weekend festivities scheduled for this weekend have been postponed until Nov. 4 to 6. The Golden Knights will face Houston Nov. 5 in their final home game of the season. Anyone holding tickets to pre-paid events for Family Weekend is encouraged to wait for the coordinating department or office for those events to contact them with information about rescheduling and refunds. More information about Family Weekend is available at www.getinvolveducf.com.

Ticket information will be released soon for fans who are unable to attend the game Friday. 740-The Team will broadcast the game live, with pre-game coverage beginning at 5 p.m. UCFathletics.com also has the broadcast, along with live statistics. CSTVs All-Access package no longer will televise the game due to the schedule change.

UCFs task force on tailgating and alcohol issues met Wednesday afternoon and decided to go ahead with plans to provide free non-alcoholic beverages to student tailgaters and to have UCF faculty, staff and alumni serve as ambassadors who will mingle with tailgaters.

Uniformed law enforcement officers will patrol all Citrus Bowl parking lots. Fans are not allowed to have glass bottles in the parking lots.
