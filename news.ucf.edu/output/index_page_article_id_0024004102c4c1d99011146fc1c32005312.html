UCF Trustees Discuss Budget Cuts; Departments Asked to Make Further Cuts

By Chad Binette July 26, 2007

Photo: Jacque Brund

Trustees Alan Florez (left), Manoj Chopra and their colleagues heard an update Thursday on the state budget outlook and the impact that it will have on UCF.

University of Central Florida departments were asked Thursday to cut 2 percent from their budgets in addition to the 4 percent that they have already eliminated.

The request from the universitys administration came as the UCF Board of Trustees discussed the impact of state budget cuts during their regular meeting Thursday. The financial resources of public universities throughout Florida are being strained by the cuts and the governors veto of a proposed 5 percent tuition increase.

Were going to have a situation in which (class) sections get larger and are fewer in number, President John Hitt told the trustees. Students are going to experience an inconvenience, as will faculty and staff members. You dont take this much money out of the budget and not see an effect.

The exact amount of the state cut to UCFs budget may not be known for a few months, long after the fall semester has started. The next set of state revenue estimates, which legislators use to set the budget, will be available in early August. However, the Legislature isnt scheduled to meet again until a special session in mid-September.

The additional 2 percent that UCF departments are being asked to cut from their budgets will be held in escrow pending further instruction from the Board of Governors, which oversees the state's 11 public universities. If the state does not mandate budget cuts beyond the existing 4 percent, those funds will be redistributed within the university.

UCF already had enacted a hiring freeze to help save money. Departments are allowed to seek exemptions from Hitt or Provost Terry Hickey to fill vacant positions. Some exemptions, such as for police officers and dispatchers, have been granted. But Hickey said he is holding off making decisions on most requests until more is known about the budget outlook.

At the request of the state Board of Governors, UCF and other state universities also will limit freshman enrollment in 2008-09 to the same number of students that they admit this year. UCF expects to admit a total of about 6,700 freshmen this summer and fall.

The universitys $950 million budget includes money from tuition and fees, other funds appropriated by the state Legislature, research grants and other sources. A cut of 6 percent to the legislatively appropriated portion translates into a reduction of $16 million to $18 million in funding.

Also at the Board of Trustees meeting Thursday:

 

-- The board learned that more than 13,200 students participated in service-learning efforts at UCF in 2006-07. Service learning is a teaching method in which students address a need in the community through volunteer activities that are connected to what they are learning in class. Agencies such as the Orange County Health Department, Ivey Lane Neighborhood Center, The Beta Center and Junior Achievement benefited from the students efforts this past year.

-- Tom Huddleston, vice president for Marketing, Communications and Admissions, reported that UCF will enroll 43 National Merit Scholars in this years first-year class. The 2006-07 first-year class included 28 National Merit Scholars.

-- Bob Holmes, vice president for Development and Alumni Relations, updated the board on the success of fund-raising efforts during the past year. Preliminary numbers show that UCF set records for number of donors (16,047) and in the total gifts received ($61.5 million).

-- Board members approved the conferral of about 2,750 degrees during summer commencement ceremonies, which will be held Saturday, Aug. 4, at the UCF Arena.

-- The board elected Rick Walsh to continue as chair and Tom Yochum as vice-chair.
