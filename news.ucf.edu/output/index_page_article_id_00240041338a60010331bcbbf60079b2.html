See Through the Eyes of a Child ... Students Capture Family, Love and Peace on Film

By Courtesy to UCF April 21, 2005

Photo: Courtesy to UCF

Photos taken by children from the Ivey Lane community will be exhibited at the UCF Downtown Media Arts Center from 5 to 7 p.m. on Monday, April 25. This photo was taken by Diandra, age 14.

Eighteen children, 12 mentors from The Howard Phillips Center for Children & Families, the University of Central Floridas Downtown Media Arts Center and a documentary film crew have combined to create the In the Life of a Child Childrens Photography Project.

 

Elementary, middle and high school students from the Ivey Lane community, will showcase their photography at a professional art gallery opening on Monday, April 25, from 5 to 7 p.m. at the Downtown Media Arts Center, 39 S. Magnolia Ave., Orlando. Tickets cost $50, which includes food and refreshments, and all of the proceeds will benefit the Howard Phillips Center.

 

Participants were asked to show what love, family and peace mean to them through black and white photography. More than 150 rolls of film were taken during the effort sponsored by the Howard Phillips Center and the Downtown Media Arts Center, or DMAC.

 

We are very excited about this project and our collaboration with the Downtown Media Arts Center, said Jill Hamilton, marketing manager of the Howard Phillips Center. It has given children an opportunity to focus on the themes of love, peace and family with their families and in their community.

 

The children were selected by members of Hearts & Hands advisory board, a parent-support initiative of the Howard Phillips Center, which works to prevent child abuse. Hearts & Hands is funded by The Dr. Phillips Foundation.

 

Participants received free cameras and film for the black-and-white photo essay, and they also received support from their community, the DMAC and from the Howard Phillips Center. The three-month project culminates with the fund-raiser to benefit programs and services that operate in the Ivey Lane community. Film students from UCF are documenting the project on film for a short feature that will be shown at the gallery event.

 

For advance tickets, contact Christina Pulley at 407-317-7430 ext. 2244, or at cpulley@orhs.org. Tickets will also be available at the event.
