Diversity Week Honors 'Courage, Commitment and Sacrifice of Freedom Fighters'

By Chad Binette Oct. 20, 2009

Retired Navy Vice Admiral David L. Brewer III

At the beginning of his distinguished career in the U.S. Navy, retired Vice Admiral David L. Brewer III heard many hateful and racist remarks.

A young Mississippi sailor told Brewer on the deck of the USS California that his parents instructed him not to salute African-Americans  -- and he used a very offensive term.

"I had to fight my way through a lot of prejudices," Brewer said Monday during UCF’s annual Diversity Breakfast in the Student Union’s Pegasus Ballroom. "I decided I was going to fight prejudice with the power of my spirit and the power of patriotism."

Brewer's presentation kicked off a week of activities aimed at promoting diversity and inclusion on campus and in the community. The week's theme is focused on veterans -- "Finding Our Way Home: A Salute to the Courage, Commitment and Sacrifice of Freedom Fighters."

At the breakfast, UCF vice president Al Harms, also a retired Navy vice admiral, unveiled a special university effort to honor the service of all veterans. The UCF Foundation is raising private funds to build a "Veterans Commemorative Site"on the south end of Memory Mall.

The site, designed by Navy veteran Don Reynolds of Sanford, will feature a U.S. flag in the center surrounded by a cast-bronze circle. Five marble replicas of the U.S. Armed Forces seals will be sculpted onto horizontal columns extending from the outside to the center of the circle.

Donations can be made online at www.foundation.ucf.edu/vets. UCF will not spend student tuition dollars or state money on the project.

Harms, UCF's vice president for Strategy, Marketing, Communications and Admissions, served with Brewer in the Navy and introduced him on Monday.

Brewer served in the Navy for more than 35 years before retiring and becoming superintendent of the Los Angeles Unified School District, the second-largest district in the country.

Americans have made a lot of progress in fighting racism since the days when he often heard hateful remarks on ships, Brewer said. However, he added, we still need to fight a "tremendous domestic enemy – ignorance, poverty and crime in disadvantaged America."

He praised UCF and former state Rep. Alzo Reddick for starting Soldiers to Scholars, a program that enables veterans to take classes for free at Valencia Community College and UCF, preparing them for careers in teaching and nursing. Soldiers to Scholars students mentor at-risk youths of all ages.

Brewer also lauded UCF trustee and Orlando hotelier Harris Rosen for his dedication to helping the youth of the Tangelo Park neighborhood. Rosen's multimillion dollar effort – a "model for the rest of the nation," according to Brewer -- has provided hundreds of high school students with full college scholarships.

Brewer, who lives in Orlando, is a graduate of Jones High School. He commanded two ships during his distinguished Navy career, and his ships won many awards for combat efficiency and community service. Under his leadership as commander, Military Sealift Command ships executed the sealift logistics for Operations Enduring Freedom and Iraqi Freedom, the largest logistics movement since World War II. They also provided disaster relief for victims of Hurricane Katrina.

During the breakfast, UCF's Office of Diversity Initiatives honored four members of the UCF community with special awards.

Aaron Distler received the President's Student Diversity Leadership Award. A Psychology major, he has been instrumental in providing UCF with important institutional diversity and inclusion best practices and working with the Greek community in support of the Allies program.

Jim Wilkening, director of the Recreation and Wellness Center, received the Unit Diversity Leadership award for his team's success in building a more inclusive and diverse area.

Terry Exum, director of Student Services at UCF Cocoa and Palm Bay, received the Faculty and Staff Diversity Award. She helped to ensure that the Diversity Breakfast and other events are broadcast at regional campuses, and she has organized diversity-related training session for students, staff and faculty.

Michael Swindle, a UCF freshman majoring in graphic design, was recognized for designing the Diversity Week poster.

Diversity Week activities will continue through Friday. The topics of events include Women in the Military, Human Trafficking Awareness, Mindful Cultural Communication and Diversity In and Out of the Real World. For more information and a full schedule of events, visit www.diversity.ucf.edu.
