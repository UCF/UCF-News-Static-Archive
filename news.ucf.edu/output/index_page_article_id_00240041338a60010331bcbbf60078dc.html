Adastra Labs Wins The Joust, UCFs Annual Business Plan Tournament

By UCF Staff April 22, 2005

Photo: Courtesy to UCF

Ricardo Martins (center) is the co-founder and chief executive officer of Adastra Labs, the company that won The Joust, the annual business plan competition held by UCF. Cameron Ford (left), the founding director of the UCF Technology Entrepreneurship Institute, and Stacey Schmidt, a program manager with the UCF Office of Research & Commercialization, also are shown.

Adastra Labs, a company that will provide lightweight, portable headsets that combine real-world scenes with 3-D, virtual reality images, has won The Joust, the University of Central Floridas annual business plan tournament. Adastra Labs recently completed a prototype for the first release of the headset, which can be used in military training simulations, medical imaging and online games.

Adastra Labs is identifying potential markets for its product and looking into the manufacturing process and methods of distribution. By winning The Joust competition last week, the company won $3,000 and assistance from the UCF Technology Incubator for one year.

Twenty teams with 67 participants entered The Joust. Twelve semi-finalists competed on April 14 and 15 and won more than $5,000 in prizes. The winners team name will also appear on a statue that will be displayed in the College of Business Administration.

The Joust competition is designed to challenge entrepreneurial UCF students and faculty to create promising new companies. The participants presented their business plans to a panel of expert judges, and finalists were chosen based on the most feasible business concepts.

Members of the Adastra Labs team were Ricardo Martins, co-founder and chief executive officer; Vesselin Shaoulov, co-founder and chief technology officer; Jannick Rolland, president of research and development; Felix Hamza-Lup, senior computer scientist; and Sandra Pinheiro, sales and marketing.

Martins graduated in 2003 with a masters degree from the UCF College of Optics and Photonics and is pursuing a doctoral degree from the UCF Institute of Simulation and Training. Rolland is a professor at the College of Optics and Photonics. Shaoulov received a doctoral degree from the college last year. Hamza-Lup received a doctoral degree in 2004 from the UCF College of Engineering and Computer Science, and Pinheiro received a bachelors degree in 2002 from the School of Business at Montclair State University in New Jersey.

Other winners of The Joust competition were:

  • 2nd Place
     Nobis Technologies  A small plastic clip that attaches to car windshields and holds an E-Pass/Sun Pass transponder.

  • 3rd Place
     C.L.T - This powerful weapons grade laser will complement and someday replace traditional military weapons thanks in part to its accuracy, reusability and cost effectiveness. 

  • 4th Place  Keyless Enterprises  Innovative, programmable and multi-functional keyless remote for home and auto use.

Lead organizers of the campuswide tournament were Cameron Ford, an associate professor in the College of Business Administration and head of the new Technological Entrepreneurship Institute at UCF, and Stacey Schmidt, program manager for the Center for Entrepreneurship & Technology Commercialization.

Schmidt said the organizers were pleased with the level and breadth of competitors this year. As a direct result of our increased outreach efforts, weve seen participation from across the UCF campus now grow, including teams with more students from science and engineering, she said.

Business leaders who participated as judges were: Edward Alexander, Jim Balaschak Kiran Chhaganlal, William Christy, Michael Cipollaro, Marty Cummins Jr., A. Maxwell Eliscu, Neil Jetter, Beat Kahli, Kelly Lucas, Tom ONeal, Donna Mackenzie, Jerry Nagel, Jerry Ross, David Stepanek and Tony Thorton.

The UCF Technological Entrepreneurship Institute seeks to provide educational opportunities to potential entrepreneurs, inventors, and small business employees.  The organization facilitates academic research related to new venture creation and success.
