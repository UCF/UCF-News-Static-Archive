Hospitality Executives Help Dedicate Rosen School

By Tom Evelyn and Chad Binette February 5, 2004

Photo: Jerry Klein

Hundreds turned out for the first event at the new campus.

Full Photo

Todays leaders of the hospitality industry joined hundreds of others Thursday to officially open the new Rosen School of Hospitality Management, where the industrys leaders of tomorrow are learning their trade at a campus like no other.

"Now we have something that no other program can claim: a gorgeous resort-style building with state-of-the-art technology that just happens to be located in the center of one of the largest tourism destinations in the world," senior Anne Cecil, a hospitality management major, told a standing-room-only crowd in the schools 400-seat Darden Auditorium.

UCF President John Hitt, who presided over the dedication ceremony, thanked Orlando hotelier Harris Rosen and other donors for their support in building the best hospitality school "in the known universe."

Rosen, a UCF trustee, and his wife, Trisha, donated $18.2 million for the new campus, which opened Jan. 5 in the heart of the Central Florida tourism corridor. The Rosens have donated another $1.1 million for scholarships for the school.

The $28.1 million campus features high-tech laboratories for food and beverage preparation and a library stocked with hospitality-related materials in a 159,000-square-foot building. Students will be able to live in on-campus apartments that should be finished by the fall of 2005.

"This educational building is more than the sum total of its brick and mortar parts," school Dean Abe Pizam told the audience. "It is composed of the values and beliefs of its designers, the greatness of its donor and the spirit, ideals and ambitions of its inhabitants."

For the first time since the Rosen School was established in 1983, its enrollment exceeds 1,000. The school has about 965 undergraduates and 50 graduate students this semester.

Several groups, including the Orlando/Orange County Convention and Visitors Bureau and the executive board of the Central Florida Hotel and Lodging Association, already have held meetings on the new Rosen School campus.

In addition to Harris and Trisha Rosen, donors include the Orlando/Orange County Convention and Visitors Bureau, $5 million; Darden Restaurants Inc., $2,000,001; Walt Disney World Co., $2 million; Universal Orlando Foundation, $1 million; the Anheuser-Busch Foundation/Sea World, $500,000; the J. Willard and Alice S. Marriott Foundation, $100,000; the American Resort Development Association International Foundation Inc., $100,000; the Hospitality Financial and Technology Professionals, $100,000; and the Central Florida Hotel and Lodging Association, $50,000.

UCFs school of hospitality management was established in 1983 as part of the College of Business. After the Rosen School became independent in 2000, it continued to operate on UCFs main campus in Orlando.

"We are currently celebrating UCFs 40th anniversary," Hitt said Thursday. "Its theme, From Promise to Prominence, also describes the remarkable journey our hospitality program has taken over the years. The opening of the Rosen School is a major milestone in the history of the university and our region  and the beginning of a new era in hospitality leadership and education."
