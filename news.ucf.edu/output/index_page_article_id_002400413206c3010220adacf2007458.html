Board of Trustees to Receive Feasibility Reports for Med School, Stadium

By UCF Staff March 29, 2005

  • Tom Evelyn

    • 407-823-5988

  • UCF Board of Trustees

  • Stadium Update

  • President, Athletic Director Unveil Renderings of Proposed Stadium

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Florida Board of Trustees on Thursday, March 31, will receive feasibility studies for a proposed medical school and an on-campus football stadium.

The board also will consider approving pursuing the medical school, and requesting the permission of the state Board of Governors to build one, at its regular meeting in the Cape Florida Ballroom at the Student Union. Committee meetings will begin at 8:30 a.m.; the full board will meet at 1 p.m.

The medical school study, which was conducted by MGT of America, addresses, among other things, the need for more physicians in Florida and why Central Florida is an ideal site for a new medical school.

The stadium feasibility study is on the agenda of the trusteesÂ’ Finance Committee, which meets at 11 a.m. Thursday, and the full board meeting, which begins at 1 p.m. During the full board meeting, trustees will hear public comments on the issue from residents.

The trustees are scheduled to meet again on April 14, when they may decide if they want to build a stadium on campus.

In the meantime, the university will host a community forum from 6 to 9 p.m. April 11 at the Student Union to discuss plans for the proposed stadium and allow for more comments from the public. UCF has also planned about a dozen meetings with homeowners groups through April and two informal, community information sessions from 6 to 8 p.m. April 13 at Lawton Chiles Middle School and April 21 at Carillon Elementary School.

Also on Thursday, the trustees will be asked to approve the collective bargaining agreements that the university has negotiated with United Faculty of Florida, the union representing UCF faculty members, and the American Federation of State, County and Municipal Employees, a union that represents staff members.

New trustees Al Weiss, president of the Walt Disney World Resort, and Tico Perez, an Orlando attorney with Baker & Hostetler, will attend their first board meeting Thursday. Weiss and Perez, both of whom are UCF alumni, replace outgoing trustees Gerri Ferris and Rick Lee.
