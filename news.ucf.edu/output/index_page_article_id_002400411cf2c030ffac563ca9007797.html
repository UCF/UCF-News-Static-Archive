UCF Forum to Focus on Successes, Flaws of Growth in Central Florida

Nov. 8, 2004

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Keisha Biggs

    • 407-823-0935

  • Metropolitan Center for Regional Studies

  • Print this Page

A former Florida Department of Community Affairs secretary and former directors of Orlandos and Orange Countys planning departments will headline a University of Central Florida forum Wednesday, Nov. 10, analyzing the regions growth during the last two to three decades.

 

Linda Loomis Shelley, who served as secretary of the Department of Community Affairs from 1992 to 1995 and was the governors chief of staff from 1995 to 1999, will give one of the main presentations at the forum, which will be from 8 to 11 a.m. at The Ballroom at Church Street, 129 W. Church St.

 

A Look Back at Growth and Central Florida: A Retrospective: What Worked and What Didnt? is free and open to the public. Registration will begin at 7:30 a.m. Call 407-823-2741 or e-mail metro@mail.ucf.edu to reserve a seat.

 

The Metropolitan Center for Regional Studies at UCF organized the forum, which is sponsored by Avalon Park.

 

Speakers include Rick Bernhardt, executive director of the Metropolitan Nashville-Davidson County Planning Department and a former Orlando planning director, and Bruce McClendon, director of Planning & Growth Management for Hillsborough County and a former director of Orange Countys Growth Management Department.
