UCF, Turtle Expert Peter Pritchard Partner for Exhibit Celebrating Darwin's 200th Birthday

June 8, 2009

Related Contacts and Links:

    • 407-977-5004

The University of Central Florida is partnering with Orlando tortoise and turtle expert Peter Pritchard to present a special exhibit dedicated to turtles, tortoises and other species from the Galapagos Islands at the UCF Library.

The exhibit, which runs through June 30, provides an intriguing look into Pritchard’s extensive turtle research for more than 30 years, particularly his work in the Galapagos Islands, located off the coast of Ecuador. The exhibit also showcases more than 50 photos, fossils, artwork and artifact displays from Pritchard’s extensive museum collection at his Oviedo-based Chelonian Research Institute.

The UCF exhibit, which is free and open to the public, is on the second floor of the UCF Library. The exhibit is part of a worldwide effort to celebrate the many accomplishments of Charles Darwin, who was born 200 years ago.

Pritchard is regarded worldwide for his turtle and tortoise research, as well as his global conservation work.  His institute houses the world’s third-largest turtle and tortoise museum collection and features 270 of the world’s 300 recognized species of turtles, along with library and reference facilities. The institute is also home to notable residents such as 35 year-old “Bernice” and 23-year-old “Moses,” both rare giant Galapagos tortoises weighing 150 to 200 pounds each.

Pritchard’s institute has garnered international acclaim as one of the foremost turtle and research institutions. His worldwide conservation efforts -- characterized as conservation without confrontation -- have led to invitations by governments of several nations to develop socially sensitive programs for wildlife conservation. He has been named “Hero for the Planet” by Time Magazine, “Champion of the Wild” by Discovery Channel and "Central Floridian of the Year" by the Orlando Sentinel. He has authored seven books, and his research has taken him to nearly 100 countries and territories. Three species of turtle are named after him – a snakeneck turtle from New Guinea, a pond turtle from northern Burma and a giant fossil sideneck turtle from Colombia.

Pritchard is preparing to embark on another research expedition to the Galapagos Islands this summer.

Call 407-823-5880 for more information about the exhibit.

Established by Pritchard in 1997, the Chelonian Institute is a private, nonprofit organization dedicated to the research and conservation of turtles and tortoises throughout the world. The institute features a research facility, library, offices, museum, scholar residence and a sanctuary housing both captive and wild groups of living turtle species. For more information, call 407-366-4020 or visit www.chelonianresearch.wordpress.com.
