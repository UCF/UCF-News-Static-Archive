Physical Plant Engineer Honored as September Employee of the Month

By Jessica Saggio Oct. 17, 2007

Photo: Courtesy of Human Resources

Saul Santiago from Physical Plant is the September USPS Employee of the Month.

Full Photo

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Employee of the Month Web site

  • Print this Page

For September employee of the month Saul Santiago, bringing his colleagues closer together is just the sugar on top of his achievements as a senior engineer in the Physical Plant.

Known as a "go-to" person at the Physical Plant, Santiago received the September Employee of the Month honors. Santiago is one of the many valued employees behind the scenes working for the Physical Plant to keep the UCF learning environment as comfortable as possible. Among Santiago's duties are managing controls for temperature and humidity in campus buildings and supervising control technicians.

"During the time Saul has been here, I have seen tremendous improvement in the work ethic and commitment of the control technicians which he supervises. They have become a close-knit group focused on furthering customer service," one of Santiago's co-workers wrote in his nomination letter.

Santiago will receive a Barnes & Noble gift card, UCF apparel, a goody bag and lunch with and an introduction to the UCF Board of Trustees. He is also eligible for the USPS Employee of the Year award. One of the 12 USPS Employees of the Month will be named Employee of the Year and will receive a $2,500 check.

Other recent Employee of the Month winners are:

August: Tracy Balkham, Student Union office assistant. Her duties include answering the hundreds of phone calls her office gets daily and making sure the student assistants are properly trained. Her co-workers state that "Tracy continually goes the extra mile for our customers and takes on every opportunity to increase the knowledge she already has in her position. Every office should have a Tracy."

July: Cathy Baust, senior secretary for Management Information Systems. Her duties include coordinating internships and software administration on top of day-to-day secretarial work. Her co-workers state that, "Cathy is a true UCF employee. She promotes and supports UCF in any way she can. I would hate to lose Cathy."

June: Margarita Garay, office manager of Course Development and Web Services. Her co-workers state, "Ms. Garay is the face of CDWS. It is my firm conviction CDWS would not be what it is today if not for her years of dedication."

May: Pauline Strauss, executive secretary for Civil and Environmental Engineering. Her duties include processing travel and coordinating faculty searches. Her co-workers state, "Pauline is so good that I cannot think of adequate words to describe her work. It is comforting to me to know that she is handling something, as I am assured that it will be done, done correctly, and on time."

April: John Morris, maintenance supervisor of the Student Union.  His co-workers state, "The maintenance and custodial staff at the Student Union is truly like a big family. A great deal of that can be attributed to the fact that John is always there to lend support when someone is in need as well as tell a joke to lighten the mood. I cannot say enough about how much of a positive influence John is on all of the customers and staff here at the Student Union."

The March Employee of the Month winner was Jeffery Golub from Judaic Studies, and Melanie David from Continuing Education won the award in February.

The Employee of the Month winners are selected by a committee comprised of staff and faculty. To nominate an employee, fill out a form available on the Human Resources website at http://hr.ucf.edu/web/employeerecognition/EOM.shtml. For more information about the Employee of the Month process, call 407-823-0440.
