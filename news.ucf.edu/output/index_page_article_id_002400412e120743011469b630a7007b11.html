UCF Prepares for Grand Opening of New UCF Arena This Weekend

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala Sept. 7, 2007

Photo: Jacque Brund

The new UCF Arena includes 10,000 seats and is one of many new amenities on the north side of UCF's main campus.

The University of Central Florida campus is getting ready for the grand opening of the new UCF Arena on Saturday, Sept. 8.

Crews are busy this week putting the final touches on the 10,000-seat facility. They are placing flat-screen televisions throughout the building, trimming trees that dot the front of the 280,000 square-foot facility and making sure each of the 180 toilets flush.

"We're really excited," said Ben Gibbs, assistant general manager of the UCF Arena. "We can't wait to share this with the students and the community. It's really a fantastic facility."

Looking out the windows of the UCF Arena atrium, a visitor will see Memory Mall, a green lawn area that links the Arena area with the Student Union and is a mini-version of the "Mall" in Washington, D.C.

Inside the Arena, all of the guests will have a great view of country music star Reba McEntire, who opens the Arena on Saturday. The community is invited to see the arena at no cost during an open house from noon to 4 p.m. on Sunday, Sept. 9.

The Arena's 16 suites offer prime seating and special features such as a party area, cushioned seats and a private entrance. The suites are for basketball season ticket holders, but if those owners don't want to attend other events, their suites become available to the public. There are also two party suites available for all games and performances. Those suites include a living room area, flat-screen televisions and space for dozens of people.

Performances that are already booked include comedian Bill Cosby and Sesame Street Live, and arena staff are busy searching for a variety of acts to fill the calendar.

"Our first few shows are a little bit of everything," Gibbs said. "That's really what we're trying to do here, have shows that draw a variety of people -- students, faculty, staff and the community at large."

Patrons driving to the UCF Arena can use any entrance to campus, and UCF Police and parking personnel will be on hand to direct traffic.

A new, 700-space parking garage that was completed behind the arena earlier this summer will help to accommodate Arena guests. That garage will be used exclusively for event parking, said Krishna Singh, assistant director of Parking and Transportation Services.

Overflow parking will be directed to Garage D, which will be reserved for events when there is a need for those spaces. Parking in either of the garages for Arena events will cost guests $5 or $8, depending on the event, even if they have UCF parking permits. Patrons with UCF parking stickers can use other parking lots or garages at no cost.

"We're working closely with Arena folks to determine ticket sales and the need for parking," Singh said. That partnership will continue throughout the year.

Mass e-mails will be sent out to students, faculty and staff when it is anticipated that Garage D will be needed, Singh added. Flyers also will be posted in that garage before closures take effect.

Singh also said that once parking lot D1 next to the garage is paved and open to the public later this month, no overnight parking will be allowed in garage D. The rule will be enforced, he said.

Students who live in the Towers will be able to enter and leave their parking garages during Arena events.

The new UCF Arena is one of many additions to the northeast section of UCF's main campus. Four new residence halls with 500 beds each are located near the Arena, and there is more than 87,000 square feet of retail space in that area. Also, Bright House Networks Stadium will open Sept. 15 when the UCF Knights face the Texas Longhorns in the first-ever college football game on campus.

For more information on the Arena, visit http://www.ucfarena.com.
