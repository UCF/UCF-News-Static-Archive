UCF Honors Outstanding Faculty, Students at Founders Day

By UCF Staff April 13, 2005

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Chad Binette

    • 407-823-6312

  • College of Educations Hynes Wins Pegasus Professor Award

  • UCF Names 14 Professors Emeriti

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Florida honored outstanding faculty members and students in several categories during the annual Founders Day honors convocation on Wednesday.

 

Excellence in Undergraduate Teaching:

 

Overall winner: Karen Verkler, associate professor in the College of Education. Verkler, who has taught at UCF since 1995, has served as the coordinator of the Foreign Language Education Program since 1998. She graduated from UCF with a masters degree in counselor education from UCF in 1988. Dean Sandra Robinson praised her energy, knowledge and can-do attitude, along with her devotion to her students.

 

College of Arts and Sciences: Costas John Efthimiou (Physics), Jana L. Jasinski (Sociology and Anthropology), Barry J. Mauer (English), Christopher L. Parkinson (Biology), Burt Pryor (Nicholson School of Communication), Blake Scott (English), Peter Telep (English) and Terry Ann Thaxton (English)      

 

College of Business Administration: Charles Kelliher and Pamela Roush, both of the School of Accounting

 

College of Education: Verkler and William Gaudelli (Teaching and Learning Principles)

 

College of Engineering and Computer Science: Larry Chew (Mechanical, Materials, and Aerospace Engineering) and Amr A. Oloufa (Civil and Environmental Engineering)

 

College of Health and Public Affairs: Lisa L. Smith (School of Nursing)

 

Rosen College of Hospitality Management: Peter Ricci (Hospitality Operations)

 

 

Excellence in Graduate Teaching

 

Overall winner: John Weishampel, associate professor of biology. Weishampel began teaching at UCF in 1995 after he received masters and doctoral degrees in environmental sciences from the University of Virginia. He strives to show students how science can make contributions to society and works with faculty members from about 40 other colleges and universities through the Faculty Institutes for Reforming Science Teaching to help students better learn science.

 

College of Arts and Sciences: Weishampel

 

College of Education: Enrique Ortiz (Teaching and Learning Principles)

 

College of Engineering and Computer Science: Linda C. Malone (Industrial Engineering and Management Systems)

 

College of Health and Public Affairs: Janice Z. Peterson (School of Nursing)

 

College of Optics and Photonics: Patrick LiKamWa

 

 

Excellence in Distinguished Research:

 

Overall winner: Martin Richardson, professor, College of Optics and Photonics. Richardson is the Northrop Grumman Professor of X-ray Photonics and a professor of Physics and Electrical and Computer Engineering. Richardson directs a research program on the development of new high-power lasers and their applications to dense laser plasma studies and X-ray generation. One of Richardsons research specialties, extreme ultraviolet lithography, involves the imprinting of smaller, denser features on computer chips to make them more powerful.

 

College of Arts and Sciences: David L. Brunner (Music) and Keith Folse (Foreign Languages and Literatures)

 

College of Education: Juli K. Dixon (Teaching and Learning Principles)

 

College of Engineering and Computer Science: Gour-Tsyh George Yeh (Civil and Environmental Engineering)

 

College of Health and Public Affairs: Karen E. Dennis (School of Nursing)

 

College of Optics and Photonics: Richardson

 

Rosen College of Hospitality Management: Dana V. Tesone (Tourism, Attractions, and Events)

 

Institutes and Centers: Yongho Sohn (AMPAC)

 

 

University Award for Excellence in Faculty Advising: Lynn McConnell Hepner, College of Arts and Sciences. Heppner coordinates undergraduate advising for the English Department and holds advising workshops for new English faculty members.

 

 

University Award for Excellence in Professional Advising: Paul Viau, First Year Advising and Exploration, Student Development and Enrollment Services. Viau is credited with improving the computer-based advising technology at UCF.

 

 

University Awards for Excellence in Professional Service: Kerstin Hamann and Tracy L. Dietz, both of the College of Arts and Sciences. Hamann has taught political science at UCF since 1995 and previously was assistant chairman of the department. She also has been a fellow with the Faculty Center for Teaching and Learning and has worked as an editor and book reviewer for several journals. Dietz, who arrived at UCF in 1997, teaches sociology and has worked extensively on assessments for the department. She also has served on several College of Arts and Sciences committees and has volunteered with the Second Harvest Food Bank, Harbor House and the Coalition for the Homeless.

 

 

Excellence in Librarianship: Elizabeth Killingsworth, a reference librarian, is praised as a natural teacher who is approachable and a good communicator with faculty and students.
