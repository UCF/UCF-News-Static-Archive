UCF-Led Mission to England Could Spawn New, Expanded Partnerships

By Johanna Marizan and Sarah Ridley Updated Sept. 12, 2006

Photo: Courtesy of UCF Global Perspectives

The Institute for Ageing and Health in Newcastle was one of the stops on the UCF presidential mission to England this summer.

As UCF begins a new academic year, it is already benefiting from a university-led summer mission to the United Kingdom. The UCF community will see the results of the mission in many ways, from British visitors to campus to new and expanding partnerships with United Kingdom organizations.

Given the UCF Global Perspectives Offices university-wide theme for 2006-2007, The Future of Europe, the timing is ideal for such connections.

Gen. James Jones, the commander of NATOs military forces in Europe, will speak during the first Europe-related event on Sept. 21. He will speak at 2 p.m. at the Fairwinds Alumni Center.

Jones leads Allied Command Europe, which consists of NATO's military forces throughout the continent. The command's goal is to preserve the peace, security and territorial integrity of NATO member nations in Europe.

In the spring, Cherie Booth Blair, the wife of British Prime Minister Tony Blair and a lawyer with the Matrix Chambers firm in London, has been invited to visit UCF. The academic year also will feature a UCF-hosted conference on the future of Europe and a visit by the dean of the University of Newcastle upon Tynes medical school.

The United Kingdom mission was requested last year by President John Hitt. In part, it developed from an invitation by Sir David Manning, Britains ambassador to the United States, during a trip to Central Florida.

An illness prevented Hitt from leading the mission team, but 18 other Central Floridians made a whirlwind series of stops from London to Sedgefield Borough to Newcastle upon Tyne to Durham to Oxford. The teams goals were to explore several areas of critical relevance to UCF and the Orlando region. These included: partnership possibilities with complementary institutions, especially in the areas of science, medicine, high technology, energy and global issues; policy matters, such as trade, counter-terrorism and U.S.-British relations; and business opportunities.

The seven UCF participants included Vice President for Finance and Administration William Merck, who stood in for President Hitt; Vice President for Research and Commercialization M.J. Soileau; Ambassador Harriet Elam-Thomas; Special Assistant to the President John C. Bersia; and Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy Executive Director Ben Noel. Other participants included Jacob Stuart, president of the Orlando Regional Chamber of Commerce, and Sibille Pritchard, president of the Global Connections Foundation, chair of the Florida Arts Council and senior vice president of Brooksville Development Corporation.

We came away with an understanding of how important international relationships and recognition are in terms of building a university and the region, Merck said.

Bersia added that the mission provided invaluable networking, information-sharing and partnership-building opportunities for all participants. It will help UCF move more quickly toward its goals of international emphasis and prominence in curricula, research and programs.

At the British Foreign and Commonwealth Office in London, the group received briefings from experts on the ideologies, tactics and long-term strategies of terrorists. According to Bersia, whose office oversees the Terrorism Studies Program at UCF, that information was extremely useful for us in our planning for future events and research. Also, it gave us a privileged perspective as the August 2006 airline-terror plot unfolded in the United Kingdom.

Another stop at the U.S. Embassy in London provided insights into the American-British relationship, particularly in the areas of science, technology and energy. Elam-Thomas said the information shared was indeed beneficial to those members of the delegation who seldom have such access. It provided them with a realistic view of the more substantive U.S. embassy role abroad.

An unexpected surprise of the U.S. Embassy visit was that a member of the briefing team had graduated from UCF. Lt. Col. Richard Gibbons (87) is an assistant U.S. Army attache at the embassy.

The mission continued in northern England, with a stop in Prime Minister Tony Blairs parliamentary district at NetPark, a new science facility dedicated to knowledge-based industries. This visit expanded the groups awareness in two ways. One was learning about the level of British research in critical areas such as energy and the environment. The other was developing an understanding of how the British government had recently divided the country into zones, with a regional development agency for each one.

The agency for this region was One Northeast, which hosted the mission during its entire stay in that part of the country and arranged meetings with multiple businesses and chambers of commerce. The overall experience was so positive that One Northeast plans to send a similar delegation to Central Florida in the near future.

There will be many positive business prospects and exciting learning opportunities presented by some type of future exchange with this newly created Regional Development Area (RDA) of the United Kingdom, said Stuart, of the Orlando Regional Chamber of Commerce. The Central Florida region has much to learn, and in the months and years ahead we would benefit greatly from focusing our efforts on partnering with the economic regeneration work now being successfully advanced by One Northeast.

With an eye toward the recently approved medical school for UCF, the mission had a strong health sciences focus. It included stops at the Institute for Ageing and Health at the Wolfson Research Centre of Newcastle General Hospital; the Stem Cell Research Institute of Durham University; and the University of Newcastle upon Tyne, which had its origins in a school of medicine and surgery established in 1834.

Oliver James, pro vice chancellor of Medical Sciences, and Reg Jordan, dean of International Medical Education at Newcastle, discussed the medical schools history, priorities and organization, as well as its advising of emerging medical schools in other countries. Jordan accepted an invitation to visit UCF and Central Florida in the spring.

In Oxford, the delegation visited Vicon Peaks offices and studios. Vicon Peak offers a complete range of products for motion-tracking needs, from 2D video analysis to the high accuracy of 3D digital optical systems.

The visit to the headquarters of Vicon Motion Systems in Oxford validated the next-generation direction that UCFs School of Film and Digital Media is entering, Noel said, explaining that Vicon is the parent company of House of Moves, Inc., the motion-capture studio company that will be partnering with UCF to build and operate a state-of-the-art studio at the downtown Orlando campus.

The technologies in the production and development of motion capture, 3D and geo-space imaging will enable UCF to position itself as a leader in university-based production and teaching, Noel said. In addition, opportunities in research for film, interactive technology and life sciences should spawn from the partnership. 

Tyler Fisher, UCFs first Rhodes Scholar, joined the mission team for a high-table dinner at Oxford Universitys Magdalen College. A discussion with the Said Business Schools Tim Cullen, Director of the Oxford Programme on Negotiation, opened up the possibility of jointly sponsored seminars at UCF.

Cullen is planning to participate in the UCF conference on Europe in November. The conference, held in cooperation with the National Conference of Editorial Writers and other partners, will be Nov. 13. The theme will be The Future of Europe: The Ties That Bind and Divide.

The UCF-led mission to the United Kingdom was co-sponsored by the Global Connections Foundation, UCFs main regional partner in building international awareness and understanding. It was facilitated and assisted by the British Embassy in Washington, D.C., other British offices in the United States, the British Foreign and Commonwealth Office in London and One Northeast.

For further information about the UCF-led mission to the United Kingdom or the university-wide focus on Europe for 2006-2007, please contact the Global Perspectives Office at 407-823-0688.
