UCF Offers 200 Scholarships to Low-Income, First-Generation College Students

By UCF Staff Dec. 6, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Lisa Minnick, UCF Office of Student Financial Assistance

    • 407-823-5393

  • Office of Student Financial Assistance

  • TG's Public Benefit Grant Program

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Florida is awarding 200 first-generation undergraduates $400,000 in scholarships this year to ease student debt and offer better access to higher-education opportunities.

UCF was awarded half of the scholarship funding through TG’s Public Benefit Grant Program, a philanthropic nonprofit that helps low- to middle-income students as an administrator of the Federal Family Education Loan Program.

As part of its “BorrowLess Grant” program, UCF will offer the $2,000 annual scholarships to first-generation undergraduate students who are financially needy, but who do not qualify for the Federal Pell Grant or the Florida Assistant Grant. University officials believe the scholarships will preclude many from taking out private student loans, in turn reducing their college debt.

“Many students are faced with having to pay their college costs solely with loans because they do not qualify for the (federal) grant programs,” said Thomas Huddleston, vice president for Marketing, Communications and Admissions. “TG’s Public Benefit Grant Program offered us the opportunity to assist some of these students from low-to-moderate-income families from accumulating such a burden of debt.”

UCF was one of 46 universities and nonprofits selected for funding through TG’s competitive grant program Overall, TG awarded more than $5.2 million for projects in 2007-08.

“Students who are the first in their family to attend college often face many challenges in pursuing an education – especially if they have high financial need,” said Jacob Fraire, TG’s assistant vice president for educational alliances. “In evaluating the many proposals we received this year, we were particularly pleased to see that the University of Central Florida is actively pursuing strategies to reduce student indebtedness among these students. We are happy to support UCF’s “BorrowLess Grant” program, which advances our mutual objective in helping first-generation college students achieve their educational goals.”

TG is a public, nonprofit organization that helps create access to higher education for millions of families and students through its role as an administrator of the Federal Family Education Loan Program (FFELP). Its vision is to be the premier source of information, financing and assistance to help all families and students realize their educational and career dreams. Additional information about TG can be found at http://www.tgslc.org.

UCF Stands for Opportunity: Established in 1963, the University of Central Florida is a diverse metropolitan research university that ranks as the 6th-largest in the country with more than 48,000 students. Located in Orlando, Fla., UCF offers high-quality undergraduate and graduate education, student development and continuing education, while conducting cutting-edge research that powers the region's economic development.
