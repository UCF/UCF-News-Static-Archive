Actors Are Dressed For The Occasion In SummerStage 08's 'Leading Ladies'

By UCF Staff July 11, 2008

Photo: Christopher Niess

Mark Koenig and Kyle Adkins portray disguised Shakespearean actors Jack and Leo in SummerStage 08's production of "Leading Ladies."

Full Photo

  • Mark Brotherton, UCF Conservatory Theatre

    • 407-261-0123

  • UCF Conservatory Theatre

  • Print this Page

SummerStage 08’s final offering is the Broadway comedy hit, “Leading Ladies,” the story of two down-on-their luck British Shakespearean actors touring the Amish countryside.

After getting rave reviews around the country, Ken Ludwig’s comedy comes to the UCF Conservatory Theatre starting Thursday, July 10, and runs through Sunday, July 20.

The characters referenced in the show’s title are named Jack and Leo, a duo touring the Moose Lodge circuit in Pennsylvania. When they hear about a dying, wealthy old matron searching for her two long-lost nieces and heirs, hilarity, romantic entanglements and mistaken identity abound.

The cast includes Kate and Tad Ingram, who both appeared in SummerStage ‘07. Kate Ingram is known to area audience through her appearances at the Orlando Shakespeare Theater, in “A Little Night Music,” “Tartuffe” and “Hamlet,” among others. She is also the artistic director and an associate professor at the UCF Conservatory Theatre.

Tad Ingram has also performed at the Orlando Shakespeare Theatre. He has appeared on cast album recordings, acted for producers and directors such as Hal Prince, Julie Styne, Jason Robert Brown, Joe Papp, Des McAnuff, and Alfred Uhry on Broadway and Off-Broadway and performed in nearly every state.

Performances will be Thursday, July 10, through Sunday, July 13, and Thursday, July 17, through Sunday, July 20, at the Mainstage theatre. All shows begin at 8 p.m., except for Sunday matinees, which will start at 2 p.m. “Leading Ladies” contains some mildly sexually suggestive situations.

Ticket prices are $17 for adults, $15 for seniors over 55 and $10 for students with ID. Reservations are recommended and available through the UCF Conservatory Theatre box office by calling 407-823-1500. The box office can assist with accommodations for patrons with disabilities.

“Leading Ladies” will also kick off the fall season of the UCF Conservatory Theatre on Thursday, Aug. 21.
