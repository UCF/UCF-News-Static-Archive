UCF Students, Families Celebrate Graduation Day

By UCF Staff Updated Dec. 19, 2009

Photo: Jacque Brund

Graduates celebrated with family and friends during two commencement ceremonies Friday.

Thousands of University of Central Florida graduates and their families celebrated during three ceremonies Friday and Saturday, and President John Hitt awarded the 25,000th master’s degree of his 17-year career at UCF.

More than 3,900 anticipated fall graduates included 3,176 students who filed for bachelor’s degrees, 660 for master’s degrees, seven for education specialist degrees and 79 for doctoral degrees.

During Friday’s morning ceremony for graduates of the College of Business Administration, College of Engineering and Computer Science and College of Optics and Photonics, Hitt also awarded an honorary doctoral degree to Jerry Roth.

Roth is the owner of Hill Dermaceuticals in Sanford, which develops and markets products for skin diseases in children. Calling Roth an “enthusiastic champion for academic and athletics programs” at UCF, Hitt thanked him for his generous support of the university over three decades.

Roth was instrumental in UCF’s dream of building an on-campus football stadium, and the Roth Tower at Bright House Networks Stadium bears his name.

A UCF alumnus and College of Business Administration Hall of Fame inductee, Andrew Titen addressed the morning graduates. Titen is president and CEO of Tampa-based Bisk Education, one of the nation’s largest providers of online degree and certificate programs for universities.

Amazed at how much the university has changed since his graduation in the 1970s, Titen encouraged Friday’s graduates to “believe in yourself,” keep a “positive mental attitude” in life and work, and most importantly, find a job about which you’re passionate.

“The degree you’re getting today may not lead to the career you’ll have in the future,” he said. Titen urged graduates to be flexible, “can-do people” in their job searches to stand out.

Friday’s afternoon ceremony featured graduates from the College of Arts and Humanities, College of Graduate Studies, College of Health and Public Affairs, Office of Undergraduate Studies and Rosen College of Hospitality Management.

State Senate President-elect Mike Haridopolos told the graduates that despite today’s struggles, the future holds many opportunities.

“We are facing a challenging time, maybe like never before,” he said. “With crisis comes opportunity, and that’s what America is all about.”

Haridopolos also praised UCF’s accomplishments as an “educational leader in so many fields” and a great source of opportunity for the region.

Saturday's ceremony featured graduates from the College of Education, College of Medicine’s Burnett School of Biomedical Sciences, College of Nursing and College of Sciences. The speaker was U.S. Rep. Suzanne Kosmas, who represents portions of Orange, Seminole, Brevard and Volusia counties.

Kosmas told the graduates about one of her grandfather's favorite sayings. He passed away in the 1960s, but his advice remains a strong guiding force for Kosmas' family.

"You have a space to fill -- fill it full," Kosmas said, adding that her grandfather's advice covers their personal growth and motivations, protecting the natural world for the sake of future generations and supporting their communities. 

UCF has awarded more than 198,000 degrees since classes began in 1968. President Hitt has awarded more than 142,000 of those degrees.

Webcasts of the three ceremonies are available at http://webcast.oir.ucf.edu.
