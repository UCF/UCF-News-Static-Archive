UCF Business Undergraduate Programs Ranked 60th in Nation

By UCF Staff May 15, 2006

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Chad Binette, News & Information

    • 407-823-6312

  • UCF College of Business Administration

  • BusinessWeek

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Floridas College of Business Administration ranked 60th in the nation in BusinessWeeks first-ever rankings of undergraduate business programs. The rankings were published in the May 8 issue.

BusinessWeek examined 1,400 U.S. colleges and universities and reviewed business programs accredited by the Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business. Criteria included SAT scores, selectivity in admissions and the number of students from the top 10 percent of their high school classes.

The median starting salaries of graduates and placement rates into top Master of Business Administration programs were among the other factors considered. BusinessWeek also surveyed students and corporate recruiters.

The college has invested in attracting and retaining high-quality faculty and students, said Dean Thomas Keon. We are pleased that BusinessWeek has recognized the efforts of our dedicated faculty and the achievements of our hard-working students.

The University of Pennsylvania topped the rankings of undergraduate business programs. The University of Miami ranked 44th, the highest rating among Florida universities. The University of Florida ranked 47th; Florida State University was 54th.

The UCF College of Business Administration enrolled 8,807 students in fall 2005. For more information on the college, go to www.bus.ucf.edu. For more information on the BusinessWeek rankings, go to www.businessweek.com.
