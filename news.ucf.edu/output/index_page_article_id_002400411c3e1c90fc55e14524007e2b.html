Wisconsin Festival to Feature Launching of Florida Space Institute Rocket

By Chad Binette May 13, 2004

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Carol Lutz (Rockets for Schools)

The debut of football coach George OLeary wont be the only highly anticipated appearance by UCF in Wisconsin this year.

 

Four months before OLearys Golden Knights open the football season at the University of Wisconsin in Madison, a rocket launch by the UCF-run Florida Space Institute will headline a Rockets for Schools festival 130 miles away in Sheboygan.

 

About 350 sixth through 12th graders from Wisconsin, Illinois, Iowa and Michigan are expected to participate in the festival Friday, May 14, and Saturday, May 15. The UCF rocket, which was once used by NASA to get weather data before space shuttle flights, will be launched on Saturday afternoon, after students launch dozens of their own rockets.

 

While the small rocket, which is 4 inches in diameter, wouldnt attract much attention at Cape Canaveral, people out in the Midwest just dont get to see rocket launches often, so its a big deal for them, said Bob Crabbs, associate director of the Florida Space Institute.

 

Members of the Wisconsin Air National Guard arrived Friday, May 7, to begin transporting the Super-Loki Dart rocket, to the Badger State. The festival is covering the costs of the transport and the launch itself.

 

Until a few years ago, NASA sent the Super-Loki Dart rocket into space before shuttle flights to get weather data such as wind speeds and directions. At about 300,000 feet, a package of weather instruments was released. The instruments then descended to earth on a parachute.

 

The rockets booster is 4 inches in diameter and 6 feet long, while the dart portion is about 3 feet long and has a diameter of 1.25 inches.

 

The Florida Space Institute received the rocket and an accompanying trailer and van for free two years ago from the Florida Space Authority, a state agency that strives to expand and diversify Floridas space-related industry. The institute conducts a few launches a year. Its only other out-of-state launch was last year in southern Texas.

 

Students at the Rockets for Schools festival will have a chance to ask questions about the rocket before it is launched. The Florida Space Institute and the University of Central Florida consider the launch to be a great way to help students learn more about space while introducing them to the types of research that the institute and university conduct.

 

Rockets for Schools festivities also include a visit from retired astronaut Winston Scott and educational sessions about rockets. Go to www.rockets4schools.org for more information about the event. Go to http://fsi.ucf.edu/ for more information about the Florida Space Institute.
