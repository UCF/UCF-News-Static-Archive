Homecoming Kicks Off With Family-Friendly Carnival and 'Movie Knight'

By Christine Dellert Oct. 24, 2007

Photo: UCF News File

UCF's Spirit Splash on Nov. 2 will draw hundreds to the Reflecting Pond.

Full Photo

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Christine Dellert

    • 407-823-2947

  • UCF Homecoming

  • UCF Alumni Association

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Florida’s Homecoming Week kicks off Saturday, Oct. 27, with a family-friendly carnival followed by a free movie on Memory Mall and sold-out rock concert next week.
 
The UCF Homecoming Carnival runs from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Saturday, Oct. 27, and Sunday, Oct. 28, in the Visual Arts Building parking lot, at the intersection of Gemini Boulevard and Centaurus Drive West.
 
The carnival is family friendly with rides for all ages. And it’s free for UCF students with valid IDs. Others can purchase tickets for $1 each or in larger packs at reduced prices, with three to five tickets required for most rides.
 
The public is also invited to a free showing of “I Now Pronounce You Chuck and Larry” at 8 p.m. Monday, Oct. 29, on Memory Mall, across from the new UCF Arena. More than 150 pounds of cheddar- and caramel-flavored popcorn will be served during the PG-13 rated comedy starring Adam Sandler and Kevin James.
 
Emo-rock band, Fall Out Boy is headlining a sold-out show Nov. 1 at the UCF Arena. About 2,500 tickets will still be handed out to students during Homecoming events. The show starts at 7 p.m. and also features Gym Class Heroes, Plain White T’s and Cute Is What We Aim For.
 
The game day waiver of open-container alcohol laws will not be in effect for any of the pre-Homecoming activities before the Nov. 3 football game in Bright House Networks Stadium.
 
To make room for carnival rides, students are reminded that the Visual Arts Building parking lot has limited availability starting Oct. 23. It will reopen Monday, Oct. 29. Students are encouraged to park in Garages A and H throughout the week, and carnival-goers are invited to park in adjoining lots and garages.
 
Other events during this year’s “Tales of Terror” themed UCF Homecoming include:
 
-- A first-ever homecoming off-campus service day starts the celebration from 12:30 to 7 p.m. Friday, Oct. 26, at the Parramore Kidz Zone in downtown Orlando. Volunteers will be painting, gardening and tutoring or playing with children. Interested parties can contact hcphilanthropy@mail.ucf.edu.

-- UCF’s mascot Knightro will be signing autographs and handing out candy at his Trick or Treat Party, starting at 6 p.m. Oct. 26 in the second floor lobby of the new UCF Arena. Beginning at 7 p.m., the party will move inside the arena where the Knights volleyball team will be hosting the UAB Blazers. Admission to the game and party is free, and fans are encouraged to come in costume.
 
-- “Trick or Treat on Greek Street” provides a safe Halloween-inspired environment for local kids and their families. Trick-or-Treating runs from noon to 4 p.m. Oct. 28 on Greek Row and around Lake Claire. All proceeds from the event’s fund-raising activities go to the Boys & Girls Club of Central Florida.
 
-- Awarded the best university tradition in the state by Florida Leader magazine, Spirit Splash begins at noon Nov. 2 at – and in – the Reflecting Pond with live music from a student band and a pep rally featuring UCF’s national champion cheerleading squad.
 
-- UCF’s Homecoming Parade will return to campus this year, starting at Central Florida Boulevard and making its way clockwise along Gemini Boulevard. The parade starts at 9:30 a.m. Nov. 3 and is expected to last about an hour and a half. Parade-goers are encouraged to arrive on campus before 9 a.m., and all fans need to be aware that traffic flow on the campus will be heavily restricted and possibly redirected from normal flow patterns from approximately 9:30 a.m. to 11:30 a.m.
 
-- An 11 a.m. men’s basketball exhibition game in the new UCF Arena on Nov. 3 coincides with Homecoming game day tailgating before the 3:30 p.m. football game against Marshall. Admission to the basketball game is free. Normal football game day parking restrictions will be in effect throughout the campus, and there will be no special parking accommodations for the basketball game.
 
-- UCF alumni can still register for the annual Homecoming golf tournament at the Villas of Grand Cypress Golf Resort, starting at 8 a.m. Oct. 31. Registration is $100 for UCF Alumni Association members and $125 for non-members.
 
Alumni are also invited to downtown Orlando next week for the Black and Gold Takeover party. The festivities begin at 10 p.m. Nov. 2 on Wall Street. Alumni Association members get in free with their membership card.
 
For more information on alumni events, visit http://ucfalumni.com.
 
To learn more about other Homecoming festivities, go to http://ucfhc.com.
