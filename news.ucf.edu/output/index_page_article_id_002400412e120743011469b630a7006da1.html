About 300 Knights Assist Charities During UCF Community Service Day

By Christine Dellert and Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala Updated Oct. 8, 2007

Photo: Courtesy of Volunteer UCF

UCF volunteers helped to clean up a Brevard County beach Saturday during one of many activities during a UCF community service day.

Full Photo

  • Megan Licata, Volunteer UCF

About 300 UCF students, faculty and alumni from around Central Florida volunteered their labor and talents Oct. 6 as part of the University of Central Flroida's first campus-wide volunteer day.

They worked at more than a dozen sites and with several organizations in Orange, Seminole, Volusia, Brevard and Osceola counties.

Knights Give Back was sponsored by the student-run Volunteer UCF organization, the Student Government Association and UCF's Office of Student Involvement.

The first-ever campus-wide community service day was a success, said Megan Licata, 20, Volunteer UCF's director and an Organizational Communication major. When everyone got back and got off the bus, they were completely exhausted, but they were thrilled to have spent the day doing something meaningful."

While the majority of volunteers were students, about 50 were faculty, staff and UCF alumni.

We wanted to create this day to be a UCF day of service -- not just a student event, but a whole UCF event, said Shane Juntunen, assistant director of the Office of Student Involvement.

That's why the students and Office of Student Involvement staff met with UCF's Alumni Association to spread the word about service activities close to where alumni around Central Florida live.

About 10 UCF students helped at Saturday's Central Florida Miracle League baseball game at Trotter's Park on Lee Road in Orlando.

You can never have too many, league president Kelly Puckett said about the volunteers who help Orlando-area children with disabilities play baseball every week. Weve been playing for five years now. If you look back, the number-one group that comes out frequently is the UCF students. They're our greatest resource.

Two volunteer shifts made Halloween decorations with the Girls and Boys Town in Oviedo and put them up in the building.

About a dozen buses took the volunters to their service sites Saturday.

Those who helped the Orlando Science Center were especially excited about their experiences, said Licata.

"They not only volunteered to help children at the center, they also got to learn themselves about the human body and other exhibits at the center," she said. "They couldn't stop talking about it."

For more information, visit www.vucf.getinvolveducf.com/knightsgiveback.

Saturday's service sites and volunteer projects included:

* Central Florida Miracle League
* Second Harvest Food Bank
* Heavenly Hoofs (Kissimmee)
* Keep Seminole County Beautiful -- St. John's River Clean-up at Lake Monroe
* Seminole County Environmental Studies Center -- Invasive Plant Removal
* HOPE Foundation SONSHINE Thrift Store
* BETA Center Fall Festival
* Give Kids the World
* Girls and Boys Town
* Maitland Art Museum Art Garden
* Wekiva Springs River Clean-up
* Orlando Science Center
* Volusia County Beach Clean-up on Flagler Avenue
* Volusia County Beach Clean-up at Frank Rendon Park
* Brevard County Beach Clean-up at Cocoa Beach
* Florida Hospital Patient Companion Program
* Shepherd's Promise (Orlando)
