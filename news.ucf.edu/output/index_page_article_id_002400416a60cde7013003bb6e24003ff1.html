Today at UCF

88°F, Fair

ABC News, New York Times Eye UCF

June 22, 2011

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Florida over the past month has made important announcements about its outreach to the Central Florida community. Outlets such as The New York Times and the Orlando Sentinel have highlighted WUCF TV, the PBS partnership between UCF and Brevard Community College, and UCF's proposed College of Dental Medicine.

For more recent UCF news, please see below for a review of selected print, broadcast and online media coverage. Also, follow UCF news on Twitter and Facebook, watch UCF TV and learn more about Strategy, Marketing, Communications & Admissions.

NATIONAL

Business Week: Oil Spill May Have Indirect Role in Dolphin Deaths
The massive BP oil spill might indirectly have contributed to the unusually high number of young dolphins dying in the Gulf of Mexico recently, a University of Central Florida marine researcher said.
-- This story, similar versions of which appeared in print, online and on television around the country, features research from UCF Biology professor Graham Worthy.

ABC News: 10 Worst Cities for Jobs Suffered From Housing Crisis
Riverside in southern California is one of many US cities that experienced a real estate boom in the past decade. It was the housing boom and bust that is now causing deep misery for jobseekers.
-- This story quotes Sean Snaith, director of the Institute for Economic Competitiveness.

The New York Times: Florida Governor Vetoes PBS Funding
Just as a deal came together late last week to keep PBS programming on the air in Orlando, Florida’s public broadcasters suffered a financial blow when Gov. Rick Scott vetoed the state’s nearly $4.8 million appropriation for public broadcasting.
-- This features the PBS partnership between UCF and Brevard Community College.

USA TODAY: Obama Will Visit Puerto Rico With 2012 on His Mind
The island's residents may not be able to vote for him on Election Day, but President Obama has good reason to head to Puerto Rico next week — and not just for the Caribbean music and the Creole cooking.
-- This story quotes History professor Luis Martinez-Fernandez.

CNN Money: Still a BBQ recovery ... Low and slow
Let's get this out of the way. The economy is in lousy shape. And it will continue to be for a long time. It's an awful truth but we must get used to it.
-- This story quotes Sean Snaith, director of the Institute for Economic Competitiveness.

USA TODAY: Are Ohio State, USC Too Big to be Hurt by NCAA Penalties?
Staffers at the University of Southern California packed a $35,000 crystal football for shipment this week, returning the 2004 Bowl Championship Series national title trophy the Trojans won on the field but then lost in a hail of NCAA penalties.
-- This story quotes Richard Lapchick, director of UCF's Institute for Diversity and Ethics in Sport.

The Columbus Dispatch: Lindey's Owner a Matriarch of Restaurant Success
One of the first and most important business lessons Sue Doody ever learned came when she was 8 years old and helping to run a lemonade stand.
-- This story quotes Chair of the Food Service and Lodging Management department in the Rosen College of Hospitality Management.

STATE/LOCAL

Florida Trend: Virtual Reality Check at UCF
The University of Central Florida is conducting leading-edge experiments in simulation and training.

Orlando Sentinel: WUCF TV Will be Central Florida's PBS Channel
PBS on Thursday gave its blessing to WUCF TV, which will start July 1 and replace WMFE-Channel 24 as Central Florida's primary public television channel.
-- This story appeared in publications around the state.

Orlando Sentinel: My Word by UCF's Deborah German: The Case for a Dental School
On May 26, the University of Central Florida Board of Trustees approved a new College of Dental Medicine at the university's Health Sciences Campus at Lake Nona. Thanks to the board's decision, our community is one step closer to realizing the benefits this college offers.

Orlando Sentinel: UCF's first medical class gets hands-on time with patients
It's a moment they've been waiting for. The 40 medical students who make up the charter class at the University of Central Florida College of Medicine, in Lake Nona, have left the campus for the hospital.

Palm Beach Post: Fresh Out of Local Colleges, Graduates See Offer, but Some Alumni Still Looking
In a sign that hiring is on the rebound, Florida Atlantic University student Balmatie Hargobin not only has a job - she had five job offers to choose from.
-- This story quotes Lynn Hanson, UCF's executive director of career services.
