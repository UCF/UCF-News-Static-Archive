UCF Students' Service in Classroom Saves School Districts Money

By Courtney Gilmartin April 2, 2009

UCF education majors have been interning in public schools across Central Florida, refining their skills and providing a valuable service.

At a time when public school districts are laying off teachers and slashing millions from their budgets, the University of Central Florida is helping school children by contributing more than $10 million worth of service.

More than 1,500 UCF students studying to become teachers in 2008-09 have interned in local school districts, polishing their teaching skills while giving teachers an extra set of hands in the classroom. They do everything from grading papers to giving lessons. 

“Students interning under the supervision of a highly qualified teacher and a university supervisor bring added value to the schools by contributing professionalism valued at millions of dollars,” said Sandra Robinson, dean of the College of Education. “In these times of fiscal and budgetary constraints, that added value is very important to the school districts where our interns serve.”

Education students intern in 11 school districts including Orange, Seminole, Brevard, Volusia, Osceola and Marion counties, among others.

This year, 667 Internship I students have spent two days a week in their assigned schools, observing teachers and working with small groups of students.  Another 918 Internship II students take on the role of a full-time teacher for a semester, spending five days a week at their assigned schools developing their teaching skills under the supervision of other teachers. 

UCF estimates that Internship I students have contributed more than $3 million worth of service to local school districts, and Internship II students have provided more than $6.6 million. The figures were calculated by multiplying the number of hours in the classroom by $17. The hourly rate is the value Orange County Public Schools gives for volunteer hours. UCF used the flat rate for all districts.

Since 2007, UCF has placed more than 3,453 interns in local schools contributing about $21.8 million worth of service.

“I believe UCF does a good job of getting their students out into the field and sending the message that education should be at the forefront of everyone’s minds,” said Hunter Cambon, a junior from Ft. Lauderdale majoring in English Language Arts Education. He is interning at Deland Middle School this semester.

Cambon’s Internship I responsibilities include helping to grade assignments, managing the classroom, preparing assignments and discussing classroom strategy with his supervising teacher.

He said the experience in the classroom has been invaluable and that he knows he’s making a difference.

“The students are very responsive to my questions and conversation,” said Cambon.  “They remember what we talk about from week to week and seem to lighten up when I approach them.”

Likewise, UCF student Lori Burgio, is thrilled to be getting experience at Timber Creek High School in Orlando. She will be graduating soon.

Burgio’s Internship II duties include preparing and giving lessons, grading, and managing the classroom.

“It’s not a lot of money—but it’s a rewarding profession,” Burgio said.  “A teacher can change a child’s life for the positive.” 

For more information on the College of Education’s Office of Clinical Experience, visit http://education.ucf.edu/clinicalexp/index.cfm.
