Tunnel of Oppression Open for Tours Tuesday and Wednesday

By UCF Staff March 6, 2006

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Tour reservations

  • Tunnel of Oppression

  • Print this Page

Tunnel of Oppression 2006, a program designed to educate the UCF community about forces of oppression in the world, will be held Tuesday, March 7, and Wednesday, March 8, in the Cape Florida Ballroom of the Student Union.

Designed to challenge community perceptions and encourage people to take action and contribute to the world, the tunnel will address several topics, including genocide, fear and anxiety, media and societal messages, spiritual abuse, poverty and homelessness, discrimination and hate, and personal empowerment.

The program consists of a walking tour of visual displays, multimedia demonstrations and acted scenarios involving individual or communal acts of violence, terror and oppression. Participants then discuss the elements of oppression with students and professional staff.

The tunnel will be open from noon to 8:00 p.m. Tuesday and from 9 a.m. to 4:40 p.m. Wednesday. The program lasts about 40 minutes. Specific times when tours are available and instructions on how to reserve spaces are posted at www.link.ucf.edu/tunnel.

The Tunnel of Oppression is sponsored by the University Counseling Center, Housing and Residence Life, LINK First Year Experience, United Resident Student Association, LEAD Scholars Program and Connecting the UCF Community.
