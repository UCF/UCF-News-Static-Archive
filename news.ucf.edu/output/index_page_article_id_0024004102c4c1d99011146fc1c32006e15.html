UCF ORBIT Executive Briefing to Feature U.S. Senate Chief Information Officer, UCF Education Professor

By UCF Staff April 18, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Karen Adams, UCF ORBIT

    • 407-882-0264

  • UCF ORBIT

  • Print this Page

J. Greg Hanson, assistant sergeant-at-arms and chief information officer of the U.S. Senate, and Lisa Dieker, an associate professor of education and director of UCFs Lockheed Martin Mathematics and Science Academy, will speak at a UCF ORBIT executive briefing on Friday, May 11.

The briefing from 7:30 a.m. to noon at the Grand Bohemian Hotel in downtown Orlando will cover Leadership in New Technology, Global Markets & Innovation & The Next Generation from UCF.

The mission of UCF ORBIT is to provide resources, content, and knowledge-based information through partnerships with business and information technology companies in the Florida High Tech Corridor. Executive briefings cover best practices and issues related to the Florida High Tech Corridor and national and international markets.

Hanson brought more than 29 years of technology leadership experience to the Senate as its first chief information officer. As CIO, Hanson is responsible for the technology vision and strategy for the Senate. He oversees the Senates information infrastructure, including data, telecommunications, back office systems, information assurance and technology for emergency operations. Hanson is also responsible for introducing new technology, along with research and development, for the Senate. Hanson was honored as a Distinguished UCF Alumnus in 2006, and he is a member of the College of Engineering and Computer Science Deans Advisory Board.

Dieker is an associate professor and a Lockheed Martin Eminent Scholar at UCF. Her primary area of research focuses on collaboration between general and special education at the secondary level with specific interests in urban schools, mathematics and science. She also is interested in how technology and virtual classrooms can help with teacher preparation. 

The $80 registration fee includes breakfast and valet parking at the Grand Bohemian Hotel, 325 S. Orange Ave. There is a $15 discount for UCF alumni, faculty and staff and for members of several other organizations. To register, for more information on the event or for a full list of groups eligible for the discount, go to www.orbit.ucf.edu/briefing or call Karen Adams at 407-882-0264.
