UCF Injects $1.3 Billion, 19,000 Jobs into Central Florida, Analysis Shows

By Tom Evelyn April 14, 2004

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Peter Panousis

    • 407-882-2104

  • Tom Evelyn

    • 407-823-5988

  • Institute for Economic Competitiveness

  • Economic Impact Study

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Florida injected nearly $1.3 billion into the Central Florida region while supporting more than 19,000 jobs in 2003, according to a study conducted by UCFs Institute for Economic Competitiveness.

Many people dont realize that UCFs economic impact on Central Florida ranks it among the largest businesses in the community, institute Director Peter Panousis said. UCF is the sixth-largest employer in the region.

The institute examined how much UCF spends to run the university and construct buildings and how much money businesses spend on university-related work. The study also examined how many people UCF employs at its 22 campuses and learning centers and the number of workers added to the region to support the university and its projects.

The analysis showed that UCFs combined direct and indirect impact on the regional economy was $1.278 billion. At the same time, the university directly and indirectly generated 19,267 jobs.

For every $100 UCF spends in the community (direct impact), other businesses spend another $141 (indirect impact), Panousis said. And for every 100 jobs UCF generates, the business community creates another 89.

The study measured UCFs impact on the regions economy using IMPLAN, an input-output economic modeling program. The data came from UCFs audited 2003 Annual Financial Report.

A preliminary study of the adjacent Central Florida Research Park showed that it directly and indirectly added another $1.371 billion and 16,161 jobs, not including UCF workers. This study included estimated employee and wage information provided by the Orange County Research and Development Authority.

UCF has a strong relationship with businesses in the Research Park and in many cases supports companies there through research and graduate students.

The Institute for Economic Competitiveness was established in 2003 to support Central Floridas focus on creating high-wage, high-skill jobs. The institute provides the community and its leaders with reliable data and detailed economic analyses that can serve as a foundation for developing economic policies. The institute also provides a strong base for research in urban and regional economics.

More information on the institute and the complete report on UCFs economic impact are available at www.iec.ucf.edu. To view the report, click on Projects.
