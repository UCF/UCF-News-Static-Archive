Knights Show Fan Fever at Parade, Party Before Liberty Bowl

By Chad Binette Dec. 28, 2007

Photo: Chad Binette

The Marching Knights played the UCF fight song and more for fans who lined Beale Street on Friday.

MEMPHIS, Tenn. -- Confident and enthusiastic, hundreds of UCF fans lined Memphis’ best-known party street Friday for the annual AutoZone Liberty Bowl parade and then flocked to a blues club for a UCF Alumni Association pre-game party.

UCF’s national-champion cheerleaders, mascot Knightro and the 300-member Marching Knights band led the colorful parade down Beale Street. Fans chanted “UCF” and the Knights’ fight song as the cheerleaders and band made their way down Beale Street, which is lined with bars.

Paradegoers stood two to three deep in many areas, occasionally jockeying for position to grab beads thrown from floats as they tried to keep warm. The hourlong event featured several high school bands from around the country, Elvis impersonators, Memphis-area Shriners and even the city’s bright-green-clad “Secret Order of Boll Weevils.”

Many of the UCF fans then got out of the cold and packed the B.B. King Blues Club, where they renewed acquaintances with old friends, discussed their expectations for Saturday’s Liberty Bowl game and even jumped up and down to the “Zombie Nation” song. Marc Daniels of Orlando’s 740 the Team also hosted a pre-game radio show from the club.

On Saturday, many Knights fans will attend the Liberty Bowl’s pre-game buffet and “Battle of the Bands” at noon CST before heading to the sold-out game, which kicks off at 3:30 p.m.

“It’s going to be an exciting game,” said Nick Fila, a UCF senior who flew to Nashville and then drove to Memphis with his girlfriend, UCF senior Nicole Bruno. “It’s going to be close, but we will win.”

The game will be UCF’s highest-profile bowl game ever, and the atmosphere will be much more electric than the Knights’ first bowl appearance in Hawaii two years ago. Because of the cost and distance involved to travel there and the fact that the game was on Christmas Eve, few Knights fans were able to make the trip.

The Knights will have much more support this year, as thousands of fans have flocked to Memphis for the weekend.

“This is a wonderful opportunity for every one that is involved with the University of Central Florida,” said Harry Johnson, a 1976 graduate of UCF who played baseball for the school. “It’s a fun gathering for all of us who are participating. I am very proud of the school. (Athletics Director) Keith (Tribble) has done an outstanding job.”

Johnson and other fans are hoping for a successful conclusion to a season in which the Knights played their home games on campus for the first time and won their first Conference USA championship. Fans also are thrilled that Smith, the nation’s leading rusher this year, has said he will return for his senior season.

UCF fans don’t seem to mind that they will be vastly outnumbered by the cowbell-ringing Mississippi State fans, many of whom only had to drive two to three hours to Memphis, which is just north of Tennessee’s border with Mississippi.

Frank and Carrie Hung, UCF alumni who live in South Florida, said they were thrilled to see so many Knights’ fans in Memphis. They also hope to make bowl trips an annual event.

“It’s a lot of fun,” Frank Hung said at the Alumni Association party. “We’re enjoying ourselves.”

The Hungs planned to attend the Liberty Bowl’s pre-game party on Saturday before watching the Knights take on the Bulldogs.

“We just want a win – that’s all we want,” Frank Hung said. “I don’t care if we win by one point or 50.”

More information about the Liberty Bowl can be found at www.libertybowl.org. For coverage of the Knights football team in Memphis, go to www.ucfathletics.com.

Liberty Bowl Notes:

-- On Friday morning, the Knights' 19 seniors spent part of their morning at St. Jude Children's Research Hospital visiting with patients at one of the world's most renowned pediatric cancer hospitals.

-- UCF President John Hitt received one of the awards handed out at the the football team's annual banquet Thursday.  He won the "Service and Recognition" honor for his contributions and the vision he set out for the UCF football program.

-- The blues band entertaining the crowd at the Alumni Association party apparently thought Kevin Smith is a quarterback, as the lead singer was urging Smith to throw the ball downfield in a tune that was adapted for the UCF fans.

-- A high school band from Huntington, W.V., received extra applause from some Knights fans during the parade. Huntington also is the home of Marshall University, which opened the door for the Knights to advance to the Liberty Bowl when it defeated then-East Division leader East Carolina late in the season.

-- UCF’s band was not the only group of “Marching Knights” in the parade. Borgia, a high school in Washington, Mo., brought its “Marching Knights” as well.
