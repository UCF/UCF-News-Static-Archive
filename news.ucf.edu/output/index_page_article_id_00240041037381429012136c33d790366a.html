Fun in the Sun: UCF Trustees Meet at Florida Solar Energy Center

By Grant J. Heston Sept. 17, 2009

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Grant J. Heston

    • 407-823-5988

  • Board of Trustees

  • Print this Page

Share this story:

  • Share on Facebook
  • Seed Newsvine

The University of Central Florida Board of Trustees met today at the university’s Florida Solar Energy Center (FSEC), the largest and most active state-supported energy research institute in the country.

Founded 34 years ago, FSEC researches and develops energy technologies that enhance Florida's and the nation's economy and environment. Research activities include solar water and pool heating, solar electric and distributed generation systems, energy-efficient buildings, alternative transportation systems, hydrogen fuel and fuel cells.

FSEC annually attracts about $12 million in research and training funds and expects that federal stimulus money will push that total to $20 million. Additionally, FSEC research helps to produce $100 million in building energy cost savings each year.

FSEC also educates the public, students and practitioners on the results of its research. FSEC has received national and international recognition for its wide range of research, education, training and certification activities.

“UCF and FSEC will be the point of the spear that drives Florida forward in the development of renewable energy, and that in turn will drive the economic development of our state,” said Tommy Burroughs, chair of the FSEC board. “We’ll be leaders in having a green environment and a green economy.”

Two Degree Programs Approved

The board voted to add two new programs to the university’s academic offerings. A masters of science in Real Estate, which will be implemented in Spring 2010, will consist of 30 credit hours of graduate level course work.

The program will be offered in a 16-month format. An estimated enrollment of 30 students -- organized in cohort groups admitted every other year – will meet two nights a week. Graduates will be knowledgeable in finance and real estate and will have advanced skills in commercial and real estate brokerage and appraisal, real estate development and mortgage brokerage.

A mandate from the Commission on Accreditation of Athletic Training Education led to the transformation of UCF’s current health sciences-athletic training tracks into a bachelor of science in Athletic Training. UCF’s bachelor of science in Athletic Training will meet the need for allied health professionals, especially athletic trainers, and will prepare students for graduate-level work in athletic training and other allied health fields.

Freshman Class Success

The projected 3,800 new freshmen who started classes this semester posted an average high school GPA of 3.8 and an average SAT score of 1225, both the best in UCF’s history, according to preliminary enrollment data.

UCF also has 55 newly enrolled National Merit Scholars in its freshman class – the second-highest number in the university’s history. This total should again place UCF in the top 50 universities in the country for the number of National Merit Scholars enrolled.

President John Hitt also said that UCF ranked in the top five among national universities in the “Top Up-and-Coming Schools” list published in August by U.S. News & World Report. UCF is the only Florida university on the list.

The list recognizes universities that “recently made the most promising and innovative changes in academics, faculty, students, campus or facilities.” UCF again made U.S. News’ list of top 20 national universities from which students graduate with the least debt.

Honoring Myles Brand

On Wednesday, NCAA President Myles Brand passed away from pancreatic cancer. Hitt asked the board and attendees to honor Brand with a moment of silence.

Hitt noted that in addition to being the first former university president to run college sports' largest governing body, Brand attended the opening game at Bright House Networks Stadium in 2007. Brand said at the time that the stadium was attractive and completed for an affordable price.
