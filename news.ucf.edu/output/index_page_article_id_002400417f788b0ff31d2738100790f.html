President Hitt Thanks Students, Staff for Patience, Hard Work During Storms

By UCF Staff Sept. 30, 2004

Photo: Jerry Klein

Students returned Tuesday to a campus ready to go.

UCF President John Hitt on Thursday thanked students, faculty and staff for their patience in dealing with the recent hurricanes.

Hitt said that while touring the emergency operations center and student shelters on campus, he observed the great spirit of students and the hard work of employees. He spoke at an open forum for students at the Student Union.

UCF's main campus reopened Tuesday morning for classes and business after university employees worked through the weekend and Monday to clear debris and repair water damage caused by Hurricane Jeanne.

The Rosen College of Hospitality Management and most UCF regional campuses also reopened Tuesday. Campuses at Cocoa and Melbourne reopened Wednesday, and the Palm Bay campus reopened Thursday. 

All main campus buildings were evaluated by Physical Plant employees Sunday evening and Monday, and all of the buildings were ready to reopen Tuesday.

Physical Plant employees worked hard during Hurricane Jeanne to stop water flowing into the Classroom Building, the Center for Research and Education in Optics and Lasers, the Teaching Academy and other buildings. Education, Business Administration II and the Library also had water damage. The storm caused moderate tree damage throughout the main campus.

The entire main campus retained power and its drinking water supply throughout the storm, and no problems were reported in the student shelters.

Students who live on campus and remained during the storm were directed to designated shelters, as were residents of Pegasus Pointe and Pegasus Landing, UCFs affiliated housing complexes. Students watched television and played cards and games to pass the time.

Recreation and Wellness Center Update

UCF plans to reopen the Recreation and Wellness Center in December or January, Tom Huddleston, vice president for Student Development and Enrollment Services, told students at an open forum Thursday morning.

The university already has ordered new exercise equipment to ensure that it will be available as soon as the building is ready to reopen, Provost Terry Hickey said.

Until the center reopens, students can use weights and cardiovascular equipment that has been moved to the Pegasus Landing apartment complex fitness rooms. Group exercise classes are being offered at the Live Oak and Garden rooms in the Marketplace building. 

Florida Hurricane Relief Fund

Gov. Jeb Bush has established the Florida Hurricane Relief Fund to assist communities in rebuilding following hurricanes Charley, Frances, Ivan and Jeanne. The fund will be used for needs unmet by other disaster relief organizations. For more information or to donate, go to www.flahurricanefund.org or call 800-825-3786.
