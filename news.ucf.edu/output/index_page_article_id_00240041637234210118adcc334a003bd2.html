Fifteen UCF Students Inducted to Order of Pegasus

By Courtney Gilmartin March 19, 2008

Photo: Courtesy of Student Leadership Development

Students who win Order of Pegasus honors receive this medal.

Full Photo

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Christine Dellert, News and Information

    • 407-823-2947

  • Order of Pegasus

  • Print this Page

Fifteen UCF students have received the Order of Pegasus, the most prestigious student award given by the university.

The award commemorates outstanding academic achievement, university involvement, leadership and community service for undergraduates. Graduate students are selected separately based on academic achievement, professional or community service, leadership, and publication or research experiences.

The names and a picture of the inductees will be added to the wall of the Pegasus Lounge, which is located on the second floor of the Student Union overlooking the Pegasus Ballroom. Inductees will be honored at a ceremony in April and will receive commemorative medals, Student Government Association scholarships and lifetime memberships to the UCF Alumni Association.

After being nominated by a committee of UCF community members, students completed a formal application, provided recommendations and interviewed with a selection committee. To qualify for the Order of Pegasus, graduate students must have completed at least two semesters of graduate work at UCF. Undergraduates must have senior standing at the end of the summer term.

“Each year, the committee is more and more impressed with the caliber of the nominees,” said Gary Morgan, director of student leadership development. “The quality of our very best and brightest students continues to grow along with the quality of the entire UCF student population.”

The following four graduate students won Order of Pegasus honors:

Kimberly E. Bryant-Davis is pursuing a doctorate from the College of Education. She has interned at the U.S. Department of Education in Washington, D.C., and has taught kindergarten and varying exceptionalities in Seminole County. She also volunteers with the Down’s Syndrome Association. After completing her Ph.D., she plans to teach students with disabilities.

Tai T. Do is pursuing a doctoral degree in Computer Science in the College of Engineering and Computer Science. He is a graduate research teaching assistant and lab instructor in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science. He received a Graduate Research Fellowship and Graduate Enhancement Award, and he plays intramural soccer. He wants to become a university professor in the field of research technology.

Samiullah “Sami” K. Durrani is an Industrial Engineering doctoral student in the College of Engineering and Computer Science. Durrani has received a Presidential Doctoral Fellowship and has led several research projects.  He also participates in the Army Corps of Engineers’ hurricane recovery efforts and helps The Citizens Foundation for underprivileged children. Durrani hopes to work as a university professor and pursue a national leadership position with the Institute of Industrial Engineers.

Maribeth L. Kuenzi is a Management doctoral student majoring in Organizational Behavior in the College of Business Administration. Kuenzi has received a UCF Award for Excellence by a Graduate Teaching Assistant, College of Business Administration Ph.D. Research Award and College of Business Administration Graduate Teaching Award. She hopes to become a university professor and continue her research on organizational work climates.

The following 11 undergraduates also were selected:

Ayoolabode “Ayo” A. Ajaja is an Electrical Engineering major in the College of Engineering and Computer Science with a minor in Finance. Ajaja is a resident assistant and a member of the National Society of Black Engineers, UCF African Association, International Students Association and President’s Leadership Council. After graduation, he plans to work as an analyst for an investment bank’s technology department and pursue his MBA. 

Bryan E. Arnette is a Health Services Administration major in the College of Health and Public Affairs. He is a LEAD Scholar and a member of the President’s Leadership Council and Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity. He is the founding chairman of the Coalition of Black Organizations and a member of the National Panhellenic Council and African American Student Union. After graduation, he hopes to pursue master’s and doctoral degrees in public health and to study health disparities from a health policy standpoint.

Lalita D. Booth is a Finance and Accounting double major in the College of Business Administration. She is the founder and program director for the Lighthouse for Dreams Financial Literacy Project and is a tax advocate and preparer for low-income filers.  She hopes to enroll in Harvard University’s concurrent Master in Public Policy and Juris Doctorate program and to establish a foundation dedicated to helping the poor transition from poverty to self-sufficiency.

Tabatha L. Calabrese is a Political Science and Legal Studies double major in the colleges of Health and Public Affairs and Sciences.  She is a LEAD Scholar, resident assistant and member of the President’s Leadership Council and Alpha Xi Delta sorority. Following graduation, Calabrese hopes to work for a presidential campaign and pursue a master’s degree in estate planning from University of Miami School of Law.

Ashley E. Conyers is a Liberal Studies major with a minor in Interpersonal Communication in Undergraduate Studies. She is a LEAD Scholar, a Burnett Honors College student and a member of the President’s Leadership Council and Pi Beta Phi sorority.  She was also the 2006 Homecoming queen. Conyers hopes to earn a master’s degree at UCF in Educational Leadership and to work as a university freshman advisor or director of student leadership programs.

Scott L. Kirkman is an Accounting and Finance double major in the College of Business Administration. He is a Burnett Honors College student and a Kappa Sigma fraternity member, and he was crowned 2007 Homecoming king. He is also founding president of Sigma Alpha Pi Leadership Fraternity and a member of the Orientation Team. Kirkman plans to work for a major accounting firm for about four years, go to law school and then return to the accounting industry as a manager-level accountant.

Cassandra A. Korsvik is a Molecular Biology and Microbiology major in the Burnett School of Biomedical Sciences in the College of Medicine. Korsvik is a National Merit Scholar, and she has received the Founders’ Day Award from her college. She was a member of the International Scholar Laureate Program Australia Medical Delegation. After graduation, she will be representing UCF with the International Scholar Laureate Program Medical Delegation to China. She later hopes to earn a Ph.D. in Microbiology.

Brittany L. Moscato is a Molecular Biology and Microbiology major in the Burnett School of Biomedical Sciences in the College of Medicine and a Spanish major in the College of Arts and Humanities. She is a member of the Pre-Professional Medical Society. She studied abroad in Cambridge, England, as a President’s Scholar and at the Universidad de Alcala in Spain.  Upon graduation, Moscato plans to pursue a joint master’s in public health and M.D. degree and then to care for underserved populations.

Maria V. Pecoraro is a Political Science major in the College of Sciences with a minor in Spanish. Pecoraro is a research assistant and student at The Burnett Honors College, and she is involved with Catholic Campus Ministries, the College Republicans and the Kappa Kappa Gamma sorority. She plans on pursuing a graduate degree in Political Science at UCF and becoming involved with the UCF Alumni Association.

Megan A. Policastro is a Legal Studies major in the College of Health and Public Affairs with a minor in Political Science. She is a Burnett Honors College student and the President’s Leadership Council chair. She volunteers with Habitat for Humanity, the Coalition for the Homeless, the American Heart Association and the American Cancer Society’s Relay for Life. She has raised more than $20,000 for charity while at UCF. She plans to attend the Duke University law school and pursue a career in environmental law.

Stephanie Kyla Tan is a Political Science major in the College of Sciences. She is a career peer advisor in Career Services and Experiential Learning, and she has an internship with a local law firm.  She hopes to attend the American University College of Law in Washington, D.C., and concentrate in trial advocacy, corporate international law and immigration law. She then wants to become an associate at a law firm.

For more information on the Order of Pegasus, visit http://www.campuslife.sdes.ucf.edu/orderofpegasus.html.
