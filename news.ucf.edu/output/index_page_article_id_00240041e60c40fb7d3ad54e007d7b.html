Ten Faculty Members Granted Emeritus Status

By Tom Evelyn April 7, 2004

Photo: Jerry Klein

President John Hitt presented this year's Emeritus Awards.

Ten faculty members who have made major professional contributions and achieved eminence in their work at UCF were awarded emeritus status Wednesday at the annual Founders Day celebration.

The 10 professors have worked at the university for at least five years. Some will retire later this year, and others already have retired. Emeritus status grants the faculty members a lifetime link to UCF.

-- Professor Emeritus John Jack Brennan, physics, a charter member of the UCF faculty, retired at the end of the spring 2003 semester after a distinguished career teaching physics in Central Florida and abroad. As UCFs first Senior Fulbright Lecturer, Brennan helped develop physics programs at universities in the Philippines. He was the technical advisor to the USAID Higher Education Development projects at nine universities in Indonesia, and he continues to work as a consultant to the Indonesian Ministry of Education. In Florida, Brennan has trained and mentored many secondary education teachers through workshops sponsored by the National Science Foundation and the Department of Education. He received the UCF Distinguished Teacher Award in 1981 and the Distinguished Service Award of the Florida Section of the American Association of Physics Teachers in 1988.

 

-- Professor Emeritus Douglas Brumbaugh, teaching and learning principles, joined the faculty of Florida Technological University (before it became UCF) in 1969. As a teacher of mathematics secondary education, Brumbaugh has distinguished himself as the ideal professor, according to his colleagues. He became a full professor of math in 1979 and was the interim associate dean in the College of Education from 1992 to 1994. Brumbaugh served as the president of the Florida Council of Teachers of Mathematics and is a founding member of the Research Council for Mathematics Learning. He has also taught and tutored students and teachers in several Central Florida high schools and at Valencia Community College. He is a winner of the Teaching Incentive Program award at UCF.

-- Professor Emeritus Wayne Burroughs, psychology, will retire from the Department of Psychology at the end of the spring semester after 35 years of service at UCF. As a faculty member, Burroughs played a key role in forming the departments masters degree program in industrial/organizational psychology and directed the program for 30 years. Under his leadership, the program became a model for other universities. Burroughs mentored a number of students who are leaders in the field, including Eduardo Salas, the colleges first Trustee Chair. Burroughs has served as an expert witness in court cases regarding discrimination and as a consultant for numerous companies, including Coca-Cola and the Bank of New Zealand.

-- Professor Emerita Marcela Del Rio, foreign languages and literatures, who joined the UCF faculty in 1990, will retire from the Department of Foreign Languages and Literature in July. Del Rio played a prominent role in creating the departments Spanish graduate program and was the heart and soul of the Latin American literature section, according to her colleagues. Her devotion to teaching was rewarded with a Teaching Incentive Program award. An accomplished scholar, Del Rio is regarded as one of Mexicos leading playwrights. Her play, Una flor para tu sueno, was designated as the official play of the state of Yucatan, Mexico, and her volume, Perfil y muestra del teatro de la Revolucion Mexicana, is considered by experts as the most definitive work on the theater of the Mexican Revolution. The College of Arts and Sciences acknowledged her research contributions with the 1998 Distinguished Researcher of the Year Award.

 

-- Professor Emeritus Richard Denning, engineering technology, who joined UCF as a professor in 1976, retired in June 2003. Denning served two of the longest tenures as chair of the Engineering Technology Department in the College of Engineering and Computer Science from 1976 to 1989 and from 1994 to 2000. He is known throughout Florida and the Southeast as an expert in engineering technology accreditation, and he has served on a number of accreditation teams. As chair of the department, Denning increased the faculty from three to 15 and the student body from 200 to 450. He won the Outstanding Teacher of the Year award for the Southeastern Section of the American Society for Engineering Education in 2003 and was a four-time winner of Teacher of the Year for the Engineering Technology Department. He is also a winner of the Teaching Incentive Program at UCF and a Faculty Leadership Award.

 

 

-- Professor Emeritus Llewellyn M. Ehrhart, biology, will retire from the Department of Biology in May after 35 years of service at UCF. Ehrhart holds the title of Pegasus Professor, the universitys highest distinction for extraordinary teaching, research and service. An internationally renowned authority on sea turtles, Ehrharts research has been featured in national TV documentaries and in National Geographic. His efforts to protect endangered sea turtles have earned him an Outstanding Effort in Sea Turtle Conservation award from NOAA and the Allan D. Crukshank Memorial Award from the Audubon Society of Florida. He is a winner of the Teaching Incentive Program award at UCF and the Carnegie Foundations Professor of the Year award. His contributions to teaching were acknowledged by the City of Orlando with the proclamation of Llew Ehrhart Day Nov. 21, 2002.

 

 

-- Professor Emeritus William McGee, chemistry, a founding member of the UCF faculty, retired from the Department of Chemistry in May 2003. McGee helped develop the Forensic Science program and served as its first director and later as director of the National Center for Forensic Science. Under his leadership, UCFs forensics program expanded rapidly and is now considered one of the top three programs in the world. For his efforts, he received the Recognition Award for Outstanding Contribution to Forensic Science Education from the American Academy of Forensic Science. Regarded as an expert in forensic science DNA, he also received the Allen Berman Award for Best Paper given by the Naval Research Laboratory.

 

 

-- Professor Emeritus Jack Rollins, psychology, retired in summer 2003 after completing 35 years of service at UCF as a psychology professor, dean and regional administrator. As a faculty member, Rollins played a leading role during the formative years of the Department of Psychology. He developed key courses in the curriculum and secured National Science Foundation and Department of Education grants that helped equip laboratories. As an administrator, he played a prominent role in consolidating the colleges of Social Sciences, Natural Sciences and Fine Arts into the College of Arts and Sciences. Rollins tenure as dean of the college was marked by growth in students and the quality of faculty. He contributed to quality education and enhanced UCFs image throughout the region during his leadership of the Daytona Beach Campus and in his last post as associate vice president and chief administrative officer of the Area Campus System.

 

 

-- Professor Emeritus John Salter, accounting, a four-time recipient of the College of Business Administration Excellence in Teaching Award, started teaching at UCF in 1975. After serving as an assistant and associate professor in accounting, Salter was designated an Ernst and Young Professor in 1994. He has won three Teaching Incentive Program awards and the College of Business Administration Excellence in Advising and Distinguished Service awards. Salter was honored for his excellence in teaching when he was promoted from associate professor to professor on a teaching track. He served as the School of Accountings Beta Alpha Psi faculty advisor from 1990 to 2001, during which the chapter was nationally recognized as a superior chapter six times.

 

 

-- Professor Emeritus Walter Taylor, biology, will retire in October after 35 years of service at UCF. Appointed to the Department of Biology in 1969, and promoted to professor in 1983, Taylor established a stellar record of teaching, research and service. His commitment to excellence in teaching and advising earned him three Teaching Incentive Program awards, four College of Arts and Sciences Excellence in Undergraduate Teaching awards, the University Excellence in Undergraduate Teaching Award and the University Excellence in Advising Award. His books, The Guide to Florida Wildflowers and Florida Wildflowers in Their Natural Communities, are considered among the best on the topic.

Other recently retired or retiring faculty who were honored Wednesday are Ellen Anderson, Glenn Cunningham, Donald Fuller, Martin Heinzer, Ronald Hofer, Thomas Kubala, Marilyn Salter, Margaret Sauer, Lawrence Tanzi and David Vickers.
