Local middle school students are analyzing fingerprints, footprints and DNA at the ExxonMobil Bernard Harris Summer Science Camp held at the University of Central Florida’s main campus.

The two-week camp, which concludes Friday, focuses on forensics. Students attend classes in natural science, engineering, mathematics and technology in the morning and participate in hands-on activities in the afternoon.

Many of the participants are women or minorities, groups that are typically underrepresented in the scientific community. 

Classes are taught by UCF faculty and local middle and high school teachers. Students are going on two field trips, including one to NASA/Kennedy Space Center.

Students experience life on a college campus and stay in residence halls on weeknights during the camp. Many UCF students serve as residential and academic counselors who plan team-building activities and talk with the participants about their goals for the future.

“The purpose is to encourage students to pursue a math or science career,” said Mike McKee, camp organizer and director of K-12 programs for UCF’s Center for Research and Education in Optics and Lasers. “We want them to see that science can be fun.” 

Students attending the camp were selected based on grade-point average, teacher recommendations and personal statements.

The camp is free for students and sponsored by Bernard Harris, the first African-American to walk in space and president of The Harris Foundation, a nonprofit organization that he founded in 1998 to develop math and science programs for at-risk youths, Harris partnered with ExxonMobil to create the camp to address the shortage of engineers, scientists and technically trained workers in the country.

“Science, technology, engineering and mathematics are critical elements to our nation’s economic, security and medical advancements in the 21st century and beyond,” Harris said.  “We are glad to extend this opportunity to the University of Central Florida and appreciate their eagerness to support our program’s efforts in encouraging students to reach higher standards of achievement.”

For more information about the Bernard Harris Summer Science Camp, visit http://www.theharrisfoundation.org/programs/summersciencecamp/index.htm.
