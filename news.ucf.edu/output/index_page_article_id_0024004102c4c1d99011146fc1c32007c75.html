Eight Students Featured in First MFA in Studio Art & the Computer Exhibition

By UCF Staff March 21, 2007

Photo: Courtesy of UCF Art Gallery

UCF graduate student Bert Bogdany works on his untitled sculpture.

Full Photo

Photographs, drawings, animations and video art by the first graduating class from the new Studio Art & the Computer program at the University of Central Florida will be featured in an exhibit March 19 to 30 at the UCF Art Gallery.

Eight students in the Master of Fine Arts program entered the program in 2004 and will graduate in May. Their MFA Thesis Exhibition will provide the first public glimpse of projects by students in a program that encourages them to combine their backgrounds in traditional art and computer-related disciplines.

The MFA graduates are Simona Balas, Urszula Blaszak, Bert Bogdany, Victor Davila, Phil Delacruz, Victoria Vevera, Betsy Walton and Roger Wolf.

Gallery hours are from 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. Monday through Friday, and admission is free. The gallery is located in the Visual Arts Building on UCFs East Orlando campus. A public reception for the MFA exhibit will be from 5:30 to 8:30 p.m. Thursday, March 22.

Courses in the Studio Art & the Computer program expose students to time-based media, performance art, video art, sound works, kinetic sculpture, computer-based art and art using the Internet so they can better understand how those forms are driving 21st-century artistic practice and informing our understanding of contemporary cultural identities.

The emphasis on electronic media is pliable enough to encompass the many ways in which technology intersects with contemporary art and design.

For more information about the MFA in Studio Art & the Computer, visit http://www.art.ucf.edu/MFAart. More information on the Art Gallery is available at http://www.art.ucf.edu/main.php?URL=galleryinfo.
