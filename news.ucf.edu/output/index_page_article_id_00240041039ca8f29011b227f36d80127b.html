Caribbean Culture, Environment Come to Life in Honors Study-Abroad

By UCF Staff July 14, 2008

Photo: Courtesy of Martin Dupuis

The 2008 President's Scholars spent two weeks in St. Kitts and Nevis, volunteering with environmental groups and learning about the islands' culture.

When Alex Kaplan applied for this year’s President’s Scholars study-abroad program, he expected to learn about Caribbean culture and its unique environmental challenges.
 
What he got was an up-close encounter with a nesting leatherback turtle.
 
“It was incredible, like seeing a dinosaur,” the 21-year-old Philosophy major said.
 
More than 1,200 miles off the Florida coast, Kaplan and seven University of Central Florida classmates spent two weeks in the tiny Caribbean islands of St. Kitts and Nevis. The young nation was the site of the 2008 President’s Scholars study-abroad program. In its fourth year, the program is housed in The Burnett Honors College at UCF.

For three weeks, the group learned about the islands’ literary history, ecosystems and economy from professors across UCF. Then they spent the middle of June traveling between St. Kitts and Nevis, visiting historical sites and local leaders and volunteering with environmental groups.
 
“One of the best ways of approaching study abroad is gaining knowledge before you go, so you can really appreciate and understand the culture,” said Martin Dupuis, assistant dean of UCF’s Burnett Honors College and this year’s trip leader.
 
The 2008 theme was environmentalism and climate change. It’s also the unifying theme for UCF’s general education program through the Office of Undergraduate Studies.
 
Students taking the six-week course in Orlando and the islands surveyed past and current environmental challenges -- deforestation, erosion and water pollution -- and considered policy solutions to those problems. They met with government officials from tourism and agriculture, hiked rainforests and snorkeled a coral reef.
 
The group also monitored St. Kitts sea turtle nests with local veterinarian students and designed an educational flier to protect juvenile conch and lobster against fishermen and tourists. The flier will be circulated around Nevis starting in the fall.
 
“I’ve been able to know the history, politics, social and cultural issues of a small country,” said Kaplan, who’s also studying nonprofit management. “Because the program was so multidisciplinary, it’s an exciting model for creating future academic research.”
 
That’s exactly what Dupuis hopes to accomplish. For him, the trip was a chance to share with students his work in the Caribbean since the 1980s. Dupuis is a founding member of the Mukti Fund, a small foundation that focused on cultural preservation and environmental conservation. Over two decades, it provided grants totaling almost $800,000.
 
The islands have a rich culture and history, stretching from volcanic mountains to rainforests and former sugar cane fields, Dupuis said. He foresees more projects between the islands and UCF.
 
“Florida is a part of the Caribbean,” Dupuis said. “We’re neighbors, and we share the same environmental concerns and coastal interests.”

About The President’s Scholars Program: Each year, the Burnett Honors College selects on a competitive basis up to 12 President's Scholars for participation in the program. President's Scholars are selected on the basis of their academic achievement, campus and community involvement, and their commitment to engaging in study abroad as a transformative experience. The President's Scholars Program was initiated by UCF President John Hitt to provide an honors study abroad experience to the highest achieving honors students at UCF. In its first year, President’s Scholars studied in Bergamo, Italy.  For the past two years, President’s Scholars have studied at Cambridge University in the UK. For more information, visit http://www.honors.ucf.edu/PS_About.asp.

UCF Stands For Opportunity: The University of Central Florida is a metropolitan research university that ranks as the 6th largest in the nation with more than 48,000 students. UCF's first classes were offered in 1968. The university offers impressive academic and research environments that power the region's economic development. UCF's culture of opportunity is driven by our diversity, Orlando environment, history of entrepreneurship and our youth, relevance and energy. For more information visit http://news.ucf.edu.
