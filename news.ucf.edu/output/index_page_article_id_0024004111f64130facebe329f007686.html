Honoring the Founding President

By Chad Binette November 26, 2003

When Charles Millican first reported to work as president of Florida Technological University on Dec. 1, 1965, he was the only employee at a school that had no buildings, no students and no academic programs.

Thirty-eight years later, FTU is now the University of Central Florida, and the campus includes about 115 buildings, 175 academic programs and 42,000 students. But one thing has remained constant amid all of the fast-paced changes: Millican, the founding president, remains an employee dedicated to improving the quality of education at what is now Floridas second-largest state university.

Millican, who now works in public relations for the UCF Foundation, will be honored Monday, Dec. 1, during a ceremony at the Reflecting Pond behind Millican Hall, the UCF administration building named in his honor. The public is invited to attend the 8:30 a.m. ceremony -- part of UCFs 40th anniversary celebration -- and then visit the nearby UCF L
ibrary to tour an exhibit of memorabilia from throughout the universitys history.

Millican was president of the university from 1965 to 1978. Before coming to UCF, he was a professor and dean of business at the University of South Floridas College of Business Administration from 1959 to 1965 and a professor and dean of the school of business at Hardin-Simmons University in Abilene, Texas, from 1956 to 1959.

Millicans key decisions at FTU included setting up the campus as a series of concentric circles and creating the first bachelors degree in the state in computer science despite concerns that such a program was unnecessary.

He led the school with a friendly, relaxed style and often walked around the campus to talk to students, faculty and staff.

"Im fortunate to still be here, still contributing in a small way and seeing all these things happening, so many positive things, great things," Millican said.

In three weeks, Millican will address College of Business and Rosen School of Hospitality Management students at their commencement ceremony. The ceremony starts at 10 a.m. Dec. 15 at UCF Arena.
