UCF Named to Higher Education Honor Roll for Community Service

By Roxanna Malidelis Feb. 9, 2009

Photo: UCF News file

UCF and Junior Achievement organize College Shadow Day, which helps local students from underserved middle schools experience a day in the life of a college student.

The University of Central Florida has been named to a higher education honor roll that recognizes “innovative and effective community service and service-learning programs.”

The 2008 President’s Higher Education Community Service Honor Roll by the Corporation for National and Community Service recognizes commitments to service and civic engagement on campus and in the nation. The honor roll is sponsored by the President’s Council on Service and Civic Participation, the USA Freedom Corps, and the U.S. departments of Education and Housing and Urban Development.

More than 11,000 UCF students enrolled last year in service-learning courses, one of many community engagement efforts that also earned the university recognition from the Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Teaching. The foundation recently recognized UCF for making substantial commitments in Curricular Engagement and Outreach & Partnership categories through its mission, culture, leadership, resources and practices.

UCF also is home to the world’s largest university-Junior Achievement partnership, which benefits thousands of youths in the community. For the 2007-08 year, UCF students contributed almost 34,500 service-learning hours through JA programs.

For more information on the honor roll, visit www.nationalservice.gov/honorroll.

UCF Stands For Opportunity: The University of Central Florida is a metropolitan research university that ranks as the 6th largest in the nation with more than 50,000 students. UCF's first classes were offered in 1968. The university offers impressive academic and research environments that power the region's economic development. UCF's culture of opportunity is driven by our diversity, Orlando environment, history of entrepreneurship and our youth, relevance and energy. For more information visit http://news.ucf.edu.
