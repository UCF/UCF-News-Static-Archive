UCF Wins EDUCAUSE Teaching and Learning Award for Online Program

By Tom Evelyn Oct. 17, 2005

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Joel Hartman

    • 407-823-6778

  • Tom Evelyn

    • 407-823-5988

  • Online@UCF

  • EDUCAUSE

  • Print this Page

EDUCAUSE, a nonprofit group that promotes the use of information technology to advance higher education, will honor the University of Central Florida this week with a Teaching and Learning Award for its Online@UCF program.

UCF will receive the award at the EDUCAUSE annual conference, Transforming the Academy: Dreams and Reality, at the Orange County Convention Center beginning Tuesday, Oct. 18. The Teaching and Learning award will be presented to UCF during the general session that begins at 9:30 a.m. Thursday, Oct. 20.

The award recognizes major improvements in campus teaching and learning through the innovative and sustainable use of information technology.

The success of UCFs online learning initiative is attributable to close alignment with institutional goals, skillful execution, a faculty-centered approach based on a nationally recognized faculty development process, and continuous, rigorous assessment, the award selection committee said.

UCF will describe its online learning program during a conference session, What We Have Learned from 10 Years of Online@UCF, at 2:30 p.m. Thursday, Oct. 20. UCF Vice Provost for Information Technologies and Resources Joel Hartman will lead the discussion.

Online@UCF began with a few online courses in 1996 to increase access to higher education throughout the Central Florida region. The program now includes tens of thousands of students on and off campus. About 75 percent of UCF faculty members use online resources to teach all or part of their courses.

The system allows the university to increase access for students in the UCF service region who require or desire the flexibility of online courses. It also provides a platform for faculty to deliver highly interactive, learner-centered online instruction.

Online@UCF encompasses 15 online degree programs and more than 3,200 online courses. Online courses generate more than 12 percent of the universitys annual student credit hour production. Student registration in online course sections almost always reaches capacity before face-to-face sections, and students generally rate their online courses as excellent.

The program is producing strategic benefits for UCF because it improves teaching, enhances student learning, expands access to high demand programs and helps the university manage growth, UCF President John Hitt said. The rapid acceptance of online learning and the resulting qualitative outcomes are transforming teaching and learning at the University of Central Florida.

Virginia Tech is also being recognized for its Math Emporium and other initiatives. Past Teaching and Learning Award-winners include Lehigh University, DePauw University, the University of Colorado at Boulder and the Wake Forest University School of Medicine.

More information on the EDUCAUSE awards is available at www.educause.edu/awards. For more on the conference, visit www.educause.edu/e05.

To learn more about Online@UCF, go to www.online.ucf.edu.
