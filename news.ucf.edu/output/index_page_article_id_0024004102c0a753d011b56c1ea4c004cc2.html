UCF Creates College of Graduate Studies

By Chad Binette and Christine Dellert July 24, 2008

Photo: Jacque Brund

Members of the UCF Board of Trustees and President John Hitt pose for their annual group photograph at the Student Union.

The University of Central Florida will establish a College of Graduate Studies to bring the structure of graduate education at UCF more in line with many other large research universities. Most universities that award at least 200 doctoral degrees per year have graduate colleges. UCF awarded 207 doctoral degrees in 2007-08.

The new college, announced today at the Board of Trustees meeting and effective on Aug. 8, will be created with no additional employees. It will replace the Division of Graduate Studies.

New Florida Board of Governors Chair Sheila McDevitt also spoke at today’s meeting. McDevitt, alongside State University System Chancellor Mark Rosenberg, stressed the need for greater collaboration among the state’s universities during tight budget times. She also praised UCF’s campus, its growth and its focus on providing students with quality education.

UCF’s new College of Graduate Studies will work to build a stronger community among graduate students and faculty and encourage them to pursue more interdisciplinary studies and research.

“The College of Graduate Studies will help to more clearly define our vision of excellence for the graduate community at UCF,” Provost and Executive Vice President Terry Hickey said. “We will work closely with administrators, faculty and staff members, and students in the coming months to determine how to best achieve our vision and bring a more collaborative and strategic approach to our excellent graduate programs.”

The college will implement more competitive financial aid packages that take effect in 2009-10. The packages include full tuition coverage and a health insurance subsidy for all graduate assistants who work at least 20 hours per week.

Patricia Bishop, vice provost and dean for the Division of Graduate Studies, will serve as the college’s founding dean.

Beginning in August, College of Graduate Studies leaders will hold meetings with other colleges and the Graduate Student Association and will host open forums to encourage feedback. Meeting notices and updates will be posted at www.graduate.ucf.edu.

In addition to the 207 doctoral degrees, UCF awarded 1,885 master’s degrees and 41 specialist degrees in 2007-08. The university anticipates that about 7,100 graduate students will be enrolled in the fall.

Also at the Board of Trustees meeting Thursday:

-- Burnett Honors College student Lalita Booth was recognized as UCF’s first Truman Scholar. Booth is studying Finance and Accounting and running a financial literacy program for teens. President John Hitt also recognized recent graduate Sarah Pullen, who was chosen this month as UCF’s third Jack Kent Cooke Scholarship winner in the past four years.

-- Board members approved a long-term financing plan to borrow up to $4 million for the purchase and improvements of the Sigma Alpha Epsilon and Sigma Phi Epsilon fraternity houses.

-- The board decided to move forward with plans for a 170-foot cell phone tower on the north end of campus to provide better coverage for residence halls, Bright House Networks Stadium, the new UCF Arena, athletics facilities and more than 7,000 residents near the campus.

-- President Hitt recognized UCF's 194 student athletes who earned recognition on the Conference USA Commissioner's Honor Roll during the 2007-08 school year. UCF placed the highest number of student athletes on the honor roll for the second year in a row. Twenty seven UCF student athletes received the Commissioner's Academic Medal for maintaining a grade point average of 3.75 or better.

UCF Stands For Opportunity --The University of Central Florida is a metropolitan research university that ranks as the 6th largest in the nation with more than 48,000 students. UCF's first classes were offered in 1968. The university offers impressive academic and research environments that power the region's economic development. UCF's culture of opportunity is driven by our diversity, Orlando environment, history of entrepreneurship and our youth, relevance and energy. For more information visit http://news.ucf.edu.
