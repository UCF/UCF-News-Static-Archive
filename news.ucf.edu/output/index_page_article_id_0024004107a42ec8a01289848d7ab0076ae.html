Former Trustees Honored; New Board Members Welcomed

By Chad Binette May 20, 2010

Photo: Jason Greene

Pat Christiansen (center) was awarded emeritus status by the Board of Trustees on Thursday. He is shown here with UCF President John Hitt (left) and Board Chair Rick Walsh.

The UCF Board of Trustees honored three former trustees and welcomed three new members on Thursday.

Trustees awarded emeritus status to charter board members Patrick Christiansen and Conrad Santiago, both whom had served since July 2001. Emeritus status recognizes their distinguished service to the university and provides them with a lifetime link to the university.

Board members also honored Alan Florez, who had served on the board for about four years.

“All three of these folks gave incredible service to this university for a long time,” said Board of Trustees Chair Rick Walsh.

New trustees James Atchison, Joseph L. Mantilla and Mike Kilbride all participated in their first board meeting Thursday. Atchison is president of SeaWorld Parks & Entertainment, and Mantilla is vice president of government and corporate affairs of Zimek Technologies LLC. Kilbride is the new president of UCF’s Student Government Association.

Ida Cook, who was re-elected as chair of the Faculty Senate, will continue serving on the board for another year.

In other action Thursday, the Board of Trustees:

-- Approved tuition and fees for the 2010-11 academic year, which begins in the fall. An undergraduate Florida resident will pay $167.35 per credit hour in tuition and fees. Tuition at Florida universities is among the lowest in the country.

-- Thanked retiring Provost and Executive Vice President Terry Hickey for his seven years of service to the university. Hickey is retiring at the end of June.

-- Approved a work plan outlining UCF’s top priorities, strategic directions and performance expectations to be submitted to the Florida Board of Governors.

-- Honored UCF’s three Pegasus Professor award winners. Michael Georgiopoulos, Jennifer Platt, and S.T. Wu were recognized for their extraordinary contributions to the UCF community through teaching, research and service.

-- Recognized founding president Charles Millican for receiving the John Young History Maker Award last month. The award is given each year to Central Floridians whose lifetimes of achievement have made a historic impact on the community.

-- Approved tenure for 35 faculty members.

-- Met UCF’s eight legislative scholars, who spent the spring interning in state legislators’ offices. Introduced to the trustees were Dacia Anderson, Christina Aiuto, Matthew Neff, Adolph “Joey” Posey, Audra Robitaille, Steven Sukow, Melissa Theodore and Regina Weiler.

-- Honored several UCF student-athletes for their accomplishments. The men’s golf team and women’s track team won Conference USA championships. Basketball student-athlete Isaac Sosa was named to the C-USA All-Academic Team, and teammate Keith Clanton was selected to the C-USA All-Freshman Team.

-- Approved a three-year collective bargaining agreement with the Police Benevolent Association, which represents UCF’s police officers.
