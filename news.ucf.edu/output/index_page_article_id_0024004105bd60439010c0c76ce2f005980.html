Carnegie Recognition to Help UCF Develop More Effective Teaching Practices

By Kristi Bartlett Oct. 3, 2006

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Kerstin Hamann, CASTL coordinator

  • Rick Schell, Undergraduate Studies

    • 407-823-4197

  • CASTL at UCF

A new partnership between UCF and the Carnegie Academy for the Scholarship of Teaching and Learning will help inspire professors to assess and improve the learning that results from undergraduate research.

The academy recently named UCF a leadership institution in the category of undergraduate research. The three-year partnership with the academys Institutional Leadership Program will provide professors from around the world with opportunities to observe and assess student learning and each others practices to improve teaching methods.

UCF was chosen because of its strong undergraduate research programs and institutional commitment to the scholarship of teaching and learning, said Rick Schell, dean of Undergraduate Studies. Faculty will be able to study the teaching and learning that takes in various undergraduate research programs, such as the more than 200 paid undergraduate research fellowships, the UCF Summer Research Academies, the Showcase of Undergraduate Research Success and the Undergraduate Research Journal.

For example, a professor could study whether students learn more if they present posters or give oral presentations during research showcases, Schell said.

This will help us provide better learning experiences for students, engaging them more fully in and out of the classroom, Schell said.

Launched in 1998, the Carnegie Academy for the Scholarship of Teaching and Learning is a major initiative of the Carnegie Foundation. It seeks to support the development of a scholarship of learning and teaching; foster significant, long-lasting learning; and enhance the practice and profession of teaching.

Teaching has typically been a private act, limited to the teacher and students, according to the foundation. With guidance from CASTL, teaching will become subject to critical evaluation and build upon the work of others. It will cross all disciplines and all colleges at UCF. Faculty members will receive incentives to participate.

Kerstin Hamann, the CASTL coordinator for UCF and an associate professor of political science, said this recognition will take us to the next level of undergraduate research and ensure our place as a top-notch undergraduate research university.

UCF shares the CASTL designation with universities such as Notre Dame and Illinois.

The first meeting of all the institutions involved in CASTL will be on Nov. 8 in Washington, D.C. The partnership agenda for participating universities will be discussed at that meeting.

The universities involved can pool resources and learn from each other, said Hamann.

The Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Teaching was founded by Andrew Carnegie in 1905 and chartered in 1906 by an act of Congress. It is an independent research and policy center that fulfills its mission to do and perform all things necessary to encourage, uphold and dignify the profession of teaching through contributions to improvements in education policy and practice.
