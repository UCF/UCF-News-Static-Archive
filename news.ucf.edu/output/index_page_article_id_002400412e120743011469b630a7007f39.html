Orlando Sentinel Awards First-Ever Digital Journalism Scholarships to UCF Students

Aug. 20, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Print this Page

As newspapers aggressively add multimedia components to their daily reporting, University of Central Florida students David How and Jordan Rogers are just the type of future employees they hope to attract. 

The seniors are recipients of the first-ever Orlando Sentinel Digital Journalism Scholarship, an award that includes more than $6,000 each in scholarships as well as the opportunity to get an up-close-and-personal look at digital journalism through an internship in the Sentinels Interactive Department.

This is an excellent opportunity to build experience in the workplace, said How.  This will allow me to improve upon my knowledge of the print and digital domain of the media industry and allow me to achieve my overall career aspirations.

How, of Melbourne, started his tenure at UCF as an art education major.  However, after working for a Web design company, he found his way to the School of Film and Digital Media. How and the other scholarship recipient, senior Jordan Rogers, are majoring in Digital Media.

Rogers, of Jacksonville, became interested in video production work when he was in the fifth grade.  Since that time, he has taken a few basic courses in video.  It wasnt until two years ago, when Rogers offered to shoot and edit a wedding video for a friend, that he realized his talent was worthy of a professional calling. 

By the time Rogers entered college, he had made 20 independent films and documentaries, both educational and entertaining.  A former graphics intern with Orange County, Roger sees his video production opportunity with the Sentinel as the pathway to a possible career after he earns his degree.

Ive always wanted to work with electronics and computers in some way, said Rogers.  Now I realize I want to use computers as a tool to accomplish something creative, and video producing does just that.

Rogers says internships in his field are fairly uncommon, so he was pleasantly surprised to find a position at a reputable company like the Sentinel. 

The growing digital journalism field includes such functions as Web producing, camera work, video editing and Flash programming.  The increasing prominence of multimedia in the Sentinels daily reporting spurred the company to create the scholarship.  
Digital media is our future, said Orlando Sentinel Vice President & Interactive Director Julie Anderson.  The Sentinel needs people with advanced skills to communicate news and information on various digital platforms.
   
The scholarship, offered to full-time seniors at UCFs School of Film and Digital Media, was a much larger gift than originally expected.  This year the first Orlando Sentinel endowed scholarship awarded almost double the intended amount of money and was awarded to two students instead of one. 

 We were fortunate because we had some really talented students apply for the scholarship, and we were happy that it worked out that we could offer two this first year, Anderson said.

Created from an endowment established in December 2005, the Sentinel plans to award the Digital Media scholarship every school year from now on. 

To qualify for the scholarship, students must possess at least a 3.0 overall Grade Point Average, complete a full application detailing their academic and extracurricular experience and submit a UCF transcript, three reference letters (including one from a UCF faculty member), a resume and a digital portfolio of work.  Applicants are also judged on essays detailing their future career plans, why they chose their major, and how the internship and scholarship will help them achieve their goals.

The scholarship money awarded by the Sentinel will help How and Rogers with their academic expenses throughout the year.  However, an excerpt from Hows application essay best describes the value of their internships at the Sentinel.

Just to be able to work with professionals who are part of the Orlando Sentinel team is an opportunity that can not be measured in dollars and cents, he said.
