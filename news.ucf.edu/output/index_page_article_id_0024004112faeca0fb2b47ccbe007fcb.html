HItt: Future is Bright

By Chad Binette January 30, 2004

Photo: Jerry Klein

John Hitt addresses hundreds at Orlando Chamber Luncheon.

Full Photo

Story Images

Click thumbnails for full-size images.

Related Contacts and Links:

  • See the talk

  • Print this Page

A capacity crowd in a downtown Orlando ballroom applauded the University of Central Floridas plans to pursue a medical school and warmly welcomed new football coach George OLeary on Friday during UCF President John Hitts "State of the University" address.

During his presentation at an Orlando Regional Chamber of Commerce trustees luncheon, Hitt said a medical school is a "necessity" for such a large university and for the community.

"A medical school in a city the size of Orlando is not a luxury but a necessity as our population continues to increase in numbers and in age," he said to applause from the audience at the Ballroom at Church Street.

Hitt discussed research, community service and statistics demonstrating the high quality of UCF students. He mentioned UCFs 11 nursing centers, the Center for Autism and Related Disabilities and a clinic that offers free counseling services to 1,100 Central Floridians a year.

The average SAT score for UCF freshmen last fall was 1176, and the 34 National Merit scholars in the fall freshman class was the second highest total in the state, behind only the University of Florida, he said.

UCF is nearing the $100 million mark in annual research funding from federal, state and private sources, Hitt said, specifically praising the College of Education and the Center for Research and Education in Optics and Lasers, or CREOL.

"UCF is committed to be the nations premier metropolitan research university -- and an institution of which you can continue to be proud," he said.

The president introduced OLeary and praised him for recruiting "the right kind of young men" who will be good representatives of the school. He also announced a new partnership with the Chamber of Commerce in which chamber officials will help UCF sell season tickets for football this fall.

Text of Speech:

It is a pleasure to be with you today. Id like to recognize audience members who have helped put our region--and your university--on the map.

All of us at UCF appreciate and value the trust you, and everyone in this room, have placed in us as we look for ways to strengthen and diversify our economy, as well as to improve the quality of life in the Central Florida communities we serve.

Your support continues to be critical as the university and our region move forward on their remarkable journey from promise to prominence.

I appreciate the opportunity to report on the state of UCF during our 40th anniversaryand, if past is indeed prologue, then I can tell you that the state of your university is good.

In fact, the future is so bright that youd all better start wearing those shades that are there on your table. Now Id like to tell you what Orlando and our region can expect from UCF in the months and years ahead.

UCF is now the second largest university in the state and the 14th largest university in the nation.

Enrollment at our Orlando and regional campuses will approach 50,000 students by 2010. More than 110,000 baccalaureate degrees have been awarded since we began offering classes in 1968.

Our 20,000th master's degree and 1,000th doctoral degree were awarded in the Spring of 2003.

UCF ranks 20th in the nation in the number of bachelor's degrees awarded to Hispanics, 51st in masters degrees, and 96th in doctoral degrees.

Approximately 25 percent of Floridas community college students who earn an A.A. degree and then continue their education at a state university enroll at UCF.

Applications for our Fall 2004 freshman class are currently running 12 percent higher than this time last yearand the number of applications from National Merit Scholars is up 25 percent.

Enrollment funding for education is an investment in our regionsince nearly 60 percent of our graduates stay in Central Florida to live and work.

Were so pleased that Governor Bush has budgeted $50 million for enrollment growth, much of which will come to UCF. This amounts to about two-thirds of the $82 million the Board of Governors estimated would be needed to meet the enrollment needs of our state universities.

We would appreciate your support for our efforts to gain enrollment funding if we are to honor our commitments to expand access to a quality education for our students and to attract high wage jobs to our region.

As you heard in the video, the quality of our students just keeps getting better. Let me remind you once again just how good our students are.

The average SAT score for our 4,200+ full-time incoming freshmen this year was 1176. The Burnett Honors College SAT average this fall was 1329 for over 500 entering students, and the 34 National Merit scholars in the fall class ranked UCF second only to UF among the state universities in the number of National Merits we enrolled.

If access to a quality education continues to be one of our priorities, so is our accent on the individual. And it pays off.

Our students are successful. Among UCF graduates you will find the former chair of the U.S. Council of Economic Advisors; chief of staff for Governor Jeb Bush; president of Walt Disney World Resorts; executive vice president and general manager of Sea World; senior vice president of Darden Restaurants; executive director of the Orange County Convention Center; a Florida State Senator or Representative; Orange County Chairman; an Orlando City Commissioner, and director of NASAs space shuttle program.

Were especially proud of Tyler Fisher, a Burnett Honors College graduate in English and Spanish, who last year was named one of 32 national Rhodes Scholars. He is the first student from UCF to be chosen for this prestigious scholarship and is currently studying at Oxford University and writing to us about having tea with the Queen.

And just this past September, Ericka Dunlap, a member of the Presidents Leadership Council, was named Miss America for 2004. After her graduation from UCF, she will use her $50,000 pageant scholarship to attend law school.

UCF is now a full-fledged residential campus, second only to the University of Florida in the number of owned, operated, and affiliated beds. 4, 259 of our students live on campus, including 400 who live in our extensive Greek Park. UCF-affiliated housing adjacent to the campus adds 3,756 additional beds, bringing the total number of beds to 8,015.

Our new shuttle service transports between four and six thousand students each day to the campus from surrounding apartment complexes. According to a recent survey, nearly 80 percent of these students would have driven their cars onto campus if the shuttles had not been not available. In addition, we operate an on-campus shuttle for the convenience of our students and to help cut down on traffic.

You or one of your neighbors, friends, family members, or colleagues has probably benefited from one of UCFs many outreach programs.

Our nursing students provide medical services to nearly 40,000 people at 11 community nursing centers in Apopka, Bithlo, Cocoa, Melbourne, Merritt Island, Sanford, and Winter Parkas well as Orlando.

Our Center for Autism and Related Disabilities serves more than 1,300 children and adults.

The UCF Community Counseling Clinic offers free individual and family counseling sessions to nearly 1,100 Central Floridians each year.

Tens of thousands of Central Floridians come to campus each year to attend conferences, athletic contests, and arts events. The UCF Arena hosted more than 225,000 people last year at events ranging from volleyball camps and basketball games to robotics competitions. The Golden Knights welcomed nearly 100,000 fans to the Citrus Bowl last seasonand we expect the attendance to rise considerably next fall as Coach George OLeary guides our football team to victory.

Shaping our academic and research programs to meet the needs of our region is embodied in our mission as a metropolitan research university.

The new Rosen School of Hospitality Management, a 159,000-square-foot campus, just opened on a 20-acre site in the heart of Central Floridas tourist corridor. It is a statement of our commitment to the industry that is the foundation of our local economy.

We participate in numerous other partnerships in business and industry, education, and the performing arts. We have, for example, a strong presence at Loch Haven Park: The Orlando Repertory Theatre and the Orlando-UCF Shakespeare Festival at Loch Haven and Lake Eola.

We have been given permission by our Board of Trustees to assess the feasibility of building a UCF Medical School. We already participate in an accelerated medical school program with the University of South Florida College of Medicine. Don Jernigan, president of Florida Hospital and John Hillenmayer, president of ORHS, support a feasibility study, and Kenneth Bradley, Senior Vice President and Administrator of Winter Park Hospital wrote recently to offer his support. He commented that Orlando is the largest city in the U.S. without a medical school and is home to two of the largest hospitals in the U.S.

Our decision to pursue the establishment of a medical school is recognition of the expanding health care needs of one of the fastest growing regions in the nation. A medical school in a city the size of Orlando is not a luxury but a necessity as our population continues to increase in numbers and in age.

It is also consistent with the research currently underway at UCF where our faculty are exploring ways to cure cancer, prevent cardiovascular disease, treat brain injuries, and understand Crohns disease. A medical school at a university the size of UCF is no longer a luxury but a necessity.

Of the top 50 research universities in the nation, 44 have such a facilitymaking the universities, and the cities in which they are located, magnets for high-paying jobs and hot beds of economic development.

Strengthening and diversifying our economic base is equally important to the long-term well-being of our community.

Led by the College of Education, the School of Optics and our Center for Research and Education in Optics and Lasers, we are quickly approaching the $100 million level in research funding from federal, state, and private sources. And, we recently received our 100th patent.

A study conducted by MIT placed UCF 2 nd in Florida only to the University of Florida and 38th in the nation in terms of overall research strength.

The same MIT study ranked UCF highest in the Southeast in terms of the current impact of technology, ahead of the University of Florida, Georgia Institute of Technology, North Carolina State University, and Duke University.

Adjacent to UCF, the Central Florida Research Park is ranked among the top ten research parks in the nation. Its annual payroll is approximately $644 million and it houses nearly 90 companies with more than 9,400 employees whose average annual salary exceeds $60,000.

Floridas High-Tech Corridor, which stretches from the Space Coast to Tampa Bay, is home to more than 158,000 employees working at nearly 6,800 companies.

Eight years ago, UCF and the University of South Florida formed the Florida High-Tech Corridor Council whose mission is to attract, retain, and expand high-tech industry in the region.

Since its inception, the Council has partnered with more than 200 companies on 400 projectsand has provided $34 million in research funding. With a $68 million match in funds from the partnering companies, the Council has dedicated more than $102 million to corridor companies working on projects in aviation and aerospace, information technology, medical technologies, microelectronics, optics and photonics, and modeling, simulation, and training.

The companies housed at the UCF High Tech Incubator hold 47 trademarks, 64 trade secrets, 74 copyrights, and 286 patentsand

revenues generated for 2003 are projected to top $75 million.

Today, it is impossible to imagine Central Florida without the University of Central Florida. It is also hard to imagine that, in just 40 years, what was once a small school in a small town has risen from promise to prominence and is now nationally and internationally recognized as a leading metropolitan research university located in a world-class community. The university is now the sixth largest employer in the region, with a current operating budget exceeding a half a billion dollars and a payroll of approximately two hundred and thirteen million dollars.

There can be no great city without a great universityand no university can be truly great without the support of the community it calls home. UCF is committed to be the nations premier metropolitan research universityand an institution of which you can continue to be proud.

Thanks to all of you, this region and your university will continue to move from promise to prominence. And our future is looking so bright that youll be needing those shades we gave you.
