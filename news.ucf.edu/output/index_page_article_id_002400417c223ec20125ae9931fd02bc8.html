Spring Break Fun? Helping Homeless Animals and Building Micro-Enterprises

By Jessica Wycoff March 5, 2010

Photo: Courtesy of the College of Business Administration

Business students will travel to Panama for the second time to help the indigenous Koskuna tribe.

More than 100 University of Central Florida students will be caring for abused animals in Puerto Rico, researching habitats at the National Key Deer Refuge and working with children and their families at homeless shelters during next week’s Spring Break.

College of Business Administration students will travel to Panama to help people create micro-enterprises, with a focus on sustainability and imparting basic business knowledge and skills. This will be the second time a UCF group has traveled to the region to help the indigenous Koskuna tribe, and sponsors include the college, Student Government Association and Princeton Review.

A group of Physical Therapy students will spend its spring break in Kingston, Jamaica, where they will be distributing medical equipment and educating caregivers of children with special needs living in orphanages. UCF students have traveled to Jamaica to help orphans for the past five years, and they are part of an ongoing partnership with the Florida Hospital Outreach program SHARES International.

About two-dozen students from UCF’s DeVos Sport Business Management graduate program and other undergraduates will travel to New Orleans to rebuild homes in the Lower Ninth Ward and St. Bernard Parish. This will be the 24th weeklong service trip for the Hope for Stanley Alliance, which started at UCF.

Teams of students and faculty advisers will volunteer in nine states through Alternative Spring Break (ASB) programs, taking part in community service projects and learning about social issues. Official Alternative Spring Break trips are coordinated through Volunteer UCF, a student-led organization that promotes community service and education on social issues.

ASB projects this year include:

-- Students will travel to San Juan, Puerto Rico, to volunteer at Save A Sato Foundation. “Sato” is slang for street dog, and the nonprofit organization is dedicated to rescuing homeless and abused animals from the streets and beaches. Volunteers will be photographing the satos, helping with food and care and maintaining the shelter.

-- Students will travel to Big Pine Key to assist with research on deer habitats at the National Key Deer Refuge in the Florida Keys.

-- Habitat for Humanity will be hosting two separate trips, to Monroe, N.C., and Danville, Va., where students will aid in building low-cost homes for families.

-- Students working with the Louisiana United Methodist Disaster Response in New Orleans will help with Hurricane Katrina reconstruction efforts by cleaning out old homes and aiding in efforts to build new ones.

-- At the Cumberland Trail in Crossville, Tenn., students will help build and maintain the 300-mile trail, which celebrates the heritage of Tennessee.

-- Students volunteering in Chestertown, Md., will assist with the rescue and placement of abandoned border collies at the Mid-Atlantic Border Collie Rescue.

-- Students will work as part of the Junior Achievement program in Birmingham, Ala., and teach elementary school students about making and managing money and contributing to their communities.

-- Other programs involve providing therapeutic horseback riding for people with disabilities in Kiln, Miss., and working with the Nicolas House in Atlanta, assisting families in transition from homelessness to self-sufficiency.
