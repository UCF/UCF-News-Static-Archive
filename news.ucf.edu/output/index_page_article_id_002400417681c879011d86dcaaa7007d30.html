Emmy-Nominated Actress, Sports VP Awarded Top Alumni Honors

By UCF Staff Nov. 10, 2008

Photo: Jeffrey Garner

Cheryl Hines (left) and Cathy Weeden (right) were honored at the annual UCF Alumni Association's Black & Gold Gala.

An Emmy-nominated actress and a top executive for Sun Sports and FSN Florida were honored during the University of Central Florida Alumni Association’s annual Black & Gold Gala on Friday, Nov. 7.

UCF’s Alumni Association presented 14 awards during the reception and banquet in the UCF Arena. Among the honorees were film and television actress Cheryl Hines and Cathy Weeden, the senior vice president and general manager for Sun Sports and FSN Florida.

Weeden received the Distinguished Alumnus Award, the association’s highest annual honor. She graduated from UCF in 1986 with a bachelor’s degree in Journalism.

Weeden is responsible for all aspects of the sports network’s ongoing operations, including programming, production, advertising sales, marketing and promotions, affiliate relations, media relations and administration. She oversees the network’s contracts and relationships with its team partners, including the Florida Marlins, Florida Panthers, Miami Heat, Orlando Magic, Tampa Bay Rays, Tampa Bay Lightning, Florida State University and the University of Florida, as well as the Southeastern and Atlantic Coast conferences.

Hines, ’90, received the Michelle Akers Award, given to alumni who are recognized in their chosen careers on an international scope. Hines earned her bachelor’s degree in Radio/Television.

Hines is a two-time Emmy nominee for her role as Cheryl David on HBO’s Golden Globe Award-winning series “Curb Your Enthusiasm,” the brainchild of Larry David, co-creator of “Seinfeld.” The show has been nominated for numerous Emmy Awards, including Best Supporting Actress for Cheryl, Best Actor for Larry and Best Comedy Series.  The show’s sixth season recently aired on HBO, and it will be returning for a highly anticipated seventh season.   
 
Hines is also establishing herself as one of Hollywood's most sought-after film actresses. She has a number of films slated for release this year, as well as two films that were recently released.  

The Sundance Film Festival hit, “Henry Poole Was Here,” was recently in movie theaters. Another of Hines’ films expected in theaters this year is the independent feature "Bart Got a Room," which stars William H. Macy and is directed by Brian Hecker.

Hines’ other memorable film roles include a starring role in the independent project "The Grand," an improvised comedy set in the world of professional poker, which co-starred Woody Harrelson, David Cross and Ray Romano.  Hines also starred opposite Keri Russell in the romantic comedy “Waitress,”a huge hit at the 2007 Sundance Film Festival.

Other winners of UCF Alumni Association awards included:

Service to UCF: JoAnne Puglisi, ’73, for her many years of serving on the UCF Alumni Association and UCF Foundation boards and committees, as well as the Dean’s Advisory Council for the College of Engineering and Computer Science.

Community Service: Larry Tobin, ’83, for his many years of volunteering in the community with the Rotary Club of Oviedo, the Central Florida American Diabetes Association and the University Presbyterian Church.

Distinguished Student: Brittany Moscato, majoring in Molecular Biology and Microbiology and Spanish. Moscato holds a 3.94 GPA and is a member of the Order of Pegasus, Burnett Honors College, Pre-Professional Medical Society, President’s Leadership Council and International Medical Outreach.

Additional award winners were: J. Carlos Gittens, ‘98, a project manager with Jacobs Engineering Group; Ron Ellis, ’95, assistant director of bands at UCF; Henry E. Davis, ’70, a circuit judge in Duval County for Florida’s Fourth Judicial Circuit; Richard Ellenburg, ’78, a science lab teacher at Camelot Elementary School who was named Florida’s Teacher of the Year; Andy Schwalb, ’84, senior vice president and chief technology officer for Walt Disney World Parks and Resorts IT; Judy Thames, ’73, the state president for Florida AARP; Bucky Boaz, ’94, co-founder of The miniER Corp. Adult and Pediatric Urgent Care Center; Jose R. Rodriguez, ’74, a circuit judge in Orange County for Florida’s Ninth Judicial Circuit; and Alinda I. Ramos, ’96, the vice president and director of operations for Premiere Show Group.
