Emeritus Awards: Lifetimes of Distinction

By UCF Staff April 6, 2011

Photo: Jason Greene

Peter Panousis of the College of Sciences was named Dean Emeritus during Wednesday's ceremony.

Fourteen University of Central Florida professors were awarded emeritus status Wednesday at the annual Founders’ Day ceremony.

Faculty and administrators who earn emeritus status have worked at the university for at least five years, and they have retired or are retiring this year. Emeritus status grants them a lifetime link to UCF and honors the major professional contributions and distinction that they achieved in their careers at the university.

William Bozeman, Professor Emeritus, has served a 26-year career as a UCF faculty member and a department chair in the UCF College of Education, where he has distinguished himself nationally as a leader in the field of educational leadership. His expertise in program design and coordination helped develop a doctoral program in educational leadership. Bozeman also led and secured funds for the Danforth Program and the Progress Energy Leadership Institute.

Kristin G. Congdon, Professor Emerita, will retire in May after a 23-year career in the UCF Department of Philosophy. She has worked with numerous students and mentored many even after graduation. She also has mentored faculty members, particularly women, in their research and in the tenure process. A champion of the arts and humanities, Congdon has fostered interdisciplinary conversation within and outside of UCF. She serves as a longstanding resource for the Florida Humanities Council. Her achievements brought her national and international recognition for which she was awarded the UCF Presidential Award for Special Merit.

Ronald D. Dutton, Professor Emeritus, recently retired after a 39-year career at UCF. He joined the university as an assistant professor of Computer Science and later became a professor, director and chair of the Department of Computer Science. He established the Computer Science Programming Team in 1986 and continues to be actively involved. Dutton published a large number of papers in international journals and conferences. He remains active in research with a journal paper recently accepted for publication and another one in review, and he continues to supervise a doctoral student.

Terry Frederick, Professor Emeritus, will retire in August after a 36-year career at UCF. Now an associate dean in the College of Arts and Humanities, he founded the UCF Department of Computer Science and served as its first chair. He designed the Computer Science curriculum, developed the first doctoral program at UCF and the state’s first doctoral program in Computer Science. Frederick also established the first departmental level e-mail system and recruited the first National Merit Finalist to UCF. He secured more than $4.5 million in funding, and his efforts led to the development of the nationally acclaimed UCF Computer Science Programming Team and the naming of the UCF Computer Science Program as a state “Center of Excellence.”

Richard Gilson, Professor Emeritus, has been with UCF for 25 years in the Department of Psychology and is a top scholar in Human Factors Psychology. He has published widely and secured more than $900,000 in funding. Gilson supervised more than 20 dissertations and regularly taught some of the largest undergraduate classes. He approaches teaching with the same enthusiasm that he shows in his research and takes great pride in being able to memorize the names of all his students. He is remarkably generous in his support and help for junior faculty members and students. Gilson chaired the committee that proposed the development of the Human Factors Psychology doctoral program.

Jean Kijek, Associate Professor Emerita, retired in 2010 after a 25-year career at UCF. She served as chair for the Department of Nursing, hired faculty members, provided oversight for the curriculum and established partnerships and outreach. Kijek led the transition from department to school to the College of Nursing. She provided the vision and strategic plan to develop a master’s and two doctoral programs in Nursing. She also helped establish nursing research committees at Orlando Regional Medical Center – now Orlando Health – and Florida Hospital. In addition, Kijek secured grant funding for many college and university initiatives.

Leslie Sue Lieberman, Professor Emerita, joined UCF in 2001 as a professor of Anthropology and founding director of the Women’s Research Center. Her professional accomplishments include scholarly success and a willingness to give back to her profession and the higher education community. She secured funding to develop programs to attract young women into science and engineering and led the Women’s Research Center into partnerships with national and local organizations. Lieberman has been active in teaching, mentoring graduate students and junior faculty members and running a summer abroad program in Croatia.

Amar Mukherjee, Professor Emeritus, recently retired after joining UCF in 1979 as a professor of Computer Science. He also served as the Department of Computer Science’s chair. He was a visiting scientist at the National Science Foundation, assisting the director of Computer and Information Science and Engineering to identify new strategic directions. Mukherjee has a distinguished record in service and research. He is the author of several books and a large number of publications on a wide range of computer science areas. Under his leadership, UCF attained its first full accreditation in mechanical and aerospace engineering in 1996.

David Nicholson, Professor Emeritus, retired after a 20-year career at UCF as a professor and chair of the Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering. Upon joining UCF, he immediately started revising and modernizing the curricula in both mechanical and aerospace engineering disciplines. He taught and developed courses, advised students, published extensively and secured more than $1 million in research funding.

Peter Panousis, Dean Emeritus, was the founding dean of the newly established College of Sciences in 2005 and focused on developing the college’s research profile and quality of education during a time of budget challenges. Under his leadership, research founding doubled in five years to $15.3 million last year and placed the college among the top three units for research funding. Panousis was a key player in the development of the High Tech Corridor Council. Upon becoming a faculty member, he established the HiTech Economics Center in the College of Business Administration now known as the Institute for Economic Competitiveness.

Roy Pickering, Professor Emeritus, will retire in October after a 32-year career in the UCF Department of Music. In addition to designing innovative courses, Pickering has been instrumental in guiding his students in obtaining accolades at prestigious competitions at different levels. He has served as chair of the Recital Hearing Committee for 20 years and was the director of the Moulin d’Ande Summer Program. Pickering also has been active in the community and in professional organizations. He has performed in dozens of international festivals and at the most prestigious music schools in the country.

Robert Pritchard, Professor Emeritus, joined UCF in 2003 and is the director of the Industrial Organizational Psychology doctoral program. He has an outstanding record of research and publication and is one of the preeminent scholars in the areas of worker motivation and productivity. His work led to the Productivity Measurement and Enhancement System, which is used by individuals to help them improve their effectiveness. Pritchard’s research is widely cited and has brought him several awards and recognitions. Throughout his career, he has edited or authored nine books, 71 journal articles and book chapters and delivered more than 150 presentations.

Burt Pryor, Professor Emeritus, retired after a 38-year career at UCF.  He served as assistant professor, professor and the director of Graduate Studies in Communication. He personally supervised more than 100 master theses to completion. Pryor was a collegial and supportive member of the faculty. He formally and informally mentored a generation of young professors. He had a solid reputation for developing collaborations that enabled his colleagues to find success. Pryor provided counsel and advice on a wide range of topics and was a generous spirit who was devoted to his work, students and the mission of the university. Pryor published 40 research articles, presented numerous papers, collaborated on textbook chapters and co-authored a textbook and workbook.

George Yeh, Professor Emeritus, joined UCF in 2000 as a professor in the College of Engineering and Computer Science. He led a distinguished career in water resources engineering research. He secured $3 million in funding from the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration to study sea level rise in the northern Gulf of Mexico. Yeh’s efforts have brought national recognition to the Coastal Hydroscience Analysis, Modeling and Predictive Simulations Laboratory at UCF.
