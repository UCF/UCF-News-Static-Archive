UCF's Rosen College Announces 2008 Inductees to Hospitality Hall of Fame

By Ashley McMeel Nov. 7, 2007

Photo: UCF News file

UCF trustee Richard A. Nunis, right, is one of two 2008 inductees to the Central Florida Hospitality Hall of Fame.

The general manager at the Hyatt Regency Orlando International Airport and a retired chairman for Walt Disney Parks and Resorts will be the 2008 inductees into the Central Florida Hospitality Hall of Fame.

Patricia J. Engfer and Richard A. Nunis were selected by industry executives and the faculty and staff of UCF’s Rosen College of Hospitality Management. They will be inducted at the 2008 Grande Hospitality Gala on March 29 at Loews Royal Pacific Resort at Universal Orlando.

A 30-year Hyatt veteran, Engfer has been general manager in Orlando since the hotel opened in 1992. She has led the Orlando/Orange County Convention and Visitors Bureau, Metro Orlando Economic Development Commission and the Central Florida Hotel & Lodging Association. Engfer also will serve as chair of the Orlando Regional Chamber of Commerce in 2009. Engfer has been honored as general manager of the year by the Hyatt Hotels and Central Florida Hotel & Lodging Association, and she also has been named best hotel general manager by Orlando Magazine.

Nunis retired as chairman of Walt Disney Parks and Resorts in 1999 after a 44-year career with the company. He then opened a consulting company, New Business Solutions Inc. Nunis also was a charter member of the UCF Board of Trustees and served as chairman from 2003 to 2006. He remains a university trustee. Nunis has served on the board of the Travel & Tourism Advisory Council for the United States Department of Commerce, and he is chairman emeritus of The Recreation Roundtable, an advisory committee of recreation industry leaders. He also was involved in the establishment of Enterprise Florida and is a past chair of the Florida Council of 100.

Proceeds raised at the gala will support academic enhancements at the Rosen College of Hospitality Management. During the last three years, the Rosen College has experienced significant growth in enrollment and an increase in the number of faculty needed to properly instruct future hospitality leaders. The growth has helped to shape the Rosen College into one of the fastest-growing hospitality programs in the world with more than 50 faculty members from more than 20 countries, three undergraduate degrees, a master’s degree and a doctoral program. Faculty and students conduct research projects that benefit the ever-changing hospitality industry.

At the 2007 Grande Hospitality Gala, William C. Peeper, former president of the Orlando/Orange County Convention and Visitors Bureau, and Richard J. Walsh, who retired last year as Darden Restaurants’ senior vice president for corporate affairs and now chairs the UCF Board of Trustees, became the first members of the Central Florida Hospitality Hall of Fame. They have received the Crystal Pineapple Award, and their names and photos are being added to the Hall of Fame located at the Rosen College.

For additional information, visit www.hospitality.ucf.edu/grandegala.htm.
