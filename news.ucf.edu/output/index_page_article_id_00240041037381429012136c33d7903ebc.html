UCF to Showcase Academic Programs During Family Weekend

By Julie Primrose Sept. 22, 2009

Photo: Jacque Brund

UCF will highlight student achievements and faculty expertise during College Open House Day.

REMINDERS: The UCF campus will open for tailgating at 8 a.m. Saturday. Glass bottles and animals (except for service animals) are not allowed on campus on game days. For more information on tailgating, parking and other game day policies, visit www.UCFGameDay.com.

Several University of Central Florida colleges will open their doors to parents, students and fans before the annual Family Weekend football game on Saturday, Oct. 3.

College Open House Day will showcase nine of the university’s colleges. Fans will have the opportunity to tour several of the university’s buildings that are typically closed on game days.

Activities are scheduled between 10 a.m. and 2 p.m. Kickoff for the football game against Memphis is at 3:30 p.m.

“We encourage fans to come early to the game and join us for College Open House Day,” said Alison Morrison-Shetlar, vice provost and dean of Undergraduate Studies and one of the event’s organizers. “It’s important to remember that academics are the foundation for our success, and we are eager to showcase our students’ achievements and faculty expertise to parents and other fans.”

UCF’s newest college, the College of Medicine, will host an event titled “The Doctor is In” from 10 a.m. until noon in the Health and Public Affairs I building. College representatives will be available to discuss the college’s progress since classes began in August and faculty advances in biomedical research.

For the College of Business Administration, Sean Snaith, the director of UCF’s Institute for Economic Competitiveness, will discuss the national economic forecast and the state of the recession at 11 a.m. in Business Administration I.

The College of Nursing will show how nursing students use simulation technology to improve patient care at 10:15 and 11:15 a.m. in Health and Public Affairs I. This event has limited seating and is available on a first-come, first-served basis.

The College of Engineering and Computer Science will hold a Senior Design Expo featuring student projects from 10 a.m. until noon in the Harris Corporation Engineering Center.

The College of Health and Public Affairs will host a free presentation at 10:45 a.m. by Mitch Salter, a certified athletic trainer and member of UCF's athletic training faculty. He will offer tips on how to stay safe on the playing field.

Other events will include an “Are You Smarter Than a Fifth Grader” competition in the College of Education and a tour of the art gallery within the College of Arts and Humanities. There also will be guest speakers and demonstrations in the College of Sciences and the Burnett Honors College.

For more detailed information on all of the college events, please visit http://www.openhouse.ucf.edu/.

Family Weekend Activities:

There will be many events held on campus during Family Weekend from Friday, Oct. 2, through Sunday, Oct. 4. They include:

-- Parents and siblings over the age of 18 are invited to the UCF Recreation & Wellness Center on Friday, Oct. 2, for free use of the gym. A picture identification is required.

-- The Rosen College of Hospitality Management will host “Rosen’s Got Talent,” a comedy showcase held in the Darden Auditorium on the Rosen campus at 7:30 p.m. Friday, Oct. 2. Admission is free with seating on a first-come, first-served basis.

-- Student Development and Enrollment Services is hosting a Parent Breakfast at 9 a.m. on Saturday, Oct. 3. UCF administrators will be in attendance to meet parents. This event is free; however, you must register by Sept. 24 to attend. To register, visit http://www.getinvolveducf.com/familyweekend/.

-- The FAIRWINDS Alumni Center will host a tent party on Saturday, Oct. 3, at 12:30 p.m. The cost for the meal is $10 for members and $20 for non-members. Packages with tickets for the football game are also available. Please RSVP by Monday, Sept. 28.

For more information, visit http://www.getinvolveducf.com/familyweekend/index.html.

UCF Stands For Opportunity: The University of Central Florida is a metropolitan research university that ranks as the 5th largest in the nation with more than 52,000 students. UCF's first classes were offered in 1968. The university offers impressive academic and research environments that power the region's economic development. UCF's culture of opportunity is driven by our diversity, Orlando environment, history of entrepreneurship and our youth, relevance and energy. For more information, visit http://news.ucf.edu.
