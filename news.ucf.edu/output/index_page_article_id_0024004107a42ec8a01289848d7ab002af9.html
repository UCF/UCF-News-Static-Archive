UCF, Experts Shine in National Media Spotlight

By Grant J. Heston June 29, 2010

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Grant J. Heston

    • 407.823.5988

  • Print this Page

Share this story:

  • Share on Facebook
  • Save This Page
  • Seed Newsvine

Please see below for a review of selected UCF print, broadcast and online media coverage. Follow UCF news on Twitter and Facebook, watch UCF TV and learn more about Strategy, Marketing, Communications & Admissions.

NATIONAL

U.S. News: Crist May Avoid Fate of Other Party-Switchers
The fall of party-switchers like Sen. Arlen Specter and Rep. Parker Griffith signaled to similar candidates that voters don't like to be manipulated, say experts. But recent polls suggest that Republican-turned-independent Florida Gov. Charlie Crist has done a decent job convincing voters that his late April move is about policy and not politics.
-- This story quotes Political Science professor Aubrey Jewett.

Association of American Colleges and Universities: Increasing Student Success: Smaller Classes, Innovative Pedagogy at UCF
When administrators at the University of Central Florida conducted an internal research study in early 2009 to determine how class size could affect student success and retention rates in gateway courses, they found that smaller classes probably wouldn’t affect student success—with two important exceptions.
 -- This story features UCF’s innovative changes in composition and algebra classes.

The Economist: Paradise Foreclosed
The boom has left Florida with an excess of houses, shops and debt.
-- This story quotes Sean Snaith, director of the Institute for Economic Competitiveness.

Christian Science Monitor: Judge Won't Budge on Deep-Water Drilling Moratorium
The New Orleans federal judge who on Wednesday struck down the Obama administration’s six-week moratorium on deep-water drilling refused a government request to delay that decision Thursday.
-- This story quotes Political Science professor Peter Jacques.

CBS News: Harry Potter Theme Park Opens to Eager Muggles
Until now, Harry Potter fans could only imagine the sensation of quaffing a butterbeer, finding a magic wand at Ollivander's or escaping the steam from a snarling dragon's snout.
-- This story quotes Rosen College of Hospitality Management professor Duncan Dickson. Professor Dickson was featured in several stories connected with the opening of the Harry Potter exhibit.

Archaeology Magazine: Lasers in the Jungle
Even when one is almost directly on top of them, many Maya sites are impossible to see. In the jungle, small palms and brush can spring to 14 feet high in a year, filling the space between towering cedar, mahogany, ramon, and ceiba trees.
-- This article is written by professors Arlen and Diane Chase of Anthropology and John Weishampel of Biology. Their research has been covered extensively by media around the world.

Associated Press: Democrat Rises in Florida Senate Race Despite Odd Baggage
Boxer Mike Tyson was the best man at his wedding. Hollywood madam Heidi Fleiss lived in his guest house. And the television ads for Jeff Greene's out-of-nowhere bid for Florida's Senate seat are financed by a fortune made from betting on the fall of the housing market.
-- This article quotes Political Science professor Aubrey Jewett.

Business Week: Junk Food Sugar May Help Some Fat Cells Proliferate
New research suggests that the ubiquitous sugar known as fructose may cause certain fat cells in children to multiply faster, which might play a key role in childhood and adult obesity.
-- This article quotes James Rippe, chairman of the Center for Lifestyle Medicine at UCF.

Houston Chronicle: Football Fuels Colleges’ Run for Conference Pay Dirt
Forget about the searing heat. Football season arrived with a vengeance this month, evidenced by backroom negotiations that almost unwound the Big 12 Conference.
-- This story quotes Bill Sutton of the DeVos Sport Business Management program.

STATE/LOCAL

Orlando Sentinel: Retiring UCF Provost Reflects
Retirement is a natural occasion that causes you to look in life's rearview mirror and evaluate where the road has taken you. As I look back on my career, the most prominent vision I see is the fortunate decision I made to come to Orlando and the University of Central Florida.
-- This My Word column was authored by Provost and Executive Vice President Terry Hickey.

Naples Daily News: Collier Leaders Visit Orlando’s Medical City for Economic Potential
Orlando is getting healthier, both physically and economically. Much of the credit goes to what is called “Medical City,” a 600-acre park where half a dozen medical facilities are under construction in the Lake Nona development outside of Orlando.

St. Petersburg Times: President’s Speech Outlines Long-Range Recovery
Using his first Oval Office speech to underscore the severity of the gulf disaster, President Barack Obama vowed Tuesday night to hold BP accountable for its "recklessness" and urged Congress to pass a broad energy plan to break the nation's dependence on oil.
-- This story quotes Political Science professor Aubrey Jewett

Tampa Tribune: Concerts Aid Rays at Gate
Not satisfied with the hot bat of Evan Longoria or the speed of Carl Crawford or the cannon arm of David Price, the Tampa Bay Rays have recruited Vanilla Ice and other bands to help fill the seats at the next three Friday night home games.
-- This story quotes Bill Sutton of the DeVos Sport Business Management program.

Florida Today: Number of Concealed Weapons Holders in Brevard Soars
The 62-year-old man ran to his car in the parking lot of a Space Coast Credit Union in Palm Bay to get his gun after he saw a robber with a grocery bag on his head slip a note to the teller. He held the man at gunpoint until help arrived.
-- This story quotes Criminal Justice professor Ross Wolf.

Orlando Sentinel: Teaching Still Hot Career Choice
Broward County schools handed pink slips to more than 500 teachers this month. More than 22,000 of cash-strapped California's 300,000 teachers could lose their jobs.
-- This story quotes Sandra Robinson, dean of the College of Education

Orlando Sentinel: UCF Students Can Carpool With an Internet Twist
Need a ride to campus, UCF students? Check your Facebook page. Next week, University of Central Florida will become the first school in the state to offer Zimride, a fast-growing online carpooling service that connects drivers and passengers through a school's social network.
