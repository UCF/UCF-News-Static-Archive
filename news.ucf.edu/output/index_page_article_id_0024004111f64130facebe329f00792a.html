An Evening of Revelry

By David Brunner October 20, 2003

Photo: News File Photo

29 years of holiday cheer continue in December.

Full Photo

Related Contacts and Links:

  • David Brunner

    • 407-823-5678

  • Print this Page

In the manner and spirit of Elizabethan England, the UCF Madrigal Singers invite one and all to an unforgettable holiday celebration as they welcome the season in the Great Hall of UCFs Live Oak Room. Fanfares on herald trumpets, intimate music on period instruments, ornate costumes, lusty singers of diverse madrigals from far and wide, jesters and buffoons, and a sumptuous feaste of roast beef await. Share in the culinary delights of the steaming Wassail Bowl, the Boar's Head, and the Flaming Plum Pudding and enjoy madrigals and songs of the Christmas season sung by the UCF Madrigal Singers under the direction of David Brunner. The evenings festivities include singing, Renaissance dancing, brass and early music consorts, jesters and jugglers, a Carol Sing and the Royal Supper of Hot Wassail, Fresh Bread Loaves, Assorted Fruit and Cheeses, Roast Beefe, Oven Roasted New Potatoes, Green Beans Almondine, Yorkshire Pudding, English Plum Pudding with Hard and Brandy Sauces and coffee. The 29th annual performance of the Madrigal Feaste will be presented December 12 and 13, 2003 in the Live Oak Room of the UCF Student Resource Center. Doors open at 7:00 p.m., the festivities beginning promptly at 7:30. Admission is $45.00 per person. Reservations must be made and paid for in advance. Early response is recommended, as seating is limited and there are no refunds or exchanges. For ticket information call 407/823-5687 at your earliest convenience.
