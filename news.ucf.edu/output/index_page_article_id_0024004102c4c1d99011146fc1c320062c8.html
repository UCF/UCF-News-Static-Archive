UCF Students Take Top Prize in Security Awareness Video Contest

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala May 15, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala

    • 407-823-6120

  • Video link

  • Print this Page

Two University of Central Florida students earned $1,000 and the top award in the 2007 Security Awareness Video Contest.

Friends Daniel Garcia and Scott Polcyn teamed up to produce a funny video highlighting the dangers of identify theft for the contest sponsored by the EDUCAUSE/Internet2 Computer and Network Security Task Force, the National Cyber Security Alliance and the ResearchChannel.

The goal of the contest was to create videos that explain computer security problems and give students specific ways they can safeguard their computers and personal information. Two categories of videos were sought: two-minute training or instructional videos, and 30-second public service announcements (PSAs).

Joel Hartman, vice provost for Information Technologies and Resources at UCF, said he was proud of the students and their work.

We recognize that today's students are not only avid consumers of information but also have the ability to be creators of information, Hartman said. It is also a positive step to involve students in a process that helps them not only understand contemporary issues of online risk and responsibility, but to also help address these issues.

Garcia and Polcyns video is a spoof that shows how easily a thief can steal your identity thanks to the dumb things we all do without thinking, such as leaving your computer connected to the Internet 24-7.

We just brainstormed and came up with the Identity Theft for Dummies idea, Polcyn said. We wanted something funny. Were glad the judges agreed with us.

The two longtime friends met as freshmen at UCF when they landed in the same dorm room. They stayed friends in part because they have many things in common including the same major - radio and television (production track) and a love of humor. They also are interns with Course Development and Web Services on the main campus.

We wanted to make it as cheesy as possible to make it stand out, Garcia said. Making it stand out was important, because by making it that way, we could deliver the important message of protecting your personal information.

Polcyns roommate, Ty Heflin, played the thief in the video, while Garcia portrays the victim. Polcyn is the narrator of the piece. Polcyn, of Lake Worth, said they plan to give Heflin some of the cash winnings for taking on the role of he bad guy. The rest of the cash they will split.

Garcia, of Miami, said he hopes to finish school and edit television shows or films for a living. Polycn wants to work in post-production.

That would be my dream job, Garcia said. Im just a movie junkie.

The winning videos are available at http://www.researchchannel.org/securityvideo2007/

In all the judges received 56 video submissions from 24 colleges and universities around the country. The first place winners in each category got $1,000, the second place winners earned $800 and bronze winners got $400. Five honorable mentions were also recognized in each category.

Winners were selected based on creativity, technical quality, content and overall effectiveness of delivery.

Other winners came from Texas A & M, Pennsylvania College of Technology, the University of Delaware, Dartmouth College and California State University at Bakersfield.
