Students Honored for Academics, Leadership and Service

Several University of Central Florida students were honored with Founders’ Awards for their commitment to excellence in academics, leadership and community service on Wednesday at the annual Founders’ Day Honors Convocation.

College of Arts and Humanities

Christine Dye, a double major in Political Science and English, found her niche in teaching English as a foreign language. She has worked with non-native speakers from Saudi Arabia, Japan, Colombia and other countries, helping them learn English and better understand American culture. She is also part of a new undergraduate initiative at the Center for Multilingual Multicultural Studies. Dye also volunteers at Union Park Elementary School and with Habitat for Humanity, the Coalition for the Homeless and other nonprofit organizations.

College of Business Administration

Lauren Rehm, a Marketing major, is a LEAD Scholar and a winner of UCF’s Bernard Kissel Outstanding Speaker Competition, and she has a 4.0 GPA. A member of the President’s Leadership Council, she also was a charter member of UCF’s Young Women Leaders Program. She has volunteered with the Salvation Army and on Alternative Spring Break service trips. She is working on an Honors in the Major thesis on the ethics of stealth marketing, and she plans to attend the University of Florida’s law school.

College of Education

Kelsey Tyler, an Elementary Education major,  is an enthusiastic champion for the cause of education, excelling academically as well as participating in countless extracurricular activities promoting education on campus and in the community. Tyler serves as president of UCF’s chapter of Kappa Delta Pi, an international honorary organization in education. She is also a UCF College of Education peer advisor, and she volunteers with a wide variety of local organizations such as Miracle Miles, the Arnold Palmer and Winnie Palmer hospitals and Hands On Orlando.

College of Engineering and Computer Science

Michael Philip Green, an Aerospace Engineering major, is a member of the Burnett Honors College who expects to graduate this spring. He serves as president of two aerospace engineering chapters at UCF, and Sigma Gamma Tau has recognized him as the top aerospace engineering student in the southeastern United States. He has mentored high school students and taught third-grade special education students. He has interned with the launch services program at Kennedy Space Center, and he is leading a group of 75 students vying to launch a rocket into space.

College of Health and Public Affairs

Brenna Egan, a double major in Legal Studies and English, has maintained a 4.0 GPA while remaining actively involved on-campus and in the Central Florida community during her four years at UCF. Egan has been an active member of UCF’s Mock Trial Team, winning five Outstanding Mock Trial Attorney awards at regional and national competitions. She has served as a volunteer teacher with Junior Achievement for the past four years, and she serves as a volunteer jury advisor for the Orange County Teen Court.

Rosen College of Hospitality Management

Renea Anderson, an Event Management major with a minor in Finance, is a mother of five children who skillfully balances a successful academic career, extracurricular activities and volunteer work. She participates in the Honors in the Major program with the Burnett Honors College and was the sole winner of the IMEX/MPI University Challenge during the 2009 Meeting Planners International World Conference Future Leaders Forum. Anderson is also active in the International Society of Event Specialists and the Professional Convention Management Association.

College of Medicine

Jennifer Bazemore, a Molecular and Microbiology major, is a LEAD Scholar, member of the Burnett Honors College and vice president of the UCF Pre-Professional Medical Society.  She has studied the therapeutic potential of drug agents to treat cholera and E. coli infections through the UCF Program for Undergraduate Research Experience (PURE). She is also involved with International Medical Outreach and volunteers locally with Camp Boggy Creek for children with illnesses and Florida Hospital as a patient companion.

College of Nursing

Jennifer Watts, a Nursing major at the Cocoa campus, already had earned a bachelor’s degree in Psychology when she enrolled. She had worked as a case manager for the Children’s Home Society, and that sparked her desire to become a nurse and provide more help for disadvantaged families. She has helped to organize walks for several charities. A former UCF cheerleader, she also coordinates Angels Baskets, an effort to provide food, clothing and toys to families. She is completing her honors thesis about how nursing interventions help patients with critical brain injuries.

College of Sciences

Theresa Trombly, a Psychology major, has excelled academically at UCF and in the scientific community. She is a participant in the Honors in the Major program. Her undergraduate honors thesis, which deals with the anxiety disorder known as selective mutism, has been described by her mentor as exceeding the scope of many master's theses in Psychology. Trombly serves as in integral member of the UCF Anxiety Disorders Clinic, providing clinical services to children. She has won several UCF and national grant competitions.

Undergraduate Studies

Ryan Chabot, an Environmental Studies track of Interdisciplinary Studies major, has balanced academics, athletics and volunteerism throughout his three years at UCF. Chabot is a member of the Burnett Honors College, and he was awarded the 2009 President’s Scholars Award for Study Abroad and service learning. He also participated in the 2009 Alternative Spring Break program in Marathon, Fla., and he is a 2009 Knights Give Back Volunteer. Chabot is the vice president of the UCF Club Tennis team.

Outstanding Thesis Awards

Arts, Humanities, and Social Sciences

First Place: Lalita D. Booth, Financial Education for Low-Income Audiences: A Guide to Program Design, Implementation, and Evaluation; directed by James Gilkeson, Finance

Runner-up: Ari S. Litwin, A Look at the International Manned Space Programs and the Potential for Cooperation; directed by Houman Sadri, Political Science

Sciences, Engineering, and Technology

First Place: Jesse M. Kelly, Numerical Solution of the Two-Phase Incompressible Navier-Stokes Equations Using a GPU-Accelerated Meshless Method; directed by Eduardo Divo, Mechanical, Materials, and Aerospace Engineering

Runner-up: Hilary R. Palmer, Bottled Water Storage Location and Its Impact on Microbiological Quality; directed by Steven Duranceau, Civil, Evironmental, and Construction Engineering
