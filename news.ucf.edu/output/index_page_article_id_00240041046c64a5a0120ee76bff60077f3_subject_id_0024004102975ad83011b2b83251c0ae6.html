UCF Taking Flu-Related Precautions at Commencement Ceremonies

By Chad Binette May 6, 2009

Photo: UCF News File

More than 6,000 UCF students expect to graduate during four ceremonies Friday, May 8, and Saturday, May 9.

University of Central Florida deans will congratulate spring graduates by giving them their diploma covers on stage instead of shaking their hands.

During previous commencement ceremonies, students received their diploma covers from university staff after leaving the stage.

The university also will provide hand sanitizer for graduates and guests at its four commencement ceremonies this weekend. Hand-sanitizing stations and signs will be placed throughout the UCF Arena lobby areas and in the waiting areas for graduates. Orlando Health is providing UCF with the hand sanitizers.

More than 6,000 UCF students expect to graduate during four ceremonies Friday, May 8, and Saturday, May 9. Ceremonies will be held at 9 a.m. and 2 p.m. each day.

Throughout the past week, UCF Health Services staff have been working closely with the Orange County Health Department, Florida Department of Health and Centers for Disease Control and Prevention to actively monitor the H1N1 (swine) flu situation. No cases have been reported at UCF.

To help prevent the spread of illness, UCF advises graduates and guests to take appropriate precautions.

If you are ill, you should stay home to avoid getting others ill. If you cough or sneeze, cover your nose and mouth with a tissue and then throw the tissue away. If you do not have a tissue, cough or sneeze into your sleeve or elbow.
 
To reduce your chance of getting ill, always wash your hands before you eat and avoid touching your eyes, nose or mouth. You can wash your hands with soap and water or use alcohol-based hand sanitizers. If you recognize that others are sick, limit your exposure to them when possible.

This weekend’s precautionary measures supplement UCF’s longstanding efforts to keep its community healthy. Hand-sanitizing stations are located throughout the UCF main campus. Throughout the year, UCF Health Services actively promotes hand washing and other measures to prevent colds and the flu, and it offers seasonal flu vaccines when they are available.

Students are reminded that the UCF Health Center remains open during the semester break. For the center’s hours and updates, go to www.hs.sdes.ucf.edu.

For more information about commencement, visit www.registrar.ucf.edu/commencement.
