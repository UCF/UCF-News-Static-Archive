Please see below for a review of selected print and online media coverage for the University of Central Florida. For a more complete account of stories and breakdown of coverage, please contact News & Information. To view a list of news, events and features from around campus, visit our Web site.

LOCAL / STATE

Orlando Sentinel: New law would let UCF shine with tuition premiums
Lawmakers are setting the stage for the University of Central Florida and two other universities to join the trio of top-ranked state schools that can charge a premium on tuition.

Orlando Sentinel: UCF committed to balance of access and quality
By any measure, it is clear that UCF attracts, educates and graduates students of the finest quality.
-- My Word column by Board of Trustees Chair Rick Walsh

Orlando Sentinel: UCF player dies after workout
The voice on the 911 call was calm as Ereck Plancher lay unconscious and dying Tuesday morning after a football workout at the University of Central Florida.
-- Story received extensive coverage around the state and nation for several days.

WMFE: UCF Study Says Region's Poor and Middle Income Residents on Economic Edge
The University of Central Florida has conducted the first-ever comprehensive study of our region's low and moderate income populations.

Orlando Sentinel: 'Medical city' not affected by Joe Lewis' Bear Stearns debacle
The subprime mortgage fiasco claimed its latest victim over the weekend in billionaire Joe Lewis, who acknowledged Monday that he lost about $1 billion in the near collapse of investment bank Bear Stearns Cos.

Palm Beach Post: Recession fears escalate
Government agencies released two gloomy jobs reports Friday that led even the most optimistic economists to acknowledge Florida and the nation are in or precariously close to a recession.
-- Quotes UCF's Sean Snaith

Orlando Sentinel: UCF continues to study why its football stadium bounces
Report into structure's swaying anomaly won't be finished for several months

OUT-OF-STATE / NATIONAL

The Chronicle of Higher Education: Colleges Put the Squeeze on Germs
If a man who carries a urinal in one hand and solicits high-fives with the other isn't enough to get college students thinking about germs, then officials at the University of Central Florida are out of luck.

Wall Street Journal: Florida Bust Spawns Vulture Culture
Home buyers from around the U.S. and abroad are descending on Florida to buy condominiums that have suffered sharp price drops amid the housing glut, subprime-mortgage crisis and credit crunch.
-- Qutoes figures from Sean Snaith, his first Wall Street Journal appearance

Science Daily: Future 'Quantum Computers' Will Offer Increased Efficiency And Security Risks
An unusual observation in a University of Central Florida physics lab may lead to a new generation of "Quantum Computers" that will render today's computer and credit card encryption technology obsolete.
-- Received extensive coverage in scientific publications

Washington Post: Fed Divisions Complicate Inflation Talks
Ben Bernanke's juggling act has gotten harder. The Federal Reserve chairman has been taking extraordinary steps to prevent credit, financial and housing problems from driving the country into a deep recession.
-- This Associated Press story contains a colorful quote from Sean Snaith and appeared in USA Today, the L.A. Times, BusinessWeek.com and Newsweek.com, among many.

Time.com: Nice Guys Don't Finish Last
Screaming sports coaches and cutthroat tycoons have it wrong: Nice guys do finish first, a new study suggests.
-- Quotes UCF economics professor Elisabet Rutstrom

Kurdish Aspect: $1M Donation Helps UCF Create Country's First Endowed Chair in Kurdish Political Studies
At a time when Kurds are becoming known as “the Iraq that works,” the University of Central Florida will receive a $1 million donation to create the first endowed chair in Kurdish Political Studies in the United States.

Chicago Sun-Times: Top seeds aren't tops in classroom
North Carolina was the only school among the four No. 1 seeds in the NCAA men's tournament to graduate at least 50 percent of its players.
-- One of multiple local, out of state and national placements for Richard Lapchick's recent study
