UCF Graduate Wins Miss Florida USA Pageant

By Courtney Gilmartin July 21, 2009

Photo: Courtesy of Miss Florida USA

Megan Clementi

Megan Clementi, an Electrical Engineering major who graduated in the spring, won the Miss Florida USA 2010 pageant on Saturday.

Clementi, who will represent Florida in the Miss USA pageant, has focused on helping children with life-threatening diseases through Give Kids the World.  As Miss Florida USA, she will be traveling throughout the state to support many other nonprofit organizations as well. 

Clementi, a Jupiter native and Orlando Magic dancer, defeated 50 other contestants during the state pageant, held in Hollywood, Fla.

She became the second spring UCF graduate to win a major statewide pageant in the past two weeks.

Rachael Todd, a Communications major from Oviedo, won the Miss Florida America title on July 11. Todd, who focused her pageant platform on “The Changing Face of Homelessness,” will compete in the Miss America pageant in January.

Clementi entered the Miss Florida USA pageant as Miss South Miami USA. If she wins the Miss USA title, she would qualify for the international Miss Universe pageant. 

Miss UCF and the first runner-up for the Miss Florida title in 2006, Clementi is no stranger to pageants. The Miss Florida USA pageant includes a press conference-style interview, eveningwear competition and swimwear competition. Contestants were judged on communication skills, personality, beauty, stage presence and physical fitness.

Clementi plans on making more than 200 appearances throughout Florida and meeting with state leaders during her year as Miss Florida USA.

Clementi also is the co-host of the new UCF-themed children's show "UKnighted" The 15-episdode "UKnighted" series launched this spring on UCFTV, airing Wednesdays at Noon and again on Saturday at 11:30 a.m.

UCF’s success in recent pageants has involved more than the students and recent graduates who are competing.

Melanie Bullock and Veronica Livesay, co-directors of the Miss UCF pageant and employees of Student Development and Enrollment Services, received the President’s Award from the Miss Florida Board during the recent pageant in St. Petersburg.  The President’s Award is presented to local executive directors who have displayed an outstanding commitment and belief in their local pageants and have demonstrated enthusiastic passion and support for the Miss Florida program.
