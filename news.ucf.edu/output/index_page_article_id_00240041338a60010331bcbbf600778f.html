Vice President Ehasz Wins National Advising Award

By Kristi Bartlett April 26, 2005

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Chad Binette, UCF

    • 407-823-6312

  • Leighi Cunningham, National Academic Advising Association

    • 785-532-5717

  • Print this Page

Maribeth Ehasz, vice president for Student Development and Enrollment Services, received the Pacesetter Award from the National Academic Advising Association.

 

Ehasz is the only recipient of the national award, which will be presented in October at the associations national conference in Las Vegas.

 

The Pacesetter Award acknowledges university provosts, chief executive officers and chief academic and student affairs officers who demonstrate a commitment to academic advising and advocate for students and advisors.

 

Ehasz was named vice president of Student Development and Enrollment Services in early April after she led the division on an interim basis. She joined UCF in 1994 and served as associate vice president for Academic Development and Retention from 1998 to 2004.

 

Prior to joining UCF, Ehasz served as assistant dean of the Walker College of Business at Appalachian State University in Boone, N.C. as well as founding director for the Office of Retention Services and assistant dean of the University College at the University of Toledo.

 

The National Academic Advising Association aims to promote quality academic advising and the professional development of its members to enhance students education. The association, which has honored many institutions and individuals every year since 1983, consists of more than 8,000 members who are involved in academic and student affairs.
