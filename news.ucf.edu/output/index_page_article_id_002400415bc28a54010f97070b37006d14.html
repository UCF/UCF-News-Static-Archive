UCF Students to Make Own Short Films During Campus Movie Fest

By Kristi Bartlett Feb. 19, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Evi Christodoulou, Campus Activities Board

    • 407-823-6471

  • Campus Movie Fest Web site for UCF

  • Print this Page

UCF students, faculty and staff will participate this week in the largest college film festival in the world.

Campus Movie Fest launches on Thursday, Feb. 22, from 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. at the Student Union patio, or in Room 208 of the Student Union in case of rain. The festival provides participating groups with all of the equipment that they need to create short films that will vie for a national grand prize of $10,000. The festival is free and open to all majors, and groups must sign up no later than Thursday.

On the launch date, groups will receive an Apple laptop and digital camcorder to film their movies. The theme for this yearÂ’s competition is what it means to discover, but the films can be on any topic. Each film can be no longer than five minutes, and experts will be available to offer help and support for the groups every day from 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. in the Student Union. Help will also be available over AOL Instant Messenger. Films must be turned in between 5 and 9 p.m. Feb. 28.

UCF is the only public university in Florida participating in the festival. A panel of UCF staff and students will judge the films in five areas: content, sound design, aesthetics, overall quality and technical excellence. The films will be showcased on March 7 at the Campus Movie Fest Finale, with the winning films receiving prizes. The finale will be held at the Backstage Diner in Ferrell Commons. Doors open at 7 p.m., and the show will start at 7:30.

The top films advance to the regional competition and will have a chance to win more than $25,000 in cash and prizes.

There are three categories of film competition. A Best Picture Award for the best overall film will be awarded at each college. On the national level, the best comedy will win the TBS Very Funny Award, and the Turner Classic Movie category gives students the option to recreate a scene from a classic movie.

Campus Movie Fest began in 2000 as an experiment by four Emory University students who wondered what would happen if they gave all of the freshman residence halls laptops, digital camcorders and a week to film short movies. The idea proved to be popular, and after graduation the four students founded the Ideas United company to take the festival international. Ideas United has since formed partnerships with Turner Broadcasting and Turner Classic Movies and has worked with more than 75,000 students.

For more information or to sign up, visit www.campusmoviefest.com.
