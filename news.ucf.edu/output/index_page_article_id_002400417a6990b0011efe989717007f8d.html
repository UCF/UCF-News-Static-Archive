UCF Expands Popular Student Shuttle Service

By Courtney Gilmartin Jan. 23, 2009

Photo: Jacque Brund

Students board a new bus that is part of UCF's student shuttle fleet.

Students in two additional apartment complexes are riding free shuttles to and from campus this semester, and UCF has added extra buses to enhance service on the busiest routes.

The shuttles have become increasingly popular as more students aim to save money during a tough economy and help the environment by keeping their cars off the road.

UCF students took 873,754 trips on the shuttles during the fall semester, an increase of 5 percent over fall 2007 ridership. Ridership on shuttles to and from the main campus to the Rosen College of Hospitality Management increased 60 percent to 20,679 in fall 2008.

“Riding the shuttles reduces traffic congestion, emissions inside garages, stress and even accidents,” said Kris Singh, interim director of UCF Parking and Transportation Services.  “It is an efficient and effective way to travel.”

The UCF shuttles connect the campus with 17 off-campus apartment complexes and the Central Florida Research Park. The new service to Jefferson Village and the Lofts is being offered on a trial basis for the spring semester.

Shuttles operate primarily from 7 a.m. to 10 p.m. Monday through Friday, with a beginning time of 6:30 a.m. on some routes. In most cases, a bus arrives at each stop about once every 15 minutes.

On campus, the Black Route runs clockwise around the campus and the Gold Route runs counterclockwise, with 13 stops on each route.

Singh credits the increased price of gasoline during the fall and the convenience of routes for the rise in shuttle ridership.

“It saves money and time, which is even more important for students during the current economic times,” he said.

Adriana Escobar, a junior Biology major who lives in the Tivoli Apartments, said she takes the shuttle at least three times a week. She said several of her friends ride the buses every day.

“The shuttle is very convenient,” she said. “I don’t mind waiting 10 or 15 minutes for it, because in the end it saves me time and money. Taking the shuttle is also a great way to meet new people.”

The shuttles are funded primarily through the transportation access fee that students pay. The current fee is $7.59 per credit hour, and it will increase to $7.94 beginning in fall 2009.

“Students are paying for the shuttles, so we encourage them to ride as often as they can,” Singh said.

Along with adding the trial service to Jefferson Village and the Lofts, UCF Parking and Transportation Services is planning how to serve the College of Medicine’s Lake Nona site.

Since UCF began the shuttle service in January 2002, it has provided a convenience for students, reduced air pollution and lowered traffic and parking congestion on campus.

The shuttles’ success in reducing air pollution was recognized in 2003 by the Central Florida Clean Air Team. The team is a coalition of businesses and government, health, transportation and environmental organizations coordinated by Metroplan Orlando.

For more information on UCF’s shuttle service, including a list of apartment complexes served, visit http://www.parking.ucf.edu/Shuttle.html.
