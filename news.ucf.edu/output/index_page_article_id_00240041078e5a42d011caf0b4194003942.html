Football Game Day Update: Memory Mall to Feature Tent Zones Beginning Saturday

By UCF Staff Sept. 29, 2008

Photo: Jacque Brund

UCF's next home game will be at 3:30 p.m. Saturday, Oct. 4, against SMU.

The first two seasons of on-campus football games at Bright House Networks Stadium have been overwhelmingly successful. Record numbers of fans have come to campus to enjoy friendship, fun and football and cheer on the Knights.

This season, many more fans have tailgated on Memory Mall than last year – mostly because the mall is seen as the “destination location” for Knights fans.  Memory Mall’s growing popularity has led UCF to make a few changes to policies in order to ensure that all fans fully enjoy their game day experience.

Beginning with the next game on Saturday, Oct. 4, UCF will set up tent zones on Memory Mall in order to better accommodate fans.  Fans will be allowed to set up tents within designated spaces and encouraged to place any tables, grills, lawn chairs or other equipment on adjacent grassy areas.  This new set up will allow fans to enjoy tailgating with more space around their tents, keeping the sidewalks clear for pedestrian  and official vehicle traffic, and ensuring quick access by emergency vehicles, if necessary. 

Memory Mall will remain the focal point of tailgating for students and other fans at UCF football games. The Student Government Association and Alumni Association were directly involved in designing this new approach and strongly support its implementation as a means of better supporting all fans.

The Student Government Association’s free activities and food offerings will remain centrally located on the mall.  In addition to filling all sections of Memory Mall, tailgaters may also choose to set up on several grassy areas adjoining the mall or other open areas of the UCF campus, including Lake Claire, near the Visual Arts Building, and around Millican Hall and the Reflecting Pond.  Wherever fans choose to set up, they are expected to help preserve UCF’s beautiful campus by cleaning up their trash and being protective of all campus buildings, landscaping and personal property. 

Tent zones will be painted on Memory Mall prior to game days, and UCF representatives will be available on game days to assist in set up coordination.  All fans are encouraged to do their part in making this change both efficient and successful for all.   

To help in keeping the campus clean, a new, roving trash and recycling pick up called “Knights Pick Up” will be conducted on Memory Mall every few hours throughout game day. Most fans have done a great job keeping their areas clean, disposing of trash and recycling aluminum cans and plastic bottles.  UCF encourages all fans to embrace the new “Knights Pick Up” service and help crews by regularly cleaning up their areas.  Last season, UCF recycled about 10 percent of trash. During the first two games this season, we have recycled about 20 percent of the trash.  Led by UCF’s Student Sustainability Alliance Team and coordinating with the university’s Housekeeping and Grounds crews, UCF hopes to approach a 50 percent or greater recycling rate, resulting in better fan service, enhanced campus appearance and improved stewardship of the environment.
