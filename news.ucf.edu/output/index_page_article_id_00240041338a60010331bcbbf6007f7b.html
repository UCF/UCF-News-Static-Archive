UCF Board of Trustees to Vote on Proposed On-Campus Football Stadium

By UCF Staff April 12, 2005

One of five renderings of the proposed stadium shows how it might look. Other renderings can be viewed at www.stadium.ucf.edu.

The University of Central Florida Board of Trustees is scheduled to vote Thursday, April 14, whether to build an on-campus football stadium.

The trustees will meet from 2 to 5 p.m. at the Student Union. Members of the public may sign up at the meeting to speak for five minutes each.

The proposed 45,000-seat stadium would be located in the north end of campus in the athletics complex that contains the Arena and new student housing that will eventually be home to 2,000 students. A new alumni center is under construction nearby, and two parking garages are planned for the area, which already contains one garage.

If the stadium is approved, construction could begin this summer and be completed by fall 2006 at an estimated cost of $43 million. The stadium would be built and supported by ticket and concessions sales, donations, corporate sponsorships and advertising. No state money or tuition would be used.

The board received feasibility and traffic studies and heard public comments on the proposed stadium at its March 31 meeting. University officials have also been meeting with area homeowners groups. UCF held a community forum Tuesday to share information with residents and hear their comments. An open house is scheduled for 6 to 8 p.m. Wednesday, April 13, at Lawton Chiles Middle School in Oviedo.

About 200 people and nine of the 13 trustees attended the community forum Tuesday. Most of the speakers encouraged the board to approve the stadium because it would add tradition and a more collegiate atmosphere on game days and provide a venue for family entertainment in East Orlando.

UCF currently plays its home football games in the Citrus Bowl in downtown Orlando.

For more information on the proposed stadium and community meetings, visit www.stadium.ucf.edu.
