Computer Programming Wins Honorable Mention in World Championships

By Chad Binette March 31, 2004

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Chad Binette

    • 407-823-6312

  • International Collegiate Programming Contest

  • Orlando Sentinel story

  • Print this Page

UCF students Raymond Ho, Casey Thurston and Scott Vogelpohl won an honorable mention award in a world computer programming championship this week in the Czech Republic.

UCF was one of 73 teams -- and one of 25 from North America -- that competed in the Association for Computing Machinery International Collegiate Programming Contest on March 31.

Each team tried to solve ten programming problems in five hours. The problems involved a variety of topics, including control towers at airports, ants burrowing into the ground and the maximum number of trees that could be planted as part of a city project. 

The St. Petersburg Institute of Fine Mechanics and Optics solved seven problems correctly and won the world championship. The Massachusetts Institute of Technology finished fifth, the highest ranking among North American teams in the IBM-sponsored contest.

 

Honorable mention awards were given to schools that did not finish 27th or higher.

 

UCF has won its regional competition 11 times in 22 years. Five UCF teams competed this year and finished first, third, sixth, 11th and 34th out of 84 teams in the Southeast Regional competition last fall in Daytona Beach. The top two teams  one from UCF and one from Georgia Tech  advanced to the world championships.

Ali Orooji, the teams faculty adviser, said UCFs consistent success in past competitions has brought a lot of recognition to the university. The students apply what theyve learned in different computer programming courses and become great problem solvers, he said.

They become exceptional programmers, he said. All of them get jobs before they graduate. They are really the cream of the crop.

The other 12 UCF programmers who participated in the regional competition were: Nick Beato, Adam Campbell, Ben Douglass, Greg Eldridge, Chandler Escude, Jason Gauci, Daniel Holbert, Walt Mundt, David Nikdel, Chris Poon, Gary Stein and John Tanner. In addition to Orooji, the teams coaches are Jason Daly, Chris Gouge, Arup Guha and Glenn Martin.

For more information about the contest, go to http://icpc.baylor.edu/icpc/Finals.
