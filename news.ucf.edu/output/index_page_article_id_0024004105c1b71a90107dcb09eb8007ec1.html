Gutierrez, Kelly Win Employee of the Month Honors

By Mallery Laing Nov. 30, 2005

Photo: Courtesy of Human Resources

Barbara Kelly of the Department of Music won September Employee of the Month honors.

Full Photo

Story Images

Click thumbnails for full-size images.

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Print this Page

The two women who won UCFs September and October Employee of the Month awards dont know much about free time.

September winner Barbara Kelly, an office assistant in the Department of Music, is busy planning a wedding. October honoree Tania Gutierrez, a program assistant at the UCF Physical Plant, is a single parent who is pursuing a masters degree.

Kelly has worked at UCF for about seven years. She graduated from UCF with a bachelors degree in psychology in 1990, and it seemed natural for the alumna of the Marching Knights Color Guard to eventually join the Music Department. She works with the concert band, the Marching Knights, the Wind Ensemble and the Jammin Knights Pep Band. Kelly also works on the scholarship and recruitment committee to bring music majors into the program.

Kelly said she was excited to hear she was named Employee of the Month. The best part is just spending time with the students, she said. I greatly enjoy it.

Another exciting event for Kelly is her upcoming wedding in January to her boyfriend of ten years.

All of my spare time goes to planning this wedding, she said, adding that she also enjoys spending time with her eight nieces and nephews.

Kellys co-workers describe her as, a tireless worker who is dedicated to the success of our department -- its faculty, students and programs.

Gutierrez has worked as a program assistant at UCF for the past five years. She acts as a translator for the Physical Plants many Hispanic employees and Human Resources. She sits in on interviews and physicals, and she translates e-mails.

Born in Ecuador, Gutierrez was raised in a bilingual environment and understands that many of the Hispanics who come to the area do not have that luxury. She is pursuing a masters degree in Spanish at UCF and hopes to eventually teach Spanish at the college level.

Her co-workers describe her as instrumental in the essential need for communication between the university and the employee. Without her hard work and dedication, Physical Plant would not be able to hire or retain the quality of people we have now."

Gutierrez said she was pleased to receive the award.

I came to Florida alone with no family, she said. This is so nice. It makes me feel that I have done well.
