'Do the Knight Thing' Campaign Promotes Respect for UCF and Opponents

By Chad Binette Sept. 5, 2008

Photo: Courtesy of UCF Marketing

"Do the Knight Thing" is a centerpiece of UCF's continuing efforts to establish positive game day traditions on campus.

C’mon Knights fans. Have fun. Enjoy the game day atmosphere and the drama, tension and spirit of competition. Most importantly, win or lose, be a good sport.

“Be a Good Sport, Respect the Opponent and Honor UCF” are the three themes of the University of Central Florida’s “Do the Knight Thing” campaign in 2008.

The campaign, featured at www.dotheknightthing.com, is a centerpiece of UCF’s continuing efforts to establish positive game day traditions on campus.

The Web site stars Knightro, the UCF mascot, and includes tips for how fans can “Be a Good Sport, Respect the Opponent and Honor UCF.” Videos show how foolish some behavior appears and how acting inappropriately prevents others from enjoying game days.

UCF’s first football season on campus was successful on and off the field last year. The Knights won the Conference USA championship and set attendance records while most fans were great ambassadors for UCF. To help ensure that game day success continues this season, university and student leaders have teamed up to launch the new Web site and renew efforts to welcome visitors to the UCF campus.

Student Development & Enrollment Services, the Student Government Association, UCF Marketing and UCF Athletics are strengthening the “Do the Knight Thing” tradition this season. And volunteers with the UCF Town & Gown chapter – which aims to strengthen relationships between the university and the community -- also are pitching in to help welcome visiting fans.

“We hope our fans cheer loudly for the Knights and give them a great home field advantage at Bright House Networks Stadium,” said Maribeth Ehasz, vice president for Student Development and Enrollment Services (SDES). “We also want our fans to welcome our guests and ensure they leave with a favorable impression of our university. As the faces of UCF on game days, fans play an important role in helping us build a strong reputation locally, statewide and nationally.”

UCF has expanded its successful ambassadors program by welcoming UCF alumni as new ambassadors who walk around campus and help visitors with directions and other information before each game.

South Florida staff also will be volunteering as ambassadors at Saturday’s game. UCF administration and student government leaders reached out to their colleagues at South Florida to encourage collaboration in fan initiatives and student activities.

In an effort led by the UCF Student Government Association, student organizations also are helping to welcome guests this season and to serve as role models for responsible tailgating. In return, those organizations will get prime tailgating areas on Memory Mall. Interested organizations can contact the SGA’s athletic relations coordinator at sga_arc@mail.ucf.edu.

Volunteers from the UCF Town & Gown chapter welcome visiting fans “to our campus and beautiful stadium” at the beginning of the third quarter of every home game, said Pam Salvetti, vice president of the chapter and a 1981 UCF graduate.

The Town & Gown volunteers pass out business cards with the chapter’s logo and Web site and the message, “We’re glad you’re here!”

“We want our visitors to feel welcome and comfortable on our campus, and we hope to set a good example for our fans,” Salvetti said. “We want them to leave and say to themselves and others that they had a fabulous experience and that they found UCF fans to be the most hospitable fans in the entire nation!”

Knights fans, please help the ambassadors, student leaders and Town & Gown volunteers welcome visitors to our beautiful campus. Let’s all have a great time and show our visitors that “Chivalry Lives Here.”
