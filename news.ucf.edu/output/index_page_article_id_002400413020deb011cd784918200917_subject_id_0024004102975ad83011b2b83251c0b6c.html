In His Words: President Hitt Looks to Next 40 Years

By UCF President John C. Hitt Oct. 7, 2008

Photo: UCF File

UCF President John C. Hitt

Full Photo

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Grant Heston, News & Information

    • 407-823-5988

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Florida is a young university, having opened our doors to students only 40 years ago today. But our achievements in academics, research, service and economic development rival those of far more-established institutions around the country.

A university’s heart is its faculty and its soul is its undergraduate students. We are as committed to undergraduate education as we were when we began 40 years ago, but in the future we will expand our graduate and research programs, both of which are already nationally recognized.

Specifically, we expect to have more faculty members who have been elected to national scientific academies.  We also look forward to the day, not far in the future, when we point with pride to a Nobel Laureate on our faculty.  But, to attract faculty members and research scientists of this caliber, we need to strengthen our financial base.
 
At UCF, we are dedicated to being a premier academic and research institution, while at the same time strengthening our role as a major economic engine for our community and the state.
 
Recognizing that graduate education and university research go hand-in-hand with economic development and innovation -- and believing that there can be no great city without a great university -- I have worked during the last 16 years to strategically align the university’s programs of graduate study and research with the needs of the region.

As we continue to support tourism and hospitality, optics, simulation, and other high-tech industries, we are prepared to help further diversify and strengthen our economy in areas such as medicine, biotechnology and renewable energy. We will continue to be a leader in helping to create the sustainable, high-wage jobs we want for our children and grandchildren, while at the same time educating our students to fill these jobs.

We like to say that UCF Stands for Opportunity, and I believe the next 40 years will hold many opportunities for our university to be an outstanding resource for our community and state.

UCF Stands For Opportunity: The University of Central Florida is a metropolitan research university that ranks as the 6th largest in the nation with more than 50,000 students. UCF's first classes were offered in 1968. The university offers impressive academic and research environments that power the region's economic development. UCF's culture of opportunity is driven by our diversity, Orlando environment, history of entrepreneurship and our youth, relevance and energy. For more information, visit http://news.ucf.edu.
