STORM UPDATE: All UCF Campuses Closed Until Tuesday Morning Due to Hurricane Jeanne

By UCF Staff Sept. 26, 2004

Photo: UCF Housing

Students staying in shelters on the main campus played games such as Uno to pass the time on Saturday night.

The University of Central Florida's offices will be closed and classes will be canceled at all campuses until 7 a.m. Tuesday, Sept. 28, due to Hurricane Jeanne.

UCF officials will continue to monitor the storm through the weekend. Updates on reopenings for all campuses will be posted on this Web site and provided to local media. Updates on regional campus closings are available by calling 407-823-5334 and at www.regionalcampuses.ucf.edu/stormsclosings.htm.

There were no reports of major damage on the main campus as of early Sunday morning. Several leaks had been reported, and Physical Plant employees were working to contain them as much as possible. The entire campus still had power.

Students who live on campus and will remain on campus during the storm were directed to designated shelters, as were residents of Pegasus Pointe and Pegasus Landing, UCF's affiliated housing complexes. Shelters for students opened at 8 p.m. Saturday. More information is available at www.housing.ucf.edu.

Students should consult with their families to determine when they wish to travel back to Orlando. Students who may miss classes because of storm-related issues should notify their professors as soon as possible.

Essential staff and their families are being housed in the Education Complex, which opened at 6 p.m. Saturday.

Campus Events

The Sunday matinee performance of "Blithe Spirit" has been canceled.

Individuals planning to attend other events scheduled at UCF campuses this week may want to check with the planners to ensure they have not been postponed or canceled.
