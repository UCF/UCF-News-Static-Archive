UCF to Host Forum Thursday on Latino Politics in Central Florida, 2004 Elections

By Chad Binette Oct. 12, 2004

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Luis Martínez-Fernández

  • Chad Binette

    • 407-823-6312

  • Latin American, Caribbean and Latino Studies Program

  • Print this Page

Hispanic advocates, state legislative candidates and representatives from two local newspapers will participate Thursday, Oct. 14, in a University of Central Florida forum on Latino politics.

Latino Politics in Central Florida: The 2004 Elections will begin at 7 p.m. in the first-floor auditorium, Room 132, of the Visual Arts Building at UCF. The forum is open to the public.

Panelists include state Rep. John Quiñones, R-Kissimmee; Israel Mercado, a Democratic candidate for Quiñones seat; and Sylvia Cáceres, regional director for the Puerto Rico Federal Affairs Administration, a group based in the United States that represents the Puerto Rican government and strives to improve economic, educational and social opportunities for Puerto Ricans.

Selena Walsh, national director of the Buena Vista Project, also will speak. Walshs organization aims to increase the voting rate of Latinos. Other speakers include Linda Hernández, executive director of El Nuevo Día, and Myriam Márquez, a columnist for the Orlando Sentinel.

UCFs newly expanded Latin American, Caribbean and Latino Studies program organized the forum to address issues such as voter registration and how political campaigns relate to the Latino culture, which often is erroneously portrayed as one voting bloc, said Luis Martínez-Fernández, the programs director.

We wanted to bring diverse voices together for an open discussion about the growing importance of Latinos in the regions political life and to demystify some perceptions, such as the notion of the Hispanic vote as a homogenous bloc, Martínez-Fernández said.

The UCF Latin American, Caribbean and Latino Studies program offers classes in art, languages, history, anthropology and politics. The program offers an 18-credit hour minor and plans to seek approval by the UCF Board of Trustees to grant bachelors degrees.

The expanded program will focus more attention on the Hispanic residents in the United States in addition to the Latin American and Caribbean populations.

Sponsors of the forum include El Nuevo Día, Chipotle restaurants, the Havana Bottling Co., the UCF Bookstore and the UCF Political Science Department.
