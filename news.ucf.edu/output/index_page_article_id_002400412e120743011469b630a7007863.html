Football Game Day Update: What Those Not Going to the Game Should Know

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala Sept. 11, 2007

Photo: UCF News File

Not everyone who's coming to campus on Saturday will be heading to Bright House Networks Stadium. Some students and faculty will be visiting the Library.

Full Photo

  • Chad Binette, News and Information

    • 407-823-6312

  • Print this Page

The excitement is building at the University of Central Florida as everyone from gardeners to the president gets ready for the first on-campus football game on Saturday, Sept. 15.

But not everyone on campus that day will be watching the Knights and Texas Longhorns -- some students will be attending classes, finishing term papers at the library or picking up allergy medicine at the Pharmacy. Here is an outline of what students, faculty and others should know if they will be on campus but not at the 3:30 p.m. game on Saturday.

For information on how game days will affect the UCF campus, visit www.ucfgameday.com.

Classes and Research

Classes that begin before noon on Saturday will be held as scheduled. No classes are scheduled to begin at or after noon.

While UCFs academic buildings will be locked at noon, professors and students who want to work in their offices or research labs later in the day will be able to do that if they have keys to get into the buildings.

Students and faculty members with classes on Saturday have been registered for parking in Garage B, formerly the South Garage.

Traffic and Parking

Everyone should be aware of that parking will be in high demand beginning early Saturday. Students, faculty and others are encouraged to arrive as early as possible to avoid the peak traffic periods.

Only members of the Golden Knights Club, patrons with disabilities, credentialed media, 500 students selected by a Student Government Association lottery and stadium operations personnel will be allowed into parking garages and areas C, D, E and F. Everyone else will have to park in the remaining garages and paved lots.

Garage B is reserved for university business, such as professors and students who have Saturday classes and campus employees in areas such as Computer Services and the Health Center who are working on campus on Saturday. All parking in Garage B must be authorized in advance. Spots in Garage B can be reserved by calling the Provosts Office.

Others who are coming to campus should plan to arrive early and be prepared to search for parking.

Students who live in the Towers residence halls will be allowed to keep their cars in their assigned spaces, but they will not be able to enter or leave those garages between 11:30 a.m. and 7:30 p.m. Saturday. Additionally, there will only be limited access from 9:30 to 11:30 a.m. and 7:30 to 9:30 p.m. due to heavy pedestrian traffic in the area. Students who have jobs or other commitments off campus on game days should move their cars out of the E and G parking garages before access is restricted.

Garages B (south), C (east), D (north) and F (behind Arena) are all off limits to general parking from 7 p.m. Friday until 7 a.m. Sunday.

Testing

Anyone who is taking an exam on a game day should plan on arriving early. The National Board of Certified Hospice Nurses Exam is scheduled from 8 a.m. to 1 p.m. in the Business Administration building on Saturday and will not be delayed because of parking issues. For information, go to http://www.testing.sdes.ucf.edu.

Library and Health Center

The Library and Health Center will operate during their regular business hours. The Library will be open from 9 a.m. to 7 p.m. Go to www.library.ucf.edu for more information. The UCF Health Center and Pharmacy will be open from 10 a.m. to 2 p.m. for walk-in acute care for students. For more information, go to www.hs.sdes.ucf.edu.

Working Out

The student Recreation and Wellness Center, leisure pool and playing fields at RWC Park will be open from 9 a.m. to 1:30 p.m. on the days of all home football games. For more information, go to www.rec.ucf.edu. The faculty and staff workout facility, the Wellness Research Center, will be closed on Saturdays when the Knights play home games. For more information, go to http://pegasus.cc.ucf.edu/~wrcenter.

Student Union and Marketplace

The Student Union will be open from 8 a.m. to midnight, and all of its food vendors will offer game day specials. For more information, go to www.studentunion.ucf.edu. The Marketplace will be open during its normal hours, 10:30 a.m. to 8 p.m. For more information, go to www.ucfdining.com.
