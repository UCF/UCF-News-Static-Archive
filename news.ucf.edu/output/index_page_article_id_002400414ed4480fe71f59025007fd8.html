UCF Welcomes Arriving Students to Clean, Safe Campus Days After Hurricane

By Chad Binette August 19, 2004

Photo: Jerry Klein

The University of Central Florida welcomed arriving students and parents Wednesday with fully restored utilities and roads and sidewalks that were clear of debris from Hurricane Charley.

The universitys main entrance on University Boulevard reopened Wednesday, as did many UCF-area restaurants that had been closed, bringing a sense of normalcy to life around campus for the first time since the storm struck on Friday.

While moving televisions, refrigerators and books into campus residence halls, parents and students said they thought the campus looked nice and praised the efforts of UCF employees who cleared away the debris so quickly, allowing classes to begin Monday as scheduled.

It looks like everyone here worked hard to get this place into shape, said Fort Lauderdale resident Dennis OBea, who was helping his daughter, Denise, move into the Apollo housing community.

Bob Milano of Port St. Lucie said UCF employees have done a helluva job getting the campus clean, clear of debris and ready for students. His daughter, Alyssa, a freshman who wants to study elementary education, said the campus looked better than she expected.

Classes will begin on schedule at all of Floridas 11 state universities.

Most of the UCF campus never lost power. While power was lost at the Lake Claire Apartments and Greek Park, electrical service was restored there by Tuesday. Pegasus Pointe and Pegasus Landing, the two UCF-affiliated complexes off campus, had power, and a UCF survey taken Monday morning showed that all but one of 11 off-campus apartment complexes near the campus had full electrical service. Power was reported out only at part of the Boardwalk complex.

The most severe damage on campus appears to be at the student Recreation and Wellness Center, where the roof will need to be repaired. Contractors already are in the building and a repair estimate may be available later this week, said Jim Uhlir, director of Environmental Health & Safety. An estimate of when the building will reopen may be published as early as next week.

President John Hitt also lauded the efforts of UCF employees, more than 100 of whom stayed on campus overnight Friday to help maintain and restore resources. Many employees worked throughout the weekend. In an e-mail to the campus community Wednesday morning, Hitt specifically praised the campus police, Physical Plant and Building Services staffs and telecommunications employees.

They put UCF ahead of their own families and concerns to ensure that we have a successful opening next week, and they have our deepest appreciation, Hitt wrote.

Hitt added that while its heartbreaking to see downed trees on campus, what is most important is that no members of the UCF family sustained serious injury.

I hope that your lives are returning to normal and that you were spared the worst of this terrible disaster, he wrote. As we begin classes and try to return to university business, I ask that each of you also be mindful that many of our UCF family will continue to deal with problems resulting from the storm and will need our understanding and assistance."

UCF plans to find a way for students to work out on campus despite the damage to the Recreation and Wellness Center.

Tom Huddleston, vice president for Student Development and Enrollment Services, said one option may be to move equipment and Recreation and Wellness Center offices to other buildings on campus. Students also may be able to use the gymnasium in the College of Education building four nights a week, he said.

We will continue to provide opportunities for recreation, Huddleston said.

STUDENT HOUSING

Check-in to all on-campus housing began Wednesday. Check www.housing.ucf.edu for updates and more information on Pegasus Landing and Pegasus Pointe check-in. Students who need more information about either of those two complexes also can call College Park Management at 1-877-924-4455 or 1-800-464-1714.

Students who live off campus can call their housing complexes for the status of move-in dates and utilities. Students with other questions or concerns about off-campus housing can call Off-Campus Student Services at 407-823-6584.

CAMPUS SERVICES

The Marketplace, which provided food to campus emergency workers over the weekend, is open with a limited menu.

The Student Union building, including various food services, is open. The Bookstore also is open, along with other student services in the Washington Center.

For safety tips related to coping with a hurricane, go to http://www.orangecountyfl.net/hurricane/safety_tips.htm .
