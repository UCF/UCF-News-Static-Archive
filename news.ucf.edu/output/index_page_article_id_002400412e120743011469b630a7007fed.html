UCF Welcomes Record Number of Merit Scholars, Adds New Degree Programs

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala Aug. 24, 2007

Photo: Jacque Brund

UCF is putting the finishing touches on campus just in time for the fall semester. The New UCF Arena is one of the facilities opening this fall.

Full Photo

  • Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala

    • 407-823-6120

  • Print this Page

A record 43 National Merit Scholars were among the more than 48,000 students that began classes at the University of Central Florida on Aug. 20.

UCF should be in the top 50 universities in the country for merit scholars in 2007-2008 thanks to the record attendance this year. Last year, 28 merit scholars attended UCF.

The dramatic increase in the number of merit scholars is another example of the high caliber of students the university attracts. UCF is becoming the first choice for more high-achieving students.

In fact this year's freshman class has an average grade point average of 3.7, an average SAT score of 1217 and an average ACT score of 26. The Burnett Honors College incoming class posted even better statistics with an average GPA of 4.075, an average SAT score of 1361 and an average ACT score of 29.7.

UCF is a first choice for many reasons. The metropolitan research university is growing in size, reputation and academic and student life offerings. When students return this fall, they will have more choices, including attending football games and more concerts on campus and enrolling in three new degree programs.

Country music star Reba McEntire will open the new 10,000-seat UCF Arena (www.ucfarena.com) on Sept. 8. Then the Texas Longhorns will help christen Bright House Networks Stadium on Sept. 15. Fans can learn more about the first UCF Knights football game to be played on campus by going to www.ucfgameday.com.

The three new degrees are a Master of Arts in Music and bachelors of science degrees in Biotechnology and Construction Engineering.

The Music degree is aimed at music educators who want to improve their teaching skills, professional musicians who need graduate degrees for career advancement and students who have earned bachelor's degrees in Music and want to take advanced courses in the Orlando area.

The Biotechnology and Construction Engineering degrees are being offered in response to a growing demand in the areas of biomedical sciences and construction. The degree in Biotechnology will prepare students to work in the biotechnology industry with applied laboratory experience and research opportunities. The construction degree will focus on the technical and managerial processes of construction.

While some UCF programs are growing, the university will begin fall classes with a lot of uncertainty surrounding its budget because of state funding shortfalls.

Students are bracing for the possibility of fewer available classes. UCF has cut 4 percent of its legislatively appropriated budget in anticipation of the statewide budget shortfall. The university is holding another 2 percent of its legislatively appropriated budget in reserve in case more cuts are required. The final outcome wont be known until September, when the Legislature meets in a special session. Provost Terry Hickey said in a letter to faculty members that everything possible is being done to lessen the impact on students.

The educational needs of our students must come first, Hickey said. The impact of removing several million dollars from our budget will be felt, but we must all work to lessen those impacts on our students.

Despite the uncertainty, some projects that were previously funded are moving forward.

Construction on the Burnett Biomedical Sciences Center at Lake Nona is progressing, and the groundbreaking ceremony for the College of Medicine (http://med.ucf.edu) building is scheduled for Oct. 3. Both buildings will be part of a burgeoning health sciences cluster at Lake Nona that also will include a veterans hospital and Burnham Institute for Medical Research laboratories.

UCF also opens its newest housing complex this week. Tower 3, next to the new UCF Arena, adds 475 beds to the existing housing options on campus. The groundbreaking for a new Physical Sciences Building near the Health Center on the main campus is scheduled for November.

The dirt parking lot on the north end of campus behind Garage D is getting paved. Once completed, the upgraded D1 lot will have a total of 630 parking spaces, 80 more slots than previously available.

Students and faculty who drive on Gemini Boulevard North near Greek Park should also be relieved. The roadway was repaved during the summer break.

Finally, university offices have been busy scheduling speakers who can educate students and generate discussions about current topics such as the roles of China and India as emerging global powers. The Global Perspectives Office also has scheduled speakers on other subjects throughout the semester. Those speakers include consumer advocate Ralph Nader, who will discuss Going Green in keeping with a universitywide focus this year on global climate change. Former first lady of Egypt Jehan Sadat will speak about Women Shaping and Changing the World. For a complete schedule of Global Perspectives events, visit http://globalperspectives.cos.ucf.edu/.
