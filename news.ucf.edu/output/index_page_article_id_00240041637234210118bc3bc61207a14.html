UCF Students Help Teen With Spina Bifida Meet President Bush, Tour D.C.

By Christine Dellert March 18, 2008

Photo: Courtesy of the White House

Glenn Stepp and his parents met with President George W. Bush and U.S. Rep. Tom Feeney (right) in the Oval Office last week.

Full Photo

  • Christine Dellert, News and Information

    • 407-823-2947

Glenn Stepp just returned from the trip of a lifetime. Touring Washington, D.C., last week, he sat down with President George W. Bush in the Oval Office and got a behind-the-scenes look at NASA.

Glenn isn't a politician or diplomat. He's a Casselberry high school senior who just turned 18. But he says it's one of the "coolest experiences" he ever had, thanks to five University of Central Florida students, a local nonprofit and U.S. Rep. Tom Feeney's office.

Because Glenn lives with spina bifida, a birth defect that left his spinal cord incompletely developed, his parents contacted New Hope for Kids last year. The Maitland-based organization helps grieving children and their families and grants wishes to children with life-threatening illnesses.

That's where Glenn's name was plucked from a pile by several UCF business students working on a service-learning project for class. Their goal: Raise enough money for his trip to D.C. and help him meet the president.

"It was complicated getting everything organized -- but worth it," said Sara Dykie, one of five business undergraduates in the service-learning group. "You realize that there are so many people out there who deserve these wishes, that if you take a couple months and volunteer your time, you can change somebody's life."

Since 1993, about 550 UCF business majors have canvassed Central Florida to raise more than $180,000 for New Hope's wish program. Their projects are part of a required course in the College of Business Administration dubbed "Cornerstone," where students volunteer for a semester with area nonprofits and philanthropic government agencies.

"We couldn't do what we do without these students," said Joy Marston, New Hope's marketing manager. "It's such a neat partnership."

To help fund Glenn's trip, Dykie, a marketing major, and four fellow students held a car wash, requested community donations and hosted a dinner at a local barbecue restaurant, meeting their $1,200 goal. They also enlisted the help of Feeney, R-Oviedo, who made arrangements with the White House and donated $250 toward Glenn's travels.

Before the trip, Feeney said he was looking forward to watching Glenn's dream become a reality.

"It has been a pleasure working with the UCF students and New Hope for Kids to arrange Glenn's trip to Washington, D.C., and his meeting with President Bush," Feeney said. "I look forward to spending a lot of time with Glenn this week and learning more about his love for basketball, math, science and space."

After months of work, Dykie learned that Glenn's trip to the White House would happen.

"I'm jealous," she joked. "I really want to do it, too."

As for Glenn -- a talkative teen who "loves" math and science -- the trip meant getting to do something that most people never will.

"It was amazing," said Glenn, who got the idea to visit D.C. from an American Government course he recently took. "I had never heard of anything like that where you could have such an awesome experience."

Plus, it'll be a cool story to share with new friends when he starts college at Liberty University in the fall.
