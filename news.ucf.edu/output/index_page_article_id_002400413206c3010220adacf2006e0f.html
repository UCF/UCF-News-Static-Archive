UCF Education Major Wins James Madison Fellowship to Teach Constitution

By Livia Jakobsson April 5, 2005

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Bill Gaudelli

    • 407-823-0215

  • Tom Evelyn

    • 407-823-5988

  • Burnett Honors College

  • College of Education

  • Print this Page

The James Madison Memorial Fellowship Foundation has honored Stacey Repass, a senior education major, and her passion for the Constitution by awarding her its prestigious Junior Fellowship.

Repass now belongs to a select group of about 50 students each year who receive the competitive scholarship created to honor James Madison, the fourth president of the United States and Father of the Constitution and Bill of Rights. The award supports graduate students focusing their studies on teaching the Constitution in secondary schools.

I plan to teach social studies at secondary level in Orlando, said Repass, an honors student who is majoring in social science education in the College of Education. Repass was planning for graduate school when Lisa Sklar, director of student development for the Burnett Honors College, told her about the fellowship and helped her apply.

As part of the application, Repass submitted several essays about teaching techniques, why she wants to become a teacher and why the study of the Constitution is important and should form a basic part of secondary school education.

 

She is gifted verbally, writes well and has a sincere interest and passion for the Constitution, said Bill Gaudelli, an assistant professor in the College of Education. She is an outstanding student."

 

I have developed my pedagogical techniques throughout my classes at UCF, said Repass, who will graduate this May and begin graduate school at UCF in the fall.

 

The maximum amount of each award is $24,000, prorated over the period of study. Repass will use her award to cover the costs of tuition, books and room and board. She will also attend the month-long James Madison Summer Institute at Georgetown University in July 2006.

 

Gaudelli said Repass earned the award because of her sincere commitment to the Constitution and the community.

 

Repass began volunteering at Lake Howell High School in Winter Park, where she was a straight-A student. In 2001 and 2003, she volunteered in Jamaica at the Caribbean Christian Center for the Deaf. She is also a member of the UCF Marching Knights Band and Tau Beta Sigma and served on the board of officers for many volunteer activities during her undergraduate studies.

 

The James Madison Fellowship acceptance letter recognized her high academic achievement, strong promise of completing a masters degree program and her convincing dedication to teaching young people in secondary schools.

 

Gaudelli said the education and honors colleges were supportive of Repass application from the beginning and they were very confident she would win the award. She was the complete picture, he said.
