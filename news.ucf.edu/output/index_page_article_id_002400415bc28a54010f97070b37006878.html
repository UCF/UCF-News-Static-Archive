News & Information Assistant VP Linda Gray Retires; Tom Evelyn Named Interim

By UCF Staff Feb. 28, 2007

Photo: Jerry Klein

Linda Gray, assistant vice president and director of News and Information, retired Wednesday, Feb. 28, after working for more than three decades in higher education, including the past five years at the University of Central Florida.

Linda Gray, assistant vice president and director of News and Information, retired Wednesday, Feb. 28, after working for more than three decades in higher education, including the past five years at the University of Central Florida.

Tom Evelyn was named interim assistant vice president for the department, effective Thursday, March 1, while the division of Marketing, Communications and Admissions conducts a national search to fill the position.

Gray, who joined UCF in 2002, worked for more than 28 years at the University of Florida, where she served in various roles in media relations, including the last 16 years as director and assistant vice president for News and Public Affairs.

Gray, who is considered to be an expert in higher education media relations and crisis communications, earned her bachelors degree in broadcast journalism and her masters degree in journalism and communications from UF.

The author of several articles and book chapters on crisis management, Gray received national attention for her handling of the 1990 serial killings of five students and two NCAA investigations while at UF.

Evelyn has served as assistant and then associate director of News and Information at UCF since 2002. Before that, he worked for more than 10 years as a reporter and an editor at several newspapers in Florida and Georgia. He also served four years in the U.S. Navy as an intelligence analyst.

Evelyn received his bachelors degree in journalism from UF and masters degree in communication from UCF.

News and Information is UCFs primary media relations office responsible for disseminating news about the universitys academic programs, research, administration and other activities to the media and the public. The department also advises the administration, faculty and staff on media and public relations; responds to media requests; arranges interviews and media visits; and coordinates news conferences and other media events.
