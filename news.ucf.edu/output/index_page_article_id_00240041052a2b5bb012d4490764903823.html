Wall Street Journal, National Media Spotlight UCF

By Grant J. Heston April 6, 2011

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Print this Page

 The Wall Street Journal features UCF three times recently, joining USA Today, CNN Money and other national media in highlighting the university.

For more recent UCF news, please see below for a review of selected print, broadcast and online media coverage. Also, follow UCF news on Twitter and Facebook, watch UCF TV and learn more about Strategy, Marketing, Communications & Admissions. 

NATIONAL

Wall Street Journal: Frozen Smoke!
"Frozen smoke” is the nickname for a substance created out of carbon nanotubes, described by its creators as the world’s lightest solid (and also called aerogel):
-- Similar stories about UCF research have appeared in many national media outlets.

Wall Street Journal: Obama Looking at High Jobless Rate During Re-Election Year
The U.S. jobless rate will be 7.7% in November 2012, the highest level for a presidential election month since Jimmy Carter defeated Gerald Ford in 1976, according to the average forecast of economists.
-- This story quotes Sean Snaith, director of the Institute for Economic Competitiveness.

Wall Street Journal: Economists Bet Long on Yuan, Mixed on Dollar
The bulk of economists surveyed by The Wall Street Journal would bet on the Chinese yuan rising over the next three years and the euro falling–but they’re split on the direction the U.S. dollar is likely to take.
-- This story quotes Sean Snaith, director of the Institute for Economic Competitiveness.

Reuters: College Football Players' Criminal Backgrounds Probed
A surprising number of players at top college football teams have criminal records, a new investigation revealed.
-- This story quotes Richard Lapchick, director of UCF's Institute for Diversity and Ethics in Sport, and was published around the country.

USA Today: Tea Party Backer Brings Conservative Message to Black Voters
Frantz Kebreau's speech of creating a colorblind society captured David Folsom's attention almost instantaneously.
-- This story quotes Political Science professor Aubrey Jewett.

Business Week: Diversity in College Sports Continues to Lag Behind the Pros
A new study gave college sports solid grades in racial and gender hiring practices, although one of the authors noted "troubling" trends regarding conference commissioners and athletic directors.
-- This story features research from UCF's Institute for Diversity and Ethics in Sport. Similar stories appeared in publications and online around the country.

U.S. News and World Report: Giving Companies Marketplace Edge
In today’s highly competitive global economy, companies always are looking for new ways to design, produce and deliver products that are innovative, high quality, cost-efficient to make, and affordable for consumers. Sometimes they need a little help.
-- This story mentions UCF as a key member of a national center that supports business innovations.

Sramana Mitra: Business Incubator Series
I am talking to Tom O’Neil, executive director of University of Central Florida’s Business Incubation Program which is a university-driven community partnership.

CNN Money: FedEx Delivers Good News for Economy
Is the economy of the United States in better shape than many people think?
-- This story quotes Sean Snaith, director of the Institute for Economic Competitiveness.

STATE/LOCAL

Orlando Sentinel: Arts Center, Orlando Made Big Mistake by Snubbing UCF
The city of Orlando and the Dr. Phillips Center for the Performing Arts (DPAC) did something very stupid in 2005. They made John Hitt feel unwelcome.

Orlando Sentinel: UCF Video-Game Grad School Ranks No. 2 Nationally
UCF is home to a new medical school, a top-ranking research group and graduate programs for the region's business leaders. And now it will be known nationwide for its ability to turn a group of people once seen as society's slackers – video gamers – into professionals capable of making big bucks in a multibillion-dollar industry.

Orlando Business Journal: UCF One of ‘Best Value’ Universities 
UCF was named one of the nation’s “best value” colleges and universities by The Princeton Review.

Orlando Business Journal: UCF Launches Job Growth Program
The Small Business Development Center at the University of Central Florida is starting a new growth acceleration program for small businesses as part of a $2.6 million grant received by the Florida SBDC Network.

Orlando Sentinel: Something's Brewing at UCF -- Budding Beer Maker Receiving Help From Business Incubator
Tom Moench's brewed his first batch of beer when he was 18 — not old enough to buy beer, but old enough to buy the ingredients.
