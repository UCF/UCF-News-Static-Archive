Nonprofit Management, Conservation Biology and Event Management Programs Lauded

By Chad Binette Sept. 20, 2007

Photo: Jacque Brund

UCF President John Hitt (left) and Board of Trustees Vice Chair Tom Yochum.

Full Photo

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Chad Binette

    • 407-823-6312

UCF administrators lauded the Nonprofit Management and Conservation Biology graduate programs and the Event Management undergraduate program during the Board of Trustees meeting Thursday.

Provost Terry Hickey described the Nonprofit Management masters program as the poster child for online programs. Ninety-nine students were enrolled in the fully online program in fall 2006, far exceeding the projected enrollment of 25. Hickey said hed like to see other deans use Nonprofit Management as a model to create online programs within their colleges.

Faculty in the Conservation Biology doctoral program have garnered about $5 million of research grants in three years, well ahead of the anticipated $209,000. Thirty-five students were enrolled in that program in fall 2006.

The Rosen College of Hospitality Management offers the only stand-alone Event Management bachelors degree program in the country. With a preliminary fall 2007 enrollment of 386, that program is well ahead of its goal of 103 students and has become a destination program for UCF, said Rick Schell, vice provost for Academic Affairs.

UCF administrators and trustees discussed those programs and others during a meeting of the boards Educational Programs Committee meeting Thursday morning.

In other action on Thursday, the board: 

-- Heard UCF President John Hitt encourage fans to demonstrate good sportsmanship and show respect to visiting fans Saturday at the Memphis game. Hitt also mentioned that the stadium vendor, Boston Culinary Group, has vowed to provide better service to fans at this weeks game. The vendor will stock three times as much water at concession stands as it provided last week, and it also is providing one free bottle of water to fans as they enter the game. UCF will be installing 50 water fountains at the stadium, and 16 will be ready for Saturdays game.

-- Heard an update on university construction projects from Vice President for Administration and Finance Bill Merck. Merck gave a slide presentation with several photos of the opening events at the new UCF Arena and Bright House Networks Stadium.

-- Discussed the future of student housing on campus and types of housing, such as for graduate students, international students and married students, that may be considered in the future. The board decided to discuss housing later at a workshop.
