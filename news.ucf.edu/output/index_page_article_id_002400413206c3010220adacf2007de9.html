Top Student Encourages Girls to Become Engineers

By Chad Binette

Photo: Jerry Klein

Andrea Wesser and friend will be featured in a program to interest girls in science.

Andrea Wesser originally figured a couple of engineering classes would help prepare her for a job with the Disney Imagineers, the creative team that designs the stories behind the rides.

 

After four years at the University of Central Florida, Wesser has helped design a wheelchair for young victims of land mines in Afghanistan, Bosnia and Vietnam. And shell begin a two-year, National Science Foundation-funded project this fall to develop skin sensors that, in one of many applications, could help allergy sufferers know if theyre close to whatever irritates them.

 

A couple of classes turned into a passion for engineering that Wesser has shared with hundreds of girls. She uses creative cartoons, videos and presentations  like the one she will give Saturday, March 5, during a career fair for elementary and middle school girls  to show girls that engineering is fun and to encourage them to study it in college.

 

Wesser, 22, of Palm Coast in Flagler County, was honored for her accomplishments and dedication to engineering last month, when she was named to the USA Today All-Academic second-team. The award places her among the top 40 college students in the United States.

 

The lack of women in minorities is a severe disadvantage to the industry, Wesser wrote in her nomination essay. Consequently, it is necessary to promote and publicize the benefits of this career choice to young women and minorities and to help them overcome obstacles such as balancing life and career, time management, low self-esteem, sexual harassment and unfair grades/treatment in the classroom.

 

Wesser, who received her bachelors degree in mechanical engineering in December, has remained at UCF to pursue a doctoral degree in mechanical engineering with an emphasis in microtechnology. She hopes to start her own business in microtechnology and later to use engineering to help with the excavation of archaeological sites.

 

Wesser, who graduated with honors from the Burnett Honors College, was part of a senior project team last year that designed the wheelchair for children who lost limbs from land mines. The group originally sought to design a wheelchair that would work well on beaches but decided to make it more affordable so it could be used in Third World countries. Wesser and her colleagues are pursuing a patent for part of their design, which allows children to drive and steer the wheelchair with their hands.

 

Wessers primary passion involves encouraging girls and women  from kindergarten to college  to learn to love physics and engineering. She builds spaghetti and jelly bean towers with elementary school students and shows them cartoons of Pete the Particle sleeping and playing at a theme park to explain the difference between static and dynamic particles.

 

She will give a presentation entitled Hi-Tech Humans at the Expanding Your Horizons career fair for elementary and middle school girls Saturday, March 5. The fair, which Wesser helped to organize, will begin at 9 a.m. at UCFs Engineering 2 building.

 

Wesser helped set up the new Office of Women in Engineering and Computer Science at UCF. The office holds educational events, promotes internships and offers tips on internships and study skills for women studying engineering at UCF, and it also reaches out to high school girls.

 

Two years ago Wesser created a video called Britney Gears, which features a girl who is an engineering student during the day and a singer at night, to appeal to girls in middle school. Shes also working on a video of a mock Grammy Awards ceremony featuring Arithmetic Franklin and Joan and Melissa Resistors and a Jimmy Buffett song in which hes wasting away again in my organic chem instead of Margaritaville.

 

The videos have earned national recognition from the Society of Women Engineers, one of the many groups for which Wesser volunteers.

 

The videos show that were fun, Wesser said. We like to give them a contemporary edge."

 

Wesser was one of 38 students statewide who participated in the College Leadership Florida class of 2003-04, and she won the UCF Student Governments 2003 eXtreme Leadership Award, which is given to one student who is heavily involved in five or more groups on campus. She also volunteers as a math tutor for underprivileged third- to fifth-graders on weekends.

 

Ranganathan Kumar, chairman of the UCF Mechanical, Materials and Aerospace Engineering department, said hes proud of Wessers accomplishments and the fact that she is such a great role model for young women who want to study engineering.

 

She is very outgoing and is always eager and willing to learn, Kumar said. She is not afraid to go and talk to somebody and ask them what they think, and she also comes up with a lot of very original ideas.
