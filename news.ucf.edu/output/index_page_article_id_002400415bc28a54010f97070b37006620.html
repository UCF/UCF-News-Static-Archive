Florida FIRST Regional Robotics Competition Inspires Excitement in Engineering

By UCF Staff March 7, 2007

Photo: Jacque Brund

Teams from Orlando, Melbourne and Kissimmee are among the Central Florida participants in the competition hosted by NASA Kennedy Space Center and the UCF College of Engineering and Computer Science.

High school students from throughout Florida, seven other states and Puerto Rico will compete in the action-packed 2007 Florida FIRST Regional Robotics Competition at the University of Central Florida Arena on Friday, March 9, and Saturday, March 10. The competition will be held each day from 9 a.m. until 3 p.m. It is free and open to the public.

Teams from Orlando, Melbourne and Kissimmee are among the Central Florida participants in the competition hosted by NASA Kennedy Space Center and the UCF College of Engineering and Computer Science. Fifty-one teams are scheduled to participate. In this years colorful game, Rack and Roll, students are designing and building robots that will place inflatable tubes on a three-level rack in the center of the playing surface.

The purpose of the regional is to grow Floridas high-tech work force and bring recognition to the state, said Neal Gallagher, dean of the College of Engineering and Computer Science. This high-spirited event has proven successful in inspiring and motivating students to stay in Florida to enter the technically advanced workplace of the future.

The Florida FIRST Regional Robotics Competition is part of an exciting, nationwide contest that teams mentors and young people together to solve an engineering design problem in an intense and competitive way. The national competition will involve more than 30,000 high school students on more than 1,130 teams this year.

NASA Kennedy Space Center has always been an advocate of FIRST Robotics mission. Kennedy Space Center Director Bill Parsons is scheduled to speak at the annual invitation-only luncheon Friday at UCFs Fairwinds Alumni Center. Parsons earned his masters degree in engineering management from UCF and has since helped to lead the nations efforts in space.

NASA Kennedy Space Center fully supports the goals of the FIRST Robotics Competition, Parsons said. At NASA, education is a primary mission, and our work with FIRST and the College of Engineering and Computer Science at UCF has been a great way to achieve that and inspire the next generation of scientific innovators.

UCFs College of Engineering and Computer Science offers scholarship awards of $2,400 to eligible Florida students and $6,800 to eligible out-of-state students who have participated in a FIRST Robotics competition. A total of $70,000 in scholarships will be available this year. For application information, go to https://www.cecs.ucf.edu/acadaffairs/scholar_first/default.asp.

For more information on FIRST Robotics, including participating schools, visit http://www.floridafirstregional.com. For more information on the UCF College of Engineering and Computer Science, please visit www.cecs.ucf.edu.
