Polasek Display Open

By UCF Staff April 27, 2004

Photo: Jerry Klein

"Man Carving His Own Destiny"

The Albin Polasek Museum and Sculpture Gardens and UCF jointly present "Albin Polasek: A Man Carving His Own Destiny" on exhibit in the UCF Library's Special Collections Department through June 11.

Materials are drawn from the UCF Library's Albin Polasek Collection along with rare, fragile and often unseen works from the collection of the Albin Polasek Museum in Winter Park. The Special Collections Department is on the 5th Floor, Room 501 of the library on the Orlando campus, and is free and open to the public and UCF community from 8 a.m. to 5 p.m. Monday through Friday.

The display inlcudes this 1916 bronze of Eliphalet Remington II, which has an interesting story. On the 100th anniversary of Remington Arms in 1916, the company commissioned Polasek to sculpt a likeness of their founder. Then, in 1926, Polasek was commissioned by the Daniel Boone family to create a portrait bust of the frontiersman for the Hall of Fame of Great Americans at New York University.

For several years the standing figure of Eliphalet Remington II was erroneously identified as an alternative version of Boone, kept by Polasek for his personal collection. Recently museum experts began to question the inaccuracy of the period costume for Boone, along with the wrong age of the firing mechanism on the rifle and an investigation was begun.

It was determined that the letters R.A.U.M.C.C. at the base of the sculpture were not foundry marks, but had been used by the Remington Arms Company in the 1920's, standing for Remington Arms Union Metallic Cartridge Company. After contacting Remington, it was found that the work was 10 years older than had been assumed and a major commission (before unknown) was unearthed.

Mysteries such as this are not unknown in the art world according to Dannie Helm, senior archivist in the UCF Library. Works are mislabeled or referred to inaccurately, and soon the inaccuracy becomes a "fact."

With the help of the special collections staff, she says, the Albin Polasek Museum and Sculpture Gardens Staff is going to go through Polasek's papers, which are part of the UCF Library's collections, to confirm the identity of some other works currently in question.

The Albin Polasek Museum and Sculpture Gardens, retirement home of 2004 Florida Artist Hall of Fame recipient Albin Polasek, is listed on the National Register of Historic Places. The museum is at 633 Osceola Ave. in Winter Park. Museum hours are Tuesday-Saturday 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. and Sunday from 1 to 4 p.m. Admission prices: $4 Adult; $3 Senior; $2 Student; Members and under 12 free.
