New Consortium of UCF, Community Colleges to Expand Access to Education

By Chad Binette April 4, 2006

Photo: Courtesy of Orlando Regional Chamber of Commerce

Graduates of four area community colleges will be guaranteed admission to the University of Central Florida through a new consortium that aims to expand students access to higher education and strengthen partnerships in academic programs, advising and financial aid.

By 2015, more than 4,000 graduates a year from Brevard, Lake-Sumter, Seminole and Valencia community colleges could receive bachelors degrees from UCF if the Central Florida Higher Education Consortium achieves its goals. UCF awarded 2,290 bachelors degrees last year to students who graduated from the four community colleges.

UCF now offers more than 35 programs at its regional campuses, and that number could double in the next 10 years if proposed joint-use facilities are built at Seminole and Valencia community colleges.

The consortium will give community college students more choices of bachelors degree programs in their home counties, allowing them to stay close to families or jobs while continuing their studies. Many of the new programs added at regional sites will be in engineering, business, health and nursing, criminology, liberal studies, education and digital media.

The success of past community college graduates at UCF demonstrates that higher standards and increased access can be achieved together, UCF President John Hitt said. We look forward to increasing opportunities for students in their home counties and helping them become teachers, nurses, engineers and other professionals who will make a difference in Central Florida.

Since the schools boards of trustees signed off on the concept of the consortium last fall, presidents Hitt, Thomas Gamble of Brevard Community College, Charles Mojock of Lake-Sumter Community College, Ann McGee of Seminole Community College and Sanford Sandy Shugart of Valencia Community College have worked out many of the details of how it will operate.

The five presidents described the Central Florida Higher Education Consortium to an Orlando Regional Chamber of Commerce luncheon audience on Tuesday. They believe it is an innovative solution to accommodate increased demand for education caused by population growth and more students aspirations to attend college. They believe their approach is a new model that could be replicated in other parts of Florida and the nation.

We are facing a crisis of access to higher education, Shugart said. Thousands of good students wont be able to go to school unless we create more capacity.

While the proposed joint-use facilities at Seminole and Valencia community colleges would allow UCF to offer more programs at those sites, Brevard Community College has space available for more daytime classes. UCF and Lake-Sumter Community College share a joint-use facility constructed in 2002 in Clermont, and there is room to expand programs at that site.

Last year, the four community colleges in the consortium awarded more than 7,600 associates degrees. Valencia and Brevard community colleges were among the top 25 colleges in the country in the number of associates degrees awarded in 2005. Seminole Community College ranks among the top 25 colleges in the country for associate's degrees awarded in liberal arts and sciences and general studies and humanities.

About three-fourths of the graduates of the four community colleges who transfer to public universities in Florida attend UCF. 

The grade-point averages at UCF of students who received associates degrees from community colleges equal the GPAs of students who spend four years at UCF.

Enhanced student services in areas such as advising, admissions and financial aid will make it easier for students -- many of whom are the first in their families to attend college or recent immigrants to the United States -- to make the transition from a community college to UCF.

Brevard Community College has enjoyed a long and productive relationship with the University of Central Florida, and this consortium will further expand the higher educational opportunities in Central Florida, Gamble said. Providing easy access to one of this countrys most valuable commodities -- education -- is what were all about.
