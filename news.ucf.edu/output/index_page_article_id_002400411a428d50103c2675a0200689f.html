UCF Trustees to Discuss New Joint Sites at Seminole, Valencia Community Colleges

By Chad Binette July 25, 2005

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Linda Gray

    • 407-823-2947

  • Tom Evelyn

    • 407-823-5988

  • UCF Board of Trustees

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Florida Board of Trustees at its regular meeting Thursday, July 28, will consider a resolution expressing support for new joint-use facilities at Valencia and Seminole community colleges.

 

Trustees from Valencia and Seminole community colleges already have supported resolutions that call for those institutions and UCF to seek funds from the state Legislature beginning next year for new buildings at Valencias West Campus and Seminoles Sanford/Lake Mary Campus.

 

The joint-use facilities would allow UCF to expand its baccalaureate degree offerings at Valencia and Seminole.

 

UCF trustees committee meetings will begin at 11 a.m. Thursday in the Cape Florida Ballroom of the Student Union. The full board meeting will begin at 2:30 p.m.

 

The trustees also are scheduled to vote on an updated capital improvement plan that covers 2006-07 through 2010-11. The plan includes requests to the Legislature for $10.5 million for the Seminole Community College joint-use facility during the next two years and $11 million for the Valencia Community College joint-use facility during the next three years.

 

The trustees are scheduled to vote on the certification of a direct support organization, or DSO, to finance the convocation center project on behalf of UCF.

 

Board members also will be asked to approve policies and procedures for academic learning compacts required by the state Board of Governors. UCF and other public universities in Florida will be creating such compacts to measure student achievement in baccalaureate degree programs, and the compacts will be available to prospective and enrolled students.

 

In other action, the board is scheduled to vote on amendments to university rules on student organizations, student rights and responsibilities, academic appeals, student discipline and other matters. State law and Board of Governors policy determined the changes that UCF must make.

 

The board also will hear an update from President John Hitt on UCFs plans to formally ask the Board of Governors in November to approve the creation of a medical school on campus.
