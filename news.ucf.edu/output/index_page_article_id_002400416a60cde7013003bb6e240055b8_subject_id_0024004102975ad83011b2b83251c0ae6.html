UCF Statement about Ereck Plancher Trial

Updated June 30, 2011

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Print this Page

This statement is from UCF spokesperson Grant J. Heston:

"Justice was not done. While we appreciate the hard work of the jury, the wrong decision was reached, although the facts were clear:

           -- Water and trainers were present at all times

           -- The response to Ereck’s distress was quick and appropriate

           -- The medical examiner failed to conduct tests that would have shown the real cause of death

We will appeal this decision. We are confident that a just and fair appellate review will find that the verdict came about after a number of decisions in the court that prevented a complete review by the jury. 

This trial called into question the leadership of our athletics program. The jury also decided UCF AA was not liable for gross negligence and should not face punitive damages. This decision is a vindication of the actions and reputations of our athletics leadership. These individuals are people of outstanding character and integrity who are devoted to the health and safety of our student athletes. They continue to have the confidence of the university’s leadership. 

As Coach George O’Leary testified during the trial, we lost a member of the UCF family when Ereck died. Nothing that happened in the past, or that will happen in the future, will change the fact that we continue to miss and grieve the loss of this outstanding young man. We will continue to honor his name and legacy."

Posted June 29, 2011

Three UCF student-athletes and an assistant coach testified Tuesday that Coach George O'Leary did not order water and athletic trainers removed from the March 18 workout.

The defense opened its case with testimony that trainers and water were present throughout the workout. 

For example, when asked whether water and trainers were removed, assistant coach David Kelly said “absolutely not.”

Coach O'Leary testified previously that water and trainers were never removed.

Posted June 20, 2011

For months, the plaintiffs in this case have said water and trainers were not present inside the Nicholson Fieldhouse during the March 18, 2008 workout.

UCF has always said trainers and water were available in the March 18, 2008 workout.

Today, the plaintiffs' own expert witness directly contradicted their prior position by saying he believes water and trainers were in the workout.

The testimony from the plaintiffs' expert witness further confirms the overwhelming majority of players, coaches and trainers who have said previously that water and trainers were available during the workout.

Additionally, the same witness testified that there is medical controversy about whether sickle cell trait causes sudden death in athletes.

The defense has said that sickle cell trait did not cause Ereck Plancher's death; rather tests have shown that it was the result of a pre-existing heart condition.

Posted Monday, June 13

On Monday, June 13, the trial began in the death of UCF student-athlete Ereck Plancher.

The day began with the plaintiffs dropping the University of Central Florida’s Board of Trustees from the lawsuit. The lawsuit will continue against the UCF Athletics Association Inc., a direct support organization created by UCF.

“This is a victory for UCF in that it demonstrates the lawsuit against the Board of Trustees lacked merit. We feel the same about the continuing action against Athletics,” said university spokesperson Grant J. Heston.

Jury selection will continue on Tuesday, June 14.

Posted Friday, June 10

On June 13, trial proceedings will begin concerning the 2008 death of UCF student-athlete Ereck Plancher.

For three years, UCF has respected the legal process and the rights of the Plancher family and the university by not publicly discussing details about the case. Now that the trial is here, the courtroom remains the most appropriate setting in which to present our case.

However, UCF will consider making public statements as necessary to correct inaccuracies. As developments warrant, the university will post updates and official statements to this web page.

Regardless of the trial’s outcome, our goal remains the same: to operate a first-class football program whose first priority is the safety of our student-athletes. We also seek to provide outstanding athletic and educational opportunities for our student-athletes so that our football team will continue to be a point of pride for our students, the university family and the Central Florida community.
