World Premiere of Shapeshifter Brings Celtic Magic, Myth and Mystery to UCF Conservatory Theatre

By Kathleen Lake Feb. 7, 2007

Photo: Jerry Klein

Myths of the Scottish Orkney Islands come to the UCF Conservatory Theatre in the new play "Shapeshifter."

In the isolated Orkney Islands off the coast of Scotland, a young girl, Midge, grieves the strange death of her mother. As she struggles to understand, she and a fisherman weave stories together of a mythical and mysterious female Shapeshifter who causes eerie storms, raging weather and men to become lost at sea.

But their stories may be more than fanciful. The haunting power of many Shapeshifters abounds in these islands, and Midge and her family appear to be caught in their thrall. Is Midges mother really gone, or has she become incarnate in the creatures of Midges visions?

The world premiere of Shapeshifter will open at 8 p.m. Thursday, Feb. 22, in the UCF Conservatory Theatres Black Box Theatre on the UCF main campus. Performances will be Feb. 22 through 25 and Feb. 28 through March 4. All shows will begin at 8 p.m., except for Sunday matinees, which will start at 2 p.m. The shows themes include some adult content.

Ticket prices are $15 for adults, $13 for seniors over 55 and $10 for students with ID. Reservations are recommended and available through the UCF Conservatory Theatre box office by calling 407-823-1500. The box office can assist with accommodations for patrons with disabilities, and group rates are available.

Shapeshifter is a new play by Laura Schellhardt, a former UCF faculty member whose play The K of D won the New Play Award from ACT Theatre in Seattle and received a workshop production at Orlando Shakespeare Festivals PlayFest last spring. Her work has also been produced by The Public Theatre in New York City, Northlight Theatre in Chicago and the Provincetown Repertory Theatre in Massachusetts.

Director Be Boyd is an associate professor at UCF whose recent directing projects include Lysistrata and The Laramie Project. She recently appeared as Calpurnia in Julius Caesar at Orlando Shakespeare Festival. Before coming to UCF, Boyd was an associate professor at Texas Christian University, where she led the Acting Program.
