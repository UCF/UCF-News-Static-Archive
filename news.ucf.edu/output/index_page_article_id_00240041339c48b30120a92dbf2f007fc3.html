Members of Congress, 'Daily Show' Comedian Analyze Obama's First 100 Days

By UCF Staff April 15, 2009

Photo: Jacque Brund

U.S. Rep. Suzanne Kosmas discussed the federal budget with House colleague Allen Boyd.

Several current and former U.S. representatives joined Daily Show “senior black correspondent” Larry Wilmore at a University of Central Florida political symposium analyzing President Barack Obama’s first 100 days in office.

The spring Lou Frey Institute of Politics and Government symposium on Wednesday, April 15, combined serious analyses of how the Obama administration has handled the economy, national security and more with an evening presentation and book signing by Wilmore. A large crowd of students from UCF and area high schools, along with community members, attended the event.

The symposium covered Obama’s relationship with the 111th Congress, an analysis of his economic policy and the future, a discussion on his security policies and a view of his administration from the perspective of historical comparisons.

“Perhaps never before has a new administration’s first 100 days come during a more pivotal moment in American history,” said Lou Frey, founder of the institute and a congressman who represented Central Florida from 1969 to 1979. “Never before has a new administration spent so much money.”

The morning session of “The First 100 Days: Policy Initiatives and Approaches for the New Administration” featured presentations by Democratic U.S. representatives Suzanne Kosmas and Allen Boyd about the federal budget.

Kosmas, celebrating her 100th day in office, discussed how the Obama administration and Congress are focusing on four priority areas: the economy, the environment, education and health care She also said the economic stimulus package will provide a needed short-term boost to the economy and create jobs in education, health care and alternative energy.

Kosmas, whose district includes UCF, also said she met recently with College of Medicine Dean Deborah German, and she praised the “major mecca of medical technology” that is being developed at Lake Nona.

Boyd, a member of the House Budget Committee, lauded Obama for submitting a budget that clearly states the true expenses for the federal government. The costs of the wars in Iraq and Afghanistan, Medicare expenses and other items were not included in President Bush’s budgets, he said.

“We can’t fix the (budget) problems until we are honest with ourselves,” he said.

Former members of Congress William H. “Bill” Zeliff Jr., a New Hampshire Republican, and Jim Slattery, a Kansas Democrat, also spoke.

Wilmore, an actor and comedian best known for his role on Comedy Central’s “The Daily Show With Jon Stewart,” gave the evening keynote address. He discussed his experiences as a stand-up comedian who focused on political and social humor in the 1980s. He later became a writer for shows such as “In Living Color” and “The Office,” and he created “The Bernie Mac Show.”

Wilmore said Hollywood has not been an easy place for black writers, especially those who want to write for traditionally white shows.

Asked whether comedians are less likely to target Obama because of his race, Wilmore said no.

“Politicians give us so much material, the racial factor really doesn’t matter,” he said.

After his presentation, Wilmore signed copies of his first book, titled “I’d Rather We Got Casinos and Other Black Thoughts.”

Other speakers included UCF political science professors and Stephen Wayne, a professor of government at Georgetown University and the author of “The Road to the White House 2008.”

The Lou Frey Institute of Politics and Government is a nonpartisan organization housed in the UCF Department of Political Science. The institute promotes civic education and engagement, particularly among young people. Institute programs encourage informed discussion of issues from diverse viewpoints, stimulate participation in the political process and support research on politics and policy.

Sponsors for the symposium include Industrial Enterprises of America; Lowndes, Drosdick, Doster, Kantor & Reed, P.A.; the United States Association of Former Members of Congress; the U.S. Department of Education; and the UCF College of Sciences, Department of Political Science, Global Perspectives Office, Kurdish Political Studies Initiative, Middle Eastern Studies Program and Terrorism Studies Program.

Visit www.loufrey.org for more information and a full symposium schedule.
