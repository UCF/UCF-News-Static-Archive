Program Led by UCF Student Teaches Lake County Kids About Nutrition, Fitness

By Kristi Bartlett March 5, 2007

Photo: Jerry Klein

Jessica Schwartz, a sports and fitness major at the University of Central FloridaÂ’s South Lake campus, lead a new after-school program that she created to promote the importance of good health and nutrition for children.

TUFF (Teaching Us Fitness is Fun) Kids began Jan. 29 at three Lake County elementary schools: Mascotte, Beverly Shores and Clermont, all of which have high percentages of students who receive free and reduced lunches. During two sessions a week, the students exercised and learned how physical activities and good nutrition help their bodies. They also made healthy snacks and took the recipes home to their families.

The six-week program, a partnership involving UCF, Lake County Schools and Leesburg Regional Medical Center , culminated March 7, with sessions at 3:30 p.m. at each of the schools. Parents and kids made the healthy snacks together during the final session.

Schwartz planned the entire program, including lesson plans, handouts and instructor training. She based the hourlong sessions on Catch Kids Club, a national fitness and nutrition program, and incorporated knowledge she gained during her sports and fitness classes at UCF and at her job as assistant director of My Gym, a kidsÂ’ gym in Oviedo .

Schwartz, a senior from Buffalo, N.Y. , said she was pleased that the Lake County pupils responded enthusiastically to the program and were active participants.
