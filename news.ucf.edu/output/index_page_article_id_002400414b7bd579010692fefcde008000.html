UCF Offers Counseling, Sets Task Force to Examine Tailgating Following Tragic Death

By Tom Evelyn Sept. 26, 2005

The University of Central Florida is offering counseling services to its students and police officers and has established a task force to examine tailgating policies following the tragic death of UCF Police Officer Mario Jenkins at the Citrus Bowl on Saturday.

Jenkins was working undercover with the state Division of Alcoholic Beverages and Tobacco to investigate underage drinking when he was shot and killed. The Florida Department of Law Enforcement is investigating the incident.

Â“Losing a member of our university family is difficult for all of us,Â” UCF President John Hitt said. Â“This is a tragic loss that is going to change a lot of lives, and we want to do everything we can to help those who are grieving and to ensure the safety of our students and others who attend UCF games.Â”

The Counseling Center is offering a special grief counseling session from 7 to 8:30 p.m. tonight, Sept. 26, for members of the UCF community. The session will be held in the Pensacola board room at the Student Union. Individual and group counseling will be available.

From 1 to 3:30 p.m. weekdays, counselors will be available at the Counseling Center located in Room 203 of Ferrell Commons, the former Student Resource Center. Ferrell Commons is located next to the Marketplace dining hall. Other group support sessions will be scheduled during the week.Â 

The UCF Police Department is providing counseling for all of its officers.

Hitt established the task force to develop policies and procedures dealing with alcohol use and security at large public gatherings sponsored by UCF, particularly home football games. The group will work quickly to provide recommendations before UCFÂ’s next home game on Oct. 8.

Retired U.S. Navy Vice Admiral Al Harms will chair the task force. Harms joined UCF this summer as assistant vice president for research and special assistant to the president. He retired from the Navy last year after more than 33 years of service.

Members of the task force, which will begin meeting this week, will include a cross-section of university officials, law enforcement leaders, students, alumni and others.

NOTE: In expressing concern for the young man who also was shot outside the Citrus Bowl Saturday, the University of Central Florida mistakenly identified him as a UCF student with a similar name.Â  FDLE has confirmed the identification of the man as Michael John Young, who is not a UCF student.
