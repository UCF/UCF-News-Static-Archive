First Restaurant Near New UCF Convocation Center Opened Saturday

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala Updated Feb. 7, 2007

Photo: Jacque Brund

On Saturday, Red Brick Pizza became the first retailer associated with the Convocation Center project to open. Employees prepared by serving lunch to invited guests on Friday.

Students and the public got their first taste of the retail center around the University of Central Florida's new Convocation Center on Saturday, Jan. 13.

Red Brick Pizza became the first retailer associated with the Convocation Center project to open. Maggie Moo's Ice Cream and Papa John's pizza also are open.

The 10,000-seat Convocation Center is on schedule to be completed in July and within budget, said Bart Bruchok, project manager for UCF's Facilities Planning. The center will host basketball games, commencement ceremonies, concerts and other events.

The $250 million project includes the new center, the refurbishing of the existing UCF Arena, new student housing that will add living space for 2,000 students, 89,000-square-feet of retail space, parking and a landscaped pedestrian mall.

The Convocation Center will double the seating capacity of the UCF Arena, which will help UCF attract more top concerts and family-oriented shows. The center also will help UCF's basketball teams recruit better student-athletes and entice more well-known opponents to play on campus. 

Three of four residence halls called The Towers at Golden Knights Plaza are already open and full. Each seven-story building houses about 500 students. The last tower is scheduled to open in August and will include a block of rooms for students attending the Burnett Honors College.

Inside the Convocation Center, patrons will also find a 24,000-square-foot, upscale, healthy-choice dining facility.

What makes this project unique is the blend of the athletics facility with vendors that include everything from restaurants to hair salons. Many universities host restaurants on their campuses, but few have a hub of storefronts. Retailers that have signed on to call UCF home include Nature's Table, Best Haircuts, Barnes & Noble, Papa John's Pizza, Ron Jon Surf Shop and Firkins, an English pub.

Most of the retail spaces along the west side of the Convocation Center, where Red Brick Pizza, Subway and Maggie Moo's are located, are already booked. Space remains available on other sides of the center.

Construction soon will start on the pedestrian mall, a large lawn in front of the new center. Trees and benches will decorate the north-to-south sidewalks, providing shade. The rest of the area will be open, allowing students to play football or hold activities throughout the year. Work is scheduled to begin on the mall in February and to be completed by late July, said Dick Paradise, director of the UCF Physical Plant.

Once complete, the north end of campus will provide everything students need to live, work and play, all within walking distance.

"We're very excited," said Bill Merck, vice president for Administration and Finance. "It's one of those projects that changes the culture of a campus in a positive way, building on the already strong sense of community that we have here at UCF."

No state dollars, tuition or student fees are being used to finance the Convocation Center. Costs will be covered with revenues generated by the center, including the sale of suites and club seats, student housing payments and concerts and other events held in the center.

Red Brick Pizza owner Jean-Philippe Simonet said he's thrilled to open his restaurant, the third franchise in Florida. Catering to the college crowd, Red Brick Pizza will be open from 11 a.m. to midnight Thursday through Saturday and from 11 a.m. to 11 p.m. Sunday through Wednesday. Take-out and delivery services will be available.

"Students don't go to bed until 4 a.m.," Simonet said. "So we have to stay open late."
