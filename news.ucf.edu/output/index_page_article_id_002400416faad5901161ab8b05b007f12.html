Consumer Advocate Ralph Nader to Discuss 'Going Green' Friday

By Mark Freeman Nov. 7, 2007

Photo: Courtesy of UCF Global Perspectives

Ralph Nader, a consumer advocate for more than four decades and a former presidential candidate, will discuss “Going Green: Getting to the Bottom Line,” Friday, Nov. 9, at the University of Central Florida.

Nader will speak at 1 p.m. in the auditorium (room 108) of the new Psychology Building. The forum, which is free and open to the public, is part of the university’s yearlong focus on the environment and global climate change.

Nader’s foray into public life began in 1965, when he wrote “Unsafe at Any Speed,” an exposé of the disregard carmakers held for the safety of their customers. The Senate hearing into Nader’s accusations and the resulting motor vehicle safety laws catapulted Nader into the public sphere.

Nader spent the next few decades as a vocal consumer advocate, and he was instrumental in the creation of the Occupational Safety and Health Administration, the Environmental Protection Agency and the Consumer Product Safety Commission. He also advocated for the creation of laws related to safe drinking water, meat and poultry inspections and the control of water pollution.

Nader has formed many citizen groups, including the Center for Auto Safety, Public Citizen, the Pension Rights Center, the National Coalition for Universities in the Public Interest and the student Public Interest Research Groups, which operate in more than 20 states.

Disillusioned with the two-party political system in the United States, Nader organized the Green Party's first presidential campaign in 1996. He received 700,000 votes with a campaign budget of $5,000. He ran again in 2000, when he received 2.8 million votes.
 
Nader has been honored by Time magazine as “one of the 100 most influential Americans of the twentieth century” and recently as “one of the 100 most influential figures in American history” by The Atlantic.

He is also the author of several books, including “Children First: A Parents Guide to Fighting Corporate Predators;” “No Contest: Corporate Lawyers and the Perversion of Justice in America;” and “Crashing the Party: How to Tell the Truth and Still Run for President.”

Nader’s presentation was organized by the UCF Global Perspectives Office. Other sponsors of the forum include UCF’s Office of Undergraduate Studies, Student Development and Enrollment Services, Student Government Association, Department of Public Administration, Burnett Honors College, Focus the Nation, Political Science Department and the Global Connections Foundation.
