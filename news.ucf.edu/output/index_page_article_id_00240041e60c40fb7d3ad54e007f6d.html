Trustees Approve Nonprofit Management Degree, Retirement Plans

By Tom Evelyn March 25, 2004

Photo: Jerry Klein

President Hitt tells trustees about the recent accomplishments of faculty and students.

Full Photo

Bolstering UCFs role as a metropolitan research university, the Board of Trustees on Thursday approved adding a nonprofit management masters degree and economics and sociology doctorates that will focus on issues affecting the Central Florida region.

 

The board also approved two new retirement plan options for employees and tenure for nearly two dozen professors and the new engineering dean at its regular meeting at the Student Union.

 

The masters degree in nonprofit management will allow students and faculty to partner more with Central Floridas nonprofit community, which includes more than 2,600 public charities and nonprofit organizations, said Patricia Bishop, vice provost and dean of Graduate Studies. Students will be required to complete a 33-hour course of online study with extensive service-learning activities.

 

The masters program, which will enroll 36 students within five years, will meet a critical need for highly skilled professionals who can effectively plan, secure funding and work with the community, Bishop said.

 

There are only 12 nonprofit management programs in the country, including one at Florida Atlantic University, she said. UCFs masters program will begin enrolling students in the fall.

 

The board also approved a sociology doctorate that will focus on metropolitan and urban issues such as crime, domestic violence and social inequality and an economics doctorate that will emphasize the environmental economics of issues such as water resources and transportation. Both programs need the approval of the state Board of Governors before they can be implemented.

 

The retirement plans approved by the trustees include an option that would allow part-time employees not covered under an existing plan to redirect Social Security and Medicare, or FICA, contributions to a UCF retirement plan. The other option would allow employees who can collect payment for accumulated leave at retirement to place that money in a qualified pension plan.

 

The board also approved tenure recommendations for 23 professors and tenure with hire for incoming College of Engineering and Computer Science Dean Neal Gallagher, who will leave his post as engineering dean at Colorado State University and come to UCF later this spring.

 

At the beginning of the regular meeting, trustees Chairman Dick Nunis honored outgoing trustee and Student Body President Brian Battles with a plaque for his yearlong service on the board. Battles will be succeeded on the board by Student Body President-elect Kevin Peters.

 

Nunis and UCF President John Hitt also congratulated the universitys mens basketball team for its successful season, donning wigs in honor of Dexter Lyons, the teams star forward known for his unique hairstyle.

 

The Golden Knights finished the season with a 25-6 record and an NCAA basketball tournament bid.

 

(Coach) Kirk (Speraw), we congratulate you on just an outstanding year, Hitt said, adding that the team played with incredible effort throughout the season and the first round of the tournament. We want to congratulate every one of you.

 

Hitt also recognized this years members of the Order of Pegasus, the highest honor given to UCF students. The award recognizes students who have excelled in university involvement, leadership, academic achievement and community service. This years class includes 13 undergraduate and graduate students.

 

The Board of Trustees Educational Program Committee will hold a workshop May 3 to discuss UCFs academic programs. The boards next regular meeting is scheduled for May 13. For more information on the board, visit www.ucf.edu/bot.
