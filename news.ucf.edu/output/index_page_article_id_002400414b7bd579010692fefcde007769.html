More Uniformed Police Officers to Work Football Game Saturday

By Chad Binette Oct. 4, 2005

Photo: Jerry Klein

Media turned out in force for the task force meeting Tuesday.

All University of Central Florida and Orlando police officers who work the UCF-Memphis football game on Saturday, Oct. 8, will wear uniforms, hoping the increased presence of uniformed officers will enhance the safety of fans, both departments announced Tuesday.

UCF Sgt. Troy Williamson and Orlando Police Capt. Val Demings announced their departments new policies on uniformed officers. All UCF police will wear uniforms for the remaining three home games.

Other changes recommended Tuesday by a task force on tailgating and alcohol include free food for UCF students and more pre-game entertainment for fans. Faculty, staff and student ambassadors may walk around tailgating areas Saturday to ensure that everything is going smoothly.  A new spirit squad of students also may be formed to walk through tailgating areas shortly before kickoff to encourage students to move into the stadium to cheer on the Golden Knights.

Lets find a neat, fun way to rally all of the students and get them into the game, said Jennifer Hartzler, a UCF student and task force member.

The task force met Tuesday for the third time since UCF police officer Mario Jenkins was shot to death outside the Citrus Bowl on Sept. 24. UCF President John Hitt, who created the panel to review policies on tailgating and alcohol, will receive the panels recommendations Wednesday and decide how to proceed before Saturdays game.

Additional recommendations included prohibiting beer funnels and letting students and other fans with open containers of alcohol know that they may be required to show an identification to prove that they are at least 21 years old.

Also on Tuesday, the Student Government Association and GAMMA announced that a memorial service for Jenkins will be held at 6:30 p.m. Thursday, Oct. 6, in front of the Reflecting Pond. The service will include a presentation by Student Body President Willie Bentley, a candle-lighting ceremony and a tribute read by Kevan Stone. Attendees are encouraged to dress nicely.

The Florida Department of Law Enforcement is investigating the situation in the Citrus Bowl parking lot that led to Jenkins death. Jenkins was working as a plainclothes officer at the game.

The task force, which includes UCF students and administrators and area law enforcement officials, plans to meet again at 1 p.m. Wednesday, Oct. 12, to discuss the effectiveness of the changes for Saturdays game. The panel will continue to meet after that to address long-term issues related to alcohol and university events.
