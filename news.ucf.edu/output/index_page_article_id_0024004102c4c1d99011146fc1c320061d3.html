UCF Ranked Among Top 100 Universities for Hispanic Students

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala May 21, 2007

Photo: Jacque Brund

UCF has seen a growth in the number of Hispanic students enrolling and graduating from the institution.

Full Photo

The University of Central Florida is becoming a destination university for Hispanic students.

According to the May edition of Hispanic Outlook in Higher Education magazine, UCF is one of the best 100 colleges in the United States for Hispanic students seeking bachelors degrees. Its also among the best for those aiming for masters and doctoral degrees.

UCF ranked 13th for undergraduate degrees awarded  an improvement of three spots over last year. The university also ranked 41st for masters degrees, an improvement of one spot, and 38th for doctorates, an improvement of 45 spots.

Thats a remarkable climb considering UCF only made the doctorate list for the first time last year. In 2005-2006, UCF awarded 12 doctoral degrees to Hispanics. The year before, only six such degrees were awarded. The University of Texas at Austin  the top-ranked university for Hispanics -- handed out 51 doctorates this past year. The University of Florida, the highest-ranked Florida school on that list, gave out 32.

"UCF's recognition as one of the top 100 colleges and universities nationwide in Hispanic Outlook is very significant and demonstrates our commitment to the success of all students at the university, said Gloria Laureano, director of First Year Advising and Exploration and UCF's representative on the ENLACE (Engaging Latino Communities for Education) Florida project.

ENLACE, a national initiative launched by the W. K. Kellogg Foundation in 1999, weaves together educational partnerships to identify and eliminate the barriers to success in higher education for diverse, underrepresented students. The goal is to improve college readiness, access and success for Latino, first-generation and low-income students and other underrepresented groups across the state.

UCF provides support services such as tutoring and counseling while looking for innovative ways to engage students and their parents throughout their careers here. For example, UCF offers orientation sessions in Spanish for parents of students who feel more comfortable speaking their native tongue, so they get all the information their children do.

It is gratifying to know that our continued focus on student success is having a positive impact on the graduation rates of our Hispanic students, said Maribeth Ehasz, vice president for Student Development and Enrollment Services. We are committed to challenging and supporting all students to make the most of their educational experience at UCF. This recognition in Hispanic Outlook demonstrates that our efforts are achieving success.

UCF also placed in the top 10 for degrees awarded to Hispanics in certain categories. UCF ranked eighth for business and marketing, seventh for health sciences and 10th for psychology.

Seven other Florida universities earned a place on the 14th-annual list. Florida International University earned the top spot in the bachelors and masters categories.

The magazine is sent to local high schools and high education educators across the country.
