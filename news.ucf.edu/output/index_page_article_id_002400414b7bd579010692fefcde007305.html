Orlando Mayor Buddy Dyer, Top UCF Donors Honored at Presidents Appreciation Gala

By Chad Binette Oct. 11, 2005

Photo: Jacque Brund

UCF President John Hitt presented Orlando Mayor Buddy Dyer with the UCF Partnership Award for 2004-05.

University of Central Florida President John Hitt presented Orlando Mayor Buddy Dyer with the UCF Partnership Award and honored several of the universitys top 2004-05 donors Tuesday during the annual Presidents Appreciation Gala.

Hitt lauded Dyers visionary goals and praised the citys leadership in helping UCF open the Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy and move other School of Film and Digital Media programs into the former Expo Centre. The city has contributed more than $4.3 million to renovate the center.

Coupled with critical support from the Florida Legislature, UCFs foothold in downtown Orlando is now not only an impressive one, but more importantly, it intimately involves us with the downtown renaissance, the creation of prosperity, the rapidly growing digital media cluster and the building of our community, Hitt told the large crowd of UCF supporters gathered in the Pegasus Grand Ballroom of the Student Union.

Hitt also presented John and Carrie Morgridge with the Presidents Award, which honors individuals who have made significant contributions to UCF. The Morgridges contributed $2 million this year to help UCF establish the Morgridge National Reading Center. The gift, which will be matched with $1.75 million from the state, is the largest ever received by the College of Education. Read more about the Morgridges donation.

Hitt welcomed three new members of the H. Trevor Colbourn Society, which recognizes donations of $1 million or more and is named in honor of President Emeritus H. Trevor Colbourn. Sonja and Tony Nicholson of Nicholson USA Properties pledged $2 million to support construction of the Nicholson Fieldhouse, an indoor practice facility for UCF sports teams. Read more about the fieldhouse donation.

The Northrop Grumman Corp. donated $22 million in intellectual property and $2 million in equipment to the College of Optics and Photonics. The gift was the largest in UCF history. Read more about the gift.

Walt Disney World was honored for supporting students and faculty in the performing arts, hospitality and engineering and for its partnerships with the Disney-SBA Entrepreneurship Center.

Other new Colbourn Society members who were unable to attend Tuesdays gala are Kenneth G. Dixon, a 1975 UCF alumnus, and Susan and Jerry S. Roth.

Also Tuesday, President Hitt thanked members of the Charles N. Millican Society, which recognizes those who have included UCF in their estate plans and is named after President Emeritus Charles Millican. Those donors are recognized individually during a separate ceremony.
