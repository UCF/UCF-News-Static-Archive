UCF Computer Programming Team Wins Regional Title, Advances to World Finals

By Sam Gardner Oct. 31, 2006

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Ali Orooji

    • 407-823-5660

  • Association for Computing Machinery International Collegiate Programming Contest

  • Print this Page

A University of Central Florida computer programming team won first place last weekend at the Association for Computing Machinery International Collegiate Programming Contest in Georgia.

The contest was a regional qualifier for the world championships in Tokyo next March. Four other UCF teams competed, and all of them placed in the top 20 out of 65 participating teams.

Senior Walter Mundt of Orlando, senior Jobby Johns of Jacksonville and first-year graduate student Nadeem Mohsin of India competed on the winning UCF team and became one of 83 teams to qualify for the world championships. UCF placed ahead of schools such as Georgia Tech and the University of Florida.

Mundt was a member of the UCF team that received an honorable mention during the 2005-06 world finals in April in San Antonio.

Ali Orooji, an associate professor in UCFs Department of Computer Science, coached the five UCF squads.

Teams of three students used their programming skills to solve 10 complex, real world problems within a five-hour deadline.  The problems were equivalent to a semesters worth of computer programming, and the team that solved the most problems correctly received the coveted spot in the world finals.

UCFs winning group solved nine of the 10 problems correctly to win the battle of the brains.  The global competition is sponsored by IBM and includes more than 6,000 university teams from 84 countries on six continents.
