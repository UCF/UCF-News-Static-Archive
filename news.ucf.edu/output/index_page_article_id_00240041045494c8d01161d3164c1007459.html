Football Game Day Update: The Championship Game

By UCF Staff Nov. 28, 2007

Photo: Jacque Brund

Knight Moves and the UCF cheerleaders participated in a pep rally for the title game on Wednesday in the Student Union.

The University of Central Florida will host the Conference USA championship game at noon Saturday at Bright House Networks Stadium. Fans attending the Knights’ game against the Tulsa Golden Hurricane are encouraged to review the following game day schedule and procedures.

Up-to-date information and procedures are available at www.ucfgameday.com. UCF has also set up a special telephone help desk that will be active on home football game days at 407-882-FANS or 407-882-3267.

Ticket information: Tickets can be purchased online at www.ticketmaster.com or by calling the UCF Athletics Ticket Box Office at 407-823-1000. The UCFAA Ticket Box Office is located at Bright House Networks Stadium on the ground floor off the IOA west entry plaza by gate 1. The ticket office is open from 8 a.m. to 6 p.m. weekdays, and it will open at 8 a.m. Saturday.

Fans who already purchased their tickets online are encouraged to print their tickets ahead of time to avoid will call lines on game day. To print tickets, follow the following instructions.

1.      Log on to your account via the following link :
https://oss.ticketmaster.com/html/home.htmI?l=EN&team=ucf

2.      Select "Get Started" under "Manage Your Tickets"

3.      Select the game and seats you wish to forward and select "Forward"

4.      Provide a valid e-mail address for the tickets to be sent to. Emailing them to yourself will allow you to print the tickets.

Please note that some spam filters on e-mail providers may view the e-mail and attachment as spam. Fans are encouraged to adjust their filters prior to e-mailing the tickets.

Please contact the Athletics Ticket Office at 407-823-1000 if you have any questions.

Student ticket information: Student-priced tickets ($10) are still available, and students are encouraged to not miss out on the biggest college football event of their student years. Student tickets can be purchased at the UCFAA Ticket Office located at Bright House Networks Stadium between Gates 1 & 18 from 8 a.m. to 6 p.m. Additionally, today and Friday, a UCFAA ticket sales table will be set up in the Student Union from 11:30 a.m. to 1:30 p.m.

Parking: Fans should use their parking passes from the UTEP game on Saturday, Nov. 24, to access the reserved Gold Zone parking areas for the championship game.

There are about 12,000 parking spots available on campus. Parking is divided into three sections -- Gold, Red and Blue.

The Gold Zone, closest to Bright House Networks Stadium, requires a reserved game day parking permit and is only for members of the Golden Knights Club, credentialed media, stadium operations personnel, patrons with disabilities and students with special permits. 
 
Only Gold Zone patrons and those with disabilities will be allowed to drive on and off campus via Orion Boulevard from three hours prior to kickoff until two hours after the game ends.

Almost all parking in the Red Zone and Blue Zone is open to everyone. The spaces are free and on a first-come, first-served basis.

Parking is not allowed along campus roadways, in residential housing parking lots or garages, or on any open grassy areas throughout campus.

Parking for fans with disabilities: Parking for fans with disabilities is available on the first floor of Garage D across from the UCF Arena. Fans with valid handicapped parking permits are allowed to enter the Gold Zone via Libra Drive or Orion Boulevard to access Garage D. While there are other handicapped parking spaces throughout the campus, shuttles to and from the stadium will be available only from Garage D.

Student parking policies on and before game day: Students are reminded that parking in any of the Gold Zone lots (C 1-2, D 1-2 and E 1-8) without a Gold Zone permit is prohibited from 7 a.m. until midnight on game day. Vehicles parked without the appropriate permits in Gold Zone lots or garages will be towed. Parking garages C, D and F will be closed from 7 p.m. Friday to 7 a.m. Sunday.

Students who live in the Towers residence halls will not be able to access parking garages E and G between 9 a.m. and 5 p.m. Saturday. They can leave their vehicles in the garages during that time or move them to another location outside of the Gold Zone before 9 a.m. 

Parking in Garage B, located across from the Recreation and Wellness Center, is prohibited from 11 p.m. Friday until 7 a.m. Sunday except for students, faculty, staff and others who are on the pre-approved list for official university business on Saturday.

Classes: UCF will follow the same policy that has been in effect throughout the season. Classes that begin before noon on Saturday will be held. All of those classes will be held in the Communications Building. While other academic buildings on campus will be locked on game day, professors and students who want to work in their offices or research labs will be able to do that if they have keys to get into the buildings.

Knightwalk: The UCF football team is expected to arrive around 9:30 a.m. Saturday at IOA Plaza on the west side of the stadium (by gates 2 and 3). The Marching Knights and cheerleaders will join the team for the Knightwalk through the plaza and into the stadium.

UCF Alumni Association Pre-Game Party: The UCF Alumni Association will host a pre-game party from 9 to 11a.m. at the Fairwinds Alumni Center. Tickets are $10 each for Alumni Association members and Golden Knights Club donors and for one guest per member. Tickets are $45 for all additional non-members.

No smoking: Fans are reminded that smoking is not permitted anywhere inside Bright House Networks Stadium, including the seating area and concourse.

No glass: Fans are reminded that glass is not allowed in the tailgating areas on campus.

Neighbors: Fans should be respectful of UCF’s neighbors and are advised to park on campus for free and not park in surrounding neighborhoods. Law-enforcement officers will be patrolling local subdivisions on game day to encourage fans not to block driveways or park on private property.

Orange and Seminole counties' ordinances prohibit open containers of alcohol on public streets or parking lots. For a more detailed news story on UCF working with neighbors, click here.

Red Cross: Volunteers from the American Red Cross will be available in front of the UCF Arena on game day for fans who need first-aid assistance. 

Sportsmanship: This year's fan motto, "Do the Knight Thing," is all about being a great fan, having fun and staying safe. Fans are encouraged to show respect to UCF's guests from Tulsa. For more information, click here.

Black and Gold Day: Orange County, the City of Orlando and all UCF campuses will celebrate “Black and Gold Day” on Friday. All UCF alumni, fans and supporters are encouraged to show their support for the Knights as they make their stand for the C-USA title.

Additional information about getting to campus, tailgating and other details of game day is below.

Traffic

Preferred routes for each of the three parking zones are listed at www.ucfgameday.com, and fans are encouraged to follow them.

Gemini Boulevard North will be closed to vehicle traffic from Alafaya Trail to North Orion Boulevard from three hours prior to game time until two hours after the game ends. This road closure is designed to ensure pedestrian safety between the main and north sections of campus.

UCF will operate about 25 shuttle buses for students to and from several nearby apartment complexes, from five hours prior to the start of the game until three hours after the game ends. The service is free. The on-campus shuttle routes (Black and Gold routes) will not be operating on game days.

For a more detailed news story on traffic, click here.

Tailgating 
 
Tailgating is allowed from 7 a.m. until two hours after the game ends on game days. No overnight tailgating will be allowed before the championship game.

On game days, tailgating is allowed in any ground-level parking lot. It is not permitted in any parking garage on any level. Tailgating activities without vehicles are allowed on most other areas on campus.

Only those 21 and older are permitted to drink alcoholic beverages. Kegs, beer funnels and glass containers are not allowed on campus.

Fans should recycle plastic and aluminum cans in the "UCF Recycles!" white cardboard containers located around campus and in the stadium. For a more detailed story on tailgating, click here.

Located in Orlando, Fla., UCF offers high-quality undergraduate and graduate education, student development and continuing education, while conducting cutting-edge research that powers the region's economic development.
