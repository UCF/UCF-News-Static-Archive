Artist, PBS Show Host Donna Dewberry Speaks to UCF Heathrow Business Class

By UCF Staff July 6, 2007

Photo: Courtesy of Joy Colarusso

Donna Dewberry led the students in designing their own hand-painted greeting cards after she spoke to Visiting Instructor Joy Colarusso’s Management of Organizations class.

Full Photo

Donna Dewberry, an artist known for her One Stroke painting technique and for hosting several PBS television shows, spoke this week at the University of Central Floridas Heathrow campus.

Dewberry spoke to Visiting Instructor Joy Colarussos Management of Organizations class about her experiences with licensing her home textile products at major retailers such as Wal-Mart, Home Depot and JC Penney and her television shows. She hosts The Donna Dewberry Show and other programs that air throughout the country on PBS affiliates.

Dewberry is a self-taught artist who learned her craft through observation and practice. The author of more than 100 instructional books and videos, she created a "One Stitch" sewing technique and is expanding her business overseas. The enterprise involves her family; her husband serves as the marketing director, and their seven children are involved in design and management.

Dewberry covered the history of her business and its growth from a hobby to an international company making appearances on HGTV, QVC and PBS and in craft stores. She spoke about the lessons she learned as she worked in both the business and art worlds. She emphasized the importance of a strong work ethic and of paying attention to trends that affect the craft industry.

After speaking, Dewberry led the students in designing their own hand-painted greeting cards. Dewberry also supplied students with paints, paint brushes, instructional booklets and autographs.

For more information on Dewberry, visit www.onestroke.com.
