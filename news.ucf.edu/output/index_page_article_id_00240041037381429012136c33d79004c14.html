Swine Flu: Update for UCF Community

Updated July 7, 2009

EDITOR'S NOTE: This e-mail was sent to UCF students, faculty and staff. The number of confirmed cases of H1N1 flu among UCF students is now 10.

This is an update to UCF faculty and staff members about the worldwide H1N1 flu pandemic (previously called swine flu) and its impact to student learning.

The outbreak of the H1N1 influenza was first detected in humans on April 15. As of June 25, nearly 28,000 cases and 127 deaths have been recorded in the U.S., with 941 cases and two deaths in Florida, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention.

To date, the university Health Services Center has tracked five UCF students confirmed with H1N1. Orange County has tracked over 100 confirmed cases.

Like seasonal flu, H1N1 flu can vary in severity from mild to severe. Most people who are sick with H1N1 flu are experiencing illness similar to seasonal flu and are recovering.

For answers to frequently asked questions about H1N1 flu, please follow this link:
http://floridashealth.com/DEMO/php/SwineFlu/documents/swinefluqanda.doc

Student Impact

With the start of Summer B and more than 30,000 students on campus, we expect more cases to be identified by UCF Health Services staff. Campus leaders have been meeting regularly to review Centers for Disease Control and Prevention guidelines for prevention and management of H1N1-confirmed cases.

Each student who presents at UCF Health Services with a presumptive diagnosis for H1N1 (specimens are sent to the state lab for confirmation) will be given a "Patient Care Advisory for H1N1" form  with guidelines on how to recover quickly and prevent the spread of the H1N1 virus.

The guidelines recommend students stay in isolation for seven days after the symptoms began or until the student is symptom-free for 24 hours, whichever is longer.

Because of privacy laws concerning protected health information, UCF Health Services staff will not notify faculty or staff members about a student's illness. The student will be told they may use the "Patient Care Advisory for H1N1" that is signed and dated by the provider as proof of illness for class absence. Make-up work for class(es) missed must be discussed with and agreed upon by the faculty member.

UCF Health Services staff appreciates any assistance faculty and staff members can give to students affected by H1N1, and in helping to promote the university's H1N1 prevention efforts.

More Information

There are some important points for faculty and staff members to understand about H1N1:

UCF officials urge everyone to reduce their chances of becoming ill by taking common prevention measures -  wash hands frequently throughout the day, and avoid touching eyes, nose and mouth. Use alcohol-based hand sanitizers when soap and water are not available. Whenever possible, limit exposure to individuals who appear ill.

If ill with influenza-like symptoms (fever, cough, and/or sore throat), contact your health care provider. Stay home, avoid social contact with others and cover coughs and sneezes with a tissue, not your hands, to prevent the illness from spreading to others.

H1N1 is circulating in the local community as well as the nation and the world. Therefore, preventive actions are important anywhere you go, on campus or off.
