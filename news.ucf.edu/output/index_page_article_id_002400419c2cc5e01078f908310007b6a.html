UCF, Rollins Students Showcase Ideas for New Businesses

By UCF Staff Nov. 18, 2005

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Technological Entrepreneurship Center

    • 407-823-3683

  • Technological Entrepreneurship Center

  • Print this Page

Two University of Central Florida students and a Rollins College student won the top three prizes last week at the first Whats the Big Idea?! business concept competition between the two schools.

The UCF Technological Entrepreneurship Center and a similar organization at Rollins initiated the competition to stimulate the creation of breakthrough business ideas and creativity from intellectual and social connectedness. The variety of ideas generated by the 18 participants included a vending machine for unforeseen weather conditions and a Web site that would help parents get through their childrens tough teenage years. 

The Astrolet, a creation by Gavin Racz from Rollins, won the competition, which was held Nov. 11 at Rollins.  His winning idea is a bracelet that would display the wearers astrological sign via fiber optic lighting and determine a persons astrological compatibility with another person if they touch bracelets.  Second prize went to Vanja Grbic of UCF for algae inhibition paint, a coating applied to the interior of pools that would prevent the growth of algae for up to five years.  This paint could be used in recreational pools and large animal tanks.

Megan ONeil of UCF won third place for Green Roof Ventures, a company that would manufacture and distribute easy-to-use green roof materials and information. A green roof, essentially a garden on a roof, creates buildings that are environmentally friendly, reduces operating costs and attracts higher-paying tenants.

It was a great experience to pitch in front of real judges, ONeil said. The whole process was really helpful in learning all about what it takes to go from just an idea to a viable business concept, she said.

Competing students were given two minutes each to pitch their business ideas and one minute to answer questions from the six judges.  After the first round, eight contestants advanced to the second round, during which four winners were chosen and cash prizes were awarded. 

The event provided a chance for judges from UCF, Rollins and local businesses to evaluate new ideas based on their business potential and the pitches.  Students gained valuable feedback and were forced to think about the real-life challenges of creating a business from an idea.

Cameron Ford, coordinator for the Management Track of the Business Administration doctoral degree program and founder of the UCF Technological Entrepreneurship Center, said he was pleased with the success of the competition.

All in all, it was an excellent first event that I hope will become a tradition between the two schools, he said. Business planning events like the What's the Big Idea?! competition are an important part of our efforts to showcase interesting ideas and technologies to Central Florida's business community.

Grbic and ONeil qualified to participate in UCFs Business Plan Tournament, The Joust, on April 13 and 14. The Joust is an annual competition designed to challenge entrepreneurial students and faculty within the UCF community to create promising young companies.

Visit the UCF Technological Entrepreneurship Center at www.tec.ucf.edu for more information about the Joust.
