UCF, New York Professors to Debate Capital Punishment on March 22

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala March 15, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Pamala Griset, Criminal Justice

    • 407-823-5811

  • Robert Bohm, Criminal Justice

    • 407-823-5944

  • UCF Criminal Justice

  • Print this Page

UCF Criminal Justice Professor Robert Bohm will debate New York Law School professor Robert Blecker about capital punishment on Thursday, March 22.

Bohm teaches several courses in the University of Central Florida's Criminal Justice program and has written several books and academic papers making a case against capital punishment. Blecker will speak in favor of the death penalty. He also will show video footage of life on death row and in a maximum-security setting where life-without-parole sentences are served.

The presentation will begin at 7 p.m. in the Fairwinds Alumni Center on UCFs East Orlando campus.

The presentation is part of the first Department of Criminal Justice and Legal Studies Spring Speakers Series. An internationally known expert on capital punishment, Hugo Adam Bedau, spoke to a UCF audience in February. The seminars, which will include extended question-and-answer periods, are free and open to the public.

This is of paramount importance. It is the most serious responsibility we vest to government, to extinguish someones life, said Pamala Griset, interim chair of the department. There is no graver decision that the state makes. Its worthy of discussion.

Florida catapulted into the national limelight after the execution of Angel Nieves Diaz on Dec. 13. It took the convicted murderer 34 minutes to die after he was given a second dose of the toxic concoction. He was executed for the 1979 murder of a manager of a topless Miami bar.

The United States is among a few industrialized countries that still allow executions. As of last year, more than 3,300 prisoners, including 374 in Florida, were on death row. Florida has executed 64 people since 1979.
