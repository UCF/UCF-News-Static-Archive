Celebrating 40 Years

By Jeanne Hartig Sept. 1, 2003

Photo: News file photo

The Orlando campus in 1982.

Today, it is difficult to imagine Central Florida without the University of Central Florida. It is also hard to imagine that, in just 40 years, what was once a small, local school is now nationally and internationally recognized as a leading metropolitan research university.

If you lived in Central Florida in the late 1960s and 1970s, you had the chance to see a new university being built in an area of Orange County that was primarily scrub oak and leaf pine.

The need for a university in the central part of Florida was evident as early as the late 1950s. Forecasts predicted tremendous growth in the area's population, and the emerging space exploration activities at nearby Cape Canaveral helped fuel the demand for engineering and technical education in the region.

Founded in 1963 as Florida's seventh public state university, Florida Technological University (FTU) opened its doors to 1,948 students on October 7, 1968. Enrollment grew as fast as the university could expand its facilities and services. On December 6, 1978, in order to more accurately reflect its expanding range of academic programs, the Florida Legislature approved changing the name to the University of Central Florida. That same year, the UCF football team played -- and won -- its first game.

UCF really came of age in the early nineties. Dramatic enrollment growth and major construction on campus transformed the university into a major local institution-and the sixth largest employer in Central Florida. The school in a remote area of Orange County became one that seemed to be Under Construction Forever in one of the fastest growing parts of Central Florida. A university that started as a promise to Central Floridians emerged as a prominent leader in regional economic development, research and academic excellence.

Today, in addition to living in new residential housing on the main campus in East Orange County, students can take courses in dozens of subjects through UCF's growing online programs -- and never leave their home. Classes are also taught at 21 regional campuses in 11 counties throughout Central Florida-giving thousands of students in the area access to a UCF education.

During the past 40 years, however, one thing hasn't changed. Finding ways to make things better for our neighbors, families and colleagues has always been a priority of the UCF community.

Sometimes better means moving high-tech research out of the laboratories and into the marketplace. It means finding ways to help our children improve their reading and math skills through the College of Education. Sometimes better means strengthening and diversifying our local economy through the efforts of the Technology Incubator and the new Rosen School of Hospitality Management.

UCF's motto is Reach for the Stars -- words that embody the energy, pride, talent and determination of the tens of thousands of UCF students, faculty, staff and alumni who are putting imagination to work here in Central Florida. Significant in size, excellent in academics and prominent in accomplishments, the University of Central Florida is committed to improving the quality of life for its neighbors -- and the promise of a better future for all of us here in Central Florida.

Thank you for your continued support. It is an investment that is paying significant dividends back to communities we serve and the metropolitan region in which UCF is located.

During our anniversary year, from August 2003 through May 2004, we hope you will visit the University of Central Florida, take a campus tour, come for a music concert and cheer on the Golden Knights football team.
