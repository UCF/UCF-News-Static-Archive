Seventh-Graders Experience UCF Life at College Shadow Day

By Courtney Gilmartin April 15, 2008

Photo: Jacque Brund

More than 500 seventh-graders learned about life at UCF during the fourth-annual College Shadow Day on Friday.

Hosted by the University of Central Florida’s Burnett Honors College and Junior Achievement of Central Florida, the program helps local students from five underserved middle schools experience a day in the life of a college student.

Friday’s event attracted about 100 students each from Carver, Lockhart, Meadowbrook, Memorial and Stonewall Jackson middle schools in Orange County.

“The event is so important because it reminds these students that they have the potential and opportunity to continue in school, and they can have fun doing it,” said Burnett Honors College student Stephanie Rash, one of 20 student captains leading Friday’s tours and activities.

After receiving a welcome from UCF cheerleaders, the seventh-graders spent the day learning about the different programs that UCF offers through presentations and interactive sessions with college students, faculty and staff.

During an art presentation, students took turns on a pottery wheel to learn about ceramics. They also cranked artwork through a printmaker during an etching demonstration.

Another session taught students a modern jazz and pop dance routine. “It was really fun to see everybody learning together,” Rash said. “A lot of the students were really into it. Some were hesitant, but at the end there were more smiles.”

Students also practiced nursing on patient dummies, explored bone structure using animal skulls and learned about theater improvisation during activities with different UCF departments.

Tours of campus highlighted the Library, Student Union, Recreation and Wellness Center and residence halls.

Throughout the day, a heavy emphasis was placed on financial aid and good study habits.

Inez Ford, assistant director of the Office of Student Financial Assistance, spoke with students about the different types of financial aid and the importance of applying to college early.
At a presentation by Student Academic Resource Center, Director Jennifer Wright told students that the keys to pursuing higher education are to get organized and “never stop asking for help.”

Students from Stonewall Jackson Middle School said that some of their favorite things about UCF were the Reflecting Pond, the artwork found around campus and their lunch, which was donated by Firehouse Subs. Each student was also given a college resource bag that included information about UCF and two tickets to the spring football game on Saturday.

At the end of the day, all of the students gathered in the Pegasus Ballroom for uplifting farewell remarks from President John Hitt and Student Government Association President Brandie Hollinger.

“A college education is possible for anybody who works hard and has the capacity to succeed,” Hitt said.  “Continue to strive for success and excel so you can be prepared to make those decisions about where you want to go to college.”

Hollinger encouraged UCF spirit by saying that “there is nothing better than being a UCF Knight,” and she said the students were already part of the UCF family, calling them “honorary Knights.”

College Shadow Day was the culmination of a six-week Junior Achievement program called the Economics of Staying in School.  The program was taught at the underserved middle schools by student volunteers from UCF’s Burnett Honors College and College of Business Administration.
