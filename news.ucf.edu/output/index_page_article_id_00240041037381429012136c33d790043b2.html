Gov. Charlie Crist Gets Sneak Peek at Student-Made Video Games

By Christine Dellert July 15, 2009

Photo: Jacque Brund

Gov. Charlie Crist this week toured UCF's Center for Emerging Media and its motion-capture studio.

Florida Gov. Charlie Crist visited the University of Central Florida’s Center for Emerging Media Monday to check out the state-of-the-art facilities and several student-made video games.

Crist’s afternoon stop at UCF’s downtown campus was part of his daylong tour of Orlando’s digital media industry, which plays an important role in the state’s economic development.

“Orlando’s digital media community demonstrates the power of innovation in Florida’s economy,” Crist said. “Florida continues to stand out as a national leader in terms of our greatest resource -- our people.”

UCF’s Center for Emerging Media offers hands-on courses that incorporate art and digital media, while emphasizing industry best practices. The facility is home to UCF’s graduate video-gaming program, the Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy (FIEA), and Studio 500, which features one of the largest motion-capture studios on the East Coast and an adjacent soundstage with editing suites for commercial and student use.

While there, Crist saw a demonstration of the motion-capture studio, which virtually copies human movement for generating animations in video games, movies and more. Recent projects completed in the motion-capture studio include the “Hannah Montana: The Movie” video game and a Gillette national advertising campaign featuring Derek Jeter, Roger Federer and Tiger Woods. Students also have access to the facility for their projects.

FIEA Executive Director Ben Noel spoke with Crist about how the Center for Emerging Media will help fuel the new economy in Central Florida and the role UCF plays in the Creative Village concept touted by the city and Orlando Mayor Buddy Dyer.

Crist also watched several FIEA students showcase two group-made, professional video game projects: "Sultans of Scratch," a self-described “new hip-hop game experience” complete with an electronic dance pad and turntable, and “Drifters,” a multiplayer action-stealth game that has players taking the roles of ethereal demons that can possess human beings.

Crist also made stops at Electronic Arts Tiburon and the Employ Florida Banner Center for Digital Media, housed at Seminole Community College’s Heathrow Center for Economic Development.

Orlando’s film, digital media and entertainment production industry accounts for $845.5 million in total sales, according to the Metro Orlando Film and Entertainment Commission. More than 380 businesses in the area are involved with the production industry, and they employ about 3,400.

Since its inception, FIEA has graduated about 100 people, with more than 90 percent landing jobs in their industry of choice. Graduates earn an average starting salary of more than $52,000.

To learn more about the UCF Center for Emerging Media, go to http://cem.ucf.edu.
