Honors Students Win Debates Over Life's Dilemmas in Regional Ethics Bowl

By Courtney Gilmartin Nov. 25, 2008

Photo: Courtesy of The Burnett Honors College

Ethics Bowl team members Ruth Stakosha and Rose Crutcher (front) and Raafia Baboolal, John Townsend and Alex Kaplan (back).

Debating whether to give a prison inmate meatloaf might not seem like a competitive sport. But finding the ethically “right thing to do” has led to a big win for five University of Central Florida honors students.

The team from UCF’s Burnett Honors College debated moral dilemmas such as nutrition for prisoners at a regional Ethics Bowl earlier this month. The team became the first from UCF to rank in the top four in the Southeast Regional Ethics Bowl and advance to the national finals.

The UCF students beat teams from 16 other schools, including the University of Florida, Florida State University and the United States Military Academy at West Point. UCF will be joined at the national Intercollegiate Ethics Bowl, held in Cincinnati, by fellow regional winners Eckerd College, St. Petersburg College and the University of Alabama at Birmingham.

“The idea behind ethics bowl competition is to come to some kind of solution to moral problems,” said Nancy Stanlick, an associate professor and assistant chair in the Department of Philosophy. Students’ participation in the Ethics Bowl is a collaboration between The Burnett Honors College and the Department of Philosophy.

For the bowl, the teams prepared positions on 15 ethical issues. Students researched positions, studied ethical theories and considered scientific facts and published works while forming their arguments.

During the competition, debate went back and forth between two teams about scenarios involving ethics in the classroom, professional workplace, politics, society and personal relationships. Teams didn’t have to disagree with each other’s positions and could expand on the other team’s ideas instead of trying to prove them wrong.

The top teams from 10 regional competitions won a trophy and earned a place in the national ethics bowl. The 32 regional winners will compete March 5 in Cincinnati for a trophy and national title. The bowl is held in conjunction with the Association for Practical and Professional Ethics’ annual meeting.

“I don’t participate in any competitive sports, so this gives me an opportunity to compete on a playing field that I am familiar with and enjoy,” said Alex Kaplan, a UCF honors team member and a sophomore majoring in Philosophy. “By participating, we raise awareness of ethical thought.”

Kaplan became involved in the ethics bowl at the suggestion of a leader from one of his Burnett Honors College classes. Other members on the team are honors students Raafia Baboolal, a senior Psychology major; Rose Crutcher, a junior Microbiology and Molecular Biology major; Ruth Strakosha, a senior Microbiology and Molecular Biology major; and John Townsend, a Philosophy major.

To prepare for the regional competition, team members held small meetings to review the cases, conducted mock debates and organized their positions in an online Ethics Bowl Wiki.

“The prep work this time around really helped us excel in the competition,” Kaplan said.

The team was coached by Stanlick; Michael Strawser, an assistant professor of Philosophy; and Madi Dogariu, director of Student Services at The Burnett Honors College.

UCF has hosted two on-campus ethics bowls this year and is scheduled to host another in February. Teams from the Department of Philosophy, Student Undergraduate Research Council, The Burnett Honors College and individual classes compete in the bowls. UCF honors and philosophy students have been participated in the Southeast Regional Ethics Bowl every year since its creation in 2004.

“I think the competition sends a message to the UCF community that ethics are highly valued here,” Kaplan said. “Whichever field you are studying, ethics matter.”

The Intercollegiate Ethics Bowl began as a small intramural program at the Illinois Institute of Technology in 1993. Since then, it has expanded into a national competition that develops students’ intellectual abilities, deepens their ethical understanding and reinforces their sense of ethical commitment.

For more information on the Intercollegiate Ethics Bowl, visit http://ethics.iit.edu/eb/index.html.
