Research Park Location of Anti-Terrorism Exercise Thursday

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala Dec. 9, 2009

Full Photo

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Michelle Milliner, U.S. Army

    • 407-247-5639

  • Print this Page

The Central Florida Research Park, adjacent to the University of Central Florida will host a six-hour anti-terrorism training exercise on Thursday, Dec. 10.

The U.S. Army, Orange County Sheriff’s Office, UCF Police, Orange County Fire Rescue and several UCF groups will be working together during the exercise that involves a mock terrorist attack on the U.S. Army Sergeant First Class Smith Simulation & Training Technology Center VICE Simulation, Training and Technology Center located in the Orlando Tech Center cluster of buildings in Research Park.

Media are advised that the exercise will produce noise and law enforcement activity in and around the Orlando Tech Center, but it is only an exercise and does not constitute a real emergency.

The exercise is closed to media and the public and access to Orlando Tech Center will be limited for the duration of the exercise between 6 a.m. and noon. Student parking at the Orlando Tech center will be closed during the exercise as well.

RDECOM (U.S. Army Research, Development and Engineering Command) is leading the exercise, which will not only give local and federal agencies the opportunity to work together, but is also a requirement of military installations around the country to ensure readiness for any emergency.
