FIEA Students' 'Opera Slinger' Recognized at Independent Games Festival

By Todd Deery Jan. 9, 2007

Photo: Todd Deery

Ten FIEA students developed "Opera Slinger." They are: Back Row: (L-R) Jennie Gritton, Paul Dutka, Gabriel Montagne, the character Aria, Ed Tiger, Krystel Guiloff and Giovanni Acosta. Front Row: (L-R) Ryan Willis, James Moore, Matthew Laurence, the character Forte and Devon Sullivan.

"Opera Slinger," the wacky platforming and singing game developed by students at the Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy, was named one of the 10 best student games by organizers of the Independent Games Festival. The 2007 Student Showcase Finalists were picked from more than 100 entries submitted by students from around the world.

"Opera Slinger" was created by 10 FIEA students in about four months. The students are producers Matthew Laurence, Krystel Guiloff, Devon Sullivan and Ryan Willis; artists James Moore and Ed Tiger; and programmers Jennie Gritton, Paul Dutka, Giovanni Acosta and Gabriel Montagne.

Each of the winning teams in the competition run by the CMP Game Group will receive a travel stipend of $500 to attend the Game Developers Conference in March in San Francisco. The winning games will be shown during the conference. Also, for the first time, one of the 10 Student Showcase winners will receive a Best Student Game prize of $2,500 at the Independent Games Festival Awards on March 7.

The Independent Games Festival honor comes just three months after FIEA students produced three of the top nine games in MtvU's Game0RZ Showcase. FIEA students submitted "Bugaholic," "Up Your Kilt" and "Serpents on a Jet," all of which placed in the showcase.

To see all of the Independent Games Festival winners, go to  www.igf.com . To find out more about "Opera Slinger," go to www.operaslinger.com. For more information on FIEA, go to www.fiea.ucf.edu.
