UCF to Host Community Forum on Proposed On-Campus Football Stadium

By Tom Evelyn April 8, 2005

The University of Central Florida will host a community forum Monday, April 11, to allow residents to learn more about and comment on a proposed on-campus football stadium.

Members of UCFÂ’s Board of Trustees are scheduled to attend the forum, which will be held from 6 to 9 p.m. in the Pegasus Ballroom at the Student Union on the main campus. Residents may sign up at the meeting to speak for five minutes each.

UCF President John Hitt will welcome the audience and Athletics Director Steve Orsini and Bill Merck, vice president for Administration and Finance, will share the findings of a feasibility study.

UCF is exploring building a 45,000-seat stadium in the north end of campus in the athletics complex that includes the Arena and student housing that will eventually house 2,000 students.

The Board of Trustees is scheduled to meet at 2 p.m. Thursday, April 14, in the Student Union to vote on whether to build the stadium. All board meetings are public.

If approved, construction of the estimated $43 million stadium could begin this summer and be completed by fall 2006.

The board received the stadium feasibility study and heard public comments at its regular meeting March 31. UCF also has been meeting with area homeowners groups to discuss the possible stadium and will hold an open house from 6 to 8 p.m. April 13 at Lawton Chiles Middle School.

For more information on the proposed stadium, visit www.stadium.ucf.edu .

Meeting schedule

April 11, 6-9 p.m.: Community Forum; Pegasus Ballroom, Student Union.

April 13, 6-8 p.m.: Community Open House to allow residents to view renderings and ask questions at Lawton Chiles Middle School, 3225 Lockwood Blvd., Oviedo.

April 14, 2 p.m.: Board of Trustees to vote on proposed stadium; Student Union.
