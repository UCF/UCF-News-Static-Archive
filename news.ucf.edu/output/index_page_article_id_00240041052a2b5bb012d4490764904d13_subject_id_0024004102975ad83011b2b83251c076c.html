'Jousting' for Entrepreneurial Dreams

By Courtney Gilmartin April 14, 2011

The Joust will begin at 1:30 p.m. Friday, April 15, in the FAIRWINDS Alumni Center on the main campus.

Four teams of entrepreneurial dreamers are vying for $10,000 to launch their own businesses, which would help with everything from study skill materials to cheap, tasty meals.

The University of Central Florida student teams will try to wow judges at the eighth-annual Joust Business Tournament, which begins at 1:30 p.m. Friday, April 15, in the FAIRWINDS Alumni Center on the main campus.

During Friday's competition, student teams will present their business ideas to a panel of judges that will include Rick Walsh, chair of UCF's Board of Trustees and CEO of the Knob Hill Companies.

There will be no curve here. The judges are successful business leaders in their specific industry, and they know how tough it is to succeed.

Teams will be graded on their overall business plans; marketing, services and products; and financial feasibility.

This year's prizes total $20,000. The grand-prize winner will earn $10,000 and ayear as a client of the UCF Business Incubation Program.

The final four teams competing in the Joust are:

Better Ways to Study, LLC, which produces materials that create a more effective studying experience for college students who are visual learners.

Dave's Market, which is a restaurant created to honor the passions of David Schaefer, a UCF student who died in an automobile accident in October.

Free In A Box, which helps businesses that target students directly connect to their market by offering interested students free promotional materials.

Oh My Groceries, which provides online subscribers with a weekly grocery list that combines local grocers’ specials with current manufacturers' coupons to provide maximum savings.

The Joust is the signature competition of the UCF Center for Entrepreneurship and Innovation. An initiative of the College of Business Administration, the center serves as a hub for academic entrepreneurship across many of UCF's colleges and works with aspiring business owners in the community.

This year's judges include: Tom Bland, an alumnus, former faculty member, inductee into the College of Business Administration's Hall of Fame and president and chief executive officer of AquaFiber Technologies; Paul P. Gregg, a FinanceDepartment faculty member, Hall of Fame inductee and business consultant; Ron Pizzuti, chairman and chief executive officer of The Pizzuti Companies, real estate development firm; Mark Plaumann, an alumnus, Hall of Fame inductee and managing member of the real estate-oriented investment firm Greyhawke Capital Advisors LLC; Rick Walsh, chair of UCF’s Board of Trustees, alumnus and chairman and CEO of the Knob Hill Companies; and Marilyn Wilson, a partner with Kurt Salmon Associates .

This year's competition sponsor is Steven Felkowitz, chief executive officer of Atico International. Felkowitz is a 1979 alumnus of the College of BusinessAdministration. He was inducted into the college’s Hall of Fame in 2007.

Learn more about the Joust at www.cei.ucf.edu.
