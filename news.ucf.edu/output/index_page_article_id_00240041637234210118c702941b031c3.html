UCF Symposium on Presidential Race to Feature MTV Reporter

By Brandon Bielich March 19, 2008

Photo: Courtesy of MTV

MTV News' SuChin Pak will speak at the March 31 symposium on young voters and the presidential campaigns.

With the presidential campaigns in full swing, the University of Central Florida's Lou Frey Institute of Politics and Government will host a symposium titled “Road to the White House: Tough Choices,” on Monday, March 31, at the Student Union.
 
The two-part event examining the issues impacting young voters and the nation will feature an evening keynote address from MTV News correspondent SuChin Pak. Pak will discuss what concerns our next president should consider after his or her inauguration.

The symposium, held in the Pegasus Ballroom, is free and open to the public. The morning session will begin at 9:30 a.m., and Pak will speak at 7 p.m.

Pak joined MTV in 2001, and she has interviewed artists such as Britney Spears, P. Diddy, Jane’s Addiction and Gwen Stefani. She also has co-hosted the MTV Video Music and Movie Award shows and has reported from the red carpet at the Oscars and the Golden Globes.

In the political field, Pak reported for MTV’s Choose or Lose campaign in 2004. The series featured live election-night reports from Washington, D.C., analysis of youth voting patterns in swing states, coverage of protests and reports from the Republican and Democratic national conventions.

“We’re excited to have SuChin speak,” said Drew Lanier, associate director of the Lou Frey Institute of Politics and Government. “She taps into a very important group in American politics today: young voters. She will focus on the issues in the presidential election that are important to younger voters and bring a dynamic perspective to the keynote presentation.”

Prior to Pak’s speech, the symposium will provide a variety of views on which direction the country should head, regardless of which candidate is elected in November.

Events scheduled for the day session include:

-- Views from Both Sides of the Aisles: What Should the President Do from Here? A discussion including UCF Political Science professor Aubrey Jewett, and Lew Oliver, executive director of the Orange County Republican Party; from 9:35 a.m. to 10:30 a.m.

-- National Security and the War on Terror: a talk featuring Ulric Haynes Jr. a former ambassador to Algeria, and Stephen Sloan a UCF terrorism expert; from 10:30 a.m. to 11:20 a.m.

-- Global Issues: What Challenges Face Us as a Nation? with Scot Schraufnagel, from the UCF Department of Political Science, and Paolo Spadoni, an expert on Cuban politics with the Rollins College Department of Political Science; from 11:30 a.m. to 12:20 p.m.

-- Historical Perspectives on Immigration: moderated by Terri Fine, of UCF’s Department of Political Science; from 12:30 p.m. to 1:30 p.m. The panel includes Carlos M. Colombo, a UCF alumnus and immigration attorney.

The Lou Frey Institute of Politics and Government is a nonpartisan organization housed in the Department of Political Science at the University of Central Florida. The institute promotes civic education for college and high school students, members of the Central Florida community and the general public. Institute programs encourage informed discussion of issues from diverse viewpoints, stimulate participation in the political process and support research on politics and policy.

For more information, go to www.loufrey.org.
