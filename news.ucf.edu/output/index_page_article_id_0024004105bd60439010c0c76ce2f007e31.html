UCF President John Hitt Returns Home Following Treatment for Heart Attack

By Tom Evelyn June 29, 2006

Photo: Jerry Klein

UCF President John Hitt speaks to reporters Thursday morning before checking out of Florida Hospital Orlando, where he was treated for a heart attack.

Saying he was darn lucky, University of Central Florida President John Hitt credited the quick care he received, being in good shape and having a cell phone on him with saving his life during his heart attack.

Hitt, 65, checked out of Florida Hospital Orlando Thursday morning. He had been at the hospital since Saturday night, when he had a heart attack while riding his bicycle in the Central Florida Research Park adjacent to the East Orlando campus.

First, I would just like to thank everyone for the thoughts and prayers that have been extended to me and (my wife) Martha over these last days, Hitt said during a news conference with Drs. Hani Seifein and Kerry Schwartz, who treated him at the hospital. Particularly, I would like to thank Dr. Seifein and Dr. Schwartz and all of the great nursing staff here at Florida Hospital for their wonderful care.

Hitt was riding his bike before dark Saturday when he began to have chest pains. After taking a short break and realizing he wasnt feeling any better, he called his wife and then 911 using his cell phone.

Emergency workers from the campus station called for a helicopter to transport Hitt to the hospital, where doctors cleared a blocked artery and inserted three stents.

Those of you who run, those of you who bicycle, those of you who do anything thats strenuous away from home, be sure you have a cell phone at all times, he said.

Hitt has lost more than 100 pounds over the past several years since beginning a regular exercise program and following a healthy diet. He encouraged others to monitor their health.

What fitness does is improve your odds, Hitt said. Youre less likely to have a heart attack if youre fit. And if you have one, youre more likely to survive.

Hitt, who has been president of UCF since 1992, could return to work full time in five to six weeks, depending on how his recovery continues. Provost and Vice President for Academic Affairs Terry Hickey will serve as acting president until then.

The outpouring of support and concern from the community over the past several days is testament to how highly regarded John Hitt is on this campus and in Central Florida, Hickey said. We look forward to his continued recovery and having him back on campus.

Since becoming UCFs fourth president in 1992, Hitt has helped the university achieve many significant milestones, most recently securing approval to establish a College of Medicine for which he helped raise more than $50 million in donations.

During his tenure, UCFs enrollment has more than doubled to 45,000 this past fall. Over the same period, he has conferred more than 103,000 degrees, about two-thirds of the universitys total.

Among Hitts other accomplishments in recent years are the openings of the Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy in 2005 in downtown Orlando and the Rosen College of Hospitality Management campus in 2004 near International Drive. The university also plans to open an on-campus football stadium in time for the 2007 season.

Fighting heart disease has been an important cause for Hitt throughout his presidency. Just a few days before his heart attack, he spoke at the UCF kick-off breakfast for the American Heart Associations Heart Walk. He encouraged students, faculty and staff to help the university raise $17,000 before the Sept. 30 event and pledged a donation toward that goal.

We appreciate all of Dr. Hitts support of the American Heart Association, said Allisson Yu, executive director of the associations Florida/Puerto Rico Affiliate based in Orlando. Hes been a wonderful advocate for us, and our thoughts are with him. We wish him a speedy recovery.
