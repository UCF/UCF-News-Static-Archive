Football Game Day Update: Limiting the Impact on Campus' Neighbors

By Christine Dellert Sept. 10, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Chad Binette, News and Information

    • 407-823-6312

  • UCF Game Day Web site

  • Print this Page

"No parking" signs will be posted and extra law-enforcement officers will be on duty Saturday to help reduce the impact that the University of Central Florida's first on-campus football game will have on the neighboring communities.

Providing free, on-campus parking on campus for all fans is one way to help keep cars off the streets of nearby subdivisions, university officials say.

"We've been trying to do everything we can to minimize the impact of having such a huge event in the neighborhood," said Cynthia Sucher, UCF's associate vice president for Community Relations.

UCF's first home game at Bright House Networks Stadium will be at 3:30 p.m. Sept. 15 against the Texas Longhorns.

There are no plans to shut down roads around UCF. However, only those who have reserved parking permits for the campus' Gold Zone, closest to Bright House Networks Stadium, will be allowed to enter campus via McCulloch Road/Orion Boulevard from four hours prior to kickoff until two hours after the game ends. That entrance will be open to anyone riding a bicycle or walking onto campus.

"By limiting it to only those people, we have the potential of 6,000 cars instead of 12,000 or more [on that road]," Sucher said.

A game-day management firm, with experience working Major League Baseball, National Football League and NASCAR special events, will help direct parking and traffic.

People will be allowed on campus beginning at 7 a.m. on game days to set up picnic areas, tents and other tailgating gear.

Orange and Seminole counties will install "no parking" signs along McCulloch Road to help ease traffic congestion and reduce the potential for disruption in neighboring communities.

Also, more than 180 officers from UCF Police, Orange and Seminole County sheriffÂ’s offices and the Florida Highway Patrol will be working in and around the campus. Police will also drive through UCF's closest neighborhoods, including University Estates and Carillon, to encourage fans not to block driveways or park on private property. Orange and Seminole counties prohibit open containers of alcohol on public streets and parking lots.

Many of the game day procedures were put in place after UCF staff heard suggestions from neighbors during several preseason community briefings that covered traffic, parking and security for home football games, Sucher said.

About 40 residents met with UCF staff Thursday night for an on-campus briefing to ease any last-minute concerns and review game day plans.

"For us the real focus now is game one, game two, so we can fine-tune the process and procedures," said Richard Vega, president of the University Estates Homeowners Association. "We've done a great job working together in trying to isolate and identify issues that needed to be addressed. But at this juncture, we're anxious to get the season underway so that we can determine conclusively where the weak spot, if any, might lie."

UCF plans to hold another community meeting after the Sept. 29 home game against Louisiana-Lafayette to address any concerns.

"Certainly this is going to be a learning process for us," Sucher said. "We're going above and beyond to work with the neighbors."

More information on game day plans is available at www.ucfgameday.com. UCF has also set up a special telephone help desk that will be active on home football game days at 407-882-FANS or 407-882-3267.
