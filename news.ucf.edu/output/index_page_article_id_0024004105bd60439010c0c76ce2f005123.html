Homecoming to Feature Spirit Splash on Friday, Parade and Game on Saturday

By Kristi Bartlett Updated Oct. 30, 2006

Photo: Courtesy of UCF Homecoming Executive Board

Chalk in the Walk pairs UCF organizations with children from the community in a competition to illustrate their teams’ interpretations of the homecoming theme, Heroes Unleashed.

UCF combined two fun, philanthropic events this year as a part of the Homecoming celebration.

Chalk on the Walk, which was held Sunday, Oct. 28, paired UCF organizations with children from the community in a competition to illustrate their teams interpretations of the homecoming theme, Heroes Unleashed. It coincided with the annual Trick or Treat on Greek Street, which provided a fun and safe trick-or-treating environment for local kids and their families.

Comedian Bob Saget from Full House and The Aristocrats will perform at 8 p.m. Thursday, Nov. 2, at the UCF Arena. The annual Homecoming parade will march through downtown Orlando at 11 a.m. Saturday, Nov. 4, leading up to the Homecoming football game at 4 p.m. against East Carolina at the Citrus Bowl.

Student Faraaz Merchant started Chalk on the Walk last year after seeing a street painting festival in West Palm Beach.

I thought a similar event at UCF would be a great idea and offer a new and unique tradition, Merchant said.

Chalk on the Walk grew this year to include more campus organizations and partnerships. Several Boys and Girls Clubs of Central Florida chapters were invited, as well as the UCF Creative School for Children; Student Development and Enrollment Services community service partner, Bonneville Elementary School; and children of the Alumni Association.

About 20 UCF teams sought children from the community to help them draw the sidewalk art near the Visual Arts Building. The children arrived one hour after the start of the event, and teams that encouraged children to help will receive extra points. The art was judged based on appearance, adherence to theme, creativity and child participation.

Volunteer UCF and the Homecoming Executive Board were instrumental in starting the program.

Trick or Treat on Greek Street paired fraternities and sororities with student organizations to plan activities for the children within the community. Trick or Treat on Greek Street benefitted the Boys and Girls Clubs of Central Florida and Big Brothers Big Sisters through fund-raising activities, t-shirt sales and business sponsors.

For more information and the complete schedule of homecoming events, visit http://hc.sdes.ucf.edu and www.ucfalumni.com/Main/Default.asp?CategoryID=2&SubCategoryID=1 .
