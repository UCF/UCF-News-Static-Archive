Workshop to Focus on Small Business Innovation Grants

By UCF Staff Feb. 21, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Christa Santos

    • 407-230-7018

  • UCF Venture Lab

  • Print this Page

A Small Business Innovation Research Phase II Grant Preparation Workshop, presented by the University of Central FloridaÂ’s Venture Lab, will be held from 8 a.m. until 5 p.m. Wednesday, Feb. 28. The workshop will be in Room 208 of the Partnership II building in the Central Florida Research Park.

Each year, more than $2 billion in grants and contracts are awarded to small businesses through U.S. government Small Business Innovation Research and Science (SBIR) and Technology Transfer Research (STTR) programs. Attendees can learn how research faculty, students and technology entrepreneurs can access these programs by creating or partnering with small technology firms to develop innovative solutions to the toughest science and technology problems.

Gail and Jim Greenwood, experts in proposal writing for both grant programs, will provide an in-depth discussion of a simple but highly effective four-step process for developing a competitive proposal. The program will include case studies of several small companies that have used a variety of commercialization models to take their innovations to market.

The workshop is ideal for researchers and small businesses preparing Phase II SBIR/STTR grant proposals, service providers who help them and others who want a solid understanding of the programs. The session will cover the requirements for the two grants, best practices in writing proposals and other related topics.

The workshop is free for UCF faculty and students and $25 for others. To register, send an e-mail with your name, department/company and e-mail address to venturelab@mail.ucf.edu. Only 40 registrants will be accepted.Â 

Preference will be given to UCF faculty members. Call 407-823-1442 with questions regarding the workshop. Payment can be made by check to the UCF Research Foundation on the day of the workshop.

The UCF Venture Lab was formed in 2004 as a joint initiative involving the UCF Office of Research, UCF's College of Business Administration and Orange County to provide the mentorship needed for technology entrepreneurs to transform innovative ideas and intellectual property into business plans with high growth potential. For more information, visit www.venturelab.ucf.edu.
