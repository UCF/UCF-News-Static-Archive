Rosen College Class to Experience French Culture and Cuisine

By Gert Garman March 8, 2006

Photo: Courtesy of Rosen College of Hospitality Management

Rosen College Associate Professor Kevin Murphy, fourth from left, will take 22 of his students to France during spring break to learn more about French culture, food and wine.

Full Photo

    • 407-903-8189

  • Rosen College of Hospitality Management

  • Print this Page

Twenty-two Rosen College of Hospitality Management students and Professor Kevin Murphy will experience French cuisine, food and wine next week in Paris and three other cities.

Murphy is teaching a course called International Gastronomy in which students learn about a destination and how the cuisine there defines it. The class is focused on France this semester. Murphy teaches his students to explore the customs and cultural differences of a region of the world and to examine what attractions define a major tourism destination.

While visiting Paris for five days, Lyon for four days and also Beaujolais and Rhone, the students will be treated to a cultural, food or wine aspect of France each day. They will enjoy a winery tour into the countryside and a gourmet banquet at Institut Paul Bocuse.  The group will return to Florida on March 19.

This is a great opportunity because we are increasingly a global marketplace, Murphy said. Hospitality and travel/tourism are the worlds largest industries, and these are our future leaders, so they need this global experience.

Murphy prepared his students for the trip with a tasting featuring wines from the areas in France that theyll be visiting. The trip coincides with UCFs spring break.

While traveling, the students will write a daily blog that will be posted at www.hospitality.ucf.edu. Look for the link on the left side of the home page.

Murphy worked more than 20 years in the foodservice and hospitality industry before coming to UCF in fall 2003. He has owned or managed hotels, restaurants and catering services and is a certified executive chef with the American Culinary Federation.

The Rosen College offers programs in Lodging Management, Restaurant Management, Theme Parks and Attractions Management, Vacation Ownership Management, Finance and Accounting Management and Event Management.
