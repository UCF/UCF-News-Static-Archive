Abstracts are 'In' at FHE

By Susan Loden December 29, 2003

Photo: FHE Photos

New York abstractionist John O'Connor created his first limited edition print as artist-in-residence at the Flying Horse Editions fine arts research press at the University of Central Florida in Orlando.

O'Connor collaborated with Flying Horse Editions Director Ryan Burkhart from Jan. 4 to 16 to make an abstract print to be offered as a gift to donors to the nonprofit press. O'Connor also lectured on his art and life as an artist in the UCF Art Gallery, in the Visual Arts Building Jan. 15.

Meanwhile, UCF art professor and Flying Horse founder Robert Reedy, through a partnership with the UCF Alumni Association, has been invited to create a limited edition FHE print in celebration of the university's 40th Anniversary.

The Reedy print, also an abstract, will be available in early 2004 to donors and honorees of the Alumni Association. OÂ’Connor usually works in graphite and colored pencil on paper, with numbers and written text a part of or accompanying many works, connecting internal thought to the external environment.

O'Connor's "Untitled Z," a series of ovals surrounded by numbers and diagrams, was chosen for the 2000 Peddie School's Mariboe Gallery national juried group exhibit, with five other artists.

O'Connor will team again with Burkhart, an internationally exhibited and collected abstract painter as well as a printmaker, in a two-artist exhibit at the Anita S. Wooten Gallery at Valencia Community College from late June through August.

For more than 10 years, Flying Horse has offered revered artists and writers, including Bill Fick, Timothy Berry, Lynwood Kreneck, David Humphrey and William T. Wiley, the opportunity to work with FHE master printers, typesetters and book binders to create unique, prized graphics, while experimenting in the field and inspiring and interacting with UCF art and English students.

Burkhart has scheduled the following artists to create Flying Horse books and or prints in 2004-05: Steven Sorman, Tony Fitzpatrick, Karen Kunc, Tom Burckhardt, Thomas Nozkowski and Melissa Harshman. Private collectors and institutions fund FHE projects through donations. In return, patrons are given museum-quality prints and artists' books created by visiting artists.

Donors are also invited to meet visiting artists at soirees in the FHE studio, gallery and reading room in at 12479 Research Parkway, Central Florida Research Park. Works by visiting artists and partner-artists are always on exhibit at no charge.
