UCF Team Creates Virtual Reality of Launch Facility and Cape Canaveral Lighthouse

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala July 19, 2007

Photo: Courtesy of Lori Walters

The Cape Canaveral Lighthouse and the keeper's cottage complex are featured on the virtual site. Fire destroyed the cottages. This picture of the lighthouse dates to the 1960s.

University of Central Florida educators and students are giving space and military enthusiasts a virtual look at Cape Canaverals first missile launch site, Launch Complex 3, just in time for the 57th anniversary of Bumper 7 and 8.

Tuesday, July 24, marks the 57th anniversary of the launch of Bumper 8, one of the two modified German V-2 rockets that were tested at Launch Complex 3. These early efforts eventually led to long-range ballistic missile testing, and the launch facilities expanded to support satellite and manned launches.

The UCF team recreated Launch Complex 3 on the Web. The site permits viewers to visit the launch facility and tap into historical facts and pop culture pertaining to 1950  a time of McCarthyism, the Korean War, China turning to communism and the dawn of television. Theres even an icon that depicts the only television station in Florida at the time  WTVJ in South Florida.

Visitors to the site use a fictitious photographer and his darkroom as a portal into the launch complex and the lighthouse. The public can access the project by going to http://virtualheritage.ist.ucf.edu and clicking on Shadows of Canaveral.

Its a way to preserve and bring to life a very important part of our national and local history, said Lori C. Walters, a research associate at UCFs Institute for Simulation and Training. Its harnessing 21st century technology to present history to an increasingly digitally savvy population.

Walters landed a $25,000 grant from the Florida Bureau of Historic Preservation to create virtual environments in an effort to preserve space-related history. In 2006 she and a team of students developed the first portion of the Web site, which created an interactive site depicting John Glenns historic 1962 launch into space. The new addition adds another phase to the ongoing Shadows of Canaveral project.

Phase II of Shadows of Canaveral incorporates pre-missile Cape history with a recreation of the Cape Canaveral Lighthouse and the lighthouse keepers cottage complex. The keepers complex was consumed by fire in the mid 1960s. The site also explains the connection between what is known today as Patrick Air Force Base to the Cape. In early 1950, the base was known as the Long Range Proving Ground and was in essence the administration center for all military launches at the Cape.

What you have to remember is that launch pads and the lighthouse are on a military installation, so access to the public is limited, Walters said. Thats why this project is important. It gives the public the opportunity to see what once was.

Students from the Digital Media program at UCF worked on all aspects of the project, from the research necessary to create an accurate virtual world to the technical design. They included art graduate student Alice Kramer, of East Orlando, and Digital Media undergraduates Travis Janzen, of Apopka, Charles Padera, of Lake Mary, and Tom Smith.
