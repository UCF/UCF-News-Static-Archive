Tale of Love and Murder Opens Valentine's Day at UCF Conservatory Theatre

By UCF Staff Feb. 6, 2008

Photo: Joseph Rusnock

Brittney Rentschler (left) plays the character Helen in UCF's production of "Machinal." Ryan Garcia stars as Helen's lover.

In the late 1920s, Ruth Snyder murdered her husband, Albert, with help from her lover. The possible motives behind Snyder’s impulsive and poorly concealed crime inspired Sophie Treadwell’s modern-age tragedy, “Machinal,” which opens Feb. 14 at the UCF Conservatory Theatre’s Black Box.

Snyder’s sensational murder trial resulted in her being the first woman executed at Sing Sing Prison. Her lover, Judd Grey, faced the same fate. Critics have said that Treadwell’s “Machinal” suggests strong sympathy for Snyder. Treadwell saw Snyder as a woman trapped in a loveless marriage and the victim of a society that, without remorse, had wiped itself clean of another imperfection in a perfectly mechanized world.

“Machinal” runs from Thursday, Feb. 14, to Sunday, Feb. 17, and from Thursday, Feb. 21, to Sunday, Feb. 24. Performances will be in the Black Box theatre in the Theatre building on the main UCF campus.  

Thursday through Saturday shows begin at 8 p.m., and Sunday matinees start at 2 p.m. Ticket prices are $15 for adults, $13 for seniors over 55 and $10 for students with ID. “Machinal” contains adult themes and is not suitable for children.

One of the production’s main staging challenges is combining the subjective views of the main character with the realism required of the play.

“The playwright’s attention is not on the actual events but on the mindscape of the central female character,” said Julia Listengarten, the play’s director and a UCF associate professor of Theatre. “Like a beautiful flower mercilessly caught in a meat grinder, Helen (as she is called in the play) cannot endure society’s unyielding pressure to submit.”

Reservations are recommended and available through the UCF Conservatory Theatre Box Office by calling 407-823-1500.  UCF offers accommodations to make the theater more accessible to patrons with disabilities -- call the box office ahead for assistance. Group rates are also available.
