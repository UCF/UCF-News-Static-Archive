Students Organizing Candlelight Vigils; UCF to Establish Task Force to Review Security Policies

By UCF Staff April 17, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Media: Tom Evelyn

    • 407-823-5988

  • UCF Counseling Center

    • 407-823-2811

  • UCF Counseling Center

  • UCF Police

  • UCF President John Hitt Response

  • Print this Page

University of Central Florida students are organizing two candlelight vigils to remember those who lost their lives Monday at Virginia Tech, and UCF President John Hitt has announced the creation of a task force to review the universitys security procedures and emergency response plans.

The first vigil will be at 9 p.m. tonight, April 17, at the Reflecting Pond between the Library and Millican Hall on the main UCF campus. The second vigil will be at 10 p.m. tonight by the fountain at the Rosen College of Hospitality Management, 9907 Universal Blvd., Orlando. The public is invited to attend both vigils.

The vigils are designed to be events at which people can offer moral support for Virginia Tech students and gather and support each other so they are not alone in their grief. No speeches are planned. UCF students are organizing the vigil on the main campus primarily through the Facebook Web site and with the support of the Student Government Association.

Hitt extended sympathies to the Virginia Tech community in an e-mail that was sent Tuesday to UCF students, faculty and staff.

Our hearts go out to the students, staff, faculty, and alumni of Virginia Tech and their families following yesterday's tragedy, Hitt wrote. As a campus community, we share in the shock and dismay over this sad loss of so many lives.

Hitt added that he will establish a task force to review UCFs security procedures and emergency response plans to ensure that UCFs campuses are as safe as possible. I want you to know that security will always be a primary concern at UCF.

UCF notifies its students, staff and faculty of emergency situations through campus-wide e-mails and messages posted on the UCF Web site. Emergency messages also are distributed through campus radio and television and Central Florida media. The university also is developing a reverse-911 system that will relay emergency information to phones on campus and exploring the use of sirens and other communication methods.

Student Government Association President Mark White said in a note to students Monday that the tragedy will forever be remembered as one of the saddest days in modern history.

More than 30 of our fellow students, young people and future leaders were taken from us without justification or right, White said. These students will never have the opportunity to use the education that they had worked so hard to obtain, to better themselves, their families or society. As a higher education community, we must come together not to mourn death, but to celebrate life at this tragic time.

Students who want to speak with a counselor about the tragedy can contact the Counseling Center at 407-823-2811.
