FIEA Defeats Ringling in First-Ever 'Virtual' Homecoming Football Game

By Todd Deery Updated Oct. 10, 2006

Photo: Joe Tanksley

Pete Rose (left) and Greg Fields led UCF's Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy to a 75-32 win over the Ringling School of Art and Design in a 'virtual' homecoming football game on the Xbox 360.

Students from the University of Central Floridas Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy defeated the Ringling School of Art and Design 75-32 Saturday in what may be the first-ever virtual homecoming football game.

The private Sarasota college has no sports programs, so students from the two schools squared off in a virtual homecoming game by playing Electronic Arts NCAA Football 2007 on the Xbox 360. The Oct. 7 game in Sarasota included many of the traditional elements of a homecoming football game, including pregame and halftime shows, giveaways, stadium-style refreshments, cheerleaders and the Ringling mascot Armadillo.

The football game was projected on large screens in Ringlings new Student Center exhibition hall, which seats 250 people. Using the UCF football team, Pete Rose and Greg Fields played for FIEA for most of the game. The Ringling students played as Rutgers because the Scarlet Knights have an 'R' on their helmets.

The game, part of the Ringling Schools homecoming weekend and yearlong 75th anniversary celebration, highlighted the strong relationship between both schools as well as their ties with Electronic Arts. Nearly two dozen Ringling graduates are employed at the game maker, and many FIEA students are interning at EAs Tiburon studios in Orlando. In addition, three of FIEAs current students got their undergraduate degrees at Ringling.
