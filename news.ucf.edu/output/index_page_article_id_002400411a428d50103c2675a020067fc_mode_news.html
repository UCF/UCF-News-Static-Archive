FIEA Hires New Faculty with Strong Industry Experience

By Todd Deery July 26, 2005

Research Associate Ron Weaver, left, has worked at the n-Space game studio and on numerous R&D projects within Walt Disney World. He also has taught Interactive Entertainment and Game Engines courses at UCF.

The Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy at the University of Central Florida on Wednesday announced the hiring of four new faculty members with substantial industry credentials. Tom Carbone, technical director; Ron Weaver, research associate; Rick Hall, production director; and Brian Salisbury, art director, will join the FIEA faculty before the inaugural class starts on Aug. 22.

 

We feel great about the group of faculty weve assembled here, said executive director Ben Noel. Weve got an excellent mix of UCF researchers and game industry professionals who bring a wealth of real-world experience to the curriculum.

 

Located in downtown Orlando, FIEAs masters degree and certificate programs in interactive entertainment offer intensive, project-based courses set in a building that mimics the production environments of successful gaming studios.

 

The accredited graduate program was started from a unique collaboration between UCF, the state of Florida and Electronic Arts. FIEA got its start in May 2004, when the Florida Legislature provided $4.2 million in start-up funding.

 

Before joining FIEA, Tom Carbone was one of the lead programmers on Madden NFL 2005 and 2006, produced by EA at its Orlando-based Tiburon studio. Prior to joining EA in 2003, he worked for Microsoft Game Studios, Kodiak Interactive and Sculptured Software Studios. His previous game credits include Amped 2 (XBOX), Inside Pitch Baseball (XBOX), WCW Backstage Assault (N64/PS), WCW Mayhem (N64/PS), Breakaway Hockey (N64/PS), Space Jam (PS/Saturn/PC), MonsterTruck (Genesis) and Tecmo Super Baseball (Genesis).

 

Ron Weavers experience with interactive entertainment includes the MMO Toontown Online from the VR Studio (of Walt Disney Imagineering, R&D), n-Space game studio, numerous R&D projects within Walt Disney World and teaching the Interactive Entertainment and Game Engines courses at UCF.

 

Rick Hall is a 15-year veteran of the gaming industry with experience as a producer, studio head, programmer and game designer. He started his career at Paragon Software, a studio that eventually became the core development group for Take 2s initial 1994 start-up. After Take 2, he joined Electronic Arts, where he served as live and executive producer of the Ultima Online franchises in Austin, Texas. Most recently, he worked as senior producer on the Madden Football handheld products at EA Tiburon.

 

Brian Salisbury has worked as a character artist for games and films for more than 12 years. After graduating from Arizona State University, he worked in color separation, 3D, animation and then as a senior digital sculptor at Viewpoint Datalabs in Orem, Utah. While there, he created 3D content for film, games, broadcasts and engineering. Notable projects included creating character models for big-budget films Antz, Face Off, Godzilla and Space Jam. In 1998, he joined Kodiak Interactive and worked as a lead modeler for WCW Mayhem (PS/N64), WCW Backstage Assault (PS/N64) and Monsters, Inc. (PS2). In 2001 he joined Microsoft Game Studios as a character lead on several projects and an art lead for Top Spin. Recent game titles include Links 2004 (XBOX), Top Spin (XBOX) and Amped 3 (XBOX360).

 

For more information about FIEA, go to www.fiea.ucf.edu.
