UCF Trustees to Consider Tuition Waivers, New Rates at Thursday Meeting

By Tom Evelyn May 24, 2006

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Linda Gray

    • 407-823-2947

  • Tom Evelyn

    • 407-823-5988

  • UCF Board of Trustees

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Florida Board of Trustees will consider tuition support for graduate students and new rates for all students, among other items, at its regular meeting Thursday, May 25.

In an effort to be more competitive in attracting high-quality graduate students and research funding, the university is proposing tuition waivers for graduate assistants and fellows. Tuition support for such students is common at universities across the country and has been implemented at several in Florida.

UCFs plan would cover all of the out-of-state portion of tuition and fees for non-resident graduate assistants and fellows. The university would also waive in-state tuition for a number of doctoral graduate assistants and fellows and provide partial waivers for a number of masters graduate assistants.

The new policy would be phased in over three years beginning this summer and would eventually cover all in-state tuition and out-of-state tuition and fees for full-time graduate assistants and fellows. Graduate tuition is $201.54 a credit hour; out-of-state graduate students pay an additional $640.44 a credit hour.

In other action, the trustees will consider a 3 percent increase for tuition and financial aid fees for all students, as recommended by the state Legislature. If approved, tuition for a resident undergraduate student would increase from $71.57 a credit hour to $73.71 beginning this fall.

The board also will consider for approval:

-- An equity accountability plan and budgetary incentive program that encourages diversity in hiring.

-- A proposed 2006-07 university operating budget of $899.3 million.

-- A proposed 2007-08 legislative budget request, which includes nearly $9.4 million in planning funds for the UCF medical college.

-- Tenure recommendations for more than two dozen faculty members.

Committee meetings begin at 8:30 a.m. in the Cape Florida Ballroom at the Student Union. The full board meeting follows at 1 p.m.

Visit http://bot.ucf.edu for an agenda and more information.
