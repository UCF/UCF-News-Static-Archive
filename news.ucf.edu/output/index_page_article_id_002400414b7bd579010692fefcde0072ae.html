Diversity Week to Celebrate Diversity Through a Generational Lens

By Chad Binette Oct. 12, 2005

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Valarie Greene King

    • 407-823-6479

  • Chad Binette

    • 407-823-6312

  • President Hitt's proclamation for Diversity Week

  • Office of Diversity Initiatives

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Floridas 13th annual Diversity Week will feature nearly 50 discussion sessions, movies and other presentations.

The theme for Diversity Week, which begins Monday, Oct. 17, is Diversity Through a Generational Lens. Events include a discussion on education and poverty, a forum on The Diverse World of Harry Potter and a session on helping children cope with disasters. Most events are free and open to the public.

A kick-off breakfast Monday will feature keynote speaker Linda Landman-Gonzalez, director of Diversity and Community Affairs for Darden Restaurants. The breakfast is sold out.

Our annual celebration of Diversity Week adds richness and texture to the growing traditions of our young university, said Valarie Greene King, director of the Office of Diversity Initiatives. This years theme is a very poignant one since we now have four generations in the workplace. Our Diversity Week activities will provide opportunities to explore various histories, cultures, issues and interests as a means of promoting understanding, communication and interaction across a myriad of human differences.

While the Office of Diversity Initiatives coordinates the full slate of Diversity Week events, many UCF departments helped by organizing presentations for their subject areas.

The College of Education is sponsoring about a dozen discussions, including one on poverty and education at 11 a.m. Monday in Room 117 of the Teaching Academy and one on how to address lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender issues in classrooms at 6 p.m. Monday in Room 322 of the Teaching Academy. A session on helping children cope with natural and manmade disasters will be at 10:30 a.m. Thursday in Room 130 of the Teaching Academy.

John Elmore, a New York state criminal defense attorney, will lead a panel discussion on criminal justice issues at 11:30 a.m. Wednesday, Oct. 19, in the Pegasus Ballroom of the Student Union. The Department of Criminal Justice and Legal Studies is sponsoring that presentation.

The Diverse World of Harry Potter, sponsored by Housing and Residence Life, will be at noon Tuesday in Garden Key Room 221 of the Student Union.

The kickoff breakfast provides an opportunity for the UCF community to celebrate the importance of diversity. Landman-Gonzalez, the keynote speaker, studied and lived in Mexico City for 18 years before moving to Central Florida in 1983. She now manages Darden Restaurants Diversity Community Alliance Project and serves on the corporate advisory boards of the Greater Dallas Asian Chamber of Commerce, the Cosmopolitan Chamber of Commerce of Illinois and the Greater Los Angeles African-American Chamber of Commerce.

Go to www.diversity.ucf.edu and click on the Diversity Week link at the bottom of the page to view a full schedule of Diversity Week events.
