UCF's Reputation, Student Quality Have Soared During Retiring VP's Tenure

By UCF Staff March 24, 2009

Photo: Jacque Brund

UCF's Board of Trustees and staff applauded Tom Huddleston for his dedication and service to the university.

Tom Huddleston’s legacy at UCF includes remarkable increases in the university’s academic reputation, the growth and quality of students and the successful “UCF Stands for Opportunity” brand. Nationally, he is known as a pioneer of enrollment management and as a leader in institutional identity and integrated communications and marketing.

Huddleston announced last week that he will retire at the end of April as vice president for Marketing, Communications and Admissions, a division that he helped to develop and launch in 2004 and that is innovative among higher education institutions for its scope. MCA addresses university-wide issues of brand identity, news and communications, integrated marketing, television services and undergraduate enrollment.

In 1993, Huddleston arrived at UCF as vice provost for Enrollment and Academic Services. Since then, UCF’s enrollment has more than doubled, its academic reputation has soared and the quality of students has increased dramatically. For example, UCF’s fall 2008 freshman class included 62 National Merit Scholars, a total that ties UCF for 34th in the nation. The same class also boasts an average SAT score of 1219, an increase from 1,097 during the fall 1993 term.

Huddleston laid the foundation that has seen retention rates increase as UCF’s enrollment has risen from 23,000 to more than 50,000. As vice president for Student Development and Enrollment Services, Huddleston helped to develop UCF’s innovative LEAD Scholars program, which prepares freshmen and sophomores to become leaders on campus and after they graduate. He also established UCF’s Order of Pegasus awards, the most prestigious honor the university gives to students. And he created Eternal Knights, a memorial service that honors students who have passed away and enables family members to join campus leaders in a solemn tribute to their loved ones.

“On behalf of the entire UCF family, I thank Tom for his strong leadership during the past 15 years and wish him the best during his retirement,” said UCF President John Hitt, who hired Huddleston a year after he became UCF president. “Tom’s expertise and dedication have helped our university strengthen and expand the many opportunities that we offer our students. The impact of his leadership will be felt for many years to come.”

At the March 19 UCF Board of Trustees meeting, President Hitt announced that Huddleston would be awarded emeritus status at the annual Founders’ Day ceremony on April 1. Emeritus status confers a lifetime link to the university and honors an individual’s major professional contributions and distinctions achieved at UCF.

In his career, Huddleston has focused on the development of comprehensive enrollment management models, institutional identity and strategies to strengthen student enrollment and the student-learning environment. Particular attention has been on the marketing of higher education, institutional identity, new student growth, integrated student development programs and student retention.

U.S. News & World Report’s “America’s Best Colleges” guide recently ranked UCF No. 7 as an "Up-and-Coming" national university that has recently made promising and innovative changes. UCF is the only Florida school in the top 10.

Huddleston has held significant leadership positions within the private and public sectors of higher education. He has published frequently for a variety of journals, books and magazines, and he is a featured speaker at national institutes, seminars and forums.

Huddleston received bachelor’s and master’s degrees from Texas A&M University-Commerce. His doctorate is from Oklahoma State University.

He and his wife, Beverly, will be moving to Birmingham, Ala.
