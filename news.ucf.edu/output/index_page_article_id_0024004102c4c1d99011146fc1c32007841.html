UCF Research Week to Feature Stem Cell, Cancer Research and Med School Talks

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala March 27, 2007

Photo: UCF News File

As part of Research Week, undergraduates will showcase their work Thursday, April 5, from 2 to 5 p.m. in the Education Complex Gymnasium.

Questions about stem cell research, future techniques for cancer treatment and how to build a medical college focused on interdisciplinary research will be featured during the University of Central Floridas second-annual Research Week from April 2 to 9.

Stem cell research and cancer treatment are among the most discussed topics in the medical community and society. Building a medical college that blends the best research from many disciplines will impact how future doctors work with their patients and what treatments are available. The topics fit well with this years theme, Celebrating Research in Society, Society in Research.

One of the primary goals of our university is to serve our community, our state and our society through our research and creative endeavors, said Max Poole, the associate dean of Graduate Studies who is coordinating Research Week activities.

The week includes showcases for graduate and undergraduate research in addition to many forums and speeches. All of the events are free and open to the public thanks to United Space Alliance, which donated $10,000 for the week.

The goal of the week is to recognize outstanding students and faculty, celebrate their accomplishments and showcase UCFs contributions to the intellectual and economic development of Central Florida.

Our faculty and students conduct a tremendous amount of research, and so often that aspect of our university is overlooked, Poole said. Research Week provides an opportunity for recognizing their accomplishments.

Highlights from the weeks planned activities include:

Monday, April 2, 3 p.m.: Dr. Rebecca Moroose, director of the Florida Hospital Cancer Institute in Orlando, will discuss Future Strategies in Cancer Research. She will speak at the Live Oak Room in the Marketplace building on the East Orlando campus.

Tuesday, April 3, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.: Art and Science Exhibit in the Student Union.
Graduate research forum in the Pegasus Ballroom.

Wednesday, April 4, 1 p.m.: Dr. Deborah German, dean of UCFs College of Medicine, will speak in the Garden Room of the Marketplace building. She will discuss the development of the medical college, and she will be available for media interviews shortly before and after her presentation.

Wednesday, April 4, 2 to 5 p.m.: WESH-Channel 2 medical reporter Dr. Todd Husty will moderate a panel regarding stem cell research. The panel includes faculty from the political science and philosophy departments, as well as the Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences. The panel will be held in the Live Oak Room. Panel members will be available to media one hour before the discussion begins.

Thursday, April 5, 11:30 a.m.: Ken Ford, director of the Florida Institute for Machine and Human Cognition, will present Toward Cognitive Prostheses in the Barbara Ying Center.

Thursday, April 5, 2 to 5 p.m.: Undergraduates showcase their research in the Education Complex Gymnasium.

Thursday, April 5, 4:15 p.m.: Professor Michael Rossman of Purdue University will present The Three Dimensional Structure of Viruses in Atomic Detail in room 101 of the Harris Corporation Engineering Center.

Co-sponsors of the event include the UCF Division of Graduate Studies, Office of Undergraduate Studies and the Office of Research and Commercialization.

For more information, including a full schedule of Research Week events, go to www.researchweek.ucf.edu/RW2007.html.
