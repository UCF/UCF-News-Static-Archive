Former Air Force Col. Kim Olson, Author of Iraq and Back, to Speak at UCF

By Mark Freeman March 23, 2007

Photo: Courtesy of Col. Kim Olson

Former Air Force Col. Kim Olson

Former Air Force Col. Kim Olson, the author of Iraq and Back: Inside the War to Win the Peace, will discuss the aftermath of the U.S.-led intervention in that country during public presentations next week.

Olson will talk about recent developments in Iraq during presentations at the University of Central Florida and elsewhere in the community from Monday, March 26, through Wednesday, March 28.

Olson, a senior female military officer, pilot, wife and mother, was part of the first generation of U.S. Air Force female pilots. She served under retired Lt. Gen. Jay Garner and Ambassador Paul L. Bremer in post-war Iraq.

In celebration of Womens History Month, Olson will be a featured guest speaker at the UCF Student Government Associations Week of the Woman public lecture series at 3 p.m. Monday in the Key West Ballroom of the Student Union.

On Tuesday, Olson will be the featured speaker in the ongoing War Series organized by the Global Perspectives Office for the Learning Institute for Elders at UCF. She will speak at 11 a.m. in the Pegasus Ballroom of the Student Union. Following that discussion, Olson will make several presentations to student groups on campus and in the community.

Sponsors of the presentations are the UCF Global Perspectives Office, LIFE at UCF, the Student Government Association, the UCF Middle Eastern Studies Program, the UCF Political Science Department, the Global Connections Foundation and Dr. Phillips High School.
