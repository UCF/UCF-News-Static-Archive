Peter Delfyett Again Named Outstanding Minority in Research Science

By Chad Binette May 16, 2006

Photo: UCF News File Photo

Science Spectrum magazine has again named Peter Delfyett, a professor of optics, physics and electrical and computer engineering at the University of Central Florida, as an outstanding minority in research science.

Delfyett, who holds the University Trustee Chair in the Center for Research and Education in Optics and Lasers at the College of Optics and Photonics, earned similar honors the past two years from Science Spectrum, a magazine for professionals and graduate students that strives to increase the number of minorities in science.

Winners of the Science Spectrum Trailblazers awards have made significant impacts on their industries and communities. They can be experienced executives who seek innovative products, researchers who break new ground in their fields or recent graduates who show promise.

Delfyett will be honored at the Science Spectrum Trailblazers recognition luncheon Sept. 15 in Baltimore.

Peter continues to excel in everything he does and inspire those around him, said Eric Van Stryland, dean of the College of Optics and Photonics and director of CREOL and the Florida Photonics Center for Excellence It is truly an honor for us to have him at UCF, and his being honored as a Trailblazer is a fitting reminder of just how lucky we are."

Delfyett and his team of doctoral students and scientists are trying to increase the speed at which data can be transmitted through semiconductor chips like the ones found in computers. Improving that speed would help computers download information from the Internet much faster and make computer networks work faster.

Delfyett, who is a fellow of the Optical Society of America and the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers, has taught at UCF for 12 years. In 2001, he won the Pegasus Professor Award, the highest honor the university gives to a faculty member who demonstrates excellence in teaching, research and service.

Delfyett visits UCFs Burnett Honors College and area middle and high schools occasionally to encourage students to pursue careers in science and engineering. He is a member of the Orlando Science Centers advisory board.
