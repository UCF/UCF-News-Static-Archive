Task Force on Tailgating, Alcohol Holds First Meeting; Will Meet Again Friday

By Chad Binette Sept. 28, 2005

Photo: Ed Heiland

From left, UCF Faculty Senate Chair Manoj Chopra, retired Navy Vice Admiral Al Harms and Student Body President Willie Bentley were among members of the task force that met on Wednesday.

A task force established following the death of a University of Central Florida police officer expects to make early recommendations about tailgating policies at home football games by next Wednesday and then will undertake a broader review of other issues related to alcohol abuse.

The task force, chaired by retired U.S. Navy Vice Admiral Al Harms, held its first meeting Wednesday afternoon. The panel includes several UCF administrators, Student Government Association President Willie Bentley, local law enforcement officials and university and community leaders who work in the drug-and-alcohol prevention field.

UCF President John Hitt, who created the task force this week, will receive the panels initial recommendations next Wednesday, three days before the next home football game against Memphis at the Citrus Bowl.

We want to be responsible and thoughtful in our approach, Harms said. We have a product that we owe our president, and I think we have an outcome that we owe our university family.

UCF students, faculty and staff continue to mourn the death of Officer Mario Jenkins, who was working with the state Division of Alcoholic Beverages and Tobacco to investigate underage drinking when he was shot and killed. The Florida Department of Law Enforcement is investigating the incident.

Funeral services for Jenkins will be held at 10 a.m. Saturday, Oct. 1, at First Baptist Church, 3000 S. John Young Parkway, Orlando. UCF and SunTrust Bank have established the Mario Jenkins Memorial Trust Fund to benefit his family. Donations can be made at any SunTrust Bank in Central Florida.

Harms urged task force members to begin thinking about meaningful and achievable short-term solutions that could be implemented before the Oct. 8 home game. The panel plans to meet at 1 p.m. Friday in the provost's conference room, Room 395E of Millican Hall, and again on Tuesday.

The task force will be divided into committees to discuss potential long-term solutions for football games and other issues related to alcohol, Harms said.

Harms stressed the importance of reaching out to the entire university community for potential solutions because everyone will have good ideas. The task force also will examine successful policies at other universities and in communities around the country.

I cant think of a more dear responsibility than the safety, security and welfare of our university family and the community, Harms said. It is our responsibility to challenge every assumption and to look at all of the options.
