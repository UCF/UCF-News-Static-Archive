UCF Economic Forecast Indicates Improved Holiday Season Before 2007 Slowdown

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala Oct. 27, 2006

Photo: Jacque Brund

A drop in energy prices, a low unemployment rate and low inflation will help consumers spend more during the 2006 holiday season, but the nations economy should then slow through the first half of 2007, according to a forecast released today by a University of Central Florida institute.

The Gross Domestic Product growth rate should be in the 2 percent range in early 2007 before accelerating in the second half of the year, according to the National Economic Forecast by Sean Snaith, director of the Thomas D. Garafolo Institute for Economic Competitiveness at UCF. Snaith is also a UCF economics professor.

Snaith predicts that consumer spending will grow at 2 percent during the fourth quarter of 2006, a jump from the 0.8 percent growth rate seen in the fourth quarter of 2005.

However, the cooling of the housing market and other factors will result in a slow down of the economy in early 2007, before GDP growth accelerates back to 3 percent in 2008, Snaith predicts.

Highlights from the report include:

-- Federal Reserve Banks efforts to corral inflation and tumbling oil prices join forces, ensuring that inflation remains under control.

-- Housing starts decline through 2008 as mortgage rates slowly creep to 7.25 in 2009. Excess supply of new and existing homes in many markets will put downward pressure on prices. This, however, does not constitute a bursting bubble.

-- Unemployment rates remain below 5 percent through 2008. Payroll job growth slows to 1 percent in 2007 before recovering to 1.5 percent in 2009.

-- The dollar will continue to depreciate against major trading partners through 2008. The trade deficit will experience a modest improvement over the same period.

To access the entire report, visit: http://www.bus.ucf.edu/hitec/Forecasts.htm

Snaith is an expert in economics, forecasting, market sizing and economic analyses. He is a member of several national forecasting panels, including the Western Blue Chip Economic Forecast panel, the National Association of Business Economics Quarterly Outlook Survey Panel, the Federal Reserve Bank of Philadelphias Survey of Professional Forecasters, Bloomberg U.S. Economic Indicator Survey and USA Today Economic Survey Panel.

The UCF Institute for Economic Competitiveness mission is to expand public understanding of the economy by convening business leaders, scholars, policy makers, civic groups and media to discuss critical issues.

Snaith is completing a comprehensive Florida and metro forecast that will be released in November. That report will include the following metropolitan areas: Daytona, Fort Myers, Gainesville, Ocala, Lakeland, Palm Bay-Melbourne, Pensacola, Miami, Jacksonville, Tallahassee, Tampa and Orlando. Quarterly reports will follow.
