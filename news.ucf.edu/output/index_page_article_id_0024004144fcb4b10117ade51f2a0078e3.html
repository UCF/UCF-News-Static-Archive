Art and Healthy Living Go Hand-In-Hand At This Year's ZORA! Festival

By Christine Dellert Jan. 31, 2008

Photo: Ted Hollins Photography

The 19th Annual Zora Neale Hurston Festival will feature a Health Village during its Feb. 1 - 3 street festival in historic Eatonville.

Along with its impressive lineup of artists, musicians and scholars, the 19th Annual Zora Neale Hurston Festival of the Arts and Humanities has something new for its thousands of visitors -- free health screenings and cooking lessons.

About 80 University of Central Florida volunteers -- from nursing students to nutrition experts -- will help host the ZORA! Health Village at this weekend’s Street Festival of the Arts in historic Eatonville, where they’ll provide visitors with free blood pressure, asthma, HIV screenings and more.

The community push to bring health awareness to one of the nation’s largest cultural festivals is a partnership between ZORA! Festival founders and UCF’s Rosen College of Hospitality Management, which together organized the event.

The Heath Village opens Friday, Feb. 1, on East Kennedy Boulevard and runs through the weekend.

"We just want to bring awareness this year," said Vivian Ray, an instructor at the Rosen College and a Health Village organizer. "We want people to come see us, to learn more about healthy cooking and exercise and benefit from the free screenings."

Also scheduled for Friday’s "Education Day," when local schools plan fieldtrips to the street festival, are healthy cooking demonstrations by Rosen College students geared toward younger audiences. "They’ll teach them how to make fruit smoothies," Ray said. "And other UCF students will lead exercise sessions."

A nutrition researcher, Ray studies childhood obesity around Central Florida and explores new ways to prevent it. So far, she said, "it’s easy to generate outside interest in such an important issue."

Other groups involved with the ZORA! Heath Village are Orange and Seminole counties’ health departments; the American Lung Association; the American Cancer Society; the American Heart Association; and a street team from the trendy, hip-hop HIV awareness group, the Swirl Girls. BlueCross BlueShield of Florida is sponsoring the Health Village.

The Zora Neale Hurston Festival of the Arts and Humanities is held in venues throughout Orange County and the Town of Eatonville, the nation’s oldest incorporated black municipality, established in 1887. The nine-day festival is a celebration of arts and culture, named after 20th-century charismatic literary hero, Zora Neale Hurston. The festival also honors the historical significance of her hometown, Eatonville, and the cultural contributions people of African ancestry have made to the country and world culture.

For more information about the festival, call 407-647-3131 or 407-647-3188 or visit http://www.zorafestival.com.
