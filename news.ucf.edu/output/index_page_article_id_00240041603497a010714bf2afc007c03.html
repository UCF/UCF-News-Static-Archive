Boeing Co. Executive Wins Top UCF Alumni Association Honor

By Joseph Shippee Oct. 28, 2005

Laurette Koellner '77 won the UCF Alumni Association's Distinguished Alumnus Award.

The UCF Alumni Association will honor 12 alumni for their professional accomplishments and service to the university and community Thursday, Nov. 3, during the Black & Gold Gala at Hard Rock Live at Universal Orlando.

The Distinguished Alumnus Award, the associations highest annual honor, will be bestowed on Laurette Koellner, 77, of Seattle.  Koellner is an executive vice president of The Boeing Company and president of Connexion by Boeing.

Connexion by Boeing provides real-time, high-speed Internet and entertainment services to mobile platforms such as commercial and executive aircraft and maritime vessels.  Koellner was appointed to her current position in December 2004, and she is a member of the Boeing Executive Council.  Prior to 2004, she was executive vice president of Internal Services for The Boeing Company.  She served as a member of the Office of the Chairman in 2002 and 2003 and as president of the Shared Services Business unit in 2001 and 2002.

Koellner began her career in the aerospace industry in 1978, when she joined McDonnell Douglas as an analyst in contracts.  She was promoted several times until 1986, when she was asked to manage contracts and pricing for the Missile Systems Company in Titusville. In that position, she led negotiations with the U.S. Department of Defense, NASA and a variety of international governments for weapons systems and related products and services. 

Koellners expertise brought her to St. Louis in 1992. She was responsible for structuring and managing the $1.2 billion overhead budget for McDonnell Douglas Aerospace.  In 1994 she became director of the Human Resources Division, and she was promoted again in 1996 to vice president and general auditor of the corporation.  She held this key position during the merger of Boeing and McDonnell Douglas in 1997, and she was selected to continue in that role for the newly merged company.

Koellner earned her bachelors degree in business management from the University of Central Florida, and her MBA from Stetson University in DeLand. In 2003 Koellner was inducted into the UCF College of Business Administration Hall of Fame. Koellner also serves on the UCF College of Business Administration Deans Executive Council and the University of Portland Board of Regents.
