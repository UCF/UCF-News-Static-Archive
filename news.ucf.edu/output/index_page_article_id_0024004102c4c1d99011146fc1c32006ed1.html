Hundreds of UCF Students, Virginia Tech Alumni Mourn at Candlelight Vigil

By Chad Binette Updated April 18, 2007

Photo: Tom Evelyn

More than 400 people gathered Tuesday night for a candlelight vigil at UCF's Reflecting Pond to remember those whose lives were lost at Virginia Tech.

University of Central Florida honors student Alexis Stangl wanted to do more than feel helpless and upset after she found out about the shootings Monday at Virginia Tech.

Stangl, a junior who knew one of the students who died, quickly organized a candlelight vigil by the Reflecting Pond on Tuesday night. More than 400 UCF students and several members of Virginia Techs Central Florida alumni chapter attended to mourn those who lost their lives and demonstrate UCFs solidarity with the Hokie nation.

Were here tonight because we understand that this tragedy does not only affect those at Virginia Tech or even in the state of Virginia, said David Givens, a UCF student and Presidents Leadership Council member who delivered the prayer at the vigil. We are all affected by this tragedy in many ways.

Stangl, who lived before in Virginia, organized the vigil largely through the Facebook Web site and by working with staff from the Student Union, Burnett Honors College and UCF Police. Shaun Notman, a UCF junior, obtained fabric for a banner that members of the UCF community signed and that will be sent to Virginia Tech families.

Inspired by Stangl's efforts, Housing and Residence Life and Rosen College of Hospitality Management staff and students organized another vigil held Tuesday night at the Rosen Colleges campus on Universal Boulevard. About 75 students, faculty and staff attended that vigil, and a past president of Virginia Tech's local alumni chapter addressed the crowd.

At the main, East Orlando campus, Gaston Blanchet, a student and also a member of the Presidents Leadership Council, sang Amazing Grace to open the vigil. Givens later rang a ball 33 times, one for each of the members of the Virginia Tech community who lost their lives.

UCF President John Hitt told the crowd that he was proud of the students who organized the event and of those who attended. He said he hoped that it would bring some comfort to the Virginia Tech alumni in attendance.

We can share with them comfort, and they can know that we share their grief, he said.

Richard Schmidt, president of Virginia Techs alumni chapter in Central Florida, acknowledged that it means a lot to him and his fellow Hokies that so many UCF students are supporting them.

The gunman may have taken their lives, but he hasnt taken our community, Schmidt said. He can never kill our Hokie spirit.

Earlier Tuesday, Hitt extended sympathies to the Virginia Tech community in an e-mail that was sent to UCF students, faculty and staff.

Our hearts go out to the students, staff, faculty, and alumni of Virginia Tech and their families following yesterday's tragedy, Hitt wrote. As a campus community, we share in the shock and dismay over this sad loss of so many lives.

UCF Task Force to Review Plans, Procedures

Hitt also announced the creation of a task force to review the universitys security procedures and emergency response plans. The task force will be chaired by Al Harms, a special assistant to the president who also is a retired U.S. Navy vice admiral.

Hitt said the task force will review UCFs security procedures and emergency response plans to ensure that UCFs campuses are as safe as possible. I want you to know that security will always be a primary concern at UCF.

UCF notifies its students, staff and faculty of emergency situations through campus-wide e-mails and messages posted on the universitys Web site. Emergency messages also are distributed through campus radio and television and Central Florida media. The university also is developing a reverse-911 system that will relay emergency information to phones on campus and exploring the use of sirens and other communication methods.

Students who want to speak with a counselor about the tragedy can contact the Counseling Center at 407-823-2811.
