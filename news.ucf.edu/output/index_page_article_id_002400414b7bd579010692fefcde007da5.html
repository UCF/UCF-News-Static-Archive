UCF Offers Counseling, Establishes Task Force to Examine Tailgating Following Tragic Death

By Tom Evelyn Updated Sept. 27, 2005

Photo: UCF Police

UCF Police Officer Mario Jenkins.

The University of Central Florida is offering counseling services to its students and police officers and has established a task force to examine tailgating policies following the tragic death of UCF Police Officer Mario Jenkins at the Citrus Bowl on Saturday.

Jenkins was working undercover with the state Division of Alcoholic Beverages and Tobacco to investigate underage drinking when he was shot and killed. The Florida Department of Law Enforcement is investigating the incident.

Losing a member of our university family is difficult for all of us, UCF President John Hitt said. This is a tragic loss that is going to change a lot of lives, and we want to do everything we can to help those who are grieving and to ensure the safety of our students and others who attend UCF games.

The Counseling Center is offering another special grief counseling session from noon to 1:30 p.m. today, Sept. 27, for members of the UCF community. The group session will be held in Cape Florida Room C at the Student Union. Other group support sessions will be scheduled during the week.

For students seeking individual counseling, the Counseling Center is open from 8 a.m. to 7 p.m. Monday and Friday and from 8 a.m. to 5 p.m. Tuesday, Wednesday and Friday for appointments. In addition, the center has walk-in availability from 1 to 3:30 p.m. Monday through Friday.

The Counseling Center is in Room 203 of Ferrell Commons, the former Student Resource Center, next to the Marketplace dining hall.

The UCF Police Department is providing counseling for all of its officers.

On Monday, Hitt established the task force to develop policies and procedures dealing with alcohol use and security at large public gatherings sponsored by UCF, particularly home football games. The group, which will hold its first meeting Wednesday, will work quickly to provide recommendations before UCFs next home game on Oct. 8.

Retired U.S. Navy Vice Admiral Al Harms will chair the task force. Harms joined UCF this summer as assistant vice president for research and special assistant to the president. He retired from the Navy last year after more than 33 years of service.

Members of the task force will include a cross-section of university officials, law enforcement leaders, students, alumni and others.

NOTE: In expressing concern for the young man who also was shot outside the Citrus Bowl Saturday, the University of Central Florida mistakenly identified him as a UCF student with a similar name.  FDLE has confirmed the identification of the man as Michael John Young, who is not a UCF student.
