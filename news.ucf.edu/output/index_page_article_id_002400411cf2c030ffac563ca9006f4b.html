Florida Attorney General, U.S. Senator Encourage Graduates to Serve Others

By UCF Staff Dec. 13, 2004

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Tom Evelyn

    • 407-823-5988

  • Chad Binette

    • 407-823-6312

  • Commencement webcast

  • Print this Page

Florida Attorney General Charlie Crist inspired UCF graduates Monday to live up to the ideals of the late college basketball coach Jim Valvano, while U.S. Sen. Bill Nelson spoke of the pioneering efforts of early space program employees.

Crist and Nelson spoke at the first two of four commencement ceremonies this week at the UCF Arena. The third and fourth ceremonies will be held at 10 a.m. and 1:30 p.m. Tuesday and will feature the colleges of Education and Arts and Sciences, respectively.

More than 3,100 students planned to graduate during the ceremonies. The expected graduates include 2,504 students who signed up to receive bachelors degrees, 565 for masters degrees and 58 for doctoral degrees. Two students are scheduled to receive education specialist degrees.

Crist addressed graduates of the College of Business Administration and Rosen College of Hospitality Management during the first ceremony Monday morning. 

Crist told graduates that he is an admirer of Valvano, the former North Carolina State basketball coach who died of cancer shortly after giving a televised speech in 1993 in which he urged the audience to never give up.

Crist said the graduates professors and family members, like Valvano, passionately believe in young people and deserve thanks for that.

He (Valvano) said that every day you need to live your life to the fullest, Crist said. You need to make the most of what God has given you.

Crist also urged the business and hospitality graduates to work for the greater good and help others whenever possible after they graduate. 

Nelson spoke to graduates of the College of Engineering and Computer Science, the College of Health and Public Affairs, the College of Optics and Photonics and the Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences during the ceremony Monday afternoon.

Nelson, who was an astronaut aboard the Space Shuttle Columbia in 1986, discussed the "optimistic spirit" of space program employees in the 1960s and of U.S. soldiers in Iraq and Afghanistan. He urged graduates to go forward with the same optimism.

"You're up to the challenge because you've earned a degree from a top-notch university," Nelson said, noting that UCF's admissions standards have become more selective as its enrollment has grown.

Like Crist, Nelson stressed the importance of community service no matter what careers the graduates pursue.

"In the end, it doesn't matter how you serve your community," Nelson said. "It just matters that you do it."

UCF President John Hitt awarded an honorary doctoral degree to G. Vince Amico, known for his innovations in modeling and simluation, during the Monday afternoon ceremony.

Charles Bronson, Floridas commissioner of agriculture, will address graduates from the College of Education at 10 a.m. Tuesday, Dec. 14. Martha Haynie, the countys auditor and chief financial officer, will speak at the College of Arts and Sciences ceremony at 1:30 p.m. Tuesday.

Guests must have tickets, which are distributed by graduating students, to attend the ceremonies. Anyone without tickets can view the ceremonies live via closed-circuit television in Room 104 of Classroom Building I. The ceremonies also will be broadcast live at http://webcast.oir.ucf.edu.
