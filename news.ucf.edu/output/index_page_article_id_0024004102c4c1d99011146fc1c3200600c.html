Recycling at UCF Benefits Families in Bithlo and Christmas

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala May 23, 2007

Photo: Jacque Brund

Still good stuff, abandoned by students, set out for adoption by needy families in Bithlo.

More than 100 people collected clothing and household goods at the Bithlo/Christmas Neighborhood Center for Families Wednesday all courtesy of the University of Central Florida.

The items, which are donated to the nonprofit center, are collected during Move Out Days at UCF, a time when students leave their campus homes and get rid of their stuff.

Rather than trash it all, Physical Plant Assistant Director Brian Wormwood and his staff have students put aside the items they dont want. They set up recycling boxes at each residence hall and encourage students to drop off anything that can be used again.

This year Wormwoods crew collected more than 1,300 pounds of clothing, more than 200 pairs of shoes and 429 pounds of bedding. There are a few microwaves and even a fridge in the lot.

Rather than see all these items jam up landfills, Wormwood makes it as easy as possible for students to recycle them. That way they go to people who really need them. This year, the items were transpoted and distrubuted on May 23. And as Wormwood expected there was a line of people waiting when the two truck loads arrived.

The Move Out recycling push really demonstrates the power of recycling in its truest sense and the partnership the university has with its community, Wormwood said.

The nonprofit center, which offers counseling, community health services, tutoring, classes for adults and a food pantry, welcomes the visits.

We are so grateful to UCF, said Roxie Webster, neighborhood coordinator. We cant say enough about the partnership we have. There really is a community need here. Its a wonderful help.

Wormwood said one of the first years UCF delivered a truckload of the items to the center, a woman came wearing a T-shirt and jeans  no shoes. Thats all she owned, he said.

It made all the difference to her, he said.

This partnership transforms lives by taking the idea of recycling beyond aluminum cans and cardboard. UCF also continues to recycle those more traditional materials. The university places collection bins throughout the campus, and students who live off campus can drop off items at UCFs Physical Plant offices.

Wormwood works with UCF groups such as Echo Adventures to promote recycling. Last year the university redirected about 500,000 pounds of materials that otherwise would have ended up in landfills.
