Summer Research Preparing Students for High-Tech, High-Wage Jobs

By Courtney Gilmartin May 25, 2010

Photo: Jacque Brund

Sixty-six University of Central Florida students are conducting research alongside professors and working with emerging business leaders this summer thanks to a partnership with WORKFORCE CENTRAL FLORIDA.

WCF provided more than $110,000 to support the students as they gain valuable work experience in UCF laboratories and with high-tech companies affiliated with the university’s Business Incubation Program.

Students’ on-campus projects include research related to neural networks, which are computer models that function similarly to the human brain, and developing a field test for how to best clean water discharged from construction sites.

In one of the high-tech company projects, an aerospace engineering student interning with RINI Technologies is working on a project related to the development of diver suits that regulate body temperature through an automated heating and cooling system.
 
“The goal is to have these students in an environment where they see what they’re learning, but they also see what life outside of that degree is going to look like,” said Gary Earl, president and CEO of WCF. “Students can imagine themselves as a professor, researcher, engineer or scientist and have experience under their belts at 18 or 19.”

WCF is committed to getting students on track for careers in new and emerging regional industries at an early age. In addition to outreach initiatives such as recruitment and retention incentives, the organization provides targeted industry work experience to groups that are typically underrepresented in their fields.

Thirty-four of the 66 students conducting summer research are women, and more than 25 are African-American or Hispanic. All of those groups are underrepresented in science, technology, engineering and math (STEM) careers. In addition, more than 20 of the students are the first in their families to attend college.

Many of the students are sophomores in the EXCEL program, which is a two-year program funded by the National Science Foundation that aims to increase student success in science and math classes and encourage them to pursue STEM careers. Other participants include Biology majors and students in the UCF Research and Mentoring Program (RAMP) and McNair Scholars Program.
 
“Our students are learning valuable research and business skills that will enhance their classroom experiences and prepare them for high-tech, high-wage jobs,” said Alison Morrison-Shetlar, UCF’s vice provost and dean of Undergraduate Studies.  “WORKFORCE CENTRAL FLORIDA is collaborating with us to prepare future leaders who will help to strengthen and diversify our region’s economy.”

Michael Georgiopoulos, EXCEL program director, said that hands-on research opportunities allow students to apply some of the abstract concepts they learn in classes to actual field work. This real-world experience can help students shine in challenging math, science and engineering classes, he said.

“It’s highly interactive,” Georgiopoulos said. “The students have opportunities to work closely with professors and other students from diverse backgrounds.”

Additionally, the students learn how research can lead to published papers and patents and how innovative work moves from the laboratory to the market.

WCF has partnered with UCF on several other programs. College Discovery Day, held in cooperation with the Burnett Honors College and Undergraduate Studies, provided a first look at college for many 5th-graders from underserved schools in Orange, Seminole, Lake and Osceola counties. WCF and the College of Education have coordinated STEM summer camps for local 5th-graders.

WCF has provided more than $200,000 to fund those initiatives since July.
