World Issues on Tap

By Chad Binette February 6, 2004

Related Contacts and Links:

  • John Bersia

    • 407-823-0687

  • Keisha Biggs, program coordinator

    • 407-823-0935

  • Print this Page

Former Gov. Buddy MacKay, Florida Supreme Court Justice Charles Wells and Amy Chua, the author of a book about how the expansion of free-market economies around the world produces hatred and instability, will headline forums here this month.

Four years after the fate of the presidency rested first with the state Supreme Court and then the U.S. Supreme Court, a forum featuring MacKay and Wells will examine the role of the judicial branch in democracies.

"Justice and Democracy: The Role of the Judiciary in Promoting a Free and Open Society" will be from 1:30 to 4 p.m. Thursday, Feb. 12, in the Cape Florida Ballroom, Room 316, at the UCF Student Union.

U.S. District Judge Patricia Fawsett, chief judge of the Orlando-based Middle District, and Herbert Kritzer, a political science and law professor at the University of Wisconsin, also will participate in the forum, which is free and open to the public. Winter Park attorney Russell Troutman, a past president of The Florida Bar and Orange County Bar Association, will serve as moderator.

Wells, an Orlando native, was chief justice of the Florida Supreme Court when it reviewed cases related to the 2000 presidential election. MacKay served as Florida's lieutenant governor for eight years and was governor for about one month in 1998 and 1999 after the death of Gov. Lawton Chiles.

Fawsett, a district judge since 1986, was appointed chief judge of the Middle District last year. Kritzer, an editor of "Legal Systems of the World," has researched the American civil justice system and other common law systems extensively.

"The justice and democracy forum is particularly timely in an election year," said John C. Bersia, special assistant to the president for global perspectives at UCF. "The panelists will present diverse perspectives, drawing on experience at all levels - from the local to the international,"

On Monday, Feb. 9, Yale law professor Amy Chua will speak on "Globalization: The Peril Behind the Promise." Chua is the author of "World on Fire: How Exporting Free Market Democracy Breeds Ethnic Hatred and Global Instability." The forum, which is free and open to the public, will be from 10 to 11:30 a.m. in the Cape Florida Ballroom, Room 316, at the Student Union.

"Chua has claimed a leading role in analyzing globalization, one of the most powerful and controversial phenomena of our time," Bersia said. "She warns about tensions that can arise as societies globalize and democratize."

The forums are part of a series of spring events supported by the UCF Global Perspectives Office. William Cohen, a former U.S. defense secretary and U.S. senator from Maine, will speak at a March 24 forum on the first anniversary of the war in Iraq.

Bob Woodward, assistant managing editor of The Washington Post, will be the keynote speaker at a forum March 29 entitled "The Impact of Watergate: A Thirty-Year Retrospective." Woodward is one of the investigative reporters who broke the news of the Watergate scandal. His presentation is part of a two-day symposium hosted by the Lou Frey Institute of Politics and Government at UCF.

In addition to the UCF Global Perspectives Office, the sponsors for the judiciary forum and Chua's presentation include the UCF Political Science Department, the Criminal Justice & Legal Studies Department, the Metropolitan Center for Regional Studies and the Student Government Association. Other sponsors include the Orlando Sentinel and Global Connections for Central Florida, a nonpartisan group that strives to improve Orlando area residents' understanding of international issues.
