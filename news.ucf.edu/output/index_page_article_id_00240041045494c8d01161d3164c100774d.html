Biblical Scholar to Discuss Dead Sea Scrolls, Historical Jesus at UCF Seminars

By Jessica Saggio Nov. 21, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Harry Coverston

    • 407-823-2904

  • UCF Philosophy Department

  • Print this Page

Biblical scholar James Robinson will discuss the Dead Sea Scrolls, the Christian Scriptures and the historical Jesus during a seminar Friday, Nov. 30, and Saturday, Dec. 1, at the University of Central Florida.

Robinson was part of a team of scholars that produced a translation of the Dead Sea Scrolls in 1992. He is a fellow at the Westar Institute, which has worked to uncover the “historical Jesus” from the Christian Scriptures. Robinson has most recently been involved in the work to reconstruct and translate the non-canonical gospels of Thomas and Judas discovered in Nag Hamadi, Egypt, during the 1940s.

The seminar, hosted by the UCF Philosophy Department, will be held in the Psychology Auditorium, room 108 of the Psychology Building located on the east side of campus, across from the UCF Arena.

The session on the Dead Sea Scrolls will be held from 6 to 8:30 p.m. Nov. 30. The sessions from 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. Dec. 1 will focus on the Nag Hamadi Gospels in the morning and the Historical Jesus and the Sayings Gospel Q project in the afternoon.

The seminar is open to the public. The cost to attend for both days is $60, or attendees can pay $20 to attend Friday’s session or $40 to attend the Saturday sessions. There is no admission fee for UCF students. 

For more information, contact UCF instructor Harry Coverston at 407-823-2904.
