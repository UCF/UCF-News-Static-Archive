UCF, City Celebrate Partnerships During FIEA Grand Opening

By Chad Binette Nov. 3, 2005

Photo: Jacque Brund

A display in a hallway of the Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy shows videos and animations produced by students.

University of Central Florida President John Hitt joined Orlando Mayor Buddy Dyer and state legislators Wednesday in celebrating the many partnerships that led to the creation of UCFs graduate game-development school.

Hitt also welcomed a new partner of the Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy during a grand opening ceremony for the school, which is a linchpin for efforts to revitalize the downtown and diversify the regions economy.

UCF and Microsoft Corp. have entered into an agreement that allows FIEA students to use Xbox Development Kits as part of their educational training. The development kits are used by licensed Microsoft game developers in creating games for operation on the Xbox console.

This is the first time Microsoft has licensed the use of its Xbox Development Kits to an academic institution. The total value of the development kits covered by the agreement is about $1 million.

FIEAs masters degree in interactive entertainment offers intensive, project-based courses. The students work together in cohort teams on projects meant to replicate the various stages of video-game production. Faculty members include UCF professors and former video-game professionals from Electronic Arts, Disney and Microsoft Game Studios.

FIEA is located in the Expo Centre, a building across from the TD Waterhouse Centre. The city contributed about $4.4 million toward renovations of the Expo Centre, which also houses other UCF School of Film and Digital Media programs. The building, which UCF leases for $1 a year from the city, now mimics the production environments of successful gaming studios.

During the grand opening ceremony, Hitt praised Dyers vision and drive and lauded the support of state legislators, Gov. Jeb Bush and Electronic Arts. The 2004 state budget included $4.2 million to start FIEA, and Electronic Arts helped with the formation of the academy and the creation of the curriculum.

One of Hitts five goals as president is to make UCF Americas leading partnership university. FIEA and the UCF School of Film and Digital Media are a great example of what I had in mind, he said.

Dyer called the grand opening a truly exciting moment for the City of Orlando. Sparked by the opening of FIEA, Orlando can move to the forefront of the digital media industry like it has in modeling and simulation and film production, he said.

State Sen. Lee Constantine, R-Altamonte Springs; state Rep. David Simmons, R-Longwood; and Charlie Sloan, an executive vice president with the Metro Orlando Economic Development Commission; also spoke during the ceremony. FIEA students, faculty and staff gave tours of the building and demonstrated some of their work.

Starting in the spring, FIEA students will use the Xbox kits in courses like Visual Development and Software Production. Programmers will learn how to create code and applications for a console game and artists will get to see their models and environments rendered on a television-based platform. In addition, producers will get to implement game designs particular to the Xbox environment.

This unique agreement with Microsoft will benefit our students and program, Executive Director Ben Noel said. Being able to enter the game industry with experience working on these kits will give our programmers, producers and artists a head start on developing games for the platform when they enter industry.

In todays world of multi-platform game production, console development opportunities have been severely limited for students, said Chris Satchell, general manager of Microsofts Game Developer Group. This unique collaboration between Microsoft and UCF targets changing this and will provide students in the FIEA program with incredibly valuable experience in gaming console development. By giving students access to a contemporary console platform, we hope to energize the next generation of gaming professionals who will go on to create new amazing experiences for consumers.
