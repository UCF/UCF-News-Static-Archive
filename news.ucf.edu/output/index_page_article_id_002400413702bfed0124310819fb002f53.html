Black & Gold Superstars: Education Commissioner, Olympian Honored as UCF Alums

By UCF Staff Nov. 12, 2009

Photo: UCF News File

More than a dozen UCF alums, including Education Commissioner Eric Smith and Olympian Phil Dalhausser, will receive awards.

Florida Education Commissioner Eric J. Smith and Olympic gold medalist Phil Dalhausser will be among more than a dozen University of Central Florida alumni to be honored tonight at the UCF Alumni Association’s annual Black and Gold Gala.

The gala will be held from 6:30 to 10 p.m. at SeaWorld Ports of Call and will recognize alumni for their professional achievements and dedication to serving the community.

The Distinguished Alumnus Award, the association’s most prestigious award, will be bestowed on Commissioner Smith, who graduated from UCF in 1978 with a master’s degree in School Administration.

Smith held several administrative positions in Florida districts, as well as serving as a district superintendent in Virginia, North Carolina and Maryland before being named commissioner of education. Smith is being recognized for his commitment to increase students’ academic success and to reduce the achievement gap, which has enabled him to change students’ lives. His efforts to instill the importance of education also have brought diverse communities together.

The Michelle Akers Award, named for the World Soccer Hall of Fame star and UCF alumna, is given each year to an alumnus who is recognized in their chosen career on an international scope. This year’s award will go to Dalhausser, a 2002 UCF graduate who won a gold medal at the Beijing Olympics.

In the 2008 Summer Olympics, Dalhausser and teammate, Todd Rodgers, won the beach volleyball gold medal for the U.S. team.
Dalhausser played on a club volleyball team while at UCF and graduated with a bachelor’s degree in Business Administration.

A more recent UCF alumna, Lalita Booth, will receive the Distinguished Student Award. Booth, who graduated earlier this year, has demonstrated leadership and academic excellence and is now acknowledged as one of UCF’s elite.

Booth, an Accounting and Finance major, graduated Summa Cum Laude and was recognized as the College of Business Administration’s Top Honor Graduate for her exceptional GPA. Formerly homeless and a high school dropout, Booth is currently a graduate student at Harvard University and is a Jack Kent Cooke Continuing Graduate Scholar and a Truman Scholar. In 2008, Booth was recognized as one of Glamour’s Top Ten College Women based on her leadership and personal involvement in the community.

In 2006, Booth founded the Lighthouse for Dreams Financial Literacy Project. Her program is dedicated to teaching disadvantaged teens, adult learners and high school students the principles of sound financial management. Booth also serves as a free tax advocate and return preparer for low-income filers.

Other winners of UCF Alumni Association awards include:

Service to UCF: Debbie Kay Phillis, ’84, posthumous, for her many years of service and volunteer work with the Criminal Justice and Legal Studies, Public Administration and UCF Police departments, as well as the UCF Alumni Association. She also established the Public Affairs Scholarship fund.

Community Service: Karl Palvisak, ’83, for his many years of volunteering in the community with the Boy Scouts of America, Tipisa Lodge, Common Cent’s Campaign and the UCF Alumni Association.

Additional award winners are: Emily Ashworth Hamilton, ’96, a vice president and chief information officer with American Water; David Blue, ’01, an actor; Alex Martins, ’01, the Orlando Magic’s chief operating officer; Roger Dearing, ’71, executive director of the Florida High School Athletic Association; Maryam Ghyabi, ’83, president of Ghyabi & Associates; Mark Nation, ’87, founder of The Nation Law Firm; Editha Dulce Ruiz, ’95, a major and 66G material-child nurse in the U.S. Army Nurse Corps; Clay Newbill, ’82, president and executive producer of 310 Entertainment Inc.; and Scott Spilker, ’99, assistant resort manager of Wyndham Vacation Ownership.
