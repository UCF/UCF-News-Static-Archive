What is News & Information?

News & Information is the primary media and public relations office for the University of Central Florida. The office’s primary mission is to enhance and protect UCF’s reputation. The university’s reputation is what attracts students, faculty and staff members, business partners and donors. 

News & Information supports the university’s goals by working together with colleges, divisions and partners across UCF to implement strategic communications initiatives.  Specifically, News & Information develops plans to share the positive, unique and exciting success stories from across the university.

The office also advises administrators, faculty and staff on policy issues that might affect the public perception of the institution.

As part of our mission, we are committed to honestly representing the university in all areas in accordance with UCF’s values of integrity, scholarship, community, creativity and excellence.

How We Can Help You

The UCF News & Information office can help you share your work, research and accomplishments. By writing a press release, posting “kudos” to the university Web site or developing communications plans, News & Information can help you with your specific need.

The office is responsible for disseminating news about the university to specific audiences, depending on the nature of the news. Among our many duties, we coordinate media interviews, set up press conferences, help write opinion pieces and speeches, coordinate public information requests from media, conduct media training for faculty and administrators, write and distribute press releases, and report, write and produce print and video stories for the UCF Web site.

Our office operates much like a newsroom. Each staff member covers beats -- specific areas of the university. Our goal is to find and promote stories that exemplify President John Hitt’s five goals and elevate the reputation of the university. The goals are:

1)    Offer the best undergraduate education available in Florida
2)    Achieve international prominence in key programs of graduate study and research
3)    Provide international focus to our curricula and research programs
4)    Become more inclusive and diverse
5)    Be America’s leading partnership university

What is News?

News is something that is happening right now. It must be timely and have a significant impact on a large number of people, and it often generates controversy or involves a well-known figure. It is something that people are talking about at the water cooler.

In a university setting, news can be a research project that has the potential to make our daily lives easier or cure a common disease. It can be a student who overcame homelessness and cared for six siblings on her way to her college degree. Or, it can be a new trend in recreation or admissions that is starting to affect universities around the country.

While very important, conferences, books, building dedications and many academic awards and honors often are not considered news by the media. We can post some of those items on the UCF News Web site.

Contact With the News Media

News & Information frequently receives calls from news reporters who want to speak with a faculty or staff member about topics such as the economy, elections and the most promising careers for college students. We may contact you if we believe your expertise fits what the reporter is seeking.

Sometimes, reporters may contact you directly. If a reporter is seeking the university’s official position on procedures, policies or a specific issue, please refer them to the News & Information office. If a reporter is calling about a topic such as the economy or elections that is related to your expertise, we encourage you to respond if your schedule can accommodate the request.
 
If you would like our assistance, feel free to contact our office before following up with a reporter. Our priorities are to ensure you are treated fairly, help you prepare for the interview and sit in on the interview if that makes you feel more comfortable.
