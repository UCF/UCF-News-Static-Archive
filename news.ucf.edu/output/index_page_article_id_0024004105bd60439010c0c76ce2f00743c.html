UCF Trustees Approve Outstanding Review, New Contract for President Hitt

By Tom Evelyn July 27, 2006

Photo: UCF File

UCF President John Hitt

The University of Central Florida Board of Trustees approved an outstanding performance review and three-year contract for President John Hitt at its regular meeting Thursday.

The contract will raise Hitts base pay to $450,000 and will have an initial three-year term beginning Aug. 1, with an automatic one-year extension each year unless Hitt or the board declines. The board also approved a one-time $100,000 bonus for 2005-06.

Under the new contract, Hitt will be eligible for additional bonuses at the end of three years and each year thereafter if certain performance goals are reached in academics, growth and fund-raising. Under state law, any presidential pay over $225,000 a year must be paid with non-public funds.

The contract and salary increase were based on an independent performance review that said Hitt has been an exceptionally effective leader during his 14-year tenure and described him as a difference maker.

Reviewer James Koch, former president of Old Dominion University, has evaluated more than 40 college and university presidents over the past 10 years. Dr. Hitt ranks among the top two or three most effective presidents in this large group and, all things considered, may well be the most effective and productive president I ever have evaluated, Koch said in his report.

Hitt, who had a heart attack last month, has been cleared by his doctors to return to work full time immediately.

Since Hitt became UCFs fourth president in 1992, enrollment has more than doubled, research funding has more than tripled and the number of doctorates awarded each year has more than quadrupled. He has conferred more than 103,000 degrees, about two-thirds of the universitys total.

This year, Hitt secured approval from the state Board of Governors to establish a College of Medicine for which he helped to raise more than $50 million in donations. He also led the effort to build an on-campus football stadium that is scheduled to open next year.

Hitt was named the most powerful person in Central Florida by Orlando Magazine in its Power 50 list in July. In January, the Orlando Sentinel named him the Central Floridian of the Year.

In other action, the board:

-- Approved a final collective bargaining agreement with the Central Florida Police Benevolent Association, which represents University Police at UCF.

-- Approved an updated 2007-08 legislative budget priorities request that includes initiatives totaling $25.6 million. The list includes $4.7 million for medical college planning and $2.5 million for life sciences. Added items include additional support for the Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy ($1.5 million) and a marine and coastal research center in which UCF will partner with the Hubbs-SeaWorld Research Institute ($1.3 million).

-- Concurred with the conferral of more than 2,600 degrees at the summer commencement ceremonies, which will be held Saturday, Aug. 5, at the UCF Arena.

-- Approved an honorary doctorate for Rita Bornstein, president emeritus of Rollins College.

The next Board of Trustees meeting is scheduled for Sept. 28 in the Student Union. Visit http://bot.ucf.edu/ for more information.
