Task Force on Tailgating, Alcohol Begins Discussing Solutions for Next Game

By Chad Binette Sept. 30, 2005

Photo: Ed Heiland

  • Task Force on Tailgating, Alcohol Holds First Meeting (Sept. 28)

  • Print this Page

Counting a few more students among its members, a University of Central Florida task force on Friday began discussing changes aimed at ensuring the safety of fans and police officers at the next football game Oct. 8.

UCF President John Hitt addressed the task force before members began talking about initial steps the university can take in response to the death of UCF Police Officer Mario Jenkins last weekend before a game at the Citrus Bowl.

Hitt stressed the importance of enforcing underage drinking laws, noting that not doing so can lead to fatal accidents caused by drunk drivers. He encouraged the group, which represents a cross-section of the UCF community and area law enforcement officials, to use common sense and good judgment in advising him on how to proceed.

I thank you for your willingness to accept a very difficult assignment but an important assignment that will make the university better and safer, he said.

After hearing updates on state and local laws governing alcohol, UCFs tailgating policies and countywide efforts to prevent drug and alcohol abuse, task force members began discussing specific solutions that could be in place for the Oct. 8 game against Memphis.

Ideas include offering more entertainment and free or discounted food for students and erecting more signs around parking lots to make sure fans understand the rules and the need to drink responsibly. Another suggestion included having more faculty and staff members mingle with tailgaters before games.

Orlando Police Capt. Val Demings also said there will be a strong, uniformed police presence at that game.

The task force will meet again at 1 p.m. Tuesday, Oct. 4, in Room 395E of Millican Hall.

The panels short-term recommendations likely will be presented to Hitt on Wednesday, Oct. 5. After the task force makes recommendations to Hitt, members will take a more extensive look at other issues related to alcohol and university events.
