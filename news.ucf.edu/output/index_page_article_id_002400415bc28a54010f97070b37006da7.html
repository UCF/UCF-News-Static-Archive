Education Activist, Author Jonathan Kozol to Speak Wednesday at UCF

By UCF Staff Feb. 16, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Peter Wallace

    • 407-823-4195

  • Office of Undergraduate Studies

  • Jonathan Kozol's Web site

  • Print this Page

Jonathan Kozol, the author of best-sellers Rachel and Her Children and Amazing Grace, will speak Wednesday, Feb. 21, at the University of Central Florida.

Kozol will discuss The Hearts of Children and the Obligations of Our Nations Schools at 7 p.m. in the Pegasus Ballroom at the Student Union. His presentation, which will be followed by a question-and-answer session and a book signing, is free and open to the public.

Kozol has spent the last 40 years writing about inequalities among race and class distinctions in urban public school systems. He has visited dozens of schools and published 13 books confronting American education, illiteracy and poverty. His most recent work, The Shame of the Nation: The Restoration of Apartheid Schooling in America, was published in September 2005.

As a young teacher in 1967, Kozol was fired from an inner-city public school in Boston for reading a Langston Hughes poem to his fourth-grade class. He published Death At an Early Age that year highlighting his experiences as an urban public school teacher in the midst of the civil rights movement. He later spent time at homeless shelters and in the South Bronx to learn about the struggles of living in poverty. Those experiences were recounted in his best-sellers.

Kozols presentation is sponsored by the Office of Undergraduate Studies. Co-sponsors are the College of Education, Student Development and Enrollment Services Campus Life division, Metropolitan Center for Regional Studies, Quality Enhancement Plan initiative and the Office of Diversity Initiatives.
