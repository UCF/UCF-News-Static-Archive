UCF Students Honored for Academics, Leadership and Service

By UCF Staff April 1, 2009

Several University of Central Florida students were honored with Founders’ Awards for their commitment to excellence in academics, leadership and community service on Wednesday at the annual Founders’ Day Honors Convocation.

College of Arts and Humanities

Chris Wiewiora, a Creative Writing major, has tackled both professional and literary challenges while maintaining an outstanding academic performance. As editor of Cypress Dome and assistant managing editor of The Florida Review, he has revived both publications on campus. Additionally, Wiewiora organized a reading series at UCF, and he was fundamental in organizing and leading a panel at a recent writing convention in Chicago.

College of Business Administration

Laura M. Stockbridge, an Economics major, has been an outstanding student and leader throughout her time at UCF. As an officer in the Honors Congress, she helped students stay active within the Central Florida community. In addition to earning Dean’s List recognition and receiving an International Business Scholar Award, Stockbridge received the inaugural R. Glenn Hubbard Undergraduate Economics Student Research Award for her term paper analyzing the performance of countries with economic unions. She plans to pursue a Ph.D. in Economics.

College of Education

Cynthia Romero, an Elementary Education major, has excelled in classrooms at UCF and in the community. She has taught elementary school students through UCF’s partnership with Junior Achievement, tutored at-risk children and participated as a child-development coordinator at the YMCA. She maintains a 4.0 GPA in her education courses and is on the Dean’s List and President’s List. Cynthia has conducted research on teachers’ understanding of state standards, and she continues her involvement with faculty-guided research as she prepares for her doctoral studies.

College of Engineering and Computer Science

Steve Braeger, a junior majoring in Computer Science, is proficient in 16 computer languages. His interests include computer simulation, computer vision, graphics and virtual reality. Braeger also is a National Merit Scholar, and he is a former LEAD scholar at UCF. He has served as a summer camp counselor for the Boy Scouts, and he has taught astronomy to children. He also teaches children in elementary schools through UCF’s partnership with Junior Achievement.

College of Health and Public Affairs

Janine Vance, a Health Information Management major, has maintained a 4.0 GPA in her major while remaining active in both campus organizations and community outreach during her four years at UCF.  She is secretary of the Student Health Information Management Association secretary, and she is a member of the Student Health Advisory Council, serving as a liaison between students and the Health Center.  A member of the Honors Congress, Vance has volunteered with Knight-Thon, Junior Achievement and Habitat for Humanity.

Rosen College of Hospitality Management

Catherine Rash, a Hospitality Management major with a minor in French, has been active in LEAD Scholars and Junior Achievement. Fluent in French, she spent a semester studying abroad in Angers, France, furthering her focus on international tourism. During her time away from school, Rash works volunteers with underprivileged elementary school students in Central Florida. She also has worked on her thesis, and she has been named to the President’s List.

College of Medicine

Ruth Strakosha, a Molecular Biology and Microbiology major, emigrated with her parents from Albania as a toddler. During her UCF years, she has worked alongside scientists in stem cell research and interned at the Oak Ridge Institute on projects related to wastewater and biotransformation. Stakosha has published her work in academic journals. She serves as an honors peer adviser and team leader in The Burnett Honors College, and she coordinated a universitywide activity to benefit multiple sclerosis patients. She wants to become a doctor.

College of Nursing

Amahra Perry, a Nursing student, is a leader in every sense of the word at UCF’s Daytona Beach campus.  As a student at the top of her class, she is also vice president of her campus’ chapter of the Student Nurses Association.  Perry has participated in clothing and school supply drives to help the homeless, and she spearheaded the implementation of a mentorship program to help incoming nursing students transition to UCF. She wants to become a nurse practitioner and clinical instructor.

College of Sciences

Jennifer Heppert, a Biology major, has excelled in both academics and athletics at UCF.  Heppert is an ESPN-recognized volleyball player, and off the court she maintains a 4.0 GPA.  Her organizational skills and competitiveness have helped her excel within her major, and her undergraduate research experience in plant molecular biology culminated in a trip to Mexico. She has taken advanced courses in which most of her classmates are graduate students. She also mentors younger student-athletes.

Undergraduate Studies

Barbara Swift, a Biological & Physical Sciences major and Women’s Studies minor, has balanced academics, research and travel during her four years at UCF.  She completed her undergraduate research experience at the University of California at Berkeley and  participated in the President’s Scholars Program at the University of Cambridge. Swift has served as a resident assistant, member of the President’s Leadership Council and a big sister mentor in the Young Women Leaders Program.

Outstanding Thesis Awards

Science, Engineering and Technology: First Place, Andres F. Osorio for “Computational Analysis of Alternative Aortic Bypass for Left Ventricle Assistance Device (LVAD),” directed by Eduardo Divo in Mechanical, Materials, and Aerospace Engineering.

Runner-up: Juan Carlos Gonzalez-Pons for “Geometrical Control of the Magnetization Direction in High-Aspect Radio PdNi Ferromagnetic Nano-Electrodes,” directed by Enrique del Barco in Physics

Arts, Humanities and Social Sciences: First Place, Kristina Kopic for “The Gift of Chains,” directed by Jocelyn Bartkevicius in English

Runner-up: Tania S. Luna for “The Columbian Crisis,” directed by Houman Sadri in Political Science
