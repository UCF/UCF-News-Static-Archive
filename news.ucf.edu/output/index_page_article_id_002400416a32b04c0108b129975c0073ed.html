Senators McCain, Martinez Speak at 'Freedom Forum' at UCF

By Chad Binette Feb. 6, 2006

Photo: Jacque Brund

Sen. John McCain, R-Ariz., spoke at UCF on Monday for the second time in nine months.

U.S. Senators John McCain and Mel Martinez discussed Irans push for nuclear weapons, the federal deficit and many other issues during a Freedom Forum town hall meeting Monday at the University of Central Florida.

More than 300 people attended the first of many Freedom Forums that Martinez, R-Fla., plans to hold around the state to celebrate freedom all over the world.

Monday marked the 44th anniversary of Martinezs arrival in the United States as a Cuban refugee. He left Cuba at age 15 as part of Operation Peter Pan, a humanitarian program that helped more than 14,000 children escape the communist country from 1960 to 1962.

Martinez praised McCain, R-Ariz., for being deeply engaged in the fight for freedom. McCain, a senator since 1986, won the Purple Heart and several other awards for his military service during the Vietnam War. He survived six years as a prisoner of war in Vietnam.

McCain said he wants Congress to cut down on the number of pork-barrel projects that it funds to benefit individual legislators, especially at a time when the nation is facing challenges with Medicare and Social Security.

He also said hes bothered by Irans efforts to secure nuclear weapons, which he said would threaten Israel and destabilize the Middle East. While a military response in Iran cannot be ruled out, many other potential solutions must be tried first, McCain said.

McCain also addressed Ukrainian students efforts to bring freedom and democracy to that nation. While visiting the Ukraine recently, he met with about 30 students who said they were inspired to stand in the cold for several days and nights to fight for their causes because of the role model that the United States provides for freedom. The students also said the Internet helped their efforts to organize and communicate with each other, McCain said.

McCain was speaking at UCF for the second time in nine months. He visited the campus with U.S. Rep. Tom Feeney, R-Oviedo, in May.

The forum on Monday was hosted by UCF and the Student Government Association.
