Perez Joins State Board of Governors; Four UCF Trustees Appointed

By Chad Binette Jan. 26, 2006

Tico Perez.

Full Photo

University of Central Florida trustee Tico Perez has been appointed to the Florida Board of Governors, and Lake Mary insurance account executive Alan Florez will replace him on the UCF Board of Trustees.

Gov. Jeb Bush announced the appointments last week, when he also reappointed Judy Albertson, Olga Calvet and Phyllis Klock to the Board of Trustees. All of the appointments require the confirmation of the Florida Senate.

Perez, an Orlando attorney with Baker & Hostetler LLP and a 1983 graduate of UCF, will serve a seven-year term on the Board of Governors, which oversees FloridaÂ’s 11 public universities.

Perez is a member of the UCF Foundation Board of Directors, and he is a past president of the Orlando Utilities Commission and a past chairman of the Greater Orlando Regional Chamber of Commerce. He has won a Distinguished Alumni Award from UCF.

Florez, an account executive for Brown & Brown Insurance, will serve a four-year term on the UCF Board of Trustees. Florez is a 1998 graduate of UCF.

Albertson, a Winter Park art consultant; Calvet, an Orlando resident and chief financial officer of Palmas Inc.; and Klock, a retired Winter Park resident, were reappointed for five-year terms as UCF trustees.

Albertson is an active supporter of UCFÂ’s arts program, and she helped to establish the Friends of UCF for the Flying Horse Press. She is a member of the UCF Foundation Board of Directors, and she has served on an advisory committee for the UCF Judaic Studies program.

Calvet, a 1971 graduate of UCF, serves as a Florida Supreme Court judicial nominating commissioner and is vice chair of the UCF Foundation Board of Directors. She has served as a board member for the St. James Cathedral School, LYNX and the Community Foundation of Central Florida.

Klock is a retired president and chief operating officer of CompBenefits Corp. She previously served as graduate programs coordinator in the College of Business Administration at UCF and in a similar position at Texas Tech University. She is a former member of the UCF Foundation Board of Directors.
