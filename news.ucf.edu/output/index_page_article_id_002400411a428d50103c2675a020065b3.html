UCF to Award 150,000th Degree Saturday at Commencement

By Chad Binette Aug. 2, 2005

Photo: Courtesy of 18th Judicial Circuit

Circuit Judge James E.C. Perry of the 18th Judicial Circuit is one of the three commencement speakers this summer.

The University of Central Florida will award its 150,000th degree during the last of three summer commencement ceremonies Saturday, Aug. 6, at the UCF Arena.

 

More than 2,500 students have filed intents to graduate during the three ceremonies. Their degrees will increase the total number awarded by UCF to about 150,600 since classes were first offered in 1968. President John Hitt will have presided over the awarding of about 95,000 of those degrees after the summer ceremonies.

 

The 150,000 milestone will be reached early during the 3:30 p.m. ceremony, which features graduates of the Rosen College of Hospitality Management and the College of Business Administration. State Rep. Sheri McInvale, D-Orlando, will be the speaker.   

 

The graduate who earns the 150,000th degree will receive a lifetime membership to the UCF Alumni Association and a diploma frame from the UCF Bookstore.

 

The first ceremony, at 8:30 a.m., will include graduates of the College of Health and Public Affairs, College of Engineering and Computer Science, College of Optics and Photonics and Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences. The speaker will be Seminole County Commissioner Randy Morris.

 

The second ceremony, at noon, will feature graduates of the College of Arts and Sciences and the College of Education. The speaker will be Circuit Judge James E.C. Perry of the 18th Judicial Circuit, which includes Brevard and Seminole counties.

 

The summer graduates include 1,956 who filed intents for bachelors degrees, 553 for masters degrees, two for education specialist degrees and 61 for doctoral degrees.

 

Guests must have tickets to attend the ceremonies. Guests without tickets can watch a live simulcast of each ceremony in Room 121 of the Classroom I building. The ceremonies also will be shown on a Web cast at http://webcast.oir.ucf.edu.
