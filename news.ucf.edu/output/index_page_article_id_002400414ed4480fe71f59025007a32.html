New UCF Program Guarantees Classes, 2008 Graduation for 59 Freshmen

By Chad Binette Aug. 30, 2004

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Rick Schell

    • 407-823-4197

  • Chad Binette

    • 407-823-6312

  • Grad on Track Web site

  • Print this Page

Fifty-nine freshmen who began their University of Central Florida careers last week are guaranteed seats in all of their classes and at the 2008 spring commencement.

 

Participants in the new Grad on Track pilot program declared a major and signed a contract listing all of the classes they plan to take over the next four years. If they stick with the major and successfully complete all of the listed courses, UCF will ensure that they get a spot in all of their classes and graduate in that time. In most cases, students also will be able to make minor changes to their planned schedules and remain enrolled in Grad on Track.

 

The main benefit is that we will guarantee every class they need in the next four years, said Rick Schell, dean of Undergraduate Studies. We've built four-year course schedules for every program and nearly every track so that we are sure students will get every class they need in order to finish in four years.

 

Grad on Track is designed for freshmen who have chosen a major and is most appealing to students in programs such as business, engineering and education that have the highest number of required courses. The program isnt appropriate for students who enter college not knowing what they want to study or unable to take full course loads because of work or family commitments.

 

Even students who are not enrolled in Grad on Track will benefit from the program, however, because they will see course schedules earlier. UCF plans to begin making class schedules available a year ahead of time in spring 2005, and the earlier notice will help students plan classes around other commitments.

 

Many other universities give students suggested four-year schedules for their majors, but its rare for a university to guarantee seats in all of those classes and a graduation date in four years.

 

If the pilot program goes well, UCF plans to expand Grad on Track next year to include more freshmen.

 

UCF wants to do all it can to assist students in achieving their goal of graduating in four years. Grad on Track removes those hurdles we can control, such as course availability, Provost Terry Hickey said. I applaud those students who have entered our inaugural program and hope others will take advantage of this program in future years.

 

Several months before UCF mailed students information about the new program and discussed it during summer orientations, university departments began creating four-year programs for almost every major and program track. Each department ensured that the classes students are required to take are held at different times so students wont have conflicts with their schedules.

 

Grad on Track students registered for their fall and spring classes while attending orientation sessions in July. Other UCF students have not yet registered for spring classes.

 

Participants said they were attracted by the guarantee that theyll graduate in four years and the extra advising they get through the new program. They said its helpful to have an entire four-year schedule planned from the start and to know that they wont have to worry about not getting into courses.

 

The big thing is that Im guaranteed to get all of the classes I need, said Matt Mylett, a student from Sarasota.

 

The four-year programs for Grad on Track are designed for students who take classes in the fall and spring sessions, but participants also will be able to register for summer classes beginning next year. Participants are required to take at least 30 credit hours of classes each year.

 

Students can try to replace classes from their original list with different ones and remain in Grad on Track. They would not be allowed to register early for the new courses and would not be guaranteed seats in them. Students may be able to change their major to a related field, such as from mechanical engineering to aerospace engineering, and remain enrolled in Grad on Track. Students who switch to an unrelated major likely will have to leave the program but will face no penalties for doing so.

 

For more information, go to http://gradontrack.sdes.ucf.edu.
