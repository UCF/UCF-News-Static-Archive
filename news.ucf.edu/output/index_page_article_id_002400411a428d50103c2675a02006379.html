Schuckman Named Assistant Vice President for University Relations

By UCF Staff Aug. 18, 2005

Greg Schuckman

Full Photo

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Tom Evelyn

    • 407-823-5988

  • UCF Federal Relations

  • Print this Page

Greg Schuckman has been promoted to assistant vice president for University Relations and director of Federal Relations and Research Advancement at UCF, effective June 22, Vice President for University Relations Dan Holsenbeck announced.

Schuckman, who joined UCF in May 2000 as director of Federal Relations, maintains a full-time presence in Washington, D.C., representing the university in numerous activities with Congress, federal agencies, national policy organizations and various educational organizations.

He serves as the Chairman of the National Conference on the Advancement of Research, on the Executive Committee of the Northern Virginia Community College Board, the executive committee of the Council of University Relations and Development of NASULGC and as an Adjunct Research Fellow with the Potomac Institute for Policy Studies.

Schuckman earned the Commanders Award for Public Service from the U.S. Army for his participation in Pentagon rescue and relief operations after the Sept. 11 terrorist attacks. As a former board member for the National Multiple Sclerosis Societys Mid-Florida Chapter, Schuckman launched the Knight Strides MS Walk at UCF/Orlando, now in its fourth year.

He received his bachelors degree from the University of Florida in 1991 and his masters degree in governmental administration from the University of Pennsylvania in 1993.
