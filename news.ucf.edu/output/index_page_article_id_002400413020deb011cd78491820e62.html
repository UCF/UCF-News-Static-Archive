Three Non-Traditional UCF Students Earn Scholarships

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala Oct. 7, 2008

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala, UCF News and Information

    • 407-823-6120

  • Print this Page

Three women who are switching to more fulfilling careers have received $1,500 scholarships from the University of Central Florida Women’s Club.

Amanda Skyrud, a single mother who lives in Kissimmee, dedicated most of her early life to the U.S. Navy as a plane captain. In 2006, she suffered a triple stroke, which left her partially paralyzed. She has recovered, but will soon face another surgery. Skyrud, a junior at UCF, is pursuing a degree in Hospitality Management. She hopes to run a fine restaurant in the Orlando area.

Crystal Crawford, of Orange City, earned her degree years ago in Marketing. She went on to work for a Fortune 500 company, but said she found it was “not her lifelong dream.” Today she is pursuing a teaching degree and wants to become a kindergarten or first-grade teacher. She hopes to inspire children and pass on her love of learning.

Danielle Key, of Casselberry, pursued dance as a career before returning to school and beginning her quest for a degree in Biology. As a child, she feared doctors because of some unfortunate experiences.  But as an adult, doctors have helped her and her aging parents. She decided to go back to school and become a doctor whom patients can trust as they seek life-saving care.

The winners were notified they had earned the scholarships at the UCF Women’s Club Luncheon on Sept. 23.

The UCF Women’s Club is a group of volunteers that aims to advance the ideals and goals of the university; provide scholarships to full-time, non-traditional students; and welcome new employees to the university community.

The club was established by Frances Millican in 1969 and held its first scholarship fundraising event the following year. 

UCF Stands For Opportunity: The University of Central Florida is a metropolitan research university that ranks as the 6th largest in the nation with more than 50,000 students. UCF's first classes were offered in 1968. The university offers impressive academic and research environments that power the region's economic development. UCF's culture of opportunity is driven by our diversity, Orlando environment, history of entrepreneurship and our youth, relevance and energy. For more information, visit http://news.ucf.edu.
