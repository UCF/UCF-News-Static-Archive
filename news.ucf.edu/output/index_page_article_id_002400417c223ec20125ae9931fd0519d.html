UCF's Energy Savings: $840,000 a Year and Growing

By Chad Binette March 25, 2010

Photo: Jacque Brund

UCF freshman Kaitlyn Chana, with the torch she carried before the Vancouver Olympics, receives her Governor's Point of Light certificate from President John Hitt.

Full Photo

Conserving energy and making buildings more energy efficient helped UCF save at least $840,000 last year, and that number should more than double this year with the opening of the new Thermal Energy Storage Facility.

Dave Norvell, director of UCF’s Department of Sustainability & Energy Management, informed the UCF Board of Trustees of the savings during the board’s meeting on Thursday. Trustees lauded the success of the energy initiatives, noting that the cost savings continue year after year.

The $840,000 in verified savings for 2009 does not include any savings generated by the new Thermal Energy Storage Facility, which opened in the late fall.

The facility chills water for the campus' cooling system at night – when electricity charges are lower -- and stores it for daytime use.

UCF expects to save a total of about $2 million this year in energy costs, Norvell said.

Norvell’s announcement follows UCF’s unveiling of an ambitious new Climate Action Plan last month. The plan strengthens the university’s status as an innovative leader in "green" operations, classes and research. The plan, available online, will guide the nation’s third-largest university as it works to meet President John Hitt's pledge to become climate neutral by 2050.

In other action, the Board of Trustees:

Honored freshman Kaitlyn Chana, who founded the charity Love Letters: Random Cards of Kindness Inc. while in high school and who was selected as a Point of Light this month by Gov. Charlie Crist. President Hitt presented Chana with her Point of Light award certificate.

Heard an update on a new parking garage that will be built next to the Psychology Building and Fairwinds Alumni Center.  Construction on the garage, with about 1,350 spaces, is scheduled to begin next month.

Approved the conferral of about 6,700 degrees at spring commencement ceremonies May 7 and 8.

Praised the College of Business Administration for ranking first in a Bloomberg BusinessWeek list of undergraduate business schools where students get the largest return in salary for the tuition they invest in their studies.

Commended the winners of nearly $1 million in university awards for projects designed to enhance the quality and efficiency of undergraduate education during the next three years.

Honored Provost and Executive Vice President Terry Hickey for being granted emeritus status, which provides a lifetime link to UCF and recognizes his significant contributions to the university.

Recognized Alison Morrison-Shetlar, vice provost for Undergraduate Studies, for being selected as Elon College’s new dean for the College of Arts and Sciences, and Dave Harrison, vice provost for Regional Campuses, for being named president of Columbus State Community College.

Honored the 16 UCF students recognized as new members of the Order of Pegasus, the university’s most prestigious student award.

Lauded Academic Services, the Registrar’s Office and Computer Services for winning a Florida Prudential Davis Productivity Award for a change in add-drop policies that has helped to increase the number of seats filled in UCF classes. The effort was led by Elliott Vittes.

Honored Essam Radwan, associate dean for Research and Administration at the College of Engineering and Computer Science, for winning the Wilbur S. Smith award from the American Society of Civil Engineers Transportation and Development Institute. He was recognized for his contributions to traffic control and transportation engineering.

Recognized Linan An, associate professor of Mechanical, Materials and Aerospace Engineering, for receiving the Chang Jiang endowed chair professorship, the highest endowed professorship award granted by the Ministry of Education in China. An is establishing a collaborative research program linking UCF and China.
