Rosen School to Open in Tourism Corridor

By Chad Binette December 29, 2003

Photo: Jerry Klein

The Rosen School of Hospitality Management will open its new, $28.1 million campus next month near International Drive, the heart of one of the world's top destinations for tourists and conventions.

Rosen School students will be a short drive away from the second-largest convention center in the United States, some of the world's best-known theme parks and first-rate hotels and restaurants - a virtual backyard laboratory for the hospitality industry's future leaders.

"We have a state-of-the-art building and state-of-the-art technology in a location that is probably the best anywhere in the universe," said Orlando hotelier and UCF trustee Harris Rosen, who donated $18.2 million for the new campus and $1.1 million for scholarships. "I think that within a relatively short period of time, this is a school that will be regarded as one of the best, if not the best, in hospitality management."

Classes at the new Rosen School campus on Universal Boulevard will begin Jan. 5. A formal dedication ceremony will be held exactly one month later.

Students will study in spacious, high-tech laboratories for food and beverage preparation. They'll use a library stocked with hospitality-related materials and a training dining room that seats 190. They'll even be able to live in nearly 400 apartments in two residence halls expected to open in the fall of 2005.

The 159,000-square-foot building will allow the Rosen School, which now has more than 900 students, to expand program offerings and enrollment. By 2005, Dean Abe Pizam hopes the school will have 1,000 undergraduate and 100 graduate students. The new school can hold about 2,000 students.

The Rosen School was established in 1983 as part of the UCF College of Business. While it became independent in 2000, the school has continued to operate on the main Orlando campus. Rosen students are required to work in the hospitality industry during at least part of their studies at UCF.

At the new site, they will be close to many of the nation's top attractions and hotels, including Universal Studios, Walt Disney World, SeaWorld, the JW Marriott and Ritz-Carlton hotels and the Orange County Convention Center.

"Located in a destination ideal to learning the ins and outs of the hospitality industry, combined with a strong curriculum, the Rosen School of Hospitality Management offers graduates the knowledge and experience necessary to succeed in Florida, the United States and internationally," said William C. Peeper, president of the Orlando/Orange County Convention and Visitors Bureau.

Students will be able to take all of their classes at the Rosen School, as UCF will offer general courses in English, mathematics and other subjects there. Students also can continue to take classes at other UCF campuses and can ride free shuttles from the Rosen School to the main campus.

The 9,000-square-foot library likely will attract industry professionals to the new campus in addition to serving students. The new school also includes 22 classrooms and a 400-seat auditorium. The wine and beer laboratory includes three large plasma screens that show students close-ups of what's being demonstrated. Two food preparation labs accommodate about 25 students each and include a variety of restaurant equipment.

"We have everything right there," said Dana Reiss, a sophomore who is studying hotel management, of both the Rosen campus and nearby hospitality venues. "We have every resource that a student could possibly need."

The new Rosen School was designed in the Mediterranean style, just like the neighboring Rosen's Shingle Creek Resort, a 1,500-room hotel and convention facility that's slated to open in the fall of 2006.

In addition to Harris and Trisha Rosen, donors who contributed to the new school include the Orlando/Orange County Convention & Visitors Bureau, $5 million; Darden Restaurants Inc., $2,000,001; Walt Disney World Co., $2 million; Universal Orlando Foundation, $1 million; the Anheuser-Busch Foundation/Sea World, $500,000; the J. Willard and Alice S. Marriott Foundation, $100,000; the American Resort Development Association International Foundation Inc., $100,000; the Hospitality Financial and Technology Professionals, $100,000; and the Central Florida Hotel and Lodging Association, $50,000. Each of those gifts is eligible for matching grants from the state. UCF has received $19.3 million in matching grants for Harris and Trisha Rosen's donations for the new building and scholarships.

An official dedication and ribbon-cutting ceremony to recognize donors and others who were instrumental in helping with the new Rosen School facilities will be Feb. 5 in the Darden Auditorium on the new campus. That ceremony will begin at 10 a.m.
