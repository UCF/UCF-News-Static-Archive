Burnett Honors College Program Assistant Wins Monthly Honors

By Kristi Bartlett Nov. 28, 2006

Photo: Courtesy of Human Resources

Emily Boyd DeArmas of the Burnett Honors College is the October Employee of the Month.

Full Photo

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Employee of the Month awards

  • Print this Page

UCFs October Employee of the Month is Emily Boyd DeArmas, who works as a program assistant in the Burnett Honors College. She oversees the Burnett Honors College Summer Institute, advises the Honors Congress and Alumni Association and maintains databases for the college.

I feel appreciated that all of my effort and work has impacted my co-workers and students enough to nominate me, Boyd DeArmas said. Winning this award makes me feel like I have made a difference within the college and my office.

Boyd DeArmas co-workers describe her as extraordinary. Her interactions with the students and staff have far surpassed expectations, they wrote in their nomination. " She can be counted on to assume responsibilities that go beyond the purview of her position.

Boyd DeArmas, who earned her masters degree in Nonprofit Management in the summer, enjoys spending time with her husband, playing tennis and volunteer work. She said she likes working with dedicated, bright students.

Boyd DeArmas will receive a $25 gift certificate from Barnes and Noble, a certificate and letter of recognition, UCF apparel from Athletics and a goody bag from Health Services. She will also be recognized at the annual employee awards dinner, on the UCF marquee and at a Board of Trustees meeting.
