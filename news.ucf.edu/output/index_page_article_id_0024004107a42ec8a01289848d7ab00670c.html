Connect the Dots: Maya Ruins, Super Bowl Ads and Flag Football

By Grant J. Heston May 27, 2010

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Grant J. Heston

    • 407.823.5988

  • Print this Page

Share this story:

  • Share on Facebook
  • Save This Page
  • Seed Newsvine

What do ancient Maya ruins, Super Bowl ads and flag football have in common? They are all UCF-related stories featured recently in the New York Times.

Please see below for a review of selected UCF print, broadcast and online media coverage. Follow UCF news on Twitter and Facebook, watch UCF TV and learn more about Strategy, Marketing, Communications & Admissions.

NATIONAL

The New York Times: Mapping Ancient Civilization, in a Matter of Days
For a quarter of a century, two archaeologists and their team slogged through wild tropical vegetation to investigate and map the remains of one of the largest Maya cities, in Central America.
-- This story, on the front cover of the New York Times Science section, features the work of Arlen and Diane Chase and John Weishampel.

The New York Times: Effort to Integrate Madison Avenue Turns to Super Sunday
A long-running effort to bring more racial diversity to the advertising industry is shining a spotlight on the biggest day of the year for advertising — Super Bowl Sunday — in an effort to bring its arguments to life.
-- This features research from Richard Lapchick, director of UCF's Institute for Diversity and Ethics in Sport.

The New York Times: No Tackling, but a Girls’ Sport Takes Some Hits
Flag football, long relegated to family picnics and gym class, has quietly become one of the fastest-growing varsity sports for high school girls in Florida.
-- This story includes the fact that the UCF women's flag football team won a 2008 national intramural title.

FOX Business News: Economic Impact of Oil Spill
Sean Snaith, director of UCF's Institute for Economic Competitiveness, appears live on the Fox Business News Channel from UCF's TV studios.

United Press International: Planet Discovered Lacking Methane
The U.S. space agency says data from its Spitzer Space Telescope revealed a distant planet that lacks methane in its atmosphere.
-- This story appeared in science publications around the country and features UCF research from Kevin Stevenson, a planetary sciences graduate student.

PBS NewsHour: State's 'Adolescent' Economy Is Still Growing Up
Florida is used to visitors - some come to see Disney World, while others relocate to the Sunshine State after they retire. According to University of Central Florida economist Sean Snaith, the state's economy has depended on constant population growth - people moving to Florida for jobs and other reasons.

The Washington Times: Earth to Mars: Beware of Bugs
Beware extraterrestrials? Perhaps they should beware Earth. Even as British theoretical physicist Stephen Hawking cautions us not to engage in small talk with nomadic, dysfunctional aliens from other planets comes this news from biologists.
-- This story features research from UCF and the Space Life Sciences Laboratory at Kennedy Space Center.

LOCAL / STATE

Florida Trend: Trendsetters -- Student Leaders
University of Central Florida student Rene Diaz calls his plan to be an astronaut “the road of Buzz Aldrin.”

Orlando Sentinel: UCF Names New Provost
Tony Waldrop, from the University of North Carolina, was chosen from 93 applicants.

Florida Today: Intersil Donates Palm Bay Building to UCF
When the University of Central Florida takes over the 100,000-square-foot Palm Bay building donated by Intersil Corp., some 300 students and university employees will work in the donated semiconductor plant in Palm Bay.

Orlando Sentinel: Building Programs Needed
Since its beginnings more than 40 years ago, the University of Central Florida has invested time, talent and treasure to grow and diversify the regional economy. Simultaneously, these economic-development ventures expand research and academic opportunities for students, faculty members and future leaders in our community.
-- This is a first-person column written by UCF President John C. Hitt.

First Monday: Influential UCF Leaders Earn Magazine Mentions
Helen Donegan, vice president for Community Relations, and Tom O’Neal, executive director of the Business Incubation Program, were included in the magazine’s “Parting Shots” section in April and January, respectively.

Orlando Sentinel: UCF Relay for Life: 1,096 Participants, One Special Guest
In January 2008, Chris Whitcomb was in his last semester at the University of Central Florida when his doctors told him he had a rare form of cancer

Orlando Sentinel: UCF May Put Up to 300 Staff in New Brevard Center
The University of Central Florida says it's planning a big Brevard County expansion that could draw as many as 300 researchers and support staff to a donated facility there.

Orlando Business Journal: IBM, UCF Partner on $2M initiative
IBM and the University of Central Florida’s Institute for Advance Systems Engineering are working together on a systems engineering initiative aimed at preparing students for jobs in systems engineering, a profession that is critical to the creation of the smart cities, health care systems, and advanced products and systems of the future.

Associated Press: Crist Continues Official Business After Switch
Florida Gov. Charlie Crist is sticking with official business two days after declaring that he would leave the Republican party and run for Senate as an independent candidate.
-- This story appeared in publications around the state and quotes Political Science professor Aubrey Jewett.

Orlando Business Journal: Five UCF Grad Programs Make Top 100 List
Five UCF programs — engineering, education, speech-language pathology, public affairs and computer science — were ranked by U.S. News & World Report’s Best Graduate Schools guide as among the nation’s best in their fields.
