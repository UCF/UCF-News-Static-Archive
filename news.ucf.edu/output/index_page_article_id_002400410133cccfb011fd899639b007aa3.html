UCF Symposium Focuses on Economy's Impact on Health Care Services in Central Floida

By Courtney Gilmartin March 11, 2009

Walt Disney World and Orlando Health representatives will speak about the economy’s impact on health care at the fifth-annual State of Health Care in Central Florida Symposium at the University of Central Florida. 

The public is invited to the symposium at the FAIRWINDS Alumni Center, across the street from the UCF Arena, from 6:30 to 8 p.m. Wednesday, March 25. 

“I don’t think we can discuss the state of health care this year without discussing how our economy is affecting health care, health insurance and health care delivery nationally and here locally,” said Kevin Lenhart, chair of UCF’s Health Services Administration Alumni Chapter, which is sponsoring the event.  “I hope attendees leave the symposium with a better understanding of how health care affects our community, from both socioeconomic and personal standpoints.”

The keynote speaker is Dr. Todd Husty, the medical expert for WESH- Channel 2 and medical director of Seminole County’s Emergency Medical Services.

Other speakers on the panel include Beth Boyer Kollas, director of business development for Orlando Health; Tom Moore, vice president of the public sector of United Healthcare; Ken Peach, CEO of Future Vision Group; Becky Quammen, president and CEO of the Quammen Group; and Tracy Swanson, vice president of workforce planning, benefits and compensation for Walt Disney Parks and Resorts.

The agenda includes an optional career workshop for students from 5 to 6 p.m. and networking and hors d’oeuvres from 6 until 6:30 p.m. UCF students can attend for free. The fee for UCF Alumni Association members with a valid membership card is $5, and it is $10 for the general public. 

For more information or to register, call 407-823-1600 or visit www.ucfalumni.com/symposium.

UCF’s Health Services Administration Alumni Chapter has sponsored previous events that have covered medical insurance, nursing shortages and patient rights.

In the near future, UCF and the medical city at Lake Nona will provide a positive impact on health care in Orlando. The biomedical cluster is helping to diversify the local economy. The new UCF College of Medicine and neighbors at Lake Nona such as Nemours, the Burnham Institute for Medical Research, the Orlando VA Medical Center and M.D. Anderson Cancer Research Institute are projected to add $7.6 billion of economic impact to Central Florida and 30,000 jobs. The unique community of health care providers, research facilities and the UCF medical school will also elevate the quality of care in the region.

UCF Stands For Opportunity --The University of Central Florida is a metropolitan research university that ranks as the 5th largest in the nation with more than 50,000 students. UCF's first classes were offered in 1968. The university offers impressive academic and research environments that power the region's economic development. UCF's culture of opportunity is driven by our diversity, Orlando environment, history of entrepreneurship and our youth, relevance and energy. For more information visit http://news.ucf.edu
