NATO Commander to Discuss Europe, Iraq and Afghanistan Thursday at UCF

By Chad Binette Sept. 19, 2006

Photo: Courtesy of NATO

Gen. James Jones

Gen. James L. Jones, the supreme allied commander of NATOs military forces in Europe, will discuss NATOs Future Role in Europe and Beyond Thursday, Sept. 21, at the University of Central Florida.

Jones will address NATOs roles in Afghanistan and Iraq during his 2 p.m. presentation at the Fairwinds Alumni Center. The forum is free and open to the public. Attendees are encouraged to arrive early for security purposes.

Jones leads Allied Command Europe, which includes the North Atlantic Treaty Organizations military forces in Europe and strives to maintain the peace, security and territorial integrity of European nations that are NATO members. He works out of Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe in Belgium.

Jones also serves as commander of the United States European Command. He commands the European components of the U.S. Army, Navy, Air Force, Marines and Special Operations Command. Together, those forces aim to support and achieve U.S. interests and objectives in 93 countries in Central and Eastern Europe, Africa and part of the Middle East. The commands responsibilities include planning for and conducting evacuations and humanitarian relief operations, intelligence activities and security assistance.

Jones served in Vietnam in 1967 and 1968 first as a Marine platoon and company commander and later as a first lieutenant. He then served in many other leadership positions in the Marines before becoming a general in June 1999 and the 32nd commandant of the Marine Corps one month later. He was assigned his NATO command positions in January 2003.

Jones has received the Defense Distinguished Service Medal with two oak leaf clusters, the Silver Star Medal, the Legion of Merit with four gold stars and many other honors.

Jones presentation will begin a yearlong series of events by the UCF Global Perspectives Office on the future of Europe. In the spring, Cherie Booth Blair, the wife of British Prime Minister Tony Blair and a lawyer with the Matrix Chambers firm in London, has been invited to visit UCF. The academic year also will feature a UCF-hosted conference on the future of Europe and a visit by the dean of the University of Newcastle upon Tynes medical school.
