Tavistock Group's Thakkar Wins Top Award from UCF Alumni Association

By Darla Olive Nov. 20, 2007

Photo: Mark Ashman

Rasesh Thakkar, left, accepts his award from UCF President John Hitt. Thakkar is the senior managing director for the Tavistock Group.

The University of Central Florida Alumni Association presented 15 awards during its recent Black & Gold Gala, recognizing alumni for their professional accomplishments and service to the university and the community.

The Distinguished Alumnus Award, the association’s highest annual honor, was bestowed on Rasesh Thakkar, ’84. Thakkar is the senior managing director for the Tavistock Group.

Thakkar played a crucial leadership role in the establishment and approval of the UCF College of Medicine. He also was instrumental in helping to bring the Burnham Institute for Medical Research to Orlando and helping to create the Health Sciences Campus at Lake Nona. The Burnham Institute is an independent, not-for-profit biomedical research institution based in California that focuses on cancer, neuroscience and aging, and infectious and inflammatory diseases.

For the Tavistock Group, Thakkar focuses on corporate finance and structure, mergers and acquisitions and strategic planning. He helps determine overall strategy, targets sectors for investment, negotiates acquisitions and sales and transitions acquisitions into the group’s holdings. He has lived in the United States, South America and Europe and previously worked as an agent with the Internal Revenue Service. Thakkar holds a bachelor’s degree with honors in Accounting from UCF. 

He currently serves on the Board of Trustees for the Greater Orlando Chamber of Commerce and the Governors Council of the Metro Orlando Economic Development Commission. He also is chairman of the Board of Governors for M.D. Anderson Cancer Center Orlando. Thakkar is a member of the Board of Trustees of the Tavistock Foundation and the boards of directors of the UCF Foundation and BioFlorida.

Additional UCF Alumni Association awards:

Ivan Lefkowitz, '73: Service to UCF – for his many years of serving on the UCF Foundation boards and committees, as well as the Dean’s Advisory Council for the College of Business Administration
 
Linda Flannery, '86: Community Service – for her 25 years of volunteering with high school students with the Central Florida YMCA Youth in Government and the YMCA Blue Ridge Assembly High School Values Conference
 
Distinguished Student: Ashley Conyers, a senior majoring in Liberal Studies with a minor in Interpersonal Communication. Conyers received a UCF Founders’ Award this year for excellence in the classroom and in community service. She is a member of the President’s Leadership Council and has volunteered with Junior Achievement, Read2Succeed, the Second Harvest Food Bank and Give Kids the World.
