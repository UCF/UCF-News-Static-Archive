UCF President John Hitt Expected to Leave Hospital Today

By UCF Staff Updated June 29, 2006

UPDATE: President Hitt is scheduled to return home this morning to continue his recovery following a heart attack he had while exercising this weekend. More details will follow after a 10 a.m. news conference at Florida Hospital.

---

University of Central Florida President John Hitt may return home in the next several days as his condition continues to improve following a heart attack he had while exercising this weekend.

He is resting comfortably and in good spirits, said Tom Huddleston, vice president for Marketing, Communications, and Admissions. Hes looking forward to returning home as soon as possible.

Hitt, 65, was riding a bicycle near his home on the East Orlando campus Saturday when he felt discomfort. He called 911 on his cell phone and was rushed to Florida Hospital Orlando, where he was treated successfully for a blocked artery.

Hitt, who has been president of UCF since 1992, is expected to return to work within the next few weeks. Provost Terry Hickey will be acting president until then.

Over his 14-year tenure, Hitt has helped UCF achieve many significant milestones, most recently conferring his 100,000th degree. He has awarded degrees to more than 103,000 of about 159,000 graduates in the universitys history. Enrollment has nearly doubled during is presidency to more than 45,000 this past fall.

During the past year, UCF has achieved $100 million in research funding for the first time and received the state Board of Governors approval to create a medical college. Hitt helped raise more than $50 million in private donations for the college.

Among Hitts other accomplishments in recent years are the openings of the Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy in 2005 in downtown Orlando and the new Rosen College of Hospitality Management campus in 2004 near International Drive in the heart of one of the worlds top tourist destinations. The university plans to open an on-campus football stadium in time for the 2007 season.

The Orlando Sentinel named him the 2005 Central Floridian of the Year.

Dr. Hitt deserves much of the credit for this great universitys growth and success, said UCF Board of Trustees Chairman Dick Nunis. He has worked tirelessly to ensure that thousands of Floridians have had access to quality higher education.

Fighting heart disease has been an important cause for Hitt throughout his presidency. Just a few days before his heart attack, he spoke at the UCF kick-off breakfast for the American Heart Associations Heart Walk. He encouraged students, faculty and staff to help the university raise $17,000 before the Sept. 30 event and pledged a donation toward that goal.

We appreciate all of Dr. Hitts support of the American Heart Association, said Allisson Yu, executive director of the Florida/Puerto Rico Affiliate based in Orlando. Hes been a wonderful advocate for us, and our thoughts are with him. We wish him a speedy recovery.

---

In lieu of flowers, Hitt has asked that friends consider contributing to the First Generation Matching Grants fund, a need-based scholarship program for Florida residents who are first in their families to attend college. Donations are matched by the state. Checks should to be sent to the UCF Foundation, Attn. Accounting, 12424 Research Parkway, Suite 140, Orlando, FL 32826-3257. Please indicate that the donation is for the First Generation Matching Grants program. Call the Foundation at 407-882-1220 for more information.
