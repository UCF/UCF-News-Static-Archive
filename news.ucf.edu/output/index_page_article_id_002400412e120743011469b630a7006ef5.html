College of Education Alumni, Faculty and Staff Invited to 40-Year Celebration

By Rich Sloane Oct. 2, 2007

Photo: Jacque Brund

The UCF Teaching Academy, located next to the Education Building on the main campus, houses many College of Education programs and classes.

Full Photo

The University of Central Floridas College of Education invites alumni, faculty and staff to return to campus and celebrate the colleges 40th anniversary at a reunion on Friday, Oct. 19.

The college was one of the original four that opened to students when the university began offering classes in 1968. The college is celebrating its 40th anniversary with several events throughout the 2007-08 academic year.

The reunion will feature a reception, dinner and ceremonies at which alumni from prior decades will be honored. The event will be held in UCFs original gymnasium in the Education Building, which hosted the universitys basketball games before the old UCF Arena opened on the north side of campus.

That gym holds fond memories for alumni of the early years, said Anthony Thompson, a graduate of the college who now serves as its director of development. So many alums have told me they will be coming to the reunion just to set foot on that floor again.

Alumni from all of the colleges graduate and undergraduate programs are invited to attend the reunion. Faculty and administrators from throughout the colleges history will be attending and renewing acquaintances with colleagues as well as their former students.

It promises to be an emotional and inspirational evening, said college Dean Sandra L. Robinson.

The reunion will begin with a reception at 5:30 p.m., followed by dinner at 7 p.m. and a dessert mixer at 8:30 p.m. Tickets cost $20 each. More information on the reunion and tickets is available by calling the UCF College of Education at 407-823-2153.
