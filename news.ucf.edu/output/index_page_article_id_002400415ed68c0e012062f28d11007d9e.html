UCF Merging Two Divisions Under Leadership of Vice President Al Harms

By Grant Heston April 2, 2009

Photo: Jacque Brund

The University of Central Florida is merging two divisions into a new organization that will strengthen how the university operates, communicates and attracts students.

Retired Vice Admiral Al Harms, who is known for achieving strategic, operational and organizational success at the university and in the Navy, will lead the new division of Strategy, Marketing, Communications and Admissions (SMCA). The new division combines the formerly separate divisions of Strategic Planning and Initiatives with Marketing, Communications and Admissions.

“Al is well-respected throughout our campus and in our community and is the ideal leader for this newly combined division,” UCF President John Hitt said. “He is eager to accept this challenge, and I am confident our students, faculty, staff and alumni will benefit enormously under his leadership.” 

Harms currently serves as vice president for Strategic Planning and Initiatives and special assistant to the president. He will assume his new responsibilities as SMCA vice president on May 1.

“There is already considerable synergy between the two divisions, and combining the two organizations should result in enhanced efficiencies and improved mission performance,” Harms said. “I am eager to join the many talented members of this new team and work to further enhance UCF’s reputation, attract the best and brightest students to UCF and help set our strategic course for the future.”

The MCA division encompasses university-wide issues related to integrated marketing, news and communications, television services and undergraduate enrollment. MCA has been led for the past five years by Tom Huddleston, who announced his retirement in March after 16 years at UCF.

Strategic Planning and Initiatives includes the departments of Institutional Research, Operational Excellence and Assessment Support and University Analysis and Planning Support.

President Hitt brought Harms to UCF in 2005. In addition to his day-to-day responsibilities, Harms has led broad-based task forces to address issues critical to UCF, including campus-wide operations on football game days, campus security and preparedness and energy efficiency initiatives.

Following a distinguished career in the U.S. Navy, Harms joined UCF as an assistant vice president for research and special assistant to the president. Harms’ naval career included numerous operational commands at sea and ashore. In his last active duty assignment, Harms was the Navy’s chief learning officer where he served as commander of the Naval Education and Training Command and director of Naval Education and Training on the Chief of Naval Operations’ staff.

Harms previously served as deputy commander of the U.S. Pacific Fleet, commander of Carrier Strike Group Three, deputy director of operations for U.S. Central Command and commanding officer of the nuclear-powered aircraft carrier USS Nimitz, the amphibious assault ship USS Shreveport, and Attack Squadron Seventy-Five. A distinguished graduate of the U.S. Naval Test Pilot School, Harms also served as a flight test officer and director of the Mission Systems Integration Division at the Naval Air Test Center in Maryland.

A native of central Illinois, Harms earned a bachelor’s degree in biology in 1971 from the University of Illinois and a master’s degree in statistics in 1978 from Texas A&M University, where he was an assistant professor of naval science.

In addition to his responsibilities on the university’s senior leadership team, Harms also serves on various advisory boards for academic, scientific, corporate and philanthropic organizations across the nation. Following his transition from active duty, Harms and his wife, Gina, settled in Central Florida.
