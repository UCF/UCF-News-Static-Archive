'Largo Desolato' Opens Thursday at UCF Conservatory Theatre

By UCF Staff Sept. 25, 2007

Photo: Joseph Rusnock

Andrew Clateman and Courtney Moors are part of the cast of 'Largo Desolato,' which opens Thursday, Sept. 27.

In a totalitarian state, Professor Leopold Nettles fears his dissident essay could send him to jail. The writer sits at home, waiting for the police to knock on his door and take him away.

Dark and politically profound, Largo Desolato will open at 8 p.m. Thursday, Sept. 27, in the UCF Conservatory Theatres Mainstage Theatre on the University of Central Floridas main campus.

Largo Desolato was written by Vaclav Havel, Czechoslovakias foremost playwright and political leader. Havel was a passionate supporter of the non-violent resistance that ended Czechoslovakias communist government in 1989. He wrote the semi-autobiographical play in 1984  his first full-length work after his release from jail.

At once ridiculously funny and deeply unnerving, the absurdity of the play reveals the dilemma of a political dissident and his struggle with paranoia, paralysis and responsibility.

The political message behind this play is relevant and topical, with themes that typical theatrical companies do not find the opportunity to address, said Christopher Niess, a professor with UCFs Conservancy Theatre.

Performances will be Sept. 27 through 30 and Oct. 4 through 7. All shows will begin at 8 p.m., except for Sunday matinees, which will start at 2 p.m. The shows themes include strong adult content, language and violence.

Ticket prices are $15 for adults, $13 for seniors over 55 and $10 for students with ID. Reservations are recommended and available through the UCF Conservatory Theatre box office by calling 407-823-1500. The box office can assist with accommodations for patrons with disabilities, and group rates are available.

Largo Desolato is directed by Lani Harris, a UCF professor and Fulbright Scholar whose international work includes theater research in Mexico. Harris has performed at the Edinburgh Fringe Festival in her original play, Air Born, and has presented at the FIRT {International Federation for Theatre Research) Conference in St. Petersburg, Russia.
