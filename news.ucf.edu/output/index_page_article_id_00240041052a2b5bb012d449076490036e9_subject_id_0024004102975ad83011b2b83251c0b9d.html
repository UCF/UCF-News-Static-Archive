Today at UCF

88°F, Fair

NY Times, USA Today, ESPN Among Media Focusing on UCF

By Grant J. Heston Feb. 10, 2011

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Grant J. Heston

    • 407.823.5988

  • Print this Page

National media such as the New York Times, ESPN, USA Today, U.S. News and World Report and more have recently highlighted UCF's programs, students and faculty experts.

For more recent UCF news, please see below for a review of selected print, broadcast and online media coverage. Also, follow UCF news on Twitter and Facebook, watch UCF TV and learn more about Strategy, Marketing, Communications & Admissions. 

NATIONAL

New York Times: A Wizard Rivals Mickey
Pottermania is supposed to be winding down. But try telling that to the throngs at the new Harry Potter theme park here, where people are routinely turned away because of capacity crowds.
-- This quotes Rosen College of Hospitality Management professor Duncan Dickson.

ESPN: Knights Have Potential to Become Elite
The recruiting pitch came from a man who said he coached at UCF. Bruce Miller, a Georgia native, wondered to himself, "What is UCF?"

USA Today: Central Florida Reaches New Heights in Football, Basketball
Todd Bowers, a former alumni association president, graduated from Central Florida in 1977. Honored with a 2009 Lifetime of Service award by the school, he still visits campus typically once a week.

Education Week: Virtual Students Used to Train Teachers
Real-time classroom simulations like TeachME, supporters say, offer promise for a host of teacher-training applications. Through them, candidates could gain hands-on practice with urban students, or practice a discrete skill such as classroom management.
-- This features the efforts of the TeachME program at UCF (registration required to view the story).

Washington Times: States Redrawing Political Maps
The new battle lines literally haven't even been drawn on the map yet, but the political jostling is well under way in states that will gain and lose U.S. House seats in 2012 because of congressional redistricting.
-- This quotes Political Science professor Aubrey Jewett.

U.S. News: Forensic Test to Identify Hair Color From DNA
Are you a natural blond? New DNA tests can reveal your true hair color, and other physical attributes
-- This story quotes Professor Jack Ballantyne, associate director of research at the National Center for Forensic Science.

Business Week: Restaurateurs Don't Just Wing It
Passion isn't enough. You'll need a sound concept and management experience, and you should know that some of the most popular assumptions about restaurant failure are wrong.
-- This quotes Professor H.G. Parsa from UCF's Rosen College of Hospitality Management.

STATE/LOCAL

Orlando Sentinel: UCF Named Among Top Tier of Research Universities
Rapidly expanding University of Central Florida has moved into the ranks of top research institutions in the country. The designation could bring wider recognition to UCF, spur more dollars for research, attract prime students and promote high tech industry in the region.

Sarasota Herald Tribune: Orlando 'Medical City' a Model of Lab's Potential
Southwest Florida needs look no further than Orlando for an example of what landing Jackson Laboratories' proposed personalized medicine institute could mean for the region's economy.

Orlando Sentinel: Central Floridians Still Contributing to Haiti's Recovery
In the year since the earthquake in Haiti prompted an outpouring of food, clothing, money and volunteers from Central Florida, efforts have shifted from disaster relief to sustainable recovery.
-- This story features the efforts of the UCF H.O.P.E for Haiti taskforce.

Florida Today: Prognosis for Success
Tapping into Orlando's 'Medical City' can create Brevard jobs, businesses.
-- Just to West, New Health Jobs

Orlando Business Journal: Solar Center Completes Research Facility
UCF's Solar Energy Center has completed its newest research facility for testing energy improvements in new and existing homes.

St. Petersburg Times: St. Pete High Alum Attends U.N. Conference
Most people go to Cancun for beer and beach. Christine Dellert went to change the world.
-- This story features Christine Dellert, a UCF graduate who works in the Office of News & Information.

Palm Beach Post: Bye, Recession; Hello, Jobless Recovery
Economists long have used the phrase "jobless recovery" to describe a tepid turnaround. For 2011, a new bit of jargon may be in order: How about the "recovery-less recovery"?
-- This story quotes Sean Snaith, director of the Institute for Economic Competitiveness.
