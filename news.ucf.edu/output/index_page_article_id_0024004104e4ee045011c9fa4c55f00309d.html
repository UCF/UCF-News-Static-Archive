UCF Joins College Portrait Project Aimed at Informing Students and Parents

By Grant J. Heston Sept. 26, 2008

Related Contacts and Links:

  • College Portrait Web site

The University of Central Florida is the first university in the state to participate in a national Web project designed to help students and parents make well-informed decisions about U.S. public universities and colleges.
 
The project, known as the College Portrait, offers online views of public universities across the country, comparing data about financial aid, housing, degree programs and academic expectations, among others.

The Web-based tool is designed to give high school students, parents, guidance counselors and others a consistent and comprehensive look at critical data – in a user-friendly format -- to help them make institutional comparisons.
 
“Participation in the College Portrait is a terrific way to showcase our great university and enable prospective students to see a credible, balanced overview of the many opportunities available at the University of Central Florida.” said Al Harms, UCF’s vice president for strategic initiatives and special assistant to the president. “As many people already know, UCF Stands for Opportunity, and our College Portrait will help even more prospective students better understand this magnificent university and its many offerings and capabilities.”
 
The College Portrait is a product of the Voluntary System of Accountability project, a partnership between the National Association of State Universities and Land-Grant Colleges (NASULGC) and the American Association of State Colleges and Universities (AASCU) with funding from Lumina Foundation for Education.
 
Since unveiling the College Portrait report format and initiating the recruiting effort in November 2007, nearly 60 percent of the 520 member institutions of the two higher education associations have agreed to participate in the project and 194 have already posted a College Portrait.  This represents four-year institutions enrolling some 3 million undergraduates and nearly 60 percent of the total undergraduate enrollment in four-year public colleges and universities.
 
UCF provided data on the characteristics of the university and students, cost of attendance, student engagement with the learning process and core educational outcomes. In addition to students and families, the information is intended for policymakers, campus faculty and staff, the general public and other higher-education stakeholders.
 
To view UCF’s profile and those of other schools, visit the project online at www.voluntarysystem.org. UCF’s data was posted to the Web site on Sept. 2.
 
Based on Fall 2007 data, some of the UCF information showed that:
 
-- 53 percent of 2006-2007 full-time undergraduates received financial aid of some type, including need-based loans, work study and non need-based scholarships.

-- 61 percent of new freshmen live on campus, and 16 percent of all undergraduates live on campus.
 
-- UCF awarded 10,912 degrees in 2006-07, including 207 associate's degrees, 8,630 bachelor's degrees, 1,863 master's degrees and 212 doctoral degrees.
 
“The College Portrait places America’s four-year public colleges and universities at the forefront of the higher education accountability movement,” said Peter McPherson, president of NASULGC. “College Portrait is designed to be a trustworthy source of reliable data for prospective students, families, policymakers and the general public. It becomes the only voluntary accountability program that includes student learning outcomes and easily comparable information for a majority of the nation’s public four-year colleges and universities.”
 
Participating colleges and universities post data using an easy-to-read template to ensure that data has a consistent look for all participating institutions to make comparisons easier.
 
The Voluntary System of Accountability is designed to help institutions meet the following objectives:
 
-- Demonstrate accountability and stewardship to the public.
 
-- Measure educational outcomes to identify effective educational practices.
 
-- Assemble information that is accessible, understandable and comparable.
 
“No one should be surprised that public higher education has taken the lead on accountability,” said Constantine W. (Deno) Curris, president of AASCU.  “Our institutions have a long history of commitment to public accountability and learning outcomes.  College Portrait is being unveiled at a time when severe financial constraints for both families and state governments increase our obligation to provide dependable, accurate information in keeping with our public trust.”

UCF Stands For Opportunity: The University of Central Florida is a metropolitan research university that ranks as the 6th largest in the nation with more than 50,000 students. UCF's first classes were offered in 1968. The university offers impressive academic and research environments that power the region's economic development.
