UCF Event Encourages Students to Conserve Energy

By Courtney Gilmartin Aug. 29, 2008

Alaina Bernard, assistant director of the UCF Environmental Initiative, encouraged students to think green and conserve energy.

UCF administrators, faculty, and student leaders gathered in the Student Union on Wednesday to encourage students to think green and conserve energy.

Throughout the day, students were given tips on sustainable living. They received pins with eco-friendly messages, along with information about how they can help to strengthen UCF’s commitment to the environment and global climate change.

Resident assistants in the on-campus residence halls also spent the day providing energy conservation tips to the students on their floors. 

“Students have an opportunity to change our society,” said Alaina Bernard, assistant director of the UCF Environmental Initiative. “We are obviously in a climate crisis, and students have the power to learn, change the technology, and divert the course of action.”

The event was organized by the UCF Sustainability Alliance, a collaborative effort of students, faculty, administrators and staff, to kick off a multi-year environmental awareness campaign. 

The Environment and Global Climate Change is the university’s undergraduate education unifying theme that will be woven throughout the curriculum for the second straight year.  At least five co-curricular activities connected to the theme will be open to students, staff and faculty this semester.

“The UCF community has over 60,000 students, faculty and staff, and if everyone did one or two things, we could reduce energy costs and waste and help people become aware of their impact on sustainability,” said Alison Morrison-Shetlar, dean of Undergraduate Studies.

“UCF is a municipality that can set a model for others,” Bernard added. “We have the education and expertise to make a change.”

In the past year, thanks to students, faculty and staff, the amount of paper, aluminum cans, plastics and other materials recycled on campus has more than tripled. In the past two years, UCF has reduced its energy consumption by 22 percent. 

In 2005, UCF became the first university in Florida to install a green roof.  Last fall, UCF became the first university in the state to convert one of its fleet vehicles to a plug-in hybrid for limited all-electric driving.

UCF is constructing all new buildings – including the College of Medicine at Lake Nona – to at least the silver standard of the Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED) standards.

For more information about the Sustainability Alliance’s commitment to the environment, visit http://www.sustainabilityalliance.ucf.edu.

Tips for students to conserve energy:

Unplug unused items in your home
Ride mass transportation, including the UCF Shuttles and Lynx buses
Take shorter, less frequent showers
Wash clothes with cold water
Air dry clothes
Use fluorescent instead of incandescent lamps
Turn up your thermostat when you leave your home to limit air-conditioning
Use window shades to cool your residence
Share refrigerator space instead of having your own
Pick up trash when you see it on campus
Recycle
Use www.greenpc.ucf.edu to help reduce your computer’s energy use.
