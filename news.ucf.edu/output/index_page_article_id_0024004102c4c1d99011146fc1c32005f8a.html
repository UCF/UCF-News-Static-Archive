UCF Student Filmmakers Voted as Finalists for MTVu Award

By Kristi Bartlett May 24, 2007

Photo: Courtesy of Dastoli Digital

Robert, left, and James Dastoli, both of whom graduated this spring, are finalists in the MTVu "Best Filmmakers on Campsu" competition.

Full Photo

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Dastoli Digital

  • MTVu Best Filmmaker on Campus site

  • Dastoli Digital

  • Print this Page

The first time Robert and James Dastoli watched Star Wars, they knew that they wanted to make movies. Many years and many films later, the twins have been honored by MTVu as one of the five "Best Filmmakers on Campus."

The University of Central Florida Film majors were encouraged to build a profile and upload some of their short films to the Best Filmmaker on Campus Web site. They were voted into the top five filmmakers based on the 13 films that they uploaded to the site. These films include "Southwestern Orange County v. the Flying Saucers," the short promoted by MTVu.

As one of the five finalists, the Dastolis attended the Tribeca Film Festival in New York for a screening of the films. The winner of the Best Filmmaker on Campus will be announced at the MTV Movie Awards on Sunday, June 3. The winner will be flown to Los Angeles for the show and receive a Golden Popcorn statue and a development deal with MTV films worth up to $25,000.

The five films can be seen at www.bestfilmoncampus.com. Voting by visitors to the site will determine which film receives the MTV award.

The Dastolis came up with the premise for "Southwestern Orange County v. the Flying Saucers" after a ride on Men in Black: Alien Attack during a visit to Universal Studios Orlando. As they were exiting the ride, they remarked that it would be funny if there were flying saucers at a theme park. They used their prior experience as theme park employees to create some of the behind-the-scenes gags. The film used UCF students as main characters and extras and shot scenes at Pegasus Landing. 

The brothers started their film company Dastoli Digital in Connecticut in 1999. When their parents decided to relocate to Orlando, the brothers researched UCF and were pleased to discover that it had a film program that fit what they wanted.

The Dastolis graduated this spring and plan on moving to California over the summer to continue making films.

There is a great support system of UCF alumni in Los Angeles, Robert Dastoli said. We are looking forward to making our first feature-length film because we have only made shorts.

The brothers list George Lucas, Steven Spielberg and Alfred Hitchcock as some of their biggest influences.

For more information about Dastoli Digital, visit www.dastolidigital.com.
