Burnham Institute to Build New Campus Next to UCF College of Medicine

By Chad Binette and Tom Evelyn Updated 5 p.m., Aug. 23, 2006

Photo: Jerry Klein

UCF President John Hitt speaks about the partnership with the Burnham Institute for Medical Research at a news conference at Orlando International Airport on Wednesday with Gov. Jeb Bush and other local and state officials. It was announced earlier in Tallahassee that the Burnham Institute would locate its Florida operation in Orlando at the planned UCF Healthcare Campus at Lake Nona.

The California-based Burnham Institute for Medical Research will build its new Florida campus next to the University of Central Floridas College of Medicine at Lake Nona, institute president John Reed announced Wednesday.

The addition of the Burnham Institute and the expected inclusion of a veterans hospital virtually assure the creation of a medical city cluster of biotechnology companies and research, UCF President John Hitt said Wednesday.

The institute selected Orlando over Port St. Lucie. Burnhams decision creates many opportunities for partnerships with UCF researchers in areas such as cancer, neurodegenerative diseases and infectious diseases. UCF has been expanding its biomedical research programs through the Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences and other colleges and in anticipation of the new medical school, which is set to open in fall 2009.

We at UCF look forward to a very beneficial relationship with Burnham, Hitt said.

The Burnham Institute is an independent, not-for-profit biomedical research institution based in La Jolla, Calif., that focuses on cancer, neuroscience and aging, and infectious and inflammatory diseases. Burnham, which employs more than 550 scientists, has contributed to the development of new drugs for Alzheimer's disease, heart disease and several forms of cancer.

Burnham plans to build a 300-person research laboratory at the Lake Nona site. The institute will locate temporarily at the Central Florida Research Park adjacent to UCF until construction is completed.

Political leaders, including Orlando Mayor Buddy Dyer and Orange County Mayor Rich Crotty, lauded the economic development benefits of strengthening the life sciences sector in Orlando and bringing in many high-wage jobs. And Reed thanked government and university leaders for their support.

This exceptional partnership will allow us to drive our scientific discoveries more rapidly toward clinical proof of concept, narrowing the gap between discovery and the development of new ways of detecting, treating, curing and ultimately preventing diseases, Reed said.

Lake Nona is a burgeoning community in south Orlando just east of the Orlando International Airport. The medical college site is just south of State Road 417 at a new interchange between Boggy Creek and Narcoosee roads.

UCF buildings at Lake Nona will include the Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences and a medical library. Construction is scheduled to begin on the Burnett College in January.

UCF has started a search for a medical college dean and hopes to have the colleges top administrator in place by this fall. The dean would direct hiring of faculty and development of the colleges curriculum and accreditation.

The medical degree program calls for a four-year curriculum that will eventually produce about 120 medical graduates each year. It will capitalize on the universitys existing strengths in biomedical sciences, modeling and simulation, and optics and photonics. Other programs in materials science, psychology, chemistry, film and digital media, and nursing also will support the medical curriculum.

Hitt said that one of his five goals for the university is to become Americas leading partnership university, and the Burnham ties will help UCF reach that goal.

We look for beneficial partnerships, true partnerships where there is mutual benefit in many, many sectors, Hitt said. I cant think of one thats going to be better all around than our partnership with Burnham.

UCF signed a joint cooperation agreement in March to foster collaborative research with the Scripps Research Institute. UCF and Scripps share many common research interests, including working toward better therapies for cardiovascular disease, cancer and neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimers.

UCF also is partnering with the University of Puerto Rico School of Medicine to collaborate on research and the curriculum and to provide student and faculty exchanges. The University of Florida also will be a partner in the medical research at Lake Nona.

Florida Hospital and Orlando Regional Healthcare, two of the largest medical systems in the country, have agreed to establish 95 new medical residencies in cooperation with the medical college. The hospitals also will pay 40 percent of the salaries for 50 full-time clinical faculty members and provide support staff and clinical and office space.

UCF has raised more than $100 million for construction of the medical and biomedical sciences college and library. The money includes private donations, state matching funds and the value of the Tavistock Groups gift of 50 acres at Lake Nona. Tavistock also led the group of donors with a pledge of $12.5 million for the medical college.

For more information about the Burnham Institute of Medical Research, go to www.burnham.org.
