Med School Leads Way as UCF Raises $356 Million in First Capital Campaign

By Chad Binette Nov. 30, 2006

Photo: Courtesy of Rosen College of Hospitality Management

UCF trustee Harris Rosen and his wife, Trisha, donated $38.6 million, including state matching funds, to build the Rosen College of Hospitality Management’s new campus near International Drive and to endow a scholarship fund.

The University of Central Florida received $356 million in donations in its first-ever capital campaign, exceeding the initial goal by more than $200 million, UCF Vice President for Alumni Relations and Development Bob Holmes announced today during a Board of Trustees meeting.

The community's support during UCF's first capital campaign far exceeded expectations as donors responded to the university's vision of improving classroom and laboratory facilities and enriching the quality of academic programs. Many of the donations are invested in endowments that support faculty and academic programs over the long term.

During the eight-year "Central to Our Future" campaign, UCF raised $80.3 million toward the medical college, $60 million for the new Rosen College of Hospitality Management campus and academic programs, $39 million for College of Business Administration programs and facilities and $27.6 million for UCF Athletics.

The campaign's success outpaced the $250 million goal that was announced in October 2005 and the $108 million goal that was set when the campaign started in 1998. The campaign includes gifts pledged through June 30.

"The number and variety of donors who invested their money and confidence in our university is testimony to the quality of our institution and its students, faculty and staff," said Al Weiss, the UCF trustee and president of Worldwide Operations for Walt Disney Parks and Resorts who chaired the campaign. "Our donors' generous support ensures that more students have access to a quality education and gives faculty members the resources they need to conduct vital research."

Donations for the College of Medicine, which will be located at Lake Nona near Orlando International Airport, accounted for nearly a third of the total amount pledged during the campaign. Eighteen "founders" committed $1 million or more toward the college. The donations include a $25 million gift, including state matching funds, from the Tavistock Group, the developers of Lake Nona. The Tavistock Group also contributed 50 acres of land valued at $8 million.

Other gifts that will benefit the medical college include $8 million from Alan Ginsburg and $5 million from Steve and Paula Walsh. The Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences, which also will be at Lake Nona, received a $20 million gift from Al and Nancy Burnett. All of those amounts include state matching funds.

"The strong support of our community, alumni and friends was critical in gaining approval for our medical college," UCF President John Hitt said. "Their support provides inspiration and motivation as we prepare to give students opportunities for a first-class medical education and increase the quality of health care in the region and the state."

Holmes said the relationships with donors that Hitt has built and strengthened during his 14 years as president were critical to the campaign's success. Holmes also praised Provost Terry Hickey's efforts related to the College of Medicine and the Health Sciences Campus at Lake Nona and the leadership of Associate Vice President for Development Kim Minana, a 1976 UCF graduate who directed the campaign.

More than 200 volunteers facilitated introductions to potential donors and solicited gifts on behalf of the university. In addition to Weiss, Board of Trustees Chair Richard Nunis and Vice Chair Thomas Yochum, Van Bogan and John Lowndes led the campaign.

"Their commitment to the campaign and the UCF Foundation helped to lay the groundwork for long-term philanthropic growth at the university," Holmes said.

Donations received by the campaign's other top beneficiaries included:

Rosen College of Hospitality Management -- UCF trustee Harris Rosen and his wife, Trisha, donated $38.6 million, including state matching funds, to build the college's new campus near International Drive and to endow a scholarship fund. The Orange County Convention and Visitors Bureau gave $10 million, including state matching funds, to set up five eminent scholar endowed chairs. Anheuser Busch, Darden Restaurants, Walt Disney World and Universal Orlando contributed a total of $9.2 million, including state matching funds, for academic programs.

College of Business Administration -- Orlando Magic owner Rich DeVos contributed $16.5 million to the DeVos Sport Business Management Program and National Consortium for Academics and Sport. Kenneth G. Dixon, a Kissimmee accountant and real estate developer who is a 1975 UCF graduate, gave more than $5 million, including state matching funds, to endow the Kenneth G. Dixon School of Accounting and support faculty. Dixon's gift is the largest single cash gift the university has received from an alumnus. Also, Dr. Phillips Inc. donated $5 million, with state matching funds, to establish the Dr. P. Phillips School of Real Estate.

UCF Athletics -- UCF's move to Conference USA and growing athletics program attracted the interest of many donors. Gifts to athletics facilities totaled $18.1 million, and annual support for student scholarships totaled $8.9 million during the campaign. An anonymous gift of $2 million led fund-raising for the new on-campus football stadium. UCF named a new field house in honor of Anthony and Sonja Nicholson after the couple gave $2 million toward its construction. Also, the Wayne M. Densch Charitable Trust donated $2.5 million to help build the Wayne Densch Sports Center.
