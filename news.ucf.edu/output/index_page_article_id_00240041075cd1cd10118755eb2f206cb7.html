UCF to Host Conference USA Women's Basketball Championship

By UCF Staff March 3, 2008

Photo: Courtesy of UCF Athletics

The Conference USA women's basketball tournament will be played March 6 to 9 at the UCF Arena.

Full Photo

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Joe Hornstein, UCF Athletics

    • 407-823-2729

  • UCF Athletics and Tournament Information

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Florida will welcome players and fans from around the country as host of the 2008 Conference USA Women’s Basketball Championship from Thursday, March 6, to Sunday, March 9, at the UCF Arena.

The 11-game tournament will feature matchups involving all 12 C-USA women’s basketball teams and exciting promotions for UCF students and staff, including free food, giveaways and drawings for trips to Las Vegas and Key West.

The Knights tip off against No. 5 seed Southern Mississippi at 5:30 p.m. Thursday. The winner will face the Houston Cougars, who earned a first-round bye, at 5:30 p.m. Friday.

Led by Tia Lewis and Emma Cannon, two of the league’s top freshmen, the Knights finished the regular season 10-19, earning the team’s highest win total in the past three seasons. Lewis is fifth in the nation in rebounds, averaging 11.6 per game. The Knights’ forward/center broke UCF’s single-season rebound record during Saturday’s season finale against Memphis. Cannon averages 11.5 points and 8.6 rebounds a game.

There are seven other freshmen on the Knights’ roster, which is the youngest in the nation.

Under first-year head coach Joi Williams the team will battle for a guaranteed spot in the 2008 NCAA women’s basketball tournament. ESPN2 will broadcast the final C-USA tournament game live from UCF at 3:30 p.m. Sunday.

UCF invites fans to come out for an entire weekend of basketball action and promotional giveaways. UCF students get in free with a student ID. Tickets are available for $10 per two-game session or $50 for the entire tournament.

Thursday night is College Student Night, and it will feature games at 5:30 and 8 p.m. The first 250 fans will receive Go Knights bracelets, and the first 200 UCF students will receive free Knightmare shirts and Firehouse subs. In addition to the giveaways, one lucky student will win a trip for two to Las Vegas, courtesy of Domino’s Pizza and Cold Stone Creamery. There will also be an iPod giveaway from Coke.

On Friday night, fans can enter for a trip for two to travel with the UCF football team to next season’s game against the University of Miami Hurricanes. The road trip includes two tickets to the UCF-Miami game at Dolphins Stadium on Oct. 11 and accommodations at the Knights’ team hotel.

On Saturday, at the 3 and 5:30 p.m. games, there will be a drawing to win tickets to the 2008 NCAA Women’s Final Four in Tampa. The first 250 fans will receive Go Knights bracelets.

Wrapping up the tournament on Sunday, during the final game at 3:30 p.m., one lucky fan will win a three-day, two-night stay at the Radisson in Key West, and the first 500 fans receive a free UCF seat cushion. ESPN2 will broadcast the game live.

On Thursday and Friday, UCF Faculty and Staff can stop by the Arena for a lunch break, including pizza, Coke and ice cream from Cold Stone Creamery, served at the front entrance from 11:30 a.m. to 12:30 p.m. Just show your UCF I.D. or business card for entry. Staff present during the noon games have a chance to win a 2008-09 academic year UCF parking pass.    

The new, 10,000-seat UCF Arena is a staple of the developing north end of the UCF campus. The Knights Plaza area also includes Bright House Networks Stadium – home of the 2007 C-USA football champion Knights – four Towers residence halls, three parking garages and retail outlets offering pizza, subs, ice cream, UCF apparel and more.

For a complete schedule of this weekend’s games and giveaways, visit www.ucfathletics.com.
