New UCF Director of Athletics Keith R. Tribble to Speak at Chamber Luncheon

By Chad Binette July 21, 2006

Photo: Jacque Brund

University of Central Florida director of athletics Keith R. Tribble will be the featured speaker at the African American Chamber of Commerces summer M-Powerment luncheon Thursday, July 27.

The luncheon will be from 11:30 a.m. to 1:30 p.m. at the Wyndham Orlando Resort, 8001 International Drive. Tribble will speak at about 12:15 p.m.

Tribble began working as UCFs athletics director in June. He directs an intercollegiate athletics department that includes nearly 600 student-athletes in 17 varsity sports.

Before coming to UCF, Tribble served for 13 years as chief executive officer of the Orange Bowl Committee in Miami. He oversaw four national championship games during his tenure there. In the past several years, Tribble has been named among the Most Influential Minorities in Sports by Sports Illustrated and one of the Most Powerful Blacks in Sports by Black Enterprise magazine.

The African American Chamber of Commerce of Central Florida serves as an advocacy organization to unify, promote and empower the African-American community in the areas of programming, economic development, education and training.

The luncheon is open to the public. Go to www.blackcommerce.org to register or for more information.
