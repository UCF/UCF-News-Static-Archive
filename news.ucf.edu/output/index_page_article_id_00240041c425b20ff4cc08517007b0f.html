UCF Students, Employees Help at National Gandhi Day of Service

By UCF Staff Oct. 8, 2004

Photo: Courtesy of Girl Scouts of Citrus Council

Volunteers from UCF and several other agencies and companies helped to rebuild the Girl Scouts Camp in Oviedo. The camp was damaged during the recent hurricanes.

More than 275 volunteers from UCF, area companies and other universities spruced up the Pine Hills YMCA and the Girl Scouts Camp in Oviedo last weekend as part of the National Gandhi Day of Service celebration.

Volunteers cleared brush on the YMCA property, painted the gymnasium and baseball dugouts and added wood to the playground. At the Girl Scouts Camp, volunteers erected tents, cleared debris and restored order to campsites that had been damaged by hurricanes.

About 40 volunteers from Sangam, the Indian Student Association at UCF, participated in the cleanup efforts on Saturday. The association became involved because it wanted to get to know the community better and because volunteering makes you feel satisfied and good, said Kiran Anna, president of Sangam.

Several UCF employees also participated in the volunteer efforts.

Im glad I came out today, because the efforts of the volunteers make a difference to the Pine Hills Y and the facilities here, said Mark Wray of the UCF Division of Finance and Accounting. I remember that there are people less fortunate then me and want to do whatever I can to help.

Groundskeepers at the Girl Scouts Camp told volunteers that they completed about four weeks worth of work and that, because of their efforts, the campground could open much sooner than expected.

The Orlando celebration of the National Gandhi Day of Service began at the UCF Barbara Ying International Center with a presentation by Dave Krepcho, director of the Second Harvest Food Bank of Central Florida. Krepcho asked audience members to close their eyes and imagine that they were driving 60 mph on a highway, passing by hungry children standing shoulder to shoulder. It would take a half-hour to drive by all of the number of children who are hungry every day in the United States, he said.

A local fifth-grade student dressed up as Gandhi and spoke about Gandhis work in Africa and India and how he inspired other leaders, such as Nelson Mandela and Martin Luther King Jr. The winners of an essay contest also were honored during the kickoff event.

The national Gandhi Day celebration is organized by the South Asian American Leaders of Tomorrow (SAALT) and is sponsored locally by a civic and community-service organization called United We Stand for Non Violence.

More than 150 universities and more than 50 professional organizations nationwide participate in annual Gandhi Day celebrations. UCF participated for the first time in 2003. For more information on the events, go to www.gandhiday.org .
