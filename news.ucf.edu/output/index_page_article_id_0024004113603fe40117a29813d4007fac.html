Election 2008: UCF Professors Can Speak On Candidates, Issues

Updated May 19, 2008

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Chad Binette, News and Information

    • 407-823-6312

  • Print this Page

Several professors at the University of Central Florida are available to talk with reporters as experts on the presidential elections.

Reporters are invited to contact faculty members directly. They also may contact the News & Information office for assistance in reaching professors. Assistant Director Chad Binette can be reached at 407-823-6312 (office), 407-247-8777 (cell) or cbinette@mail.ucf.edu. Writer Christine Dellert can be reached at 407-823-2947 (office) or cdellert@mail.ucf.edu.

Presidential primaries and the impact of both parties’ decisions to withhold Florida delegates:

Aubrey Jewett, associate professor of Political Science, can discuss issues related to Florida politics, policies and elections. He is the co-author of the recently published "Politics in Florida, 2nd edition." 407-823-6030 (office), 407-222-7582 (cell) or ajewett@mail.ucf.edu.

Scot Schraufnagel, assistant professor of Political Science, 386-506-4007 (office), 407-913-1902 (cell) or sschrauf@mail.ucf.edu.

Barack Obama:

Martin Dupuis, associate professor of Political Science, volunteered with Obama's U.S. Senate campaign in Illinois, organizing community fundraisers and outreach events at Western Illinois University. He is the lead author of "Barack Obama: The New Face of American Politics," a book that analyzes Obama's success then, tying it to his widespread appeal today and his ability to connect with a voting population that transcends race, class and gender, 407-823-5948 (office), 407-595-3739 (cell) or mdupuis@mail.ucf.edu.

Campaign strategies:

Roger Handberg, professor of Political Science and department chair, teaches presidential campaigning, media and politics, security policy, space policy, government and business and science policy, 407-823-2608 (office) or handberg@mail.ucf.edu.

Lisa Mills, assistant professor of Film, wrote her Ph.D. disseration on political communication with a specialty in campaign strategies, including political ads, debates and Web campaigning, 407-823-3606 (office), 407-227-5111 (cell), lmills@mail.ucf.edu

Candidate personalities and images:

Terri Fine, professor of Political Science, research interests include political parties, minority politics and gender and politics, 407-823-2081 (office), 407-963-6094 (cell) or tfine@mail.ucf.edu.

Terrorism:

Roger Handberg, professor of Political Science and department chair, teaches presidential campaigning, media and politics, security policy, space policy, government and business and science policy, 407-823-2608 (office) or handberg@mail.ucf.edu.

Economic aspects of presidential candidates’ proposals:

Mark Soskin, associate professor of Economics, can discuss the potential impact of proposals pertaining to health care reform, Social Security, the "fair tax," immigration policies and other key issues, 386-506-4015 (office), 386-290-3481 (cell) or soskin@mail.ucf.edu

UCF Stands For Opportunity: Established in 1963, the University of Central Florida is a diverse metropolitan research university that ranks as the 6th-largest in the country with more than 48,000 students. Located in Orlando, Fla., UCF offers high-quality undergraduate and graduate education, student development and continuing education, while conducting cutting-edge research that powers the region's economic development.
