UCFs Growing Dance Program to Hold First Concert April 19 to 22

By Kathleen Lake April 3, 2007

Photo: Jay Spence

Dancer and UCF Faculty member Brian Vernon directs A Celebration of Central Florida Dance, opening April 19

The University of Central Floridas expanding dance program will hold its first concert, A Celebration of Central Florida Dance, Thursday, April 19, through Sunday, April 22, at Orlando Repertory Theatre.

The concert will feature an exciting, creative synthesis of music, movement and design. Orlando Ballet will perform a work by George Balanchine, and Kip Watson, founder of Southern Ballet Theatre, and Eliza Harwood-Watson will be featured along with original works by UCF dancers, faculty and choreographers.

A Celebration of Central Florida Dance, which is sponsored by Johnny Holloway, will be performed at 8 p.m. April 19, 20 and 21 and at 2 p.m. April 22. Opening night will begin at 7 p.m. with a reception and awards ceremony honoring a significant contributor to Central Florida dance. Tickets cost $15 each.

The annual concert, which will become part of the UCF Theatre Departments annual subscription series, is another milestone in the growth of the dance program. Dance became a minor at UCF in fall 2005, and the program has grown from 16 students then to 70 this spring.

Students can study jazz and tap dance in addition to modern dance and ballet. Director Brian Vernon, an associate professor and coordinator of dance at UCF, has developed 11 courses, including Dance History, Improvisation/Composition I and World Dance.

Vernon said the new minor and the concert represent strong steps in completing the spectrum of the arts at UCF Conservatory Theatre.

Before, dance helped to facilitate other programs, but now we are forging a distinct identity, he said.  The popularity of the dance minor is paving the way for the growth of dance in its own right.

The concert, A Celebration of Central Florida Dance, provides unique opportunities for audiences and artists, Vernon said.

The benefit to students is that theyre being afforded the chance to share the stage with the best of Floridas professional dance community, he said. And in addition to ballet, swing, tap, jazz and West African dances, audiences will witness the debut of pre-professional talent that will become the next generation of professional dancers. All of Central Florida wins because more talent is being developed and celebrated in the region.

Reservations are recommended and are available through the UCF Conservatory Theatre box office by calling 407-823-1500. Patrons with disabilities may call the box office ahead for assistance and accommodations.
