Two Iraqis to Speak to UCF Class About Improved Lives of Women in Iraq

By UCF Staff Aug. 26, 2004

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Sarah Mathews Bascom (Iraq-America Freedom Alliance)

    • 850-294-6636

  • Chad Binette

    • 407-823-6312

  • Iraq-America Freedom Alliance

  • Print this Page

Two women whose families suffered under Saddam Husseins brutal regime will visit a UCF political science class this afternoon to discuss how Iraqi womens lives improved after coalition troops liberated the country.

 

Taghreed Al-Qaraghuli and Surood Ahmed Falih will speak at 1:30 p.m. today, Aug. 26, to The Causes of War class taught by Professor Bernadette Jungblut. Their presentation will be in Room 219 of the Classroom I building on the UCF main campus. Reporters are invited to attend.

 

During their visit to the United States, Al-Qaraghuli and Falih are discussing the changing roles of women in Iraq and are thanking Americans for liberating the country. The tour is sponsored by the Iraq-America Freedom Alliance, a nonpartisan coalition of Iraqi and American groups and individuals committed to fostering goodwill between the two nations and supporting Iraqs reconstruction and movement toward democracy.

 

Al-Qaraghuli, who was born and raised in Baghdad, graduated from Al-Athar University College in Baghdad with a bachelors degree in English literature. Under the Baath regime, she wanted to pursue a masters degree but was prevented from doing that because she was not a member of the Baath party. Now the administrative director of the Iraqi Independent Democrats, she typed the new Iraqi constitution. Al-Qaraghuli is also an active member of the Iraqi Independent Womens Group, a political party formed after the liberation to promote equal rights for Iraqis.

 

Surood Ahmed Falih, a Kurd from the city of Kirkuk, received a degree in agronomy from the University of Mosul. During the 1991 uprising, she was injured while fleeing Kirkuk after Saddam Husseins Republican Guard started slaughtering the cities residents, including her sister, aunt and stepmother. After the liberation of Iraq, she moved back to Kirkuk and reunited with her family. Her goal is to help train women to become involved in the political process.

 

Go to www.untoldiraq.org for more information on the Iraq-America Freedom Alliance.
