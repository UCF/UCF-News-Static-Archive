UCF Trustees Discuss Med School Economic Impact, Approve New Degree Programs

By Chad Binette and Tom Evelyn March 16, 2006

Photo: Jerry Klein

One of the slides in consultant Donna Arduin's presentation showed how the presence of a medical college and life sciences cluster increases research grants awarded in a community.

A University of Central Florida medical college would be the right investment at the right time with the right partners for the State of Florida, consultant Donna Arduin told the UCF Board of Trustees on Thursday.

Arduin outlined the extensive economic benefits of a proposed medical college during the trustees regular meeting.

A study by her firm, Arduin, Laffer & Moore Econometrics, and the Milken Institute determined that a college of medicine alone could generate $1.4 billion in economic activity and add more than 6,400 jobs in Central Florida. The impact could be as much as $6.4 billion and nearly 26,000 jobs if a life-sciences cluster of research and business develops around the med school, similar to the clusters that have formed in other cities with med schools.

UCF President John Hitt also stressed the proposed medical colleges role in improving health care in the community. Florida Hospital and Orlando Regional Medical Center have agreed to add and pay for 95 residencies initially to address the shortage of doctors in the area.

Including state matching funds and the value of the Tavistock Groups pledge of 50 acres at Lake Nona, UCF has received $97.3 million of pledges for the medical college. A $1 million donation from Walt Disney World was announced during the trustees meeting Thursday.

The state Board of Governors, which oversees Floridas 11 public universities, is scheduled to vote March 23 on UCFs proposal to create a med school. The Board of Governors will meet at Florida State University in Tallahassee.

On Thursday, the Board of Trustees also approved new bachelor degree programs in event management, religious studies and real estate that will begin this summer.

The event management degree will help students prepare for managing conventions, conferences, festivals and sporting events. The religious studies program, which examines the academic and non-sectarian aspects of religions in the world, will move up from a track within humanities to a full degree offered through the Department of Philosophy. The new Dr. Phillips School of Real Estate will offer a degree that will help students prepare for careers as construction managers, property managers and real estate appraisers and assessors.

In other action, the Board of Trustees:

-- Increased the health, transportation access and parking decal fees. The fee increases -- $1.35 a credit hour for health, $1.50 a credit hour for transportation and 5 percent for parking -- will take effect in the fall.

The increases are necessary because of inflation and increased demands for services from students, said Vice President for Finance and Administration Bill Merck. For example, the university wants to add more buses to popular UCF Shuttle routes, as ridership increased 34 percent from February 2005 to February 2006.

The equipment fee will be established on a case-by-case basis and only will affect students who use equipment supported by the fee. Music majors, for example, might pay the equipment fee to help purchase new instruments. Most UCF students will not be affected by the fee.

-- Approved a minor amendment to the campus master plan to add office and laboratory space for the Advanced Materials Processing and Analysis Center and an expansion to the University Police building.

-- Welcomed UCF alumnus Alan Florez during his first meeting as a trustee.

-- Congratulated several students who were selected to the Order of Pegasus, which recognizes outstanding academic achievements, leadership and community service. The following students were selected this year: graduate students Jennifer Curry and Ravi Todi and undergraduates Maria Noel Blanchet, Katherine Brown, Arianne Buchanan, Morgan Cyphers, Sohang Gandhi, Jennifer Hartzler, Melissa Lanzone, Amy Schwartz, Trent Scott, Kelly Simonz and Alison Valentine.
