School of Social Work Appoints Director

By Karen Guin August 11, 2003

Photo: COHPA Photo

R.Paul Maiden.

Full Photo

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Print this Page

R. Paul Maiden, a member of the faculty since 1999 and a licensed clinical social worker with more than 25 years of national and international experience in social work and education, has been appointed director of the School of Social Work. As director, Maiden will oversee the schools academic programs in social work, participation in the College of Health and Public Affairs doctoral program in public affairs, and 15 faculty members who are actively engaged in research and community-based projects. He will also continue to direct the schools study abroad program in South Africa. Previously, he developed and coordinated the graduate certificate program in addictions and served as graduate coordinator of the Masters in Social Work program. From 1986 to 1999, Maiden was chair of the occupational social work program and a faculty member at the Jane Addams College of Social Work at the University of Illinois at Chicago. While there he developed curricula in substance abuse treatment, employee assistance programs, managed care and occupational policy and services. He also directed a faculty exchange program in South Africa, where he was a visiting scholar on several occasions, and initiated and directed a scholarship program for members of the South African Black Social Workers Association. He has also held faculty positions at the Virginia Commonwealth University in Richmond, Va., and Abilene Christian University in Abilene, Texas, and he was a doctoral fellow at the University of Maryland at Baltimore. Maiden has published extensively in the areas of employee assistance programs, substance abuse, work-place legislation, evaluation of work-based human services, AIDS in the work place, alcohol abuse and domestic violence, and managed behavioral health care. He was recently appointed editor of the journal Employee Assistance Quarterly and serves on the editorial board of the employee assistance program magazine EAP Digest. He has consulted with a wide range of organizations and employers in the public and private sectors on the development, administration and evaluation of employee assistance and managed-care programs, work-place policies and education programs. He has also consulted with organizations in southern Africa on the development of employee assistance programs to address alcohol and drug abuse, mental health problems, HIV/AIDS and related health-care costs. Maiden earned his doctorate in social work management and policy from the University of Maryland in Baltimore; masters degree in social work from the University of Tennessee in Knoxville, Tenn.; and bachelors degree in communications and psychology from Angelo State University in San Angelo, Texas.
