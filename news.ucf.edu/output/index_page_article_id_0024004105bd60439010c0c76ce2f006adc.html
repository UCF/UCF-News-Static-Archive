UCF President Hitt Returns to Work After Treatment for Blood Clot

By Tom Evelyn Aug. 28, 2006

Photo: Florida Hospital

UCF President John Hitt, center, discusses his treatment for a blood clot at Florida Hospital Orlando during a news conference on Monday. He was joined by his physicians, Dr. Jose Arias, left, and Dr. Mark Milunski, right.

University of Central Florida President John Hitt is scheduled to return to work full time Tuesday after being treated over the weekend for a small blood clot in a heart artery.

Im very happy to be here today and feeling great, Hitt said during a news conference Monday at Florida Hospital Orlando. He was joined by his cardiologist, Dr. Mark Milunski, who said he expected Hitt to have another full recovery.

I have a lot of things I want to accomplish yet at UCF, Hitt said. So Im delighted that it appears that I am as able now as I was before the heart attack (in June) to continue that agenda.

Hitt, 65, has been resting at home since being discharged from the hospital Sunday afternoon. He did not have a heart attack Friday but had symptoms similar to the attack he had June 24.

On Friday, he contacted his doctor after feeling mild, intermittent chest discomfort over several days. Milunski recommended that he come in for tests as a precaution. Hitt drove to Florida Hospital, where he received a heart catheterization and angiography that revealed the blood clot partially blocking one of the three stents inserted in June after his heart attack.

Doctors on Friday removed the clot and inserted two new stents. Additional tests are being conducted to determine the cause of the clot and any further treatments that may be necessary, said Dr. Jose Arias, who also treated Hitt.

I cant emphasize to you enough how well he has done from a cardiac standpoint to come from what was really a tough situation back in June, Milunski said. Not only is the heart functioning normally, but its overall physical functional status is excellent.

Hitt had a heart attack two months ago while riding his bicycle in the Central Florida Research Park adjacent to the East Orlando UCF campus. He called 911 and was rushed to Florida Hospital. Hitt credited his quick care, being in good shape and having a cell phone on him with saving his life.

Hitt has lost more than 100 pounds over the past several years since beginning a regular exercise program and following a healthy diet. He has encouraged others to monitor their health.

Since becoming UCFs fourth president in 1992, Hitt has helped the university achieve many significant milestones, most recently securing approval to establish a College of Medicine, for which he helped raise more than $50 million in donations. He was also instrumental in persuading the Burnham Institute for Medical Research to locate its Florida operation next to the college at the UCF Healthcare Campus at Lake Nona.

During his tenure, UCFs enrollment has more than doubled to about 47,000 this fall. Over the same period, he has conferred more than 103,000 degrees, about two-thirds of the universitys total.

Among Hitts other accomplishments in recent years are the openings of the Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy in 2005 in downtown Orlando and the Rosen College of Hospitality Management campus in 2004 near International Drive. The university also plans to open an on-campus football stadium by next year.
