The stories of Central Florida African-Americans who demonstrated at white-only lunch counters and aided the civil rights movement in many other ways will be preserved in a new archive at the University of Central Florida library.

UCF public history professor Vibert White is overseeing interviews with 150 to 200 people who participated in the local civil rights movement. The interviews will be conducted this year and early next year by White, community leaders and students from UCF and the Florida A&M University law school.

The group plans to produce a television documentary and a traveling museum exhibition in addition to archiving the videotaped interviews at the library.
