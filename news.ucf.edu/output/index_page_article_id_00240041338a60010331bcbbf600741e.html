UCF Institute Gives NBA an A Grade for Race Hiring Practices

By UCF Staff May 4, 2005

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Jay Porcher Yasuda

    • 407-895-1600

  • NBA Racial and Gender Report Card

  • Print this Page

A report card issued by the University of Central Floridas Institute for Diversity and Ethics in Sport showed that the National Basketball Association continues to be the industry leader in race and gender hiring practices.

 

The NBA received an A for race and a B for gender in the study, which was released today by institute director Richard Lapchick. The NBA was the only mens league to receive an A for race, and its overall grade of B-plus topped mens leagues.

 

The annual Racial and Gender Report Card asks, Are we playing fair when it comes to sports? Does everyone, regardless of race or gender, have a chance to make and run the team? The study examines racial breakdowns of players, managers, coaches, top team administrators and support staff, along with the management of the NBA league office.

 

Findings of the NBA report, which analyzed data for the 2003-04 season, show that:

 

-- While the percentage of white men playing in the NBA increased, 78 percent of the players were people of color. That is the highest percentage among the professional sports covered by the report cards.

 

-- Professional opportunities for people of color in the NBA league offices -- at 29 percent -- were the best among the mens leagues. That was the highest percentage in the NBAs history.

 

-- Professional opportunities for women in the mens leagues were also best in the NBA league office, where 43 percent of the professionals were women.

 

The Institute for Diversity and Ethics in Sport publishes the Racial and Gender Report Card to indicate areas of improvement, stagnation and regression in the racial and gender composition of professional and college sports personnel and to help improve integration in front office and college athletics department positions.

 

To view the full report, go to http://www.bus.ucf.edu/sport/cgi-bin/site/sitew.cgi?page=/ides/index.htx
