Jehan Sadat, Former First Lady of Egypt, to Speak Nov. 13 at UCF

By Mark Freeman Nov. 9, 2007

Photo: Courtesy of UCF Global Perspectives

Jehan Sadat is a former first lady of Egypt who championed her late husband's drive for peace.

Jehan Sadat, a former first lady of Egypt and widow of the late President Anwar Sadat, will discuss “Women Shaping and Changing the World” on Tuesday, Nov. 13, at the University of Central Florida.

Sadat will speak at 2 p.m. in the Pegasus Ballroom of the Student Union. The forum, organized by the UCF Global Perspectives Office, is free and open to the public.

Sadat was among the first to visit and comfort the wounded on the Suez front of the 1967 War with Israel. In 1972 she founded the Wafa Wa Amal (Faith & Hope) Rehabilitation Center, which is a fully integrated city for handicapped war veterans and civilians, complete with clinics, rehabilitation centers, vocational training and recreation facilities. The center, the first of its kind in Egypt, can accommodate 2,000 patients and depends completely on donations from all over the world.

In 1977, Sadat initiated the concept behind S.O.S. Children's Villages in Egypt, which are designed to offer orphans the opportunity to lead a normal family life within a happy and healthy atmosphere. The first of these was established in Cairo, followed by ones in Alexandria and Tanta, and many more are planned around the country.

Sadat has founded or headed many other charitable organizations and associations, including the Egyptian Red Crescent Society, which cares for disaster victims and promotes international humanitarian law and values; Egyptian Blood Bank Society; Egyptian Society for Cancer Patients; Egyptian Society for Biomedical Engineering; Scientific Association for Egyptian Women; Higher Committee for Children; and Egyptian Society for the Welfare of University and Higher Institute Students.

In addition to her work with these organizations, Sadat encouraged and championed her late husband's drive for peace, which helped him earn a Nobel Peace Prize in 1978.

Sadat has taught at Cairo University and has been a visiting professor at American University in Washington, D.C., the University of South Carolina and the University of Maryland. She earned her Ph.D. from Cairo University.

In her autobiography, “A Woman of Egypt,” Sadat candidly shares her story as patriot, wife, mother, feminist and first lady. She is also in the process of building a museum in Cairo in her late husband’s honor.

In addition to the UCF Global Perspectives Office, sponsors of this forum include the UCF Student Government Association, Islamic Society of Central Florida, UCF Office of International Studies, UCF Burnett Honors College, Arab American Community Center of Central Florida, UCF Political Science Department, UCF Middle Eastern Studies Program, UCF Diplomacy Program, UCF International Services Center and the Global Connections Foundation.
