UCF Honors Student Wins Public Service Award from Junior Achievement

By Kristi Bartlett July 7, 2006

Photo: Courtesy of Burnett Honors College

Brittany Camp (center) joined Kelly Astro, director of Research and Civic Engagement for the Burnett Honors College, and college Dean Alvin Wang at the Junior Achievement award ceremony.

Full Photo

Brittany Camp, a senior at the University of Central Floridas Burnett Honors College, was presented with the Jefferson Award for Public Service at the annual Junior Achievement meeting last month.

Much of Camps extracurricular work revolves around the Burnett Honors Colleges partnership with Junior Achievement, an organization that educates and inspires youths to improve the quality of their lives. The partnership began in 2004 and now has more than 400 student participants from UCF.

Camp, of Cape Coral, has taught more than 11 classes for Junior Achievement, which develops programs for grades one through 12. The Burnett Honors College works with the JA elementary school program. Lessons focus on the importance of becoming engaged citizens and socially responsible adults. The six JA themes are: Ourselves, Our Families, Our Community, Our City, Our Region and Our Nation.

My biggest passion has always been tutoring and teaching, Camp said. Ive had many wonderful experiences through JA. My volunteer work is one of the things Im most proud of, so receiving such a prestigious award is a huge honor.

Camp is majoring in creative writing and history. As the Honors Educational Reach Out coordinator, she recruits, trains and coordinates volunteers for two of the civic engagement programs. Last year she founded one of those programs, Pineloch Reading Buddies, which has recruited more than 25 Burnett Honors College students to serve as reading tutors in a local elementary school.

I was excited when I was chosen as the HERO coordinator, but I wanted to expand our program offerings, Camp said. Pineloch seemed like a perfect fit, and I am thrilled with the response Ive received from honors students."

Camp plans to study abroad after graduation and would like to complete a masters program in history in Ireland. She intends to return to the United States to teach high school English and history.
