Marriott Gives $100,000 to Hospitality

By Tom Evelyn September 29, 2003

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Print this Page

The J. Willard and Alice S. Marriott Foundation donated $100,000 to the University of Central Florida Rosen School of Hospitality Management, joining a list of premier hospitality companies supporting the school and hospitality education in Central Florida. Foundation trustee and Marriott International Chairman and CEO J.W. Marriott Jr. presented the gift Sept. 26, at the dedication ceremony of Grande Lakes Orlando. Gov. Jeb Bush attended the ceremony dedicating the 1,000-room JW Marriott, 584-room Ritz-Carlton, 40,000-square-foot Ritz-Carlton Spa and an 18-hole championship Greg Norman designed golf course. The UCF Rosen School of Hospitality Management will open its new, 159,000-square-foot state-of-the-art facility on Universal Boulevard near International Drive in January. The $28.1 million facility, which will feature classrooms, laboratories, conference rooms and a library, will allow the Rosen School, now based on UCFÂ’s main Orlando campus, to expand its offerings and enrollment. The Marriott Foundation gift will establish the J. Willard and Alice S. Marriott Academic Excellence Fund. Earnings on the endowment will support scholarships, purchase lab equipment supplies and library books, offer grants to faculty to participate in industry and academic conferences, and support an Academic-in-Residence program for faculty and an Executive-in-Residence program for industry professionals. "The university appreciates the generous support of the J. Willard and Alice S. Marriott Foundation," UCF President John Hitt said. "The foundations gift will provide for an enhanced educational experience for our students and faculty at the Rosen School." Orlando businessman and hotelier Harris Rosen, who also serves on the UCF Board of Trustees, has donated more than $20 million to UCF and the Rosen School, which was named for him in 2000. Other hospitality giants, including the Orlando/Orange County Visitors & Convention Bureau, Darden Restaurants Inc., Walt Disney World Co., the Universal Orlando Foundation and the Anheuser-Busch Foundation/SeaWorld, have donated a total of more than $10.5 million to the Rosen School. All donations are eligible for state matches. The Rosen School has more than 900 undergraduates, 40 graduate students and 23 full-time faculty members, and offers studies in hospitality management, restaurant management, theme parks and attractions management, time share management and event management. Rosen School Dean Abe Pizam said that by 2005 the school expects to have 1,000 undergraduates, 100 graduate students and 35 full-time faculty members.
