Faculty Honored at Founders Day for Teaching, Service and Research Efforts

By Chad Binette and Kristi Bartlett April 4, 2007

Mubarak Shah, a professor in the College of Engineering and Computer Science and the director of UCF’s Computer Vision Laboratory, receives the University Award for Excellence in Distinguished Research from Vice President for Research MJ Soileau.

The following awards were given to UCF faculty members during the annual Founders Day ceremony. The winners of the Pegasus Professor Awards and professors who were granted emeritus status are featured in separate stories. The students who were awarded Founders Day honors are also featured separately.

University Award for Excellence in Undergraduate Teaching: John J. Schultz, Anthropology, College of Sciences. Schultz, an assistant professor who began working at UCF in fall 2003, specializes in forensic anthropology. He has developed a certificate program in crime scene investigations with help from the Criminal Justice faculty. He earned praise for having a significant impact on undergraduate enrollment in Anthropology and for his hands-on teaching efforts that prepare students for their careers.

Winners from each college were: Patricia Angley, Lisa Roney, Claudia Schippert and Michael Strawser, Arts and Humanities; Cynthia Gundy, Barbara Moore and Mihir Parikh, College of Business Administration; Gina Gresham and Trae Stewart, College of Education; Eduardo Divo and Michael Georgiopoulos, College of Engineering and Computer Science; Kenyatta O. Rivers and Judith P. Ruland, College of Health and Public Affairs; Seth Elsheimer, Otto Phanstiel, Houman A. Sadri and John J. Schultz, College of Sciences.

University Award for Excellence in Graduate Teaching: Mohamed A. Abdel-Aty, Civil and Environmental Engineering, College of Engineering and Computer Science. In the past three years, Abdel-Aty has co-authored 24 journal publications with doctoral students and 11 with masters students, and he has worked on more than 30 conference presentations with graduate students. His students frequently win awards for their outstanding publications and presentations. Abdel-Aty received the University Distinguished Researcher award in 2003.

Winners from each college were: Keith Folse, College of Arts and Humanities; Cameron Ford, College of Business Administration; Debbie Hahs-Vaughn, College of Education; Mohamed A. Abdel-Aty, College of Engineering and Computer Science; Diane M. Wink, College of Health and Public Affairs; Guifang Li, College of Optics and Photonics; and Andres D. Campiglia, College of Sciences.

University Award for Excellence in Distinguished Research: Mubarak Shah, College of Engineering and Computer Science. Shah is the director of UCFs Computer Vision Laboratory, which he started two decades ago. He is a perennial member of UCFs Millionaire Club, which honors researchers who earn more than $1 million in research funding in a year. He started the first Research Experience for Undergraduates program sponsored by the National Science Foundation in 1987. His research includes work related to monitoring railroad crossings for hazards, improving coordination among unmanned military vehicles and studying real-time nighttime surveillance.

Winners from each college were: Tison Pugh, College of Arts and Humanities; Maureen L. Ambrose, College of Business Administration; Vicky Zygouris-Coe, College of Education; Mubarak Shah, College of Engineering and Computer Science; Mary Lou Sole, College of Health and Public Affairs; Robin B. DiPietro, Rosen College of Hospitality Management; Aristide Dogariu, College of Optics and Photonics; Andres D. Campiglia, College of Sciences; and Raj Vaidyanathan, Institutes and Centers.

University Award for Excellence in Faculty Academic Advising: Patricia Angley, College of Arts and Humanities, and Costas John Efthimiou, College of Sciences. Angley is the departmental advisor to all English majors and oversees the departments orientation sessions. She mentors McNair Scholars and serves on the advisory board for McNair scholarships. Efthimiou has redefined the Physics computer room into an informal advising center where he helps students develop problem-solving skills and engages in discussions about ethics. As faculty advisor, he has helped the UCF chapter of the Society of Physics Students become one of the most active in the Southeast.

University Award for Excellence in Professional Academic Advising: Kim A. Small, College of Engineering and Computer Science. Small is the primary advising contact for more than 400 engineering technology students. She earned praise for her efforts to expand service-learning opportunities and ensure student success.

Award for Excellence in Librarianship: Terrie K. Sypolt, a reference librarian. Sypolt earned praise for frequently helping people find answers to difficult and unusual questions and for her direct mentoring and instruction to students. She also helped the university and College of Education prepare for accreditation renewals, and she serves on the board of the Florida Association of Teacher Educators.

University Awards for Excellence in Professional Service: J. Blake Scott, College of Arts and Humanities and Cynthia Y. Young, College of Sciences. Scott has served as chair of a composition writing center on campus and an ad-hoc committee on the undergraduate rhetoric and writing track. He is a member of the College of Arts and Humanities Deans Advisory Council and is a service-learning coordinator at the Faculty Center for Teaching and Learning. Young has served as a conduct hearing officer and as a member of the Faculty Senate steering committee. She also is the NCAA faculty representative for athletics and is chair of the UCF Athletics Committee. She has chaired an initiative to encourage girls to become more involved in science and engineering.

Taylor, College of Engineering and Computer Science; Cheryl Evans Green, College of Health and Public Affairs; Bob Davis and Joe Hall, College of Sciences; and Judith E. Boyte, Regional Campuses.
