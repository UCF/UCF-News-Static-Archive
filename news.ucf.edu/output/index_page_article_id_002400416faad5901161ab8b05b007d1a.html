UCF Faculty, Alumni and Students Featured in 3 Orlando Film Festival Screenings

By Christine Dellert Nov. 7, 2007

This year’s Orlando Film Festival will feature several films with strong University of Central Florida ties.

At least three of the 19 films showing Thursday, Nov. 8, through Sunday, Nov. 11, during the city’s second-annual film festival showcase work by UCF professors, alumni and students.

The films range from a documentary about the plight of Cuban-Americans who fled Fidel Castro’s regime to a fictional feature about a small-town karaoke dive to a film that asks whether millennials are the “next great generation.”

All of the screenings are free, but seating is limited in the two downtown venues. For more information, go to http://www.orlandofilmfest.com.

A Wave of Stories: “Lejos de la Isla”

Luis Martínez-Fernández, a professor of History at UCF, provided the historical backdrop to the film, “Lejos de la Isla” (Far from the Island), which chronicles several waves of Cuban refugees.

The 90-minute documentary was a labor of love for first-generation Cuban-American and New Jersey native L.E. Salas, 27, who reached out to Martínez-Fernández, a native of Havana, and another UCF professor to help with the film.

“Lejos de la Isla” will make its local debut Saturday at the Orlando Film Festival.

During the film, UCF English professor Cecilia Rodríguez Milanés, whose family hails from Cuba, performs a poem she penned several years ago called the “Cuban-American Manifesto.” Salas uses it to weave together the stories of his characters’ journey from Cuba to the United States and their continuing cultural struggles here.

“It is something that hits so close to home,” Rodríguez Milanés said. “They (the Cuban-American viewers) completely understand many of the statements and feelings that come out in the piece…and they were impressed with this young filmmaker who didn’t actually go through exile, but seemed to get it so right.”

Salas was inspired by his father, who fled Cuba when he was 16. He grew up in New Jersey with the Cuban culture, ate his mother’s Cuban dishes and spoke Cuban Spanish at home.

Salas will join Martínez-Fernández and Rodríguez Milanés for a Q&A after the 4 p.m. screening Nov. 10 at the Gallery at Avalon Island, 39 S. Magnolia Ave.

Rock-Star Dreams: “The Karaoke King”

More than 30 UCF alumni and faculty were involved in the making of “The Karaoke King,” the fictional saga of one night at Lil’s, America’s first karaoke bar, its rock-star-like karaoke competition and its dysfunctional regulars.

When Eddie Bowman’s girlfriend of 14 years gives him an ultimatum, he must choose between love or his weekly reign as the small-town’s “Karaoke King.”

Directed and co-produced by UCF Theatre professor J.J. Ruscella, “The Karaoke King” has already been labeled an instant “cult classic” by WKMG-Channel 6.

Its Orlando debut will be 3 p.m. Thursday, Nov. 8, at CityArts Factory, 29 S. Orange Ave., with a post-screening Q&A with Ruscella and local filmmakers.

Gauging a Generation: “Who’s Next”

This UCF-produced documentary explores the theory that those born in or after 1982 are the “next great generation.”

“Who’s Next” was developed and produced by Linda Chapin, director of the Metropolitan Center for Regional Studies at UCF, and Lisa Mills, a professor in the School of Film and Digital Media.

The 45-minute documentary examines historians and social researchers William Strauss and Neil Howe’s theory about the millennial generation. It was filmed at several Central Florida locations, including UCF’s main campus during a student orientation and on the Lake County campaign trail with UCF alumnus Robert Thompson during his campaign for the state Legislature. The film features many UCF students and graduates, including a biology graduate student and an Army ROTC cadet.

About a dozen UCF students gained hands-on experience and college credits for their work on the film. Students researched, filmed and edited segments of the documentary, and two students served as associate producers.

The screening is scheduled for 4 p.m. Friday, Nov. 9 at the Gallery at Avalon Island, 39 S. Magnolia Ave. Mills and graduate student associate producer Abeer Abdalla will hold a post-screening Q&A.
