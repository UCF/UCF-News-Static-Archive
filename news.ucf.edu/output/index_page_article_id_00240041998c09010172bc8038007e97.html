UCF to Compete at Kennedy Center Regional Theater Festival

By Mallery Laing Jan. 21, 2005

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Roberta Sloan

    • 407-823-2862

  • UCF Theatre Department

  • Print this Page

Competing for the first time in the Kennedy Center American College Theatre Festival in Palm Beach County, the UCF Conservatory Theatre will try next month to win a chance to perform The Woman Who Fell From the Sky at the Kennedy Center in Washington, D.C.

 

Originally performed at UCF in September and October, the drama is one of only seven chosen to compete in the regional festival. Colleges and universities in the 10-state region entered 240 plays in the competition.

 

The Woman Who Fell From the Sky, which will be performed Feb. 3 at the festival and Jan. 31 at UCF, tells the true story of a Tarahumara Indian woman from Mexico who was institutionalized in a mental hospital in Kansas because nobody understood her indigenous language and culture.

 

Associate Professor Lani Harris saw the play in Mexico City last year while on a Fulbright-Hays fellowship trip designed to help professors and teachers learn more about intercultural communications and the differences between societies. Because playwright Victor Hugo Rascon Bandas play focuses on those issues, Harris decided to perform it for UCF audiences.

 

Started in 1969 by Roger L. Stevens, the Kennedy Center American College Theatre Festival is a national theatre program involving 18,000 students from colleges and universities. It has served as a catalyst for improving the quality of college theater in the Unites States.

 

The plays in the Kennedy Center regional festivals are selected by committees that look at videotapes and other production materials and read comments of representatives who attended the productions. About seven plays nationwide will win the honor of performing at the Kennedy Center in Washington, D.C.

 

 We are thrilled that the UCF Conservatory Theatre has received such high accolades, said Roberta Sloan, professor and chair/artistic director of UCFs Theatre Department. We are proud of our theater program, and it is always nice to have these kinds of commendations from such highly respected theatre organizations.

 

In The Woman Who Fell From the Sky, Rita, a woman from Mexico, travels to the United States and is placed in a mental institution for 12 years. Because American authorities regard her language as gibberish and her native customs as crazy, she endures psychotropic treatments that cause permanent neurological damage. But when a Mexican man by the name of Miguel Giner recognizes her language and realizes that the woman was never mentally impaired, he embarks on a court battle that spans several years and concludes with Ritas release.

 

Two UCF students will direct short plays at the Kennedy Center festival in Palm Beach County. Rachel Yadanza will direct a ten-minute play, and Haley Gurr will participate in the 2005 Region IV directors project.

 

Ticket information:

 

The Woman Who Fell From the Sky will be performed at the 2005 Kennedy Center American College Theatre Festival, Region IV, at 8:30 p.m. Feb. 3 at the Duncan Theatre on the campus of Palm Beach Community College in Lake Worth. To reserve tickets, call the festival box office at 561-868-3309.

 

The UCF community will get one more chance to see the performance on campus, at 8 p.m. Jan. 31. To reserve tickets for that performance, call the UCF box office at 407-823-1500. Tickets are $12.50 for the general public, $10 for senior citizens and $7.50 for students.
