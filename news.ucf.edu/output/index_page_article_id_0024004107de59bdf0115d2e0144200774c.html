Homecoming Parade Heads Back to Campus With Football Game, Statue Unveiling

By Christine Dellert Updated Nov. 2, 2007

Photo: Jacque Brund

Student Government President Brandie Hollinger got in the Reflecting Pond on Friday after telling the Spirit Splash crowd that she had never done that before.

The University of Central Florida’s Homecoming Parade will return to campus Saturday, Nov. 3, starting at Central Florida Boulevard and heading clockwise on Gemini Boulevard through Greek Park and past the new UCF Arena.

The UCF Marching Knights, several dozen student-built floats and others will pass through the changing face of UCF’s campus and christen a new Homecoming tradition before the Knights play Marshall at 3:30 p.m. at Bright House Networks Stadium.

Fans, students and alumni are also invited to a statue unveiling at 2 p.m. outside the stadium. "The Charging Knight" statue by Sanford-based artist Don Reynolds embodies UCF’s push toward national prominence. The afternoon unveiling at IOA Plaza will feature remarks from UCF President John Hitt and Student Government Association President Brandie Hollinger.

Parade-goers are encouraged to arrive on campus by 9 a.m. Saturday and follow normal game-day parking guidelines as highlighted on the Game Day Web site, http://www.ucfgameday.com.

The Homecoming parade starts at 9:30 a.m. and is expected to last about an hour and a half. Judging will be staged on Memory Mall across from the new UCF Arena.

Traffic on and around campus will be restricted and redirected as follows to accommodate the procession:

-- Central Florida Boulevard will be closed from 5 a.m. to 10 a.m. for parade staging.

-- Northbound traffic on Alafaya Trail between Research Parkway and Central Florida Boulevard will be temporarily redirected around 7 a.m. while police escort parade floats to campus.

-- Northbound lanes of Gemini Boulevard from Central Florida Boulevard clockwise to North Orion Boulevard will be sequentially closed from 9:30 a.m. to about noon. Southbound lanes will generally remain open, but traffic is expected to be slow-going during the parade.

Also scheduled for the Homecoming game day is an 11 a.m. basketball exhibition game in the new UCF Arena. There will be no special parking accommodations for the free basketball game against St. Leo, and normal game-day parking restrictions will be in effect throughout the campus.

The statue unveiling will be at 2 p.m. on IOA Plaza, on the west side of Bright House Networks Stadium. UCF’s new "Charging Knight" was completely funded by donations from alumni and the university’s Student Government Association, which committed $50,000 toward the project. UCF officials believe it will become a "must visit" piece of art and a new campus focal point.

Enthusiastic Students Pack Reflecting Pond for Spirit Splash

A large crowd of students jumped up and down to "Zombie Nation" and cheered loudly in and around the Reflecting Pond on Friday as several speakers urged them to support the Knights loudly against Marshall on Saturday.

"The word is getting out around the conference that Bright House is a tough place to play because of the student section," UCF President John Hitt said during the annual Spirit Splash pep rally.

Athletics Director Keith Tribble, Coach George O'Leary and the football team captains also addressed the crowd. The Knights' national champion cheerleading squad and KnightMoves, Starlet Knights and Ruckus dance squads performed along with the Marching Knights band.

For more information about this week’s UCF Homecoming events and the parade route, go to http://ucfhc.com. To learn more about alumni events, visit http://www.ucfalumni.com.
