UCF Professor Honored by National Restaurant Association

By Chad Binette Feb. 4, 2005

Bill Fisher has taught at the Rosen College of Hospitality Management since 2001.

The National Restaurant Association Educational Foundation will give a lifetime achievement in education award this spring to Bill Fisher, a professor at the University of Central Floridas Rosen College of Hospitality Management.

 

The Michael E. Hurst, FMP Lifetime Achievement in Education Award honors a professor or administrator who has devoted an entire career to educating and enhancing the lives of young people preparing for a career in the restaurant and foodservice industry and who is passionate and enthusiastic about his or her work.

 

Fisher, the Darden Eminent Scholar in Restaurant Management at the Rosen College, began teaching at UCF in August 2001. He was chief executive officer of the American Hotel & Lodging Association from 1996 to 2001 and previously was chief executive officer of the National Restaurant Association. He helped to found the National Restaurant Association Educational Foundation in 1987.

 

It is a great honor for UCF and the Rosen College to have this prestigious award given to one of our faculty members, said Dean Abraham Pizam. Dr. Fisher is one of our most popular and highly effective instructors, and he has made a difference in the lives of many of our students. He fully deserves this award.

 

Fisher is the author of Fishers Laws: The Thinkers Guide to Management Action, and he has edited several books about the hospitality industry. He also taught accounting, finance and general management courses for four years at Cornell Universitys School of Hotel Administration during the 1970s.

 

Fisher will receive his award at a National Restaurant Association awards gala May 21 in Chicago. He was selected unanimously by the trustees of the National Restaurant Association Educational Foundation on Jan. 24.

 

Go to www.hospitality.ucf.edu for more information on the Rosen College of Hospitality Management or www.nraef.org for more information on the National Restaurant Association Educational Foundation.
