Election 2008: UCF Professor Who Worked on Obama Campaign Releases Book

Dec. 20, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Christine Dellert

    • 407-823-2947

  • Print this Page

Presidential hopeful Barack Obama is taking the lead in Iowa with many of the same tactics that helped him win a U.S. Senate seat three years earlier, says a University of Central Florida professor.

Martin Dupuis, an associate professor of Political Science, volunteered with Obama's U.S. Senate campaign in Illinois, organizing community fundraisers and outreach events at Western Illinois University. He is the lead author of a book that analyzes Obama's success then, tying it to his widespread appeal today and his ability to connect with a voting population that transcends race, class and gender.

"Obama was a lesser-known state senator, and yet he beat a multimillionaire," Dupuis said of the candidate's 2004 victory over businessman Blair Hull. "He's still got that level of appeal, and some of the predictions we made in the book are starting to pan out as he takes the lead in Iowa."

Dupuis says it's Obama's knack for "surrounding himself with smart people, [creating] a strong campaign organization and a message of pragmatic policy proposals" that ultimately mobilized grass-roots supporters to go out and vote for him then. And he predicts similar results at Iowa's caucus next month.

"Barack Obama: The New Face of American Politics" from Praeger/Greenwood is expected out Dec. 30.  Dupuis wrote the book with a former colleague, Keith Boeckelman of Western Illinois University.

Dupuis is also the assistant dean of The Burnett Honors College at UCF and a member of the Florida Bar. Before that, he taught at Western Illinois University for nine years. He is a faculty adviser for the group, University of Central Florida Students for Barack Obama.

He can be reached at 407-823-5948 (office), 407-595-3739 (cell) or mdupuis@mail.ucf.edu.
