Gala Featuring Singer Natalie Cole Celebrates UCF's Fund-Raising Success

By Chad Binette March 28, 2007

Grammy Award-winning singer Natalie Cole serenaded a crowd of about 500 Saturday evening at a gala commemorating the success of the University of Central Florida's first comprehensive campaign.

The UCF Foundation organized the gala at Disney's Contemporary Resort to honor the many donors who helped the university raise $364 million during the eight-year "Central to Our Future" campaign. 

The community's support during the campaign far exceeded expectations as donors responded to the university's vision of improving classroom and laboratory facilities and enriching the quality of academic programs. Many of the donations are invested in endowments that support faculty and academic programs over the long term.

UCF President John Hitt, Campaign Chairman and UCF Trustee Al Weiss and UCF Foundation Chief Executive Officer Bob Holmes all thanked donors for their generosity.

They were joined by former Miss America and UCF alumna Ericka Dunlap, who served as the eveningÂ’s emcee, and by Cole, whose hits include "Unforgettable," "When I Fall in Love" and "Pink Cadillac."

"The campaign has transformed the university physically and strategically," Hitt said. "And through partnerships with local industry and organizations, and the introduction of the Health Sciences Campus at Lake Nona, UCF has repositioned itself as a strong economic driver within the region."

The campaign's success far outpaced the $250 million goal that was announced in October 2005 and the $108 million goal that was set when the campaign started in fall 1998. The campaign includes gifts pledged through June 30, 2006.

Donations for the College of Medicine accounted for nearly a third of the total amount pledged during the campaign. Eighteen "founders" committed $1 million or more toward the college. The donations include a $25 million gift, including state matching funds, from the Tavistock Group, the developers of Lake Nona. The Tavistock Group also contributed 50 acres of land valued at more than $8 million.

Hitt praised the Tavistock Group and several other donors for making "transformational gifts" to the university. Donors whom Hitt thanked included:

Al and Nancy Burnett, longtime supporters whose name graces the university's honors and biomedical sciences colleges and whose contributions will help construct a new building for the biomedical sciences program at Lake Nona.

Trustee Harris Rosen and his wife, Trisha, whose generosity allowed UCF to build the Rosen College of Hospitality Management campus on Universal Boulevard and to endow a scholarship fund.

Rich and Helen DeVos, supporters of UCF's nationally recognized DeVos Sport Business Management program and the National Consortium for Academics and Sport.

Kenneth G. Dixon, a Kissimmee accountant, real estate developer and 1975 UCF graduate whose gift endowed the Kenneth G. Dixon School of Accounting and supports faculty there.

Dr. Phillips Inc. for its contribution leading to the establishment of the Dr. P. Phillips School of Real Estate.

Walt Disney World for its ongoing commitment to UCF and support of the Rosen College of Hospitality Management and the College of Medicine.

Holmes and Weiss, a 1976 UCF graduate who is president of worldwide operations for Walt Disney Parks and Resorts, thanked the hundreds of volunteers who cared deeply enough about the university to encourage others to donate.

"As a UCF alumnus, I feel an additional measure of pride to see that the university that did so much for me now stands ready to do so much more for so many more students who will walk through its doors in the days and years and generations to come," Weiss said.
