Trustees Support Research Network, Approve International Student Fee

By Tom Evelyn Sept. 23, 2004

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Tom Evelyn

    • 407-823-5988

  • UCF Board of Trustees

  • Print this Page

 

The UCF Board of Trustees on Thursday approved a resolution supporting a high-performance data network that will connect UCF and other research universities and a new fee for international students to help cover the costs of new federal tracking rules.

 

The fiber-optic Florida LambdaRail will allow institutions to efficiently share large amounts of information through a separate, Internet-like network to support education, research and library communication. The boards endorsement ensures that UCF will participate in and support the network and allows the consortium developing the system to borrow $6.6 million to build it.

 

UCF will spend $500,000 a year to support the network, which is expected to be completed by mid-2005. Other partner institutions include Florida Atlantic University, the Florida Institute of Technology, Florida International University, Florida State University, Nova Southeastern, the University of Florida and the University of West Florida.

 

The service fee of $50 per semester for international students will help UCF pay for increased costs imposed by the federal government since the Sept. 11 terrorist attacks.

 

The university spent an extra $1 million last year adding staff, equipment and software to meet new requirements for tracking international students and to provide workshops for employment, status and immigration rules. UCF tracks more than 1,000 international students.

 

Other universities around the country, including UF and USF, charge international students service fees ranging from $40 to $125 per semester. UCFs fee, which will cover less than 13 percent of the actual cost of meeting the new requirements, will take effect in fall 2005.

 

In other action during its regular meeting, the board:

 

-- Approved three new masters degree programs in film and digital media and a masters degree in marriage and family therapy. The masters degrees in film and digital media will prepare filmmakers and media entrepreneurs to create stories, produce interactive and immersive environments and conduct technological research in the digital manipulation of images and sounds. UCF will be the first state university in Florida to offer the marriage and family therapy masters. The four new degree programs are scheduled to begin in fall 2005.

 

-- Approved a joint-resolution with the Seminole Community College Board of Trustees to build a joint-use facility at the Sanford-Lake Mary SCC campus. UCF, which already offers five bachelors degree programs on the campus, will request $6.4 million in state funds for the project; SCC will ask for an additional $8.6 million.

 

-- Approved tenure with hire for 17 new faculty members. Normally, faculty members hired with tenure have earned tenure at their previous institutions and more than meet UCFs requirements for tenure. Tenure is also included in hiring packages when senior faculty members are hired for administrative positions.

 

The board also heard a presentation on Research and Economic Development Opportunities in the Health Sciences by Ken Roozen, executive director of the University of South Carolina Medical School Foundation for Research and Development.

 

UCF is considering adding a medical school and has begun a study to determine the feasibility and need. In July, the board approved creating the Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences, providing a foundation for a medical school should UCF pursue one.

 

The next Board of Trustees meeting is scheduled for Nov. 18 at the Student Union. For more information on the board, go to www.ucf.edu/bot.
