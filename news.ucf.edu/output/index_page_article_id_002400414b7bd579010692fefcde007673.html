UCFs Indian Students Volunteer at Gandhi Day of Service

By Shobana Daniell Oct. 5, 2005

Photo: Courtesy of Sangam

Sangam students helped to clean statues around Lake Eola on Saturday to celebrate "Be The Change – Gandhi Day of Service."

Mahatma Gandhi, the leader of Indias freedom movement and a champion of community service and social justice said, The best way to find yourself is to lose yourself in the service of others.

On Saturday, people jogging or soaking in the sun at Lake Eola saw those words in action when a group of UCFs Indian students joined a service project to clean the International Bridge area of the downtown Orlando park.

More than 100 people came to celebrate the 136th birth anniversary of Gandhi, a global leader famous for his message of peace and non-violence.

UCFs Indian student organization, Sangam, helped to scrub grime and bird droppings off of the statues of leaders from mostly Latin American countries and cleared the area of fallen leaves. Local high school students and other community members also joined the effort.

For the last three years, UCFs Sangam has participated in Be The Change  Gandhi Day of Service, a nationwide day of service that started at University of Michigan in 1998. More than 200 universities and organizations in the United States now participate in the annual event, and they contributed a total of more than 15,000 hours of volunteer work.

I find a real joy in being a part of making positive changes to the world, and it is also very interesting to meet such a diverse group of people, said Drishtie Patel, Sangams vice president and a communications major. I believe, if we all put in a little effort, we could really make a big difference in one day."

Abhishek Karnik, a doctoral student in Computer Science, said last years volunteer project of clearing hurricane damage to the Girl Scouts camp in Oviedo was so rewarding that I made it a point to help this year too.

Sangams other recent community service activities included visiting the Orlando Science Center on Sept. 16 to help with the opening day festivities for the new IMAX documentary Mystic India. A large group of UCF Indian students answered questions from hundreds of people who came to see the India-related exhibits and the documentary.

For more information on Be The Change  Gandhi Day of Service,  go to www.saalt.org/bethechange.php. For more information on Sangam, go to http://pegasus.cc.ucf.edu/~sangam/home.html.
