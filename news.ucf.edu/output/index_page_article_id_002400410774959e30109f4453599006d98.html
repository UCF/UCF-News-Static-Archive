Information Fluency Effort to Help Students with Research, Careers

By Chad Binette April 19, 2006

Photo: Jacque Brund

Students walk past banners promoting the new Information Fluency initiative on campus. The new program will help students in their schoolwork, research and careers.

A new Information Fluency Initiative at the University of Central Florida will help students gather information, evaluate it critically and use it properly and effectively in their class projects, research and careers.

During the 2006-07 academic year, students, faculty and staff will plan ways to focus more on information fluency throughout academics and campus life. In most cases, the plans will be implemented in 2007-08.

Four pilot programs will be developed to integrate information fluency into the curriculum. The Philosophy Department will strive to improve critical thinking skills for students in its ethics program, while the School of Nursing will increase training to help students evaluate and determine what research and information would be most effective for their jobs.

The Strategies for Success course that helps freshmen make the transition from high school to UCF also will begin emphasizing information fluency skills. The Burnett Honors College will introduce information fluency skills in freshman symposia and train students to work as peer mentors in the colleges computer lab.

UCF also is encouraging individual students and faculty and staff members to develop ways to emphasize information fluency through classes, campus organizations or other methods. Students, faculty and staff have until April 30 to submit proposals for 10 grants worth $1,000 each that will be awarded in the summer.

The overarching plan is to create a culture of inquiry on campus for everybody, said Martha Marinara, a chair of the Quality Enhancement Plan development. We want to take enough time planning this effort so that when it is implemented, it has the strongest effect on the largest number of students.

The Information Fluency, or if, effort was designed to meet a new demand for quality enhancement programs from the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools, which accredits UCF every 10 years. SACS representatives heard a presentation on the information fluency proposal this week and will approve it, possibly with recommended changes, during the summer.

The plan covers five years at an estimated cost of $4 million, including $3 million from the UCF budget and $1 million of in-kind contributions, mostly staff time provided by colleges and departments. Grant funding could be used to augment the university money.

Many of the initiatives primary goals involve improving faculty development and student learning. New initiatives include:

-- Four faculty members each year will serve as fellows in the Faculty Center for Teaching and Learning. They will specialize in information literacy, computer and information technology literacy, critical thinking about information and effective communication of information.

-- Faculty development conferences will be held each winter and summer, and awareness of information fluency will be promoted more in online faculty development courses.

-- More writing consultants will be hired at the University Writing Center, which will be open for online help for students 18 hours a day, six days a week. The expanded hours could take effect in August.

The topic of information fluency was selected by teams of UCF faculty members, students, staff and administrators. During focus groups with area employers, many of them stressed the importance of students having good information literacy and technology skills when they graduate.

Marinara and Chuck Dzubian, a professor in the Research Initiative for Teaching Effectiveness program, are co-chairs of the if initiative. Kelley Melendez is the programs coordinator. Joel Hartman, vice provost for Information Technologies and Resources, serves on an eight-member National Advisory Board that will help UCF plan and implement information fluency programs.

For more information, go to www.if.ucf.edu . Information on the $1,000 grants for which students, faculty and staff can apply is posted under Resources for Faculty.
