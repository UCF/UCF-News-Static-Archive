Jones High Band to Play

By Koren Bedeau October 22, 2003

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Koren Bedeau, African American Studies

    • 407-823-0026

  • Print this Page

As a part of the 2nd annual James Weldon Johnson Lecture Series, the UCF African American Studies Program will present the Jones High School Alumni and Community Band. This performance and lecture entitled "From Spirituals to Soul: A Journey Through African American Musical History" will be held Friday, November 14th, from 7 p.m. to 9 p.m. in the UCF Visual Arts Building Auditorium. This event is free and open to the public. The great African American composer and pianist Mary Lou Williams (1910-1981) wrote that jazz music has four basic roots - spirituals, ragtime, blues, and swing. Together with latin rhythms and melodies, these styles form the basis not only of jazz but all African American music. From Spirituals to Soul will be a musical presentation with narrations that chronicle the African American experience from the 1740s through 1960s. Formed in 1996 the Jones High School Alumni Band, under the musical leadership of founder and director, James W. "Chief" Wilson, has achieved local, national, and international status as musical performers, educators, and cultural custodians. Over the years, graduates of Jones High have filled the ranks of historically black college bands, military bands, and top professional and amateur orchestras. Drawing on a long history of regimental military bands, New Orleans brass bands, and the orchestras of Ellington, Basie, and more, the Alumni Band embodies a living legacy of African American musical contributions to United States and world culture.
