UCF Invites Parents, Fans to Academic Showcase During Family Weekend

By Courtney Gilmartin Sept. 25, 2008

Photo: UCF News File

The Teaching Academy will be one of several buildings on campus hosting college open houses on Oct. 4.

The University of Central Florida invites parents, students and other fans to learn more about its academic programs before the Knights play their annual Family Weekend football game on Saturday, Oct. 4.

Nine UCF colleges will host open houses – most of which feature tours and opportunities to speak with faculty and staff -- from noon to 2 p.m. Kick off for the Knights’ game against Southern Methodist University will be at 3:30 p.m.

“We encourage fans to arrive early and learn more about the many first-class academic programs that make our university great,” said Rick Schell, vice provost for Academic Affairs and one of the organizers of the College Open Houses. “Parents also can meet many of our dedicated faculty and staff members, who provide first-class educational experiences to their daughters and sons.”

At the open houses, visitors can challenge business students in the College of Business Administration’s “Are You Smarter Than a Business Student?” computer game. The College of Education will host “Are You Smarter Than a Fifth-Grader” and serve milk and cookies at the Teaching Academy.

Visitors can enjoy a barbecue with College of Engineering and Computer Science faculty at the Harris Corporation Engineering Center or view exhibits, watch a video presentation and meet the dean of the College of Sciences.

The College of Medicine, College of Nursing and College of Health and Public Affairs will hold open houses at the Health and Public Affairs I building.

The Burnett Honors Colleges’ activities begin at 9:30 a.m. and include a parents meeting, mini lectures and a cook out. At the Visual Arts Building, the College of Arts and Humanities will offer free food, giveaways and information about all of its academic programs.

To learn more about the College Open Houses, visit http://www.openhouse.ucf.edu.

Family Weekend Activities:

Several Family Weekend Activities also will be held on campus Friday, Oct. 3, through Sunday, Oct. 5.  They include:

-- The Fairwinds Alumni Center will host a family-friendly tent party featuring games and a BBQ feast at 12:30 p.m. on Saturday.  Dinner prices start at $10, and packages including game tickets are available.

-- Student Development and Enrollment Services will sponsor a talk for parents of freshmen with their academic support staff from 10 a.m. to noon Saturday.

-- The Rosen College of Hospitality Management will host an open house and brunch at its campus in the heart of Orlando’s tourism district.  For more information or to make a reservation, call 407-903-8849.

-- The Recreation and Wellness Center will have a free open gym for parents and siblings 18 or older.  Picture identification is required.

-- The Darden Auditorium at the Rosen campus will host a comedy show on Friday at 7:30 p.m.  Admission is free, and seating is available on a first-come, first-serve basis.

-- Late Knights Kandy Land will be held in the Student Union on Friday from 9 p.m. until 2 a.m.  Free food, fun, games and giveaways will be provided.

More information on Family Weekend is available at http://www.getinvolveducf.com/familyweekend/index.html.
