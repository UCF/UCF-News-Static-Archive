UCF Trustees Honor Cheerleaders and Approve New Degrees, Student Fees

By Tom Evelyn Jan. 18, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Tom Evelyn

    • 407-823-5988

  • UCF Board of Trustees

  • Print this Page

The University of Central Florida Board of Trustees approved five new degree programs and new rates for various student, housing and parking fees at its regular meeting Thursday.

The board also showed its school spirit when it honored UCFs newly anointed national champion cheerleaders. President John Hitt praised the squad for winning its second national title Sunday at the College Cheerleading and Dance Team Nationals at Disneys Wide World of Sports.

Let us cheer you, Hitt said to the team and coach Linda Gooch before a standing ovation at a luncheon ceremony. Congratulations on some well-deserved recognition.

During the regular meeting, the trustees established new doctoral degrees in nursing practice and physical therapy.

The nursing practice Ph.D. program, which will be implemented this fall, will help to address critical shortages of nursing faculty and nurse practitioners in Florida. The physical therapy Ph.D. program that will begin this summer will replace an existing masters program, adding coursework in health care and human resource management and other areas.

The Board of Governors, which oversees the states public universities and must approve new doctoral degrees, has indicated that it will accept both programs.

A new master of arts in music will offer study and training in music theory, performance and conducting. The program will begin this fall.

The board also approved a bachelors of science degree in biotechnology that will prepare students to work in the biotechnology industry with applied laboratory experience and research opportunities. A new bachelors of science degree in construction engineering will focus on the technical and managerial processes of construction. Both programs will begin this fall.

In other action, the board approved new rates for several student fees that will take effect this fall. Fees are reviewed each year by committees including students, staff and faculty members that recommend new rates to the board. The following increases were approved Thursday:

-- Activity and service fee, $9.37 to $9.73 a credit hour.
-- Health fee, $7.95 to $8.15 a credit hour.
-- Transportation access fee, $5.84 to $7.09 a credit hour.
-- Athletics fee, $11.09 to $11.88 a credit hour.

The board also approved a 5 percent increase in the parking decal fee and an average 3 percent increase in the on-campus housing rates.

The increased fees will be used to help pay for, among other things, staffing and renovation of student housing, the growing costs of operating the student Recreation and Wellness Center, the needs of sports programs that do not produce revenue, and various parking and transportation projects, including future garages and shuttle service to the Rosen College of Hospitality Management and downtown and regional campuses.

Walsh Elected Board Chair

At the end of Thursdays meeting, charter board Chairman Dick Nunis announced that he was stepping down as chair after five years. Nunis, a retired Walt Disney Attractions executive, said he was going back to work and felt he could not dedicate enough time to being chair. He will remain on the board.

Charter trustee Rick Walsh, who recently retired as a vice president from Darden Restaurants, was elected by the board to be the new chair.

The next board meeting is scheduled for March 28 in the Student Union. Visit www.bot.ucf.edu for more information.
