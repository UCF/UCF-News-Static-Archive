UCF Foundation Announces $250 Million Fund-Raising Campaign

By Tom Evelyn Oct. 11, 2005

The University of Central Florida Foundation announced a $250 million comprehensive campaign, a milestone in fund-raising for the young university, at a donor appreciation event Tuesday night.

University leaders outlined the Central to Our Future campaign and honored significant donors from this past year at the Presidents Appreciation Gala, which was held in the Pegasus Grand Ballroom at the Student Union on the universitys East Orlando campus.

The UCF Foundation has already raised more than $207 million during the quiet phase of the campaign, which began July 1, 1998. Foundation leaders expect several large donations in the next several months related to a proposed medical college and on-campus football stadium to help UCF reach the $250 million goal. The campaign concludes June 30.

The success of the campaign so far is a reflection of dedicated donors, institutional commitment and the leadership of this university, said UCF Vice President for Alumni Relations & Development and Foundation CEO Bob Holmes. This is a remarkable effort by the entire community.

The money raised by UCFs first comprehensive campaign benefits students, faculty and staff by supporting education, research and the construction of classrooms, laboratories and buildings that house these efforts. A substantial amount of the money raised supports faculty and program endowments. The results benefit the community.

Our generous donors have responded to our call and are making a difference by supporting high-quality education and cutting-edge research that provides opportunity and changes lives, said UCF President John Hitt, who will acknowledge this past years top donors at the gala.

The campaign would receive a large boost from the Tavistock Group if the state Board of Governors approves a medical college for UCF. The private investment company announced last week it would donate more than $20.5 million in cash and land to establish the UCF Healthcare Campus at Lake Nona, which would be home to the proposed medical college.

Several other major gifts over the past few years have played a key role in helping the university reach its campaign goal:

Harris Rosen -- $36.4 million, including a state matching grant, for land and construction of the Rosen College of Hospitality Management building. Rosen, a UCF trustee, also gave $2.17 million with a state match to endow a scholarship fund in the college.

Nancy and Al Burnett  $20 million, including a state matching grant, to create the Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences. $15 million of the gift will be used to construct a planned building that will house the college, which will be the foundation of a proposed medical college. The rest will provide an endowment in the biomedical sciences. The Burnetts also gave $2.96 million with a state match for construction of the Burnett Honors College building.

Orlando/Orange County Convention and Visitors Bureau  $10 million, including a state matching grant, to establish five $2 million eminent scholar endowed chairs at the Rosen College of Hospitality Management.

Harris Corporation  $6 million, including a state matching grant, to provide laboratory and other equipment for the Harris Corporation Engineering Center in the College of Engineering and Computer Science.

Kenneth G. Dixon  $5 million, including a state matching grant, to create the Kenneth G. Dixon School of Accounting in the College of Business Administration. Dixon is a 1975 UCF alumnus.

Helen and Richard DeVos  $5 million, including a state matching grant, to establish an endowment to support the DeVos Sport Business Management Program in the College of Business Administration.

Dr. Phillips Inc.  $5 million, including a state matching grant, to create the Dr. P. Phillips School of Real Estate in the College of Business Administration.

Sonja and Tony Nicholson  $4 million, including a state matching grant, to establish an endowed fund for the Nicholson School of Communication in the College of Arts and Sciences. The Nicholsons also provided a $250,000 challenge grant to support the school. In addition, the Nicholsons gave $2 million to build the Sonja and Anthony Nicholson Fieldhouse and $100,000 to support the Wayne Densch Sports Center.

UCF Board of Trustees Chair Dick Nunis is serving as the national campaign chair for Central to Our Future. Al Weiss, a 1976 UCF alumnus, is chairing the campaigns steering committee. Weiss is also a UCF trustee.

For information on supporting UCF, call 407-882-1220 or visit www.foundation.ucf.edu.
