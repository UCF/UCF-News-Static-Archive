UCF to Hold Commencement Ceremonies April 30 and May 1

By Chad Binette April 23, 2004

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Linda Gray

    • 407-823-2947

  • Tom Evelyn

    • 407-823-5988

  • Webcast of ceremonies

  • Print this Page

More than 4,700 University of Central Florida students are scheduled to graduate during five ceremonies Friday, April 30, and Saturday, May 1, at the UCF Arena.

The expected graduates include 3,837 UCF students who signed up to receive bachelors degrees, 823 students for masters degrees and 65 for doctoral degrees, based on intent-to-graduate forms students fill out before final grades are available. Another 18 students signed up to receive education specialist degrees.

The first ceremony, for the College of Engineering and Computer Science, the School of Optics, and College of Arts and Sciences graduates majoring in anthropology, economics, political science, psychology and sociology, will begin at 10 a.m. Friday, April 30. The speaker will be Bill Parsons, manager of the Space Shuttle Program for NASA.

At 2 p.m. Friday, UCF will award degrees to the remaining Arts and Sciences graduates. Dr. Geraldine Gerri Ferris, a UCF trustee and retired periodontist, will be the speaker.

The first ceremony on Saturday, May 1, will begin at 8:30 a.m. and will feature College of Business Administration graduates. U.S. Rep. John Mica, R-Winter Park, will address the business graduates.

At noon Saturday, College of Health and Public Affairs graduates will receive their degrees. The speaker will be U.S. Rep. Dave Weldon, R-Palm Bay.

The last ceremony, at 3:30 p.m. Saturday, will be for College of Education and Rosen School of Hospitality Management graduates. The speaker will be Allen Trovillion, an emeritus director of the UCF Foundation who is a former state representative and a former mayor of Winter Park.

Guests must have tickets, which are distributed by graduating students, to attend the ceremonies. Guests without tickets can view the ceremonies live via closed-circuit television from Room 121 of the Classroom I building. The ceremonies also will be broadcast live online at http://webcast.oir.ucf.edu.
