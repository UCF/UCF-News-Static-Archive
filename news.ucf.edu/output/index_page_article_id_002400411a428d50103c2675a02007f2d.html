Three UCF Students Honored at National Theater Festival

By Kristi Bartlett May 10, 2005

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Chad Binette

    • 407-823-6312

  • UCF Theatre Department

  • Print this Page

Tramaine Berryhill, a spring graduate of the UCF theater program, will study for two weeks this summer with Broadway designers Ming Cho Lee and Constance Huffman.

 

Berryhill, of Miami, is one of three designers in the United States who won the fellowship through the Kennedy Center American College Theater Festival. He graduated with a bachelor's degree in fine arts and is working this summer at the Utah Shakespearean Festival. Graduate students and young professionals also were eligible for the fellowship.

 

The rigorous class in Washington will focus on relationships between designers and directors by exploring works by Shakespeare and from Greek performances and operas.

 

Two students in the University of Central Floridas Musical Theatre program also were honored last month at the festival.

 

Desmond Newson, of Jacksonville, won the VASTA Award and the TVI Award. VASTA, the Voice and Speech Trainers Association, and TVI, Talent Venture Inc., reward students who show promise and exhibit excellence in the fields of theater, television and studio work.

 

Ed Davis, of Delray Beach, won the Best Scene Partner Award, a national award given to one performer out of the 16 who qualified.

 

Newson and Davis, who are now seniors, represented UCF beautifully and professionally. I think that we will be seeing a lot more of them in the future, said Professor Mark Brotherton, who worked with them on their performances.

 

Both students were invited to attend the national festival after their performances at the Irene Ryan Acting Competition at the regional festival in February in Palm Beach County.

 

Newson is currently in South Korea as a part of a tour with a production of Fame, and Davis is performing this summer in Texas.

 

Artistic Director Roberta Sloan of the UCF Conservatory Theatre said that it has now joined the ranks of national prominence.

 

The Kennedy Center American College Theater Festival was founded in 1969 by Kennedy Center chairman Roger L. Stevens. It involves 18,000 students from colleges and universities nationwide and serves as a catalyst in improving the quality of college theater.
