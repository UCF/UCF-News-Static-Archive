UCF College of Medicine Milestone: First Concrete Poured for Classroom Building

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala June 12, 2008

Photo: Jacque Brund

Construction crews poured the first concrete for the foundation of the College of Medicine classroom building at Lake Nona.

UCF’s College of Medicine reached another milestone Thursday. From 7 a.m. to 11 a.m., crews began pouring the foundation for the school’s classroom building.

About 20 construction workers spent three hours dumping 350 cubic yards of concrete – the equivalent of 35 concrete mixer trucks -- for the foundation of the classroom building. The building is one of two UCF facilities that will anchor the medical city at Lake Nona.

The classroom building, located next to the UCF Burnett Biomedical Sciences building, has a view of the Orlando Utilities Commission chiller plant and Burnham Institute for Medical Research, which are also under construction.

“It’s very exciting,” said Maher Chatila, the construction manager of the UCF project. “We are moving quickly. The Burnett building is about 55 percent complete, and this is a major step in getting the classroom building off the ground.”

The classroom building (168,000 square feet) will be home to medical students, an anatomy lab, simulations labs and a medical library. The biomedical sciences building (198,000 square feet) will house state-of-the-art research labs, as well as the M.D. Anderson Cancer Institute.

The research building’s estimated completion date is May 2009, and the classroom building’s is March 2010. Both facilities are being constructed to meet Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED) certification.

The College of Medicine began taking applications for admissions this month. The school, which has preliminary accreditation, will open its doors in the fall of 2009. It is the first medical school in the nation to offer full scholarships to its entire incoming class. The scholarships, worth $160,000, cover tuition and living expenses for all four years of the M.D. program.

Earlier this month, UCF Pres. John Hitt and first lady Martha Hitt held an event at the Burnett House to recognize those who have donated to the college.

About 120 guests attended the evening event at the president’s Burnett House. It was a time to celebrate and to thank the many people who contributed to the college’s facilities, under construction at Lake Nona. Donors gave more than $100 million for the Burnett Biomedical Sciences building, the medical library and the classroom building. Those who contributed to the scholarship campaign also were honored. The unique scholarships were possible because the community donated more than $6.4 million to fund them.

President Hitt kicked off the evening by recognizing the vital community contributions. Michael Minton and Rasesh Thakkar, co-chairs of the Scholarship Committee spoke about the scholarship campaign and Dean Deborah German M.D. gave those in attendance an update on the college.

The College of Medicine was established in 2006 to increase opportunities for medical education in Florida, address the growing physician shortage nationwide and provide economic benefits to Central Florida and the state through research and technology.

The four-year medical program will capitalize on UCF’s existing strengths in biomedical sciences, modeling and simulation, and optics and photonics. Other programs in materials science, psychology, chemistry, film and digital media, hospitality and nursing will support the College of Medicine. The college will eventually produce about 120 medical graduates each year.
