UCF Art Gallery Will Showcase Painter Gary Bolding's Latest Work in February

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala Jan. 26, 2007

Photo: Courtesy of the UCF Art Gallery

A sample of artist Gary Bolding's work. Bolding is an art professor at Stetson University.

An exhibit that marks the end of one journey and the start of another is coming to the UCF Art Gallery.

Gary Bolding will be showcasing paintings from two different periods of his work from Feb. 1 to March 2. The UCF Art Gallery, located in the Visual Arts Building on the East Orlando campus, is open from 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. Monday through Fridays. There is no cost to view the exhibit.

Bolding, an art professor at Stetson University in DeLand, creates polished, surreal paintings that take thoughtful but humorous looks at historical and pop culture references. He is known for his highly realistic paintings. His latest endeavor has been making large abstract paintings.

"This exhibition marks a point of transition," Bolding said. "It includes the end of one body of work and the beginning of another. In these two groups of paintings I attempt to establish a non-ideological position for myself as a painter, to retrace my steps and explore a road not taken earlier."

Boldings work has been shown at the Bernice Steinbaum Gallery in Miami and the Tatistcheff Gallery in New York City. Bolding has one-person exhibitions in venues throughout Florida, the Southeast, Europe and Mexico. Hes received several awards and honors throughout his career and has been the recipient of two Individual Artist Fellowships from the State of Florida. He also was a finalist for a NEA/Southern Arts Federation Fellowship.

Bolding earned his bachelors degree at Hendrix College in Arkansas before studying at the Art Students League of New York and the New York Academy of Art. He completed his masters degree in fine arts at Brooklyn College. He has been teaching at Stetson University since 1989.

The Polk Museum of Art in Lakeland organized the UCF exhibit. For more information, call 407-823-3161 or go to www.art.ucf.edu.
