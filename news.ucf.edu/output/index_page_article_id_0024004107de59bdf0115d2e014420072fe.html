UCF Hosts College Access Summit With K-12 Leaders, Community Colleges

By Christine Dellert Oct. 31, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Christine Dellert, News and Information

    • 407-823-2947

  • UCF Regional Campuses

  • Print this Page

With college admissions processes and enrollment demands changing, the University of Central Florida is reaching out to regional K-12 leaders about higher-education access.

UCF is hosting the first College Access Summit on Monday, Nov. 5, at its Orlando campus. The event is expected to draw K-12 educators and administrators from 10 Central Florida school districts and officials from the region’s community colleges.

The summit is scheduled from 9 a.m. to 2 p.m. at the Live Oak Room in Ferrell Commons behind Parking Garage B. UCF President John Hitt will give a keynote luncheon address at about 12:30 p.m.

The summit will feature discussions and break-out sessions about the current state of Florida colleges’ admissions, focusing on increasing selectivity and geographic reach, anticipated enrollment freezes due to budget constraints and a program that guarantees access to a UCF bachelor’s degree for graduates of partner community colleges.

“Monday’s summit is an initial conversation between K-12 leaders and higher-education leaders on what college access looks like today and what some of the trends are saying it may look like in the future,” said David Harrison, vice provost for UCF’s 11 regional campuses.

The meeting should generate ideas among K-12 and post-secondary education officials about expanding the college attendance rate of Central Florida high school graduates despite current challenges, Harrison said. The admissions process is becoming more competitive, and the state’s growth and budget cuts are presenting obstacles for higher education.

To improve access, UCF became the first state university to sign a resolution with regional community college leaders that enhances Florida’s 2+2 agreement, which allows community college graduates to transfer to a state university for a bachelor’s degree. The 2005 resolution more closely aligned UCF’s admissions policies and procedures with its community college partners, resulting in an easier transition for students, preferential admissions for graduates of partner community colleges, the expansion of joint-use facilities and joint faculty and staff and immediate data sharing.

“Even as we’re capping freshman enrollment, we are still keeping that guarantee with the consortium partners strongly in place,” Harrison said.

UCF’s partners in the DirectConnect to UCF program are Brevard, Lake-Sumter, Seminole and Valencia community colleges. Central Florida Community College is also participating in the summit.

Invitations to the event were distributed in conjunction with UCF’s community college partners. For more information or to RSVP, call UCF’s Regional Campuses administrative offices at 407-823-4547.
