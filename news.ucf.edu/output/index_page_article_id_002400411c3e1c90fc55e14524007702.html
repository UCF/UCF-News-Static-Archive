Hospitality Graduates Heading to Olympics to Help Serve Athletes

By Tom Evelyn June 8, 2004

Photo: Jerry Klein

Anne Cecil will join six other UCF hospitality management graduates who will work as team leaders at the Olympics.

Full Photo

Seven of the University of Central Floridas top hospitality management graduates are heading to Athens this summer to help feed hundreds of the worlds best athletes.

The Rosen College of Hospitality Management graduates will work as team leaders in a number of positions with ARAMARK, the company providing food service in the Olympic Village for the athletes, coaches and staff at the Summer Games in Greece. ARAMARK has been feeding Olympic athletes since the Mexico City Summer Games in 1968.

ARAMARK came to speak in my Leadership Strategies class and talked about the opportunity in Greece, said Cecily Moll, who graduated in the spring with a degree in hospitality management. I was amazed at what they were offering. I thought that working with ARAMARK would be a great starting management position, and the chance to start off in Greece at the Summer Games was too good to be true.

Moll will join hospitality graduates Anne Cecil, Meredith Eberhardt, Elizabeth Rodriguez, Lisa Savitch, Julie Stockton and Arin Sugerman. Theyll start working in Athens July 15, but several of the graduates are leaving early to tour Europe.

The team leaders will work in the main dining and casual dining halls of the Olympic Village, which seat about 6,000 and 800 respectively. They will be placed in a variety of positions, including food service and preparation and administration.

The greatest gift I received as a student of the Rosen College is the opportunity to serve as a team leader for ARAMARK during the Summer Games, said Arin Sugerman, who graduated with a hospitality management degree in May. I truly believe that without the skills and experiences I had as a student at the Rosen College, this opportunity would have passed me by.

ARAMARK, which also provides food service at UCF, recruited students from hospitality programs at UCF, Penn State University, the University of Massachusetts, Boston University, Johnson and Wales University, Cornell University, the University of Delaware and Texas Tech University. The company chose three to seven students from each university and several students already working for ARAMARK at other schools.

The company chose from a pool of students who had two years of experience working in the industry and who were interested in working for ARAMARK after graduation. More than 60 students were selected based on the competitive process.

The recruiters were extremely selective and having the Rosen College on my resume gave me the edge I needed to move on in the interviewing process, said Anne Cecil, who graduated with a hospitality management degree in the spring. The professors at the Rosen College not only lecture about industry trends and case studies, they teach us how to be leaders in an evolving field.

The Rosen College of Hospitality Management moved into its new, $28 million campus near the International Drive tourist district in January. The former school, which became a college last month, was based on the main UCF campus for 20 years.

Students are now a short drive from Orlandos major attractions, top hotels, the Orange County Convention Center and numerous chain and independent restaurants. Students are required to work in the hospitality industry while studying at the Rosen College.

Serving the athletes at the Summer Games in Athens will provide yet another educational opportunity that will serve the hospitality graduates well, Cecil said.

The Games should serve as a uniting force to pull everyone back together and remind us that our similarities are more prevalent than our differences, Cecil said. I hope that I will gain a new perspective of global diversity as I believe this knowledge will serve me well as I continue my career in the field of tourism.

For more information on the UCF Rosen College of Hospitality Management, visit www.hospitality.ucf.edu . For more on ARAMARK, visit www.aramark.com.
