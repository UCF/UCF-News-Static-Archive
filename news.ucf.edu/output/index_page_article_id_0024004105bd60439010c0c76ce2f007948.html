Neighbors Encouraged to Attend Open House on Stadium Traffic, Parking

By Chad Binette July 11, 2006

Photo: Courtesy of UCF Athletics

A rendering of the UCF on-campus football stadium.

Full Photo

Neighbors and others interested in making suggestions for traffic and parking plans for the University of Central Floridas new on-campus football stadium are encouraged to attend an open house Wednesday, July 19.

The open house will be held from 6:30 to 8:30 p.m. in the first-floor art gallery at the Visual Arts Building on UCFs East Orlando campus. Free parking is available in the lot just west of the building.

Residents will be able talk one-on-one with UCF staff and representatives from PBS&J, the consulting firm that is helping the university develop a Transportation Management Plan for the stadium. A traffic simulation video and campus area maps will be on display to facilitate discussions between the project team and the public.

UCFs new stadium is scheduled to open for the 2007 season on the northern part of the campus, near the UCF Arena and several athletic fields.

The universitys goal is to develop an efficient traffic and parking plan that will minimize the impact on adjacent neighborhoods. The Transportation Management Plan will address on- and off-campus traffic flow, parking and the use of shuttle buses to take fans to and from games.

Neighborhood groups, business leaders and others who would like UCF representatives to attend their meetings and discuss the stadium can contact Cynthia Sucher, associate vice president for Community Relations, at 407-823-6609 or csucher@mail.ucf.edu.

Anyone with questions about the open house can contact Gary Carlin of PBS&J at 407-806-4482, 800-284-5182 or gacarlin@pbsj.com.

Anyone with disabilities who may require special accommodations under the Americans With Disabilities Act should contact Abra Horne, PBS&Js public involvement coordinator, at 482 S. Keller Road, Orlando, FL 32810; at 407-806-4493 or 800-284-5182 or at aehorne@pbsj.com by July 17.
