'MuscleBound' Performance Calls Attention to Male Body Image Concerns

By Sam Gardner Updated Oct. 3, 2006

Photo: Courtesy of Michael Feldman

Michael Feldman, an actor, writer, playwright and former assistant on “The Daily Show with Jon Stewart,” will perform "MuscleBound" Monday night at UCF.

Full Photo

Whoever said size doesnt matter has never met Michael Feldman.  The actor, writer, playwright and former assistant on The Daily Show with Jon Stewart brought his latest project, MuscleBound, to the UCF campus on Monday, Oct. 2. 

MuscleBound, a 90-minute, one-man show, discusses the state of male body image and the damaging measures men go to for the sake of their own appearances. Feldman tells the story of three men -- a gay college student, a personal trainer and a newlywed documentary filmmaker -- who all rely on different destructive extremes to alter their bodies over the course of one year.

About 300 people attended the presentation. Following the performance, a 30-minute panel discussion on male body image issues featured Feldman; "MuscleBound" director Adam Laupus; UCF dietitian Preeti Wilkhu; David L. Wallace, director of the UCF Counseling Center; and Reach for Wellness peer educator Kelly Ferris.

Incorporated into the live show is a filmed documentary featuring interviews with several men discussing what they secretly think about their bodies.

The show presents a thorough depiction of the life cycle of an obsession, from the first thoughts to the subconscious impulses that eventually overtake the mind and body.  Feldman looks head-on at the body image issues that drive steroid and supplement abuse. His performance brings to light the confrontations men have with anorexia and bulimia, diseases rarely considered to affect males.

In the documentary, directed and edited by New York University graduate and actor Adam Laupus, men share their thoughts on binge eating and the sharp rise of muscle dysmorphia, the disorder that leads a person to believe that he or she is too small or not muscular enough.

Through MuscleBound, Feldman and Laupus aim to redefine the meaning of the word masculinity and enlighten those who are unaware of the lengths men go to have the body of their dreams.

The UCF Recreation and Wellness Center sponsored Mondays performance.
