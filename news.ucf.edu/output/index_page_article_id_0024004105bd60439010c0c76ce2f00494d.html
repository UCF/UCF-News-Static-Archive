Notable UCF Alumni Honored at Black & Gold Gala

By UCF Staff Nov. 3, 2006

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Darla Olive

    • 407-823-3288

  • UCF Alumni Association

  • Print this Page

The chief information officer for the U.S. Senate and the executive director of a local nonprofit agency that provides health care for uninsured residents were honored by the UCF Alumni Association during a banquet Thursday evening.

J. Greg Hanson and Ana Hanshuh were among 14 graduates honored at the Black & Gold Gala at Hard Rock Live. The banquet was held during Knightfest, the weeklong Homecoming celebration at UCF.

The UCF Alumni Awards program honors alumni who reach for the stars, bringing distinction to themselves and the university through professional achievement, volunteer work and community service. 

There are five award categories at the Black & Gold Gala. Hanson, a 1987 graduate, received the Distinguished Alumnus Award. Hanson earned a doctoral degree in Computer Science from UCF and a masters of science degree in Information Systems from the U.S. Air Force Institute of Technology. Hanson oversees the Senates information infrastructure, including data, telecommunications, back office systems, information assurance and technology for emergency operations and continuity of operations. 

Hanshuh, a 1998 graduate of UCF, received the Community Service Award. As executive director of the nonprofit Mark Dogoli MD Wellness Center, Hanshuh works to bring primary wellness care to uninsured residents of Central Florida. The sudden, unexplained death of Dogoli, Hanshuhs friend and a UCF alumnus, inspired her to open medical wellness centers in Dogolis name throughout the country. 

Manny Rodriguez, who graduated from UCF in 1975, received the Service to UCF Award. He devoted his life to serving the university before he died in 2005, three weeks after the grand opening of the Fairwinds Alumni Center. Rodriguez long dreamed of a permanent campus home for all of UCFs graduates, and he served as the director of community and government relations for the UCF Alumni Association until he died. 

Julie Helton won the Distinguished Student Award. Helton impacts the lives of children daily as a senior administrator of Curriculum Services for Orange County Public Schools, and she is also in pursuit of her third UCF degree, a doctorate in Elementary Education.

As a senior consultant for DuPont, Professional Achievement Award winner Michael H. Schollas expertise has been sought in many areas, including research, regulatory affairs, marketing and business management. Scholla has worked with DuPont for 15 years.

Other award winners at the Black & Gold Gala were: Robin Cowie (1993), best known as one of the producers of The Blair Witch Project; Damien F. Madsen (1986), a partner with Broad Street Partners, LLC; Jessica Mock (2002), a fluids principal engineer for NASA; Kristen J. Springfield (1991), a math teacher at Sanford Middle School who was named Floridas top math teacher and selected as Floridas representative for the 2005 Presidential Awards for Excellence in Mathematics and Science Teaching; and Keith Flannery (1986), the founder and owner of Medware, Inc., a medical transcription company.

Also, Vincent G. Torpy, Jr. (1981), a judge who sits on the Court of Appeal for the Fifth District; Dr. Marcy Shapiro (1991), speech pathology director at Florida Hospital Waterman; Robert M. Easton Jr. (1977), who was appointed by Gov. Jeb Bush to the State Board of Optometry and recognized as one of the top optometrists in America since 2001; and Charles Zell (2003), a former managing partner at Rhythm & Flow Ultralounge and now managing partner at Bong Spirit Import Company.

For more information on the UCF Alumni Association, go to www.ucfalumni.com.
