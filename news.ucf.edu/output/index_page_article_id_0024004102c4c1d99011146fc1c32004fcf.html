UCFs Al Harms Promoted to Vice President for Strategic Planning and Initiatives

By Chad Binette Aug. 8, 2007

Photo: UCF News file

Retired Navy Vice Admiral Al Harms began working at UCF in 2005 after a distinguished 33-year career in the Navy.

Full Photo

  • Chad Binette, News and Information

    • 407-823-6312

  • Print this Page

Retired Navy Vice Admiral Al Harms has proven that he can bring diverse groups of people together to address challenging issues such as campus security, energy conservation and preparing for UCFs first football games on campus.

Pleased with Harms leadership on task forces that covered all of those issues, University of Central Florida President John Hitt has promoted him to vice president for strategic planning and initiatives.

Harms new responsibilities will reflect much of the work that he has undertaken since his arrival on campus in 2005. He will oversee the universitys strategic planning efforts, and he will manage various strategic initiatives at the request of the president. In addition to campus security and energy efficiency, other examples of such initiatives include cost containment and reduction measures and improvements to operational effectiveness and administrative efficiency.

During his two years at UCF, Admiral Harms has demonstrated an exceptional ability to bring people on and off campus together even in the most challenging and difficult circumstances, Hitt said. His leadership, optimism and dedication to our students, faculty and staff are invaluable assets to our university.

Following a 33-year career in the Navy, Harms joined UCF as an assistant vice president for research and special assistant to the president. Two months after he arrived, Harms was appointed to lead a task force that updated UCFs tailgating policies following the shooting death of Officer Mario Jenkins before a football game at the Citrus Bowl.

With his promotion, Harms will retain his special assistant to the president role. In that capacity, he will continue to represent Hitt on internal and external matters, task forces, committees and commissions as assigned. Harms' salary will remain the same.

In the Navy, Harms distinguished career included major operational commands at sea and ashore. His last active duty assignment was as commander of the Naval Education and Training Command and director of Naval Education and Training on the Chief of Naval Operations staff.

As the Navys chief learning officer, he was credited with transforming the Navys educational curricula and expanding its technical and distributed learning capabilities for more than 380,000 officers and enlisted personnel. He also implemented sweeping information technology and infrastructure upgrades to support a worldwide learning system.
