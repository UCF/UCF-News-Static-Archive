Fall Semester to Feature New Student Health Center, Student Housing, Programs

By Chad Binette Aug. 19, 2005

Photo: Jacque Brund

As many as 45,000 students may be enrolled for the fall. The semester started Monday.

The University of Central Florida will open a video game academy in downtown Orlando and a new Student Health Center on the East Orlando campus during the fall semester, which begins Monday.

 

Students are moving into new housing in the Convocation Center area on the East Orlando campus and at the Rosen College of Hospitality Management near International Drive. And UCF will begin offering several new Business Administration programs at its regional campuses.

 

About 44,500 to 45,000 students are expected to attend UCF during the fall semester, which concludes with commencement ceremonies Dec. 12 and 13.

 

The Florida Interactive Entertainment Academy will be joined by other School of Film and Digital Media programs in the former Expo Centre, across the street from the TD Waterhouse Centre. Created through a partnership with the state and Electronic Arts, FIEA offers a team-based, industry-led education that prepares students to succeed in interactive entertainment industries like video gaming.

 

The new Student Health Center, which is scheduled to open in October, will feature a much larger pharmacy, a new high-tech digital X-ray department and a large, private Womens Clinic. Services that could be added later include sports medicine, orthopedic, dermatology and a dental clinic. A grand opening ceremony is scheduled for Oct. 21.

 

Students already have started moving into new housing near the planned, 10,000-seat Convocation Center and at the Rosen College. Five hundred of the 2,000 beds in the Convocation Center area became available this fall, and all 392 beds at the new Rosen College housing opened.

 

Working professionals with at least three years of experience will take classes two nights a week in a new, 21-month professional MBA program at the Daytona Beach campus. A new bachelors degree in business administration is being offered for the first time at the Daytona Beach, Cocoa and Palm Bay campuses.

 

Other new academic programs this fall include a golf management track at the Rosen College. Students will take courses about topics such as the rules of golf, golf course management and golf course facility design. The College of Education is offering a minor in early childhood education at UCF/DeLand for the first time.

 

New College of Arts and Sciences masters programs this fall include creative writing, entrepreneurial digital cinema and visual language and interactive media. A doctoral program in sociology also debuts this fall. New bachelors programs include nanoscience and nanotechnology studies and photography.

 

The School of Nursing is launching two new tracks -- clinical nurse leader and nurse educator -- in its masters program.

 

The College of Optics and Photonics plans to begin construction on a 21,000-foot addition to its building on the East Orlando campus. The new addition will provide more laboratory and office space for optics and photonics research, along with an incubator area designed to help small businesses get started.

 

A Lou Frey Institute of Politics and Government symposium on religion and politics will feature Ralph Reed, former executive director of the Christian Coalition, and Nadine Strossen, national president of the American Civil Liberties Union. Reed and Strossen will give the keynote addresses at 7 p.m. Sept. 26 at the Student Union. More information on the symposium is available at www.loufrey.org.

 

Three community lectures about Puerto Rican history will be coordinated by the Latin American, Caribbean and Latino Studies Program, which is also offering students a course in Puerto Rican history for the first time. More information on the lectures and a series of art and music events is posted at www.cas.ucf.edu/lacls.

 

The Office of Global Perspectives Distinguished Visitors Series will include classroom presentations and community discussions by Myles Frechette, a former U.S. ambassador to Colombia; businessman Thomas DiBacco, author of Made in the U.S.A.: The History of American Business; and Azizah Al-Hibri, author of Womens Rights and Islam, Islam in America. Go to www.cas.ucf.edu/global for more information.
