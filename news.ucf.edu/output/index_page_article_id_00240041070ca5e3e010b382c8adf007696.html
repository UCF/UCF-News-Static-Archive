UCF Hosts Free Public Viewing of Soccer World Cup Matches Throughout June

By Zenaida Gonzalez Kotala June 5, 2006

Photo: Courtesy of FIFAworldcup.com

Italy and Mexico, two of the participants in the 2002 World Cup, will be among the 32 teams competing for the 2006 title.

World Cup mania is coming to the University of Central Florida beginning at noon on June 9.

The International Services Center and the International Students Association have teamed up to sponsor a month-long World Cup Community Festival. Students and the public can watch the soccer games on a 150 inch-LCD projection screen at the Barbara Ying Center.

"Its an opportunity for our students and the public to come enjoy the games," said Rachid Bendriss, assistant director for the International Services Center. "Its a chance to be part of the excitement of the games."

Think of "el Mundial," as it is referred to in Latin America, as a month-long Super Bowl.

Soccers popularity has boomed in the United States over the past few years. There are three professional leagues, and more than 3.2 million boys and girls ages 5 to 19 are playing in U.S. Youth Soccer.

This is the first time the International Services Center -- which provides support to foreign students on campus -- and the student association have jointly sponsored an event for the World Cup. The last time the student association showed the European Championship League final soccer match between two European teams, 45 students showed up with only e-mail notice on the day of the event, the association president said.

"Its a cultural thing," said Cornelius Mamboleo, president of the ISA and a die-hard soccer fan. "Every male grew up watching it and playing it, so the games, they are special."

Mamboleo, a native of Kenya and a senior majoring in organizational communication at UCF, said his father will likely be cooking up barbecue with friends for the games back home. For his part, he plans to watch as many games as possible, including matchups that involve Brazil.

Bendriss expects the first game between host country Germany and Costa Rica on June 9 to be popular as well as those featuring teams from Japan and South Korea, because of the number of Asian students enrolled at UCF.

The festival and free public viewing will continue weekdays from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. through July 5 -- just before the finals. Light food and refreshments will be provided by ISA, which is funded by the Student Government Association.
