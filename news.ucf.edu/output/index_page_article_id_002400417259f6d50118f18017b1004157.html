UCF's Third-Annual Research Week to Focus on Environment

By Brandon Bielich March 27, 2008

Photo: Jacque Brund

The University of Central Florida will showcase some of its best environmental research, beginning on Monday, March 31, which marks the start of Research Week.

This year’s theme of the environment includes projects that deal with conservation, energy and quality of life. The series of events will feature presentations of environmental research conducted by UCF faculty, undergraduates, and graduate students.

“UCF Research Week is really an opportunity for the university and members of the university community to recognize some of the quality research being done here and explore its impact beyond the university,” said Patricia Bishop, vice provost and dean of Graduate Studies and co-chair of the event.

Monday will include overviews of sustainability projects on campus, featuring UCF’s Sustainability Alliance, and a research summary from UCF’s Florida Solar Energy Center, which has worked to develop innovative energy solutions. While solar technology remains a big part of the center’s focus, FSEC scientists are among the top in the world in hydrogen and fuel-cell development and research, as well as in energy-saving construction research.

Director Jim Fenton will give the FSEC presentation at 9 a.m. Monday in the Key West Ballroom of the Student Union.

At 12:30 p.m. Monday, a short ceremony at Ferrell Commons will feature the planting of five Nuttall Oak trees, a symbolic gesture to offset any carbon emissions participants in the week may have expended to get to and from campus. 

Highlighting Monday’s presentations at 1 p.m. in the Live Oak Room will be a panel discussion regarding, “Water’s Journey through the Everglades.” The exhibit is a sustainability simulation that allows viewers to experience the formation, destruction and restoration of the Florida Everglades, a main source of the state’s drinking water.

Faculty from UCF’s Institute for Simulation and Training (IST) and the Computer Science, Biology, Statistics and English departments are developing the exhibit, which is a four-year project funded by a $2.7 million grant from the National Science Foundation.

“The goal is to make people more aware of the impact they have on the environment,” said Eileen Smith from IST. “Although humans have had a great impact on the Everglades, we can’t throw in the towel. The Everglades is still beautiful; this asks the question, ‘How do we make sure it is still here in 100 years?’”

Upon completion, the exhibit will become part of the new EcoDiscovery wing at the Museum of Discovery and Science in Fort Lauderdale.

UCF researchers who are developing the project will share their perspectives on how the habitat has changed and what the future holds.

Sessions on Tuesday and Wednesday will cover conservation and economic issues related to the environment. UCF biologists, as well as members of myregion.org, a regional organization working to best prepare Central Florida for growth and change in the 21st Century, will participate.

Throughout the week, undergraduate and graduate students will present posters about their research. UCF faculty will serve as judges for the poster sessions. Several researchers will present a panel discussion on career pathways in their disciplines.

Student researchers who win awards during the week will be recognized, along with their faculty mentors, at an invitation-only breakfast on April 11.

“Students who become involved in Research Week will get a clearer understanding of the diverse options available to them if they pursue the arts, humanities or sciences as a career,” said Alison Morrison-Shetlar, dean of Undergraduate Studies and event co-chair.  “Community members will be able to learn about specific projects under way at UCF to make the world a better place to live.”

Research Week is free and open to the public. Most events will take place in the Student Union. Parking information for visitors coming from off campus can be obtained by contacting Lindsay Newell at 407-823-1927 or lnewell@mail.ucf.edu

For more information and for a complete schedule of events, visit: http://www.researchweek.ucf.edu.
