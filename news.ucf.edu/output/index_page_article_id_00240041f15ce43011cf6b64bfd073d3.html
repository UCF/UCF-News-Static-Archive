UCF Diversity Week Focuses on Building Communities of Justice

By Courtney Gilmartin Oct. 13, 2008

Photo: Jacque Brund

A participant in an origami class blows air into his project during one of many Diversity Week activities on campus.

The University of Central Florida’s 16th-annual Diversity Week features presentations, activities and film screenings centered on diversity and creating understanding and tolerance.

The theme for Diversity Week, which begins Monday, Oct. 13, is “Building Communities of Justice.”  Events include origami; foreign language and self defense classes; and discussions on environmental justice, hate groups and ethics in sports.

On Monday, the kick-off breakfast featured a keynote address from Angela King, a UCF graduate student and former neo-Nazi who is now involved in social justice work. 

She told the more than 350 people gathered in the Student Union about her journey from a child with low self-esteem to her days as a neo-Nazi. Then she talked about her road to understanding and wanting to improve and not destroy the world. The revelations happened while she was in federal prison at age 23.

“Although I found incarceration, I also found my liberation,” King told the audience. “I learned self respect. I learned value in the human life.”

She credited UCF for giving her the opportunity to reinvent herself and said she dedicates her life to helping others find the same road to justice.

“It’s my hope that my experiences and my story will motivate others into action,” she said. 

The breakfast is one of many events planned for the week.

Anthony Rapp, of the Tony award-winning musical “Rent,” will discuss his memoir and experiences as a member of the Gay, Lesbian, Bisexual and Transgender (GLBT) community during a presentation in the Pegasus Ballroom of the Student Union at 8 p.m. Monday. A book signing and autograph session will follow the event.

Scott Turner Schofield, a transgender performance artist, will discuss creating a trans-friendly campus during his 2 p.m. presentation on Wednesday.

On Thursday, the United Nations Association will sponsor “Nurture the Nations: A UN Day Celebration.”  The event begins at 6:30 p.m. and will feature musical and cultural dance performances.  Hiro Sakurai, a representative of the United Nations, will give a keynote speech on humanitarian, political and environmental efforts throughout the world.

Films such as “American History X” and Spike Lee’s award-winning documentary “When the Levees Broke” will be shown in the Student Union during the week.

The Library Film Festival throughout the week will feature “Crash,” “Life is Beautiful” and other films that highlight diversity topics.

The Office of Diversity Initiatives will be collecting canned goods for Second Harvest Food Bank this week. Collection boxes will be available all week at the Student Union and at the Research Pavilion in Research Park.

Paintings and other art by children at the Creative School for Children will be displayed throughout October at the UCF Library. The school collaborated with the Office of Diversity Initiatives on the project.

Diversity Week is coordinated by the Office of Diversity Initiatives.  Student groups such as the Student Government Association; Campus Peace Action; and the Gay, Lesbian, Bisexual Student Union were also involved in planning events.

For a complete schedule of events, visit http://www.diversity.ucf.edu/docs/DiversityWeek_2008_schedule.pdf.
