Plans for UCF Med School Campus at Lake Nona Begin to Take Shape

By Tom Evelyn April 23, 2006

Photo: UCF/Lake Nona

This early conceptual rendering shows the medical library (in purple) in the center of the campus with the Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences just to the east and the medical college to the northeast.

A team of planners, designers and architects has started the master planning process for the UCF Healthcare Campus at Lake Nona, where the colleges of medicine and biomedical sciences and a medical library will be nestled among homes, shops and a research park.

The team unveiled early plans Friday for the 50-acre campus that include a quadrangle of classroom and research buildings intersected by a north-south, tree-lined grassy mall. The 300-foot green space will provide a dramatic view to the neoclassical-design library, the campus centerpiece.

The campus will be bordered by shops and restaurants to the north and a scenic wetland to the south. A boulevard with wide sidewalks and fountains will connect apartments and condominiums on the east-side of campus with the research park on the west.

The design of the campus will facilitate and encourage instruction and research between the colleges of medicine and biomedical sciences and other health sciences, and it will leave room for expansion as UCFs biomedical programs grow, UCF Provost Terry Hickey said.

We have the opportunity to build collaborations between medicine and other health professions -- nursing, allied health, communicative disorders -- so students share time and training opportunities, Hickey said following a presentation of the preliminary master plan on Friday. The design of the space can reinforce that.

UCF is planning the campus with the developers of Lake Nona, a burgeoning community in south Orlando just east of the Orlando International Airport. The campus is located in Lake Nona, just south of State Road 417 at a new interchange between Boggy Creek and Narcoosee roads.

The team is working together to help the campus blend in with and take advantage of the amenities of the surrounding community while retaining the atmosphere of a classic college campus.

The Florida Board of Governors, which oversees the states 11 public universities, approved creating medical colleges at UCF and Florida International University on March 23. The colleges need final approval and funding from the state Legislature.

In the meantime, UCF plans to begin construction in January on the Burnett College of Biomedical Sciences building as part of the campus first phase that will include the medical college and library. The biomedical sciences building could be completed by fall 2008, when classes may start.

The campus will eventually accommodate as many as 5,000 students and faculty who will share up to 2 million square feet of research space and 500,000 square feet of classrooms.

UCF has raised more than $100 million for construction of the medical and biomedical sciences college and library. That total includes private donations, state matching funds and the value of the Tavistock Groups gift of 50 acres at Lake Nona. Tavistock also led the group of donors with a pledge of $12.5 million toward the project.

Pending funding from the Legislature, UCF will begin hiring faculty and developing a curriculum for the college of medicine. The degree program calls for a four-year curriculum that will eventually produce about 120 medical graduates each year.

The college and its degree program will capitalize on the universitys existing strengths in biomedical sciences, modeling and simulation, and optics and photonics. Other programs in materials science, psychology, chemistry, film and digital media, and nursing also will support the program.

A joint cooperation agreement signed last month by UCF and the Scripps Research Institute will foster collaborative research in some of these areas. UCF and Scripps share many common research interests, including working toward better therapies for cardiovascular disease, cancer and neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimers.
