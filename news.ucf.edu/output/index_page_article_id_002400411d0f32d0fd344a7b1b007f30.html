Several professors at the University of Central Florida are available to talk to reporters as experts on various aspects of the 2004 presidential campaign. All of the faculty members listed below except Stephen Sloan and Mason Cash can be contacted at 407-823-2608 or by fax at 407-823-0051. Contact information for Sloan and Cash are included in their entries.

Reporters also can contact the News & Information office for assistance in reaching the professors. Writer Chad Binette can be reached at 407-823-6312 or cbinette@mail.ucf.edu . Assistant Director Tom Evelyn can be reached at 407-823-5988 or tevelyn@mail.ucf.edu .

CANDIDATE STRATEGIES AND ISSUES

Candidate Strategies:

Philip Pollock III, professor of political science; teaches voting and elections, campaign finance, interest groups. Pollock@mail.ucf.edu

Roger Handberg, professor of political science and department chairman; teaches presidential campaigning, security policy, space policy, government and business, science policy. handberg@mail.ucf.edu

Aubrey Jewett, associate professor of political science; research deals with American, state and local politics and Southern politics. ajewett@mail.ucf.edu

Candidate personalities, images:

Philip Pollock III, professor of political science; teaches voting and elections, campaign finance, interest groups. Pollock@mail.ucf.edu

Terri Fine, associate professor of political science; research on political parties, minority politics, gender and politics. tfine@mail.ucf.edu

Foreign policy:

Waltraud Q. Morales, professor of political science; teaches American foreign policy, international drug policy, international politics, Latin American politics, issues involving Third World nations and developing areas, and revolutionary change. morales@mail.ucf.edu

Houman Sadri, associate professor of political science; teaches Middle East politics, international politics, international communications. sadri@mail.ucf.edu

Chris Dolan, assistant professor of political science; teaches U.S. foreign policy, executive actions in foreign policy, public opinion and foreign policy. cdolan@mail.ucf.edu

Iraq and Middle East:

Houman Sadri, associate professor of political science; teaches Middle East politics, international politics, international communications. sadri@mail.ucf.edu

Terrorism:

Stephen Sloan, a fellow in the Global Perspectives Office, has worked for three decades as a consultant to governments and law enforcement agencies worldwide on terrorism and peacekeeping issues. He has conducted simulated terrorist attacks to help agencies prepare for them and is the co-author of "Terrorism: Assassins to Zealots" and the "Historical Dictionary of Terrorism. 407-823-0688, carlos@mail.ucf.edu

Roger Handberg, professor of political science and department chairman; teaches presidential campaigning, security policy, space policy, government and business, science policy. handberg@mail.ucf.edu

Economy:

Roger Handberg, professor of political science and department chairman; teaches presidential campaigning, security policy, space policy, government and business, science policy. handberg@mail.ucf.edu
