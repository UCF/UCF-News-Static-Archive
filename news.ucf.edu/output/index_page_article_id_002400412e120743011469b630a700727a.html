Football Game Day Update: What Fans Should Know

By UCF Staff Sept. 21, 2007

Photo: Jacque Brund

The Marching Knights band will warm up outside the Arena at 11:30 a.m. Saturday before heading inside the facility to play a concert at noon.

As fans get ready for the second game at Bright House Networks Stadium, the University of Central Florida is encouraging them to take another look at game day procedures and guidelines.

The Knights and Memphis Tigers will kick off at 3:30 p.m. at Bright House Networks Stadium.
Up-to-date information and procedures are available at www.ucfgameday.com. UCF has also set up a special telephone help desk that will be active on home football game days at 407-882-FANS or 407-882-3267.

Tickets: Tickets for the UCF-Memphis game can be purchased online at www.ticketmaster.com or by calling the UCF Athletics Ticket Box Office at 407-823-1000. The UCFAA Ticket Box Office is located on the ground floor off the IOA west entry plaza.

Arrive early: All fans are encouraged to arrive on campus early, ideally before 1 p.m., to help avoid last-minute traffic crunches before the game.

Pre-game entertainment: The new UCF Arena will host outdoor entertainment starting at 11 a.m. The Marching Knights band will warm up outside the Arena at 11:30 before heading inside the facility to play a concert at noon. The Arenas outdoor entertainment will continue following the Marching Knights concert.

Knightwalk: The UCF football team is expected to arrive around 1 p.m. at IOA Plaza on the south side of the stadium (by gates 2 and 3). The Marching Knights and cheerleaders will join the team for the Knightwalk through the plaza and into the stadium.

Water and concessions: All fans attending the Memphis game will receive a complimentary bottle of water upon entry. Food and beverages will be sold at several new kiosks and concessions throughout the concourse in addition to the 11 existing locations. Sixteen water fountains are ready for fans to use, and a total of 50 eventually will be installed.

Student entry: Ticketed students with valid IDs are encouraged to enter at the following gates:  2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 11, 12, 13, 14, and 15.

Sportsmanship: This years fan motto  Do the Knight Thing  is all about being a great fan, having fun and staying safe. Fans are encouraged to show respect to UCFs guests from Memphis.

No glass: Fans are reminded that glass is not allowed in the tailgating areas on campus.

Below is a refresher on information about parking, traffic, tailgating and other details of game day.

Parking

There will be about 12,000 parking spots available on campus. Parking is divided into three sections  Gold, Red and Blue.

The Gold Zone, closest to Bright House Networks Stadium, requires a reserved game day parking permit and is only for members of the Golden Knights Club, credentialed media, stadium operations personnel and students with special permits.

Only Gold Zone patrons and those with disabilities will be allowed to drive on and off campus via Orion Boulevard from three hours prior to kickoff until two hours after the game ends.

Almost all parking in the Red Zone and Blue Zone is open to everyone. The spaces are free and on a first-come, first-serve basis.

Parking is not allowed along campus roadways, in residential housing parking lots or garages, or on any open grassy areas throughout campus.

For a more detailed story on parking, click here. 

Traffic

Preferred routes for each of the three parking zones are listed at www.ucfgameday.com , and fans are encouraged to follow them.

Fans should note that Libra Drive will be a one-way street heading toward campus northbound from the Central Florida Research Park starting three hours before game time until halftime. Libra will be a one-way street southbound from campus to the Research Park from halftime until two hours after the game concludes.

Gemini Boulevard North will be closed to vehicle traffic from Alafaya Trail to North Orion Boulevard from three hours prior to game time until two hours after the game ends. This road closure is designed to ensure pedestrian safety between the main and north sections of campus.

UCF will operate about 25 shuttle buses for students to and from several nearby apartment complexes, from five hours prior to the start of the game until three hours after the game ends. The service is free. The on-campus shuttle routes (Black and Gold routes) will not be operating on game days.

For a more detailed news story on traffic, click here.

Tailgating

Tailgating is allowed from 7 a.m. until two hours after the game ends on game days. It is allowed in any ground-level parking lot. It is not permitted in any parking garage on any level. Tailgating activities without vehicles are allowed on most other areas on campus.

UCFs Good Sport program plans on hosting three Good Sport fan zones tied into its Do the Knight Thing fan pledge. The fan zones will have games and music, free pizza, Coca-Cola products, water bottles and other giveaways. The spots are located at Memory Mall from 10 a.m. to 2 p.m., Lake Claire from 11 a.m. to 2:30 p.m. and the Lake Claire residential area from 12 to 2 p.m.

Only those 21 and older are permitted to drink alcoholic beverages. Kegs, beer funnels and glass containers are not allowed on campus.

Fans should recycle plastic and aluminum cans in the 360 UCF Recycles! white cardboard containers located around campus and in the stadium. For a more detailed story on tailgating, click here.

Neighbors

Fans should be respectful of UCFs neighbors. Posted No Parking signs will be along portions of McCulloch Road. And law-enforcement officers will be driving through several subdivisions on game day to encourage fans not to block driveways or park on private property. Parking on campus is free.

Orange and Seminole counties ordinances prohibit open containers of alcohol on public streets or parking lots. For a more detailed news story on UCF working with neighbors, click here.

Stadium security

No alcohol is allowed inside the stadium, except in stadium suites and the club lounge.

Stadium gates will open 90 minutes before kickoff for the general public, and police encourage fans to arrive early because everyone entering the stadium is subject to searches.

Fans are reminded not to bring any prohibited items, including weapons of any sort; video cameras, horns, whistles or artificial noisemakers; alcoholic beverages; bags and backpacks; balloons; banners, signs and flags; and balls of any type.
