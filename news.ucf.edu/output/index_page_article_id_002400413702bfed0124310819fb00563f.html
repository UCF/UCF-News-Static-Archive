WUCF-FM Celebrates 30 Years With Jazz Concert, Station Tour

By Julie Primrose Oct. 27, 2009

Photo: UCF Photo File

The legendary Lou Donaldson performed at the March UCF-Orlando Jazz Festival, co-sponsored by WUCF-FM.

The University of Central Florida’s listener-supported jazz radio station will celebrate its 30th anniversary next month with an on-campus concert and special brunch.

When it first started, WUCF-FM 89.9 broadcast from an old student center and later from studios constructed in the Library basement. The station now has its own state-of-the-art facility within the Nicholson School of Communication, where it provides programming that enriches, educates and entertains listeners.

“For 30 years, our programming has reflected our commitment to deepening the cultural awareness of our community,” said Jan Whitehouse, WUCF’s marketing specialist. Whitehouse added that the station has focused its efforts on the entire Central Florida area -- not just the university.  

To celebrate its success, the station will host a studio tour and reception at its on-campus facility Friday, Nov. 13. Afterward, guests and the Central Florida community are invited to a “Jazz Concert Celebration” in UCF’s Visual Arts Building. The concert, which starts at 8 p.m., will feature world-renowned clarinetist Allan Vaché performing with the UCF Jazz Ensemble. Tickets are $10. Admission is free for students with a UCF ID card.

WUCF will also be featured at the Grand Bohemian Hotel’s weekly Jazz Brunch on Sunday, Nov. 8, with UCF Jazz Studies instructors Jeff Rupert and Per Danielsson. The brunch runs from 10:30 a.m. until 2:30 p.m. Reservations are recommended through http://www.theboheme.com

“We’re proud to feature jazz – one of America’s greatest cultural contributions – on WUCF-FM,” said Kayonne Riley, the station’s director. “On our sister broadcast, WUCF-FM HD2, we feature award-winning programs from National Public Radio, Public Radio International and the BCC. Our two broadcast services provide the community with high-quality programming not available on any other local radio station.”

WUCF also will be honored next month with a resolution from Orange County Government recognizing its 30 years of broadcasting.

Longtime WUCF disc jockey Jack Simpson, who hosts the popular Saturday program “Jazz on the Beach,” looks forward to the anniversary celebrations and says the station has undergone many positive changes during the past three decades.

An on-campus radio station was originally requested by Florida Technological University’s Student Senate. The station switched its name to WUCF-FM when the school was renamed the University of Central Florida in 1979. WUCF became a member station of the Corporation for Public Broadcasting in 1994.

During the past decades, WUCF has kept up-to-date with rapidly changing technology, and it became one of Orlando’s first radio stations to have an all-digital studio.

The station also has continued to benefit the university and students as it has reached out to the community, said Simpson, who has been with WUCF for 26 years.

“There has been a growing emphasis on jazz in the university’s Music Department, and I believe that WUCF has done them a great favor by providing them with a jazz station,” Simpson said. “UCF has become a destination for students wanting to pursue jazz music, due in part to the station’s commitment to the students.”

For a schedule of programs and events or to find out more about the station, go to http://www.wucf.org or contact the station at 407-823-0899.
