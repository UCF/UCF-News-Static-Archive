UCF Students Help Build Playground, Landscape Volusia County Homeless Shelter

By Mallery Laing April 12, 2005

Photo: Courtesy of Team Sixth Cents

Children enjoy the swing set at the Family Renew Community in Holly Hill.

Through a service-learning project, five University of Central Florida students landscaped and helped to build a playground at a homeless shelter in Holly Hill and raised nearly $7,000 for scholarships for homeless children.

Service learning, a growing trend on UCF campuses, is a curriculum program designed to apply classroom knowledge to projects that will benefit the community. Students gain hands-on experience while doing good deeds for nonprofit organizations. At the end of every year, students present their projects and compete for scholarships at the Service Learning Student Showcase. The 2005 showcase, which is open to the public, will be from 1 to 5 p.m. April 14 at the Student Union.

Family Renew Community is a refurbished motel in Holly Hill that was converted into a homeless shelter for young, two-parent families working toward economic stability. Founded in 1989 by churches in the community, the Family Renew Community can host up to 50 families a year.

Students Mary Bonitati, Brenda Boone, Garrett Donigan, Armando Mustiga and Edwin Hadnot, as part of a service-learning business course, decided to work with Family Renew. Bonitati said the project began merely as a landscaping effort and grew quickly.

If we can fix one big problem, it can fix a lot of others, she said.

The one traditional and four nontraditional students set up a fund-raiser to park cars during Race Week in Daytona and raised about $8,200. The Raydon Corp., where Bonitati works as a configuration manager and Boone works as a cost management manager, allowed students to use its parking lots for free. Home Depot of Daytona supplied most of the materials for a playground and landscaping and sent a team of employees to help the students build the playground.

Home Depots help allowed Team Sixth Cents, the name of the students group, to use $6,850 of the money it raised to set up scholarships for the families. Some of the money also may be used to refurbish the Family Renew sign that was damaged during the hurricanes last year.

Claris MacKie, executive director of Family Renew Community, praised the students and the service-learning program.

They did so much work. Some of this we have been wanting to do for over eight years, she said. They have absolutely moved me to tears. These students have immense hearts, all five of them. They went above and beyond the call of this project.

Team Sixth Cents will be one of the groups presenting projects during the second-annual Service Learning Student Showcase on Thursday. Four scholarships provided by the Student Government Association will be given to the top presenters.

I love service-learning courses, Bonitati said. Even as a manager, I can always learn more. The project itself was an experience in volunteering, time management and being faced with homelessness. We can all learn from that."
