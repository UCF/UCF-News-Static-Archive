Richard Walsh, William Peeper Will Be Inducted into New Hospitality Hall of Fame

By Ashley McMeel April 26, 2007

Photo: UCF News file

Richard Walsh, chairman of the UCF Board of Trustees and an inductee into the Central Florida Hospitality Hall of Fame.

Full Photo

University of Central Florida Board of Trustees Chairman Richard J. Walsh and former Orlando/Orange County Convention and Visitors Bureau President William C. Peeper will be inducted into the new Central Florida Hospitality Hall of Fame on Saturday, April 28.

The hall of fame has been established by the Rosen College of Hospitality Management at UCF to honor industry professionals who have made significant contributions to the hospitality industry in Central Florida.

The inductions will be part of the Grande Hospitality Gala, an annual event that will be held at different Central Florida venues. The inaugural gala this year will be hosted by the Rosen College and held at SeaWorlds Ports of Call. The reception will begin at 6 p.m., with dinner and the induction ceremony beginning at about 7 p.m. The event is sold out.

The honorees were selected after consultations with the faculty and staff of the Rosen College and industry executives. Inductees will have their names and photos in the hall of fame at the Rosen College.

Walsh, who retired last year as Darden Restaurants senior vice president for corporate affairs, is president of KnobHill Group, an investment and strategic counseling company. In addition to being a charter member of the UCF Board of Trustees, Walsh serves as a trustee for the UCF Foundation.

Peeper, who retired in January, is the past president of the Orlando/Orange County Convention and Visitors Bureau. He serves on the Rosen College advisory board and in other leadership roles in the community.

Proceeds from this event support the Dick Pope Sr. Institute for Tourism Studies at the Rosen College. The institute was founded in 1979 and was funded through a donation by the Orange Blossom Chapter of the Florida Public Relations Association in honor of Dick Pope Sr., the owner of Cypress Gardens and the first commercial tourist entrepreneur in Florida. Pope was a visionary who forged a path for tourism development in Central Florida. The institutes mission is to improve the quality of the tourism product and increase the benefits of the tourism industry to the State of Florida. 

Concierge services will be provided to all guests by Rosen College students.
