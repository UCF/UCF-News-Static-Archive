Ombuds Officer Helps Students, Faculty and Staff

By Kristi Bartlett Aug. 3, 2005

Photo: Courtesy of Vicky Brown

Vicky Brown has served as UCF's ombuds officer for 11 years.

Full Photo

  • Vicky Brown

Eleven years after she became the first ombudsman at the University of Central Florida, Vicky Brown continues to help UCF students, faculty and staff solve problems.

 

Brown, whose position was established in 1994 by President John Hitt, helps members of the UCF community in an informal, impartial and confidential manner to facilitate fair resolutions to problems that arise on campus, especially those that have not been adequately addressed through normal channels.

 

Brown once worked at the United Nations in New York City before joining UCF in 1990 as an executive assistant in the Presidents Office. She graduated summa cum laude from UCF in 1999 with a bachelors degree in Liberal Studies and received the Presidents Medallion, an award given to the student with the highest GPA in her college.

 

Brown said her experiences in life have helped her become an understanding and capable ombudsman who can try to make a difference in someones life every day.

 

I came to the United States from Havana, Cuba, when I was only 18 and also enrolled in UCF at a later age, she said. I can approach situations from a cultural standpoint and from a students point of view. Going back to school gave me the opportunity to experience UCF as a student, which allows me to better understand issues facing them.

 

Brown credited her position as a United Nations secretary in the Department of Development and Economic Affairs in 1960 as her first professional encounter with diversity and acceptance of different cultures.

 

Shreya Trivedi, the ombuds assistant since 2001, said she believes that Brown is successful because of her patience, warmth and ability to listen.

 

She opens herself up to everyone and treats each case like it is a new one, Trivedi said. Vicky is a compassionate listener and aware of the cultural differences that affect a major university such as UCF. I have learned a lot from watching her work.

 

Brown was recently elected to serve on the inaugural board of directors of the International Ombudsman Association, which formed out of a merger between The Ombudsman Association and the University and College Ombuds Association. Brown is the only ombudsman in the state university system to have ever served on a national ombuds board.

 

For more information on the University Ombuds Office, go to http://pegasus.cc.ucf.edu/~ombuds/.
