UCF Finishes 2010 with National Media Push

By Grant J. Heston Jan. 3, 2011

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Grant J. Heston

    • 407.823.5988

  • Print this Page

Share this story:

  • Share on Facebook
  • Save This Page
  • Seed Newsvine

When Oprah Winfrey decided to salute do-gooders on her "favorite things episode" in November, UCF was well represented. UCF researcher James Turkson, and his wife Jennifer, were invited to the 300-strong audience of the show.

“The whole experience was humbling,” Jennifer Turkson said. “To have Oprah honor you for your work is the biggest gift of all that. Oprah was so genuine and sincere when we met after the show.”

For more recent UCF news, please see below for a review of selected print, broadcast and online media coverage. Also, follow UCF news on Twitter and Facebook, watch UCF TV and learn more about Strategy, Marketing, Communications & Admissions. 

NATIONAL

The Chronicle of Higher Education: Tomorrow's College
Jennifer Black isn't a fan of technology. Until college, she didn't know much about online classes. If the stereotypical online student is a career-minded adult working full time, she's the opposite—a dorm-dwelling, ballet-dancing, sorority-joining 20-year-old who throws herself into campus life here at the University of Central Florida.

U.S. News: Carbon-Rich Planet: A Girl's Best Friend?
Astronomers discover a planet orbiting a star 1200 light-years away; the first carbon-rich world ever observed
-- This story, carried in media around the world, features research from Physics professor Joseph Harrington.

CSPAN: Lectures in History -- Ed Kallina on Richard Nixon
History professor Edmund Kallina teaches American history from Reconstruction to the present at the University of Central Florida.  Today’s “Lecture in History” focuses on the Nixon administration prior to Watergate.
-- This UCF lecture was broadcast nationally on CSPAN.

USA Today: 1.2 Million People Want Jobs But Aren't Looking
A heartening jobs report last week masked an ominous statistic: Discouraged workers hit a record 1.2 million.
-- This story quotes Sean Snaith, director of the Institute for Economic Competitiveness.

L.A. Times: More Global Trade Means More Forest Pests
Along with U.S. manufacturing jobs, you can count another victim of global trade: American trees.
-- This story, also featured on the National Science Foundation's website, features research from UCF Biology professor Betsy Von Holle.

Good Morning America: The Ethics of Cheating
Hundreds of students caught in a cheating scandal at the University of Central Florida in Orlando have been given a choice: Come clean or face the consequences.
-- This story received national and international attention, much of it focused on the efforts UCF makes to combat cheating and our successful College of Business Administration's testing lab.

STATE/LOCAL

Orlando Sentinel: UCF Professor Develops New Cancer Treatment
Dr. James Turkson, an associate professor at the University of Central Florida's Burnett School of Biomedical Sciences, co-developed a chemical compound that may one day cure breast cancer.

Orlando Sentinel: Proud to be a UCF Knight
Nationally, UCF was one of only six schools ranked in the top 25 for football success and APR. While we need to climb back in the polls, our APR standing is solid.
-- This was written by Richard Lapchick, chairman of the DeVos Sport Business Management Graduate Program and director of the Institute for Diversity and Ethics in Sport.

Orlando Sentinel: The 25 Most Powerful People in Central Florida
Collectively, they control tens of thousands of jobs, billions of dollars in tax money, a sports franchise — and one very famous mouse.
-- This list includes UCF President John Hitt, Deborah German, College of Medicine dean and vice president for medical affairs, Board of Trustee member Harris Rosen and several UCF graduates.

Orlando Sentinel: Cheating Isn't UCF's Way
A recent incident of academic misconduct in the University of Central Florida's College of Business Administration has received national attention. Much of that attention, and the subsequent feedback that we have received, has focused on the university's exceptional efforts to create a campus environment where cheating is unacceptable.
-- This is a first-person column written by Tony G. Waldrop, provost and vice president for academic affairs.

Orlando Sentinel: Charles Millican, UCF's Founding President, Dies at Age 94
Charles N. Millican spent his life urging everyone around him to chase big dreams. And he always took his own advice.

Florida Today: Third-Party Groups Try to Sway Elections
Despite some residents saying political advertisements do not influence their vote, experts expect campaign spending this year to surpass money spent in the last mid-term election.
-- This quotes Political Science professor Aubrey Jewett.

Orlando Sentinel: UCF’s New Recruitment Tool -- $25M School of Performing Arts
As prospective students of the performing arts arrive at the University of Central Florida for a special event on Saturday, they will be treated to faculty recitals, speeches and refreshments.
