Richard Zotti Named November Employee of the Month

By Jessica Saggio Dec. 20, 2007

Photo: Courtesy of Human Resources

Employee of the Month Richard Zotti holds his certificate as he is honored by colleagues at the College of Optics and Photonics.

Full Photo

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Employee of the Month Web site

  • Print this Page

With a keen eye for detail and an enthusiastic attitude, Richard Zotti has been named the November Employee of the Month.

Working as an engineer at the College of Optics and Photonics, Zotti teaches machining classes while overseeing students’ work and safety. In addition, he also constructs parts for the optics and photonics labs.

“Richard is always enthusiastic about new and challenging projects,” Zotti’s co-workers said in their nomination. “He takes a very professional approach to tasks at hand. He is highly proficient with job knowledge. His unique deliverables consistently function properly and reflect his exceptional attention to detail.”

For this special honor, Zotti will receive a Barnes and Noble gift certificate, an introduction to the UCF Board of Trustees, a marquee announcement, UCF apparel, a goody bag and a surprise presentation. Zotti also is eligible for the USPS Employee of the Year award, which is announced annually at the Employee Awards Program. One of the 12 USPS employees of the month will be named Employee of the Year and receive a $2,500 check.

If you would like to nominate an employee, fill out the nomination form available on the Human Resources Web site http://hr.ucf.edu/web/employeerecognition/EOM.shtml.

The USPS Employee of the Month is selected by a committee comprised of staff and faculty. If you have questions about the USPS Employee of the Month process, call 407-823-0440.

UCF Stands for Opportunity: Established in 1963, the University of Central Florida is a diverse metropolitan research university that ranks as the 6th-largest in the country with more than 48,000 students. Located in Orlando, Fla., UCF offers high-quality undergraduate and graduate education, student development and continuing education, while conducting cutting-edge research that powers the region's economic development.
