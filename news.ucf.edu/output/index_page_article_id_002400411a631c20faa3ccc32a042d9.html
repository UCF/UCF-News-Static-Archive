Academy Begins New Era

By Tom Evelyn October 2, 2003

Photo: CoE.

Sweeping lines dominate the new education building.

The University of Central Florida began a new era of partnering with educators, business and citizens to produce the best teachers and education research in the nation when it officially opened the Academy for Teaching, Learning and Leadership on Thursday. "The courage, the vision, the hard work that brought us to today, its a synergy I havent seen in our community in a long time," Lt. Gov. Toni Jennings said at the opening ceremony. "The Academy of Teaching, Learning and Leadership will become and already has become a model for our state and our nation." The $11 million, 45,000-square-foot Academy, which was completed in August, will support lifelong learning, partnerships with education and the community, and developing proven and promising practices in teaching, College of Education Dean Sandra Robinson said. Its adjacent to the college, which produces 22 percent of Floridas teachers. "The UCF Academy of Teaching, Learning and Leadership will be a cornerstone of excellence," UCF President John Hitt said, adding that the entire university had a hand in making the Academy a reality. Other deans agreed to give the creation and construction of the Academy priority, moving it to the top of a long list of projects. The Academy houses one-of-a-kind programs for teaching teachers, who "are the foundation of all we do," Jennings said. "Its more than reading, writing and reasoning. Its character-building. Its about preparing our future." The Academy includes the Toni Jennings Exceptional Education Institute, named for the former state legislator who has been instrumental in supporting education initiatives. Other programs include the Floridas Literacy and Reading Excellence Center, or FLaRE; the Lockheed Martin Academy for Mathematics and Science; the Consortium for Social Responsibility and Character in Education; and the UCF Fellows, a collaboration on intercollegiate, multidisciplinary projects that advance research focused on K-20 education. The Academy is partnering with other educational institutions, including the Department of Education, the 11 school districts in Central Florida, businesses and community agencies to prepare teachers, counselors, school psychologists and school administrators to be competent and caring professional educators. "Education and economic development go hand-in-hand," said Jennings, adding that the Academy has partnered with more than 30 companies, Lockheed Martin, Progress Energy and Toyota. Robinson and Academy Executive Director Donna Leinsing gathered focus groups  parents, teachers, students and researchers  to get a sense of what was needed in the new structure. They developed the programs and then designed the building. Supporting the idea of the Academy being a cornerstone of excellence in education, they narrowed its mission: Support pre-service and experienced educators who come together as a community of learners to acquire knowledge and develop skills. Build relationships with partners focused on the needs of a diverse community of learners. Research and document the effectiveness of proven practices as well as effective models focused on innovative and promising educational practices.
