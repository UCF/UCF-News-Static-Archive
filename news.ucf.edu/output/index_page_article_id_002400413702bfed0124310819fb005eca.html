UCF On Stage: Swash-Buckling Musical Opens This Week

By UCF Staff Oct. 22, 2009

Photo: Matthew Brandt

Rachel Davis (left), Terry Alfaro and James Rinaldi appear in UCF's rendition of a Gilbert & Sullivan classic.

The University of Central Florida Conservatory Theatre’s latest performance will tickle your fancy, excite your imagination and delight your eyes.

“The Pirates of Penzance” opens Thursday, Oct. 22, and runs through Sunday, Nov. 1. The musical is directed by Brian Vernon, a UCF dance and theatre professor.

Gilbert & Sullivan are famous for their fun-filled parodies. Together, they created many wryly-satirical operettas, such as “H.M.S Pinafore” and “The Mikado.” Good social satire remains timeless -- and the music and dance in the duo’s “The Pirates of Penzance” is gleefully fun, with acts such as “Poor Wandering One” and “I Am the Very Model of a Modern Major General.”

“The Pirates of Penzance” will be at the Main Stage theatre on UCF’s main campus. Performances will run Oct. 22, 23, 24, 25, 29, 30 and 31 and Nov. 1. All shows begin at 8 p.m., except for Sunday matinees, which will start at 2 p.m.

Ticket prices are $17 for adults, $15 for seniors and $10 for students with their ID.  Reservations are recommended and available through the UCF Conservatory Theatre box office at 407-823-1500. Group rates are available. The box office can assist with accommodations for patrons with disabilities.

UCF Stands For Opportunity: The University of Central Florida is a metropolitan research university that ranks as the third largest in the nation with more than 53,500 students. UCF's first classes were offered in 1968. The university offers impressive academic and research environments that power the region's economic development. UCF's culture of opportunity is driven by our diversity, Orlando environment, history of entrepreneurship and our youth, relevance and energy. For more information visit http://news.ucf.edu.
