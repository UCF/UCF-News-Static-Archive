Medical College Dean Candidate to Return for Follow-Up Interviews, Forum

By Tom Evelyn Oct. 3, 2006

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Tom Evelyn

    • 407-823-5988

  • UCF College of Medicine

  • Deborah German vita

  • Print this Page

Deborah German, a candidate in the search for a founding dean of the UCF College of Medicine, will return to Orlando next week for follow-up interviews and an open forum.

Dr. German is one of five dean candidates who interviewed with a search committee two weeks ago. Two of the candidates who also had been scheduled to return for more interviews withdrew from the search this week.

German will meet with members of the universitys Board of Trustees, several administrators and the deans of UCFs other colleges beginning Monday, Oct. 9. She is scheduled to participate in the open forum from 9:30 to 10:50 a.m. Tuesday, Oct. 10, in the Pegasus Ballroom, AB, at the Student Union.

For the forum, German has been asked to present her ideas about medical education, talk about why she is interested in the position and answer questions from the audience.

German is a Petersdorf Scholar in Residence at the Association of American Medical Colleges in Washington, D.C., where she has studied since 2005. She previously served as president and chief executive officer at Saint Thomas Hospital and as senior vice president and chief academic officer for Saint Thomas Health Services in Nashville from 2002 to 2004. German also served as senior associate dean of Medical Education at the Vanderbilt University School of Medicine from 1999 to 2002.

Her vita is available at the provosts Web site at www.provost.ucf.edu.

A search committee, UCF President John Hitt and Provost and Executive Vice President Terry Hickey interviewed five dean candidates Sept. 19. Dr. Bruce Jarrell withdrew shortly after that initial interview. Drs. Bruce Meyer and Mark Pfeifer, withdrew from the search this week.

Dr. Thomas Schwenk, a professor and chair of Family Medicine at the University of Michigan Medical School, could not return next week for additional interviews because of schedule conflicts.

In March, the state Board of Governors approved the establishment of a college of medicine at UCF. The medical dean will have to focus immediately on getting accreditation for the college and developing its curriculum. The college is scheduled to start its first class in fall 2009.
