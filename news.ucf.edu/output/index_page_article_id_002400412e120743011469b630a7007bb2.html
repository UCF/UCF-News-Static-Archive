UCF Engineering and Computer Science Ranks Seventh In the Nation for Hispanic Students

By Suhtling Wong Sept. 5, 2007

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Suhtling Wong

    • 407-823-1698

  • College of Engineering and Computer Science

  • Print this Page

For the third consecutive year, the University of Central Floridas College of Engineering and Computer Science has been ranked as one of the top colleges for graduate studies in engineering for Hispanic students by Hispanic Business magazine.

The college was ranked along with Purdue University and the University of California at Irvine, and it edged out Massachusetts Institute Technology for seventh position. The rankings are in this months magazine available on newsstands on Sept. 1. The rankings are based on several factors: enrollment, faculty diversity, student services, recruiting, retention rate and reputation.

This speaks volumes about the quality of the programs we offer and our commitment to recruiting and retaining a diverse faculty and student body, said Jamal Nayfeh, associate dean of Academic Affairs for the College of Engineering and Computer Science. We are extremely proud of being recognized and ranked with the best in the nation, he added.

The college actively recruits minority students and offers a wealth of resources so students will be successful.

We offer our students financial help and career placement after they graduate, said Cristina Dos Santos, director of diversity for the College of Engineering and Computer Science. Dos Santos also helps mentor underrepresented students and participates in outreach initiatives to encourage minority enrollment through the UCF branch of the Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers.

Efforts are paying off. Just over nine percent of the students in the college are Hispanic and university-wide Hispanics make up more than 12 percent of students. Earlier this year Hispanic Outlook in Higher Education Magazine also recognized UCF as one of the top 100 universities for Hispanic students.

The College of Engineering and Computer Science employs 244 faculty and staff to assist 5,849 students and strives to offer a high-quality education that encourages the exploration and creation of new solutions to problems.

For more information, go to www.cecs.ucf.edu.
