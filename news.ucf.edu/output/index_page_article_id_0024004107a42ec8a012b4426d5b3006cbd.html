Want National Media to Call? Be a UCF Expert

By Grant J. Heston Oct. 4, 2010

Related Contacts and Links:

  • Grant J. Heston

    • 407.823.5988

  • Print this Page

Share this story:

  • Share on Facebook
  • Save This Page
  • Seed Newsvine

National media, including Fortune Magazine, Time, USA Today and more, recently turned to UCF staff and faculty members for expert commentary.

Please see below for a review of selected UCF print, broadcast and online media coverage. Follow UCF news on Twitter and Facebook, watch UCF TV and learn more about Strategy, Marketing, Communications & Admissions.

NATIONAL

Fortune Magazine: Disconnect Between Productivity and Hiring
Generally employers hire more employees to boost productivity. There are reasons why, despite dropping productivity numbers, it's not happening this time around.
-- This story was generated by a new weekly UCF TV feature, "Money Clips," with Sean Snaith, director of the Institute for Economic Competitiveness.

Time Magazine: House Races to Watch
Until a couple of years ago, the Republicans' grip on Florida's Eighth Congressional District felt as tight as Disney World's hold on the family vacation.
-- This quotes Political Science professor Aubrey Jewett.

USA TODAY: WNBA's Female Owners Open Doors, But What Is Next?
Four teams in the WNBA are owned by women, a full third of the league, and rock star Eddie Van Halen can take a bit of back-handed credit for that.
-- This story quotes Richard Lapchick, director of UCF's Institute for Diversity and Ethics in Sport.

Business Week: Disney Launches Website for User-Generated Content
Disney upped its social-media profile big-time Thursday by launching a website with the sole purpose of showcasing photos, videos and other user-generated content from park visitors and fans.
-- This story quotes Duncan Dickson, a professor in the Rosen College of Hospitality Management, and was published in media outlets around the country.

Business Week: Economic Pain Failed to Ease in July
Americans' economic struggles persisted in July, largely unchanged from the previous month, according to The Associated Press' monthly analysis of conditions around the country.
-- This story quotes Sean Snaith, director of the Institute for Economic Competitiveness, and was published in media outlets around the country.

Campus Technology: A Better Blend
The hybrid approach to learning has benefited both from the trials of early pioneers and the technological advances of recent years.
-- This story features interviews and data from UCF leaders.

STATE/LOCAL

Orlando Sentinel: UCF Plans $50 Million Expansion of Dorms
The additions would help the university get closer to its goal of providing on-campus housing to at least 80 percent of fall freshmen, up from the current 67 percent.

Florida Trend: Must Know Floridian
Meet Dr. Pappachan E. Kolattukudy, Professor and Director, Burnett School of Biomedical Sciences at UCF. Under Dr. Kolattunkudy's direction, BSBS conducts cutting edge research in biomedicine with the potential to cure major diseases.

Orlando Sentinel: Beacon of Light in Dark Economy
College students pursuing health-care careers are finding plenty of opportunity in an otherwise bleak job market.

Florida Today: Solar's Time to Shine in Florida?
Jacque Wirth was a local pioneer in getting consumers interested in harnessing the sun for electricity.
-- This story highlights UCF's solar leadership and a possible Sematech - UCF partnership.

WMFE: Mosquito Concerns in Florida
With mosquito-borne diseases on the rise, public health officials are increasing mosquito control and surveillance efforts.
-- This story features the work of, and an interview with, professor Henry Daniell from the Burnett School of Biomedical Sciences in the College of Medicine.

Orlando Sentinel: Engineering Program Ranked High for Hispanic Students
Hispanic Business magazine ranked the University of Central Florida College of Engineering and Computer Science at the top of its list of “Best Engineering Schools” in the September 2010 edition of its national publication.

Tampa Tribune: Gay Advocates Praise Crist's Position Paper
In a new Senate campaign position paper, Gov. Charlie Crist backs a broad swath of gay rights, including one major change in his past stance – he favors allowing adoption by same-sex couples.
-- This quotes Political Science professor Aubrey Jewett.
