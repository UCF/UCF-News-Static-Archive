Marketplace, New Dining Hall Going Trayless to Save Energy and Water

By UCF Staff Aug. 20, 2008

Photo: Courtesy of UCF Dining Services

Knightro's, an all-you-care-to-eat restaurant where food is prepared in front of customers, will open Thursday, Aug. 21, by the UCF Arena.

To save water and energy, the University of Central Florida’s two main dining halls will go trayless this week, just in time for the fall semester.

UCF Dining Services will remove trays from the Marketplace dining hall and open the new Knightro’s dining facility on the east side of the Arena without trays.

Research by ARAMARK, UCF’s dining provider, found that trayless dining decreases food waste by 25 to 30 percent, in addition to conserving water and energy by reducing the number of dishes that need to be washed. The ARAMARK study also found that customers are ready for trayless dining.

ARAMARK estimates that 50 to 60 percent of its 500 campus partners will go trayless in the upcoming school year.

“Trayless dining, while often overlooked, is an initiative that has immediate and tangible environmental, social, and economic benefits for UCF,” said Julie Bream, marketing manager for UCF Dining Services.  “Trayless dining decreases waste and saves water and energy. Socially, it encourages all students to participate in a ‘green’ initiative that has personal and community impact.  Economically, going trayless reduces the cost of energy, water, cleaning agents and waste removal.”

Knightro's, an all-you-care-to-eat restaurant where food is prepared in front of customers, will open Thursday, Aug. 21, on the east side of the UCF Arena.

Knightro's seats 350 and will be open for lunch and dinner throughout the academic year and on days when there are UCF Arena events and football games. Like the Marketplace, Knightro’s will accept student, faculty and staff meal plans, and patrons also can pay with cash.

Knightro's, which is operated by UCF Dining Services and ARAMARK, represents the latest transformation in on-campus dining, presenting fresh, healthy food at nine different stations: Southern Kitchen, Deli, Produce Market, Pizza, Vegetarian, Smoothie, Dessert, Soup and the Grill. The Vegetarian Station offers vegetarian students the opportunity to enjoy options made especially for them. Dishes include vegan rosemary polenta with broccoli or sweet and sour tofu stir fry. The Southern Station is home to Knightro's smoker, cooking delectable meats such as pork, beef and turkey to perfection.

“UCF Dining Services is excited about the addition of Knightro’s to our dining program,” said Marvin Grand, resident district manager for UCF Dining Services. “With this new restaurant, we are expanding our services to all areas of the campus and know that this state-of-the-art facility will surely enhance the student experience at UCF.”

The kitchens at Knightro's are in front of the visitors, making special requests easy for the staff to prepare. All fried food at Knightro's is prepared in trans-fat free oil, and nutritional information is available at a kiosk in Knightro's or on the UCF Dining Services Web site, www.ucfdining.com.

For hours and more information about the Marketplace and Knightro’s, go to www.ucfdining.com.
